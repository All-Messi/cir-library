Jennifer Chantell Wenzl

On behalf of the Wenzls, I want to thank you for this wonderful demonstration of support for the family and for such an overwhelming expression of love for Jenny in this time of intense sorrow.

Grief is our response to loss, and the deepest loss is the loss of a person.

Not so much what a person does for/with us, but who she is as expressed through her actions toward us.

says by what she says to us.

can do by how she uses those abilities

Our sense of loss is primarily over a loss of who someone in relation to us.

Identity: Who she is.

Our identity is what counts. It is out of who we are that flow our deeds and our words.

  1. First dimension: Identity consists of our attitude toward other people. It is love in the best sense of that word.

“ “ beliefs and values

This is what we mean by “heart”: “I guess it all comes down to where your heart is.”

HEART

  1. Second dimension: Who we are consists of what we have gone through and how we have handled it.

Who we are involves the sum total of what we have done.

EXPERIENCE

Then finally, that heart and that experience are brought to bear in our interactions with other people:

In our minds Jenny is who she has been toward us. Again, that means love again. That is what the Bible means when it says, 1 Corinthians 13:4-8a.

To the extent that these qualities are woven together in our personalities, we approach the ideal of what it means to be a person—to be who we ought to be with other people.
