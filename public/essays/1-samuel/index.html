1 SAMUEL

Conversational Version

The Birth of Samuel

1:1Elkanah was from Ramathaim-zophim in the hill country of Ephraim. He was the son of Jeroham son of Elihu (son of Tohu son of Zuph), an Ephraimite. 2He had two wives: Hannah and Peninnah. Peninnah had children but Hannah didn’t.

3He went up to Shiloh every year to worship and sacrifice to the LORD of Hosts. Eli’s two sons, Hophni and Phinehas, were priests there. 4When Elkanah sacrificed, he’d give portions to Peninnah and her sons and daughters; 5but he’d give Hannah a double portion because he loved her. But the LORD had closed her womb, 6and her rival would bully her mercilessly about it 7year after year. As often as she went up to the LORD’s Tabernacle, Peninnah would torment her. She’d cry and wouldn’t eat. 8Elkanah asked her, “Hannah, why are you crying and not eating? Why are you sad? Am I not better to you than ten sons?” 1 Sam 1:1-8

9Hannah got up after the meal, while Eli the priest was sitting on the seat by the doorpost of the LORD’S Tabernacle. 10She was distressed, and prayed and cried bitterly. 11She took a vow,

“LORD of Hosts, if you’ll look on my grief and give me a son, I’ll give him to you all his life. He’ll never shave his head.”

12Eli was watching her mouth as she prayed. 13Her lips were moving, but she wasn’t making any sound. Eli thought she was drunk.

14“How long will you be drunk? Put your wine away.”

15“No, my lord, I’m depressed. I haven’t drunk wine or alcohol. I’m pouring out my heart to the LORD. 16Don’t consider me some worthless woman. I’ve been speaking out of my great anguish and distress.”

17“May God grant your petition.”

18“Be gracious to me.” So she went away and ate and wasn’t sad anymore.

19They got up early and worshiped in the LORD’s presence and went back to Ramah. Elkanah had relations with Hannah, and the LORD remembered her. 20In due time, she had a son and named him Samuel, “Because I’ve ‘asked’ the LORD for him.” 1 Sam 1:9-20

Hannah Dedicates Samuel

21Elkanah went up with his whole family to offer the yearly sacrifice and pay his vow. 22But Hannah wouldn’t go, “I won’t go till I’ve weaned him. Then I’ll present him to the LORD to stay there.”

23Elkanah said, “Do what you think’s best. Wait till you’ve weaned him. May the LORD confirm his word.”

She stayed and nursed him till she weaned him. 24Then she took him up with her, along with a three-year-old bull, 1.1 bushel 1 ephah of flour, and a skin of wine. She took him to the LORD’s Tabernacle in Shiloh even though the child was so young. 25They butchered the bull and took the boy to Eli. 26She said,

“As sure as you’re alive, Sir, I’m the woman that stood here beside you, praying to the LORD. 27I prayed for this boy, and the LORD gave me what I asked for. 28I’ve dedicated him to the LORD for as long as he lives.”

He worshipped the LORD there. 1 Sam 1:21-28

Hannah’s Prayer

2:1Then Hannah prayed,

“My heart celebrates in the LORD;

you’ve honored me.

I speak boldly against my adversaries

because I celebrate over your salvation.

2There’s no one holy like the LORD;

indeed, there’s no one besides you at all.

There’s no rock like our God.

3Don’t brag anymore with such pride,

because the LORD is a God who knows;

he weighs what we do.

4The bows of the strong are shattered,

but the feeble increase strength.

5Those who were full are hiring themselves out for bread,

but those who were hungry are now full.

6The LORD kills and makes live;

he takes down to Sheol and raises up.

7He makes people poor and rich;

he brings them low and honors them.

8He lifts the poor up out of the dust;

he raises the needy off the trash pile

to seat them with nobles,

to inherit places of honor.

The pillars of the earth belong to him;

he’s set the world on them.

9He protects his holy people,

but silences the sinful in darkness

because people don’t prevail by their own power.

10The LORD shatters those who contend with him,

and thunders in the sky against them.

He’ll judge the whole earth;

he’ll give strength to his king

and elevate the one he’s anointed.”

11Then Elkanah went home to Ramah, but the boy served the LORD in Eli’s presence.

1 Sam 2:1-11

Eli’s Wicked Sons

12Eli’s sons were worthless men that didn’t know the LORD 13or the custom of priests with the people. When anybody was offering a sacrifice, the priest’s servant would come with a three-pronged fork while the meat was boiling. 14He’d thrust it into the pan or kettle, caldron, or pot. Everything the fork brought up, the priests would take. They did that to all the Israelites that came to Shiloh. 15Before they burned the fat, the priest’s servant would say, “Give the priest meat for roasting. He won’t accept boiled meat from you, only raw.” 16If the man said, “They have to burn the fat first, and then take as much as you want,” he’d say, “Give it to me now, or I’ll take it by force.” 17The young men were sinning greatly because they didn’t respect the LORD’s offering.

1 Sam 2:12-17

18Samuel was serving the LORD as a boy wearing a linen ephod. 19His mother would make him a small robe and bring it to him each year when she came up with her husband to offer the sacrifice. 20Eli would bless Elkanah and his wife, “May the LORD give you children from this woman to replace the one she dedicated to him.” They went home, 21and the LORD visited Hannah; she had three more sons and two daughters. Samuel grew up in the LORD’s presence.

22Eli was old. He heard what his sons were doing—lying with the women that served at the doorway of the Tent of Meeting. 23He said,

“Why do you do the sinful things I’m hearing? 24Sons, the report is not good that I hear the LORD’s people spreading around. 25If one man sins against another, God can intercede for him; but if a man sins against the LORD, who can intercede for him?”

But they ignored him in keeping with the fact that the LORD wanted to put them to death. 26The boy Samuel was growing in height and favor with the LORD and the people. 1 Sam 2:18-26

Warning About Eli’s Family

27Then a man of God came to Eli,

“The LORD says, ‘I revealed myself to your ancestor’s house when they were in bondage to Pharaoh. 28I chose them from Israel’s tribes to be my priests: to go up to my altar, to burn incense, to carry an ephod in front of me. I gave your ancestor the fire offerings of Israel. 29Why are you greedy about the sacrifices and offerings I’ve commanded? Why are you honoring your sons more than me by making yourselves fat with the best of every offering my people make?’

30“So the LORD God of Israel says, ‘I did say that your house and your father’s would follow me forever, but I’m certainly not going to carry through on that now. I’ll honor the ones that honor me, and care less about the ones that despise me. 31The time’s coming when I’ll get rid of your strength and the strength of your ancestor’s house. There’ll never be an old man in your family. 32In distress you’ll see the prosperity I’ll put on Israel. 33But I won’t remove every man of yours from my altar and make your eyes fail from crying, make you grieve, make the added number in your house die in their prime. 34This will be the sign about your sons Hophni and Phinehas: they’ll die the same day. 35But I’ll raise up a faithful priest to do what’s in my heart and soul. I’ll build him a lasting lineage, and he’ll follow my anointed ways. 36Everybody that’s left in your house will come and bow down to him for a piece of silver or a loaf of bread, and say, “Please assign me to one of the priest’s offices so I’ll have something to eat.”’” 1 Sam 2:27-36

The Call of Samuel

3:1The boy Samuel continued to serve the LORD with Eli. A message from the LORD rarely came at that time; visions weren’t frequent. 2Eli was lying down. (His eyesight had gotten bad.) 3The LORD’s lamp hadn’t gone out yet, and Samuel was lying down in the Tabernacle where the Ark of God was. 4The LORD called out to him. He said, “Yes?” 5He ran to Eli and said, “You called.” But he said, “I didn’t call; lie back down.” So he went and lay down. 6The LORD called out again, “Samuel.” He got up and went to Eli, “You called.” But he answered, “I didn’t call you, son; lie back down.” 7Samuel didn’t know the LORD yet, and the LORD hadn’t revealed a message to him before. 8The LORD called out to him the third time. He got up and went to Eli, “You called.” Eli surmised that the LORD was calling the boy. 9He told him, “Go lie down. If he calls you, say, ‘Yes, LORD; I’m listening.’” So he went and lay down. 1 Sam 3:1-9

10The LORD came and stood and called out like before, “Samuel!”

He said, “Yes, LORD, I’m listening.”

11He said,

“I’m about to do something in Israel that will make everybody’s ears ring. 12I’ll carry out against Eli everything I’ve said about his house from beginning to end. 13I told him I’m about to condemn his house forever for the sin he knew about. His sons have brought a curse on themselves, and he didn’t restrain them. 14I’ve sworn to his house that sacrifice and offering won’t atone for its great sin.”

15Samuel lay down till morning. He opened the doors of the LORD’s Tabernacle, but he was afraid to tell Eli about the vision.

16Then Eli called him.

17“What did he say? Don’t hold it back from me. God do so to you and more if you keep back anything he said.”

18Samuel told him everything.

“It’s the LORD. Let him do what he thinks is right.”

19Samuel grew, the LORD was with him, and didn’t let any of his message fail. 20Israel from Dan to Beersheba knew he was confirmed as a prophet for the LORD. 21[4:1]The LORD appeared again at Shiloh and revealed himself to Samuel in a message. 1 Sam 3:10-21

The Philistines Capture the Ark

4:1Samuel’s word went out to all Israel.

[4:1]Israel had gone out to meet the Philistines in battle and camped beside Ebenezer. The Philistines camped in Aphek 2and drew up in formation against Israel. When the battle spread, the Philistines defeated Israel and killed about 4,000. 3When the troops came to camp, Israel’s elders said,

“Why has the LORD routed us in front of the Philistines? Let’s bring the Ark of the Covenant from Shiloh so he can come among us and save us from our enemies.”

4The people sent to Shiloh and brought the Ark of the Covenant of the LORD of Hosts Enthroned Above the Cherubs. Hophni and Phinehas were there with it. 1 Sam 4:1-4

5When the Ark came into camp, Israel shouted so loud the earth shook. 6The Philistines heard it, “What does that loud shouting mean in the Hebrews’ camp?” When they learned that the Ark of the LORD had come into camp, 7they were afraid,

“God has come into the camp. Nothing like this has ever happened before. 8Who can deliver us from these powerful gods? They’re the ones that struck the Egyptians with all kinds of plagues. 9Take courage, Philistines, so you won’t become slaves to the Hebrews like they’ve been to you. Be men and fight!”

10They fought and defeated Israel, and every man fled home. There was a great massacre; 30,000 of Israel’s foot soldiers fell. 11The Philistines captured God’s Ark and killed Eli’s two sons.

1 Sam 4:5-11

The Death of Eli

12A man from Benjamin ran to Shiloh the same day with his clothes torn and dust on his head. 13Eli was sitting on his seat by the road watching because he was worried about the Ark. When the man came into the town with the news, the whole town cried out. 14Eli heard the outcry and asked what it was about. The man hurried over to tell him. 15He was 98 and couldn’t see.

16The man told him, “I fled from the battle today.”

“How’d it go?”

17“Israel fled. The Philistines have slain a great number, including your two sons, and they’ve captured the Ark.”

18When he mentioned the Ark, Eli fell backward off his seat by the gate, broke his neck, and died. He was old and heavy. He’d judged Israel for 40 years. 1 Sam 4:12-18

19His daughter-in-law, Phinehas’s wife, was pregnant. When she heard that the Ark had been captured and that her father-in-law and husband were dead, she went into labor, kneeled, and gave birth. 20About the time she died, the women attending her said, “Don’t be afraid; you’ve had a son.” But she didn’t respond. 21She named him Ichabod, “The ‘glory is gone’ from Israel because they’ve captured God’s Ark,” and because her father-in-law and husband had died. 1 Sam 4:19-22

The Philistines and the Ark

5:1The Philistines carried the Ark from Ebenezer to Ashdod 2to Dagon’s temple and set it up beside him. 3When people got up early the next morning, Dagon had fallen face down on the ground in front of the Ark of the LORD. They put Dagon back in place. 4But when they got up early the next day, he’d fallen face down on the ground in front of the Ark of the LORD, and his head and hands were lying cut off on the threshold. Only his trunk was left. 5That’s why Dagon’s priests and everybody that goes in his temple still don’t step on his threshold in Ashdod.

1 Sam 5:1-5

6The LORD heavily oppressed the people in Ashdod and around. He terrified them by striking them with tumors. 7When the men in Ashdod saw what was happening, they said, “The Ark of Israel’s God must not stay here. He’s heavily oppressing us and our god Dagon.” 8They gathered the Philistine lords, “What can we do with it?”

“Take it to Gath.”

So they did, 9but the LORD oppressed the city and caused a panic. He struck the people, young and old, with tumors. 10They sent it to Ekron. The people there cried out, “They’ve brought the Ark here to kill us.” 11They gathered the lords of the Philistines, “Send it back where it belongs so it won’t kill us.” A deathly panic gripped the city. God heavily oppressed it. 12People that didn’t die developed tumors, and the outcry from the city rose to the sky. 1 Sam 5:6-12

The Ark Returns to Israel

6:1The LORD’s Ark was in Philistine territory for seven months. 2They called for the priests and fortunetellers, “What should we do with it? Tell us how to send it back.”

3“Send it back with a guilt offering to Israel’s God. You’ll be healed, and you’ll find out why he keeps working against you.”

4“What guilt offering should we send him?”

“Five gold tumors and five gold mice, to match the number of Philistine lords. The same plague has struck you and your lords. 5So make images of your tumors and the mice that are ravaging the land and honor Israel’s God. Maybe he’ll lighten his oppression on you, your gods, and your land. 6Why harden your hearts like Pharaoh and the Egyptians did? After he’d mocked them, didn’t they end up letting the people go? 7Build a new cart and take two milk cows that have never worn a yoke. Yoke them to the cart but take their calves away from them. 8Put the Ark on the cart. In a box beside it put the gold figurines you’re returning to him as a guilt offering. Send it off, and 9watch it. If it goes on the road to Beth-shemesh in its own land, he’s the one that has done us this great harm. If not, we’ll know it happened by chance.” 1 Sam 6:1-9

10They yoked two milk cows to the cart, shut up their calves at home, 11and put the Ark on the cart along with a box containing the gold mice and the images of their tumors. 12The cows went straight toward Beth-shemesh along one road without veering off, bawling as they went. The lords of the Philistines followed them as far as the outskirts of Beth-shemesh. 13The people there were harvesting wheat in the valley. When they looked up and saw the Ark, they were overjoyed. 14It came and stopped in a field that belonged to Joshua of Beth-shemesh. There was a large rock there. They split up the wood from the cart and offered the cows as a burnt offering to the LORD. 15Levites took down the Ark and the box and set them on the large rock. The men of Beth-shemesh offered sacrifices and burnt offerings that day. 16The five lords of the Philistines saw it and went back to Ekron.

17As a guilt offering to the LORD, the Philistines returned one gold tumor each for Ashdod, Gaza, Ashkelon, Gath, and Ekron. 18The gold mice matched the number of fortified towns and unwalled villages that belonged to the five Philistine lords. The large rock they set the Ark by is still a witness in the field of Joshua of Beth-shemesh. 1 Sam 6:10-18

19The LORD killed 70 men from Beth-shemesh because they looked in the Ark. The people mourned because he’d killed so many. 20Then the men of Beth-shemesh said, “Who can stand against this holy God? Who will go up when he leaves us?” 21They sent messengers to Kiriath-jearim, “The Philistines have brought back the Ark of the LORD. Come down and get it.” 7:1The men came and took it to Abinadab’s house on the hill. They consecrated his son Eleazar to take charge of it. 2It was there some 20 years, and Israel mourned after the LORD. 1 Sam 6:19-7:2

Samuel Judges Israel

3Then Samuel told Israel, “If you’re coming back to the LORD with all your heart, put away the foreign gods and the images of Ashtoreth. Direct your heart to serve him only, and he’ll deliver you from the Philistines.” 4Israel got rid of the images of Baal and Ashtoreth and served the LORD only.

5Samuel said, “Gather Israel to Mizpah, and I’ll pray to the LORD for you.” 6They gathered there, drew water, and poured it out to the LORD. They fasted that day and said, “We’ve sinned against the LORD.” Samuel judged Israel at Mizpah.

7When the Philistines heard that Israel had gathered at Mizpah, they went up to attack them. The people heard about it and were afraid. 8They told Samuel, “Don’t stop calling out to the LORD our God for us to save us from the Philistines.” 9He offered up a suckling lamb as a whole burnt offering. He appealed to the LORD for Israel, and he answered him. 10As he was offering it, the Philistines came to attack; but the LORD thundered with a loud noise that threw them into a panic. 11Israel chased them and kept striking them down as far as below Beth-car. 1 Sam 7:3-11

12Then Samuel set up a “rock” between Mizpah and Shen. He called it Ebenezer because the LORD had ‘helped’ them that far. 13The Philistines didn’t invade Israel again. The LORD opposed them Samuel’s whole life. 14The towns they’d taken reverted to Israel from Ekron to Gath. Israel rescued its territory from them. Israel and the Amorites were at peace too.

15Samuel judged Israel his whole life 16on a circuit each year to Bethel, Gilgal, and Mizpah. 17Then he’d come back to his house in Ramah. He administered justice there as well and built an altar to the LORD. 1 Sam 7:12-17

Israel Demands a King

8:1When Samuel was getting older, he made his sons judges over Israel: 2Joel his firstborn and Abijah. They judged in Beersheba, 3but they didn’t follow Samuel’s ways. They pursued dishonest gain, took bribes, and perverted justice.

4Israel’s elders gathered to Samuel at Ramah, 5“You’re getting old, and your sons don’t follow your ways. Appoint a king to govern us like the other nations. 6That displeased Samuel. He prayed to the LORD, 7and the LORD said,

“Listen to them. They’re not rejecting you as much as rejecting me from being their king. 8As they’ve done ever since I brought them out of Egypt—leaving me and serving other gods—so they’re doing to you. 9Give in to them but warn them solemnly how kings will rule.” 1 Sam 8:1-9

Samuel Warns About Having Kings

10Samuel told the people everything the LORD said.

11“Be aware that this is how a king will rule. He’ll take your sons and appoint them to his chariots, to be his cavalry, to run ahead of his chariots. 12He’ll appoint commanders of thousands and fifties, and some to plow his ground, reap his harvest, and make weapons and equipment for his chariots. 13He’ll take your daughters for perfumers, cooks, and bakers. 14He’ll take your best fields, vineyards, and olive orchards and give them to his officials. 15He’ll take a tenth of your grain and your vineyards, and give it to his attendants. 16He’ll take your male and female servants and the best of your livestock and donkeys and put them to his work. 17He’ll take a tenth of your flocks, and you’ll be his slaves. 18You’ll cry out because you’ve chosen a king, but the LORD won’t answer you on the matter.”

The LORD Grants Israel ‘s Request

19But the people wouldn’t listen. They said, “We want a king 20so we’ll be like other nations. He can govern us and lead us in battle.”

21When Samuel heard what they said, he repeated it to the LORD.

22The LORD said, “Give in to them and appoint a king.”

Samuel told Israel, “Every one of you, go home.” 1 Sam 8:10-22

Saul Chosen as King

9:1A wealthy man in Benjamin named Kish son of Abiel (son of Zeror son of Becorath son of Aphiah) 2had a son named Saul, a handsome young man. There wasn’t a man in Israel more handsome than he was. He was head and shoulders taller than everybody.

3Kish’s donkeys were lost so he told Saul to take a servant and look for them. 4They went through the hill country of Ephraim, through the territory around Shalishah, Shaalim, and Benjamin, but couldn’t find them.

5When they came to the area around Zuph, Saul told his servant, “Let’s go back so my father doesn’t stop caring about the donkeys and start worrying about us.”

6But the servant said, “There’s a man of God in this town that people regard highly. Everything he says comes true. Let’s go there; maybe he can tell us about the trip we’re on.”

7Saul said, “If we go, what can we take him? We don’t have any bread left in our sacks; we don’t have a present. What do we have?”

8The servant answered, “I have a tenth of an oz a fourth of a shekel of silver. I’ll give it to him to tell us where to go.” (9Earlier in Israel, when someone went to ask God, they’d say, “Let’s go to the seer.” What they call a prophet they used to call a seer.)

10Saul said, “All right.” They went to the town where the man of God was. 1 Sam 9:1-10

11As they went up the hill to the town, they met some young women coming out to draw water and asked them if the seer was there.

12“Yes, he’s up ahead of you. Hurry; he just now got here for a sacrifice the people have on the high place today. 13As soon as you go into town, you’ll find him before he goes up to the high place to eat. The people won’t eat till he comes to bless the sacrifice. Afterwards the ones that are invited will eat. Go up; you’ll meet him right away.”

14They went up to the town. As they were going in, they saw Samuel coming out toward them on his way to the high place.

15The day before Saul came, the LORD had revealed to Samuel,

16“About this time tomorrow I’ll send you a man from Benjamin. Anoint him to rule over my people. He’ll save them from the Philistines. I’ve seen my people’s suffering; their outcry has come to me.”

17When Samuel saw Saul, the LORD told him, “He’s the man I told you about.”

1 Sam 9:11-17

18He approached Samuel in the gate, “Where’s the seer’s house?”

19Samuel answered him,

“I’m the seer. Go on up ahead of me to the high place and eat with me. In the morning I’ll let you go and tell you everything that’s on your mind. 20Don’t worry about the donkeys you lost three days ago. They’ve been found. Who is to receive everything that’s desirable in Israel? Isn’t it you and your father’s family?”

21Saul answered, “Am I not a Benjamite, from the smallest tribe in Israel? Isn’t my family the least important one in the tribe? Why did you say that?” 1 Sam 9:18-21

22Samuel took him and his servant to the hall and put them at the head of the guests, about 30 people. 23Samuel told the cook, “Bring the portion I gave you to put aside.” 24The cook picked up the leg and upper portion and put them in front of Saul. Samuel said, “See what I kept back for you till the appointed time so you could eat with the guests.” Saul ate with Samuel that day.

25When they came down from the high place into town, a bed was unrolled for Saul on the roof, and he lay down to sleep. 26At dawn Samuel called to him, “Get up so I can send you off.” Saul got up, and he and Samuel went out into the street.

27As they were going down to the outskirts of town, Samuel told Saul, “Have the servant go on ahead. When he’s past, stop a minute so I can tell you God’s message.” 1 Sam 9:22-27

Saul Anointed as King

10:1Samuel took a vial of oil, poured it on Saul’s head, and kissed him,

“Hasn’t the LORD anointed you to rule his people? You’ll save Israel from their enemies round about. This will be the sign that the LORD has anointed you ruler over his heritage. 2When you leave me today, you’ll meet two men by Rachel’s tomb in Benjamin at Zelzah. They’ll say, ‘The donkeys you went to look for they’ve found. Now your father has stopped caring about them and is worrying about you, “What am I going to do about my son?”’ 3You’ll go on to the Oak of Tabor. Three men going up to God at Bethel will meet you, one carrying three kids, another carrying three loaves of bread, and another carrying a skin of wine. 4They’ll greet you and give you two loaves of bread; accept them. 5After that, you’ll come to Gibeah-elohim, where the Philistine outpost is. As you come to the town, you’ll meet a band of prophets coming down from the high place, prophesying behind a harp, tambourine, flute, and lyre. 6The Spirit of the LORD will come on you powerfully, and you’ll prophesy with them and become another man. 7When these signs meet you, do whatever you find to do, because God is with you. 8Go down to Gilgal ahead of me. I’ll come to offer burnt offerings and peace offerings. Wait for a week till I come and show you what to do.” 1 Sam 10:1-8

9When he turned to leave Samuel, God gave him another heart, and those signs happened that day. 10When they got to Gibeah, a band of prophets met him, and the Spirit of God stirred him powerfully, and he prophesied with them. 11When everybody that knew him before saw how he prophesied with them, they said to one another, “What has come over Kish’s son? Is Saul among the prophets too?” 12A man at that place answered, “Who is their father?” It became a saying, “Is Saul among the prophets too?” 13When he finished prophesying, he went to the high place.

14Saul’s uncle asked him and his servant, “Where’d you go?”

“To look for the donkeys. When we couldn’t find them, we went to Samuel.”

15“What did he tell you?”

16“That they’d been found.” He didn’t tell him anything Samuel said about the kingdom.

1 Sam 10:9-16

17Samuel called the people to the LORD at Mizpah 18and told them,

“The LORD God of Israel, says, ‘I brought you out of Egypt and delivered you from the Egyptians and the kingdoms that oppressed you.’ 19But today you’ve rejected the God who saved you from your calamities and distresses. You’ve said, ‘No, install a king over us.’ Present yourselves to the LORD by tribes and by thousands.”

20Samuel brought the tribes forward, and Benjamin was taken by lot. 21He brought Benjamin forward by families, and the Matrite family was taken by lot. Finally, he brought the Matrite family forward one by one, and Saul son of Kish was taken by lot. But they couldn’t find him. 22They asked the LORD again, “Did the man come here?” The LORD said, “He hid in the baggage.” 23They ran and got him. When he stood among the people, he was head and shoulders taller than any of them.

24Samuel said to the people, “See who the LORD has chosen? There’s none like him among you.”

The people shouted, “Long live the king!”

25Samuel told them the rights and duties of kings. He wrote them in a scroll and laid it up in the LORD’s presence and sent everyone home. 26Saul went to his home in Gibeah, and brave men whose hearts God had touched went with him. 27But some worthless fellows said, “How can this fellow save us?” They scorned him and wouldn’t bring him a gift, but he didn’t react to them. 1 Sam 10:17-27

Saul Defeats the Amonites

11:1Nahash the Ammonite went up to lay siege to Jabesh-gilead. The men there said to him, “Make a treaty with us, and we’ll serve you.”

2But he said, “I’ll make one with you on this condition: I’ll gouge out your right eyes so you’ll be a disgrace in Israel.”

28:1At that time, the Philistines gathered their armed camps to fight Israel. Achish told David, “You can be sure that you and your men will go out with me in the camp.”

2“All right, you’ll find out what I can do.”

“I’ll make you my bodyguard for life.”

3Samuel was dead, and Israel had mourned him and buried him in Ramah, his own town. Saul had removed the mediums and spiritists from the land. 4The Philistines gathered and camped in Shunem, and Saul gathered Israel and camped at Gilboa. 5When Saul saw the Philistine camp, he was afraid, and his heart trembled greatly. 6When he asked the LORD, he didn’t answer him by dreams, Urim, or prophets. 7Saul told his advisors, “Find a medium so I can go ask through her.” They told him, “There’s a woman in En-dor.” 1 Sam 28:1-7

8Saul disguised himself in different clothes and went to her with two men at night and said, “Conjure up for me and bring up the one I name.”

9“Surely you know Saul has expelled the mediums and spiritists from the land. Why lay a trap to get me killed?”

10Saul swore by the LORD, “As sure as the LORD lives, you won’t be punished.”

11“Who do you want me to bring up?”

“Samuel.”

12When she saw Samuel, she cried out, “Why have you misled me? You’re Saul.”

13“Don’t be afraid; what do you see?”

“A god coming out of the ground.”

14“What’s he look like?”

“An old man, wearing a robe.”

Saul knew it was Samuel and bowed to the ground in homage. 1 Sam 28:8-14

15Samuel said, “Why have you disturbed me by bringing me up?”

“I’m greatly distressed. The Philistines are waging war against me. God has left me and doesn’t answer me anymore by prophets or dreams. I’ve called you to tell me what I should do.”

16“Why ask me since the LORD has become your adversary? 17He’s done what he said through me: he’s torn the kingdom from you and given it to David. 18As you didn’t carry out his fierce anger on Amalek, so he has done this to you today. 19He’ll deliver you and Israel’s army to the Philistines. Tomorrow you and your sons will be with me!” 1 Sam 28:15-19

20Saul fell headlong on the floor terrified. There wasn’t any strength in him; he hadn’t eaten anything all day and all night. 21The woman came and saw he was terrified. She said,

“I’ve done what you asked. I’ve taken my life in my hands and listened to you. 22Let me fix you some bread so you can have strength when you leave.”

23“I’ll don’t want anything.”

But she and his advisors kept urging him, so he gave in, got up off the floor, and sat on the bed.

24The woman had a grain-fed calf in the house. She hurried and butchered it, kneaded flour, and baked bread without yeast. 25She brought it to Saul and his men to eat. He got up and left.

1 Sam 28:20-25

The Philistines Reject David

29:1The Philistines gathered their forces to Aphek while the Israelites were camping by the spring in Jezreel. 2The Philistine lords were moving forward by hundreds and thousands; David and his men were moving forward in the rear with Achish. 3The commanders of the Philistines said, “What are these Hebrews doing here?” Achish said, “Isn’t this David, Saul’s servant, that’s been with me these days, or years? I haven’t found a fault in him from the day he deserted to me till now.” 4But the commanders were mad,

“Make him go back to where you’ve assigned him. Don’t let him go down to battle with us and become our adversary during the fight. For how could he make himself acceptable to his lord? Wouldn’t it be with the heads of these men? 5Isn’t this David they sang about in the dances,

‘Saul has slain his thousands,

David his ten thousands!’?” 1 Sam 29:1-5

6Achish called David,

“As sure as the LORD lives, you’ve been upright. Your conduct with me in the army is fine. I haven’t found anything bad in you since you came, but the other Philistine lords don’t want you here. 7Go back quietly so you won’t upset them.”

8David asked him, “What have I done? What have you found in me since I came to you, that I can’t go and fight my king’s enemies?”

9“You’re pleasing to me, like an angel of God, but the commanders have said you can’t go into battle with us. 10Get up early with your men and leave as soon as it gets daylight.”

11David and his men got up early to go back to the land of the Philistines. Then the Philistines went up to Jezreel. 1 Sam 29:6-11

David’s Wives Are Captured

30:1When David and his men got home to Ziklag on the third day, the Amalekites had made a raid on the Negev, attacked Ziklag, and burned it. 2They took the women captive and everybody in it, big and small, but didn’t kill anybody. 3When David and his men got there, it had been burned, and their wives, sons, and daughters had been captured. 4They cried till they couldn’t cry anymore. 5David’s two wives had been taken, Ahinoam and Abigail. 6In addition, David was distressed because the people were talking about stoning him. They were bitter over losing their sons and daughters, but David took courage in the LORD his God.

7He told Abiathar the priest, son of Ahimelech, to bring him the ephod. 8He asked the LORD, “Should I chase this band? Will I overtake them?”

“Yes, you’ll overtake them and rescue everyone.”

9He and his 600 men went to the Brook Besor, where the ones left behind remained. 10But David and 400 men pursued on. 200 stayed behind too exhausted to cross Brook Besor.

1 Sam 30:1-10

11They found an Egyptian in the countryside and brought him to David. They gave him bread and water, 12a piece of fig cake and two clusters of raisins. He ate them and revived. He hadn’t eaten or drunk for three days. 13David asked him, “Who do you belong to? Where are you from?”

“I’m a young man from Egypt, an Amalekite’s slave. My master left me behind when I got sick three days ago. 14We raided the Negev of the Cherethites that belongs to Judah, and the Negev of Caleb. We burned Ziklag.”

15David said, “Will you bring me down to this band?”

He said, “Swear to me by God that you won’t kill me or turn me over to my master, and I’ll take you down to them.” 1 Sam 30:11-15

David Defeats the Amalekites

16When he’d taken him down; they found them spread out all over the ground eating and drinking and dancing because of the large amount of spoil they’d taken from the Philistines and Judah. 17David slaughtered them from twilight till evening the next day. Not a man escaped, except for 400 young men that fled on camels. 18He recovered everything the Amalekites had taken and rescued his two wives. 19Nothing was missing, big or little, sons or daughters. He brought it all back. 20He’d captured the sheep and cattle the people drove ahead of the other livestock. They said, “This is David’s spoil.” 1 Sam 30:16-20

21David came to the 200 men at Brook Besor who were too exhausted to go with him. They went out to meet him and the people that were with him, and he greeted them. 22Then some ruffians among David’s men said, “They didn’t go with us. We’re not going to give them any of the spoil we’ve recovered. Every man can take his wife and children.”

23David said, “Brothers, don’t do that with what the LORD’s given us. He protected us and delivered to us the band that attacked us. 24Who’d listen to you on this? The one that guards the luggage gets the same share as the one that goes to battle.”

25He made it an ordinance in Israel ever since. 1 Sam 30:21-25

26When David got to Ziklag, he sent some spoil to the elders in Judah, his friends, and said, “A gift from the spoils of the LORD’s enemies,” 27and to the people in Bethel, Ramoth of the Negev, Jattir, 28Aroer, Siphmoth, Eshtemoa, 29Racal, the towns of the Jerahmeelites, Kenites, 30Hormah, Bor-ashan, Athach, 31Hebron—everywhere he and his men circulated. 1 Sam 30:26-31

The Death of Saul

31:1The Philistines attacked Israel, and Israel fled and fell slain on Mount Gilboa. 2The Philistines overtook Saul and his sons, and killed Jonathan, Abinadab, and Malci-shua. 3The battle pressed Saul hard, and the archers seriously wounded him. 4He told his armor bearer, “Draw your sword and run me through so these uncircumcised Philistines won’t come and run me through and torture me.” But his armor bearer was afraid to do it. So Saul took his own sword and fell on it. 5When he saw that Saul was dead, he fell on his sword too and died with him. 6Saul died with his three sons, his armor bearer, and all his men that day. 7When the men of Israel on the other side of the valley and beyond the Jordan, saw that Israel had fled and Saul and his sons were dead, they abandoned the towns and fled. The Philistines came and lived in them. 1 Sam 31:1-7

8The next day when the Philistines came to strip the slain, they found Saul and his sons on Mount Gilboa. 9They cut off his head, stripped off his weapons, and sent them all over Philistine territory to carry the good news to the temple of their idols and to the people. 10They put his weapons in the temple that held Ashtoreth’s images and fastened his body to the wall of Beth-shan. 11When the people in Jabesh-gilead heard about it, 12brave men walked all night and took down the bodies of Saul and his sons. They went to Jabesh and burned them, 13buried their bones under the tamarisk tree, and fasted a week. 1 Sam 31:8-13
