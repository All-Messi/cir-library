1 Corinthians Chapter 3

1And-I, brothers, was┘not└able to-speak to-you as spiritual but as physical, as infants in Christ. 2I-gave┘you milk└to-drink, not solid-food. For you-are still physical, for where jealousy and strife (are) among you, are not you fleshly and walk according-to man? 4For when anybody (of you) says, “I am of-Paul,” and another (says), “I (am) of-Apollos,” are-you not men? 5What then is Apollos? And what is Paul? Ministers by whom you-came-to-believe, and to-each as the Lord gave. 6I-planted, Apollos watered, but God made-(it)-grow. 7So neither is the-one planting anything, nor the-one watering, but God the-One making-(it)-grow. 8Now the-one planting and the-one watering are one, but each will-receive [the] his-own pay according-to [the] his-own work. 9For we-are fellow-workers of-God; you are God’s field, God’s building.

0According-to the grace of-[the] God [the] given to-me as (a) wise general-contractor I-laid (a) foundation, and another builds-on (it). But let┘each└watch how he builds-on (it). 11For nobody is-able to-lay another foundation than the-one laid, which is Jesus Christ. 12And if anybody builds on the foundation gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, straw, 13the work of-each will-become evident, for the day will-make-(it)-clear, because it-will-be-reveled by fire. And what-kind the work of-each is the fire itself will-test. 14If the work of-anybody will-remain that he-build-on (the foundation), he-will-receive pay. 15If the work of-anybody will-be-burned-up, he-will-suffer-loss, but he himself will-be-saved, but so as by fire. 16Do-youknow that you-are (a) temple of-God and the Spirit of-[the] God lives in you? 17If anybody destroys the temple of-[the] God, [the] God will-destroy this-one. For the temple of-[the] God is holy, which you are. Let┘nobody└deceive himself. If anybody thinks (himself) to-be wise among you in this [the] age/world, let-him-become foolish in-order-that he-might-become wise. 19For it-has-been-written, “(He is) the One catching the wise in the craftiness of-them,”

20and again, “(The) Lord knows the reasonings of-the wise that they-are empty.”

21So-that let┘nobody└boast in men. For all-things are of-you, 22whether Paul, whether Apollos, whether Cephas, whether (the) world, whether life, whether death, whether things-present, whether things-to-come. 23You (are) of-Christ, and Christ (is) of-God.
