Galatians Chapter 4

1Now I-say, for as-long┘(a) time└as the/an heir is (a) minor, he-differs with-respect-to-nothing from-(a)-slave, though-being lord of-all, 2but is under guardians and trustees until the pre-set-time of-the father. 3So also we, when we-were minors, were enslaved by the basic-rules of-the world; 4but when the fullness of-[the] time came, [the] God sent-out the Son of-him, becoming from woman, become under law, 5in-order-that he-might-redeem the-ones under law in-order-that we-might-receive [the] sonship-by-adoption. 6And because you-are sons, [the] God sent-out the Spirit of-the Son of-him into the hearts of-us, crying-out, “Abba, [the] Father.” 7So-that yousg are no-longer (a) slave, but (a) son; and if (a) son, also (an) heir through God. 8But at-that-time, not knowing God, you-served the-ones by-nature not being gods. 9But now, having-come-to-know God—but rather having-been-known by God, how do-you-turn-back again to the weak and beggarly elements which you-desire to-serve back again? 10You-observe days and months and seasons and years. 11I-am-afraid-for you lest somehow I-have-labored for you for-nothing.

2Behold, I, Paul, tell you that if you are circumcised, Christ will┘not└benefit you anything. 3And I-testify again to every man (that is) circumcised that he is (a) debtor to-do the whole Law. 4You-have-been-cut-off from Christ, (you) who are-justified by law; you-fell from [the] grace. 5For we by-(the)-spirit out-of trust are-waiting-for (the) hope of-righteousness. 6For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision accomplishes anything nor uncircumcision, but trust working through love.

7You-were-running well. Who prevented you not to-obey the truth? The persuasion (was) not from the-One calling you. 9(A) little yeast leavens the whole lump. 10I am-confident in you in (the) Lord that you-will-adopt no other thing, but the-one disturbing you will-bear [the] judgment, whoever2 he-is. 11But I, brothers, if I still preach circumcision, why am-I┘still└being-persecuted? Then the stumbling-block of-the cross would-disappear. 12I-wish the-ones troubling you will┘even└mutilate themselves!

12Become as I (am), because I┘(am)└also like you (are), brothers; I-beg you. You-wronged me with-respect-to-nothing. 13And you-know that through (a) weakness of-the flesh I-preached-the-gospel-to you the first/former-time, 14and the trial of-youpl in the flesh of-me you-did┘not└look-down-on-with-contempt nor spit-at, but like (an) angel of-God you-welcomed me, like Christ Jesus. 15Where then (is) the blessing of-you? For I-testify to-you that, if possible, having-gouged-out the eyes of-you, you-would-have-given (them) to-me. 16So-that have-I-become (an) enemy of-you telling┘you└ (the)-truth? 17They-do┘not└seek you well, but they-want-to-exclude you in-order-that you-might-show-great-interest-in them. 18But (it is) good always to-be-shown-great-interest in (a) good (way) and not only in my (the) being-present with you. 19My children, whom again I-am-in-labor-pains-for till2 Christ is-formed in you. 20And I-was-wanting to-be-present with you now and to change the voice of-me, because I-am-disturbed by you.

3For you were-called for freedom, brothers. Only (do) not (use) [the] freedom as (an) occasion for the flesh, but through [the] love serve one-another. 14For all the Law has-been-fulfilled in one statement—in [the], “You-shall-love the neighbor of-you as yourself.” 15And if you bite and devour one-another, beware lest you-get-consumed by one-another.

6I tell you, walk by (the) spirit and you-will┘certainly not└be-fulfilling (the) desire of-(the)-flesh. 17For the flesh desires against the spirit, and the spirit (desires) against the flesh, for these are-opposed to one-another, in-order-that not what you want these-things you-do. 18But if you-are-led by (the) spirit, you-are not under law. 19Now the deeds of-the flesh are evident, which are sexual-immorality, uncleanness, sensuality, 20idolatry, sorcery, hostilities, rivalry, jealous, rages, disputes, divisions, factions, 21envyings, drunkenness, carousings, and the-things like these, which I-say┘to-you└ahead-of time, as I-told-(you)-before, [that] the-ones doing [the] such-things will┘not└inherit (the) kingdom of-God.

21Tell me, the-ones wanting to-be under law, are-you┘not└listening to the Law? 22For it-has-been-written that Abraham had two sons, one by the/a slave-girl and one by the/a free woman. 23But the-one has-been-born by the slave girl according-to (the) flesh, but the-one by the free (woman) through promise—24which-things are allegorically-spoken. For these are two covenants, one from Mount Sinai, bearing to slavery, which is Hagar. 25And [the] Hagar is Mount Sinai in [the] Arabia, and parallels the present Jerusalem, for she-is-(a)-slave along-with the children of-her. 26But the Jerusalem above is free, who is (the) mother of-us. 27For it-has-been-written, “Celebrate, barren-one the-one not bearing (children). Cry-out and scream, the-one not being-in-labor, because many more (are) the children of-the desolate than of-the-one having the/a husband.”

2But the fruit of-the spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, 23gentleness, self-control. No law exists against [the] such-things. 24But the-ones of-Christ [Jesus]ms crucified the flesh with the/its passions and the/its desires. 25If we-have-life by-(the)-spirit, let-us┘also└conduct-ourselves by-(the)-spirit. Let-us┘not└become-boastful, irritating one-another, envying one-another.

28But you, brothers, are children of-promise according-to Isaac. 29But as then the-one having-been-born according-to (the) flesh was-persecuting the-one (having-been-born) according-to the spirit. So also now. 30But what does┘the scripture└say? “Cast-out the slave-girl and the son of-her; for the son of-the slave-girl will┘certainly not└inherit with the son” of-the free (woman).

31Wherefore, brothers, we-are not children of-(a)-slave-girl, but of-the free (woman). 5:1Christ certainly set┘us└free3; so stand-firm and do┘not again└be-subject to-(a)-yoke of-bondage.
