9And sufficient time passing and already the voyage being risky on-account-of even the Fast already [the] having-gone-by, [the] Paul kept-urging, 10saying to-them, “Men, I-see that with injury and much loss, not only of-[the] cargo and the ship but also of-the lives of-us, the voyage will be.” 11But the centurion was-being-persuaded more by the captain and the ship-owner than by the things spoken by Paul. 12And the harbor being unsuitable for wintering, the majority set plan(s) to embark from there, if somehow they-might-be-able, having-reached [the] Phoenix, to-winter—(a) harbor of-[the] Crete facing according-to southeast and according-to northeast.
