2 Thessalonians Chapter 3

1 Finally2, brothers, pray for us in-order-that the word of-the Lord may-make-progress and be-glorified even-as also (it was) with you, 2and in-order-that we-may-be-delivered from harmful and evil men, for [the] faith (is) not of-all. 3But the Lord is faithful who will-strengthen you and keep (you) from the evil-(one). 4And we-are-confident in (the) Lord over you, that what-things we-command you-are┘both└doing and will-do. 5And may┘the Lord└direct the hearts of-you into the love of-[the] God and into the endurance of-[the] Christ.

6 Now we-command you, brothers, in (the) name of-the Lord [of-us]ms Jesus Christ (that) you stay-away from every brother walking disorderly and not according-to the tradition that they-received from us. 7For you┘yourselves└know how it-is-necessary to-imitate us, because we-were┘not└idle among you, 8neither did-we-eat bread from anyone without-paying-for-it; but in labor and toil night and day, working unto [the] not burdening any of-you, 9not that we-do┘not└have (the) right, but in-order-that we-might-give ourselves (as an) example to-you unto [the] your imitating (us). 10For even when we-were with you, we-were-commanding this to-you: that if anybody is┘not└willing to-work, neither let-him-eat. 11For we-hear that some (are) walking among you disorderly, not working but being-busy-bodies. 12Now we-command and encourage [the] such-persons in (the) Lord Jesus Christ in-order-that working with quietness they-may-eat the bread of-themselves. 13But, brothers, do┘not└become-discouraged doing-good. 14And if anybody does┘not└obey the word of-us through the epistle, make-note-of this (person) not to-associate-with him in-order-that he-may-be ashamed. 15And do┘not└consider (him) as (an) enemy, but warn (him) as (a) brother.

16 Now may the Lord of-[the] peace himself give you [the] peace through everything in every circumstance. The Lord (be) with you all.

17 The salutation of-Paul by-[the] my-own hand, which is (the) sign in every letter. Thus I-write. 18The grace of-the Lord of-us Jesus Christ (be) with you all.
