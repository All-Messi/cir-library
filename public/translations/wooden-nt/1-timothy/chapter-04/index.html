1 Timothy Chapter 4

1Now the Spirit says expressly that in (the) last times some will-fall-away-from the faith, paying-attention-to deceiving spirits and doctrines of-demons, 2in (the) hypocrisy of-liars, having-been-seared with-respect-to [the] their conscience, 3preventing to-marry, abstaining-from foods, which [the] God created for reception with thanksgiving by-the-ones faithful and having-known the truth, 4because every creation of-God (is) good and nothing rejected, being-received with thanksgiving; 5for it-is-sanctified by (the) word of-God and prayer.

6Pointing-out these-things to-the brothers, you-will-be (a) good minister of-Christ Jesus, being nourished by-the words of-the faith and the good teaching that you-have-followed. 7But stay-away-from [the] profane, silly myths. But exercise yourself unto godliness. 8For [the] bodily exercise is profitable unto (a)-little/little, but [the] godliness is profitable unto all-things, having promise of-life [the] now and [the] coming. 9Faithful/reliable (is) the saying and worthy of-all acceptance: 10“For this we-labor and struggle, Because we-have-hoped on (the) living God, Who is (the) Savior of-all men, specifically of-believing-ones.”

1Command and teach these-things. 12Let┘nobody└disregard the youth of-you, but become (an) example of-the faithful-ones in word, in conduct, in love, in faith, in purity. 13Until I-come, give-attention to-[the] reading, to-[the] exhortation, to-[the] teaching. 14Do┘not└neglect the spiritual-gift in you, which was-given to-you by prophecy with (the) laying-on of-[the] hands of-the eldership. 15Cultivate these-things, be in these-things in-order-that the progress of-you may-be evident to-all. 16Pay-close-attention to-yourself and the teaching; persevere in-respect-to-them; for doing this, you-will-save both yourself and the-ones hearing you.
