1 John Chapter 2

1 My little-children, I-am-writing these-things to-you in-order-that you-may┘not└keep-sinning. And if anybody does-sin, we-have (an) Advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the just. 2And he┘himself└is (the) propitiation for the sins of-us, and not only for [the] our (sins), but also for the whole world. 3And in this we-know that we-have-known him: if we-are-keeping the commandments of-him. 4The-one saying [that], “I-have-known him,” and is┘not└keeping the commandments of-him, is (a) liar, and in this-one the truth is not. 5And whoever2 keeps the word of-him—truly in this-one the love of-[the] God has-been-completed/perfected. By this we-know that we-are in him. 6The-one saying to-reside in him—he himself ought to-walk thus, even-as that-One walked.

7 Beloved, I-am┘not└writing (a) new commandment to-you, but (an) old commandment that you-were-having from (the) beginning. The old [the] commandment is the word that you-heard. 8Again, I-am-writing (a) new commandmentfem to-you thatneut is true in him and in you, because darkness is-passing-away, and the true [the] light is┘already└shining. 9The-one saying to-be in the light and hating the brother of-him is in [the] darkness until now. 10The-one loving the brother of-him remains in the light, and (a) cause-of-stumbling is not in him. 11But the-one hating the brother of-him is in [the] darkness and walks in [the] darkness, and does┘not└know where he-is-going, because the darkness blinded the eyes of-him.

12 I-am-writing to-you, little-children,

13 I-am-writing to-you, Fathers,

14 I-wrote to-you, children,

15 Do┘not└love the world neither the-things in the world. If anybody loves the world, the love of-the Father is not in him. 16Because everything [the] in the world—the lust of-the flesh and the lust of-the eyes and the pride/arrogance of-[the] life—is not from the Father but is from the world. 17And the world is-passing-away/passes-away and the lust of-it, but the-one doing the will of-[the] God remains forever3.

18 Children, it-is (the) last hour, and even-as you-heard that antichrist is-coming, and now have-come-to-be many antichrist, whence we-know that it-is (the) last hour. 19They-went-out from us but they were not of us; for if they had-been of us, they-would-have remained with us—but (they went out) in-order-that they-might-be-manifested that they┘all└were not of-us. 20And you have (an) anointing from the Holy-One, and know all-things. 21I-did┘not└write to-you because you-do┘not└know the truth, but because you-know it, and because every lie is not of the truth. 22Who is the/a liar except2 the-one denying that Jesus is [not] the Christ? This-one is the antichrist: the-one denying the Father and the Son. 23Every the-one denying the Son neither has the Father; the-one confessing the Son also has the Father. 24Let┘what you heard from (the) beginning └remain in you. If what you-heard from the beginning remains in you, you also will-remain in the Son and in the Father. 25And this is the promise that he promised you: [the] eternal [the] life.

26 I-wrote these-things to-you about the-ones deceiving you. 27And the anointing that you received from him remains in you, and you-do┘not└have (a) need in-order-that anybody should teach you, but as the anointing of-him teaches you about all-things and is true and is not (a) lie and even-as he-taught you, remain in him.

28 And now, little-children, remain in him, in-order-that if he-is-manifested, we-have boldness and are┘not└ashamed of him at the coming of-him. 29If you know that he-is righteous, you-know also that every the-one doing [the] righteousness has-been-begotten by him.
