Job Chapter 12

1Job answered: 2“No doubt, you’re the people, and wisdom will die with you!

3But I’m as smart as you are; I’m not your inferior. Who doesn’t know things like that?

4I’m a laughingstock to my friends, one who called on God and he answered, a just and blameless man; yet I’m a laughingstock.

5In the thoughts of people at ease, there’s contempt for disaster; they’re ready to give a shove to those whose feet are slipping.

6Destroyers prosper; those who provoke God may feel secure, but he’ll bring them under his control.

Job 12:1-6

7“Ask the animals, they’ll teach you; the birds will explain.

8 8Speak to the earth; it will inform you; have the fish in the sea tell you.

9Who among them doesn’t know that the LORD has brought this disaster on me?

0The life of every living thing lies in his hand, including the breath of each person.

1Doesn’t the ear test words like the palate tastes food?

2Old people have wisdom; long life brings insight.

Job 12:7-12

13“He has wisdom and power; counsel and insight lie in him.

4He tears down, and no one can rebuild; he shuts people in, and nobody can release them.

5If he withholds water, things dry up; if he sends it, the land floods.

6Strength and sound wisdom lie in him; deceived and deceiver are under his control.

7He makes advisors walk barefoot and makes fools out of judges.

8He takes off kings’ belts and binds their waists with a rope.

Job 12:13-18

9He makes priests walk barefoot and overthrows those who feel secure.

0He silences the ones that people trust and takes away the discernment of elders.

1He pours contempt on nobles and disarms the strong.

2He reveals what’s deep in the dark and brings darkness into light.

3He makes nations great, then destroys them; he enlarges nations, then lets them go into exile.

4He takes away understanding from the chiefs of the people and makes them wander in pathless desolation.

5They grope in the dark with no light; he makes them stagger like drunks.

Job 12:19-25
