Job Chapter 29

1Job took up his discourse again: 2“Oh that I could be like I was just a few months ago when God was watching over me,

3 3when his lamp shined above my head and I walked through darkness by his light,

4 4while I was in the prime of life and his friendship hovered above my tent,

5 5when the Almighty was still with me and my children were around me,

6 6when my feet were drenched with cream and the millstone poured out streams of olive oil for me!

7 7when I went to the city gate and took my seat in the town square.

Job 29:1-7

8Young men saw me and stepped aside, old men stood up,

9 9officials stopped talking and put their hands over their mouths;

10 10nobles’ voices got quiet and put their tongues against their palates.

1When people heard me, they praised me; when they saw me, they spoke well of me

12 12because I helped the poor that asked for it and the orphan that had no advocate.

13People about to die blessed me; I made widows sing for joy.

4I dressed in goodness; justice covered me like a robe and turban.

Job 29:8-14

5I served as eyes for the blind and feet for the lame.

6I became a father to the needy and helped strangers I didn’t know.

7I broke the jaws of the wicked and snatched the prey from their teeth.

8Then I supposed, ‘I’ll die in my nest and multiply my days like sand.

9My roots reach out to the water; dew lies all night on my branches.

0My honor is always fresh; my bow stays new in my hand.’

Job 29:15-20

21“They listened to me, waited, and kept quiet to hear my advice.

2When I spoke, they had nothing to add; what I said was sufficient for them.

3They waited for me like spring rain and drank in my words like fresh showers.

4I smiled at them when they weren’t sure what to do; they valued my approval.

5I told them what course of action to take and sat as their chief; I lived like a king among his troops, like somebody that comforted mourners.

Job 29:21-25
