Job Chapter 39

1“Do you know when mountain goats give birth? Do you ever see a deer calving?

2Can you count the months of their gestation or learn when they have their fawns?

3They crouch down to deliver their young.

4Their offspring grow strong out in the open; they leave home and never return.

Job 39:1-4

5“Who turned the wild donkey loose? Who untied its ropes?

6 6I gave it the open country for a home, desert land to live in?

7It scorns city turmoil and doesn’t have to listen to a driver shout at it.

8It roams the mountains for pasture, looking for anything green.

Job 39:5-8

9“Will a wild ox consent to serve you and spend the night at your manger?

0Can you harness it to plow in a furrow? Will it harrow your fields?

1Will you depend on it because it’s strong and leave your work for it to do?

2Will you trust it to bring your grain home and deliver it to your threshing floor?

Job 39:9-12

13“The ostrich’s wings flap happily, but they don’t compare with the wings and feathers of a stork.

4She abandons her eggs in the ground to stay warm in the sand.

5She forgets that a foot may crush them or a wild ox may tramp on them.

6She’s harsh toward her young as if they weren’t hers; though her work may be pointless, she doesn’t care,

17because God hasn’t made her wise or given her intelligence.

8When she jumps up to run, she laughs at a horse and its rider.

Job 39:13-18

19“Do you give a horse its strength or cover its neck with a mane?

0Can you make it leap like a locust? Its majestic snorting is terrifying!

1It paws in the valley and frolics in its strength and charges out to battle.

2It mocks fear and doesn’t shy away from the sword.

3The quiver rattles against it, the flashing spear and javelin too.

4It doesn’t stand still when the bugle signals; with trembling rage, it races over the ground.

5As often as the bugle blows, it says, ‘Aha!’ It smells a battle a long way off, the captain’s thundering, and the battle cry.

Job 39:19-25

26“Does a hawk soar because you’re so smart as it stretches its wings toward the south?

7Does an eagle mount up at your command and build its nest up high?

28It lives and lodges on the cliffs, in inaccessible places on the rocky crag.

29From there it spies out prey that it can see from far away.

30Even its young gulp down blood; they’re right there when the kill goes down.

Job 39:26-30
