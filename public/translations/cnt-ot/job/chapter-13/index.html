Job Chapter 13

1 I’ve seen that;

2 What you know, I know too;

4 You men are smearing me with things that aren’t true;

5 Oh that you’d just be quiet

6 Hear me out;

7 Will you lie to defend God?

8 Will you favor him by how you testify?

9 How will you fare when he examines you?

10 He’ll rebuke you for sure

11 Doesn’t his majesty terrify you,

12 Your memorable sayings are proverbs made of ashes,

14 Why should I bite my body

15 Even if he kills me, I’ll trust him;

16 That will also be to my salvation

17 Listen carefully to what I’m saying;

18 I’ve prepared my case;

19 Will you defeat me in this argument?

20 He silences the ones that people trust

21 He pours contempt on nobles

22 Then call and I’ll answer;

23 How many iniquities do I have?

24 Why do you hide from me

25 Do you make a driven leaf tremble

26 You list bitter accusations against me

27 You put my feet in shackles
