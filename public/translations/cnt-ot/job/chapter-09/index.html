Job Chapter 9

1Job answered: 2“I know that’s true, but how can anyone be innocent in God’s sight?

3If you wanted to dispute with him, you couldn’t answer him once in a thousand times.

4He’s so wise and powerful; who has ever defied him and succeeded?

Job 9:1-4

5“It’s God that moves mountains; they don’t know it when he overturns them in his anger.

6He can shake the earth and make its pillars tremble;

7 7he can tell the sun not to rise and the stars not to shine.

8He alone stretched out the sky and tramped on the ocean waves.

9He made the Bear, Orion, the Pleiades, and constellations in the southern hemisphere.

0He does great, unimaginable things, countless amazing deeds.

Job 9:5-1

0 11“If he passes by me, I don’t see him; if he goes by, I can’t sense him.

2He snatches away; who can stop him? Who can say to him, ‘What are you doing?’

3God won’t turn away his anger; Rahab’s helpers cower below him.

Job 9:

11-13 14“How then can I answer him or argue with him?

5Even if I were innocent, I couldn’t answer him; I’d have to appeal to my accuser for mercy.

6If I called and he answered me, I wouldn’t expect him to pay attention to me.

7He attacks me with a storm and multiplies my wounds for no reason.

8He won’t let me catch my breath, but saturates me with bitterness.

9If it’s a matter of power, look at him! If it’s a matter of justice, who can sue him?

0Though I’m good, my mouth will condemn me; though I’m innocent, it’ll pronounce me guilty.

Job 9:14-20

21“I’m innocent, but it doesn’t matter; I despise my life.

2Innocent and guilty are the same to him; so I say, ‘He destroys the innocent with the guilty.’

3When disaster brings sudden death, he mocks innocent people’s despair.

4The earth has been turned over to sinners; he blinds the eyes of judges. If it’s not him, who is it?

Job 9:21-24

25“Now my days go past faster than a runner; they hurry away; they don’t see anything good.

6They slip by like a reed skiff, like an eagle swooping on prey.

7If I say, ‘I’ll forget about my complaint; I’ll leave my sad countenance and be cheerful,’

8I’m afraid of my sufferings; I know he won’t declare me innocent.

9He’ll condemn me anyway; why should I try for nothing?

0Even if I wash myself with snow and clean my hands with lye,

31he’d still throw me into a pit; even my clothes would abhor me.

Job 9:25-

31 32“He’s not a mortal like me that I can argue with, that I could go to court against.

3There’s no mediator between us that could hold us apart.

4Let him take his rod away from me; don’t let my dread of him terrify me.

5Then I’d speak without fearing him, but I can’t do that on my own.

Job 9:3
