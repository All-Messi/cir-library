Job Chapter 8

1Bildad the Shuhite answered: 2“How long will you talk like that? You make your mouth a blustering wind?

3Does God pervert justice? Does the Almighty twist the truth?

4When your children sinned against him, he delivered them to the consequences of their sin.

5If you look for God and beg for mercy from the Almighty,

6 6if you’re pure and just, he’ll certainly act on your behalf and restore you to your good position.

7Though you started with little, you’ll end up with a lot.

Job 8:1-7

8“Ask previous generations and find out what the ancestors learned;

9 9we’re only like yesterday and don’t know much; our short time on earth has been like a shadow.

0Won’t they teach you and offer their thoughts?

Job 8:8-10

11“Can papyrus grow where there’s no marsh? Can reeds grow without water?

2While they’re still growing and not ready to be cut, they wither sooner than grass.

3The paths of everyone that forgets God are like that; the hopes of godless people perish.

14What they depend on breaks down; what they trust is like a spider’s web.

15They trust it, but it doesn’t hold firm; when they take hold of it, it mashes in their hand.

16It’s like a lush plant that thrives in the sunshine and spreads its shoots across the garden,

17 17whose roots twine around a rock pile and grow among the stones.

18 18But if it’s pulled loose, you can’t tell it was ever there.

19 19That’s the extent of its ‘joy’; others sprout up to replace it.

Job 8:11-19

20“God doesn’t reject blameless people or give sinners a hand.

1Yet he’ll fill your mouth with laughter, your lips with shouting.

2In the end, those who hate you he’ll cover with disgrace; the tents of sinners won’t exist anymore.”

Job 8:20-22
