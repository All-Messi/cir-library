Job Chapter 24

1“Why doesn’t the Almighty maintain a regular court schedule for judging? Why do those who know him wait for him in vain?

2Some remove landmarks, seize flocks and eat them,

3 3drive away donkeys that belong to the fatherless, take widows’ oxen for pledges.

4They run the needy off the road; the poor people have to hide from them.

5Like wild donkeys in the desert, the poor labor at foraging for food, looking for prey in the wild to feed their children.

6They gather fodder in the field and glean sinners’ vineyards.

7They spend the night without enough clothes to protect them against the cold.

8They’re soaked from mountain rain and huddle in the rocks for lack of shelter.

Job 24:1-8

9“Others snatch the fatherless from the breast and take pledges against the poor.

0They make the naked go without clothes and take away sheaves from the hungry.

1They press out oil between the olive rows and tread winepresses for others yet suffer thirst.

2From the city, the people groan, the wounded cry out for help, but God ignores their prayers.

Job 24:9-12

13“Others rebel against the light; they refuse to know its ways or stay on them.

4A murderer gets up at dawn to kill the poor and needy; at night he’s a thief.

5An adulterer waits for twilight and says, ‘Nobody will see me’; he disguises himself.

6In the dark they break into houses and sleep in the daytime; they’re not accustomed to light.

7Morning is the same to them as dense darkness; they’re allies with the terrors of deep gloom.

Job 24:13-17

18“But they’re swiftly carried away like foam on a creek; their land is cursed; they’re afraid to go in their own vineyards.

9Sheol consumes sinners like drought and heat consume snow.

0Their mothers forget about them, maggots feed on them till they’re not remembered; they’re blown over like trees in a windstorm.

1They prey on barren and childless women and fail to help needy widows.

2But God uses his power to drag away the rich; they may get established, but they have no guarantee of life

3He may let them live in security, but he keeps an eye on their progress in life.

4They’re honored for a little while; then they’re gone; they’re brought low and fade away like everything else, cut off like heads of grain.

5If that’s not so, who can prove me wrong and make my speech pointless?”

Job 24:18-25
