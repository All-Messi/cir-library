1 Samuel Chapter 21

1Then David went to Nob to Ahimelech the priest. He came cringing to meet David, “Why are you by yourself?”

2David took those comments to heart and was afraid of Achish. 13So he acted like he was crazy. He made marks on the doors of the gate, and let spit run down his beard. 14Achish told his men, “You can tell the man’s crazy. Why did you bring him to me? 15Am I short on crazy people? Will he come into my house?”

1 Sam 21:10-15

David at the Case of Adullam

4The priest answered him, “There’s no ordinary bread here; there’s consecrated bread if the young men have not been with women.”

5David answered, “Women have certainly not been with us; that’s always the case on an expedition. The young men are holy even on an ordinary journey, much less today.”

6The priest gave him the Bread of the Presence, which is removed from the LORD’s presence and replaced with fresh bread the same day.

1 Sam 21:1-6

7Saul’s chief shepherd was there that day, detained in the LORD’s presence: Doeg the Edomite.

8David asked Ahimelech, “Is there a spear or sword here? I didn’t bring either; the king’s matter was urgent.”

9The priest said, “The sword of Goliath that you killed in the Valley of Elah is here wrapped in a cloth behind the ephod. If you want to, take that. It’s the only one here.” David said, “There’s none like that one. Let me have it.”

1 Sam 21:7-9

10David fled that day from Saul to Achish, king of Gath. 11His officers asked him, “Isn’t this David, the king of the land? Didn’t they sing about him in dances. “Saul has slain his thousands, David his ten thousands!’?”
