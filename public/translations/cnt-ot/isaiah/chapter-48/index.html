Isaiah Chapter 48

1“Listen to this, Jacob called ‘Israel,’ that came from Judah’s family, that swear by the LORD and invoke Israel’s God, but not sincerely and rightly,

2 2that call yourself The Holy City and depend on the LORD of Hosts:

Is 48:1-2

3 3long ago, I told you what was going to happen; all at once it has; my prediction has come true.

4 4I know how stubborn you are, that your neck is as stiff as iron, and your head is as hard as bronze.

5 5I made my prediction long ago so you couldn’t say your idol did it, that your carved image, your molten god commanded it.’

Is 48:3-5

6“You’ve heard my predictions and seen them fulfilled, but you still won’t own up to them; now I’ll tell you things you haven’t heard.

7They’re not things from long ago; so you can’t claim you knew them all along.

8I know what traitors you are; you’ve been rebels from birth.

9 9Yet for my name’s sake, I’ve delayed expressing my anger; for my honor I’ve restrained it to keep from wiping you out.

10I’ve refined you, but not like silver; I’ve refined you in the furnace of suffering.

1For my own sake—yes, I’ll do it for my own sake— I won’t let my reputation be tarnished; I won’t share my honor with idols.

Is 48:6-11

Israel Freed from Babylon 12“Listen to me, Jacob, Israel that I’ve chosen; I’m the only One, the first and the last.

3I’m the One that founded the earth and spread out the sky; when I call out the stars, they come out in force.

4Come here, all of you, and listen! Which of your idols has told you this? I’ve set my regard on Cyrus; he’ll carry out my intentions on Babylon; he’ll oppose the Babylonians.

5There I’ve said it; I’ve called him; I’ve brought him; he’ll succeed.

6Come close and listen: from the beginning I’ve spoken plainly about what would happen.” Already the Lord GOD and his Spirit have sent me with that message.

Is 48:12-16

7This is what the LORD says, your Redeemer, the Holy One of Israel: “I’m the LORD your God that teaches you how to succeed and leads you the way you should go.

8If only you’d paid attention to what I told you, you’d have had peace like a river and goodness like ocean waves washing over you.

19Your descendants would have been too numerous to count, like grains of sand by the seashore; I wouldn’t have needed to cut them off or destroy them out of my presence.”

Is 48:17-19

20“Get out of Babylon! Escape from the Babylonians! Shout the news everywhere with joy: the LORD has rescued his servant Jacob!

21They weren’t thirsty while he led them through the desert; he made water gush out of rock for them to drink; 22“But there’s no peace for the wicked,” the LORD says. Is 48:20-22 The Servant of the LORD
