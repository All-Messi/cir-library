Nahum Chapter 3

1Misfortune awaits bloody Nineveh, filled with lies and pillage and plunder without limit.

2Do you hear the crack of whips, rattling wheels, galloping horses, rushing chariots!?

3Do you see cavalry charging, swords flashing, spears gleaming, many slain, piles of corpses, countless bodies, and people stumbling over them!?

4That’s all because of your continual prostitution, that charming mistress of sorceries; you sell nations by your prostitution, countries by your sorceries. 5“I’m your enemy,” the LORD of Hosts says; “I’ll pull your skirts up over your heads, and show the nations your nakedness, the kingdoms your disgrace.

6I’ll throw filth on you, make you disgusting, make a spectacle out of you!

7Everybody that sees you will back away and say, ‘Nineveh lies in ruins! Who’s going to grieve over her?’ Where will her comforters come from?”

Nah 3:1-7

8Are you any better than Thebes that sat by the Nile with water all around it, with the river as its wall and rampart?

9Nubia was its strength, Egypt too, with no limit; Put and the Libyans allied with it.

10Yet Thebes was carried into captivity. Its little ones were dashed to pieces at the head of every street; they cast lots for its honorable men and tied up its leaders in chains.

Nah 3:8-10

11You’ll get drunk too, Nineveh, go into a stupor, and look for refuge from your enemy.

12Your fortresses are like fig trees with ripe fruit; if you shake them, their figs fall into your mouth.

13Your troops are women! Your gates stand wide open for your enemies; fires consume your bars.

Nah 3:11-13

14Draw water for your siege! Strengthen your fortifications! Get in the clay pits to tread mortar! Put it in brick molds!

15But fire will burn you up; the sword will cut you down and eat you up like locusts. Though you have multiplied like crawling locusts and propagated like grasshoppers,

16though your traders have come to outnumber the stars— nevertheless, like crawling locusts your enemy will strip your land and fly away.

17Though your guards are like grasshoppers and your marshals resemble locust swarms settling in stone walls on a cold day, when the sun comes up, they fly away, nobody knows where.

Nah 3:14-17

18Your shepherds are asleep, king of Assyria; your nobles lie dead on the ground. Your people have scattered to the mountains; and there’s nobody to round them up.

19There’s no relief for your injury; your wound won’t heal. Everybody that hears about your destruction claps their hands. Who hasn’t come under your endless cruelty?

Nah 3:18-19
