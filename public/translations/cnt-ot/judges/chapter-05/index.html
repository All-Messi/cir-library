Judges Chapter 5

1Deborah and Barak sang that day, 2“Bless the LORD! because the leaders led in Israel, the people volunteered.

3Hear, you kings; listen, you officials; I’ll sing to the LORD; I’ll praise Israel’s God.

4LORD, when you went out from Seir, when you marched from Edom, the ground shook, the sky dripped, yes, the clouds dripped water.

5The mountains shook in front of the LORD; you, Sinai, shook in front of Israel’s God.

Jud 5:1-5

6“In the days of Shamgar son of Anath, in the days of Jael, caravans ceased; travelers kept to the back roads.

7The rural people ceased in Israel; they ceased till you arose, Deborah, as a mother in Israel.

8When the people chose new gods, war was in the gates; not a shield or spear was seen among 40,000 in Israel.

9My heart goes out to Israel’s commanders, who volunteered willingly among the people. Bless the LORD.

Jud 5:6-9

10“Sing, you that ride white donkeys, who sit on rich carpets and travel on the highway.

11At the sound of those who separate flocks by the watering places, they repeat the LORD’s triumphs, the triumphs of his rural people in Israel. Then the LORD’s people went down to the gates.

Jud 5:10-11

12“Wake up, wake up, Deborah! Wake up, wake up, and sing! Arise, Barak; lead away your captives, son of Abinoam.

3The remnant of the nobles came down; the LORD’s people came down for him against the powerful.

4They set out to the valley from Ephraim; they followed you, Benjamin, with your kin. The commanders marched down from Machir; from Zebulun they marched who carry the marshal’s staff.

Jud 5:12-14

15 15The officials of Issachar came with Deborah; as Issachar came so did Barak. They rushed out to the valley at his heels, but the clans of Reuben held back.

16 16Why did you linger among the sheepfolds? To hear the piping for the flocks? The clans of Reuben held back.

17 17Gilead stayed put beyond the Jordan. Why did Dan stay with his ships? Asher sat still at the coast by the sea and settled down by his landings.

18 18Zebulun was a people that risked their lives, Naphtali, too, on the high places in the countryside.

Jud 5:15-18

19“The kings came and fought; they fought the kings of Canaan at Taanach by the water of Megiddo; they got no spoils of silver.

20 20The stars fought from the sky; from their courses they fought Sisera.

21 21The current of the Kishon swept them away, the rushing torrent in the Kishon. March on, my soul, with confidence! 22“Then the horses’ hoofs beat loud with the galloping of steeds.

Jud 5:19-22

23“‘Curse Meroz,’ the angel of the LORD says; ‘put its people under a severe curse. They didn’t come to help the LORD against the powerful.’

24 24May Jael be most fortunate among women, the wife of Heber the Kenite, most fortunate among women in the tent.

25 25He asked for water; she gave him milk. She brought him curds in a beautiful bowl.

26 26She took a tent peg and a workman’s hammer; she struck Sisera a blow and crushed his head. She shattered and pierced his temple.

27 27He sank, he fell; he lay still at her feet. He sank and fell down dead at her feet.

Jud 5:23-27

28“She looked out the window; Sisera’s mother gazed through the lattice. ‘Why is his chariot so long in coming? Why do the hoof beats with his chariots tarry?’

29 29Her wise ladies answer; no, she answers herself, 30‘Aren’t they finding and dividing the spoil— a young woman or two for every man. spoil of embroidered dyed work for Sisera, two pieces of dyed work for the spoiler’s neck?’ 31“May all your enemies perish that way, LORD! But may your friends be like the sun when it rises at it brightest.” The land had peace

for 40
