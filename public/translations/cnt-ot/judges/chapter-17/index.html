Judges Chapter 17

1 A man named Micah in the hill country of Ephraim 2told his mother, “I took the 1,100 pieces of silver that were stolen from you, that you told me about and I heard you speak a curse on; I have them.” She said, “May the LORD bless my son.” 3He gave them back to her. She said, “I consecrate the silver to the LORD for my son to make a carved image and a molten image. Now I’ll return it to you.” he gave 200 pieces of it to a silversmith to make a carved image and a molten image. It was in Micah’s house. 5He had a shrine and appointed one of his sons as his priest. 6In those days, there wasn’t any king in Israel. Everyone did what seemed right to them.

7 A young Levite from the family of Judah was living temporarily in Bethlehem in Judah. 8He left to find a place to live. As he traveled, he came to Micah’s house in the hill country of Ephraim. 9Micah asked him where he was from. 10"Stay with me. Be a father and a priest for me. I’ll give you 10 pieces of silver a year, a suit of clothes, and living expenses."

11 The Levite was content to live with him and became like one of his sons. 12Micah installed the Levite as his priest in his house. 13Then he said, “Now I know the LORD will prosper me; I have a Levite as a priest."
