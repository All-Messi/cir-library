Judges Chapter 3

1The LORD left those nations to test the Israelites that hadn’t experienced war in Canaan. [2] 3The nations were the five lords of the Philistines, all the Canaanites, Sidonians, the Hivites on Mount Lebanon from Mount Baal-hermon to Lebo-hamath.

4They served to test whether Israel would obey the LORD’s commandments to their ancestors by Moses. 5So the Israelites lived among the Canaanites, Hittites, Amorites, Perizzites, Hivites, and Jebusites. 6They intermarried with them and served their gods. Othniel Judges Israel

7Israel forgot the LORD its God and served the images of Baal and Ashtoreth. 8He was angry and sold Israel to Cushan-rishathaim, king of Mesopotamia, for 8 years. 9When they appealed to the LORD, he raised up Othniel son of Kenaz, Caleb’s younger brother, to deliver them. 10The Spirit of the LORD stirred him up to judge Israel. He went out to war, and the LORD delivered Cushan-rishathaim to him. 11The land had peace for 40 years. Then Othniel died.

He said, “I have a message from God for you.” He got up from his seat. 21Ehud reached with his left hand, took his sword from his right thigh, and thrust it into Eglon’s belly. 22The hilt went in after the blade, and the fat closed over the blade. He didn’t pull it out, and the refuse came out. 23Ehud went out to the anteroom, closed the doors to the roof room, and locked them. Jud 3:12-23

24When he’d gone, the servants came and saw the doors locked, and thought he was relieving himself in the cool room. 25They waited till they were completely confused. When he still didn’t come out, they took the key and opened the doors. There he was on the floor, dead.

26While they delayed, Ehud escaped past the idols to Seirah. 27When he arrived, he blew the ram’s horn in the hill country of Ephraim. The people went down from the hill country with him leading. 28He told them, “Follow me.
