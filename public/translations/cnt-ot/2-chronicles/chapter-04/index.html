2 Chronicles Chapter 4

1 He made

2 Ch 4:11-22

3 Figures of gourds were under it, entirely circling the sea (45ft). The oxen were in 2 rows cast in one piece with the Sea. 4It stood on 12 oxen, 3 facing out in each direction with their hindquarters inward. 5It was 4ins thick, with a brim like a cup—like a lily blossom. It could hold 27,000 gallons 3,000 baths.

6 He made 10 basins to wash in, and set 5 on each side for rinsing things for the burnt offering; but the sea was for the priests to wash in; 710 golden lampstands in the way prescribed, and set them in the Temple, 5 on each side; 2 Ch 4:1-7

10 He set the sea on the right side of the Temple toward the southeast. 2 Ch 4:8-10

11 Huram made the pails, shovels, and bowls, and finished the work for King Solomon on God’s Temple: 12the 2 pillars, the bowls and 2 capitals on top of the pillars, the 2 networks to cover the 2 bowls on the capitals; 13the 400 pomegranates for the 2 networks, 2 rows for each network to cover the 2 bowls of the capitals, 14the stands and the basins on the stands, 15the sea with 12 oxen under it.

16 Huram made the pails, shovels, forks, and utensils out of polished bronze. 17The king had them cast in the clay soil between Succoth and Zeredah on the Jordan plain. 18Solomon made these utensils in great numbers. The weight of the bronze couldn’t be calculated.

19 Solomon made everything in God’s Temple out of purest gold: the altar, the tables for the Bread of the Presence, 20the lampstands with their lamps to burn in front of the inner sanctuary as prescribed, 21the flowers, lamps, tongs; 22the snuffers, bowls, spoons, firepans; the entrance to the Temple, its inner doors for the Holy of Holies, and the doors of the Temple (the nave).
