2 Samuel Chapter 1

1After Saul died, David came back from massacring the Amalekites and stayed two days in Ziklag. 2The third day, a man came from Saul’s camp with torn clothes and dust on his head. He prostrated himself to David.

3David said, “Where have you come from?” “I’ve escaped from Israel’s camp.” 4“How did things go?” “They’ve fled from the battle; many have fallen. Saul and his son Jonathan are dead.” 5“How do you know?” 6“I happened to be on Mount Gilboa, and Saul was leaning on his spear. The chariots and cavalry were coming up on him. 7When he looked back, he called out to me, 8‘Who are you?’ “I’m an Amalekite.’ 9“He said, ‘Please finish me off. I’m in agony; my life lingers on.’ 10“So I stood beside him and killed him. I knew he couldn’t live. I took the crown off his head and the armband from his arm and brought them to you.”

2 Sam 1:1-10

11David and his men tore their clothes. 12They mourned and cried and fasted till evening for Saul, Jonathan, the LORD’s people, and Israel, because they’d fallen by the sword.

13David asked the young man, “Where are you from?” “I’m the son of a foreigner, an Amalekite.”

14David said, “How is it you weren’t afraid to destroy the LORD’s anointed?”

15He called one of the young men, “Cut him down.” So he struck him.

16David told him, “Your blood is on your own head. You testified against yourself that you killed the LORD’s anointed.” 17David chanted this dirge over Saul and Jonathan. 18He told them to teach Judah the Son of the Bod; the Book of Jashar records it:

2 Sam 1:11-18

David’s Lament for Saul and Jonathan 19“Your beauty, Israel, is slain on your high places! How the strong have fallen!

0Don’t tell it in Gath or announce it in the streets of Ashkelon; the Philistines would celebrate; the uncircumcised would be elated.

1Mountains of Gilboa, may there be no dew or rain on you or fields to produce offerings because the shield of strong men was defiled there, Saul’s shield, not anointed with oil.

2From the blood of the slain, from the fat of the strong, Jonathan’s bow didn’t retreat; Saul’s sword didn’t come back empty.

2 Sam 1:19-22

3Saul and Jonathan, beloved and pleasant in life, died together. They were faster than eagles, stronger than lions.

4Young women of Israel, mourn for Saul, who dressed you luxuriously in scarlet, who put gold sequins on your clothes.

5How the strong have fallen in battle! Jonathan, slain on your high places.

6I’m distressed over you, Jonathan my brother; you were so pleasant to me. Your love for me was more wonderful than the love of women.

7How the powerful have fallen, and the weapons of war have perished!”
