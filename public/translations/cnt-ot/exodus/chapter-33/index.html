Exodus Chapter 33

1 The LORD told Moses, “Leave here and go to the land I swore I’d give the descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. 2I’ll send an angel ahead of you to drive out the Canaanite, Amorite, Hittite, Perizzite, Hivite, and Jebusite. 3Go up to a land flowing with milk and honey. I won’t go up with you, because you’re stubborn, so I won’t destroy you on the way."

4 When the people heard that discouraging word, they went into mourning; nobody put on jewelry. 5The LORD had told Moses, “Tell them, ‘You’re being stubborn. If I’d go up among you for a moment, I’d destroy you. So take off your jewelry so I’ll know what I’m to do with you.’” 6They didn’t wear jewelry from Mount Horeb/Sinai on.

7 Moses would pitch a tent a good distance outside the camp—what he called The Tent of Meeting. Anybody that wanted to consult the LORD would go out to it. 8Whenever Moses went out, everybody would stand at the entrance of his tent and watch him till he went into the tent. 9Then the pillar of cloud would come down and stand at the entrance, and the LORD would talk to him. 10When people saw the pillar, they would worship at their own tents. 11The LORD would speak to Moses directly, the way you talk to a friend. When Moses would come back to the camp, his assistant Joshua son of Nun, a young man, would stay behind at the tent.

12 Then Moses told the LORD, “You tell me to bring the people up, but you haven’t let me know who you’ll send with me. You’ve said you knew me by name, and you’ve been gracious to me. 13Let me know your ways so I can know you and please you. Consider this nation your people. 15"If you don’t go with us, don’t take us away from here. 16How can your people and I know we’re being pleasing to you? Isn’t it by having you with us so we can tell the difference between ourselves and other people?” 17"I’ll do what you’ve asked, because you’re pleasing to me, and I know you by name.” 19"I’ll make my goodness pass by in front of you, and show you who I am. I’ll decide who to give grace to and who to show mercy to. 20But you can’t see my face. Nobody can see me and live! 21Here’s a place beside me; stand on that rock. 22While my glory is passing by, I’ll put you in a crevice in the rock and cover you with my hand till I’m past. 23Then I’ll take my hand away, and you can see my back; but you won’t see my face."
