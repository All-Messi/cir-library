Exodus Chapter 15

1 Then Moses and the Israelites sang this song to the LORD:

2 The LORD is my strength and song;

3 He’s a soldier;

4 He has thrown Pharaoh’s chariots and army into the Red Sea;

5 The depths covered them;

6 Your right hand, LORD, was majestic in its power;

7 In your extreme excellence, you overthrew your opponents.

8 At the blast of your nostrils, the water piled up;

9 The enemy said, ‘I’ll pursue,

10 You blew with your wind; the sea covered them;

11 Who is like you, majestic in holiness,

12 You reached out your hand,

13 In your kindness you led the people you redeemed;

14 The nations have heard and tremble;

15 The chiefs in Edom are scared;

16 Panic and dread have fallen on them;

17 You’ll take them and plant them in the mountain

18 The LORD will reign forever."

19 Pharaoh’s horses, chariots, and cavalry went into the sea, and the LORD washed the water back over them; but the Israelites walked through on dry seabed. 20Miriam the prophetess, Aaron’s sister, took a tambourine and went out with the women with tambourines and dancing.

21 She lined out a song to them,

22 Moses led the Israelites from the Red Sea to the Wilderness of Shur. They traveled three days without finding water. 23When they got to Marah, the water was too “brackish” to drink. That’s why it was called Marah. 24The people complained to Moses, “What are we going to drink?” 25He called out to the LORD, and he showed him a tree. Moses threw it in the water and made it drinkable. The LORD made a statute and regulation to test them. 26"If you sincerely pay attention to me, do what I consider right, listen to my commandments, and keep my statutes, I won’t put any disease on you that I put on the Egyptians, because I’m your healer."

27 They came to Elim with its 12 springs and 70 date palms, and camped there beside the water.
