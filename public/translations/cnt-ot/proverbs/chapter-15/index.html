Proverbs Chapter 15

1A gentle answer deflects anger; harsh words make tempers flare.

2The wise dispense knowledge; fools expound foolishness.

3The LORD sees everything; he watches the evil and the good.

4Gentle words are a tree of life, but perverse talk breaks people’s spirit.

5Fools reject their father’s discipline, but those attentive to correction become prudent.

6There’s wealth in the house of the godly, but the earnings of the sinful bring trouble.

Prov 15:1-6

7The lips of the wise give good advice; the hearts of fools have nothing to offer.

8The LORD detests the sacrifices of sinners, but delights in the prayers of the righteous.

9He detests the way of the wicked, but loves people who pursue goodness.

10Those who abandon the way incur harsh discipline; those who reject discipline die.

11Sheol and Abaddon lie open before the LORD; how much more our hearts!

12Scoffers don’t like people that rebuke them; so they stay away from the wise.

Prov 15:7-12

13A cheerful heart makes a happy face, but a sad heart breaks the spirit.

14The mind of the wise is hungry for insight, but the mouth of fools feeds on foolishness.

15Negative people have nothing but bad days, but with a happy heart they enjoy a continual feast.

16It’s better to have a little with reverence for the LORD than to have a lot with turmoil attached.

17A plate of vegetables with someone you love tastes better than a roast from a grain-fed cow with someone you don’t like.

18Hot-tempered people stir up quarrels, but controlling anger calms contentions.

Prov 15:13-18

19Briers overgrow lazy people’s paths, but the upright travel on an open road.

20Wise children make a happy father; foolish ones despise their mother.

21Foolishness is fun for the foolish, but the wise walk on a straight path.

22Without advice plans go awry, but with many advisors they succeed.

23Everyone enjoys giving a good answer, and a timely word delights those who hear it!

24The path of the wise leads upward away from Sheol below.

Prov 15:19-24

25The LORD tears down the house of the proud but protects the widow’s home.

26The LORD detests evil plans but delights in pure words.

27Those greedy for gain bring trouble home; those who refuse bribes live in peace.

28The godly think before they speak; sinners blurt out evil without thinking.

29The LORD stands far away from the sinful, but he listens to the prayers decent people offer.

30A cheerful look makes the heart happy; good news refreshes us deep inside.

Prov 15:25-30

31If you listen to constructive correction, you’ll live among the wise.

32If you ignore instruction, you only hurt yourself; by heeding correction, you gain insight.

33Respecting the LORD teaches wisdom; humility comes before honor.

Prov 15:31-33
