Proverbs Chapter 17

1 A dry morsel eaten in peace tastes better

2 A wise servant will rule a shameful son

3 Fire refines silver and a furnace purifies gold,

4 Wrongdoers pay attention to gossip;

5 People that mock the poor insult their Maker;

6 Grandchildren are the crown of old age;

7 Fine speech doesn’t fit with being a fool;

8 A bribe is a lucky charm in its owner’s sight;

9 Forgiving fosters love,

10 A rebuke penetrates deeper into thoughtful people

11 Rebellious people are looking for trouble;

12 Facing a bear robbed of her cubs

13 Misfortune never leaves the house of anyone

14 Starting an argument is like opening a floodgate;

15 Acquitting the guilty and condemning the innocent

16 Why spend money on educating fools

17 A friend loves despite the circumstance;

18 It’s not good judgment

19 Loving sin amounts to loving conflict;

20 A crooked heart doesn’t prosper;

21 The parents of a fool feel discouraged;

22 A happy heart is good medicine;

23 Sinners accept secret bribes

24 Sensible people find wisdom nearby;

25 Foolish children are a grief to their father

26 It’s wrong to punish the godly for their godliness

27 Wise people speak with restraint;

28 Even fools, people consider wise
