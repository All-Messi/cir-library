Proverbs Chapter 30

1The Words of Agur Son of Jakeh, an Oracle Agur says to Ithiel and Ucal:

2Please listen, son, son of my womb, son of my vows;

3don’t waste your strength on the kind of women that destroy kings.

4Lemuel, it’s not for kings to guzzle wine or for rulers to get addicted to alcohol;

5the fear is that they’ll forget the Law and not give justice to the oppressed.

6Give alcohol to the dying and wine to those in pain;

7let them drink and forget their condition, and not remember their suffering anymore.

8Speak up for those who can’t speak for themselves;

9judge justly and defend the poor and needy.

Prov 31:1-9

Tribute to a Worthy Woman

10Who can find a capable wife of noble character? She’s worth more than jewels.

11Her husband can rely on her, and he’ll not lack anything of value.

12She’ll do him good her whole life.

13She looks for wool and linen, and enjoys working with her hands.

14Like a merchant ship, she brings her food from a distance.

15She gets up before daylight to feed her family and assign the day’s jobs to her maids.

16She inspects a field and buys it, and plants a vineyard from her earnings.

Prov 31:10-16

17She wraps her waist with strength and makes her arms strong.

18She makes sure her efforts are profitable, and her lamp doesn’t go out at night.

19She reaches for the distaff, and her hands hold the spindle.

20She reaches out to the poor and needy.

21She’s not worried when it snows because she has dressed her family in scarlet.

22She wears purple and fine linen and makes her own bedclothes.

Prov 31:17-22

23The community knows her husband in the city gates, where he sits among the leaders of the land.

24She makes sashes and linen garments to sell to the merchants.

25She dresses in strength and dignity and smiles at the future.

26She speaks wisely and teaches kindly.

27She takes care of her household and doesn’t idle away her time.

28Her children grow up and bless her; her husband, too, and praises her: 29“Lots of women do nobly, but you excel them all.”

29Three things are stately in their stride, no, four that walk with majestic bearing:

30Charm is deceitful and beauty is vain, but praise a woman that respects the LORD.

31Reward her for what she does, and let her work speak for her in the community.

Prov 31:23-31

32If you’ve been foolish by acting proud or putting other people down, cover your mouth in shame.

33As churning cream makes butter and punching a nose makes it bleed, so stirring up anger causes quarrels.
