Proverbs Chapter 19

1The honest poor are better off than dishonest fools.

2It’s not good to not know; enthusiasm doesn’t compensate for ignorance.

3When people’s foolishness brings them to ruin, they blame the LORD.

4Wealth draws lots of friends that desert you if poverty comes.

5False witnesses will get their just due; perjurers won’t get off free.

6Many ask for generous people’s blessing; everybody makes friends with bighearted people.

Prov 19:1-6

7Relatives of the poor despise them, and their friends leave! Appealing to them won’t bring them back.

8People that gain wisdom care about themselves; they prosper who cherish understanding.

9False witnesses won’t go unpunished; liars perish.

10Living in luxury isn’t fitting for a fool, much less for a slave to rule over princes.

11Discretion gives you control over anger; you learn to delight in overlooking sin.

12A king’s anger is like a lion’s roar; his blessing falls like dew on grass.

Prov 19:7-12

13A foolish child destroys a father; a nagging wife is like constant dripping.

14Houses and wealth people inherit from parents, but a prudent wife comes from the LORD.

15Laziness lulls people to sleep; shiftless people go hungry.

16Those who keep the commandments keep their life; people that are careless about their way will lose it.

17Those gracious to the poor lend to the LORD; he’ll reward them for the good they do.

18Discipline your children while there’s hope so you won’t ruin their lives.

Prov 19:13-18

19Let angry people suffer the consequences; bail them out, and you’ll have it to do again.

20Listen to advice and respond to discipline so you can gain wisdom for later on.

21People make their plans, but the LORD’s counsel is what prevails.

22Loyalty makes everyone better; poor people are better at it than liars.

23Respecting the LORD leads to life; you can sleep in peace without worrying about misfortune.

24Lazy people stick their fingers in the plate, but they don’t even bring the food to their mouth.

Prov 19:19-24

25Punish scoffers so the simpleminded can learn prudence; reprove the wise and they’ll be wiser still.

26Children that mistreat their parents are an embarrassment and a public disgrace.

27If you stop paying attention to discipline, child, you’ll never learn what you need to know.

28A worthless witness makes mockery of justice; a sinful mouth eats sin.

29Penalties are designed for scoffers, and scourging for the backs of fools.

Prov 19:25-29
