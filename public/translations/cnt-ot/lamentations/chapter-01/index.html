Lamentations Chapter 1

1 How lonely the city sits that used to bustle with people!

2 She cries bitterly at night; tears are on her cheeks.

3 Judah has gone into exile in suffering and hard servitude;

4 The roads to Zion are in mourning

5 Her adversaries have become her masters; her enemies prosper.

6 The majesty is gone from Zion;

7 In her time of suffering and homelessness,

8 Jerusalem sinned grievously, so she’s become filthy.

9 Her uncleanness was in her skirts;

10 The foe has reached out for her treasured things;

11 The people are groaning as they look for bread;

12 Is it nothing to you that go by?

13 He sent fire from on high into my bones, and it descended on them.

14 He has tied my sins together into a yoke around my neck;

15 The Lord has rejected the strong men that are with me;

16 I weep for those things; my eyes run down with tears

17 Zion holds out her hands, but there’s no one to comfort her;

19 I called to my lovers, but they deceived me;

20 Look, LORD, because I’m in distress; my soul is in turmoil.

21 They’ve heard that I’m groaning with nobody to comfort me.
