Lamentations Chapter 5

1 LORD, remember what’s happened to us;

2 Our inheritance has been turned over to foreigners,

3 We’ve become orphans without fathers;

4 We have to pay for drinking water and buy wood.

5 Our drivers put yokes on our necks;

6 We’ve submitted to Egypt and Assyria to get enough bread.

7 Our fathers sinned, and they’re gone,

8 Slaves rule us; nobody can deliver us from them.

9 We risk our lives just getting bread

10 Our skin is hot like an oven,

11 They rape the women in Zion

12 They hang up officials by their hands

13 Young men work at the grinding mill

14 Elders are gone from the city gate,

15 The joy in our hearts is gone;

16 The crown has fallen from our head;

17 Our heart is faint because of it;

18 Jackals prowl in desolate Zion.

19 LORD, you rule forever;

20 Why don’t you keep us in mind forever?

21 Restore us to yourself, LORD, so we can be restored;
