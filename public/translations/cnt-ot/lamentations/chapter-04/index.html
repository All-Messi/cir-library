Lamentations Chapter 4

1 How the gold has tarnished; how pure gold has changed!

2 The precious sons of Zion, worth their weight in gold,

3 Even jackals nurse their young,

4 The infant’s tongue sticks to the roof of its mouth for thirst;

5 People that used to eat delicacies sit desolate in the streets;

6 The iniquity of my people is worse than the sin of Sodom,

7 Zion’s Nazirites were purer than snow, whiter than milk,

8 Now they’re darker than soot, not recognizable in the streets;

9 Those killed with the sword are happier than those slain by hunger;

10 Merciful women boiled their own children;

11 The LORD has accomplished his wrath,

12 The kings and inhabitants of the region

13 Because of its prophets’ sins and its priests’ iniquities—

15 They shouted at them, “Get away! Unclean! Don’t touch!"

16 The LORD has scattered them;

17 Our eyes failed us; looking for help was useless;

18 People dogged our steps so we couldn’t walk in the streets;

19 Our pursuers were faster than eagles in the sky;

20 Our very breath, the LORD’s anointed, was captured in their pits;

21 Celebrate and be happy, Edom that lives in Uz;

22 The punishment for your sinfulness is finished, Zion;
