1 Chronicles Chapter 21

1Satan stood up against Israel and tempted David to take a census. 2David told Joab and the commanders of the troops, “Go count Israel from Dan to Beersheba and bring me the results so I can know how many there are.”

2David issued an order to gather the foreigners in Israel and set stonecutters to dress stones for God’s Temple. 3He provided large stores of iron for making nails for the doors in the gates and for clamps, more bronze than could be weighed, 4and cedar logs without number. The Sidonians and Tyrians brought him large supplies of cedar timber. 5He said, “My son Solomon is young and inexperienced, and Yahveh’s Temple will be magnificent, famous, and glorious all over the world. I’ll stockpile materials for it.” David gathered lots of material for it before he died.

6Then he called Solomon and charged him to build a temple for Yahveh God of Israel, 7“Son, I intended to build a temple for Yahveh, 8but his message came to me, ‘You won’t build me a temple; you’ve shed so much blood and fought great wars. 9You’ll have a son that’s not a military figure. I’ll give him rest from his enemies everywhere. His name will be Solomon because I’ll give Israel ‘peace’ and quiet during his life. 10He’ll build a temple for me. He’ll be my son, and I’ll be his father. I’ll make his kingdom permanently secure.’ 11Yahveh be with you so you can succeed at building it. 12May he give you with discretion and intelligence, and give you charge over Israel so you can keep his Law. 13You’ll prosper if you observe carefully the statutes and ordinances he commanded Moses. Be strong and courageous; don’t be afraid or intimidated. 14I’ve taken great pains to stockpile materials: 3775 tons 100,000 talents of gold, 37,750 tons 1,000,000 talents of silver, bronze and iron past weighing, timber, and stone. You can add to that. 15You have lots of stonecutters, stone masons, and carpenters skilled in all kinds of work. 16There’s no limit to the gold, silver, bronze, and iron. Get started. Yahveh be with you.”

1 Ch 22:6-16

8David said to God, “I’ve committed an awful sin. Please take it away. I’ve done something really foolish.”

9Yahveh told Gad, David’s seer, 10“Tell David, ‘Yahveh says 11to take 12either three years of famine, three months of being swept away by your enemies’ sword, or three days of the sword of Yahveh when his angel destroys throughout Israel.’ What should I report to the One that sent me?”

1 Ch 21:8-12

13David told Gad, “I’m in great distress. I’ll cast myself on Yahveh’s mercy because it’s so great. Don’t let me fall into human hands.” 14Yahveh sent a plague on Israel; 70,000 people died. 15He sent an angel to destroy Jerusalem; but as he was about to do it, he looked and was sad over the catastrophe. He told the destroying angel, “That’s enough; relax your hand.” The angel was standing by the threshing floor of Ornan/Araunah the Jebusite. 16David looked up and saw him standing between earth and sky with his sword held out over Jerusalem. David and the elders, covered with burlap, fell on their faces. 17He told God, “I’m the one that sinned. What have these sheep done? Yahveh my God, please plague me and my father’s house, not your people.”

1 Ch 21:13-17

David Builds an Altar

17He directed Israel’s leaders to help Solomon, 18“Isn’t Yahveh with you? Hasn’t he given you rest in every direction? He’s delivered to you the nations in the land, and it’s under control. 19Set your heart and soul to look to him. Build his sanctuary so you can bring the Ark and God’s holy articles into it.”

1 Ch 22:17-19

Duties of Levites

18Then the angel of Yahveh had Gad tell David to build an altar on Ornan’s/Araunah’s threshing floor. 19David went up. 20Ornan/Araunah turned and saw the angel, and his four sons hid. He was threshing wheat. 21As David came to him, he left the threshing floor and prostrated himself. 22David told him, “Sell me this threshing floor so I can build an altar on it. Give it to me at full price so God will restrain the plague.”

23Ornan/Araunah said, “Take it and do what seems good. I’ll give the oxen for burnt offerings, the threshing sledges for wood, the wheat for the grain offering—all of it.”

24But King David said, “I won’t take what’s yours for Yahveh or offer a burnt offering that doesn’t cost me anything.” 25He paid

him 15

lbs 600 shekels of gold for the site. 26He built an altar there and offered burnt offerings and peace offerings. He called to Yahveh, and he answered him with fire from the sky on the altar. 27Yahveh told the angel to sheath his sword.

28When David saw that Yahveh had answered him, he offered sacrifice there, 29because the Tabernacle and altar that Moses made were in the countryside at Gibeon. 30But David couldn’t go to it to ask God because the angel’s sword terrified him. 22:1David said, “This is the house of Yahveh God and the altar of burnt offering for Israel.”
