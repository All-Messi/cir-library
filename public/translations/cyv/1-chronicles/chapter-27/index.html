1 Chronicles Chapter 27

1These were heads of families, commanders of thousands and hundreds, and their officers that served the king in regard to the divisions that came in and went out month by month all year. Each division num

bered 24

,000.

2Jashobeam Ben-Zabdiel was responsible for the first division for the first month. 3He descended from Perez and was chief of the commanders of the army for the first month.

4Dodai the Ahohite and his division were responsible for the second month with Mikloth as chief officer.

5The third commander of the army for the third month was Benaiah Ben-Jehoiada the priest, as chief. 6This Benaiah was the most elite soldier of

the 30

and responsible for 30. His son Ammizabad was responsible for his division. 1 Ch 27:1-6

7The fourth for the fourth month was Joab’s brother Asahel and his son Zebadiah.

8The fifth for the fifth month was commander Shamhuth the Izrahite.

9The six for the sixth month was Ira Ben-Ikkesh the Tekoite.

10The seventh for the seventh month was Helez the Pelonite, descendants of Ephraim.

11The eighth for the eighth month was Sibbecai the Hushathite from the Zerahites.

12The ninth for the ninth month was Abiezer the Anathothite from the Benjamites.

13The tenth for the tenth month was Maharai the Netophathite from the Zerahites.

14The eleventh for the eleventh month was Benaiah the Pirathonite, descendant of Ephraim.

15The twelfth for the twelfth month was Heldai the Netophathite of Othniel.

1 Ch 27:7-15

Tribal Leaders

16In charge of the tribes of Israel The chief officer for the Reubenites was Eliezer Ben-Zichri; for the Simeonites, Shephatiah Ben-Maacah;

17for Levi, Hashabiah Ben-Kemuel; for Aaron, Zadok;

18for Judah, Elihu, David’s brother; for Issachar, Omri Ben-Michael;

19for Zebulun, Ishmaiah Ben-Obadiah; for Naphtali, Jeremoth Ben-Azriel;

20for Ephraim, Hoshea Ben-Azaziah; for the Manasseh half-tribe, Joel Ben-Pedaiah;

21for the Manasseh half-tribe in Gilead, Iddo Ben-Zechariah; for Benjamin, Jaasiel Ben-Abner;

22for Dan, Azarel Ben-Jeroham. Those were the officers of the tribes of Israel.

1 Ch 27:16-22

23But David didn’t count the ones

under 20

because Yahveh had said he would multiply Israel like the stars in the sky. 24Joab Ben-Zeruiah had started to count them but didn’t finish. Wrath came on Israel because of it, and the number wasn’t included in The Chronicles of King David.

25Azmaveth Ben-Adiel oversaw the king’s storehouses. Jonathan Ben-Uzziah oversaw the storehouses in the country, towns, villages, and towers.

26Ezri Ben-Chelub oversaw the farmers that plowed the ground.

27Shimei the Ramathite oversaw vineyards. Zabdi the Shiphmite oversaw what the vineyards produced and what was stored in cellars.

28Baal-hanan the Gederite oversaw the olive and sycamore trees in the western foothills. Joash oversaw the stores of oil.

1 Ch 27:23-28

29Shitrai the Sharonite oversaw the cattle that grazed in Sharon. Shaphat Ben-Adlai oversaw the cattle in the valleys.

30Obil the Ishmaelite oversaw the camels. Jehdeiah the Meronothite oversaw the donkeys.

31Jaziz the Hagrite oversaw the flocks. Those oversaw David’s property.

1 Ch 27:29-31

32Jonathan, David’s uncle, was an intelligent advisor and secretary. Jehiel Ben-Hachmoni tutored David’s sons.

33Ahithophel was advisor to the king. Hushai the Archite was the king’s friend.

34Jehoiada Ben-Benaiah and Abiathar succeededpl Ahithophel. Joab commanded the king’s army.

1 Ch 27:32-34

David’s Charge to Israel
