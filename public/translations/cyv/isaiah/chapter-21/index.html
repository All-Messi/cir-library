Isaiah Chapter 21

1Oracle About the Sandy Wastes Beside the Sea Like a haboob sweeping in from the Negev, catastrophe will sweep in through the desert from a terrible land.

2I see a harsh vision: plunderers plundering; destroyers destroying. Go on, Elam; lay siege, Media. I’m going to put an end to the groaning Babylon’s caused.

3My body’s in agony; pangs have seized me like a woman in labor. I’m so faint I can’t hear, so scared I can’t see.

4My mind is reeling, the horror is overwhelming; I longed for the twilight; now I’m afraid of the dark.

5The people are setting tables and spreading rugs to sit on; they’re eating and drinking. But— “Get up, captains; oil the shields for battle!” Is 21:1-5

6Then Yahveh said to me, “Station a sentry to report what he sees.

7When he sees riders, horsemen in pairs, a donkey train, a camel caravan, he needs to stay alert.”

8Then the sentry shouted like a lion, “Yahveh, every day I’ve manned my station on the tower; every night I’ve stood at my post.

9Now at last, here come riders, horsemen in pairs. They’re reporting, ‘Fallen! Babylon’s fallen! its images shattered on the ground.’”

0My threshed people, winnowed on the threshing floor! I’m reporting what I’ve heard from Yahveh of Hosts, the God of Israel. Is 21:6-10

11Oracle About Edom Someone keeps calling to me from Seir, “Watchman, what time is it? How long till morning?”

2The watchman answers, “Morning is coming, but night will soon return; if you want updates, come back later and ask again.”

Is 21:11-12

13Oracle About Arabia Caravans of Dedanites, you have to spend the night hiding in the deserts of Arabia.

4People in Tema, bring water for these thirsty refugees; meet them with food,

15because they’re running from the drawn sword, the bent bow, the press of battle.

16This is what Yahveh told me, “A year from now, as a hired hand’s contract would read, Kedar’s splendor will end. 17Its surviving archers and strong men will be few; I, Yahveh God of Israel, have spoken. Is 21:13-17
