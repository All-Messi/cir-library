Isaiah Chapter 42

1“My Servant that I uphold, my Chosen One I delight in, I’ve put my Spirit on him; he’ll bring righteousness to the Gentiles.

2 2He won’t holler or be boisterous; he won’t make a racket in the streets.

3 3He won’t break a bruised reed or put out a flickering wick; he’ll faithfully render justice.

4 4He won’t falter or lose heart; he’ll establish justice on earth; distant lands will rely on his instruction.”

Is 42:1-4

5 5God Yahveh says— who created the sky and stretched it out, who made the earth and what comes from it, who gives breath to the people on it and spirit to those who walk in it— 6“I’m Yahveh; I’ve called you to demonstrate my goodness; I’ll hold you by the hand and watch over you. I’ll appoint you to be a covenant for the people, a light for the Gentiles

7 7to open the eyes of the blind, to free prisoners from dungeons and those living in darkness from prison.

8 8I’m Yahveh; that’s my name. I won’t share my honor with anyone else or my praise with carved images.

9 9My former prophecies have already happened; now I’m prophesying new ones; I’ll tell you about them before they occur.”

Is 42:5-9

A Song of Praise to Yahveh

10Sing a new song to Yahveh; sing his praise from the ends of the earth, you that sail the ocean, you distant lands and people that live there!

11Let the desert towns join the chorus, the villages where Kedar lives; let the people of Sela sing for joy and shout from the mountaintops.

12Let them honor Yahveh and declare his praise in distant lands.

13HeY will rush out like a soldier and stir up his fury like a warrior; he’ll raise his battle cry and prevail over his enemies.

Is 42:10-13

14“I’ve kept quiet,” God says, “and restrained myself for a long time; now like a woman in labor, I’ll groan and gasp and pant.

15I’ll make mountains and hills desolate and wither their vegetation; I’ll turn rivers into wadis and dry up the ponds.

16I’ll lead the blind on a new road, guide them in unfamiliar paths; I’ll make darkness into light ahead of them and make rough places smooth; I’ll do these things; I won’t leave them undone.

17I’ll drive back and completely disgrace those who trust in idols and say to molten images, ‘You’re our gods.’

Is 42:14-17

Israel Deaf and Blind 18“Listen, you deaf; look, you blind.

19Who is as blind as my servant, as deaf as my messenger? Who is as blind as my chosen people, as blind as Yahveh’s servant?

20You look at many things, but don’t see; your ears are open, but you don’t listen.”

21Because he’s good, Yahveh was pleased to make his Law great and glorious.

22But his people have been robbed and plundered, trapped in caves and hidden in prisons; they’ve become prey with no savior, spoil with nobody to demand, “Give them back!”

Is 42:18-22

23Who among you will learn from the past? Who will listen about the future?

4Who gave Jacob up for spoil and Israel to plunderers? Wasn’t it Yahveh that you’ve sinned against, whose ways you wouldn’t follow, whose Law you didn’t obey?

5So he poured out his hot anger on you in fierce warfare and enveloped you in flames; you didn’t catch on, and it burned you; you didn’t take it to heart.

Is 42:23-25

Israel’s Only Savior
