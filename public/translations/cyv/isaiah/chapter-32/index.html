Isaiah Chapter 32

1A righteous king is coming; his princes will rule honestly.

2Each will provide a refuge from the wind, a shelter from the storm, a stream of water in the desert, the shade of a big tree in parched land.

3Then the seeing will perceive; the hearing will listen.

4The rash will use good judgment; stuttering tongues will speak fluently.

Is 32:1-4

5Fools won’t be called noble anymore; rogues won’t be considered generous;

6because fools speak nonsense; they’re inclined to practice ungodliness, to lie about Yahveh, to keep hungry people hungry, and deprive the thirsty of a drink.

7Their methods are evil; they design plots to destroy the poor with slander even when the plea of the needy is just.

8But the truly noble make noble plans and stick with them. Is 32:5-8

9Get up, women of leisure; listen, complacent daughters.

10In a little over a year, trouble will come, complacent daughters; because the grapes will fail, the fruit harvest won’t happen.

11Tremble, women of leisure; shudder, complacent daughters. Strip down to the skin and wrap burlap around your waist.

Is 32:9-11

12Beat your breasts for pleasant fields and fruitful vines,

13for joyful houses and happy towns, for my people’s land, where thorns and thistles grow;

14because the palace will be abandoned, the populated towns will be empty, hill and watchtower will become permanent dens, a delight for wild donkeys, a pasture for flocks;

15till the Spirit is poured out on us from on high, till the desert becomes a fertile field, and the fertile field becomes a forest. Is 32:12-15

6Then justice will thrive in the countryside; goodness will grow in the fertile field.

7Goodness will lead to peace and bring extended calm and confidence.

8Then my people will live undisturbed in safe houses and peaceful retreats.

9Though hail flattens the forest and the city’s leveled,

0you’ll still be blessed who sow near water, who let livestock and donkeys range free. Is 32:16-20 An Oracle About Assyria
