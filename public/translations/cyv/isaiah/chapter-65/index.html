Isaiah Chapter 65

1 I let people look for me who weren’t asking about me; people that weren’t looking for me found me. I said, ‘I’m here! Over here!’ to people that didn’t call on me.

2 All day long I spread out my hands to a rebellious nation that follows its own ideas,

3 that continually angers me to my face by offering sacrifices to idols in sacred gardens and burning incense on pagan altars,

4 that sit in tombs and spend the night in secret places, that eat pig meat and have broth from unclean meat in their pots,

5 that say, ‘Stay away from me; don’t touch me; I’m holier than you are!’ They’re smoke in my nose, a fire that burns all day.

6 I’ve written it out in front of me: I won’t stand idly by; I’ll repay. I’ll pay back into their laps

7 the sins they and their ancestors committed; since they’ve burned incense on the mountains and scorned me on the hills, I’ll measure out their former deeds into their arms," Yahveh says. 8"As new wine comes from clusters and somebody says, ‘Don’t ruin it; it’s useful’; that’s what I’ll do with my servants and not destroy them all.

9 I’ll preserve a remnant from Jacob and Judah to inherit my mountains; my chosen ones will inherit it; my servants will live there.

10 Sharon will be a pasture for flocks; the Valley of Achor will be a resting place for herds— for my people that look to me. 11-12But I’ll destine for the sword you that leave me, that forget my holy mountain, that set a table for Fortune and fill cups with mixed wine for Destiny. You’ll bow down to slaughter, because I called, but you didn’t answer; I spoke, but you ignored me; you did what I consider sinful; you chose what I don’t delight in."

13 So the Lord Yahveh says, “My servants will eat, but you’ll be hungry; my servants will drink, but you’ll be thirsty; my servants will celebrate, but you’ll be humiliated;

14 my servants will make a joyful sound with a happy heart, but you’ll cry with a heavy heart and wail with a broken spirit.

15 The name you leave behind will be a curse to my chosen ones, and the Lord Yahveh will slay you; but I’ll call my servants another name

16 because those who bless themselves on earth will bless themselves by the true God; the ones that take an oath in the land will swear by the true God; because I’ll forget the former troubles and hide them from me. New Earth and Sky 17"I’m creating a new earth and sky; people won’t think about previous things anymore.

18 Be happy and celebrate forever in what I create, because I’ll create Jerusalem for celebrating and its people for happiness.

19 I’ll celebrate over Jerusalem and be happy with my people; they won’t hear weeping and crying anymore.

20 No longer will babies live just a few days, or adults die young; they’ll die when they’re 100; anybody that dies young will be considered cursed.

21 They’ll build houses and live in them, plant vineyards and eat their fruit.

22 They won’t build and someone else occupy them; they won’t plant and somebody else eat the fruit. The lifetime of my people will be like the lifetime of a tree; my chosen ones will enjoy what they make.

23 They won’t work to no end or have children that face catastrophes; they’ll be the offspring of people Yahveh has blessed— their descendants as well.

24 Before they call, I’ll answer; while they’re speaking, I’ll listen.

25 Wolf and lamb will graze together; a lion will eat straw like an ox, but snakes will eat dust. They won’t hurt anything in all my holy mountain,” Yahveh says. Judgment and Hope
