Exodus Chapter 39

1From the blue, purple, and scarlet material, they made finely woven vestments for Aaron to perform service in the Holy Place—like Yahveh commanded Moses.

2He made the ephod out of gold; blue, purple, and scarlet material; and finely twined linen. 3They hammered out gold sheets and cut them into threads that were woven in with the blue, purple, and scarlet material; and the fine linen—an artisan’s work. 4They made attaching shoulder pieces for the ephod—attached at the two upper ends. 5The skillfully woven band on it had the same workmanship: gold; blue, purple, and scarlet material; and finely twined linen. 6They made the onyx stones, set in delicate gold ornamentation. They engraved the onyx stones like the engravings on a signet, showing the names of Israel’s sons. 7He placed them on the shoulder pieces of the ephod as memorial stones for them.

Ex 39:1-7

22Then he made the robe of the ephod out of blue woven work. 23The opening of the robe was in the center at the top, like the opening of a coat of mail with a binding around its opening so it wouldn’t tear. 24They made pomegranates out of blue, purple, and scarlet material; and twined linen, [25] 26and alternated them with pure gold bells and pomegranates around the hem.

Ex 39:22-26

7They prepared tunics out of finely twined linen for Aaron and his sons. 28They made the turban, decorated caps, and the pants out of finely twined linen. 29They wove the sash out of finely twined linen and blue, purple, and scarlet material.

0They shaped the plate for the holy crown out of pure gold and inscribed it like the engravings of a signet: Holy to Yahveh. 31They fastened a blue cord to it and attached it to the top of the turban.

32That completed the work on the Tabernacle of the Tent of Meeting. The Israelites did everything Yahveh told Moses.

Ex 39:27-32

Moses Inspects the Work

33They brought the Tabernacle to Moses: the tent and its furnishings, its clasps, boards, bars, posts, and bases;

34the covering made of rams’ skins dyed red and goatskin leather; the screening curtain;

35the Ark of Testimony, its poles, and the mercy seat;

36the table, its utensils, and the Bread of the Presence;

37the pure gold lampstand, its arrangement of lamps, utensils, and olive oil for its lights;

38the gold altar, anointing oil, and fragrant incense; the curtain for the door to the tent;

39the bronze altar, its bronze grating, poles, and utensils; the laver and its stand;

40the curtains for the court, its posts and sockets, the screen for the entrance to the court, its cords, and pegs; the equipment for the service of the Tabernacle for the Tent of Meeting;

41the woven holy vestments for Aaron and his sons to serve as priests in the Holy Place.

42The Israelites did everything like Yahveh told Moses. 43Moses examined their work and blessed them.
