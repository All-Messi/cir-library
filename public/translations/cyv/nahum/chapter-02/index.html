Nahum Chapter 2

1Attackers are advancing to crush you, Nineveh. Man the ramparts, guard the road, muster your forces, get ready.

2Yahveh is going to restore majesty to Jacob, splendor to Israel; because plunderers have stripped it and cut the branches off its grapevines.

Nah 2:1-2

3The shields of the valiant flash red; the soldiers wear scarlet uniforms; the chariots glitter like steel as they muster into formation; they rattle their cypress spears.

4The chariots rush madly down the streets and race wildly around the squares. They shine like torches, dashing here and there like lightning.

5The king orders his officers; they scramble around in their haste and hurry to the wall to set up defenses.

6But their enemy tears open the gates of the river to undermine the palace.

7They strip it and loot it. Its young women moan like pigeons and beat their chests.

Nah 2:3-7

8Nineveh is like a leaking reservoir. “Stop! Stop! deserting residents!” but none of them even look back.

9Plunder the silver! Plunder the gold! Treasure without limit, wealth of all kinds!

10The city is desolate and in ruins! Hearts melt and knees knock! Loins throb in pain; faces turn pale!

Nah 2:8-10

11Where’s that lion’s den now, that feeding place for young lions, where the lion brought its prey, where its cubs stayed unthreatened,

2where it tore off enough for its cubs and strangled prey for its lionesses? It filled its lair with prey, with mutilated meat.

13Yahveh of Hosts says, “I’m your enemy. I’ll burn your chariots in smoke, kill your young lions with a sword, and leave you with no nations to prey on. Your ambassadors won’t be bringing reports anymore.”

Nah 2:11-1

Woe to Nineveh
