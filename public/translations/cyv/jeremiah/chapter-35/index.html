Jeremiah Chapter 35

1 A Message from Yahveh to Jeremiah at the Time of Jehoiakim Ben-Josiah, King of Judah The Obedience of the Racabites 2"Go bring the Rechabites to a room in Yahveh’s Temple and offer them wine."

3 I took Jaazaniah Ben-Jeremiah Ben-Habazziniah, his brothers and sons—the whole family of Rechabites—4to Yahveh’s Temple to the office for the sons of Hanan Ben-Igdaliah, the man of God. It was close to the officials’ quarters, above the room for Maaseiah Ben-Shallum, the doorkeeper. 5I put pitchers of wine and cups in front of the Rechabites, “Have a drink!"

6 But they said, “We won’t drink wine. Our ancestor Jonadab/Jehonadab Ben-Rechab told us, ‘Never drink wine, you or your descendants, forever. 7Don’t build houses, sow seed, plant vineyards, or own them. Live in tents so you’ll live a long time in the land.’ 8We, our wives, sons, and daughters have obeyed him. 9We don’t build houses or own vineyards, fields, or seed. 10We live in tents. 11But when Nebuchadnezzar invaded the land, we came to Jerusalem before the Babylonian and Syrian armies got here. So we’ve been living in Jerusalem."

12 Then a message from Yahveh came to Jeremiah,

13 Yahveh of Hosts, the God of Israel, says, “Tell the people in Judah and Jerusalem, ‘Won’t you receive instruction and listen to me? 14The descendants of Jonadab still observe his command not to drink wine. I’ve spoken to you time and again, but you’ve ignored me. 15I’ve sent you prophets time and again and said, “Turn from your sinful way, correct your deeds, stop worshiping other gods so you can live in the land I’ve given you and your ancestors.” But you’ve ignored me. 16Jonadab’s descendants have observed their ancestor’s command, but you’ve ignored me. 17So, I’m bringing on Judah and Jerusalem the disaster I’ve threatened. I spoke to them, but they ignored me; I called out to them, but they didn’t answer.’"

18 Then Jeremiah told the Rechabites, “Yahveh of Hosts says, ‘Because you’ve obeyed Jonadab, 19he’ll always have descendants to serve me.’” 35:12-19
