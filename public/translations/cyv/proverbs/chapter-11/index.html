Proverbs Chapter 11

1Yahveh detests a false balance; he delights in accurate weights.

2Dishonor follows pride; but wisdom lies with the humble.

3The integrity of the wise gives them direction; the dishonesty of the treacherous destroys them.

4Wealth won’t help you when judgment comes, but goodness delivers you from death.

5Goodness smooths the way for the blameless, but the sinful collapse under the weight of their own sin.

6Their goodness delivers the godly, their greed snares deceivers.

Prov 11:1-6

7When sinners die, their aspirations die as well; hope perishes with the godless.

8Good people leave trouble behind, but sinners head right for it.

9The godless destroy their neighbors with their mouths, but knowledge lets the righteous escape.

10A city celebrates when things go well for the godly; there’s also happy shouting over the demise of the sinful.

11The godly are a blessing that builds up a city; the mouths of sinners tear it down.

12Belittling you neighbor doesn’t help anybody; be silent and just let it rest.

13Gossips go around telling secrets; reliable people keep private matters private.

14Without wise leaders a nation falls; victory comes from having many advisors.

Prov 11:7-14

15Standing good for a stranger’s debt means you’re likely to pay it; avoid guaranteeing it and stay safe.

16A gracious woman earns respect; a violent man just gets rich.

17Kindness repays you; cruelty destroys you.

18Sinners get rich by deception; people that sow goodness make a legitimate living.

19Steadfast goodness promotes life; chasing after evil gets you killed.

20Yahveh detests the perverse in heart; he delights in those blameless in their living.

21Sinful people will certainly suffer for it, but the children of the just will be rescued.

22A beautiful woman with no discretion is like a gold ring in a pig’s snout.

Prov 11:15-22

23The godly look forward to good things; the sinful are simply waiting for wrath.

24Give liberally and grow richer; withhold what you should give and be in need.

25The generous prosper; those who refresh other people will be refreshed.

26People curse the one that hoards grain but bless the one that sells it to people that need it.

27Whoever seeks the good finds favor, and trouble comes to the one that’s looking for it.

28People that rely on their wealth will fall; but the godly will flourish like green leaves.

Prov 11:23-28

29Bring trouble on your family, and you’ll inherit the wind; the foolish serve the wise.

30Good people become a tree of life, the wise win friends.

31If the righteous are rewarded on earth, how much more godless sinners!

Prov 11:29-31
