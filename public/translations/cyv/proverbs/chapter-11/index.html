Proverbs Chapter 11

1 Yahveh detests a false balance; he delights in accurate weights.

2 Dishonor follows pride; but wisdom lies with the humble.

3 The integrity of the wise gives them direction; the dishonesty of the treacherous destroys them.

4 Wealth won’t help you when judgment comes, but goodness delivers you from death.

5 Goodness smooths the way for the blameless, but the sinful collapse under the weight of their own sin.

6 Their goodness delivers the godly, their greed snares deceivers.

7 When sinners die, their aspirations die as well; hope perishes with the godless.

8 Good people leave trouble behind, but sinners head right for it.

9 The godless destroy their neighbors with their mouths, but knowledge lets the righteous escape.

10 A city celebrates when things go well for the godly; there’s also happy shouting over the demise of the sinful.

11 The godly are a blessing that builds up a city; the mouths of sinners tear it down.

12 Belittling you neighbor doesn’t help anybody; be silent and just let it rest.

13 Gossips go around telling secrets; reliable people keep private matters private.

14 Without wise leaders a nation falls; victory comes from having many advisors.

15 Standing good for a stranger’s debt means you’re likely to pay it; avoid guaranteeing it and stay safe.

16 A gracious woman earns respect; a violent man just gets rich.

17 Kindness repays you; cruelty destroys you.

18 Sinners get rich by deception; people that sow goodness make a legitimate living.

19 Steadfast goodness promotes life; chasing after evil gets you killed.

20 Yahveh detests the perverse in heart; he delights in those blameless in their living.

21 Sinful people will certainly suffer for it, but the children of the just will be rescued.

22 A beautiful woman with no discretion is like a gold ring in a pig’s snout.

23 The godly look forward to good things; the sinful are simply waiting for wrath.

24 Give liberally and grow richer; withhold what you should give and be in need.

25 The generous prosper; those who refresh other people will be refreshed.

26 People curse the one that hoards grain but bless the one that sells it to people that need it.

27 Whoever seeks the good finds favor, and trouble comes to the one that’s looking for it.

28 People that rely on their wealth will fall; but the godly will flourish like green leaves.

29 Bring trouble on your family, and you’ll inherit the wind; the foolish serve the wise.

30 Good people become a tree of life, the wise win friends.

31 If the righteous are rewarded on earth, how much more godless sinners!
