Proverbs Chapter 19

1 The honest poor are better off than dishonest fools.

2 It’s not good to not know; enthusiasm doesn’t compensate for ignorance.

3 When people’s foolishness brings them to ruin, they blame Yahveh.

4 Wealth draws lots of friends that desert you if poverty comes.

5 False witnesses will get their just due; perjurers won’t get off free.

6 Many ask for generous people’s blessing; everybody makes friends with bighearted people.

7 Relatives of the poor despise them, and their friends leave! Appealing to them won’t bring them back.

8 People that gain wisdom care about themselves; they prosper who cherish understanding.

9 False witnesses won’t go unpunished; liars perish.

10 Living in luxury isn’t fitting for a fool, much less for a slave to rule over princes.

11 Discretion gives you control over anger; you learn to delight in overlooking sin.

12 A king’s anger is like a lion’s roar; his blessing falls like dew on grass.

13 A foolish child destroys a father; a nagging wife is like constant dripping.

14 Houses and wealth people inherit from parents, but a prudent wife comes from Yahveh.

15 Laziness lulls people to sleep; shiftless people go hungry.

16 Those who keep the commandments keep their life; people that are careless about their way will lose it.

17 Those gracious to the poor lend to Yahveh; he’ll reward them for the good they do.

18 Discipline your children while there’s hope so you won’t ruin their lives.

19 Let angry people suffer the consequences; bail them out, and you’ll have it to do again.

20 Listen to advice and respond to discipline so you can gain wisdom for later on.

21 People make their plans, but Yahveh’s counsel is what prevails.

22 Loyalty makes everyone better; poor people are better at it than liars.

23 Respecting Yahveh leads to life; you can sleep in peace without worrying about misfortune.

24 Lazy people stick their fingers in the plate, but they don’t even bring the food to their mouth.

25 Punish scoffers so the simpleminded can learn prudence; reprove the wise and they’ll be wiser still.

26 Children that mistreat their parents are an embarrassment and a public disgrace.

27 If you stop paying attention to discipline, child, you’ll never learn what you need to know.

28 A worthless witness makes mockery of justice; a sinful mouth eats sin.

29 Penalties are designed for scoffers, and scourging for the backs of fools.
