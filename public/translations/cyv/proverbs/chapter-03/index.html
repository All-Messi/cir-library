Proverbs Chapter 3

1Son, don’t forget what I’ve taught you; take my commandments to heart;

2they’ll add peace and length to your life.

3Don’t let kindness and truth slip away from you; tie them around your neck; write them on your heart.

4That way you’ll find blessing and have a good reputation with God and people.

5Trust in Yahveh with all your heart; don’t rely on your own perception.

6Acknowledge him in everything you do, and he’ll make straight paths for you to follow.

7Don’t get the idea that you’re smart; respect the LORD and turn your back on sin.

8It will heal your body and refresh your very bones.

Prov 3:1-8

9Honor Yahveh with your substance, the firstfruits of your crops.

10Your barns will be full; your vats will overflow with new wine.

Prov 3:9-10

11Son, don’t reject Yahveh’s discipline or give up when he corrects you;

12heY corrects the ones he cares about, like a father that disciplines a son he delights in.

13They are blessed who find wisdom, that gain insight.

14It’s more profitable than silver; it brings greater gains than fine gold.

15It’s more precious than jewels; nothing you desire can compare with it.

16Its left hand holds wealth and honor; long life is in its right hand.

7Its ways are pleasant; its paths are peaceful.

18It’s a life-giving tree for people that pick its fruit; everybody that holds onto it is happy.

Prov 3:11-18

19Yahveh founded the earth with wisdom and established the heavens with understanding.

20He broke up the depths with his knowledge, and caused dew to settle from the sky.

Prov 3:19-20

21Son, keep sound wisdom and discretion nearby; don’t let them out of your sight.

22They’ll refresh your soul and adorn your neck.

23Then you’ll walk along your road surefooted without stumbling.

24When you lie down, you won’t feel afraid; when you go to bed, your sleep will be sweet.

25Don’t be afraid of sudden panic or the ruin that sinful people may bring;

6because you’ll have confidence with Yahveh; he’ll keep your foot out of the snare.

Prov 3:21-26

27Don’t withhold good from people that need it when you can give it.

28Don’t tell them, “Come back tomorrow, and you can have it.”

29Don’t devise misfortune against the one that’s living safely beside you.

30Don’t be difficult with people if they’ve done you no wrong.

31Don’t envy the violent or choose to do what they do.

32Yahveh detests crooked people, but he’s close to godly ones.

33HeY puts a curse on the house of the sinful, but blesses the homes of the righteous.

34He scoffs at scoffers, but is gracious to the humble.

35The wise inherit honor; the foolish get disgrace.

Prov 3:27-35
