Proverbs Chapter 15

1 A gentle answer deflects anger; harsh words make tempers flare.

2 The wise dispense knowledge; fools expound foolishness.

3 Yahveh sees everything; he watches the evil and the good.

4 Gentle words are a tree of life, but perverse talk breaks people’s spirit.

5 Fools reject their father’s discipline, but those attentive to correction become prudent.

6 There’s wealth in the house of the godly, but the earnings of the sinful bring trouble.

7 The lips of the wise give good advice; the hearts of fools have nothing to offer.

8 Yahveh detests the sacrifices of sinners, but delights in the prayers of the righteous.

9 He detests the way of the wicked, but loves people who pursue goodness.

10 Those who abandon the way incur harsh discipline; those who reject discipline die.

11 Sheol and Abaddon lie open before Yahveh; how much more our hearts!

12 Scoffers don’t like people that rebuke them; so they stay away from the wise.

13 A cheerful heart makes a happy face, but a sad heart breaks the spirit.

14 The mind of the wise is hungry for insight, but the mouth of fools feeds on foolishness.

15 Negative people have nothing but bad days, but with a happy heart they enjoy a continual feast.

16 It’s better to have a little with reverence for Yahveh than to have a lot with turmoil attached.

17 A plate of vegetables with someone you love tastes better than a roast from a grain-fed cow with someone you don’t like.

18 Hot-tempered people stir up quarrels, but controlling anger calms contentions.

19 Briers overgrow lazy people’s paths, but the upright travel on an open road.

20 Wise children make a happy father; foolish ones despise their mother.

21 Foolishness is fun for the foolish, but the wise walk on a straight path.

22 Without advice plans go awry, but with many advisors they succeed.

23 Everyone enjoys giving a good answer, and a timely word delights those who hear it!

24 The path of the wise leads upward away from Sheol below.

25 Yahveh tears down the house of the proud but protects the widow’s home.

26 Yahveh detests evil plans but delights in pure words.

27 Those greedy for gain bring trouble home; those who refuse bribes live in peace.

28 The godly think before they speak; sinners blurt out evil without thinking.

29 Yahveh stands far away from the sinful, but he listens to the prayers decent people offer.

30 A cheerful look makes the heart happy; good news refreshes us deep inside.

31 If you listen to constructive correction, you’ll live among the wise.

32 If you ignore instruction, you only hurt yourself; by heeding correction, you gain insight.

33 Respecting Yahveh teaches wisdom; humility comes before honor.
