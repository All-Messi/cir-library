Proverbs Chapter 10

1 Proverbs of Solomon A wise son makes a happy father, but a foolish one grieves his mother.

2 Ill-gotten gain doesn’t gain you anything, but goodness delivers you from death.

3 Yahveh won’t let good people go hungry; he thwarts what the wicked want.

4 A lazy hand makes for poverty; a diligent one produces wealth.

5 They who gather crops in the summer are smart; those who sleep at harvest time are shameful.

6 Blessings come to the good, but the sinful harbor violent intentions.

7 Good people leave blessed memories behind, but sinful people’s names rot away.

8 The wise in heart accept commands, but babbling fools come to ruin.

9 If you live with integrity, you’ll live with confidence, but people soon find out if you’re crooked.

10 The one that winks causes trouble; babbling fools come to ruin.

11 Good people’s words are life-giving fountains; What sinners harbor discord.

12 Hatred stirs up conflict, but love atones for all sins.

13 Wisdom comes from people that have insight; but a rod is for the back of fools.

14 The wise store up knowledge, but ruin is at hand for foolish people.

15 For the rich, wealth is a fortress; For the poor, poverty is their ruin.

16 Life is good people’s pay; punishment is bad people’s payback.

17 Listening to instruction puts you on the route to life; abandoning correction gets you lost.

18 Hiding hatred makes you a liar; spreading slander makes you a fool.

19 Talking too much makes you likely to sin; so be wise and limit what you say.

20 The words of good people are like fine silver; what sinners say isn’t worth repeating.

21 What good people say feeds many; foolish people starve to death from ignorance.

22 Yahveh’s blessing makes you rich, and he doesn’t add sorrow to it.

23 Sin is fun for fools; so is wisdom for the wise.

24 What sinners fear tends happen; what good people want they tend to receive.

25 After a windstorm passes, sinners have been blown away, but the godly have a firm foundation.

26 To those who hire them, lazy people irritate like vinegar on teeth and smoke in the eyes.

27 Respecting Yahveh lengthens your life, sinfulness cuts it short.

28 Good people’s hopes result in happiness, but the expectations of the sinful fade away.

29 Yahveh’s way is your fortress if you’re godly, but it destroys sinners.

30 Good people won’t be displaced, but the sinful will be expelled from their land.

31 The mouth of good people overflows with wisdom, but the perverse tongue of the wicked will be cut off.

32 The good say what’s acceptable, but the sinful say perverse things.
