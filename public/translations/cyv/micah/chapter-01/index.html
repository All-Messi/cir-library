Micah Chapter 1

1 A Message from Yahveh to Micah from Moresheth at the Time of Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah, Kings of Judah. What He Saw About Samaria and Jerusalem Judgment Against Samaria and Jerusalem

2 Listen, every nation; listen, world and everybody in it. Let the Lord Yahveh testify against you from his holy Temple.

3 He’s coming down from his place to walk on the mountaintops.

4 The mountains will mash down under his feet and cascade into the valleys— like wax in fire or water on a slope.

5 That’s because Jacob rebelled and Israel sinned. Where’s Jacob’s rebellion? Isn’t it in Samaria? Where’s Judah’s main high place? Isn’t it in Jerusalem?

6 I’ll make Samaria a pile of ruins in the countryside, a place to plant vineyards. I’ll drag the stones of its walls down to the valley and expose its foundations.

7 I’ll smash its idols and burn everything in it. I’ll destroy its images because they came from a prostitute’s pay; that’s all they’ll be.

8 Because of this, I’ll mourn and wail, and walk around barefoot and naked. I’ll howl like a jackal and moan like an owl.

9 My people’s wound is too deep to heal; it’s penetrated into Judah all the way up to Jerusalem’s gates.

10 Don’t “tell” it in Gath; don’t weep at all; roll in the “dust” at Beth-leaphrah.

11 Just move on into captivity naked and embarrassed, residents of Shaphir; the people in Zaaman don’t dare “come outside” its wall; there’s mourning in Beth-ezel because its people have no back up.

12 Those living in Maroth grow weak waiting for relief, because “bitter” calamity from Yahveh is reaching the gates of Jerusalem.

13 Harness a “team of horses” to a chariot and flee, people in Lachish, where Zion’s sin came from, where Israel’s rebellious acts spread from into Judah.

14 Send farewell gifts to Moresheth-gath; the houses in Achzib have “deceived” the kings in Israel.

15 I’ll bring a “conqueror” on you, people of Mareshah; Israel’s leaders will flee to the cave of Adullam.

16 Shave off your hair in despair for the children you delight in; make yourself as bald as an eagle because they’re going into exile. Judgment Against Wealthy Oppressors
