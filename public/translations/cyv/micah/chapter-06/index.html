Micah Chapter 6

1 Listen to what Yahveh says: “Present to the mountains your case against me; let the hills hear your complaint.

2 But then listen, mountains, to Yahveh’s complaint, you enduring foundations of the world. He has a suit against his people; he’ll bring charges against Israel. 3"What have I done to you, my people? How have I wearied you? Answer me.

4 I brought you out of Egypt and ransomed you from slavery. I sent Moses, Aaron, and Miriam to lead you,

5 My people, remember now the curse that Balak, king of Moab, proposed, the blessing that Balaam Ben-Beor answered with, the laws that led you from Shittim to Gilgal, so you could learn about myY faithfulness."

6 What should I bring to Yahveh? What should I bow with before God on high? Should I come to him with burnt offerings and yearling calves?

7 Does Yahveh delight in thousands of rams, in 10,000 rivers of olive oil? Am I to present my firstborn for my transgression, the fruit of my body for the sin of my soul?

8 No, he’s told you what’s good: what does Yahveh require from you but to do good, love kindness, and walk humbly with him? Israel’s Guilt and Punishment

9 Yahveh will call out to the city— it’s wise to respect him— “Listen, tribe and city council.

10 Can I overlook the wealth gained from cheating that’s in sinners’ houses and the short measure that’s accursed?

11 Can I acquit a businessman that uses dishonest scales and a bag of false weights?

12 The rich in the city extort; its residents lie and deceive.

13 So I’ll send diseases to strike you down, and bring you to ruin for your sins.

14 You’ll eat, but you won’t be satisfied; there’ll be hunger among you. You’ll store up for safe keeping, but I’ll deliver most of it to the sword.

15 You’ll plant, but not harvest; you’ll tread olives, but not anoint yourself with the oil; you’ll tread grapes, but not drink the wine.

16 You’ve followed the statutes of sinful Omri and the example of Ahab; you’ve taken their advice. So I’ll deliver you to destruction and your people to ridicule; you’ll endure the nations’ scorn.” Israel’s Misery Turned to Hope
