1 Samuel Chapter 6

1Yahveh’s Ark was in Philistine territory for seven months. 2They called for the priests and fortunetellers, “What should we do with it? Tell us how to send it back.” 3“Send it back with a guilt offering to Israel’s God. You’ll be healed, and you’ll find out why he keeps working against you.” 4“What guilt offering should we send him?” “Five gold tumors and five gold mice, to match the number of Philistine lords. The same plague has struck you and your lords. 5So make images of your tumors and the mice that are ravaging the land and honor Israel’s God. Maybe he’ll lighten his oppression on you, your gods, and your land. 6Why harden your hearts like Pharaoh and the Egyptians did? After he’d mocked them, didn’t they end up letting the people go? 7Build a new cart and take two milk cows that have never worn a yoke. Yoke them to the cart but take their calves away from them. 8Put the Ark on the cart. In a box beside it put the gold figurines you’re returning to him as a guilt offering. Send it off, and 9watch it. If it goes on the road to Beth-shemesh in its own land, he’s the one that has done us this great harm. If not, we’ll know it happened by chance.”

1 Sam 6:1-9

3Then Samuel told Israel, “If you’re coming back to Yahveh with all your heart, put away the foreign gods and the images of Ashtoreth. Direct your heart to serve him only, and he’ll deliver you from the Philistines.” 4Israel got rid of the images of Baal and Ashtoreth and served Yahveh only.

5Samuel said, “Gather Israel to Mizpah, and I’ll pray to Yahveh for you.” 6They gathered there, drew water, and poured it out to Yahveh. They fasted that day and said, “We’ve sinned against Yahveh.” Samuel judged Israel at Mizpah.

7When the Philistines heard that Israel had gathered at Mizpah, they went up to attack them. The people heard about it and were afraid. 8They told Samuel, “Don’t stop calling out to Yahveh our God for us to save us from the Philistines.” 9He offered up a suckling lamb as a whole burnt offering. He appealed to Yahveh for Israel, and he answered him. 10As he was offering it, the Philistines came to attack; but Yahveh thundered with a loud noise that threw them into a panic. 11Israel chased them and kept striking them down as far as below Beth-car.

1 Sam 7:3-11

10They yoked two milk cows to the cart, shut up their calves at home, 11and put the Ark on the cart along with a box containing the gold mice and the images of their tumors. 12The cows went straight toward Beth-shemesh along one road without veering off, bawling as they went. The lords of the Philistines followed them as far as the outskirts of Beth-shemesh. 13The people there were harvesting wheat in the valley. When they looked up and saw the Ark, they were overjoyed. 14It came and stopped in a field that belonged to Joshua of Beth-shemesh. There was a large rock there. They split up the wood from the cart and offered the cows as a burnt offering to Yahveh. 15Levites took down the Ark and the box and set them on the large rock. The men of Beth-shemesh offered sacrifices and burnt offerings that day. 16The five lords of the Philistines saw it and went back to Ekron.

12Then Samuel set up a “rock” between Mizpah and Shen. He called it Ebenezer because Yahveh had ‘helped’ them that far. 13The Philistines didn’t invade Israel again. Yahveh opposed them Samuel’s whole life. 14The towns they’d taken reverted to Israel from Ekron to Gath. Israel rescued its territory from them. Israel and the Amorites were at peace too.

15Samuel judged Israel his whole life 16on a circuit each year to Bethel, Gilgal, and Mizpah. 17Then he’d come back to his house in Ramah. He administered justice there as well and built an altar to Yahveh.

1 Sam 7:12-17

Israel Demands a King

17As a guilt offering to Yahveh, the Philistines returned one gold tumor each for Ashdod, Gaza, Ashkelon, Gath, and Ekron. 18The gold mice matched the number of fortified towns and unwalled villages that belonged to the five Philistine lords. The large rock they set the Ark by is still a witness in the field of Joshua of Beth-shemesh.
