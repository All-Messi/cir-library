Zephaniah Chapter 1

1A Message from Yahveh to Zephaniah Ben-Cushi (Ben-Gedaliah Ben-Amariah) Ben-Hezekiah at the Time of Josiah Ben-Amon, King of Judah The Coming Judgment on Judah 2“I’ll remove everything from the earth,” Yahveh says, 3“people and animals, birds in the sky, fish in the sea. I’ll destroy sinful people. 4“I’ll reach out against Judah and those who live in Jerusalem. I’ll remove the remnant of Baal from this place and the idolatrous priests,

5the ones that bow down on housetops to the host in the sky, that swear to Yahveh and swear by Milcom too,

6the ones that quit following Yahveh, that haven’t sought him or inquired from him.”

Zeph 1:1-6

The Near Day of Yahveh

7Be silent in front of the Lord Yahveh! His day is near; he’s prepared a sacrifice and consecrated his guests. 8“On the day of myY sacrifice, I’ll punish the officials, the king’s sons in Judah, everyone that dresses in foreign clothes,

9that breaks into houses, and fills their masters’ homes with violence and deceit.

10On that day,” Yahveh says, “there’ll be cries from the Fish Gate, wails from the Second Quarter, loud crashes from the hills.

Zeph 1:7-10

11Wail, people in the marketplace, because your merchants will be wiped out; every tradesman that weighs out silver will be gone.

12I’ll search Jerusalem with lanterns and punish people that are complacent about their sin, who tell themselves that Yahveh won’t do anything to them good or bad.

13Their wealth will become plunder, and their houses demolished. They’ll build houses, but not live in them; they’ll plant vineyards, but not drink their wine.”

Zeph 1:11-13

14That great day of the LORD is near and coming fast, a bitter day: even soldiers will cry out bitterly,

5a day of anger, a day of distress and anguish, destruction and desolation, a day of darkness and gloom, clouds and dense blackness,

6a day of bugle blasts and battle cries against fortified towns and high battle towers

Zeph 1:14-16

17He’ll bring such a distress on you that you’ll stumble around like you’re blind, because you’ve sinned against him. Your blood will pour out and become like dust, and your flesh will be like manure on the ground.

18Your silver and gold won’t save you at the time of Yahveh’s wrath; he’ll consume the whole region with his burning anger and make a complete, terrifying end for people living in the land.

Zeph 1:17-18

A Call for Repentance
