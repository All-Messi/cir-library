Job Chapter 11

1Zophar the Naamathite answered: 2“Should your barrage of words go unchallenged, and you exonerate yourself by talking a lot?

3Will your babble shut people up? Will you mock and nobody rebuke you?

4You claim your beliefs are pure and you’re innocent in God’s presence;

5but I just wish God would speak out against you

6 6and disclose to you the secrets of wisdom, because sound wisdom is not simple; be assured that God doesn’t remember just one part of your sin.

Job 11:1-6

7“Can you find out the deep things of God or probe the limits of the Almighty?

8They’re higher than the sky—what can you do? They’re deeper than Sheol—what can you know?

9They measure longer than the world and wider than the sea.

0If he passes through or imprisons or calls to judgment, who can stop him?

1He knows worthless people; he knows about their sin without having to investigate.

2A stupid man can no more become intelligent than a wild donkey can have a baby.

Job 11:7-12

13“If you set your heart right, you can raise your hands and pray to him.

4If there’s sin in you, get rid of it; don’t let it live in your house.

5Then you can hold your head up, be steadfast and unafraid.

6You can forget your misery and remember it like water gone by.

7Your life will become brighter than noonday; darkness will be like morning.

8Then you’ll have confidence because of hope, be protected, and rest in safety.

9You’ll lie down, and no one will disturb you; others will ask for your blessing.

0But the eyes of sinners will be blinded; they can’t escape; their only hope is death.”

Job 11:13-20
