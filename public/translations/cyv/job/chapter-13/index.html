Job Chapter 13

1“I’ve seen that; I’ve heard it and understand it.

2What you know, I know too; I’m not more ignorant than you.

Job 13:1-2

3“But I’m willing to speak to the Almighty himself; I want to argue with God.

4You men are smearing me with things that aren’t true; you’re untrained doctors.

5Oh that you’d just be quiet and show your wisdom that way!

6Hear me out; listen to what I’m arguing.

7Will you lie to defend God? Will you speak deceitfully on his behalf?

8Will you favor him by how you testify? Will you argue his case for him?

Job 13:3-8

9How will you fare when he examines you? Will you try to deceive him like you deceive people?

0He’ll rebuke you for sure if you secretly skew your testimony to favor him.

1Doesn’t his majesty terrify you, and the dread of him fall on you?

2Your memorable sayings are proverbs made of ashes, defenses made of clay.

Job 13:9-12

13“Be quiet so I can talk; then let come on me what may.

4Why should I bite my body and take my life in my hands?

5Even if he kills me, I’ll trust him; but I’ll still defend myself to him.

6That will also be to my salvation because godless people can’t approach him.

7Listen carefully to what I’m saying; let what I’m claiming fill your minds.

8I’ve prepared my case; I know I’ll be acquitted.

9Will you defeat me in this argument? If so, I’ll shut up and die.

Job 13:13-19

20“Just grant me two things, God; then I can face you:

21don’t remove your hand from me or let my dread of you terrify me.

2Then call and I’ll answer; or let me speak, then reply to me.

3How many iniquities do I have? Tell me what my rebellions are.

4Why do you hide from me and treat me like your enemy?

5Do you make a driven leaf tremble or chase dry chaff?

6You list bitter accusations against me and charge me with sins from my youth.

7You put my feet in shackles and hover over the path as I walk. You set a boundary for where I could go

28while I was decaying like something rotten, like a moth-eaten shirt.

Job 13:20-28
