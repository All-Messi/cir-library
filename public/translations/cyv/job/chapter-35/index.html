Job Chapter 35

1Elihu continued: 2“Do you think it’s righteous for you to say you’re upright before God?

3You say, ‘What advantage will I have? What benefit will I get more than if I’d sinned?’

4I’ll answer you and your friends.

5Look at the sky; the clouds are higher than you are.

6If you’ve sinned, what do you gain against God? if you have lots of sins, what harm are you doing to him?

7If you’re good, what good are you doing for him? What good does he get out of you?

8Your goodness is for other people like you.

Job 35:1-8

9“They cry out because they’re oppressed; they call for help against powerful people.

10But nobody says, ‘Where’s God my Maker, who gives songs at night,

11who teaches us more than the animals on the ground and makes us wiser than the birds in the sky?’

12There bad people call out, but he doesn’t answer because they’re arrogant.

13Indeed, God will ignore empty cries; the Almighty doesn’t pay any attention to them.

14How much less when you say you don’t see him; he has your case, but you’re having to wait for him to give notice!

15Now, since he hasn’t visited in his anger and acknowledged sin enough,

16you open your mouth with empty talk, Job, not knowing what you’re talking about.”

Job 35:9-16
