Job Chapter 15

1Eliphaz the Temanite answered: 2“Should you answer with empty talk and fill yourself with the east wind?

3Should you argue with useless speech, with words that do no good?

4You’re losing reverence for God and hindering meditation in front of him.

5Your sin is telling you what you say, and you’re choosing misleading language.

6You condemn yourself; I don’t; you’re testifying against yourself.

Job 15:1-6

7“Were you the first person ever born? Were you brought forth before the hills?

8Can you hear God’s secret counsel? Do you think you’re the only one that’s wise?

9What do you know that we don’t know? What do you understand that we don’t?

0There are gray-haired, aged men around here that are older than your father.

1Are God’s consolations too small for you or spoken too gently to you?

Job 15:7-11

2Why does your heart carry you away? Why do your eyes flash

13that you turn against God, and say such things?

4Can mortals be pure? Who born of women can be good?

5God doesn’t even trust his angels absolutely or regard the sky as perfectly pure.

16How much less someone disgusting and corrupt that drinks sin like water!

Job 15:12-16

17“Listen, I’ll tell you something; I’ll tell you what I’ve seen—

18 18what the wise have said, and their ancestors haven’t kept to themselves,

19 19people the land was given to and no foreigners passed through—

0the sinful writhe in pain their whole life; the ruthless have a limited number of years.

1They hear terrifying sounds coming; while they’re at peace, destroyers attack them.

2They don’t expect to come back from darkness; they feel destined for the sword.

3They wander around asking for food; they know darkness is on its way.

Job 15:17-23

4Distress and anguish terrify them like a king preparing for battle,

25because they shake their fists at God and defy the Almighty.

6They attack him with abandon, with reinforced shields.

27They’re stout in face and thigh,

28but they’ll live in desolate towns, in houses nobody wants to occupy, homes destined for demolition.

9They won’t get rich, their wealth won’t last; their grain won’t be so heavy it bends over.

0They won’t escape from darkness; the hot sun will wither their roots; the wind will blow away their blossoms.

Job 15:24-30

1They shouldn’t trust in emptiness and deceive themselves; emptiness will be their payback.

2It will be paid in full before their time; their palm fronds won’t turn green again.

3Their unripe grapes will fall from the vine; their olive trees will lose their blossoms.

4The godless live in barren company; fire consumes the tents of those who bribe.

5They conceive schemes and give birth to evil; their mind prepares deceit.”

Job 15:31-35
