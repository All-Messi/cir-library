Job Chapter 27

1Job continued his comments: 2“As God lives, who has taken away my rights, the Almighty, who has made me bitter,

3as long as I’m alive and God’s breath is in me,

4I won’t speak wickedness or lie.

5I’ll never concede that you’re right; I’ll defend my integrity till I die.

6I’ll hold onto my claim about my goodness, and my conscience won’t accuse me for it.

Job 27:1-6

7“May my enemies be like the wicked; may my attackers be like the sinful.

8What hope do the godless have when God cuts them off, when he takes their life away?

9Will he listen to their outcry when they get in trouble?

10Will they be able to delight in the Almighty and call on him at any time?

11Let me teach you about God’s power; I won’t conceal anything about the Almighty.

12You’ve all seen that; so why are you saying such foolish things to me?

Job 27:7-12

13“This is sinful people’s lot from God, the fate tyrants receive from the Almighty:

14their many sons he destines for the sword; their descendants won’t have enough to eat;

15disease buries the ones that outlive them; their widows won’t feel like crying.

16Though sinners pile up silver like dust and make clothes as plentiful as clay,

17the good are the ones that will wear them; the innocent will divide up their silver.

18The house they build is as fragile as a spider’s web or a watchman’s hut.

19They go to bed rich, but only once; when they open their eyes, their wealth is gone.

20Panic overwhelms them like a flood; a tornado carries them off at night.

21The east wind picks them up and sweeps them away;

22it whirls around them without sparing though they try to escape its power.

23People will clap their hands and jeer at them.

Job 27:13-23
