Job Chapter 20

1Zophar the Naamathite answered: 2“My thoughts make me answer because I’m in turmoil inside.

3I’ve listened to your insults, and I’m answering from what I understand.

4Don’t you know from of old— from the time God put people on earth—

5 5that the triumph of sinners is short-lived; the joy of the godless is temporary.

Job 20:1-5

6Though they’re as tall as the sky, though their heads are in the clouds,

7 7they’ll perish forever like their own feces; people that saw them before will say, ‘Where’d they go?’

8They’ll fly away like a dream, and nobody will find them; they’ll vanish like a nightmare.

9People that saw them before won’t see them again; their family won’t have them around anymore.

0Their offspring will have to give back the wealth they’d confiscated and beg from the poor

1They may have youthful health, but it’ll lie down with them in the dust.

Job 20:6-11

12“Though evil is sweet in their mouth and they hide it under their tongue,

13though they like the taste of it and won’t spit it out,

14yet the food in their stomach will ferment into the venom of asps.

5Though they swallow wealth, they’ll vomit it up; God will purge it from their stomach.

6They’ll suck the poison of asps; the viper’s tongue will slay them.

7They won’t admire the streams that flow with honey and curds.

8They’ll lose everything they worked for and couldn’t swallow; they won’t even enjoy the profits from their trading,

19because they’ve oppressed the poor and seized houses they didn’t build.

Job 20:12-19

20“Since their greed is never satisfied, they won’t keep anything they enjoy.

1There won’t be anything left for them to devour; their prosperity won’t last.

2They’ll have so much stuff they can’t move; the full force of their misery will stack up against them.

3While they’re filling their stomach, God will be sending his fierce anger at them and raining it on them.

4They may escape an iron weapon only to have a bronze arrow point run them through.

25It’s thrust will come out of their back; the glittering point will puncture their gall; death-terrors will descend on them.

Job 20:20-25

6Their treasures are destined for total darkness; unfanned flames will consume them; whatever’s left in their tent will burn up.

7Sky will reveal their sin; earth will condemn them.

8Others will carry off what’s in their house at the time when God demonstrates his anger.

9That’s sinful people’s plight, the inheritance God decrees for them.”

Job 20:26-29
