Ruth Chapter 22

1 Then the people in Jerusalem made Ahaziah/Jehoahaz, his youngest son, king. The band of men that came with the Arabs to the camp had killed the older sons. 2Ahaziah was 22 and ruled for 1 year in Jerusalem. His mother was Athaliah, granddaughter of Omri. 3To his destruction, he also followed Ahab’s practices because his mother gave him bad counsel. 4He sinned in 5going with Jehoram Ben-Ahab to fight Hazael, king of Aram, at Ramoth-gilead. But the Arameans wounded Joram/Jehoram. 6He came back to heal in Jezreel from his wounds at Ramah. Ahaziah Ben-Joram/Jehoram, king of Judah, went down to see Joram/Jehoram in Jezreel because he was wounded. 2 Ch 22:1-6

2 David issued an order to gather the foreigners in Israel and set stonecutters to dress stones for God’s Temple. 3He provided large stores of iron for making nails for the doors in the gates and for clamps, more bronze than could be weighed, 4and cedar logs without number. The Sidonians and Tyrians brought him large supplies of cedar timber. 5He said, “My son Solomon is young and inexperienced, and Yahveh’s Temple will be magnificent, famous, and glorious all over the world. I’ll stockpile materials for it."

3 In the eighteenth year of King Josiah, he sent Shaphan Ben-Azaliah Ben-Meshullam, the secretary, to Yahveh’s Temple, 4"Go up to Hilkiah the high priest so he can count the money the people have brought to Yahveh’s Temple that the doorkeepers have gathered. 5Have them deliver it to the workmen that oversee the Temple so they can repair it—6carpenters, builders, and masons—and buy the timber and cut stone for repairs. 7They won’t need to account for the money they receive. They’re honest."

4 I called on him who deserves praise,

5 The waves of death entangled me;

6 Then he called Solomon and charged him to build a temple for Yahveh God of Israel, 7"Son, I intended to build a temple for Yahveh, 8but his message came to me, ‘You won’t build me a temple; you’ve shed so much blood and fought great wars. 9You’ll have a son that’s not a military figure. I’ll give him rest from his enemies everywhere. His name will be Solomon because I’ll give Israel ‘peace’ and quiet during his life. 10He’ll build a temple for me. He’ll be my son, and I’ll be his father. I’ll make his kingdom permanently secure.’ 11Yahveh be with you so you can succeed at building it. 12May he give you with discretion and intelligence, and give you charge over Israel so you can keep his Law. 13You’ll prosper if you observe carefully the statutes and ordinances he commanded Moses. Be strong and courageous; don’t be afraid or intimidated. 14I’ve taken great pains to stockpile materials: 3775 tons 100,000 talents of gold, 37,750 tons 1,000,000 talents of silver, bronze and iron past weighing, timber, and stone. You can add to that. 15You have lots of stonecutters, stone masons, and carpenters skilled in all kinds of work. 16There’s no limit to the gold, silver, bronze, and iron. Get started. Yahveh be with you.” 1 Ch 22:6-16

7 The destruction of Ahaziah was from God for going to Joram/Jehoram. He went out with Jehoram against Jehu Ben-Nimshi that Yahveh had anointed to eliminate Ahab’s family. 8When Jehu was executing judgment on that house, he found the officials of Judah and the sons of Ahaziah’s brothers serving Ahaziah and killed them. 9His men also looked for Ahaziah and caught him hiding in Samaria. They brought him to Jehu, put him to death, and buried him; because they said, “He is the son of Jehoshaphat, who sought Yahveh with all his heart.” There wasn’t anybody from Ahaziah’s family capable of ruling the kingdom.

8 Then Hilkiah the priest told Shaphan the secretary, “I’ve found the Book of the Law in the Temple.” He gave it to him to read, 9and Shaphan brought word to the king, “Your officials have emptied out the money they found in the Temple and delivered it to the workmen that oversee the Temple. 10Hilkiah has also given me a scroll.” He read it to the king. 11When he heard it, he tore his clothes. 12He told Hilkiah, Ahikam Ben-Shaphan, Achbor Ben-Micaiah, Shaphan the secretary, and Asaiah the king’s adviser, 13"Go ask Yahveh for me, for the people, and for Judah about what this scroll says. Yahveh’s great anger is burning against us. Our ancestors ignored what it says and haven’t done what’s written for us to do."

9 Smoke poured out of his nostrils;

10 When his mother Athaliah saw he was dead, she killed the royal offspring in Judah. 11But Jehoshabeath, King Joram/Jehoram’s daughter, stole Joash Ben-Ahaziah from among the king’s sons—who were being murdered—and placed him and his nurse in a bedroom. Jehoshabeath, wife of Jehoiada the priest and Ahaziah’s sister, hid him so Athaliah couldn’t kill him. 12They hid him in God’s Temple for 6 years while Athaliah ruled the country. 2 Ch 22:7-12

11 He rode on a cherub

12 He made blackness a canopy around him,

13 The messenger to Micaiah told him, “The prophets agree in giving a favorable report to the king. Make your report like theirs."

14 They went to Huldah the prophetess, wife of Shallum Ben-Tikvah Ben-Harhas, keeper of the wardrobe. (She lived in Jerusalem’s Second Quarter.) 15She told them, “Yahveh God of Israel says to tell the man that sent you, 16’I’m going to bring disaster on this place and its people like the scroll says. 17They’ve left me and burned incense to other gods. My anger burns against this place, and it won’t be put out. [18] 19But I’ve listened to you because you became sensitive and humbled yourself when you heard about the desolation I’ve cursed this place and its people with; you tore your clothes and cried. 20I’ll let you pass on to your ancestors in peace. You won’t see the disaster I’m going to bring here.”’"

15 When he came, the king said, “Micaiah, should we attack Ramoth-gilead?"

16 The king said, “How many times do I have to make you swear not to tell me anything but the truth in Yahveh’s name?"

17 He directed Israel’s leaders to help Solomon, 18"Isn’t Yahveh with you? Hasn’t he given you rest in every direction? He’s delivered to you the nations in the land, and it’s under control. 19Set your heart and soul to look to him. Build his sanctuary so you can bring the Ark and God’s holy articles into it.” 1 Ch 22:17-19

18 The king told Jehoshaphat, “Didn’t I tell you he wouldn’t prophesy anything good about me?"

19 Micaiah said, “Listen to Yahveh’s message. I saw himY on his throne and the host in heaven standing on his left and right. 20He said, ‘Who will lure Ahab to go up and fall at Ramoth-gilead?’ One said this, another said that. 21Then a spirit came forward, ‘I’ll lure him.’

20 He brought me out into a safe place

23 Micaiah said, “Yahveh has put a deceiving spirit in your prophets. He’s declared disaster for you.” 1 Kg 22:18-23

24 Zedekiah came up and hit him on the cheek, “How did Yahveh’s Spirit pass from me to speak to you?"

25 So Yahveh has rewarded me according to my goodness—

26 The king said, “Take Micaiah to Amon, governor of the city, and to Joash my son. 27Tell them the king says to put this man in prison and feed him sparingly with bread and water till I come back safely.’"

28 Micaiah said, “If you ‘come back safely,’ Yahveh hasn’t spoken by me. Listen to that, all of you."

29 Ahab and Jehoshaphat went up against Ramoth-gilead.

30 Ahab told Jehoshaphat, “I’ll disguise myself and go into the battle, but you wear your royal robes.” So the king of Israel disguised himself and went into battle. 31The king of Aram had told the 32 captains of his chariots, “Don’t fight anybody great or small except the king of Israel.” 32When the captains saw Jehoshaphat in his royal robes, they said, “That has to be the king of Israel,” and they turned aside to attack him, and he cried out. 33When they saw it wasn’t the king of Israel, they turned back. 1 Kg 22:24-33

31 God’s way is perfect;

34 An Aramean archer drew his bow at random and hit the king of Israel in a joint in the armor. He told the chariot driver, “Turn around and get me out of the fight. I’m seriously wounded.” 35The battle raged that day; the king was propped up in his chariot in sight of the Arameans and died that evening. The blood from the wound ran down onto the bottom of the chariot. 36Then a shout went through the army just before sundown, “Every man to his city and country."

37 The king died; they took him to Samaria and buried him. 38They washed the chariot by the pool of Samaria. The dogs licked up his blood (where the prostitutes took baths) like Yahveh had said. 39The rest of the acts of Ahab, the ivory palace he built, the towns he built are recorded in The Chronicles of the Kings of Israel. 40His son Ahaziah became king. 1 Kg 22:34-40

38 I chased my enemies and overtook them;

39 I thrust them through so they couldn’t get up again.

41 Jehoshaphat Ben-Asa became king in Judah in the fourth year of Ahab, king of Israel. 42He was 35 and ruled for 25 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Azubah Bat-Shilhi. 43He followed the ways of Asa in doing what was right, but he didn’t take away the high places. People sacrificed and burned incense there. 44He also made peace with the king of Israel.

42 They looked around for help, but there wasn’t any;

43 I pulverized them like dust,

45 The rest of the acts of Jehoshaphat, the strength he showed in his rule, the wars he fought, are recorded in The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah. 46He expelled from the land the remnant of shrine sodomites from his father’s reign. 47There wasn’t any king in Edom; a deputy served as king. 48Jehoshaphat built ships from Tarshish to go to Ophir for gold, but they didn’t make it; they wrecked at Ezion-geber. 49Then Ahab’s son Ahaziah said to Jehoshaphat, “Let my men go with yours in the ships.” But Jehoshaphat wouldn’t let them. 50He died and they buried him in The City of David. His son Jehoram became king. 1 Kg 22:41-50 51Ahaziah became king of Israel in Samaria in the seventeenth year of Jehoshaphat, king of Judah, and ruled for 2 years. 52He did evil like his parents and JeroboamI that got Israel to sin. 53He served and worshiped Baal and made Yahveh God of Israel angry like his father had done. 1 Kg 22:51-53

50 So I’ll thank you among the Gentiles, Yahveh, 51You’re a tower of victory for your king
