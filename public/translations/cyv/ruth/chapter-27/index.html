Ruth Chapter 27

1 Jotham was 25 and ruled for 16 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Jerushah Bat-Zadok. 2He did what was good like his father, but he didn’t go into the Temple. The people kept acting corruptly. 3He built the upper gate of Yahveh’s Temple and built extensively the wall of Ophel. 4He built towns in the hill country of Judah, and fortresses and towers on the wooded hills. 5He fought the Ammonites and prevailed; for three years they paid him 7550 lbs 100 talents of silver, 110,000 bushels 10,000 kors of wheat, and 110,000 bushels 10,000 kors of barley a year. The Ammonites also paid that amount in the second and third years. 6He became powerful because he ordered his ways in Yahveh his God. 7The rest of the acts of Jotham, including his wars, are recorded in The Book of the Kings of Israel and Judah. [8] 9He died, and they buried him in The City of David. His son Ahaz became king. 2 Ch 27:1-9

2 Jashobeam Ben-Zabdiel was responsible for the first division for the first month. 3He descended from Perez and was chief of the commanders of the army for the first month.

4 Dodai the Ahohite and his division were responsible for the second month with Mikloth as chief officer.

5 The third commander of the army for the third month was Benaiah Ben-Jehoiada the priest, as chief. 6This Benaiah was the most elite soldier of the 30 and responsible for 30. His son Ammizabad was responsible for his division. 1 Ch 27:1-6

7 The fourth for the fourth month was Joab’s brother Asahel and his son Zebadiah.

8 The fifth for the fifth month was commander Shamhuth the Izrahite.

9 The six for the sixth month was Ira Ben-Ikkesh the Tekoite.

10 The seventh for the seventh month was Helez the Pelonite, descendants of Ephraim.

11 The eighth for the eighth month was Sibbecai the Hushathite from the Zerahites.

12 The ninth for the ninth month was Abiezer the Anathothite from the Benjamites.

13 The tenth for the tenth month was Maharai the Netophathite from the Zerahites.

14 The eleventh for the eleventh month was Benaiah the Pirathonite, descendant of Ephraim.

15 The twelfth for the twelfth month was Heldai the Netophathite of Othniel. 1 Ch 27:7-15

16 In charge of the tribes of Israel

23 But David didn’t count the ones under 20 because Yahveh had said he would multiply Israel like the stars in the sky. 24Joab Ben-Zeruiah had started to count them but didn’t finish. Wrath came on Israel because of it, and the number wasn’t included in The Chronicles of King David.

25 Azmaveth Ben-Adiel oversaw the king’s storehouses. Jonathan Ben-Uzziah oversaw the storehouses in the country, towns, villages, and

26 Ezri Ben-Chelub oversaw the farmers that plowed the ground.

27 Shimei the Ramathite oversaw vineyards.

28 Baal-hanan the Gederite oversaw the olive and sycamore trees in the western foothills.

29 Shitrai the Sharonite oversaw the cattle that grazed in Sharon.

30 Obil the Ishmaelite oversaw the camels.

31 Jaziz the Hagrite oversaw the flocks.

32 Jonathan, David’s uncle, was an intelligent advisor and secretary.

33 Ahithophel was advisor to the king.

34 Jehoiada Ben-Benaiah and Abiathar succeededpl Ahithophel.
