Ruth Chapter 23

1 In the seventh year, Jehoiada summoned the courage to make a pact with the commanders of hundreds: Azariah Ben-Joram/Jeroham, Ishmael Ben-Johanan, Azariah Ben-Obed, Maaseiah Ben-Adaiah, and Elishaphat Ben-Zichri. 2They gathered to Jerusalem the Levites from the towns in Judah and the heads of families. 3When the assembly made a pact with the king in God’s Temple, Jehoiada told them, “The king’s son will reign like Yahveh has said regarding David’s descendants. 4A third of you, priests and Levites that come in on the Sabbath, will be gatekeepers. 5A third will be at the king’s palace, and a third at the Foundation Gate. The people will be in the courts of theY Temple. 6Don’t let anybody go into theY Temple except the priests and servicing Levites. They can go in because they’re holy. Have the people keep the charge of Yahveh. 7The Levites will surround the king with weapons in hand. Kill whoever comes into the Temple. Be with the king when he comes in and goes out."

2 Kg 23:19-23

3 The God of Israel said,

4 Then he told Hilkiah the high priest, the priests of the second order, and the doorkeepers to remove from the Temple the items made for Baal, Asherah, and the host of heaven. He burned them outside Jerusalem in the fields by the Kidron and carried their ashes to Bethel.

5 He did away with the idolatrous priests Judah’s kings had appointed to burn incense in the high places in the towns of Judah and around Jerusalem, those who burned incense to Baal, the sun, moon, and constellations—the host of heaven.

6 He removed the Asherah pole from Yahveh’s Temple to the Kidron and burned it. He ground it to powder and threw it on the graves of the common people.

7 The sons of Gershon were Ladan and Shimei.

8 The Levites and Judah did what he said. Each one brought his men that were to come in on the Sabbath with the ones that were to go out on the Sabbath, because Jehoiada didn’t dismiss any of the divisions. 9Jehoiada gave the commanders of hundreds King David’s spears and large and small shields that were in the Temple. 10He stationed the men around the king from the south side of the Temple to the north, near the altar. 11Then they brought out the king’s son, put the crown on him, gave him the Testimony, and declared him king. Jehoiada and his sons anointed him and said, “Long live the king!” 2 Ch 23:1-11

9 Three sons of Shimei were Shelomoth, Haziel, and Haran. 10-11The sons of Shimei were Jahath, first; Zina/Zizah, second. Jeush and Beriah didn’t have very many sons, so they were all counted as one family. 1 Ch 23:7-11

10 He defiled Topheth in the Valley of Hinnom so nobody could burn a son or daughter as an offering to Molech.

11 He removed the horses Judah’s kings had dedicated to the sun—at the entrance of Yahveh’s Temple, by the quarters of Nathan-melech the official, which were in the precincts.

12 When Athaliah heard people running and praising the king, she came into theY Temple. 13She saw the king standing by his pillar at the entrance, and the commanders and trumpeters beside him. The common people were celebrating and blowing trumpets. The singers with their musical instruments were leading the praise. She tore her clothes, “Treason! Treason!” 14Jehoiada brought out the commanders over the army and told them, “Bring her out between the ranks and put to death with a sword anybody that tries to rescue her.” The priests said, “Don’t do it in theY Temple.” 15They seized her, and when she got to the entrance of the Horse Gate of the king’s palace, they put her to death. 2 Ch 23:12-15

13 The sons of Amram were Aaron and Moses.

14 The sons of Moses, the man of God, were included in the tribe of Levi.

15 The sons of Moses were Gershom and Eliezer.

16 Jehoiada made a covenant with the people and the king that they’d be Yahveh’s people. 17The people tore down Baal’s temple, broke his altars and images into pieces, and killed Mattan his priest by the altars. 18He placed the officers of theY Temple under the Levitical priests that David had assigned over theY Temple, as written in the Law of Moses—with celebration and singing according to David’s order. 19He stationed gatekeepers for theY Temple so nobody ceremonially unclean could go in. 20He took the commanders of hundreds, the nobles, rulers of the people, and common people, and brought the king down from theY Temple through the upper gate to the king’s palace and placed him on the throne. 21So the people celebrated, and the city grew quiet because they’d put Athaliah to death with a sword. 2 Ch 23:16-21

17 The only son of Eliezer was Rehabiah.

18 The son of Izhar was Shelomith the chief.

19 The sons of Hebron were Jeriah, first; then Amariah, then Jahaziel, then Jekameam.

20 The sons of Uzziel were Micah, first, then Isshiah.

21 The sons of Merari were Mahli and Mushi.

22 Eleazar died and had only daughters, so their cousins, Kish’s sons, married them.

23 The sons of Mushi were Mahli, Eder, and Jeremoth. 1 Ch 23:12-23

24 Those were Levi’s descendants by heads of families as counted in their census. They served in Yahveh’s Temple from 20 years old and up. 25David said, “Yahveh God of Israel has given his people rest, and he lives in Jerusalem permanently. 26The Levites won’t need to carry the Tabernacle and the utensils for its service.” 27By David’s last words, Levi’s descendants were counted from 20 years old and up. 28They were to assist Aaron’s descendants with the service in the courts and rooms in Yahveh’s Temple. They were to purify the holy things 29and help with the showbread, the fine flour for grain offerings, wafers without yeast, what’s baked in the pan, or what’s well-mixed, and all measures of volume and size. 30They were to stand each morning and evening to thank and praise Yahveh. 31They offered burnt offerings on Sabbaths, new moons, and scheduled festivals in the number set by the Law. 32They maintained the Tent of Meeting and the Holy Place and helped Aaron’s descendants with the service in Yahveh’s house. 1 Ch 23:24-32

25 Shammah and Elika from Harod,

26 But Yahveh didn’t withdraw his fierce anger against Judah that Manasseh had provoked, 27"I’ll remove Judah from me like I did Israel. I’ll throw Jerusalem aside, my chosen city, and the Temple that I said I’d identify with."

27 Abiezer from Anathoth,

28 The rest of the acts of Josiah are recorded in The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah. 29During his reign, Pharaoh Neco, king of Egypt, went up to the king of Assyria to the River Euphrates. King Josiah went to engage him. When Pharaoh saw him, he killed him at Megiddo. 30His officers hauled his body in a chariot from Megiddo to Jerusalem and buried him in his own tomb. The people took his son Jehoahaz and anointed him king. 2 Kg 23:24-30

30 Benaiah from Pirathon,

31 Jehoahaz was 23 and ruled for 3 months in Jerusalem. His mother was Hamutal Bat-Jeremiah of Libnah. 32He did evil like his ancestors. 33Pharaoh Neco imprisoned him at Riblah in Hamath so he couldn’t reign in Jerusalem. He imposed on the country a fine of 3¾ tons 100 talents of silver and 75½ lbs a talent of gold. 34He made Eliakim Ben-Josiah king in place of Josiah and changed his name to Jehoiakim. He took Jehoahaz away to Egypt, and he died there. 35Jehoiakim gave Pharaoh the silver and gold, but he taxed the land to do it. He exacted the silver and gold according to his evaluation of their wealth.

32 Eliahba from Shaalbon,

34 Eliphelet Ben-Ahasbai from Maacah,

35 Hezro from Carmel,

36 Jehoiakim was 25 when he became king and ruled for 11 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Zebidah Bat-Pedaiah from Rumah. 37He did evil like his predecessors. 2 Kg 23:31-37

37 Zelek from Ammon,

38 Ira and Gareb the Jattir,

39 Uriah the Hittite—37 in all.
