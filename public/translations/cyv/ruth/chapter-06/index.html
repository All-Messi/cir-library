Ruth Chapter 6

1 Then Solomon said,

2 Ch 6:36-42

3 Then he faced the people and blessed the assembly standing there, 4"Bless Yahveh God of Israel, who said to my father David, 5’From the time I brought my people out of Egypt, I hadn’t chosen a city to build a temple in for my name. I hadn’t chosen any man as a leader over Israel. 6But I’ve chosen Jerusalem as the place to identify with and David as leader over Israel. 7My father wanted to build him a temple, 8but Yahveh said, ‘You meant well, 9but you won’t build me a temple; your son will.’ 10He has fulfilled that promise; I’ve risen to the throne and built the temple for his name. 11That’s where I’ve set the Ark that holds the covenant he made with Israel." 12-13Solomon had built a bronze platform 7½ x 7½ x 4½ft high beside the altar in the middle of the court. He stood on it, knelt, and spread out his hands toward heaven. 2 Ch 6:1-13 14"Yahveh God of Israel, there’s no God like you in heaven or on earth. You keep covenant and show enduring love to everyone that follows you with all their heart. 15You’ve kept your promise to David. You’ve said it with your mouth and fulfilled it with your hand. 16KeepY this promise too, ‘You’ll always have a descendant on Israel’s throne if they follow my Law like you have.’ 17Confirm that promise now. 18"But will you live with people on earth? The sky and highest heaven can’t contain you, much less this Temple I’ve built. 19Yet attend to my petition. Listen to my call 20so you’ll be looking toward this Temple day and night where you’ve said you’d put your name. 21Listen when your people and I pray toward this place. Listen from heaven and forgive. 22"When someone sins against another and comes here to take an oath at your altar, 23then listen from heaven and judge them. Punish the sinful by bringing their way on their own head and justify the good by rewarding them according to their goodness. 2 Ch 6:14-23 24"When an enemy defeats your people because they’ve sinned, if they repent, confess your name, and petition you in this Temple, 25then listen from heaven, forgive them, and bring them back to the country you’ve given them and their ancestors. 26"When there’s no rain because they’ve sinned against you and they pray toward this place, confess your name, and turn from their sin when you punish them, 27then listen from heaven and forgive your people. Teach them the good way to live and send rain on the land you’ve given them to inherit. 2 Ch 6:24-27 28"When there’s a famine in the country; disease, blight or mildew; locust or grasshopper; a sickness, or an enemy laying siege to our towns—29whatever petition your people make, each knowing their own suffering and pain and spreading their hands toward this Temple, 30then listen from where you live, forgive, and render to each as they’ve done—only you know everyone’s heart—31so they’ll respect you and follow your ways as long as they live in the country you’ve given their ancestors. 32"When foreigners come from a distant country because of your great name, your powerful hand and outstretched arm, and pray toward this Temple, 33then listen from where you live, and do everything they call to you for, so every nation will know you and respect you like your people do, and so they’ll know that the Temple I’ve built wears your name. 34"When your people go out to battle by whatever way you send them and they pray to you toward this city you’ve chosen and the Temple I’ve built, 35then listen from heaven and support them. 2 Ch 6:28-35 36"When they sin against you (everybody sins), you’re angry with them, and deliver them to an enemy that takes them captive near or far, 37if they take it to heart, repent, and petition you, ‘We’ve sinned, we’ve committed iniquity, we’ve acted wickedly’; 38if they come back to you with all their heart and soul, and pray toward the land you’ve given their ancestors, the city you’ve chosen, the Temple I’ve built, 39then listen from where you live, support them, and forgive them. 40"Open your eyes and ears to answer the prayer I’ve offered here. 41Rise, Yahveh God, to your resting place, you and your powerful Ark. Cover your priests with salvation; may your holy people celebrate what’s good. 42Don’t turn away from your anointed one. Remember your enduring love for your servant David."

4 Eleazar was father of Phinehas Avi-5Abishua Avi-Bukki Avi-6Uzzi Avi-Zerahiah Avi-7Meraioth Avi-Amariah Avi-8Ahitub Avi-Zadok Avi-9Ahimaaz Avi-Azariah Avi-10Johanan Avi-Azariah (priest in the Temple that Solomon built in Jerusalem), and 11Azariah was father of Amariah Avi-12Ahitub Avi-Zadok Avi-13Shallum Avi-Hilkiah Avi-14Azariah Avi-Seraiah Avi-15Jehozadak, who went into exile when Yahveh took Judah and Jerusalem away by Nebuchadnezzar. 1 Ch 6:1-15

5 But as one of them was cutting down a tree for a beam, the axe head fell in the water. He cried out, “Master! I borrowed it!"

6 The man of God said, “Where did it fall?” When he showed him, he cut off a stick and threw it in there and made the iron float.

7 He said, “Pick it up.” He reached out and took it. 2 Kg 6:1-7

8 The king of Aram was at war with Israel. He advised his officers, “I’m going to camp in such and such a place.” 9The man of God sent a message to the king of Israel, “Don’t go near that place; the Arameans are coming down there.” 10The king of Israel sent to where the man of God told him. He warned him more than once, so he guarded himself there.

10 They yoked two milk cows to the cart, shut up their calves at home, 11and put the Ark on the cart along with a box containing the gold mice and the images of their tumors. 12The cows went straight toward Beth-shemesh along one road without veering off, bawling as they went. The lords of the Philistines followed them as far as the outskirts of Beth-shemesh. 13The people there were harvesting wheat in the valley. When they looked up and saw the Ark, they were overjoyed. 14It came and stopped in a field that belonged to Joshua of Beth-shemesh. There was a large rock there. They split up the wood from the cart and offered the cows as a burnt offering to Yahveh. 15Levites took down the Ark and the box and set them on the large rock. The men of Beth-shemesh offered sacrifices and burnt offerings that day. 16The five lords of the Philistines saw it and went back to Ekron.

11 The king of Aram was mad and called his officers, “Who’s been an informant for the king of Israel?"

12 One of the officers said, “No, my lord the king! Elisha the prophet in Israel tells him what you say in your bedroom."

14 The king of Aram sent horses, chariots, and a big army and surrounded the city at night.

15 When the man of God’s attendant got up early and went out, an army was circling the city with horses and chariots. He said, “Master! What are we going to do?” Yahveh let him see that the mountains all around were full of horses and flaming chariots. 18When they came down to him, Elisha prayed, “Strike them with blindness.” He did. 19Elisha told them, “This is not the way, and this is not the city. Follow me and I’ll take you to the man you’re looking for.” He took them to Samaria.

16 The sons of Levi were Gershom, Kohath, and Merari.

17 The sons of Gershom were Libni/Ladan and Shimei.

18 The sons of Kohath were Amram, Izhar, Hebron, and Uzziel.

19 The sons of Merari were Mahli and Mushi.

20 The sons of Gershom were Libni Avi-Jahath Avi-Zimmah Avi-21Joah Avi-Iddo Avi-Zerah Avi-Jeatherai.

21 He told her, “I celebrated in front of Yahveh, who chose me over your father and his family, and appointed me to rule his people. 22I’ll be even less dignified than this and humble in my own eyes, but I’ll be respected by the servant girls you’ve mentioned."

22 The sons of Kohath were Amminadab Avi-Korah Avi-Assir Avi-23Elkanah Avi-Ebiasaph Avi-Assir Avi-24Tahath Avi-Uriel Avi-Uzziah Avi-Shaul.

23 He prepared a big banquet for them. When they’d eaten, he sent them away. Marauding bands from Aram never came into Israel again. 2 Kg 6:15-23

24 After that, Benhadad, king of Aram, gathered his army, went up, and laid siege to Samaria. 25There was a severe famine in Samaria, and they laid siege to it till a donkey’s head sold for 2 lbs 80 shekels of silver and a pint ¼ cab of pigeon manure sold for 2 oz 5 shekels of silver.

25 The sons of Elkanah were Amasai and Ahimoth, 26The sons of Elkanah were Zophai Avi-Nahath Avi-27Eliab Avi-Jeroham Avi-Elkanah.

26 As the king was passing by on the wall, a woman cried out to him, “Help, my lord the king.” 27He said, “If Yahveh doesn’t help you, where will I help you from? From the threshing floor or the winepress? 28What’s the matter with you?” “This woman said to me, ‘Give your son so we can eat him today, and we’ll eat my son tomorrow.’ 29We boiled my son and ate him, and I told her the next day, ‘Give your son so we can eat him’; but she has hidden her son."

28 The sons of Samuel were Joel his firstborn and Ahijah second.

29 The sons of Merari were Mahli Avi-Libni Avi-Shimei Avi-Uzzah Avi-30Shimea Avi-Haggiah Avi-Asaiah. 1 Ch 6:16-30

30 When the king heard it, he tore his clothes—he was passing by on the wall—and the people looked, and he had burlap under his body. 31Then he said, “God do so to me and more if Elisha’s head remains on him today.” 2 Kg 6:24-31

31 These are the ones David appointed to sing in Yahveh’s Tabernacle after the Ark rested there. 32They served in song at the Tent of Meeting till Solomon built Yahveh’s Temple in Jerusalem. They served in their order.

32 Elisha and the elders were sitting in his house. The king sent a man; but before he got there, Elisha told the elders, “Do you see how this murderer’s son has sent to take away my head? When the messenger comes, shut the door and hold it shut against him. Isn’t the sound of his master’s feet behind him?"

33 These are the ones with their sons that served. From the Kohathites were Heman the singer son of Joel Ben-Samuel (34Ben-Elkanah Ben-Jeroham Ben-Eliel Ben-Toah 35Ben-Zuph Ben-Elkanah Ben-Mahath Ben-Amasai 36Ben-Elkanah Ben-Joel Ben-Azariah Ben-Zephaniah 37Ben-Tahath Ben-Assir Ben-Ebiasaph Ben-Korah 38Ben-Izhar Ben-Kohath) Ben-Levi Ben-Israel.

37 In the fourth year, the month Ziv, the foundation of Yahveh’s Temple was in place. 38In the eleventh year, eighth month (Bul), it was finished as designed. It took 7 years to build.

39 Heman’s brother Asaph stood on his right, that is, Asaph Ben-Berechiah (Ben-Shimea 40Ben-Michael Ben-Baaseiah Ben-Malchijah 41Ben-Ethni Ben-Zerah Ben-Adaiah 42Ben-Ethan Ben-Zimmah Ben-Shimei 43Ben-Jahath Ben-Gershom) Ben-Levi.

44 On the left were their relatives the sons of Merari: Ethan Ben-Kishi (Ben-Abdi Ben-Malluch 45Ben-Hashabiah Ben-Amaziah Ben-Hilkiah 46Ben-Amzi Ben-Bani Ben-Shemer 47Ben-Mahli Ben-Mushi Ben-Merari) Ben-Levi.

48 Their relatives the Levites were appointed for the service of the Tabernacle, God’s house. I Ch 6:31-48

49 Aaron and his sons offered on the altar of burnt offering and the altar of incense for the work of the Holy of Holies, and atoned for Israel like Moses had commanded.

50 These are the sons of Aaron: Eleazar Avi-Phinehas Avi-Abishua Avi-51Bukki Avi-Uzzi Avi-Zerahiah Avi-52Meraioth Avi-Amariah Avi-Ahitub Avi-53Zadok Avi-Ahimaaz. 54These are their settlements according to their camps within their borders. To the descendants of Aaron of the clans of the Kohathites (Theirs was the first lot.), 55they gave Hebron in Judah with its pastures around it. 56But the fields around the town and its villages they gave to Caleb Ben-Jephunneh. 57To Aaron’s descendants they gave the following cities of refuge: Hebron, Libnah with its pastures, Jattir, Eshtemoa with its pastures, 58Hilen with its pastures, Debir with its pastures, 59Ashan with its pastures, and Beth-shemesh with its pastures. 60From Benjamin they gave Geba with its pastures, Allemeth with its pastures, and Anathoth with its pastures—13 towns. 1 Ch 6:49-60 61To the rest of Kohath’s descendants, they gave 10 towns by lot from the Manasseh half-tribe. 62To Gershom’s descendants according to their families, they gave 13 towns in Bashan from the tribes of Issachar, Asher, Naphtali, and Manasseh. 63To Merari’s descendants according to their families, they gave by lot 12 towns from the tribes of Reuben, Gad, and Zebulun. 64Israel’s descendants gave the Levites the towns with their pastures. 65From the tribes of Judah, Simeon, and Benjamin, they gave the towns by lot. 66Some families of Kohath’s descendants had towns from the tribe of Ephraim. 67They gave them the following cities of refuge with their pastures: Shechem in the hill country of Ephraim, Gezer, 68Jokmeam, Beth-horon, 69Aijalon, and Gath-rimmon. 70From the Manasseh half-tribe they gave towns with their pastures: Aner and Bileam for the rest of the descendants of Kohath. 1 Ch 6:61-70 71To Gershom’s descendants they gave towns with their pastures from the family of the Manasseh half-tribe: Golan in Bashan and Ashtaroth. 72From the tribe of Issachar they gave towns with their pastures: Kedesh, Daberath, 73Ramoth, Anem. 74From Asher they gave towns with their pastures: Mashal, Abdon, 75Hukok, and Rehob. 76From Naphtali they gave towns with their pastures: Kedesh in Galilee, Hammon, and Kiriathaim. 77To the rest of the Levites, the sons of Merari, from the tribe of Zebulun they gave towns with their pastures: Rimmono and Tabor. 78On the east side of the Jordan, from Reuben they gave towns with their pastures: Bezer in the open country, Jahzah, 79Kedemoth, and Mephaath. 80From Gad they gave towns with their pastures: Ramoth in Gilead, Mahanaim, 81Heshbon, and Jazer. 1 Ch 6:71-81
