Ruth Chapter 8

1 At the end of the 20 years it took Solomon to build Yahveh’s Temple and his own palace, 2he rebuilt the towns Hiram had given him and settled Israelites in them. 3Then he captured Hamath-zobah 4and built Tadmor in the open country and storage cities in Hamath. 5He built Upper and Lower Beth-horon, fortified towns with walls, gates, and bars. 6He built Baalath, the storage cities he had, towns for his chariots and cavalry, and everything he delighted to build in Jerusalem, Lebanon, and the rest of the territory he ruled.

2 He defeated Moab and counted them off in lines and made them lie down on the ground. Two lines he counted off to be put to death, and one line he let live. The Moabites served him and brought tribute.

3 Bela was father of Addar, Gera, Abihud, 4Abishua, Naaman, Ahoah, 5Gera, Shephuphan, and Huram.

4 Israel’s elders gathered to Samuel at Ramah, 5"You’re getting old, and your sons don’t follow your ways. Appoint a king to govern us like the other nations. 6That displeased Samuel. He prayed to Yahveh, 7and Yahveh said, “Listen to them. They’re not rejecting you as much as rejecting me from being their king. 8As they’ve done ever since I brought them out of Egypt—leaving me and serving other gods—so they’re doing to you. 9Give in to them but warn them solemnly how kings will rule."

5 When the Arameans from Damascus came to help Hadadezer, David killed 22,000 of them 6and put garrisons around Damascus, the Aramean capital. The Arameans served him and paid tribute money. 7The gold shields that Hadadezer’s officers carried, he took to Jerusalem. 8He took a large store of bronze from Betah and Berothai, towns that belonged to Hadadezer.

6 These are the sons of Ehud, heads of families among the people in Geba. They were exiled to Manahath: 7Naaman, Ahijah, and Gera Avi-Uzza and Ahihud.

7 The people left over from the Hittites, Amorites, Perizzites, Hivites, and Jebusites, who weren’t Israelites 8the Israelites hadn’t destroyed, Solomon has made forced laborers ever since. 9He didn’t make slaves out of Israelites for his work. They were soldiers, chief captains, and commanders of his chariots and cavalry. 10Solomon had 250 chief officers that oversaw the people. 11Then he brought Pharaoh’s daughter up from The City of David to the house he’d built for her, because he said, “My wife won’t live in David’s palace, because the places are holy where Yahveh’s Ark has been.” 2 Ch 8:1-11

8 Shaharaim was the father of children in Moab after he’d sent away his wives Hushim and Baara. 9By his wife Hodesh he became father of Jobab, Zibia, Mesha, Malcam, 10Jeuz, Sachia, Mirmah, sons and heads of families. 11By Hushim he’d become father of Abitub and Elpaal.

9 Toi/Tou, king of Hamath, heard that David had defeated Hadadezer’s whole army. 10He sent his son Joram to greet him and congratulate him. Hadadezer had fought several wars with Toi/Tou. Joram brought articles made out of silver, gold, and bronze. 11David dedicated them to Yahveh together with the silver and gold from the nations he subdued: 12Aram, Moab, the Ammonites, the Philistines, Amalek, along with the spoils from Hadadezer.

10 Elisha said, “He’ll recover, but Yahveh has shown me that he’ll certainly die.” 11He stared at him till he was embarrassed, and the man of God cried.

12 Then Solomon offered burnt offerings on the altar he’d built in front of the vestibule. 13He followed the daily rule, offering them according to the commandment of Moses for Sabbaths, new moons, and the three annual festivals: The Festival of Unleavened Bread, The Festival of Weeks, and The Festival of Booths.

13 I’ve built you a glorious temple, a permanent place to live."

14 He followed David’s ordinance and appointed the priestly divisions for their service: the Levites for their duties of praise and serving in the presence of the priests by the daily rule; the gatekeepers by their divisions at every gate. 15They kept his commandments to the priests and Levites about the treasuries.

15 Zebadiah, Arad, Eder, 16Michael, Ishpah, and Joha were sons of Beriah.

16 Solomon carried out his work from the day he laid the foundation of Yahveh’s Temple till he finished it. 17Then he went to Ezion-geber and Eloth on the shore in Edom. 18By his own officers, Hiram sent him ships and sailors that knew the sea. They went with Solomon’s servants to Ophir and brought back 17 tons 450 talents of gold. 2 Ch 8:12-18

17 Zebadiah, Meshullam, Hizki, Heber, 18Ishmerai, Izliah, and Jobab were sons of Elpaal.

18 Benaiah Ben-Jehoiada was captain of David’s bodyguard;

19 Jakim, Zichri, Zabdi, 20Elienai, Zillethai, Eliel, 21Adaiah, Beraiah, and Shimrath were sons of Shimei.

20 During his reign, Edom revolted from Judah and appointed a king. 21Joram and his chariots crossed over to Zair. He got up at night and struck at the Edomites that had surrounded him and the captains of the chariots, but his army fled to their tents. 22Edom has been in revolt against Judah ever since. 23The rest of the acts of Joram/Jehoram are recorded in The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah. 24He died and they buried him in The City of David. His son Ahaziah became king. 2 Kg 8:16-24

21 When Samuel heard what they said, he repeated it to Yahveh.

22 Ishpan, Eber, Eliel, 23Abdon, Zichri, Hanan, 24Hananiah, Elam, Anthothijah, 25Iphdeiah, and Penuel were the sons of Shashak.

25 In the twelfth year of Joram/Jehoram Ben-Ahab, king of Israel, Ahaziah Ben-Jehoram began to rule. 26He was 22 and ruled for 1 year in Jerusalem. His mother was Athaliah, granddaughter of Omri, king of Israel. 27He did evil like Ahab’s family had done because he was Ahab’s son-in-law. 28He went to war along with Joram against Hazael, king of Aram in Ramoth-gilead, and the Arameans wounded him. 29King Joram came back to Jezreel to heal up. Then Ahaziah went down to see Joram because he’d been wounded. 2 Kg 8:25-29

26 Shamsherai, Shehariah, Athaliah, 27Jaareshiah, Elijah, and Zichri were sons of Jeroham.

28 These were heads of families in their generations, chief men that lived in Jerusalem. 1 Ch 8:12-28

29 Jeiel Avi-Gibeon lived in Gibeon; his wife was Maacah. 30His firstborn son was Abdon, then Zur, Kish, Baal, Nadab, 31Gedor, Ahio, Zecher, and 32Mikloth Avi-Shimeah/Shimeam. These families lived near each other in Jerusalem.

33 Ner was father of Kish Avi-Saul Avi-Jonathan, Malchi-shua, Abinadab, and Eshbaal.

34 The son of Jonathan was Merib-baal Avi-35Micah Avi Pithon, Melech, Tarea, and Ahaz. 36Ahaz was father of Jehoaddah Avi-Alemeth, Azmaveth, and Zimri; Zimri was father of 37Moza Avi-Binea Avi-Raphah Avi-Eleasah Avi-Azel. 38Azel had six sons: Azrikam, Bocheru, Ishmael, Sheariah, Obadiah, and Hanan. 39Azel’s brother Eshek was father of Ulam his firstborn, Jeush second, and Eliphelet third. 40The sons of Ulam were brave soldiers, archers, and had many sons and grandsons—150 of them.
