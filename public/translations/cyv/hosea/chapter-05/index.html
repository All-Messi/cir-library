Hosea Chapter 5

1“Hear this, priests! Pay attention, Israel! Listen, family of the king! Your idolatry at Mizpah has set a trap and cast a net on Tabor.

2You rebels have gone deep in slaughter, but I’ll punish you all for it.

3I know what you’re doing, Ephraim; you can’t hide from me, Israel. Ephraim, you’ve left me like a prostitute; Israel, you’ve defiled yourself.

4Your deeds keep you from coming back to me. You’re totally a prostitute; you don’t know Yahveh. Hos 5:1-4 5“Israel’s arrogance testifies against him; Israel and Ephraim stumble under the weight of their guilt; Judah stumbles with them.

6They’ll go with their flocks and herds to seek Yahveh, but they won’t find him; he’s withdrawn from them.

7They’ve dealt deviously with Yahveh because they’ve borne illegitimate children. Now the new moon will devour them along with their fields.

Hos 5:5-7

8“Blow the ram’s horn in Gibeah; blow the bugle in Ramah. Sound an alarm at Beth-aven: cringe, Benjamin!

9Ephraim will become a pile of rubble when he’s rebuked; that’s for certain.

10Judah’s officials are like people that remove landmarks; I’ll pour out my anger on them like a waterfall.

11Ephraim will be oppressed, crushed in condemnation, because he was determined to follow vanity.

12So I’ll be like a moth in Ephraim, like rot in Judah. 13“When Ephraim saw his sickness and Judah saw his wound, Ephraim went to Assyria; he sent for King Jareb. But he can’t heal you or cure you from your wound.

14I’ll be like a lion to Ephraim, like a young lion to Judah. I’ll tear them to pieces and take them away; nobody will rescue them.

15I’ll go back to my place till they admit their guilt and turn to me; when trouble comes, they’ll earnestly rely on me.”

Hos 5:8-15

Israel and Judah Unrepentant
