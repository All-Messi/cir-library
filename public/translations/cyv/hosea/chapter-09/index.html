Hosea Chapter 9

1 Israel, don’t celebrate like other nations; you’ve carried on prostitution by leaving your God. You’ve loved prostitutes’ earnings on every threshing floor.

2 Threshing floor and winepress won’t feed you; you won’t have any new wine.

3 You won’t remain in Yahveh’s land; you’ll return to Egypt and eat unclean food in Assyria.

4 You won’t be pouring out liquid offerings of wine to Yahveh there; your sacrifices won’t please him. Your bread will be unclean like mourners’ bread; everybody that eats it will be defiled because it will only be for your hunger; you can’t bring it to Yahveh’s Temple.

5 What will you do on stated festival days, Yahveh’s festival days?

6 Even if you escape being destroyed by Assyria, Egypt will round you up, and Memphis will bury you. Weeds will take over your silver troves; thorns will invade your tents.

7 The time for punishment has come; the time for retribution is here. Let Israel know that! Because of how gross your sin is, how great your hostility is, you say, “Prophets are fools; inspired men are crazy."

8 Prophets are watchmen for my God over Ephraim; but bird catchers’ snares lie on all their paths; there’s hatred for them in the temple of their God.

9 The people have made themselves as depraved as they were at Gibeah long ago. He’ll remember their iniquity and punish their sins.

10 Yahveh says, “At first, finding Israel was like finding grapes in the desert; I saw their ancestors like the first fruit on a fig tree’s first season. But they came to Baal-peor and devoted themselves to their shameful idol; they became as detestable as the idol they loved.

11 Ephraim’s glory will soon fly off like a bird because there’ll be no conception, no pregnancy, no birth!

12 Even if they do raise children, I’ll take them away till there aren’t any left. Misfortune awaits them when I leave them!

13 Ephraim is planted in a pleasant meadow like Tyre, but he’ll bring out his children for slaughter."

14 Give them, Yahveh—what will I say to give them? Give them miscarrying wombs and dry breasts.

15 Yahveh says, “Their sins began in Gilgal; I started to despise them there! Because of their sinful deeds, I’ll drive them out of my house! I won’t love them anymore; their officials are rebels.

16 The people of Ephraim are stricken, their root has dried up, they won’t bear any more children. If they do, I’ll kill their precious ones at birth."

17 My God will expel them because they’ve ignored him. So they’ll wander without progeny among the nations.
