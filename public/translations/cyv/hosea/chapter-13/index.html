Hosea Chapter 13

1 When Ephraim spoke, people cringed; he made himself important in Israel, but because of Baal he became guilty and died.

2 Now his people sin more and more and make molten images, idols skillfully formed from silver, all made by craftsmen. They say, “Sacrifice to them, kiss the calf idols!"

3 So they’ll be like morning mist, like dew that dries off soon, like chaff blown from a threshing floor, like smoke from a chimney. 4"Yet I’ve been Yahveh your God since Egypt; you weren’t to know any God but me because there is no savior but me.

5 I took care of you in the desert, in a land of drought.

6 But when you got to pasture, fed to the full and satisfied, you got independent and forgot about me.

7 So I’ll be like a lion to you and lie in wait by the road like a leopard.

8 I’ll attack you like a bear robbed of her cubs and tear your chests open. I’ll devour you like a lioness and maul you like a wild animal. 9"It’s to your destruction, Israel, that you’re against me, your help.

10 Where’s your king that’s going to save you in all your towns? Where are the officials you requested, ‘Give me a king and officials’?

11 I gave you a king in my anger and took him away in my wrath. 12"The iniquity of Ephraim has been collected and stored up for retribution.

13 The pains of childbirth have come on him; he’s not a very smart son; it’s too late to keep from being born. 14"Should I ransom him from Sheol? Should I redeem him from death? No, I won’t show him mercy; so Death, where are your thorns? Death, where is your sting? Bring it.

15 Though he’s flourishing like a reed, an east wind will come— Yahveh’s wind from the desert— and dry up his fountain; his spring will go dry. It will ‘blow away’ every precious article in his treasury.

16 Samaria will bear the consequences of his guilt because he rebelled against his God: his residents will fall by the sword; his little ones will be dashed to pieces; his pregnant women will be ripped open.” Healing for the Repentant
