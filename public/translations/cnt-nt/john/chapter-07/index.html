John Chapter 7

1After this, Jesus traveled around Galilee. He didn’t want to travel in Judaea because the Jews were trying to kill him.

Jn 7:1

2The Jews’ Feast of Unleavened Bread was coming. 3So Jesus’ brothers said to him, “Go to Judaea so your disciples can see what you can do. 4Nobody does something in private when he’s trying to be known publicly. If you’re doing these things, show yourself to the world.” 5(Not even his brothers believed in him.)

6Jesus told them, “It’s not time for me to go yet. You can go anytime. 7The world can’t hate you, but it hates me because I testify that it does evil things. 8You go on up to the feast. I’m not going up to this feast, because it’s not time for me to go yet.”

9His brothers went on up to the feast; he stayed in Galilee.

Jn 7:2-9

6When evening came, his disciples went down to the sea, 17got in a small boat, and headed over to Capernaum. It was dark now, and Jesus hadn’t come. 18The sea was getting rough because a strong wind was blowing. 19When they’d rowed three miles or so, they saw Jesus walking on the sea, getting close to the boat; and it scared them. 20But he said, “It’s me; you don’t to be afraid.” 21So they came to take him into the boat, and presently the boat came to land where they were headed. Jn 6:16-21

2The next day the crowd, standing on the other side of the sea, saw that no other boat was there except one and Jesus had not got in the boat with his disciples; they had left alone. 23Other small boats came from Tiberias near where they ate the bread the Lord had blessed. 24When the crowd saw that Jesus and his disciples were not around, they got in the small boats and went to Capernaum, looking for him.

5When they found him on the other side of the sea, they said “Rabbi, when did you get here?”

6Jesus answered, “You’re looking for me, not so much because you saw signs, but because you ate the loaves and were full. 27Don’t work for bread that perishes, but for bread that lasts forever, which the Son of Man will give you, because he’s the One the Father has marked with a seal.”

8They asked him, “What’ll we do to be working the works of God?”

9Jesus answered, “This is the work of God: believe on the One he sent.”

0They said to him, “What sign do you do so we can see and believe? What ‘work’ do you do? 31Our ancestors ate manna in the desert, as scripture says, ‘He gave them bread from heaven to eat.’”

2Jesus said, “Moses didn’t give you bread from heaven. My Father is giving you the real ‘bread’ from heaven. 33God’s ‘bread’ is the One that comes down from heaven to give life to the world.”

4They said, “Sir, give us this bread all the time.”

5Jesus said, “I’m the life-giving bread. Whoever comes to me will never get hungry, and whoever believes in me will never get thirsty. 36But I tell you, you’ve seen me and not believed. 37Everything the Father gives me will come to me, and whoever comes to me I won’t reject, 38because I’ve come down from heaven, not to do my will, but the will of the One that sent me. 39This is his will: that I not lose anything he’s given me, 40that everybody who sees the Son and believes in him may have eternal life, and I’ll raise him up in the last day.”

38: Jesus’s Walking on the Water

40: Jesus’ Refusal to Be a Political Messiah

41: Attack of the Jerusalem Pharisees About Traditions

3Jesus answered, “Don’t grumble with one another. 44Nobody can come to me unless the Father that sent me is drawing him. 45The prophets wote, ‘God will teach them all.’ Everybody that listens to the Father and learns comes to me—46not that anybody has seen the Father except the One that has come from the Father; he’s seen him. 47Whoever believes has eternal life. 48I’m the life-giving ‘bread.’ 49Your ancestors ate manna in the desert and died. 50ANobody that eats this ‘bread’ from heaven will die. 51I’m the life-giving ‘bread’ that comes from heaven. If anybody eats this ‘bread,’ he’ll live forever. Yes, and the ‘bread’ I’ll give for the life of the world is my own flesh.”

2The Jews started quarreling with one another, “How can he give us his flesh to eat?”

3Jesus said, “Unless you ‘eat’ the flesh of the Son of Man and ‘drink’ his blood, you don’t have any life in you. 54Whoever ‘eats’ my flesh and ‘drinks’ my blood has eternal life, and I’ll raise him up at the last day. 55My flesh is true ‘food,’ and my blood is true ‘drink.’ 56Whoever ‘eats’ my flesh and ‘drinks’ my blood remains in me and I in him. 57As the living Father has sent me and I live because of him, whoever ‘eats’ me will live because of me. 58This is the ‘bread’ from heaven, not like what the ancestors ate and died. Whoever ‘eats’ this bread will experience eternal life.”

57: Jesus and His Unbelieving Brothers

9He said this as he was teaching in the synagogue in Capernaum. 60Upon hearing it, many disciples said, “This is a hard message. Who can understand it?”

1But Jesus, knowing that his disciples were grumbling about it, said, “Does this shock you? 62What if you see the Son of Man ascending to where he was before? 63The spirit of what I’ve said is the point. The literal words don’t carry the meaning. The words I’ve spoken are figurative and meaningful. 64But some of you don’t believe. [Jesus knew from the start who were not believers and who would betray him.] 65That’s why I told you that nobody can come to me unless the Father lets him.”

6At this point many disciples quit traveling with him. 67Jesus said to the twelve, “You don’t want to go too, do you?”

8Simon Peter answered, “Lord, who would we go to? You have the life-giving message. 69We believe and know that you’re God’s Holy One.”

0Jesus answered them, “Didn’t I choose you twelve, and one of you is a devil?” 71(He was referring to Judas, son of Simon Iscariot; he’s the one that would betray him—one of the twelve.)

Jn 6:22-71
