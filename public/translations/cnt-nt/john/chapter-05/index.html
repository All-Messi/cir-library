John Chapter 5

1After this came a feast of the Jews, and Jesus went up to Jerusalem. 2At the Sheep Gate in Jerusalem there was a pool with five porches called Bethesda in Hebrew. 3In these porches lay a crowd of infirm, blind, lame, and paralyzed people. [4] 5One fellow there had been in his condition for thirty-eight years.

6When Jesus saw him and knew he’d already been there a long time, he said, “Are you wanting to be cured?”

7The crippled man answered, “Sir, I don’t have anybody to take me in the pool when the water stirs up. While I’m making my way down, somebody else gets there ahead of me.”

8Jesus told him, “Get up, pick up your bedroll, and walk.”

9Right away the fellow was cured, took up his bedroll, and started walking.

0That day was the Sabbath. So the Jews started telling the cured man, “It’s the Sabbath; 11it’s against the law for you to be carrying your bedroll.” He answered, “The One that cured me told me, ‘Pick up your bedroll and walk.’”

2They asked him, “Who told you that?”

13The cured man didn’t know who it was, because Jesus had conveyed himself away in the crowd. 14Later, Jesus found him in the temple and said, “You’ve been cured; don’t sin anymore so something worse doesn’t happen to you.”

5The man went and told the Jews it was Jesus that cured him. 16On this account the Jews began to harass Jesus, because he did it on the Sabbath.

7But Jesus answered them, “My Father has been working till now, and I am working.”

8Now the Jews tried to do more to him and put him to death. Not only had he broken the Sabbath, he God was his Father, making himself equal to God.

9Jesus answered them, “The Son of Man can’t do anything on his own unless he sees the Father do it. What he does the Son does too. 20The Father loves the Son and shows him what he’s doing, and will show him greater deeds than these so you’ll be amazed. 21As the Father raises the dead and makes them live, so also the Son makes alive the ones he wants to. 22The Father doesn’t judge anybody; he has given all judgment to the Son, 23so everybody will respect the Son as they do the Father. Whoever doesn’t respect the Son doesn’t respect the Father that sent him. 24The one that listens to what I’m saying and believes the One that sent me has eternal life and doesn’t wns upcondemned; he has crossed over from death to life. 25The time’s coming—and is now—when the dead will hear the Son and come to life. 26As the Father has life in himself, he has enabled the Son to have life in himself, 27and has given him the authority to render judgment, because he is a human being. 28“Don’t be amazed that the time’s coming when everybody in the tombs will hear him. 29Those who did good things will come out into the resurrection of life, and those who did evil things into the resurrection of condemnation. 30“I can’t do anything on my own. As I hear I judge, and my judgment is just, because I’m not trying to accomplish my own purpose, but the purpose of the One that sent me. 31“If I testify about myself, you might consider my testimony suspect. 32There is somebody else that testifies about me, and I know his testimony is true: 33you have sent to John, and he testified to the truth. 34I don’t receive testimony from man, but I do say this so you can be saved: 35John was a burning, shining lamp, and you were willing to be happy for a while in his light. 36But I have greater testimony than John’s: the deeds the Father has given me to do testify that he sent me. 37The Father has testified about me. You’ve not heard him speak or seen him, 38and you don’t believe in the One he sent. 39Search the scriptures, because you suppose you have eternal life in them, and they testify about me. 40You don’t want to come to me to have life. 41“I don’t accept glory from man, 42but I know you don’t love God. 43I’ve come in my Father’s name, and you don’t accept me. If somebody comes in his own name, youpl accept him. 44How can you believe when you accept glory from one another, and don’t seek glory from God alone? 45Don’t suppose I’m going to accuse you to the Father. Moses accuses you, the one you’ve put your faith in. 46If you believed Moses, you’d be believing me, because he wrote about me. 47If you don’t believe what he wrote, how will you believe what I say?”

Jn 5:1-47
