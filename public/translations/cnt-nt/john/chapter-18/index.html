John Chapter 18

1When Jesus finished, he went out with his disciples past the valley Kidron to an orchard. 2Judas knew about the place, because Jesus often went there with his disciples. 3Judas had received a cohort as well as officers of the chief priests and Pharisees, and went there with lanterns, torches, and weapons. 4Jesus knew what was coming on him, and went out to them, “Who are you looking for?” 5“Jesus the Nazarene.” “I’m Jesus.” Judas was standing with them. 6When he said, “I’m Jesus,” they shrank back and fell to the ground.

7He asked them again, “Who are you looking for?” “Jesus the Nazarene.” 8“I told you, I’m Jesus. If you’re looking for me, let these go” 9(in keeping with what he had said, “I haven’t lost any you gave me.”).

0Then Simon Peter, who had a sword, drew it and struck the high priest’s servant and cut off his right ear. His name was Malchus.

1Jesus said, “Put you sword back in its sheath. Am I not going to drink the cup the Father gave me to drink?”

2Then the cohort, the tribune, and the officers of the Jews arrested Jesus, bound him, 13and led him to Annas first, because he was the father-in-law of Caiaphas, the high priest that year. 14He was the Caiaphas who counseled the Jews that it was expedient for one man to die for the people.

5Simon Peter and another disciple followed Jesus. That disciple was acquainted with the high priest, and went in with Jesus to the courtyard. 16Peter stood outside at the gate. The other disciple went out and spoke to the gatekeeper and brought Peter in.

7The maid, the gatekeeper, said to Peter, “Aren’t you one of this man’s disciples?” He said, “No.”

8Servants and officers were standing there. They had made a charcoal fire because it was cold, and they were warming themselves. Peter was standing with them, warming himself.

Jn 18:1-18

The Trial Before Annas

9The high priest asked Jesus about his disciples and his teaching.

20Jesus answered, “I spoke to the world openly and taught constantly in the synagogue and the temple, where all the Jews gather. I haven’t done anything secretly. 21Why are you asking me? Ask the ones that heard what I said. They know what I said.”

2One of the officers standing by slapped him, “Do you answer the high priest that way?” 23“If I said something wrong, tell me what it was. If I said something good, why are you hitting me?”

4Then Annas sent him bound to Caiaphas the high priest.

Jn 18:

19-24 Peter’s Final Denials

5Simon Peter was standing there, warming himself. They said to him, “You’re not one of his disciples too, are you?” “No.”

6One of the high priest’s servants, a relative of the one whose ear Peter cut off, said, “Didn’t I see you in the orchard with him?”

7Again Peter denied it, and a rooster crowed.

Jn 18:25-27

The Trials Before Pilate

8They led Jesus from Caiaphas to the Praetorium. It was early, and they didn’t go in the Praetorium themselves. They didn’t want to get defiled and nkot be able to eat the Passover. 29So Pilate came out to them, “What charges are you bringing against this man?” 30“If he weren’t a criminal, we wouldn’t have delivered him to you.”

1Pilate said, “You take him and judge him by your law.” The Jews said, “It’s illegal for us to execute anybody” 32(in fulfillment of Jesus’ statement about how he would die).

3Pilate went back in the Praetorium and called Jesus, “Are you the king of the Jews?” 34“Are you asking this yourself, or have other people prompted you?”

5Pilate answered, “I’m not a Jew, am I? Your nation and the chief priests delivered you to me. What have you done?” 36“My ‘kingdom’ is not an earthly kingdom. If it were, my servants would be fighting to keep me from being delivered to the Jews.”

7Pilate said to him, “So you’re a ‘king’ then?” “Yes; I’m a ‘king.’ That’s why I was born—the reason I came: to testify to the truth.”

8Pilate said, “What’s truth?” When he heard this, he went out to the Jews, “I don’t find him guilty of anything. 39You have a custom that I release a prisoner to you at the Passover. Do you want me to release ‘the king of the Jews’?”

0They shouted again, “Not him! Barabbas!” (Barabbas was an insurrectionist.)
