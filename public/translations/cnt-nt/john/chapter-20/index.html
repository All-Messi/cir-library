John Chapter 20

1On the first day of the week, Mary the Magdalene came to the tomb early while it was still dark, and saw the stone rolled away from the tomb. 2So she ran to Simon Peter and the disciple Jesus loved, “They’ve taken the Lord out of the tomb, and we don’t know where they put him.”

3So Peter and the other disciples ran to the tomb; 4the other disciple outran Peter and got there first. 5He stooped down and saw the linen wrappings lying there, but didn’t go in.

6Then Simon Peter got there and went on in, and saw the linen wrappings 7and the facecloth they had put over his head. It wasn’t lying with the linen wrappings; it was folded up off by itself. 8Then the other disciple went in, and saw, and believed. 9They didn’t yet understand the scripture that he had to rise from the dead. 10So they went back to their quarters.

Jn 20:2-10

Appearance to Mary

1Mary the Magdalene stood outside the tomb crying. As she was crying, she stooped down and looked in the tomb, 12and saw two angels in white sitting at the head and foot of where Jesus’ body had lain.

3They said, “Ma’am, why are you crying?” “They’ve taken my Lord, and I don’t know where they’ve put him.”

4When she said this, she turned around and saw Jesus standing there, but didn’t recognize him. 15“Ma’am, why are you crying? Who are you looking for?” Supposing he was the gardener, she said, “Sir, if you’ve carried him off, tell me where you’ve put him, and I will take him away.”

6Jesus said, “Mary.” Turning around, she said in Hebrew, “Rabbi [Teacher].”

7Jesus said, “You don’t need to cling to me; I haven’t gone up to the Father yet. Go tell my disciples, ‘I’m going up to my Father and yourpl Father, my God and yourpl God.’”

8Mary went and told them, “I’ve seen the Lord,” and what he had said to her.

Jn 20:11-18

Appearance to the Ten

9In the evening that first day of the week, the disciples had locked the doors where they were, for fear of the Jews. Jesus came and stood among them and said, “Peace to you.” 20He showed them his hands and side. The disciples were excited when they saw him.

1He said again, “Peace to you. I’m sending you like the Father has sent me, I’m sending you.” 22Then he blew on them, “Receive the Holy Spirit. 23Whose sins you forgive are forgiven; whose sins you hold against them are held against them.”

Jn 20:

19-23 Appearance to the Eleven

4Thomas, one of the twelve, called Didymus, wasn’t with them when Jesus came. The other disciples told him, “We’ve seen the Lord.”

5But Thomas said, “Unless I can see the imprints from the spikes in his wrists and put my finger in them and put my hand in his side, I’m not going to believe this.”

6Eight days later his disciples were inside again with the doors locked. Jesus came and stood among them said, “Peace to you.” 27Then he said to Thomas, “Look at my wrists and put your finger here. Put you hand in my side. Don’t keep disbelieving; believe.”

8Thomas answered, “My Lord and my God!”

9Jesus said, “You believe because you’ve seen. They are blessed who haven’t seen and believe.”

0Jesus did many other signs in the presence of his disciples that are not recorded in this book. 31But I written these down so you’ll believe Jesus is the Messiah, the Son of God, and that, because you believe, you’ll have life in his name.
