John Chapter 4

1When Jesus found out that the Pharisees heard he himself was making and baptizing more disciples than John 2(though Jesus didn’t perform the baptisms himself; his disciples were doing it), 3he left Judaea and headed back to Galilee. 4He needed to go through Samaria. 5He came to a town in Samaria called Sychar near the plot of land Jacob gave his son Joseph. 6Jacob’s well was there. Jesus was sitting on the well, tired from the trip. It was about noon.

7A Samaritan woman came to draw water, and he said to her, “Give me a drink.” 8(His disciples had gone in town to buy food.)

9The woman said, “How do you, a Jewish man, ask me, a Samaritan woman, for a drink? Jews don’t associate with Samaritans.”

0Jesus answered her, “If you knew God’s gift and who’s saying, ‘Give me a drink,’ you’d be asking him to give you living water.” 11“Sir, you don’t even have a bucket, and the well is deep. Where are you getting ‘living water’? 12You aren’t greater than our ancestor Jacob, are you, who gave us the well and drank from it himself, his sons, and his herds?”

3Jesus answered, “Everybody that drinks this water will get thirsty again. 14Whoever drinks the water I give him won’t ever get thirsty again. It’ll become a well inside of him, springing up into eternal life.” 15“Sir, give me this water so I won’t get thirsty or have to come here to draw it.”

6Jesus told her, “Go call your husband and come back.” 17“I don’t have a husband.” 18“You said it well, ‘I don’t have a husband.’ You’ve had five husbands, and the one you’re living with now is not your husband. You’ve told the truth.”

9The woman said, “Sir, I sense that you’re a prophet. 20Our ancestors worshiped on this mountain; youpl say people should worship in Jerusalem.”

1Jesus told her, “Believe me, the time’s coming when youpl won’t worship the Father on this mountain or in Jerusalem. 22You don’t know what you’re worshiping. We know what we’re worshiping, because salvation does come from the Jews. 23But, the time’s coming—and is here now—when true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth. The Father’s looking for people like that to worship him. 24God’s a spirit. and those who worship him need to worship him in spirit and truth. 25“I know Messiah is coming [called Christ]. When he comes, he’ll explain everything to us.”

6Jesus said, “I am the Messiah.”

7At this point his disciples got back, and were surprised that he was talking to a woman. But nobody said, “What are you trying to find out?” or, “Why are you talking to her?” 28She left her water pot, and went in town, and said to the men, 29“Come see a guy that told me everything I ever did. This isn’t the Messiah, is it?” 30They left the town and were coming to him.

1Meanwhile the disciples kept saying, “Rabbi, eat something.”

2But he said, “I have ‘food’ you don’t know about.”

3They started saying to one another, “Nobody brought him anything to eat, did they?”

4Jesus said, “My ‘food’ is doing the will of the One that sent me and finishing it up. 35Aren’t youpl saying, ‘Harvest is still four months away’? I tell you, look; the fields are ready for harvest now. 36Whoever reaps earns wages and gathers grain for eternal life. 37In this respect the saying rings true, ‘One sows; another reaps.’ 38I’ve sent you to reap what you haven’t worked on. Other people have worked, and you’ve joined in their work.”

9From that town many Samaritans believed in him because of the woman’s report, “He told me everything I ever did.” 40So when the Samaritans got to him, they asked him to stay with them; and he stayed two days. 41Many more believed because of what he said, 42and told the woman, “We don’t believe any longer because of what you said. We’ve heard him ourselves, and know that this really is the Savior of the world.”

Jn 4:1-42
