John Chapter 21

1After this, Jesus appeared again to his disciples at the Sea of Tiberias. This is what happened. 2Together were Simon Peter, Thomas, Nathanael from Cana of Galilee, the sons of Zebedee, and two other disciples.

3Simon Peter said, “I’m going fishing.” “We’ll go with you.” They went out and got in the boat, and that night they didn‘t catch anything. 4When morning was coming, Jesus stood on the shore, but the disciples did not know it was Jesus. 5“Children, you haven’t had anything to eat, have you?” “No.” 6“Cast the net on the right side of the boat and you’ll find some.” So they cast it, and they weren’t strong enough to haul it in because ot the number of fish.

7The disciple Jesus loved said to Peter, “It’s the Lord!” When Simon Peter heard it was the Lord, he put on his outer garment (he had stripped down) and jumped in the sea. 8The other disciples came in the small boat, because they were not far from land—only about three hundred feet away, dragging the net full of fish. 9When they got out of the boat, they saw a charcoal fire laid with a fish and a loaf laid on it.

0Jesus told them, “Bring some of the fish you just caught.”

11Simon Peter got up and drew the net onto land full of large fish—153; and though there were so many, the net didn’t tear.

2Jesus said, “Come and eat breakfast.” None of the disciples dared to ask him, “Who are you?” They knew it was the Lord. 13Jesus came and took the bread and gave it to them, and the fish as well. 14This was a third time Jesus appeared to his disciples after being raised from the dead.

5When they had finished breakfast, Jesus said to Peter, “Simon, son of John, do you love me more than these do?” “Yes, Lord, you know I love you.” “Tend my lambs.”

6He said a second time, “Simon, son of John, do you love me?” “Yes, Lord, you know I love you.” “Tend my sheep.”

7He said the third time, “Simon, son of John, do you love me?” Peter was pained that Jesus said a third time, “Do you love me?” and said, “Lord, you know everything; you know I love you.”

8Jesus said to him, “Tend my sheep. I’m telling you, when you were younger, you’d dress yourself and walk around wherever you wanted to. When you get old, you’ll stretch out your hands, and somebody else will dress you and carry you where you don’t want to go.” 19(He said this to indicte by what kind of death he would glorify God.) When he had said this, he said to him. “Follow me.”

0When Peter turned around, he saw the disciple Jesus loved, following, who had leaned back on Jesus’ chest at the supper and said, “Lord, who’s the one that’s going to betray you?” 21When Peter saw him, he said, “Lord, what about him?”

2Jesus said, “If I want him to live till I come back, what’s that to you? You follow me.”

3So the idea circulated among the brothers that he wouldn’t die. But Jesus didn’t day he wouldn’t die; he said, “If I want him to live till I come, what’s that to you?”

Jn 21:1-23

Epilogue

4This is the disciple that wrote down these matters, and we know his witness is true.

5Jesus did many other things. If every one was written down, the world couldn’t hold the books.

Jn 21:24-25
