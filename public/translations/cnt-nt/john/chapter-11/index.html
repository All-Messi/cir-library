John Chapter 11

1There was a sick man, Lazarus of Bethany, the village of Mary and Martha her sister. 2This was the Mary that anointed the Lord with perfume and wiped his feet with her hair. Lazarus was her brother. 3His sisters sent to Jesus, “Lord, the one you love is sick.”

4When he heard it, he said, “This sickness isn’t to death; it’s for the glory of God so the Son of God can be glorified by it.”

5Now Jesus loved Martha and her sister and Lazarus. 6So when he heard that Lazarus was sick, he stayed where he was for two days. 7Afterwards he said to the disciples, “Let’s go back to Judaea.”

8They said, “Rabbi, the Jews were just now trying to stone you, and you’re going back there?”

9Jesus answered, “Aren’t there twelve hours of daylight? If a person walks in the daylight, he doesn’t stumble, because he sees the light of the world. 10If a person walks in the dark, he stumbles, because he doesn’t have light in himself.”

1After he said this, he told them, “Our friend Lazarus is asleep, but I’m going to wake him up.”

2His disciples said, “Lord, if he’s asleep, he’ll get better.”

3Jesus had been talking about the death of Lazarus, but they thought he was talking about sleep as in rest. 14Then he told them plainly, “Lazarus has died. 15I’m glad for your sake that I wasn’t there, so you can believe. Let’s go to him.”

6Then Thomas, called Didymus, said, “Let’s go too so we can die with him.”

7When Jesus got there, he found that Lazarus had already been in the tomb four days. 18Now Bethany was a little less than two miles from Jerusalem. 19Many Jews had come to console Mary and Martha about their brother. 20When Martha heard Jesus was coming, she went to meet him, but Mary continued to sit in the house. 21Martha said, “Lord, if you’d been here, my brother wouldn’t have died. 22But I know even now that whatever you ask God for he’ll give you.” 23“Your brother will rise again.”

4Martha said, “I know he’ll rise again in the resurrection at the end of time.”

5Jesus said, “I am resurrection and life. Whoever believes in me will live even though he’s died. 26Everybody that lives and believes in me will not die forever. Do you believe that?” 27“Yes, Lord; I’ve believed you’re the Messiah, God’s Son, who comes into the world.”

8Having said this, she went and called Mary privately, “The Rabbi’s here and is asking for you.”

9When she heard this, she got up quickly and went to him. 30Now he had not come in the village yet; he was still where Martha met him. 31 When the Jews in the house with Mary saw her get up quickly and leave, they followed her, supposing that she was going to the tomb to mourn. 32Mary came to where Jesus was and fell at his feet, “Lord, if you’d been here, my brother wouldn’t have died.”

3When Jesus saw her crying and the ones coming with her crying, he was deeply moved and troubled in himself, 34“Where have you laid him?” They said, “Come and see.”

35Jesus began to cry.

36The Jews said, “See how much he loved him.”

37Some said, “Couldn’t he who gave sight to the blind man have kept this man from dying?”

38When Jesus got to the tomb, he was deeply moved. It was a cave with a stone lying against it. 39He said, “Take the stone away.” Martha said, “Lord, by now he smells; he’s been dead four days.”

40Jesus said to her, “Didn’t I tell you that if you believe, you’d see God’s glory?” They took the stone away. 41Then he looked up and said, “Father, thank you for hearing me. 42I know you always hear me, but I said it for the crowd standing by, so they’d believe that you’ve sent me.” 43When he said this, he shouted, “Lazarus, come out!” 44The dead man came out bound hand to foot with strips of cloth, and his face wrapped in a facecloth. Jesus said, “Unwrap him and free him.”

Jn 11:1-44
