John Chapter 19

1Then Pilate took Jesus and had him flogged. 2The soldiers twisted thorns together to make a crown, and put it on his head. They put a purple robe on him, 3and started coming up to him and saying, “Hail, king of the Jews!” and slapping him.

4Pilate brought him out again, “I’m bringing him out to let you know I don’t find him guilty of anything.” 5So he brought Jesus out wearing the crown of thorns and the purple robe. “Look at the man!”

6When the chief priests and officers saw him, they shouted, “Crucify him! Crucify him!” Pilate said, “You take him and crucify him. I don’t find him guilty of anything.”

7The Jews answered, “We have a law; according to that law he should die, because he made himself God’s Son.”

8When Pilate heard this, he became more concerned 9and went in the Praetorium and said to Jesus, “Where are you from?” Jesus didn’t answer him.

0Pilate said to him, “You aren’t talking to me? You know, don’t you, that I have the authority to release you or crucify you?” 11“You wouldn’t have any authority over me if God hadn’t allowed it. So the one that betrayed me to you has committed a worse sin.”

2Pilate tried to release him, but the Jews shouted, “If you release this man, you’re no friend of Caesar. Anybody that makes himself king opposes Caesar.”

3When Pilate heard this, he brought Jesus out and sat on the judgement seat at a place called The Pavement, but in Hebrew, Gabbatha. 14It was Preparation during the Passover,

about 6:00

a.m. He said to the Jews, “Behold your king!”

5They shouted, “Away with him! Away with him! Crucify him!” “You want me to crucify your king?” The chief priests answered, “We don’t have any king but Caesar.”

6Then he delivered him to them to be crucified.

:16 The Way to Golgotha

7They took Jesus and he went out carrying the cross for himself to the place called The Place of the Skull (Golgotha in Hebrew). 18They crucified him along with two others, one on each side of him.

Jn 19

:17-18 The Death of Christ

9Pilate wrote an inscription and put it on the cross: “Jesus the Nazarene, King of the Jews.” 20Many Jews read it, because the place where he was crucified was close to the city. It was written in Hebrew, Latin, and Greek.

1The chief priests said to Pilate, “Don’t write ‘King of the Jews,’ but that he said, ‘I am king of the Jews.’”

2Pilate answered, “What I’ve written I’ve written.”

3When the soldiers had crucified Jesus, they took his clothes and divided them into four sets, one for each soldier—except for the tunic. It was seamless, woven from the top throughout. 24They said, “Let’s not tear it apart; cast lots for it”—in fulfillment of the scripture that said, “They divided my garments among them, and cast lots for my clothes.” So that’s what the soldiers did.

5Beside Jesus’ cross stood his mother, the sister of his mother; Mary, wife of Clopas; and Mary the Magdalene. 26When he saw his mother and the disciple he loved standing there, he said to his mother, “Your son.” 27Then he said to the disciple, “Your mother.” From that time the disciple took her to his home.

8Knowing that everything was done in fulfillment of the scripture, Jesus said, “I’m thirsty.”

9A jar was sitting there full of sour wine. They put a sponge full of the sour wine on a hyssop branch and held it up to his mouth. 30When he had taken the sour wine, he said, “It’s done!” He dropped his head, and quit breathing.

Jn 19:19-30

ebrewHebrew)) The Burial

1Because it was Preparation, the Jews asked Pilate to break their bones and take them away so the bodies wouldn’t be on the cross on the Sabbath. (That Sabbath was a high day.) 32So the soldiers went and broke the bones of the first and the other man crucified with him. 33But when they came to Jesus and saw he was already dead, they didn’t break his bones. 34But, one soldier pierced his side with a spear, and blood and water drained out immediately. 35(The one that has seen has borne witness; his witness is true; he knows he’s telling the truth so you can believe too.) 36This reminds us of the scripture, “Not a one of his bones will be broken.” 37Another scripture says, “They’ll look at the one they pierced.”

8After this, Joseph of Arimathea, a disciple of Jesus—but a secret one for fear of the Jews—asked Pilate for permission to take the body of Jesus, and Pilate granted it. He went and took his body away. 39Nicodemus—who at the first came to Jesus at night—also came with some seventy pounds of myrrh mixed with aloes. 40They took the body and wound it in linen wrappings along with the aromatic spices, which is the Jews’ burial custom. 41There was a garden near where he was crucified, and there was a new tomb in it that no one had been buried in. 42That’s where they put Jesus, because it was the Jews’ Preparation and the tomb was close by.
