## Philippians Chapter 3


### No Confidence in the Flesh


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So, family <i>of Christ</i>, be happy in the Lord. Writing things like these is not hard for me to do, and it reinforces you. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Watch out for dogs, evil doers, the ‘false Circumcision.’ </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> are the ‘Circumcision,’ who serve by God’s spirit, who take pride in Christ Jesus, who don’t put confidence in what’s physical. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If anybody <u>could</u> put confidence in that, I sure <i>could</i>: </span></span>

As to the Law, <i>I was </i>a Pharisee. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As to zeal, <i>I </i>persecuted the church. As to legal goodness, <i>I was </i>blameless.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But</span></i><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ms</span></sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> what were gains to me I consider losses for Christ. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Everything’s a loss compared to knowing Christ Jesus my Lord. I lost everything on account of him and considered it rubbish so I could gain him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I don’t have a goodness of my own—from keeping the Law; <i>I have</i> a goodness <i>that comes</i> from trusting Christ, a goodness that God gives conditioned on trust.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I want</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> to know him, the power his resurrection has, and fellowship with his sufferings. <i>I want to live in </i>conformity with his death </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>so I can attain resurrection from the dead, whatever it takes.</span></span></p>

### Pressing on Toward Perfection


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’m not already perfect, but I press on toward what Christ laid hold of me for. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I don’t consider myself to have achieved it; but forgetting what’s behind and reaching out for what’s ahead, </span></span>

<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As many as are perfect should take that attitude. If in anything you have a different attitude, God will make it clear for you. </span></span>
