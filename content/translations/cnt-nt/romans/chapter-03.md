## Romans Chapter **3**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>1</sup></strong><sup> </sup>So, what advantage does a Jew have? What’s the benefit of circumcision? <strong><sup>2</sup></strong>A lot, in a lot of ways. <em>The </em>first <em>advantage is</em> that God entrusted <em>the Jews</em> with his messages. <strong><sup>3</sup></strong>So what if some <em>Jews</em> were unfaithful? Their <u>un</u>faithfulness doesn’t do away with <u>God’s</u> faithfulness, does it? <strong><sup>4</sup></strong>Goodness, no! Let God be true and everyone else a liar, like the scripture says, <strong><em>“ . . .</em></strong><strong><em> that you </em></strong><strong><em>can</em></strong><strong><em> be regarded </em></strong> <strong><em>	</em></strong><strong><em>	</em></strong><strong><em>	as </em></strong><strong><em>right</em></strong><strong><em> in </em></strong><strong><em>what you say</em></strong> <strong><em>	</em></strong><strong><em>	</em></strong><strong><em>	and correct in the judgments you make</em></strong><strong><em>”</em></strong><strong> </strong><strong>[Ps</strong><strong> </strong><strong>51:4</strong><strong>].</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>If our unrighteousness highlights God’s righteousness, we wouldn’t say, would we, that God’s not fair in inflicting <em>his</em> displeasure? (I’m speaking here for the sake of argument.) <strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Goodness, no! How would he judge the world? <strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>We</em><em> wouldn’t say, would we, that </em>since our lie highlights God’s truth, he shouldn’t consider us sinners? <strong><sup>8</sup></strong>We wouldn’t <em>say, would we</em>—as some accuse us of saying, <em>“</em><em>Let’s do evil to bring about good”</em>? (They deserve condemnation!)</span>
<p class="section-ref">Rom 3:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>We know that what the Law is saying <em>here</em>, it’s saying to people that are under it; so <em>by saying “not a one,”</em> it shuts everybody up, <em>including Jews.</em> The whole world—<em>Gentiles </em><em><u>and</u></em><em> Jews</em>—becomes exposed to God’s condemnation, <strong><sup>20</sup></strong>because what people <u>do</u> under <u>law</u> doesn’t make them good; law <em>only</em> tells them what’s sinful.</span>

### Goodness by Trust


<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>Now, aside from <u>law</u>, a goodness from <u>God</u> has come to light, <em>a goodness</em> the Law and prophets talk about: <strong><sup>22</sup></strong>a goodness from <u>God</u> through <u>trusting</u> in <u>Jesus </u><u>Christ</u> whether by<em> </em><u>Jew or Gentile</u>. <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>Everybody has sinned and falls short of God’s glory; <strong><sup>24</sup></strong>his grace freely justifies them<em> </em>through the redemption that comes through Christ Jesus. <strong><sup>25</sup></strong>He’s the One that God appointed <em>as a </em>basis for forgiveness by trusting in his blood. <em>Putting him forward</em> demonstrates God’s goodness in overlooking sins previously committed <strong><sup>26</sup></strong>during the time when he puts up with <em>them</em>. <em>Putting Christ forward</em> demonstrates <em>God’s </em>goodness now, so he’s both just and justifier of people that trust in Jesus.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Rom 3:21-26</p>

### Humility Under Justification by Trust


<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup>So where can boasting come in? It can’t. By what kind of “law”? <em>Can it come by a “law” </em>caused by what we do <em>under law</em><em>? </em>No, <em>it’s replaced</em> by a “law” conditioned on trust <em>in another—God</em>. <strong><sup>28</sup></strong>We reason that trust justifies us apart from what we do under law. <strong><sup>29</sup></strong>Or is God the God over Jews only? Isn’t he God over Gentiles too? Yes. <strong><sup>30</sup></strong>Since there’s one God, he’s the One that justifies the Circumcision <u>and</u> the Uncircumcision <em>and does it</em><em> the same way—</em>by trust. <strong><sup>31</sup></strong>So are we contradicting law by <em>advocating</em> trust? Goodness, no! We’re establishing <em>the purpose of </em>law <em>another way</em>.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Rom 3:27-31</p>
