## Romans Chapter 2


### God’s Just Judgment


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So you<sup>sg</sup> that condemn don’t have any excuse. When you condemn someone else, you condemn yourself, because you’re doing the same things. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You know it’s right for <u>God</u> to condemn people that do things like that. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Do you suppose—condemner of another while doing the same things yourself—that <u>you</u> are going to escape his condemnation? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Do you disregard his rich kindness, tolerance, and patience, and not understand that his kindness is for leading you to repentance? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Because of your stubborn, unrepentant heart, you’re storing up displeasure for yourself when God reveals his just condemnation. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He’ll pay back every person for what they’ve done. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That will mean </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>eternal life for people who strive for glory, honor, and immortality by carrying through in good works. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’ll mean </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>displeasure and anger for people that chase after injustice. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There’ll be</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> trouble and distress for everybody that does evil, for Jews first and for Greeks. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But <i>there’ll be</i> honor and peace for everybody that does good, for Jews first and for Greeks. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God doesn’t play favorites: </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>as many as have sinned without having the Law will perish without <i>having</i> it; as many as have sinned under it will be condemned by it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s not who <u>hears</u> the Law that’s good, as far as God’s concerned; it’s who <u>does</u>
<i>the Law</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Gentiles that don’t have the Law, naturally do what’s in it, they are governing themselves. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They show that the Law is written on their hearts. Their conscience testifies, and <i>their</i> thoughts defend or accuse one another. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That’s the basis for judgment</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> when God by Christ Jesus judges people’s secrets by the good news I’m presenting.                                                                                                 </span></span></p>

### Criticism and Moral Consistency


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But if you wear the name Jew, rely on the Law, take pride in God—</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>if you </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>know</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>his will, approve of superior things because of what the Law teaches you—</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>if you</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’re confident that you’re a guide to the blind, a light to people in the dark, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>a corrector of the foolish, a teacher of the ignorant because you have the knowledge and truth that’s in the Law—</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>then do you teach others, but not learn yourself? You that preach against stealing, do you steal?</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You that say people shouldn’t commit adultery, do you commit adultery? You that detest idols, do you steal from temples? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You that take pride in the Law, do you dishonor God by breaking it? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Gentiles blaspheme God because of you” </span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Is 52:5]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>like the scripture says. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Circumcision benefits you if you practice <i>the</i> Law. If you transgress it, your circumcision amounts to uncircumcision. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If then the uncircumcised keep the Law’s standards, won’t their uncircumcision be considered circumcision? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If the naturally uncircumcised keep the Law, won’t they condemn literal-and-circumcised transgressors of the Law? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s not the Jew outwardly that’s <i>a Jew</i>, and it’s not outward circumcision that’s <i>circumcision</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s the Jew inwardly that’s a Jew, and it’s the circumcision of the heart—spirit, not letter—that’s circumcision. Their praise doesn’t come from people, but from God.                                                                                    </span></span></p>
