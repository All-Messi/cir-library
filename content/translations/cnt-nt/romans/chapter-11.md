### The Olive Tree Parable


<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> If some branches were removed and you, a wild olive tree, were grafted in among them and partook of the nourishment from the root of the olive tree, <strong><sup>18</sup></strong>don’t feel superior to the <em>original</em> branches: you don’t <em>support</em> the root; it <em>supports</em> you. <strong><sup>19</sup></strong>You may say, <em>“Branches were removed so I could be grafted in.”</em><em> </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong>Right. But they were removed because they didn’t trust, and <u>you</u> stand by trust <em>as well</em>. Don’t get smug; be reverent. <strong><sup>21</sup></strong>If God didn’t spare the natural branches <em>because they </em><em>didn’t trust</em>, he won’t spare you <em>either</em>. <strong><sup>22</sup></strong>So pay attention to God’s kindness <u>and</u> severity. For the ones that fell, severity; but for you, God’s kindness—<u>if</u> you stay in his kindness. Otherwise, <u>you</u>’ll be removed too. <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>And if <u>they</u> don’t keep distrusting, God will graft them back in; he can do that. <strong><sup>24</sup></strong>If God took <u>you</u> out of a naturally wild olive tree and grafted you unnaturally into a cultivated olive tree, much more will he graft <u>them</u> into their own olive tree.</span>
