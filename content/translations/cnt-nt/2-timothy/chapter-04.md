## 2 Timothy Chapter 4


### Maintaining Timothy’s Ministry


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I solemnly charge </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>you</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> in front of God and Christ Jesus—who will judge the living and the dead—and by his appearing and kingdom: </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>proclaim the message. Be ready in normal or unusual times. Refute, reprimand, encourage patiently with teaching. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The time will come when </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>people</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> won’t pay attention to sound teaching. They’ll draw teachers to themselves that reinforce their own desires and say what they want to hear. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They’ll wander off from the true message and turn to myths. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> be self-controlled, suffer hardship, do the work of an evangelist, fulfill your ministry.                                                                                                                                                </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Tim 4:1-5</p>


### Paul’s Near Prospect of Death


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’m already being poured out like a drink offering. The time for me to depart. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ve fought a good fight, I’ve finished the course, I’ve kept the faith. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From now on, a crown for righteousness waits for me. The Lord, the just Judge, will give it to me that day, and not just to me, but to everybody that has loved his coming.                                                                                </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Tim 4:6-8</p>


### Urgent Call for Timothy’s Coming


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Do your best to get here soon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Demas has deserted me and gone to Thessalonica—he loved the present age. Crescens </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>went</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> to Galatia; Titus to Dalmatia. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Luke’s the only one here. Pick up Mark and bring him with you; I can use him for ministry. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I sent Tychicus to Ephesus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When you come, bring the coat I left in Troas with Carpus, and get the scrolls, especially the parchments. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Alexander, the precious-metal worker, did me a lot of harm. The Lord will repay him accordingly. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> be careful around him, too, because he really opposed our message.                                                                                                                                                                                      </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Tim 4:9-15</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At my first hearing, nobody came to my defense. May </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the Lord</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> not hold it against them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But, the Lord stood by me and strengthened me so I could fully deliver the proclamation for the Gentiles to hear. </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> delivered me out of the lion’s mouth. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He’ll deliver me from every evil deed and save me into his heavenly kingdom. To him </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>be</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> glory forevermore. Amen.                               </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Tim 4:16-18</p>


### Final Greetings

<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Erastus stayed in Corinth, and I left Trophimus in Miletus sick. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Do your best to get here before winter. Eubulus, Pudens, Linus, Claudia, and all the believers send their greetings. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Lord </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>be</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> with your spirit. </span></span></p>
