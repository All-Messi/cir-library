## Mark Chapter 6


### Last Visit to Nazareth


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He left for his hometown with his disciples. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Sabbath came, he taught in the synagogue, and many were astonished when they heard<sup>p</sup> <i>him</i>: <i>“Where’d he get that wisdom and those miracles he does with his hands? </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Isn’t this the builder, Mary’s son and brother of James, Joseph, Judas, and Simon? Aren’t his sisters here?”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> They were put off by him. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jesus told them, <i>“A prophet is respected everywhere except in his hometown, among his relatives, in his own family.”</i>
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He couldn’t do any miracle there—except he did lay hands on a few sick people and healed them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was amazed at their disbelief. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He continued to move around the villages and teach.                                               </span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Mk 6:1-6</p>


### Mission of the Twelve


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He called<sup>p</sup> the Twelve aside and got them ready to go out in pairs with authority over unclean spirits. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He instructed them not to take anything except a rod—no bread, traveler’s bag or money in their belt, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>but to wear sandals. <i>“Don’t take two shirts to wear. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Stay at whatever house you enter till you leave the area. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If a place doesn’t welcome you or listen to you, leave and shake the dust off your feet as a statement to them.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They went out and preached that people should repent. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They cast out demons and anointed many sick people and healed them.</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Mk 6:7-13</p>


### Herod’s Stricken Conscience


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height: normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>King Herod heard about <i>him</i> because Jesus had become well known. People were saying John the Baptist had arisen from the dead and that was why miraculous powers were working in him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Others were saying he was Elijah or like one of the prophets. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Herod heard about it, he said, <i>“John that I beheaded has resurrected.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Herod himself had sent and arrested John and bound him in prison on account of Herodias, his brother Philip’s wife, because he’d married her. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>John kept telling Herod, <i>“It’s against the Law for you to be living with your brother’s wife.”
</i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Herodias had a grudge against him and wanted to have him killed; but she couldn’t </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because Herod was afraid of him. He knew he was upright and holy, and he protected him. He listened to him many times and was confused, but he liked to listen to him.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height: normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A chance came when Herod threw a banquet on his birthday for his officials, military commanders, and prominent men of Galilee. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When his daughter of Herodias came in and danced, it pleased Herod and the ones reclining at table with him. The king said to the girl <i>“Ask me for whatever you want, and I’ll give it to you.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He strongly swore to her, <i>“I’ll give you whatever you ask for up to half my kingdom.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>She went out and asked her mother what she should<i> </i>ask for. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“The head of John the Baptist!”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>She hurried back and asked, <i>“I want you to give me right now the head of John the Baptist on a platter.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Herod became deeply grieved; but because of his oaths and the guests, he wasn’t willing to refuse her. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He sent the executioner to bring John’s head. He went and beheaded him in prison, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>brought his head on a platter and gave it to the girl, and she gave it to her mother. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When his disciples heard about it, they came and got his corpse and put it in a tomb.                     </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Mk 6:14-29</p>


### Feeding the 5,000


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The apostles came back<sup>p</sup> to Jesus and reported what all they’d done and taught. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said<sup>p</sup>,
<i>“Come off by yourselves away from the crowds and rest awhile.”</i> So many people were coming and going they didn’t have time to eat. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They left in a boat to a place off by themselves. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>People saw them leaving and figured out where they were going. They ran as a group from the towns and got there first. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he got out <i>of the boat</i>, he saw a large crowd and felt sorry for them: they were like sheep without a shepherd. He began to teach them a lot of things. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When it was getting quite late, his disciples came to him, <i>“Nobody lives around here, and it’s getting late. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Dismiss them so they can go into the countryside and villages to buy food.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height: normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But he answered, <i>“<u>You</u> feed them.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height: normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>             <i>“Should we go spend 200 denarii on bread to feed them!?”</i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height: normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Go see how many loaves you have.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height: normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            They found five, and two fish<i>.</i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told <i>his disciples</i> to have everybody sit down on the green grass </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>in groups of 50 and 100. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He took the five loaves and two fish, and looked up at the sky, blessed and broke the loaves, and started giving <i>them</i> to his disciples to distribute to the crowd, and divided up the two fish for everybody. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v42" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They all ate till they were full. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v43" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The disciples </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>picked up twelve baskets of bread and fish pieces. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v44" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>5,000 men had eaten the loaves.                                                                                                                                            </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Mk 6:30-44</p>


### Jesus Walking on the Water


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v45" id="v45"><sup class="verse-num">45</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jesus urged his disciples to get in the boat right away and go on to Bethsaida, on the other side, while he dismissed the crowd. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v46" id="v46"><sup class="verse-num">46</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he’d said goodbye to everybody, he went up on the hill to pray. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v47" id="v47"><sup class="verse-num">47</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the evening the boat was in the middle of the lake, and he was alone on land. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v48" id="v48"><sup class="verse-num">48</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He saw them straining at the oars because the wind was against them. About 4 in the morning, he went to them walking on the water as if he intended to go on by. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v49" id="v49"><sup class="verse-num">49</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they saw him walking on the water, they thought he was a ghost and cried out terrified. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v50" id="v50"><sup class="verse-num">50</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At once he spoke<sup>p</sup>,
<i>“It’s me. Don’t let me scare you.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v51" id="v51"><sup class="verse-num">51</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He climbed up into the boat with them and the wind died down. They were astounded. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v52" id="v52"><sup class="verse-num">52</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They hadn’t got the point about the loaves. They weren’t catching on.                                                                                     </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Mk 6:45-52</p>


### Miracles at Gennesaret


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span class="verse verse-v53" id="v53"><sup class="verse-num">53</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they’d crossed over to land, they tied up at Gennesaret. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v54" id="v54"><sup class="verse-num">54</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As soon as they got out of the boat, people recognized him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v55" id="v55"><sup class="verse-num">55</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They hurried around the vicinity and started bringing sick people on stretchers to wherever they heard he was. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v56" id="v56"><sup class="verse-num">56</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Whatever villages, towns, or countryside he’d go to, they’d put sick people in the marketplaces and appeal to him just to touch his clothes. As many as touched him were cured.  </span></span></p>
