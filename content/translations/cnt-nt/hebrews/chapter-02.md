## Hebrews Chapter 2


### Heeding the New Revelation


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So we need to pay close attention to what we’ve heard so we never drift away from it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>What God said through angels stood firm, and every sin received the punishment it deserved. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So how will we get off if we neglect such a great salvation? After the Lord spoke it, the ones that heard it confirmed it to us. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God testified alongside them with signs, wonders, and various miracles and gifts that the Holy Spirit dispensed as he saw fit.                                                                                                                                  </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 2:1-4</p>


### Mankind as Caretaker of Creation


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It wasn’t to angels that God subjected the future world we’re talking about. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In a certain place one has testified,</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        <b><i>“What are people that you remember them or visit them?</i></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        </span></i></b><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You made them lower than angels for a little while,</span></i></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                    crowned them with glory and honor, </span></i></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span></i></b><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and put everything under their authority”</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Ps 8:5-7 LXX]</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Putting everything under their authority” means God didn’t leave anything that wasn’t under them, but we don’t see that yet. </span><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We do see Jesus, however, <i>“made lower than angels for a little while</i>,<i>”</i> so by God’s grace he could taste death for every<i>one</i>. Now we see him “crowned with glory and honor” because he suffered death.                                                                                 </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 2:5-9</p>


### The Son’s Incarnation


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Everything is through him and for him. It was appropriate, then, in bringing many children to glory, that he make perfect the Author of their salvation with suffering. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Both the sanctifier and the sanctified have the same <i>nature</i>. That means he’s not ashamed to call them <i>“siblings.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        </span><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ll present you to my siblings</span></i></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                    and sing your praises in the congregation”</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Ps 22:22]</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>.</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Again,</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        <b><i>“I’ll trust him”</i> </b></span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Is 8:17 LXX]</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,
</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                    <b><i>“ . . . I and the children God gave me”</i> </b></span><b><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Is 8:18]</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
                        </span></b></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 2:10-13</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Since
<i>“the children”</i> exist in flesh and blood, he took on flesh and blood so that by dying he could take the power of death away from the one that has it—the devil. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That way</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> he could free people that the fear of death had enslaved as long as they were alive. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s clear he’s not <i>here </i>to help angels, but <i>to help</i> Abraham’s descendants. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So he needed to be like people in every respect so he could become a merciful, faithful high priest in things that have to do with God and could atone for people’s sins. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Since he faced temptation in what he suffered, he can help those who face temptation.
                                                                                                               </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 2:14-18</p>
