## Hebrews Chapter 7


### Christ’s Melchizedekian Priesthood


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That
</span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Melchizedek—king of Salem, priest of God Most High—met Abraham when he came back from killing the kings</span></i></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and <b>blessed him.</b> </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abraham</span></i></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> separated off to him </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>a tenth of everything </span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Gen 14:17-20]</span></b><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.</span></i></b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> First, by translation, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Melchizedek” </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>was
</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“king of righteousness</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and then </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>“king of Salem,” </span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>meaning </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“king of peace.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Without
</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>record of </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>father, mother, genealogy, birth, or death—like God’s Son—he remains a priest for all time.                                                                                                                                 </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 7:1-3</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Look how great the man was that Abraham the patriarch gave a tenth of the spoils </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>of war</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the Law the Levites that receive the priesthood have a commandment to receive a tenth from the people, that is, from their kin though they’ve descended from Abraham. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the one whose genealogy <i>wasn’t </i>from them received a tenth from Abraham and blessed the one that had the promises. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Without argument, the greater blesses the lesser. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Here men that die receive a tenth, but there </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>one received it who is </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>pictured as alive. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So to speak, through Abraham even Levi, who receives a tenth, paid a tenth. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was in his ancestor’s loins when Melchizedek met him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If perfection could come through the Levitical priesthood (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>by which the people received the Law</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>), why </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>would there be </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>another priest </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“after the order of Melchizedek”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Changing the priesthood requires changing the Law, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because the One these things speak about belonged to different tribe; nobody from that tribe ever served at the altar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s clear that our Lord descended from Judah; Moses never said anything about priests from that tribe. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s even clearer if another priest arises like Melchizedek, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>who has become </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>a priest</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, not by law based on physical requirement, but by power based on indestructible life. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Scripture testifies, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You’re a priest <u>forever</u></span></i></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                    after the order of Melchizedek”</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Ps 110:4]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>.</span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That statement</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> annuls the preceding commandment because it was weak and unprofitable—</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the Law didn’t make anything perfect—and brings in a better hope by which we can draw near to God.                                                          </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 7:4-19</p>


### God’s Oath About Christ’s Priesthood


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20-21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Furthermore, Christ’s priesthood </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>came in</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> with an oath. The Levites became priests without an oath, but <u>he</u> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>became a priest</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> with an oath from the One that said to him,</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“The L</span></i></b><b><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i></b><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> <u>swore</u></span></i></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                    and won’t change his mind,</span></i></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        ‘You’re a priest forever’”</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Ps 110:4]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>.</span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>By that much Jesus also guaranteed a better covenant. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There were lots of former </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>priests</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> because death prevented them from continuing. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> holds his priesthood without successors because he remains forever. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So he can save “forever” the ones that draw near to God through him. He’s always alive to intercede for them.                                                                                   </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 7:20-25</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That’s the kind of high priest we need—holy, innocent, undefiled, separate from sinners, higher than the sky. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Unlike those high priests, he didn’t need to make an offering every day first for his own sins and then for the people’s. He made an offering once and for all when he offered himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>T</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>he Law appoints men high <u>priests that have weakness</u>, but the oath that came after the Law </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>appointed</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> forever <u>a perfected Son</u>.      </span></span></p>
