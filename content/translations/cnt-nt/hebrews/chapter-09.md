## Hebrews Chapter 9


### Service in the First Sanctuary


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now the first </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>covenant</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> had an earthly sanctuary and regulations for service. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The first tabernacle, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“the Holy Place,”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> had a lampstand and a table with sacred bread on it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Behind the second curtain stood another tabernacle, </span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“the Holy of Holies.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It had a gold altar of incense and the Ark of the Covenant that was gold-plated all over. Inside the ark there was a gold pot with manna in it along with Aaron’s rod that budded and the tablets that contained the covenant. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Above the ark the glorious cherubs hovered over the mercy seat. We can’t speak in detail about these now.          </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 9:1-5</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>With things arranged that way, the priests regularly go into the first tabernacle to perform rites of divine service. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the high priest goes into the second tabernacle by himself once a year and takes blood to offer for himself and the sins people committed in ignorance. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Holy Spirit signified by that design that the way into the Holy of Holies wasn’t obvious while the original tabernacle was standing. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The tabernacle figuratively pictured its own time when gifts and sacrifices couldn’t clear a worshiper’s conscience. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Those gifts and sacrifices only dealt with foods, drinks, various washings—physical regulations in place till the new order would come in.           </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 9:6-10</p>


### Christ as Priest of What’s Real


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Christ appeared as a high priest of good things that are here now. He went in by the greater and more perfect tabernacle not made with hands, that is, not of this creation. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He didn’t go into the Holy of Holies on the basis of the blood of goats and calves, but on the basis of his own blood once and for all to obtain eternal salvation. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If the blood of bulls and goats and the ashes of a heifer sprinkling the defiled, sanctifies to cleanse the <u>flesh</u>, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>how much more will the blood of Christ—who offered himself without blemish to God through the eternal Spirit—cleanse our <u>conscience</u> from dead works to serve the living God.                                                                         </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 9:11-14</p>


### Christ’s Mediating the New Covenant


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So he’s the mediator of a new covenant. His death redeemed sins that people committed under the <u>first covenant</u> so the heirs of the <u>eternal covenant</u> could receive the promise. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A testamentary covenant involves the death of the one that made it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s never in force while he’s alive. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The first covenant was instituted with blood. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Moses had spoken every commandment to the people, he took the blood of goats and young bulls—with water, scarlet wool, and hyssop—and sprinkled the scroll itself and the people, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>saying,
</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“This is <b>the blood of the covenant God commanded you”</b></span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Ex. 24:8]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>.</span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i></b></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He also sprinkled blood on the tabernacle and the vessels used in it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I can</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> almost </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>say</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, according to the Law, blood cleanses everything; there’s no forgiveness without shedding blood.                              </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 9:15-22</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>These things cleansed the copies of heavenly things, but something better needed to cleanse the heavenly things themselves. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Christ didn’t go into a handmade copy of the real</span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>sanctuary; he went into heaven itself to appear for us now in God’s presence. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>And, he didn’t need to offer himself many times like the high priest does in the Holy Place every year with blood that’s not his own. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Otherwise, Christ would’ve had to suffer many times since the world began. But now, once and for all at the end of the ages, he has appeared to get rid of sin by sacrificing himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s appointed to men to die once and after that </span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>to face</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> judgment. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the same way,<sup>
</sup>Christ offered himself once to bear the sins of many. He’ll appear a second time—apart from sin—to the ones who wait expectantly for him to save us.      </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Heb 9:23-28</p>
