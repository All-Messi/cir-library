## Hebrews Chapter 10

### Replacing Shadows with Realities

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> So we have confidence about entering the Holy Place by the blood of Jesus. <strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>We go in </em>by the new and living way that he has opened for us through the curtain, that is, his flesh. <strong><sup>21</sup></strong>We have a great priest over God’s house; <strong><sup>22</sup></strong>so let’s draw near with a true heart in full assurance that comes from trust. <em>Let’s draw near</em> with hearts sprinkled from a guilty conscience and bodies washed with pure water. <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>Let’s hold onto <em>our</em> hope confidently without wavering; we can trust the One that promised. <strong><sup>24</sup></strong>Let’s think carefully how we can encourage one another to love and good works. <strong><sup>25</sup></strong>Let’s not quit going to church like some do; let’s encourage one another, especially as we see “the time” coming close. <strong><sup>26</sup></strong>If we keep on sinning deliberately after we know the truth, there’s no more sacrifice <em>to be offered</em>. <strong><sup>27</sup></strong>There’s a fearful dread of judgment and the furious fire that will consume <em>God’s </em>opponents. <strong><sup>28</sup></strong>Anybody that disregards the Law of Moses, dies without mercy at the testimony from two or three witnesses. <strong><sup>29</sup></strong>How much more severe punishment do you suppose people deserve who trample the Son of God under their feet, regard as unclean the blood of the covenant that sanctified them, and insult the Spirit of grace? <strong><sup>30</sup></strong>We know who said, 	<strong><em>“Revenge </em></strong><strong><em>is my business</em></strong><strong><em>;</em></strong> <strong><em>	</em></strong><strong><em>	I’ll </em></strong><strong><em>take care of</em></strong><strong><em> it”</em></strong><strong><em> </em></strong><strong>[Deut. 32:35]</strong>; and, <strong><em>“The </em></strong><strong><em>L</em></strong><strong><em>ORD</em></strong><strong><em> will judge his people</em></strong><strong><em>”</em></strong><strong> </strong><strong>[Deut 32:36; Ps</strong><strong> 135:14]</strong>.</span>
