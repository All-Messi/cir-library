## Acts Chapter 20


### Paul Travels Through Macedonia and Greece


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After the uproar, Paul sent for the disciples. When he’d encouraged them and said goodbye, he left for Macedonia. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After he passed through that area and encouraged them a lot, he went to Greece </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>for three months. The Jews laid a plot against him as he was getting ready to sail to Syria; so he decided to go back through Macedonia. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>With him were Sopater son of Pyrrhus from Berea; the Thessalonians Aristarchus and Secundus; Gaius of Derbe; Timothy, and the Asians Tychicus and Trophimus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> went on ahead and waited for us in Troas. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After the days of Unleavened Bread, <u>we</u> sailed from Philippi and met them in Troas in five days and stayed seven.     </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 20:1-6</p>


### Paul’s Farewell to Troas


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>On the first day of the week, when we got together to break bread, Paul started speaking, He intended to leave in the morning and kept speaking till midnight. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Quite a few lamps were in the upstairs room where we gathered. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A young man named Eutychus was sitting on the windowsill. As Paul kept speaking for a long time, Eutychus went sound asleep and fell from the third floor. They picked him up for dead. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Paul went down, he knelt beside him, took him in his arms, and said, <i>“Don’t worry; he’s alive.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went back up and broke the bread and ate. After he talked for a long time till daybreak, he went out directly like he was. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They brought the boy alive and were really relieved.    </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 20:7-12</p>


### Paul Sails from Troas to Miletus


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We went on ahead to the ship and sailed to Assos, intending to pick up Paul there. That’s the arrangement he’d made; he planned to go on foot himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We took him on board at Assos and went on to Mitylene. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From there, we arrived the following day across from Chios, the next day came near Samos, and the day after that made it to Miletus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Paul had decided to sail on past Ephesus to keep from spending time in Asia. He was pushing to get to Jerusalem by Pentecost if he could.                                                    </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 20:13-16</p>


### Paul Addresses the Ephesians


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From Miletus he sent to Ephesus for the church elders. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they came, he said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“From the day I first came to Asia, you know how I operated among you. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I served the Lord with humility, tears, and trials that came on me from plots by the Jews. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I told you everything profitable as I taught you publicly and from house to house. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>To Jews and Greeks I solemnly preached repentance toward God and trust in our Lord Jesus. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now I’m determined to go to Jerusalem. I don’t know what will happen there. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In every city the Holy Spirit declares that bonds and sufferings are waiting for me. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But I don’t consider my life of any value to myself, so I can finish my course in the ministry I received from the Lord—to speak the good news about God’s grace.
                                                                     </span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 20:17-24</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Now I’ve presented God’s kingdom among you, and I know you won’t see me again. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So I tell you today, I’m innocent of everybody’s blood. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ve explained to you God’s whole plan. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Pay attention to yourselves and the flock the Holy Spirit has given you to oversee. Shepherd God’s church that he bought with the blood of his own Son. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After I leave, I know fierce wolves are going to come in and not spare the flock. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Even from <u>you</u>, men will rise up and say perverse things to lure away disciples. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So pay attention; remember that for three years day and night I warned each one with tears. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now I entrust you to God and his grace. It can build you up and provide you an inheritance among the sanctified. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I haven’t coveted anybody’s gold, silver, or clothes. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You know these hands have provided for my needs and the ones that were with me. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In everything I’ve shown you that by working this way you should help the weak and remember the words of the Lord Jesus, </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>‘It’s more blessed to give than to receive<i>.’”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he knelt down with them and prayed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They began to cry out loud, hug him, and kiss him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They grieved especially over his comment that they wouldn’t see him again. They went with him to the ship.                               </span></span></p>
