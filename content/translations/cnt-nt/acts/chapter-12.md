## Acts Chapter 12


### Herod Kills James, Jails Peter


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>About that time, King Herod <i>Agrippa I </i>reached out to abuse some in the church.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He killed James, John’s brother, with a sword. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he saw the Jews liked that, he arrested Peter too (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>during the days of Unleavened Bread</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He put him in jail, with four detachments of four soldiers to guard him. After Passover he meant to bring him out to the people, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>but the church prayed sincerely to God about him.  </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 12:1-5</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The night before Herod meant to bring him out, Peter was sleeping between two soldiers, bound with two chains. There were guards at the door, keeping the jail. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>An angel of the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> stood beside him, and a light glowed in the cell. He tapped Peter on the shoulder to wake him up, <i>“Get up quick!”</i> The chains fell off his hands.</span></span></p>


### Peter Delivered from Jail


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Fasten your belt and put your sandals on</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. <i>Put your cloak on and follow me.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He followed him, not knowing it was real; he thought he was dreaming. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They went through the first and second guard posts and came to the iron gate into the city. It swung open on its own, and they went out and passed down one street. Then the angel disappeared.           </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 12:6-10</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Peter came to himself, he said, <i>“Now I’m sure the </i>L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> sent his angel to deliver me from Herod and the Jews.”           </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After he came to grips with what happened, he went to the house of Mary, John Mark’s mother, where a large number had gathered to pray. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He knocked on the door in the gate, and a girl named Rhoda came to answer it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>She recognized Peter’s voice. Instead of letting him in, she ran excited to tell them that he was standing at the gate.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They said, <i>“You’re out of your mind.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But she insisted. </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They started saying, <i>“It must be his angel.”</i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Peter kept knocking; and when they opened the door, they were surprised to see him.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He motioned for them to hold it down and recounted how the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> had led him out of the jail, <i>“Tell James and the brothers what’s happened.”</i> He left and went somewhere else.           </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next morning there was a huge stir among the soldiers over what had become of Peter. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Herod looked for him and didn’t find him, he examined the guards and commanded to lead them off for execution. He went down from Judaea to Caesarea and spent some time there.
</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 12:11-19</p>


### The Death of Herod


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was mad at the residents of Tyre and Sidon. As a group they came to him and befriended Blastus, who was in charge of the king’s sleeping quarters. They asked for peace because his country supplied their food. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>On a set day, Herod dressed in royal garb, sat on the judgment seat, and gave an oration. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The public assembly shouted, <i>“</i>It’s<i> the voice of a god, not a man!”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Immediately an angel of the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> struck him because<i> he accepted the credit </i>and didn’t give it to God. He got worms and died. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God’s message grew and multiplied. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Barnabas and Saul went back from Jerusalem when they’d finished their service and took John Mark with them.</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 12:20-25</p>
