## Acts Chapter 28

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> When we'd made it safely, we learned the island was Malta. 2The locals were especially nice to us and received us all. Because of the rain and cold that had set in, they built a fire. 3When Paul had gathered a bundle of sticks and laid them on the fire, a snake came out of the wood from the heat and fastened on his hand. 4The locals saw it and said to one another, "He must be a murderer that survived the sea, but Justice didn't let live." 5He shook it loose into the fire and wasn't harmed. 6They were expecting him to swell up or suddenly fall down dead. But after a good while not seeing anything unusual, they started saying he was a god.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Around that place were lands that belonged to Publius, the Principal in the island. Paul went in to see him, prayed, laid hands on him, and healed him. 9After that, other sick people on the island started coming to be healed. 10They greatly honored us; and later, when we set sail, they put on board what we needed.</span>
