## Acts Chapter 28


### Paul’s Winter Stay on Malta


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When we’d made it safely, we learned the island was Malta. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The locals were especially nice to us and received us all. Because of the rain and cold that had set in, they built a fire. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Paul had gathered a bundle of sticks and laid them on the fire, a snake came out of the wood from the heat and fastened on his hand. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The locals saw it and said to one another, <i>“He must be a murderer that survived the sea, but Justice didn’t let live.</i></span><i><span style='font-size:14.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>”</span></i><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> 5</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He shook it loose into the fire and wasn’t harmed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They were expecting him to swell up or suddenly fall down dead. But after a good while not seeing anything unusual, they started saying he was a god.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Around that place were lands that belonged to Publius, the Principal in the island. He welcomed us and entertained us for three days. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His father lay sick with dysentery and bouts of fever. Paul went in to see him, prayed, laid hands on him, and healed him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After that, other sick people on the island started coming to be healed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They greatly honored us; and <i>later,</i> when we set sail, they put on board what we needed.    </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 28:1-10</p>


### Paul’s Arrival in Rome


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After three months, we sailed on an Alexandrian ship that had wintered on the island—it had The Twin Brothers (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Dioscuri/Twin Sons of Zeus</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>) as its figurehead. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We harbored at Syracuse for three days, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>then sailed around to Rhegium. A day later, a south wind came up, and the second day we made it to Puteoli. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We found disciples there, who invited us to stay with them for a week. And so we got to Rome. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From there disciples heard about us and came to meet us as far as Three Taverns and The Forum of Appius. When Paul saw them, he thanked God and took heart. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When we went into Rome, <i>the centurion</i> let Paul stay by himself with a soldier guarding him.     </span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the Jews objected, I had to appeal to Caesar, though I didn’t have anything against my nation. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I asked to speak to you because I’m wearing this chain on account of Israel’s hope.”</span></i></span></p>
