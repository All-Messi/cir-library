## Acts Chapter 27


### Paul’s Voyage to Rome


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they decided we would sail to Italy, they turned Paul and some other prisoners over to Julius, a centurion of the Augustan cohort. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We boarded a ship out of Adramyttium, ready to sail to places along the coast of Asia. We put out to sea along with Aristarchus, a Macedonian from Thessalonica.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day we harbored at Sidon. Julius was nice to Paul and let him go to friends and refresh himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We launched from there and sailed under the shelter of Cyprus because the winds were against us. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After we navigated the open sea along the coast of Cilicia and Pamphylia, we landed at Myra in Lycia. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The centurion found an Alexandrian ship there that was sailing for Italy and put us on board.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For a number of days, we sailed slowly, and barely made it to Cnidus. Since the wind didn’t let us sail under the shelter of Crete along Salmone, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>we drifted along it with difficulty and came to a place called Fair Havens near the city of Lasea.                                                                                                 </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When considerable time had passed and the voyage was getting risky—even the Fast <i>[Yom Kippur] </i>was past, Paul started advising them, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Men, I sense the voyage will involve damage and considerable loss of cargo, the ship, and our lives.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the centurion listened more to the captain and the ship owner than to Paul. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Because the harbor wasn’t suitable for wintering, the majority decided to sail from there if they could just get to Phoenix—a harbor at Crete facing southwest and northwest—and winter there.   </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 27:1-12</p>


### The Storm at Sea


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When a gentle breeze came up, they supposed they’d achieved their purpose. They weighed anchor and started sailing along Crete as close to shore as they could.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But not long afterwards, a nor’easter called Euraquilo rushed down from land </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and caught the ship. It couldn’t face the gale; so we gave way to it and scudded along. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We ran under a small island called Cauda and were barely able to secure the dinghy. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After they hoisted it up, they used cables to gird up the ship. They were afraid they might run aground on Syrtis, so they let down the sail and drifted along. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day, the storm tossed us violently, and <i>the crew</i> began to throw cargo overboard*. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The third day, they threw the ship’s tackle overboard by hand. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the sun and stars didn’t appear for several days, and the monstrous storm bore down on us, we lost all hope of surviving.       </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 27:13-20</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they’d gone a long time without eating, Paul stood up among them, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Men, you should’ve listened to me and not sailed from Crete and incurred this damage and loss. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now I urge you to take heart. There won’t be any loss of life, just the ship. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Last night an angel of the God I serve stood by me, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>‘Don’t be afraid, Paul; you need to stand in front of Caesar, and God has granted you everybody that’s sailing with you.’ </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So take heart; I believe it will turn out like he said, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>but we have to run aground on an island.”</span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 27:21-26</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The fourteenth night, the storm was driving us around in the <i>Sea of</i> Hadria. About midnight the sailors sensed we were nearing land, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>so they took a sounding and found the depth was 120 feet. A little farther on, another sounding showed 90 feet. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Fearing we might run aground on a rocky coast, they cast four anchors out the stern and longed for daylight. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The sailors tried to jump ship by lowering its dinghy into the water on the pretense of laying out anchors from the bow. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Paul told the centurion and <i>his</i> soldiers, <i>“If those </i>sailors<i> don’t stay on the ship, you<sup>pl</sup> won’t make it.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The soldiers cut the ropes to the dinghy and let it fall off.               </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At early dawn, Paul started urging everybody to eat, <i>“Today makes two weeks you’ve been on edge, not eating. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Eat for your survival. Not a hair of your head will perish.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he’d said that, he took a loaf, thanked God in front of them all, broke it up, and starting eating. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They took heart and started eating too. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There were 276 of us on board. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they’d eaten enough, they lightened the ship by throwing the wheat overboard.</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 27:27-38</p>


### The Shipwreck on Malta


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When it got daylight, they didn’t recognize the land, but they noticed a creek with a beach. They decided, if possible, to save the ship. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They cut the anchors loose and left them in the sea. At the same time, they took loose the ropes of the rudders, hoisted the foresail to the wind, and headed for the beach. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But they struck a mud bar where two currents met. The prow stuck fast and wouldn’t budge, and the force of the waves started tearing the stern apart. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v42" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The soldiers intended to kill the prisoners so none of them could swim away and escape. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v43" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the centurion wanted to take Paul safely through and stopped them. He told the ones that could swim to jump overboard and get to land first. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v44" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest would float there on planks or items from the ship. That way everybody got to shore.                             </span></span></p>
