## Acts Chapter 9


### Saul’s Conversion


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul was still making murderous threats against the Lord’s disciples. He went to the high priest </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and asked for letters to synagogues in Damascus so, if he found any belonging to The Way—men or women, he could bring them bound to Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As he was getting close to Damascus, all of a sudden, a light from the sky shined around him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He fell to the ground and heard a voice, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting me?”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said, <i>“Who are you, Sir?”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <i>“I’m Jesus that you’re persecuting. </i></span><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Get up and go into the city. Someone will tell you what to do.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The men traveling with him stood speechless. They heard the voice but didn’t see anyone. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul got up; and when he opened his eyes, he couldn’t see. They took him by the hand into Damascus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For three days he couldn’t see and didn’t eat or drink anything.           </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 9:1-9</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There was a disciple in Damascus named Ananias. The Lord said to him, <i>“Ananias.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            “Yes, Lord.”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Go to Straight Street and ask for Saul of Tarsus in Judas’ house. He’s praying </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and has seen a man named Ananias coming in and laying hands on him so he could see.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ananias answered,<i> “Lord, I’ve heard from lots of people how many evil things he’s done in Jerusalem. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He has authority from the chief priests to bind everybody here that calls on you.”
</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Lord said, <i>“Go on; I’ve chosen him to carry my name to Gentiles, kings, and Israelites. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ll show him how much <u>he</u> has to suffer for me.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ananias went into Judas’ house, laid hands on him, and said, <i>“Brother Saul, the Lord Jesus that appeared to you on the road has sent me so you can see again and be filled with the Holy Spirit.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Immediately scales, as it were, fell from his eyes, and he could see again. He got up and was baptized. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After he ate, he regained his strength.                                                   </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 9:10-19a</p>


### Saul Preaches in Damascus


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>             He was with disciples in Damascus for some days. </span><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Right away in the synagogues, he started presenting Jesus as God’s Son. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The ones that heard him were amazed, <i>“Isn’t he the one that executed people in Jerusalem for calling on this Name and has come here to lead them bound to the chief priests?”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Paul grew stronger and confounded the Jews in Damascus, proving that this was the Messiah.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After a while the Jews plotted to kill him, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>but he found out about it. They were watching the gates day and night. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>During the night his disciples lowered him in a big basket through <i>an opening in </i>the wall.                          </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 9:19b-25</p>


### Jewish Plot Against Saul


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he got back to Jerusalem, he tried to associate with the disciples, but they were afraid of him. They didn’t believe he’d become a disciple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Barnabas took him to the apostles, and told them how he’d seen the Lord, how the Lord had spoken to him, how he’d presented the Lord boldly in Damascus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul was moving freely among them in Jerusalem, presenting the Lord boldly. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was disputing with the Hellenistic Jews, who were trying to get hold of him to kill him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the brothers found out, took him down to Caesarea, and sent him to Tarsus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then, all over Judaea, Galilee, and Samaria the church had peace and multiplied. They were living with respect for the Lord and comfort from the Holy Spirit.                                                                                                                                          </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 9:26-31</p>


### Peter Heals Aeneas


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As Peter circulated through the region, he went down to the saints in Lydda. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He found a man there named Aeneas, who’d been confined to for bed eight years paralyzed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Peter said, <i>“Aeneas, Jesus the Messiah is healing you. Get up and make your bed.”</i> Immediately he stood up. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Everyone in Lydda and Sharon saw him, and turned to the Lord.</span></span></p>


### Peter Resurrects Dorcas


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In Joppa a disciple named Tabitha (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Dorcas</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) was always doing good things for people and giving gifts to those in need. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>She got sick and died. They bathed her and laid her in an upstairs room. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Lydda was close to Joppa, and the disciples heard that Peter was there. They sent two men, <i>“Come right away.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Peter went with them. When he got there, they took him upstairs. The widows stood beside him mourning and showing him shirts and <i>other </i>clothes Dorcas had made for them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Peter had them step out; he knelt and prayed, and turned to the body, <i>“Tabitha, get up.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When she opened her eyes and saw Peter, she sat up. </span><span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He gave her his hand and raised her up. He called the saints and widows and presented her alive. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v42" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The news spread all over Joppa, and many came to believe in the Lord. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v43" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For some time, Peter stayed in Joppa with Simon the tanner.                                                                                                                                           </span></span></p>
