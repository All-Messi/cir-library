## Acts Chapter 18


### Paul’s Ministry in Corinth


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After that, he left Athens for Corinth. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He found a Jew named Aquila, a native from Pontus, who was recently displaced from Italy with his wife Priscilla. Claudius had ordered all Jews to leave Rome. <i>Paul</i> went to them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Since he had the same trade, he stayed with them and worked because they were leatherworkers. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He reasoned in the synagogue every Sabbath in an effort to persuade Jews and Greeks.       </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 18:1-4</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Silas and Timothy came down from Macedonia, Paul started devoting himself to the message. He was solemnly telling the Jews that Jesus was the Messiah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they opposed and slandered <i>him</i>, he shook <i>the dust from</i> his clothes <i>at them</i>, <i>“Your blood’s on your own heads; I’m innocent. From now on I’m going to the Gentiles.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He moved from there to the house of Titius Justus, a God-fearer whose house was next to the synagogue. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Crispus, the synagogue ruler, believed in the Lord along with his household. Many Corinthians heard and believed and were baptized. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At night the Lord told Paul in a vision, <i>“Don’t be afraid. Speak out </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because I’m with you; nobody will harm you. I have lots of people in this city.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For a year and a half he settled there and taught God’s message.                                                                                               </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 18:5-11</p>


### Paul Before Gallio


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>While Gallio was proconsul of Achaia, the Jews as a group rose up against Paul and took him to the judgment seat, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“This man is persuading people to worship God contrary to </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>our<i> Law.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Paul was about to speak up when Gallio told them, <i>“Jews, if it were some crime or fraud he committed, I’d have a reason to hear your case. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But if there are questions about words, names, and your Law, see to it yourselves. I’m not willing to render judgment on such things.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He made them leave the judgment seat. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The bystanders grabbed Sosthenes, the synagogue ruler, and started beating him in front of the judgment seat. Gallio didn’t concern himself with any of that.                                                              </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 18:12-17</p>


### Paul Returns to Antioch


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Paul stayed quite a few more days, said goodbye to the converts, and set sail for Syria with Priscilla and Aquila. He cut his hair in Cenchrea to keep a vow. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They arrived at Ephesus, and Paul left them there while he went into the synagogue and dialogued with the Jews. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Although they asked him to stay longer, he declined, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ll come back, God willing”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>; and sailed from Ephesus.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he landed at Caesarea, he went up and greeted the church and went down to Antioch. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After he spent some time there, he left and passed through Galatia and Phrygia, strengthening the disciples.                                  </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 18:18-23</p>


### Apollos Preaches at Ephesus


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A Jew named Apollos, born in Alexandria, an eloquent speaker, arrived at Ephesus. He knew the scripture well. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He’d been orally instructed in the way of the Lord. An enthusiastic man, he was teaching accurately things about Jesus, but he only knew about John’s baptism. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He began to speak boldly in the synagogue. When Priscilla and Aquila heard him, they took him aside and presented the way of God to him more completely. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he wanted to cross over to Greece, the disciples encouraged him and wrote letters to them to welcome him. When he arrived, he greatly helped the ones that believed by <i>God’s</i> grace. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He powerfully refuted the Jews in public and showed by the scriptures that Jesus was the Messiah.                                                                                                                          </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 18:24-28</p>
