### Paul Kept in Custody


<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Because he knew more accurately about The Way, Felix put them off, <em>“When Lysias the tribune comes down, I’ll decide your case.”</em> <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>He ordered the centurion to keep him in custody but give him some freedom and let his friends help him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>After some time, Felix with Drusilla his wife, a Jewess, sent for Paul and was listening to him about faith in Jesus the Messiah. <strong><sup>25</sup></strong>As he talked about right living, self-control, and judgment to come, Felix became uneasy, <em>“Go away for now; I’ll call you when I have time.”</em> <strong><sup>26</sup></strong>Besides, he was hoping Paul would give him a bribe; so he would send for him often and talk to him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup>After two years, Porcius Festus succeeded Felix. To gain favor with the Jews, Felix left Paul behind in prison.</span>
