## Acts Chapter 17


### Paul in Thessalonica


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They traveled through Amphipolis and Apollonia to Thessalonica. A Jewish synagogue was there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As usual, Paul went in and for three Sabbaths reasoned with them from the scriptures. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He showed from predictions that the Messiah had to suffer and arise from the dead, and that, <i>“This Jesus I’m presenting to you is the Messiah.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Some were persuaded and joined Paul and Silas—a good number of God-fearers and prominent women. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Jews got jealous and took some riffraff from the marketplace to form a mob. They set the city in an uproar and assaulted Jason’s house, intending to bring <i>Paul and Silas</i> out to the public assembly. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they didn’t find them, they dragged Jason and some <i>other </i>converts to the city authorities, shouting, <i>“These insurgents against the empire elsewhere have come here too. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jason has welcomed them. They act contrary to Caesar’s decrees, saying there’s another king—Jesus.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That stirred up the crowd and the authorities, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>who made Jason and the rest post bond, then released them.       </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 17:1-9</p>


### Paul in Berea


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That night their converts sent Paul and Silas to Berea. When they arrived, they went into the Jews’ synagogue. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>These Jews were more open-minded than the ones in Thessalonica. They welcomed the message and examined the scriptures daily to check these things out. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Many of them believed, together with quite a few prominent Greek men and women.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Jews from Thessalonica heard Paul was presenting God’s message in Berea, they went there, too, and upset the crowds. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Right away the converts sent Paul away as far as the sea, but Silas and Timothy stayed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The ones that went with Paul conducted him to Athens and brought back word for Silas and Timothy to join him as soon as they could.                </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 17:10-15</p>


### Paul Goes to Athens


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>While Paul waited for them in Athens, seeing the city full of idols upset him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He reasoned with the Jews and God-fearers in the synagogues and every day with people in the marketplace. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Some Epicurean and Stoic philosophers were bantering with him, <i>“What’s this scavenger trying to say?”</i> Others commented, <i>“He seems to be presenting strange gods” </i>(</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because he was presenting Jesus and the resurrection</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).   </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They escorted him to the Areopagus, <i>“Can we know what this new teaching is you’re talking about? </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You’re bringing some strange things to us. We want to know what they mean.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> (</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Athenians and foreign visitors typically spent their time telling or hearing something new.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 17:16-21</p>


### Paul’s Sermon on the Areopagus


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Paul stood up in the Areopagus and said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Athenians, I observe that you’re quite religious in every way. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As I was looking at your objects of worship, I ran across an altar inscribed, ‘To the Unknown God.’ What I’m presenting to you then is what you worship without knowing him. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The God who made the world and everything in it, is L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> of earth and sky; </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>so he doesn’t live in handmade sanctuaries. People don’t serve him as if he needs anything; he <u>gives</u> everybody life, breath, and everything. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He made the whole human race with one nature to live all over the world, and appointed their times when and boundaries where they live. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>People are to look for God if by chance they grope around and find him, though he’s not far from any of us.                      </span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 17:22-27</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        </span></i><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We live and move and exist in him, like some of your poets have said,</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                    ‘We’re his offspring.’</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Since we’re God’s offspring, we shouldn’t think of deity as like gold or silver or stone or an image made from human art and imagination. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God has overlooked the times of ignorance, but now he tells people everywhere to repent. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So he has established a time to judge the world in regard to goodness as measured by the man he’s appointed. He’s given assurance to everybody by resurrecting him.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they heard “resurrection,” some made fun of it; others said, <i>“We’ll hear you again about this.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So Paul left them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Some <i>listeners</i> joined him and believed. Among them was Dionysius the Areopagite, a woman named Damaris, and some others.         </span></span></p>
