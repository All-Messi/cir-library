## Acts Chapter 21


### Paul Continues to Jerusalem


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When we’d left them, we sailed directly to Cos, the next day to Rhodes, and from there to Patara. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We found a ship crossing over to Phoenicia, boarded it, and set sail. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We came within sight of Cyprus; we passed it on the left, kept sailing to Syria, and landed at Tyre, where the ship was to unload its cargo. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We looked up the disciples and stayed there a week. They kept telling Paul by the Spirit not to go to Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When our time there was over, we started our trip. With their wives and children, they escorted us outside the city. After kneeling on the beach and praying, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>we said goodbye to one another. We got on the ship, and they went back home.                           </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 21:1-6</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When we’d finished the voyage from Tyre and to Ptolemais, we greeted the disciples and stayed with them for a day. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day we reached Caesarea and went into the house of Philip the evangelist—one of the seven—and stayed with him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He had four virgin daughters that prophesied.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>During our several days there, a prophet from Judaea named Agabus came down. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He took Paul’s belt and tied up his own hands and feet, and said, <i>“This is what the Holy Spirit says, ‘The man this belt belongs to, the Jews in Jerusalem are going to tie up and deliver to Gentiles.’” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When we heard it, we and the locals urged and urged Paul not to go up.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He answered, <i>“What are you doing, crying and breaking my heart? I’m ready to be bound and even to die in Jerusalem for the Lord Jesus.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When we couldn’t change his mind, we quit <i>trying</i>, <i>“The Lord’s will be done.”   </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After those days we got ready and went up to Jerusalem </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>along with some disciples from Caesarea. They took us up to Mnason of Cyprus, a long-time disciple we would stay with.</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 21:7-16</p>


### Paul Visits James


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The believers in Jerusalem welcomed us warmly. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day Paul went in with us to see James, and the elders were there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After he’d greeted them, he recounted one thing after another that God had done among the Gentiles through his ministry. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The ones that heard it, began to praise God, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You see, how many thousands among the <u>Jews</u> believe, all zealous for the Law.
</span></i><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>People have told them that you teach the Jews among the Gentiles to leave Moses, not circumcise their children, or keep the customs. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So what can we do? They’ll certainly find out you’re here. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We have four men under a vow. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Take them and purify yourself with them; sponsor them so they’ll shave their heads. Everybody will know there’s nothing to what people have told them about you, but that you live orderly and keep the Law. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As to <u>Gentile</u> believers, we wrote that they should abstain from meat offered to idols, blood, things strangled, and sexual immorality.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day Paul took the men, purified himself with them, went into the Temple, and gave notice that they’d completed <i>their</i> days of purification, till the sacrifice for each one was offered.                                                                                                     </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 21:17-26</p>


### Paul’s Arrest in the Temple


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Near the end of the seven days, Jews from Asia saw him in the Temple and stirred up the crowd and attacked him, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Men of Jerusalem, help. This is the man that teaches everybody against the people, the Law, and this place. Now he’s brought Gentiles into the Temple and profaned this holy place.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Previously they’d seen Trophimus the Ephesian in the city with Paul and supposed he’d taken him into the Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The city erupted into an uproar. The people ran together, grabbed Paul, and dragged him out of the Temple. Immediately they shut the doors. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>While they were trying to kill him, a report went up to the tribune of the Roman cohort that Jerusalem was in an uproar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At once he took soldiers and centurions and ran down to them. When they saw the tribune and the soldiers, they stopped beating Paul. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The tribune came and took him and told <i>the soldiers</i> to bind him with two chains. He began asking who he was and what he’d done. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Some in the crowd shouted one thing, some another. He couldn’t find out the truth because of the uproar, so he told <i>the soldiers</i> to take him into the barracks. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When <i>Paul</i> got on the stairs, the soldiers carried him because of the crowd violence. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The people were following, yelling, <i>“Away with him!”  </i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 21:27-36</p>


### Paul Speaks to the Tribune


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At the entrance to the barracks, Paul said to the tribune, <i>“Can I speak to you?”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said, <i>“You know Greek?</i> </span><span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So you’re not the Egyptian that master-minded a revolt some time back and led the 4,000 Assassins out into the desert?”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Paul said, <i>“I’m a Jew from Tarsus in Cilicia, a citizen of a prominent city. Please, let me speak to the people.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>With his permission, Paul stood on the stairs and motioned to the people. After a great hush settled in, he spoke in Hebrew.    </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Acts 21:37-40</p>
