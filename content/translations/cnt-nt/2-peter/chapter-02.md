## 2 Peter Chapter 2


### Watch Out for False Teachers


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As false prophets arose among the people before, false teachers will arise among <u>you</u>. Under false pretenses they’ll bring in destructive heresies. They’ll even deny the Master who bought them and bring swift destruction on themselves. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Many will follow their sensual practices, and people will slander the true Way because of them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In their greed they’ll exploit you with <i>their </i>made-up ideas. Their sentence from of old won’t delay; their destruction won’t sleep.                                                                                   </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Pet 2:1-3</p>


### God’s Judgment on Evil


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God didn’t spare angels that sinned; he imprisoned them with chains in the dark underworld [Tartarus] to hold them for judgment. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God didn’t spare the ancient world; he preserved Noah, one of eight, a man who preached righteousness when <i>God</i> brought a flood on the ungodly world. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God destroyed Sodom and Gomorrah and reduced them to ashes. He made an example out of them for ungodly people later on. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But he rescued righteous Lot; their corrupt conduct weighed on that good man. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>While he lived among them, the immoral deeds he saw and heard about day after day tormented him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, the Lord knows how to rescue godly people from temptation and keep ungodly ones under punishment till judgment day—</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>especially those who chase after physical lusts and despise authority.                        </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Pet 2:4-10a</p>


### Evil People Described


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Daring and arrogant, they rail at angels without qualms. </span><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But angels, though more powerful, don’t rail out condemnations at <u>them</u> in front of the Lord. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>People like that </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>are like wild animals that can’t reason, creatures that live by instinct from birth who need to be trapped and exterminated. They rail out about things they don’t know. In their destroying, they’ll get destroyed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They’ll suffer wrong as payback for doing wrong. They think it’s fun to carouse in broad daylight. They are stains and blemishes who enjoy deceiving you while they eat with you. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They’re always looking around for someone to commit adultery with. They sin without ceasing, they lure unsteady souls, they have hearts well-practiced in greed. They’re accursed offspring </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>that forsake the right way to follow Balaam son of Beor that loved to make money from his sin. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But a donkey, talking like a person, reprimanded him for it and stopped him from carrying out his madness.    </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Pet 2:10b-16</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They’re springs without water, fog swirled by the wind. It’s pitch dark where they’re going. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They say arrogant, meaningless things, and lure with lust the ones just escaping from error. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They promise freedom while themselves being slaves to corruption. What overcomes people puts them in servitude. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>f they’ve escaped the defilements of worldly living by knowing the Lord and Savior Jesus Christ and then get tangled up in them again, they’re worse off than before. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They’d been better off not to know how to live God’s way, than to know <i>it</i> and turn away from the holy commandment they’d received. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They illustrate the proverb,</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        <b><i>“The dog returned to its vomit”</i></b> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Pro 26:11]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,</span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and,</span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        <i>“the washed sow went back to wallowing in the mud.”                          </i></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Pet 2:17-22</p>
