## 1 Corinthians Chapter 9


### Paul’s Defense of His Apostleship


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Am I not free? Am I not an apostle? Haven’t I seen Jesus our Lord? Aren’t you my work in the Lord? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If I’m not an apostle to other people, I am to you. You’re the seal of my apostleship in the Lord.                                                                                                        </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Here’s my defense to the ones who are criticizing me. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t I have a right to eat? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t I have a right to have a wife </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>that’s</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> a sister </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>in the faith</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> like Cephas, the rest of the apostles, and the Lord’s brothers? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Are Barnabas and I the only ones that don’t have a right not to work? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>What soldier ever serves at his own expense? Who plants a vineyard and doesn’t eat its fruit? Who tends sheep and doesn’t drink their milk? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’m not just talking about human custom, am I? Doesn’t the Law </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>of Moses</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> say that too? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t put a muzzle on an ox while it’s</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>threshing”</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Deut 25:4]</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>.</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> God’s not concerned </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>here</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> about oxen, is he? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Isn’t he speaking for <u>our</u> benefit? He wrote it for our benefit so the one who’s threshing should work in hopes of sharing in the crops. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If we sowed spiritual things to you, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>is it</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> too much to reap material things from you? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If other people share that right, don’t we e</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ven</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> more?                                                                                                       </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">1 Cor 9:1-12a</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But we haven’t used that right. We endure things to keep from hindering the good news of Christ in any way. </span><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t you know that the ones who perform sacred services eat from the Temple, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and that</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> the ones who wait on the altar have a share from the altar? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the same way the Lord has directed that the ones who deliver the good news should have a living from it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But I haven’t taken advantage of that provision, and I’m not writing this to change what we’ve been doing. I’d rather die than let anyone make my pride hollow. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If I deliver the good news, I don’t have anything to take pride in. I <u>have</u> to do that. Woe to me if I don’t! </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If I do it because I want to, I’m entrusted with a stewardship. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Where’s my reward then? That I deliver the good news for free and not make use of my rights in it.  </span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>To Jews I’ve become like a Jew to win Jews. To people under law I’ve become like somebody under law—though I’m not </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>really</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>—to win people under law. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>To people not under law I’ve become like somebody not under law—though I am under God’s law, Christ’s ‘law’—to win people not under law. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>To the weak I’ve become weak to win the weak. By all means I’ve become all things to all </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>people</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> to save some. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I do everything for the sake of the good news so I can partake of it.                                                             </span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Everybody that competes in the games exercises self-discipline in everything. <u>They</u> do it for a crown that deteriorates; <u>we</u> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>do it</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> for one that doesn’t. </span></span>
