## 2 Corinthians Chapter 3


### Ministry of the New Covenant


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Are we beginning to commend ourselves again? We don’t need letters of recommendation to you or from you like some do, do we? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’re our letter that everybody can know and read, written on our hearts. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You’re an open letter from Christ, served by us, not written with ink, but with the spirit of the living God, not on stone tablets, but on human hearts.       </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We have so much confidence in God through Christ, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>not because <u>we’re</u> adequate to consider anything as our own accomplishment, but adequate because of what comes from God. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He has made us adequate ministers of a new covenant, not of the letter, but of the spirit. The letter deadens; the spirit enlivens.                                                                                 </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The ministry that brought death—engraved with letters on stone—came with so much glory that the Israelites couldn’t look at Moses’ face—a fading glory. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Won’t the ministry of the spirit have <i>even </i>more glory? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If the ministry that brought condemnation had glory, how much more glory does the ministry have that brings goodness. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>What
<i>had</i> glory doesn’t have any in comparison to what <i>has</i> greater glory. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>What faded away had glory, but what doesn’t fade away has a lot more glory.                                                  </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Cor 3:1-11</p>


### Freedom from Law


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>With that kind of hope, let’s be bold. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Let’s not be like Moses, who put a veil on so the Israelites wouldn’t have to look at what was fading away. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14-15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Their minds were insensitive. To this day when they read the Old Covenant, the same veil is there. They don’t understand that Christ<sup>Messiah</sup> removes it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When
<i>their heart</i> turns to the Lord, the veil disappears. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now the Lord <i>is</i> the spirit <i>of the Old Covenant</i>, and where his spirit is, <i>there’s</i> freedom. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When with unveiled face we look at the glory of the Lord in a mirror, the Lord, the spirit, transforms us into that same image from glory to <i>more</i> glory.                                                                                                                  </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Cor 3:12-18</p>
