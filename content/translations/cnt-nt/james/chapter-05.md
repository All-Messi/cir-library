## James Chapter 5


### Futility of Wealth


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>5</span></b><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>:</span></sup><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Come, come, rich people. Cry out loud over the miseries heading your way. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Your wealth has decayed; your clothes are moth-eaten; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>your gold and silver have tarnished. Their tarnish will testify against you and consume you like fire. They’re the ‘wealth’ you’ve laid up for your last days! </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You haven’t paid the workers that mowed your fields. Their complaints have gone up to the Lord of hosts. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You’ve lived in self-indulgence and luxury. You’ve fattened yourselves up at the expense of people you’ve ruined. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You’ve condemned, you’ve murdered the Righteous One; he doesn’t resist you.                                                                                                              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 5:1-6</span></b></span></p>


### Exhortation to Endurance


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So be patient till the Lord comes. A farmer patiently waits for his valuable crop till it gets <i>its</i> early and late rains. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You be patient too; strengthen your hearts because the Lord’s coming soon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t complain about each other or you’ll be complained about; the judge is standing right outside your doors. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Take the <i>L</i></span><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s prophets as models of suffering and endurance. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We consider them blessed that endured. You’ve heard about Job’s patience, and you know the Lord’s compassionate and merciful in the end.          </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 5:7-11</span></b></span></p>


### Dispensing with Oaths


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Above all, don’t swear by the sky, the earth, or anything else. Just say “yes” or “no”; otherwise, you fall under condemnation.            </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 5:12</span></b></span></p>


### Calling the Elders


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Is anybody suffering among you? He should pray. Is anybody happy? He should sing. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Is anybody sick? He should call the church elders. They should pray over him and anoint him with olive oil in the name of the Lord. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Prayer with faith will save the sick, and the Lord will raise them up. If they have sinned, God will forgive them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Admit your sins to each other and pray for each other so you’ll be healed. A good person’s prayer can accomplish a lot. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Elijah was a man like us, and he prayed sincerely that it wouldn’t rain, and it didn’t for three and a half years. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He prayed again and it rained, and the ground grew its crops.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                        James 5:13-18</span></b></span></p>


### Returning the Stray


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Fellow believers, if somebody wanders off from the truth and somebody gets him turned around, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>he can be sure that returning a sinner from error will save him from death and cover a host of sins.                                      </span><b><span style='font-size: 10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 5:19-20</span></b></span></p>
