## James Chapter 2


### No Discrimination Against the Poor


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2</span></b><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>:</span></sup><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Fellow Christians, don’t discriminate against people while claiming to have faith in our glorious Lord Jesus Christ. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If a man comes into your assembly dressed nice with a gold ring on his finger, and a poor man comes in wearing shabby clothes, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and you give preference to the one in fine clothes and say, <i>“You sit here in a nice place,”</i> and you say to the poor man, <i>“You stand over there,”</i> or
<i>“Sit by my footstool,”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>haven’t you discriminated and made judgments based on bad motives?</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                              James 2:1-4</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Listen, hasn’t God chosen poor people to be rich in faith and inherit the kingdom he promised to people that care about him? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But you’ve looked down on poor people. Don’t rich people bully you and take you to court? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t they slander the good name you wear? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But, you do well to fulfill the royal law according to the scripture, <b><i>“Love your neighbor as yourself”</i></b> </span><b><span style='font-size:8.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Lev 19:18].</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If you discriminate, you sin, and the Law convicts you of being a sinner. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Whoever keeps the whole Law but stumbles in one point, has become liable to the Law <i>as such</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The One that said,
<b><i>“Do not commit adultery”</i></b> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Ex 20:14; Deut 5:18]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> is the same One that said, <b><i>“Do not murder”</i></b> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Ex 20:13; Deut 5:17]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. If you don’t commit adultery, but commit murder, you’re still a law breaker. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Speak and do like people that are evaluated by a “law” of liberty. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Condemnation is without mercy to the one that hasn’t shown mercy. Showing mercy is better than criticizing.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                 James 2:5-13</span></b></span></p>


### Doing
vs. Believing


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>What good is it to say you believe but don’t do? Believing can’t save you, can it? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> When a brother or sister needs clothes or daily food, what good is it </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>if somebody says, <i>“Have a good day; keep warm and eat well,”</i> but doesn’t give them what they need? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s the same way if belief doesn’t do anything; it’s dead by itself.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                         James 2:14-17</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Somebody might say, <i>“<u>You</u> have belief; <u>I</u> have deeds.”</i> Show me your belief without doing anything, and I’ll show you my belief by what I do. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You believe there’s one God; that’s nice. Demons believe <i>that</i> too—and tremble. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Can’t you see that believing without doing is useless? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Wasn’t our ancestor Abraham justified by what he did when he sacrificed his son Isaac?
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Belief combined with his deeds; his deeds completed his belief. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He fulfilled the scripture that said, <b><i>“Abraham believed God, and God considered him righteous”</i></b> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Gen 15:6]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,<b>
</b>and he was called “God’s friend” </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[<b>2 Ch 20:7; Is 41:8]</b></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That shows we’re justified by what we do, not simply by what we believe. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The same with Rahab the prostitute; wasn’t she justified by what she did when she received the messengers and sent them out a different way? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As the body is dead if it doesn’t breathe, believing is dead if it doesn’t do anything.                                                                    </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 2:18-26</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>                                                    </span></span></p>
