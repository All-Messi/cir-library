## James Chapter 4


### Effects of Self-Centeredness


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>4</span></b><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>:</span></sup><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Where do conflicts and disagreements come from? Don’t they come from pleasures at conflict inside you? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You want and don’t have, you kill and covet and can’t get, you quarrel and fight and don’t obtain because you don’t ask. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When you do ask, you don’t receive because you ask with the wrong motive: <i>you ask
</i>so you can spend it on your pleasures. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Adulterers, you know, don’t you, that friendship with the world amounts to hostility toward God? So whoever wants to be friendly with the world makes himself hostile toward God. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Do you suppose the scripture says for no reason, <i>“The Spirit he put among us yearns to receive exclusive loyalty”?</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He gives more grace, <b><i>“God resists the proud, but he gives grace to the humble”</i></b> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Pro 3:34 LXX]</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.<b><i>
</i></b></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So submit to God. Resist the devil, and he’ll run from you. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Come close to God, and he’ll come close to you. Clean <i>your</i> hands, you sinners; purify <i>your</i> hearts, you with divided loyalties. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Be sad, mourn, and cry. Turn your laughing into mourning, and your lightheartedness into seriousness. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Humble yourselves in front of the Lord, and he’ll lift you up.              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 4:1-10</span></b></span></p>


### Ban on Criticism


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t gossip about each other. When you criticize your brother or sister, you’re acting like a judge. If you do that, you go from being a doer of the law to serving as a judge for the law. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There is one lawgiver and judge that can acquit or execute. Who are you to serve as a judge over your neighbor?                                                                                                               </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 4:11-12</span></b></span></p>


### Recognizing Your Dependent Place


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Come, come, now, you that say, <i>“Today or tomorrow we’ll go to such and such a city, spend a year, carry on business, make money.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You don’t know what tomorrow holds. What’s your life but a vapor that appears for a while and vanishes? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You should say, <i>“The Lord willing, we’ll live and do this or that.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But you take pride in your bragging; that’s not right. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If you know to do good and don’t do it, you’re sinning.                                                                                                                                              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James 4:13-17</span></b></span></p>
