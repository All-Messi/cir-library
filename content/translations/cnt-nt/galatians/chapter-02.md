## Galatians Chapter 2


### Opposition to the Judaizers


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Fourteen years later, I went up to Jerusalem again, that time with Barnabas, and took Titus along. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I<i> </i>went up, directed by revelation, to lay out for them the message I was presenting to Gentiles. I met privately with the recognized leaders so I wouldn't be running or had run for nothing. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They didn’t consider it necessary to circumcise Titus, a Gentile. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That trip came about</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> because false believers had slipped in to spy out the freedom we have in Christ Jesus and to enslave us </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>to the Law</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>To maintain the truth about the good news, we didn’t back down for one minute. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The recognized leaders </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>in Jerusalem</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> didn’t contribute anything to me. (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Who they were didn’t matter. God doesn’t pay any attention to that.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Instead, they saw that God had entrusted me with presenting the good news to the uncircumcised like he’d entrusted Peter with presenting it to the circumcised. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The One at work in Peter’s apostleship was at work in mine for the Gentiles. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>James, Cephas, and John—recognized pillars—shook hands with me and Barnabas to go to the Gentiles. They’d go to the Circumcision. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The only thing was, they wanted us to remember the poor, and I was eager to do that.                                                                                                                                 </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gal 2:1-10</p>


### Paul Confronts Cephas (Peter)


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Cephas came to Antioch, I confronted him because he’d condemned himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Before some men came from James, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[the Lord’s brother],</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> he’d been eating with the Gentiles. But when they got there, he started separating himself because he was afraid of the Circumcision. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the Jews joined him in that hypocrisy—even Barnabas got caught up in it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When I saw they weren’t conforming to the true message, I said to Cephas, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“If you’re a Jew but live like a Gentile, how is it that you require Gentiles to live like Jews? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We’re naturally Jews, not Gentile sinners. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We know that no person’s made good by doing the Law, but by trusting in Christ. We’ve put trust in Christ Jesus so trusting <u>him</u> can justify us. What people do under <u>law</u> doesn’t make them good. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But if while we’re trying to be good in Christ, we turn out to be sinners, does that make Christ a minister of sin? Goodness, no! </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If I rebuild what I used to tear down, I make myself a sinner. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>By the Law I died to law so I could live to God. I’ve been crucified with Christ. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s not me that’s living, but Christ that’s living in me. What I live now in the flesh, I live by trusting God’s Son, who loved me and gave himself for me. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I don’t cancel out God’s grace. If law can make us good, there was no reason for Christ<sup>Messiah</sup> to die.                                                                                                                                             </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gal 2:11-21</p>
