## Galatians Chapter 3


### Law
vs. Trust


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Foolish Galatians! Who cast a spell on you? Right in front of your eyes I portrayed Jesus Christ crucified. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>This is what<b> </b>I’d like to know: did you receive the Spirit by working under <u>law</u> or by trusting the <u>Person</u> you listened to? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Are you foolish enough to start with spirit and try to finish with flesh? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Did you suffer so much for nothing—if it was for nothing? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Does the one that provides you the Spirit and works miracles among you </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>do it</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> by working under law or trusting the Person he listened to? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In that way, Abraham </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“<u>trusted</u> God, and God <u>considered</u> him good”</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Gen 15:6]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.<b>
                                                                                                                                                                                                                      </b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gal 3:1-6</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Rest assured, then, the ones who “trust” are like Abraham. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The scripture looked forward to when God would make Gentiles good by trusting. So it “presented the good news” to Abraham, </span><b><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“In you I will bless all the nations”</span></i></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><b><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Gen 12:3; 18:18]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.<b>
</b></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So God blesses people that trust like Abraham did. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Those who work under law are under a curse: scripture says, </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Everybody’s under a curse that doesn’t do everything all the time that the Law requires”</span></i></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Deut 27:26]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>.<b> </b></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Obviously, law can’t make anyone good in front of God:</span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> <b>“A good person will have life by trusting”</b></span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Hab 2:4]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Law doesn’t involve trusting: </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“The one that <u>does</u> them is the one that has life by them”</span></i></b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Lev 18:5]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.</span><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Christ redeemed us from the curse that law brings us under by becoming a curse on our behalf. Scripture says, </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Everybody’s under a curse that hangs on a tree”</span></i></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Deut 21:23]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So the blessing associated with Abraham can come to Gentiles in Christ Jesus; and by trusting, we can receive the Spirit Christ promised </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>us.
    </span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gal 3:7-14</p>


### Law
vs. Promise


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Take an example from everyday life. Even with a human covenant, once it’s ratified, nobody annuls it or adds to it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God made promises to Abraham and his seed. The text doesn’t say “seeds,” plural, but “seed,” singular, </span><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“to your seed”</span></i></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>[Gen 12:7; 13:15; 17:7; 24:7]</span></b><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,</span><span style='font-size:8.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>that is</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, Christ. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Here’s the point: the Law, which came 430 years after the promise, didn’t annul </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the
</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>promise;
</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>it didn’t </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>annul the covenant God had put in place earlier. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If inheritance comes by law, it doesn’t come by promise. But God gave it to Abraham by </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>making a</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> promise. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So, what was the Law for? God added it to deal with sins till the “seed” came that he made </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>his </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>promise to. Angels ordained the Law through a mediator. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A mediator mediates between two parties, not one; but God is </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>only</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> one party.                                                                </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gal 3:15-20</p>


### Law Prepares for Trust


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So, does the Law conflict with God’s promise? Goodness, no! If there could’ve been a law that gave life, the Law would make people good. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But scripture locks up everything under sin so trusting could </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>directly</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> deliver the promise to the ones who trust </span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the promise</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Till trust came into play, law held us in custody. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It served as an attendant to lead </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>us</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> to Christ so trusting in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>him</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> could make us good. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now that trust has come, we aren’t under an attendant anymore. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We’re God’s children by trusting in Christ Jesus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Those of us who were baptized into Christ have put on Christ. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jew-Greek, slave-free, male-female is irrelevant. We’re all alike in Christ Jesus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If we’re Christ’s, we’re Abraham’s seed<sup>sg</sup> and heirs by promise.                                                                            </span></span></p>
