<h3 class="topic-header">The Birth of John Announced</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(<span class="gospel-lk">Lk</span> 1:5-25)</p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">	5</span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>At the time of Herod, king of Judaea, there was a priest named Zachariah from the Abijah division. His wife Elizabeth descended from Aaron. <sup class="verse-num">6</sup>They were both righteous before God and conducted themselves blamelessly by his commandments. <sup class="verse-num">7</sup>Elizabeth couldn’t have children, and both were getting old.</strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>	<sup class="verse-num">8</sup>Zachariah was carrying out his priestly duties before God in the sequence of his division. <sup class="verse-num">9</sup>He’d been chosen by lot—according to the custom of the priestly office—to go in the sanctuary of the Lord to offer incense. <sup class="verse-num">10</sup>A crowd outside was praying. <sup class="verse-num">11</sup>An angel of the Lord appeared to him, standing on the right side of the altar of incense. <sup class="verse-num">12</sup>When Zachariah saw him, fear gripped him. <sup class="verse-num">13</sup>The angel said, </strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“You don’t need to be afraid, Zachariah; God has heard your prayer. Your wife will have a son, and you’re to name him ‘John.’ </em></strong></span><sup class="verse-num">14</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>You’ll be so happy, and many will celebrate his birth. </em></strong></span><sup class="verse-num">15</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’ll be great before the Lord: </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He won’t drink wine or strong beer. </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’ll be full of the Holy Spirit from birth. </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’ll return many Israelites to the Lord their God.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’ll go ahead of the Lord in the spirit and power of Elijah. </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’ll return the hearts of fathers to their children, </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>and the disobedient to the attitude good people have. </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’ll prepare a people for the Lord.” </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup class="verse-num">18</sup>Zachariah said, </strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“How am I going to know this? I’m an old man; my wife is up in years.” </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“I’m Gabriel; I stand beside the Lord. He sent me to bring you this good news. </em></strong></span><sup class="verse-num">20</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>You won’t be able to speak till it happens, because you didn’t believe what I told you; but it will come about in due time.”</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup class="verse-num">21</sup>The people that were waiting for Zachariah couldn’t believe how long he’d been in the sanctuary. <sup class="verse-num">22</sup>When he did come out, he couldn’t talk to them. They suspected he’d seen a vision in the sanctuary. He kept making gestures to them but couldn’t speak.</strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup class="verse-num">23</sup>When his term of service was up, he went back home. <sup class="verse-num">24</sup>Later Elizabeth became pregnant. She hid herself five months, 25</strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“This is how the Lord treated me when he looked at me and took away my disgrace among people.” 	</em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>		      	      </strong></span></p>

<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup class="verse-num">26</sup>In </strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Elizabeth’s</em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong> sixth month, God sent Gabriel to Nazareth, a town in Galilee. <sup class="verse-num">27</sup>He went to a virgin named Mary, who was engaged to Joseph, a descendant of David. <sup class="verse-num">28</sup>He went inside, and said, </strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“Hello, favored one; the Lord’s with you.”</em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong> <sup class="verse-num">29</sup>What he said startled her; she wondered what it meant.</strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>	<sup class="verse-num">30</sup>The angel said, </strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“You don’t need to be afraid, Mary. God’s pleased with you. </em></strong></span><sup class="verse-num">31</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>You’ll be pregnant and have a son; you’re to name him ‘Jesus.’ </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’ll be great; </em></strong></span></p>

<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“The Holy Spirit will come on you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you. <p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>	<sup class="verse-num">38</sup>Mary said, </strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“I’m the Lord’s servant; it can be like you’ve said.”</em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong> </strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>The angel left.								    </strong></span></p>

<h3 class="topic-header">Jesus’ Birth Announced to Joseph</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(<span class="gospel-mt">Mt</span> 1:18-25)</p>
<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup><span class="gospel-mt">This is the way the birth of Jesus Messiah took place. Mary his mother was engaged to Joseph. Before they got married, it was discovered that she was pregnant—by the Holy Spirit. </span><sup class="verse-num">19</sup><span class="gospel-mt">Joseph her husband was a good man and didn’t want to disgrace her publicly; he intended to break off the engagement privately. </span><sup class="verse-num">20</sup><span class="gospel-mt">As he was thinking about it, an angel of the Lord appeared in a dream, </span><span class="gospel-mt"><em>“Joseph, son of David, don’t be afraid to marry her. She’s pregnant by the Holy Spirit. </em></span><sup class="verse-num">21</sup><span class="gospel-mt"><em>She’ll have a son. Name him ‘Jesus,’ because he’ll ‘save’ his people from their sins.” </em></span><sup class="verse-num">22</sup><span class="gospel-mt">This fulfilled what the Lord had said by Isaiah the prophet,</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup><span class="gospel-mt"><strong><em>“A VIRGIN WILL HAVE A SON;</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt"><strong><em>			THEY’LL CALL HIM ‘EMMANUEL’ [‘God with us’]”</em></strong></span><span class="gospel-mt"><strong> [Is 7:14].</strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup><span class="gospel-mt">Joseph got up and did what the angel said, and had no relations with her till she had a son, and he named him “Jesus.”			    			    	    </span></p>
