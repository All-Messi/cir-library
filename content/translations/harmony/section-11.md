<h3 class="topic-header">Sermon on the Level Place</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(<span class="gospel-lk">Lk</span> 6:17-49)</p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>	<sup>17</sup>He came down with them and stood on a level place. There was a big crowd of disciples and a throng of people from Jerusalem, all over Judaea, and the coastal region of Tyre and Sidon. <sup>18</sup>They came to listen to him, and be healed from their diseases and the unclean spirits that were harassing them. <sup>19</sup>The </strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>people in the </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>crowd were trying to touch him, because power went out from him and healed everybody. </strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>	<sup>20</sup>He looked up at his disciples and said, </strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“You that are poor are blessed;</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>	God’s kingdom belongs to you.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>21</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>You that are hungry now are blessed;</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>	you’ll be filled.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>You that mourn now are blessed;</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>you’ll laugh.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>22</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>You’re blessed when people hate you, shun you, insult you, reject you as bad because of the Son of Man. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>23</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Rejoice in times like that.  Jump up and down for joy, because you have a great reward in heaven. Their ancestors used to do the same thing to the prophets.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>24</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>But woe to you rich people;</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>		you have your comfort.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>25</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Woe to you that eat well now;</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>		you’ll be hungry.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>	Woe to you that laugh now;</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>		you’ll grieve and cry.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>26</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Woe </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong>to you </strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>when everybody speaks well of you. Your ancestors used to do the same thing to the false prophets.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>27</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“</em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em><u>I’m</u></em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em> telling you, love your enemies. Treat them well that hate you. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>28</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Bless them that curse you. Pray for them that mistreat you. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>29</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>To the one that slaps you on the cheek offer the other one too; from the one that takes your coat, don’t withhold your shirt. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>30</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Give to anybody that asks you; whoever steals what’s yours, don’t demand it back. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>31</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Treat people the way you want them to treat you. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>32</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>If you love the ones that love you, what credit is that to you? Even sinners love the ones that love them. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>33</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>If you do good to the ones that do good to you, what credit is that to you? Even sinners do that. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>34</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>If you lend to the ones you expect to repay it, what credit is that to you? Even sinners lend to sinners they expect to repay in full. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>35</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>On the contrary, love your enemies. Do good to them and lend to them, expecting nothing in return. You’ll be greatly rewarded, and you’ll be like the Most High, because he’s gracious to ungracious and bad people. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>36</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Be merciful like your Father.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>37</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“Don’t criticize, and you won’t be criticized. Don’t condemn, and you won’t be condemned. Pardon and you’ll be pardoned. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>38</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Give and people will give to you. Good measure, pressed down, shaken together, and running over they’ll give into your lap. The measure you measure with you’ll be measured by.”</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>39</sup>He gave them an illustration</strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>. </em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“A blind man doesn’t lead a blind man, does he? Wouldn’t they both fall in the ditch? </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>40</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>A student is not better off than his teacher, but everybody fully taught will be like his teacher. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>41</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>Why look at a speck of sawdust in your brother’s eye, and not notice the log in your own? </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>42</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>How can you say to your brother, ‘Let me get the speck of sawdust out of your eye,’ when you don’t see the log in yours? Hypocrite, get the log out of your eye first; then you can see clearly to get the speck out of your brother’s eye.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>43</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“A good tree doesn’t bear bad fruit, and a bad tree doesn’t bear good fruit. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>44</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>That’s what being a good or bad tree means—its fruit is good or bad. People don’t gather figs from thorns or grapes from briars. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>45</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>A good man brings good things out of the good treasure in his heart; a bad man brings bad things out of his bad treasure. A man’s mouth speaks from what overflows from his heart.</em></strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>46</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>“Why call me, ‘Lord, Lord,’ and not do what I tell you? </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>47</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>I’ll tell you what everybody’s like that comes to me, and listens to what I say, and does it. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>48</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>He’s like a man that was building a house: he dug down and set the foundation on bedrock. When a flood came, the river swept against that house and couldn’t budge it, because he’d built it right. </em></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>49</sup></strong></span><span class="gospel-lk"><strong><em>The one that listens and doesn’t do is like a man that built his house on dirt with no foundation. The river swept against it and it fell; it was a total loss.”		</em></strong></span>    </p>
