<div id="the-temptation-of-jesus" class="harmony-topic">
<h3 class="topic-header">The Temptation of Jesus</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(Mt 4:1-11; Mk 1:12-13; Lk 4:1-13)</p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-mt">Then </span><span class="gospel-lk">4:1Jesus came away from the Jordan full of the Spirit. The Spirit </span><span class="gospel-mt">took him directly to the desert, and the devil tempted him there for forty days. </span><span class="gospel-lk">The Spirit led him during the forty days </span><span class="gospel-mt">while he was with the wild animals, and angels were helping him.</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-lk">2He didn’t eat anything during that time. When it was over, he was hungry. </span><span class="gospel-mt">3The tempter came, “If you’re God’s Son, tell these stones to become loaves of bread.”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-lk">4Jesus answered, “The scripture says, </span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">		<span class="gospel-mt">‘PEOPLE ARE NOT TO LIVE BY BREAD ALONE,</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">			<span class="gospel-mt">4BUT BY EVERY WORD THAT COMES FROM GOD’S MOUTH’” [Deut 8:3].</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-mt">The devil took him to the Holy City and stood him on the pinnacle of the temple, 6“If you’re God’s Son, jump. The scripture says, </span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">		‘HE’LL COMMAND HIS ANGELS </span><span class="gospel-lk">TO GUARD YOU,</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">	AND TO CARRY YOU IN THEIR HANDS,</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">	SO YOU’LL NEVER TRIP ON A ROCK’ [Ps 91:11-12].</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">	7Jesus said, “Again scripture says, </span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">		‘DON’T TEST THE LORD YOUR GOD’” [Deut 6:16].</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">	8The devil took him to a high mountain and showed him the kingdoms of the world and their splendor </span><span class="gospel-lk">in an instant. </span><span class="gospel-mt">9“If you fall down and worship me, </span><span class="gospel-lk">6I’ll give you all this authority and their splendor. It’s been given to me, and I can give it to whoever I want.”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-mt">10Jesus said, “Go away, Satan. </span><span class="gospel-lk">The scripture says,</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">		‘WORSHIP THE LORD YOUR GOD,</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-mt">			AND </span><span class="gospel-lk">only </span><span class="gospel-mt">SERVE HIM’” [Deut 6:12].</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-lk">13The devil finished tempting him and left till another chance would come. </span><span class="gospel-mt">Angels came to give him support.				      	      </span></p>
</div>

<h3 class="topic-header">John’s Defense of His Ministry</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(Jn 1:19-28)</p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-jn">19This is what John said when Jews from Jerusalem sent priests and Levites to ask him, “Who are you?”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	20“I’m not the Messiah.”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn"> 	21“Are you Elijah, then?”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	“I’m not Elijah.”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	22“Are you ‘the prophet’?”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	“No.”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	“Who are you, so we can answer the ones that sent us? What do you say about yourself?”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	23“I’m the one SHOUTING IN THE DESERT, ‘MAKE THE WAY OF THE LORD STRAIGHT’</span><span class="gospel-mt"> [Is 40:3], </span><span class="gospel-jn">like Isaiah the prophet said.”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	24The Pharisees had sent the ones that came 25to ask him, “Why are you baptizing if you’re not the Messiah, Elijah, or the prophet?”</span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">	26“I’m immersing in water. There’s a man standing among you that you don’t know. 27He’ll succeed me, a man whose sandal strap I’m not worthy to take loose.”		     </span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">28This took place at Bethany on the other side of the Jordan River where John was baptizing. 	     									     </span></p>

<div id="the-messiah" class="harmony-topic">
<h3 class="topic-header">the Messiah</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(Jn 1:29-34)</p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-jn">29The next day he saw Jesus coming, </span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn">“Look, the Lamb of God that takes away the sin of the world! 30He’s the One I was talking about when I said, ‘After me a man’s coming that has come to be before me, because he existed before me.’ 31I didn’t know who he was; but to point him out to Israel, I came immersing people in water. 32I’ve seen the Spirit coming down from the sky like a dove and remaining on him. 33The One that sent me to immerse people in water told me, ‘Whoever you see the Spirit coming down and remaining on is the One that will immerse them in the Holy Spirit.’ 34I’ve seen that, and I’m telling you, this is God’s Son.”	     		</span>     </p>
