<h3 class="topic-header">Luke’s Prologue</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(<span class="gospel-lk">Lk</span> 1:1-4)</p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-lk"><strong><sup>1:1</sup>Quite a number of people have undertaken to put together an account of what’s been fulfilled among us. <sup>2</sup>Eyewitnesses from the beginning and ministers of the message have handed down those events to us. <sup>3</sup>It seemed fitting to me as well, to trace them from the start and write you an orderly account, most excellent Theophilus. <sup>4</sup>That way you can know for sure that your oral instruction was accurate. 			</strong></span></p>

<div id="the-pre-existence-and-incarnation-of-the-messiah" class="harmony-topic">
<h3 class="topic-header">The Pre-Existence and Incarnation of the Messiah</h3>
<p class="topic-reference">(<span class="gospel-jn">Jn</span> 1:1-18)</p>
<p class="harmony-text">	<span class="gospel-jn"><strong><sup>1:1</sup>In the beginning the Word was there. The Word was with God, and the Word was deity. <sup>2</sup>He was in the beginning with God. <sup>3</sup>He made everything; nothing came into existence without him. <sup>4</sup>He had life in himself, life that gave light to mankind. <sup>5</sup>The light shined in the dark, but the darkness tended not to accept it.</strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn"><strong>	<sup>6</sup>God sent a man named John <sup>7</sup>to tell people about the light so everybody would believe through him. <sup>8</sup>He wasn’t the light, but came to talk about <sup>9</sup>the light, the true light that enlightens every person in the world. <sup>10</sup>He was in the world and made the world, but it didn’t know him. <sup>11</sup>He came into his own things, but his own people didn’t accept him. <sup>12</sup>But, to as many as did accept him and trust him, he gave the right to become God’s children. <sup>13</sup>They weren’t born from blood, physical desire, the will of a husband, but from God. <sup>14</sup>The Word took on a physical nature and lived among us for a while. We saw his glory, glory like the only One the Father begot, full of grace and truth.</strong></span></p>
<p class="harmony-text"><span class="gospel-jn"><strong>	<sup>15</sup>John shouted, </strong></span><span class="gospel-jn"><strong><em>“This is the One I was talking about when I said, ‘There’s a man coming after me who has come to be before me, because he existed before me.’”</em></strong></span><span class="gospel-jn"><strong> <sup>16</sup>From his full measure we’ve all received grace on top of grace. <sup>17</sup>Moses gave law, but Jesus Messiah brought grace and truth. <sup>18</sup>Nobody has ever seen God; the only begotten deity—the One dear to the Father—has shown what he’s like.							     </strong></span></p>
</div>
