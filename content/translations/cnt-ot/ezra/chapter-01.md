## Ezra Chapter 1

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>In</strong><strong> the first </strong><strong>year of Cyrus, king of Persia, to fulfill</strong><strong> </strong><strong>his</strong><strong> message through Jeremiah, the </strong><strong>L</strong><strong>ORD</strong><strong> stirred up Cyrus. He sent a written proclamation everywhere in his kingdom:</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> God stirred up the leaders of the tribes from Judah and Benjamin along with the priests and Levites to go to Jerusalem to rebuild his Temple. <strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Their neighbors encouraged them with silver and gold items, with goods, livestock, and valuables, plus freewill offerings. <strong><sup>7</sup></strong>Cyrus brought out the items from the LORD’s Temple that Nebuchadnezzar had carried to the temple of his gods. <strong><sup>8</sup></strong>He had Mithredath the treasurer bring them out and count them out to Sheshbazzar, ruler of Judah: <strong><sup>9</sup></strong>30 gold basins, 1,000 silver basins, 29 censers, <strong><sup>10</sup></strong>30 gold bowls, 410 silver bowls, 1,000 other articles—<strong><sup>11</sup></strong>5,400 gold and silver items in all. Sheshbazzar took them up with the exiles from Babylon to Jerusalem.</span>
