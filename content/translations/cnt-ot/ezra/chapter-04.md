## Ezra Chapter 4


### Enemies Oppose the Rebuilding


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the enemies of Judah and Benjamin heard that the exiles were rebuilding a temple to the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> God of Israel, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>they approached Zerubbabel and the other leaders, <i>“Let us build with you. We worship your God too. We’ve been sacrificing to him since Esarhaddon, king of Assyria, sent us here.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Zerubbabel, Jeshua, and the other leaders said, <i>“You don’t have anything in common with us building a temple to our God. We’ll build it like King Cyrus decreed.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Other residents in the land tried to discourage the people and scare them away from building. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They bribed advisors to work against them all during Cyrus’ reign till the reign of <u>Darius</u>, king of Persia.                                           </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ezra 4:1-5</p>


### Later Opposition Under Xerxes and Artaxerxes


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Years
</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>later, at the beginning of the reign of Ahasuerus/<i>Xerxes</i>, they lodged a complaint against Judah and Jerusalem. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Still later</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>, Bishlam, Mithredath, Tabeel, and their associates wrote to Artaxerxes. The letter was in Aramaic <i>script </i>and translated <i>into the Aramaic</i> <i>language</i> <i>for the king</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Rehum the governor and Shimshai the secretary wrote out the letter against Jerusalem to King Artaxerxes—</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>they and their associates <i>on behalf of</i> the judges and local leaders, officials, and secretaries over the people of Erech, Babylonia, and Susa (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Elam</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>),
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and the rest of the nations that the great and honorable Osnappar/<i>Ashurbanipal</i> deported and settled in the city of Samaria and in the rest of the province Beyond the River [<i>Avar-naharah</i>, Aramaic]. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Here’s a copy of the letter:</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ezra 4:6-11a</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><a name="_Hlk124869577"><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“To King Artaxerxes </span></i></a></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:1.0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From your subjects in Beyond the River </span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Jews that came up from you have come to us at Jerusalem. They’re rebuilding the rebellious, evil city, finishing its walls and repairing its foundation. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If it’s rebuilt and its walls are finished, they won’t pay tribute, custom, or toll, and it’ll damage the king’s revenue. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Because we serve the palace, it’s not fitting for us to see your dishonor. We’ve sent to inform you </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>so a search can be made in your ancestors’ records. You’ll discover that the city has been rebellious and damaging to kings and provinces, that they’ve incited revolt in the past, and that’s why it was destroyed. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If it’s rebuilt and its walls finished, you won’t have any possession in Beyond the River.”</span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ezra 4:11b-16</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk124869644"><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king answered Rehum, Shimshai, and their associates in Samaria and the rest of Beyond the River:</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:1.0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Greetings,</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The document you sent us has been translated and read to me. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ve issued a decree. A search has been made, and it’s been discovered that the city has rebelled against kings in the past, that revolution and sedition have occurred in it; </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>that strong kings have ruled Jerusalem, governing the whole province Beyond the River; and that they received tribute, custom, and toll. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So issue a decree to stop those men from rebuilding the city till I issue a decree. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Take care of this matter promptly. We must not let this situation harm the royal interests</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><a name="_Hlk124869717"><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As soon as Rehum, Shimshai, and their associates read a copy of King Artaxerxes’ letter, they hurried to Jerusalem and stopped the Jews by force. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The work on the Temple halted till the <b>second year of Darius</b>, king of Persia.<b>          </b></span></span></p>
