## Deuteronomy Chapter 15

### The Year for Canceling Debts

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“At</em><em> the end of every seven years, cancel debts. </em><strong><sup>2</sup></strong><em>Creditors</em><em> are to let go of what they’ve loaned to their </em>fellow<em> </em>Hebrews<em>. Don’t exact it from them; Yahveh has canceled the </em>debt<em>. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>You</em><em> can exact it from a foreigner, but not from fellow citizens. </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>There</em><em> won’t be any poor among you because Yahveh will bless </em><em>you like he promised </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>if</em><em> you obey this commandment. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Lend</em><em> to other nations, but don’t borrow </em>from them<em>. <span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>“If</em><em> there are poor fellow citizens in your town, be sensitive. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>Be</em><em> willing to lend them what they need. </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> harbor the selfish thought, ‘The seventh year is coming, the year of debt cancelation,’ and not help them. They may call out to Yahveh, and it’ll be a sin on you. </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><em>Give</em><em> generously without regret so Yahveh will bless what you undertake. </em><strong><sup>11</sup></strong><em>You’ll</em><em> always have poor people in your land.
