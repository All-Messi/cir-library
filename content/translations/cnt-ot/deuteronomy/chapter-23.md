## Deuteronomy Chapter 23

### Exclusion from the Assembly

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“Nobody</em><em> that’s been castrated or has his penis cut off, can go into Yahveh’s assembly. </em><strong><sup>2</sup></strong><em>No</em><em> illegitimate person or his descendant can go into Yahveh’s assembly for ten generations. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>No</em><em> Ammonite, Moabite, or their descendant can go into Yahveh’s assembly for ten generations. </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>They</em><em> didn’t meet you with food and water when you came out of Egypt. They hired Balaam Ben-Beor from Mesopotamia to curse you, </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>but</em><em> Yahveh wouldn’t listen to him. He turned the curse into a blessing because he loves you. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Never</em><em> seek peace or prosperity with them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> detest Edomite</em><em>s</em><em>; </em><em>they’re</em><em> </em><em>kin</em><em>.</em><em> </em><em>Don’t detest Egypt</em><em>ians; you lived as foreigners</em><em> in his land.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>T</em><em>he</em><em> third generation </em><em>can</em><em> enter </em><em>Yahveh’s </em><em>assembly</em><em>. </em> 	<strong>Uncleanness in Camp</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>“When</em><em> you go out as an army, keep yourselves away from anything impure. </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> a man is ceremonially unclean from night emissions, he must leave the camp the rest of the day</em><em>.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>11</sup></strong><em>In</em><em> the evening, he can take a bath and come back. </em><strong><sup>12</sup></strong><em>Have</em><em> a place outside the camp to go</em><em> </em><em>to. </em><strong><sup>13</sup></strong><em>Keep</em><em> a spade among your tools. When you defecate, dig </em>a hole<em> and cover up your feces. </em><strong><sup>14</sup></strong><em>Since</em><em> Yahveh lives in your camp to deliver you from your enemies, your camp must be holy. He must not see anything detestable among you, or he’ll turn away from you.</span>

### Miscellaneous Laws


<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> hand over to their master any slaves that escape to you. </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>Let</em><em> them live among you where they choose. Don’t mistreat them. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>“No</em><em> son or daughter will be a cult prostitute. </em><strong><sup>18</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> take the hire of a female or male prostitute* to Yahveh’s house for an offering to fulfill a vow. They’re detestable to your God. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> charge your fellow citizens interest on money, food, or anything that can be loaned at interest, </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>so</em><em> Yahveh can bless what you undertake. You can charge a foreigner interest.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>“When</em><em> you vow to Yahveh, don’t delay paying it. That would be a sin, and he’ll certainly hold you to it. (</em><strong><sup>22</sup></strong><em>Not</em><em> taking any vow isn’t a sin.) </em><strong><sup>23</sup></strong><em>You</em><em> have to do what you voluntarily promise your God. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>“You</em><em> can go into someone else’s vineyard and eat as many grapes as you want, but you can’t put any in a container. </em><strong><sup>25</sup></strong><em>You</em><em> can go into someone’s standing grain and pick off heads, but you can’t use a sickle on it.</span>
