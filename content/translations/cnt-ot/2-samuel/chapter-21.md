## 2 Samuel Chapter 21


### David Avenges the Gibeonites

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ll do whatever you say.”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Give us seven descendants of the man that tried to destroy us and eliminate us from staying inside Israel’s border. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We’ll hang them in the </span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>L</span></i><i><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s presence in Gibeah of Saul, the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s chosen one.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ll give them </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>to you<i>.”</i><b>

<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He gave them to the Gibeonites, who hanged them on the mountain in the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s presence. <p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Rizpah spread burlap on the rock, from the beginning of harvest till it rained on them. <span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When David found out what Saul’s concubine had done, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>he took Saul and Jonathan’s bones away from the men of Jabesh-gilead, who had taken them from the open square in Beth-shan. <span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He brought their bones and the bones of the ones they hanged. </span></span>

### Wars with the Philistine Giants


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Philistines were at war again with Israel. When David and his men went down and fought them, David got tired. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ishbi-benob</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, a descendant of the giants <i>in Gath</i>—whose spearhead weighed 7½ lbs <sup>300 shekels</sup>—was armed with a new sword and tried to kill David. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Abishai son of Jeruiah helped him, struck the Philistine, and killed him. Then David’s men swore to him, <i>“Do not go out with us again to battle so you won’t snuff out the lamp in Israel.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There was war again with the Philistines at Gob. Sibbecai the Hushathite struck down <u>Saph</u>, a descendant of the giants <i>in Gath</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Another war with the Philistines took place at Gob. Elhanan son of Jaare-oregim from Bethlehemite killed <i><u>Lahmi</u>, the brother of</i> Goliath of Gath, the shaft of whose spear was like a weaver’s beam. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In another war at Gath, there was <u>a very big man</u> with six digits on each hand and foot. He, too, was a descendant of the giants <i>in Gath</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he defied Israel, Jonathan, son of David’s brother Shimei, struck him down. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Those four sons of the giants in Gath fell by David and his troops.<b>
