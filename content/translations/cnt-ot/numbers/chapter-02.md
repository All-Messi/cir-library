## Numbers Chapter 2

### The Layout of Israel’s Camp 2:[1] 2“The Israelites will camp with the banners of their families facing the Tent of Meeting. 3-4On the east side of the Tabernacle, Judah will camp by its standard—the army of Nahshon (74,600). 5-6Issachar will camp next to him—the army of Nethanel (54,400). 7-8Zebulun will camp with its leader Eliab (57,400). 9The Judah camp totals 186,400. It will move out first.  	Num 2:1-9

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The Israelites will camp with the banners of their families facing the Tent of Meeting. 3-4On the east side of the Tabernacle, Judah will camp by its standard—the army of Nahshon (74,600). 5-6Issachar will camp next to him—the army of Nethanel (54,400). 7-8Zebulun will camp with its leader Eliab (57,400). 9The Judah camp totals 186,400. It will move out first. 10-11"On the south side of the Tabernacle, Reuben will camp by its standard—the army of Elizur (46,500). 12-13Simeon will camp next to him—the army of Shelumiel (59,300). 14-15Gad will camp with its leader Eliasaph (45,650). 16The Reuben camp totals 151,450. It will move out second.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> "Then the Tent of Meeting will move out with the Levites in the middle of the camps. 18-19"On the west side of the Tabernacle, Ephraim will camp by its standard—the army of Elishama (40,500). 20-21Manasseh will camp next to him—with its leader Gamaliel (32,200). 22-23Benjamin camps next—the army of Abidan (35,400). 24The Ephraim camp totals 108,100. It will move out third. 25-26"On the north side of the Tabernacle, Dan will camp by its standard—the army of Ahiezer (62,700). 27-28Asher will camp next to him—with its leader Pagiel (41,500). 29-30Naphtali camps next—with its leader Ahira (53,400).  31The Dan camp totals 157,600. It will move out last."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> The total men numbered 603,550, 33not including the Levites. 34The Israelites did everything the LORD told Moses. They camped by their standards and went out in that order, each by family and ancestral house.</span>
