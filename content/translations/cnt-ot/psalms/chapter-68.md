## Psalms Chapter 68

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Arise, God, scatter your enemies; chase off those who hate you!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Blow them away like smoke; melt them like wax near fire; make sinners perish in your presence.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> But may good people be happy, delight in your presence, celebrate and be glad.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> So sing to God, sing praises to him; lay out a highway for the One that rides through deserts, whose name is Yah, and celebrate with him.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 68:1-4</p>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> God in his holy Temple is a father for the fatherless and a judge for widows;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> he makes a home for the lonely and leads prisoners out to prosperity; only the rebellious live in a parched land.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> God of Israel, when you went out ahead of your people and marched through the desert,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> the ground shook, the sky poured down rain, Sinai itself quaked in front of you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> You sent widespread, abundant rain, God, and refreshed your inheritance when it was parched.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The animals settled in it; in your goodness you provided for the poor.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 68:5-10</p>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> The Lord gives the command, and a host of women sing the good news:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Kings and their armies flee the women that stayed home divide the spoil!”</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Even those who stayed among the flocks found treasure like the wings of a dove covered with silver and pinions covered with glistening gold.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> The Almighty scattered the enemy kings there like blowing snow in Zalmon.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> The mountain in Bashan is majestic, a mountain range with many peaks.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Mountain with many peaks, why do you look with envy at the mountain God wanted for his home? He’ll certainly live there forever.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> God had twice ten thousand chariots, thousands upon thousands; Yahveh came with them from Sinai to the holy place.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> When you ascended on high and led captivityLXX captive, and received gifts among people, even rebellious people— so Yahveh God might live there.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Bless Yahveh, who carries our burdens every day, who keeps us safe 20and rescues us; deliverance from death comes from Yahveh the Lord.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> He’ll bash in the heads of his enemies, the hairy crowns of those who keep doing their guilty deeds.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> The Lord said, “I’ll bring them down from Bashan and bring them up from the ocean depths</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> so you can wash your feet in their blood and your dogs can have their share of your enemies.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 68:19-23</p>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> They’ve seen your solemn procession, God, the procession of God, my King, into the sanctuary—</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> singers first, then musicians, with young women playing tambourines.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Bless God as a community, Yahveh, the fountain of Israel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> There’s Benjamin, the youngest, leading them, the officials of Judah in the throng, the officials of Zebulun and Naphtali.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Summon your strength, God; show us your power like you have before.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Kings will bring you gifts because of your Temple in Jerusalem.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> Scare off the beast that lives in the reeds, the herd of bulls among the calves of the nations. Trample underfoot its bars of silver; scatter the nations that delight in war.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> Let envoys bring gifts out of Egypt; let Nubia quickly submit to God.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 68:24-31</p>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> Sing to God, kingdoms on earth; sing praises to Yahveh,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> that rides high in the ancient sky, that thunders out his loud voice.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> Attribute power to God, whose majesty shines on Israel, whose strength is displayed in the sky.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> He’s awesome in his sanctuary; the God of Israel gives strength and power to his people. Bless God!</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 68:32-35</p>
