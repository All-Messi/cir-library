## Psalms Chapter 105

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Be grateful to the LORD; call on him; tell the nations what all he’s done.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Sing praises to him; talk about the amazing acts.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Take pride in his holy name; let the people be happy that rely on him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Rely on the LORD and his strength; depend on him always.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Remember his amazing miracles, the judgments he’s rendered,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> descendants of Abraham his servant, children of Jacob, his chosen ones!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> He’s the LORD our God, who renders judgment all over the world.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:1-7</p>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> He remembers his eternal covenant, the message he commanded to a thousand generations,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> his covenant with Abraham, his oath to Isaac.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> his statute confirmed to Jacob as an ongoing covenant.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> He swore, “I’ll give you Canaan to inherit.”</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> When they were just a few, temporary residents,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> they wandered from nation to nation, from kingdom to kingdom.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> He didn’t let anybody oppress them; he reprimanded kings on their behalf:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Don’t touch my anointed ones; don’t harm my prophets.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:8-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> He called for a famine in the land and cut off their food supply.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> He sent a man ahead of them, Joseph, sold as a slave.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> They hurt his feet with restraints and put an iron collar around his neck</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> till God’s message came to him and revealed what kind of man he was.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> The king released him; the people’s ruler let him go.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> He made him master of his household, steward over everything he owned,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> even to imprison his officials if he saw fit so he could teach his elders wisdom.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:16-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> Then Israel/Jacob went down to Egypt and lived as a foreigner in the land of Ham.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> God multiplied his people and made them stronger than their enemies,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> whose hearts he turned against his people to mistreat them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Then he sent Moses his servant and Aaron his chosen one</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> to perform his amazing acts among them, miracles in the land of Ham:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> he engulfed their land in darkness when they defied him and his message;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> he turned their water to blood and killed their fish;</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:23-29</p>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> frogs overran the land even into the king’s bedrooms;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> swarms of flies came, and gnats infested the whole region;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> he gave them hail for rain, and lightning flashed in the land;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> he struck down their grapevines and fig trees and stripped their trees;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> he summoned countless young locusts to come</span>

<span class="verse" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> and eat the vegetation in the country and devour the produce from the ground;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> he struck the Egyptians’ firstborn, the first issue of their strength.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:30-36</p>

<span class="verse" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> Then he brought Israel out with silver and gold; nobody among the tribes faltered.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> The Egyptians were glad when they left because they’d come to dread them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> He spread a cloud over them and sent fire to light up the night.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> They asked, and he brought quail and satisfied them with manna from the sky.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> He opened a rock and water came out and ran into the desert like a stream.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> He remembered his sacred promise to Abraham his servant.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> He brought his people out with joy and celebration.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> He gave them the lands of nations, and they harvested what those nations had grown;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v45"><sup class="verse-num">45</sup> he did it so they would keep his statutes and observe his laws. Hallelujah!</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:37-45</p>
