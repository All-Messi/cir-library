## Psalms Chapter **137**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>We sat by the rivers in Babylon and cried when we remembered Zion.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>We hung our harps on its willows.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Our captors required us to sing there; our tormentors demanded happy songs, <em>“Sing </em><em>us </em><em>a song from</em><em> Zion.”</em><em>	</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 137:1-3</p>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>How can we sing about the LORD in a foreign land?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>If I forget you, Jerusalem, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>may my right hand wither.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>May my tongue stick to the roof of my mouth if I don’t remember you, if I don’t regard Jerusalem above my greatest joy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>Remember, LORD, against the Edomites the day of Jerusalem, when they said <em>to the Babylonians</em>, <em>“</em><em>Tear</em><em> it</em><em> down</em><em>, </em><em>tear</em><em> it </em><em>down </em><em>to its foundation.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>Young woman of Babylon, you devastated one, how happy will be the one that repays you with what you did to us.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>How happy will the one be that grabs <u>your</u> infants and dashes them against the rocks!</span>
