## Psalms Chapter 137

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> We sat by the rivers in Babylon and cried when we remembered Zion.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> We hung our harps on its willows.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Our captors required us to sing there; our tormentors demanded happy songs, “Sing us a song from Zion.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 137:1-3</p>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> How can we sing about the LORD in a foreign land?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> If I forget you, Jerusalem, may my right hand wither.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> May my tongue stick to the roof of my mouth if I don’t remember you, if I don’t regard Jerusalem above my greatest joy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Remember, LORD, against the Edomites the day of Jerusalem, when they said to the Babylonians, “Tear it down, tear it down to its foundation.”</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Young woman of Babylon, you devastated one, how happy will be the one that repays you with what you did to us.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> How happy will the one be that grabs your infants and dashes them against the rocks!</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 137:4-9</p>
