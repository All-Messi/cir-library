## Psalms Chapter 72

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Give the king your judgments, God; give your goodness to his son.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> May he judge your people fairly, your oppressed ones with justice.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Let the mountains bring prosperity to the people; may the hills bring goodness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> May he defend the downtrodden, save the children of the needy, and crush their oppressors.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 72:1-4</p>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Let them respect you as long as the sun lasts, as long as the moon endures—throughout all generations.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> May the king rule like rain on mown grass, like showers that water the ground.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> May good people flourish in his time and have abundant peace till the moon disappears.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 72:5-7</p>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> May he rule from sea to sea, from the Euphrates River to the ends of the earth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Let desert nomads bow to him, and his enemies lick dust.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Let the kings of Tarshish and the islands bring presents, and the kings of Sheba and Seba offer gifts.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Let all kings bow to him and every nation serve him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> He’ll deliver the needy when they call for help, the oppressed with no one to help them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> He’ll have mercy on the poor and needy and save their lives.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> He’ll rescue them from oppression and violence; their blood is precious to him;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> so may they live long and receive the gold of Sheba; let them pray to him continually and bless him all day long.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 72:6-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> May grain be abundant in the land; may it wave on tops of the hills. May it produce like Lebanon; may its people blossom in the city like grass in the pasture.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> May the king’s name last forever and grow as long as the sun shines. Let people bless themselves by him and all nations call him blessed.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Bless the LORD, the God of Israel, who alone does wonderful things.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Bless his glorious name forever; may the whole world be full of his glory. Amen and Amen.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> The end of the prayers of David son of Jesse</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 72:12-20</p>
