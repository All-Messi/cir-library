## Psalms Chapter **147**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><strong>Hallelujah!</strong> It’s good to sing praises to our God; praising him is fitting because he’s gracious.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>The LORD builds up Jerusalem and gathers the outcasts in Israel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>He heals the brokenhearted and bandages up their wounds.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>He counts the stars <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and calls them each by name.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Our Lord is great and has great power, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and understanding without limit.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>He supports the abused and takes sinners down to the ground.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 147:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>Sing thanksgiving to the LORD; sing praise to our God with a lyre.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>He covers the sky with clouds, provides rain for the ground, and makes grass grow on the hills.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>He gives animals their food and <em>feeds</em> the young ravens when they caw.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>He doesn’t delight in the strength of horses or take pleasure in human might.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>The LORD favors those who respect him, who place their hopes in his enduring love.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 147:7-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>Praise the LORD, Jerusalem; praise your God, Zion.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>He’s reinforced the bars on your gates and blessed those who reside in you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>He brings peace to your borders and satisfies you with the finest wheat.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>He issues his command to the world, and his message travels swiftly.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>He sends snow like wool and scatters frost like ashes.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>He makes it hail; who can stand his freezing cold?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>He speaks and it melts; he makes wind blow and water flow.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>He declares his message to Jacob, his statutes and ordinances to Israel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup>He hasn’t dealt this way with any other nation; they haven’t known his ordinances. <strong>Hallelujah!</strong><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 147:12-20</p>
