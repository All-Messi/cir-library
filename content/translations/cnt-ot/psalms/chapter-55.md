## Psalms Chapter **55**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Hear my prayer, God; don’t ignore my plea.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>Listen and answer; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>trouble has overwhelmed me; I’m distracted</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>by the threats of my enemies, by bringing suffering on me and bearing angry grudges against me.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 55:1-3</p>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>My heart’s pounding; terror of death has fallen on me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Fear and trembling have come over me; horror has overwhelmed me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>I said, <em>“</em><em>I wish</em><em> I had wings like a </em><em>dove</em><em>!</em> <strong><em><sup>	</sup></em></strong><em>I’d fly </em><em>off</em><em> and find </em><em>peace</em><em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><em>I’d </em><em>flee</em><em> far away </em> <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><em>and lodge </em><em>out </em><em>in the</em><em> countryside</em><em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup><em>I’d hurry to my </em><em>shelter</em><em> </em> <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><em>from the </em><em>howl</em><em>ing wind and </em><em>raging storm</em><em>.”</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 55:4-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>Lord, destroy <em>their plans</em>; confuse their languages, because all I see is violence and conflict in the city.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>Day and night they patrol the walls, <em>but</em> sin and scheming threaten inside it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>Ruin is rampant within it; oppression and cheating never leave the streets.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 55:9-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>It’s not an enemy that’s disparaging me; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong>I could handle that; it’s not foes that hate me and rise up against me, I could hide from them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>But it’s you, my equal, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>my companion, my familiar friend.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>We used to be close friends that walked in God’s Temple together.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>Let death overtake my enemies; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>let them go down alive to Sheol, because there’s evil where they live.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 55:12-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>I’ll call on the LORD my God to deliver me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>Morning, noon, and night, he’ll hear my groaning</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>and deliver my life in peace from my attackers, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>because lots of people are opposing me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>The One who sits enthroned from of old will punish the ones<em> </em>that don’t change or respect him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>As for that</em><em> companion </em><em>of mine, </em> <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>he betrays his friends and breaks his promises against those who are at peace with him.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 55:16-20</p>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>His speech is smoother than butter, but there’s war in his heart; his words are more soothing than oil, but secretly they’re drawn daggers.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup>Put your burdens on the LORD, and he’ll take care of you; he won’t let the godly fall.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>You, God, will bring the wicked down to the deepest pit; bloodthirsty, devious people won’t live half their time, but I’ll trust in you.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 55:21-23</p>
