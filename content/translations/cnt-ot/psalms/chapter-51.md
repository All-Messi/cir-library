## Psalms Chapter 51

### Psalm for the Choir Director. A Psalm of David, When Nathan the Prophet Came to Him, After He’d Slept with Bathsheba

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Be gracious to me, God, in proportion to your enduring love; according to your great mercy, blot out my transgressions.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Wash me thoroughly from my sin; cleanse me from my iniquity.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> I know my failings; my sin always weighs on my mind.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> I’ve sinned against you and you only; I did what you consider evil; so you’re justified in what you say to me, blameless when you condemn me.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 51:1-4</p>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> I was born in sin; in iniquity my mother conceived me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> You want truth in the inward self; you’ll make me know wisdom in myself.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Purify me with hyssop, and I’ll be clean; wash me, and I’ll be whiter than snow.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Fill me with joy and happiness again, and let the bones you broke mend up.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Don’t look at my sins; blot out my iniquities.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 51:5-9</p>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Create a clean heart in me, God; renew a steadfast spirit within me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Don’t banish me from your presence or take your Holy Spirit away from me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Restore to me the joy your salvation brings; grant me a willing attitude to obey.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Then I’ll teach transgressors your ways; sinners will be converted to you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Deliver me from being guilty of murder so I can joyfully sing about your goodness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> LORD, open my lips so I can express your praises.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> You don’t delight in sacrifice; otherwise, I’d give it; you’re not pleased with burnt offerings.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> The sacrifice you want is a broken spirit; you appreciate a repentant heart.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> In your good favor, do good to Zion; build up the walls around Jerusalem.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Then you’ll delight in godly sacrifices, in burnt offerings and whole burnt offerings; then young bulls will again be offered on your altar.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 51:10-19</p>
