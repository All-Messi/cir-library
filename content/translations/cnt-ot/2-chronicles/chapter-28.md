## 2 Chronicles Chapter 28


### Ahaz Rules Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahaz</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was 20 and ruled for <b>16 years in Jerusalem</b>. He didn’t do what was good like David did. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He followed the kings of Israel, made molten images for the worshipping Baal, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>burned incense in the Valley of (Ben)Hinnom, and burned his sons like the nations the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> had driven out. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He sacrificed and burned incense on the high places on the hills under every green tree.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> delivered him to the king of Aram. They defeated </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahaz</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and took a large number of captives to Damascus. </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God
</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>also delivered him to the king of Israel, who inflicted great casualties on him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Pekah son of Remaliah killed 120,000 in Judah in one day, all trained soldiers, because they’d left the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> God of their ancestors. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Zichri, a powerful man in Ephraim, killed Maaseiah, the king’s son; Azrikam, the palace commander; and Elkanah, the king’s second in command.<b>                                                               </b></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                                                                               </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>28:1-7</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Israel took away 200,000 women, sons, and daughters; and a great deal of spoil. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s prophet Oded met the army coming to Samaria, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Because the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> God was angry with Judah, he delivered them to you. You’ve killed them in a rage that has reached heaven.
</span></i><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now you intend to make slaves out of the men and women from Judah and Jerusalem. Don’t you have sins of your own against your God? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Take back the ones you’ve captured from your kin. The L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s furious anger has turned against you.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Some leaders of Ephraim—Azariah son of Johanan, Berechiah son of Meshillemoth, Jehizkiah son of Shallum, and Amasa son of Hadlai—confronted those returning from battle, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t bring the captives in here. You’re about to bring guilt on us against the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and add to our sins. We have a lot of guilt, so his furious anger has turned against us.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The armed men left the captives and spoil with the officers and the assembly. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Designated men took the captives and put clothes from the spoil on the naked ones. They gave them clothes and sandals, fed them and gave them something to drink, put olive oil on their wounds, led their feeble ones on donkeys, and took the captives to their fellow countrymen at Jericho, the City of Palms. Then they came back to Samaria.<b>        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 28:8-15</span></b></span></p>


### Ahaz Closes the Temple


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At that time, King Ahaz sent to the kings of Assyria for help. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Edomites had attacked Judah again and taken captives. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Philistines had invaded the towns in the western foothills and the Negev of Judah, and had taken Beth-shemesh, Aijalon, Gederoth, Soco with its villages, and settled there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That was </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> humbled Judah because of Ahaz; he’d brought about a lack of restraint in Judah and was terribly unfaithful to the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Tilgath-pilneser, king of Assyria, came and attacked him instead of reinforcing him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahaz took a portion out of the Temple, the king’s palace, and the officials’ houses and gave it to the king of Assyria; but he didn’t help him. <b>            </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 28:16-21</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In his distress, this same King Ahaz became more unfaithful to the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He sacrificed to the gods of Damascus that had defeated him, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Since the gods of Aram helped them, I’ll sacrifice to them so they’ll help <u>me</u>.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> But they became his and Israel’s downfall. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Ahaz gathered the utensils from God’s Temple, he cut them in pieces. He closed the doors of the Temple and built altars in every corner of Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In every city in Judah he made high places to burn incense to other gods and made the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> angry. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of his ways and deeds, first to last, are recorded in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Book of the Kings of Judah and Israel</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahaz died, and they buried him in Jerusalem but not in the tombs of the kings of Israel. His son Hezekiah reigned.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 28:22-27</span></b></span></p>
