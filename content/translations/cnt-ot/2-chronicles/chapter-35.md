## 2 Chronicles Chapter 35


### Josiah Celebrates the Passover


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Josiah celebrated Passover to the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> in Jerusalem. They killed the Passover </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>animals</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> on the fourteenth of the first month. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He appointed the priests to their offices and encouraged them in the service for the Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told the Levites that taught Israel and were holy to the L</span><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,
</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Put the holy Ark in the Temple Solomon built. It won’t be a burden on your shoulder anymore. Serve the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> your God and his people Israel. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Prepare yourselves by divisions within families like David and Solomon wrote. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Stand in the Holy Place by those divisions of your relatives the lay people. With the Levites make divisions within families. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Slaughter the Passover </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>animals</span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, sanctify yourselves, and be ready to do for your relatives what the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> said by Moses.”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>  </span></b><b><span style='font-size: 10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 35:1-6</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For Passover offerings, Josiah contributed out of his own stock 30,000 lambs and kids for the lay people that were present plus 3,000 bulls. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His officers also contributed a freewill offering to the people, priests, and Levites. For the Passover, Hilkiah, Zechariah, and Jehiel, the officials of God’s Temple, gave the priests 2,600 </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>from flocks </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and 300 bulls. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For the Passover, Conaniah—and his brothers Shemaiah and Nethanel—and Hashabiah, Jeiel, and Jozabad, officers of the Levites, contributed to the Levites 5,000 </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>from the flocks</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> and 500 bulls.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So the service was ready, and the priests and Levites stood at their stations by their divisions. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They killed the Passover </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>animals</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. While the priests sprinkled the blood, the Levites skinned them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then they removed the burnt offerings so they could give them to the divisions of families in the lay people to present to the L</span><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> the way the Book of Moses says. They did the same thing with the bulls. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They roasted the Passover </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>animals</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> on the fire as required by the Law, boiled the holy things in pots, kettles, and pans; and carried them quickly away to the people. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Afterward they prepared for themselves and the priests, because the priests, descendants of Aaron, were offering the burnt offerings and fat till dark. So the Levites prepared for themselves and the priests. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The singers, descendants of Asaph, were also at their stations like David commanded: Asaph, Heman, and Jeduthun the king’s seer. The gatekeepers didn’t have to step aside from serving at each gate because their relatives, the Levites, prepared for them.                                                                                                                                                             </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                                                                              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 35:7-15</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So the service of the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was ready that day to celebrate Passover and to offer burnt offerings on the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s altar like King Josiah commanded. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Israelites that were present celebrated Passover and the Festival of Unleavened Bread for a week. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Israelites hadn’t celebrated Passover like that since Samuel the prophet. No king in Israel celebrated such a Passover as Josiah did with the priests, Levites, Judah, and Israel present with the people in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They celebrated it in the <b>eighteenth year</b> of Josiah’s reign. <b>         </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 35:16-19</span></b></span></p>


### Josiah Dies in Battle


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After Josiah had set the Temple in order, Neco, king of Egypt, went up to fight at Carchemish on the Euphrates, and Josiah went out to engage him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Neco sent ambassadors to him, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“What do we have to do with each other, King of Judah? I’m not coming against you; I’m fighting another nation, and God has ordered me to hurry. For your own sake, stop interfering with God, who is with me, so he doesn’t destroy you.” </span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 35:20-21</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Josiah wouldn’t turn away. He disguised himself and ignored what God said from Neco and went ahead to fight him on the plain of Megiddo. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The archers shot him, and he told his officers, </span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Take me away; I’m badly wounded.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They took him out of the chariot, put him in a second one he had, and took him to Jerusalem, where he died. They buried him in the tombs of his ancestors. Judah and Jerusalem mourned for him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jeremiah chanted a lament for him. The male and female singers still speak about him in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Lamentations</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. They made them an ordinance in Israel; they’re written in T</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>he Lamentations</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the deeds and devotions of Josiah are written in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Law of the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His acts, first to last, are recorded in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Book of the Kings of Israel and Judah</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 35:22-27</span></b></span></p>
