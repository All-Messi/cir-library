## 2 Chronicles Chapter 4


### Temple Furnishings


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He made </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                  <u>a bronze</u> <u>altar</u> 30 x 30 x 15ft high;  </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         2</span></sup></b><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>a circular, cast-metal sea</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> 15ft from brim to brim, 7<b>½</b>ft deep and 45ft</span><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>around.   </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         3</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Figures of gourds were under it, entirely circling the sea (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>45ft</span><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>). The oxen were in 2 rows cast in one piece with the Sea. </span><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It stood on 12 oxen, 3 facing out in each direction with their hindquarters inward. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It was 4ins thick, with a brim like a cup—like a lily blossom. It could hold 27,000 gallons </span><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>3,000 baths</span></sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         6</span></sup></b><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He made 10 basins</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> to wash in, and set 5 on each side for rinsing things for the burnt offering; but the sea was for the priests to wash in; </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         7</span></sup></b><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>10 golden lampstands</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> in the way prescribed, and set them in the Temple, 5 on each side; <b>                  </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 4:1-7</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         8</span></sup></b><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>10 tables</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> placed in the Temple, 5 on each side; </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      <u>100 gold bowls</u>; </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         9</span></sup></b><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the court of priests</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, the great court, and bronze-plated doors for the court. </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He set the sea on the right side of the Temple toward the southeast.<b>                 </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 4:8-10</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Huram made the <u>pails, shovels, and bowls</u>, and finished the work for King Solomon on God’s Temple:
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the 2 pillars, the bowls and 2 capitals on top of the pillars, the 2 networks to cover the 2 bowls on the capitals; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the 400 pomegranates for the 2 networks, 2 rows for each network to cover the 2 bowls of the capitals, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the stands</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> and the basins on the stands, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the sea with 12 oxen under it. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Huram</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> made the pails, shovels, <u>forks</u>, and <u>utensils</u> out of polished bronze. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king had them cast in the clay soil between Succoth and Zeredah on the Jordan plain. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Solomon made these utensils in great numbers. The weight of the bronze couldn’t be calculated.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Solomon made everything in God’s Temple out of purest gold:  the altar, the tables for the Bread of the Presence, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the lampstands with their lamps to burn in front of the inner sanctuary as prescribed, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the <u>flowers</u>, lamps, <u>tongs</u>; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the
<u>snuffers</u>, bowls, <u>spoons</u>, <u>firepans</u>; the entrance to the Temple, its inner doors for the Holy of Holies, and the doors of the Temple (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the nave</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).<b>
</b></span></span></p>
