## 2 Chronicles Chapter 26


### Uzziah Rules Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The people in Judah took <b>Uzziah</b>/</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Azariah</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> at 16 and made him king after his father Amaziah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He built Eloth and restored it to Judah, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and ruled for <b>52 years in Jerusalem</b>. His mother was Jechiliah of Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did what was good like everything </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>good</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> his father Amaziah had done. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He continued to look to God in the time of Zechariah, who had understanding through the vision of God. As long as he sought the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, God caused him to flourish.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 26:1-5</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went out and fought the Philistines, and tore down the walls of Gath, Jabneh, and Ashdod. He built towns around Ashdod among the Philistines. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God helped him against the Philistines, the Arabians in Gur-baal, and the Meunites.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Ammonites paid him tribute, and his reputation extended to the border of Egypt, because he became very strong. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In addition, he built towers in Jerusalem at the Corner Gate and the Valley Gate at the Angle, and fortified them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He built towers in the countryside and hewed out many cisterns, because he had lots of livestock in the western foothills and in the plains. He had farmers and vinedressers in the hill country and fertile fields because he loved the soil. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Uzziah had an army ready for battle, which entered combat by divisions, according to the number of their muster, prepared by Jeiel the secretary and Maaseiah the official under the direction of Hananiah, one of the king’s officers. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The number of family leaders over trained soldiers was 2,600. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They directed an elite army of 307,500 that could wage war with great power against the king’s enemies. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For the whole army, Uzziah/</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Azariah</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> prepared shields, spears, helmets, body armor, bows, and sling stones. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In Jerusalem he constructed engines of war invented by skilled men, and put them on the towers and the corners, to shoot arrows and </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>catapult</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> large stones. His reputation spread because he was marvelously helped until he became strong.<b>               </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 26:6-15</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But when he became strong, his pride led him to act corruptly. He became unfaithful by going into the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple and burning incense on the incense altar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Azariah the priest came in after him with 80 of the priests, brave men, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>to oppose him, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You’re not supposed to burn incense. That’s for the priests to do, consecrated descendants of Aaron. Get out of the sanctuary. You’ve been unfaithful and won’t have any honor from the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> God.” </span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Uzziah/</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Azariah</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, with a censer in hand for burning incense, was mad. While he ranted at the priests, leprosy broke out on his forehead beside the altar of incense. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Azariah and the priests saw it and hurried him out of there. He hurried to get out himself because the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> had struck him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>For the rest of his life, he lived as a leper in a house separate from the Temple. His son Jotham managed the king’s palace and judged the people. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Uzziah/</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Azariah</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, first to last, the prophet Isaiah son of Amoz has recorded. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Uzziah died, and they buried him in a burial field the kings owned, because he was a leper.</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His son Jotham became king.<b>              </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 26:16-23</span></b></span></p>
