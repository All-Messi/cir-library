## 1 Chronicles Chapter 18

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> After that, David defeated the Philistines and took Gath and its surrounding towns. 2He defeated Moab and they brought tribute. 3He defeated Hadadezer, king of Zobah, as far as Hamath and secured his rule all the way to the Euphrates River. 4He took 1,000 chariots, 7,000 cavalry, and 20,000 foot soldiers. He hamstrung the chariot horses but reserved enough for 100 chariots.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> When the Arameans from Damascus came to help Hadadezer, David killed 22,000 of them. 6He put garrisons in Aram of Damascus, and the Arameans brought tribute. 7He took the gold shields that Hadadezer's officers carried and brought them to Jerusalem. 8From Tibhath and Cun, towns of Hadadezer, he took a large amount of bronze, which Solomon used to make the bronze sea, pillars, and bronze utensils.	 1 Ch 18:1-8</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Tou/Toi, king of Hamath, heard that David had defeated Hadadezer's whole army. 10He sent his son Hadoram to greet him and bless him for it. Hadoram brought all kinds of gold, silver, and bronze articles. 11David dedicated them to the LORD with the silver and gold he'd carried off from Edom, Moab, the Ammonites, Philistines, and Amalek.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> In addition, Abishai son of Zeruiah defeated 18,000 Edomites in the Valley of Salt. 13He put garrisons in Edom, and the Edomites served him. The LORD helped him wherever he went.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> David ruled Israel and administered justice and goodness for his people.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Joab son of Zeruiah was over the army;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Zadok son of Ahitub and Abimelech son of Abiathar were priests;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Benaiah son of Jehoiada was captain of David's bodyguard;</span>
