## 1 Chronicles Chapter 5

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>5<sup>:1, 3</sup></span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>The sons of <b>REUBEN</b>, Israel’s firstborn:</span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> 3</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hanoch and Pallu, Hezron and Carmi</span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Because he defiled his father’s bed, his birthright was given to the sons of Joseph son of Israel; so he’s not enrolled in the genealogy by birthright. </span><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Although Judah prevailed over his brothers and the leader came from him, the birthright was Joseph’s.) </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The sons of Joel </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>were </span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Shemaiah, Gog, Shimei, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Micah, Reaiah, Baal, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Beerah that Tilgath-pilneser, king of Assyria, took away into exile. He was leader in Reuben. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His relatives by clans—when their genealogy was figured—</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>were</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> Jeiel the chief, then Zechariah, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Bela son of Azaz son of Shema son of Joel that lived in Aroer as far as Nebo and Baal-meon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He lived to the east as far as the entrance to the desert this side of the River Euphrates because their livestock had multiplied in Gilead. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At the time of Saul, they conquered the Hagrites and occupied their tents in the whole region east of Gilead.<b>                                                                                                         </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Ch 5:1-10</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The descendants of <b>GAD</b> lived across from them in Bashan as far as Salecah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joel was the chief, Shapham second, then Janai and Shaphat in Bashan. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Their kinsmen in their clans </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>were</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> Michael, Meshullam, Sheba, Jorai, Jacan, Zia, and Eber—7. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>These
</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>were</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> the descendants of Abihail son of Huri son of Jaroah son of Gilead son of Michael son of Jeshishai son of Jahdo son of Buz </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and Ahi son of Abdiel son of Guni </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>were </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>heads of their clans. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The descendants of Gad lived in Gilead, in Bashan and its surrounding towns, in the pastureland of Sharon as far as their boundaries. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>These were enrolled in the genealogies at the time of Jotham, king of Judah, and Jeroboam</span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I</span></sup></b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, king of Israel.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The descendants of Reuben, Gad, and the Manasseh<i> </i></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>half-tribe<i> were</i> 44,760—brave men who carried shield and sword, shot bow and arrow, were capable in battle. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They fought the Hagrites, Jetur, Naphish, and Nodab. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God helped them and delivered the Hagrites and everybody that was with them, because they cried out to God in the battle. He granted their petition because they trusted in him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They took their livestock: 50,000 camels, 250,000 sheep, 2,000 donkeys, and 100,000 men. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Many fell slain because the war came from God. They settled in their place. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The descendants of the Manasseh </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>half-tribe<i> </i>lived in the land. They were numerous from Bashan to Baal-hermon, Senir, and Mount Hermon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The heads of their clans were Epher, Ishi, Eliel, Azriel, Jeremiah, Hodaviah, and Jahdiel, trained soldiers, famous men. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But they sinned against their ancestors’ God and committed prostitution with the gods of the people in the land God had destroyed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The God of Israel stirred up Pul, king of Assyria—also known as Tiglath-pilneser</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>/Tiglath-pileser</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>—and he took Reuben, Gad, and the Manasseh </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>half-tribe<i> </i>into exile, to Halah, Habor, Hara, and the Gozan River ever since.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                       1 Ch 5:11-26</span></b></span></p>
