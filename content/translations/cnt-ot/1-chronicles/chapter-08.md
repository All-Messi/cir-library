## 1 Chronicles Chapter **8**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>BENJAMIN was father of <u>Bela</u> his firstborn, Ashbel second, Aharah third, <strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Nohah fourth, and Rapha fifth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><u>Bela</u> was father of Addar, Gera, Abihud, <strong><sup>4</sup></strong>Abishua, Naaman, Ahoah, <strong><sup>5</sup></strong>Gera, Shephuphan, and Huram.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>These are the sons of Ehud, heads of families among the people in Geba. They were exiled to Manahath: <strong><sup>7</sup></strong>Naaman, Ahijah, and Gera, father of Uzza and Ahihud.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>Shaharaim was the father of children in Moab after he’d sent away his wives Hushim and Baara. <strong><sup>9</sup></strong>By his wife Hodesh he became father of Jobab, Zibia, Mesha, Malcam, <strong><sup>10</sup></strong>Jeuz, Sachia, Mirmah, sons and heads of families. <sup>11</sup>By Hushim he’d become father of Abitub and <u>Elpaal</u>.</span>
<p class="section-ref">1 Ch 8:1-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>The sons of <u>Elpaal</u> <em>were</em> Eber, Misham, Shemed—who built Ono and Lod with their villages—<strong><sup>13</sup></strong><u>Beriah</u> and Shema (heads of families among the people in Aijalon, who routed the people in Gath); <strong><sup>14</sup></strong>Ahio, <u>Shashak</u>, and Jeremoth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><strong><sup>5</sup></strong>Zebadiah, Arad, Eder, <sup>16</sup>Michael, Ishpah, and Joha <em>were</em> sons of <u>Beriah</u>.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><strong><sup>7</sup></strong>Zebadiah, Meshullam, Hizki, Heber, <sup>18</sup>Ishmerai, Izliah, and Jobab <em>were </em>sons of<em> </em>Elpaal.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><strong><sup>9</sup></strong>Jakim, Zichri, Zabdi, <strong><sup>20</sup></strong>Elienai, Zillethai, Eliel, <strong><sup>21</sup></strong>Adaiah, Beraiah, and Shimrath <em>were</em> sons of Shimei.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Ishpan, Eber, Eliel, <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>Abdon, Zichri, Hanan, <strong><sup>24</sup></strong>Hananiah, Elam, Anthothijah, <strong><sup>25</sup></strong>Iphdeiah, and Penuel <em>were</em> the sons of Shashak.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Shamsherai, Shehariah, Athaliah, <strong><sup>27</sup></strong>Jaareshiah, Elijah, and Zichri <em>were</em> sons of Jeroham.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup><strong>28</strong></sup>These were heads of families in their generations, chief men that lived in Jerusalem.<strong> </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">1 Ch 8:12-28</p>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>Jeiel</em> father of Gibeon lived in Gibeon; his wife was Maacah.<strong> </strong><strong><sup>30</sup></strong>His firstborn son <em>was</em> Abdon, then Zur, Kish, Baal, Nadab, <strong><sup>31</sup></strong>Gedor, Ahio, Zecher, and <strong><sup>32</sup></strong>Mikloth father of Shimeah/<em>Shimeam</em>. These families lived near each other in Jerusalem. <strong><sup>33</sup></strong>Ner was father of Kish father of Saul father of <u>Jonathan</u>, Malchi-shua, Abinadab, and Eshbaal.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><strong><sup>34</sup></strong>The son of Jonathan <em>was</em> Merib-baal father of <strong><sup>35</sup></strong>Micah father of Pithon, Melech, Tarea, and <u>Ahaz</u>. <strong><sup>36</sup></strong>Ahaz was father of Jehoaddah father of Alemeth, Azmaveth, and Zimri; Zimri was father of <strong><sup>37</sup></strong>Moza father of Binea father of Raphah father of Eleasah father of <u>Azel</u>. <strong><sup>38</sup></strong>Azel had six sons: Azrikam, Bocheru, Ishmael, Sheariah, Obadiah, and Hanan.<strong> </strong><strong><sup>39</sup></strong><strong>Azel’s</strong> brother Eshek <em>was father of</em> Ulam his firstborn, Jeush second, and Eliphelet third. <strong><sup>40</sup></strong>The sons of Ulam were brave soldiers, archers, and had many sons and grandsons—150 <em>of them</em>. <u>Those </u><em><u>were all </u></em><u>descendants of Benjamin.</u></span>
