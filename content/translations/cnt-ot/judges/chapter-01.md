## Judges Chapter 1


### Partial Conquest of Remaining Territory


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After Joshua died, the people asked the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, <i>“Who will go up first to fight the Canaanites?”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said, <i>“<u>Judah</u>. I’ll deliver the land to him.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judah said to his brother <u>Simeon</u>, <i>“Go with me to my territory to fight the Canaanites, and I’ll go with you to yours.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>So he went with him. </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judah went up and the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> delivered 10,000 Canaanites and Perizzites to him at Bezek. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5-6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They attacked Adoni-bezek </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and he fled, but they caught him and cut off his thumbs and big toes. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said, <i>“Seventy kings with their thumbs and big toes cut off used to pick up scraps under my table. God has done to me what I’ve done.”</i> They took him to Jerusalem, where he died.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judah fought Jerusalem and took it. They struck it with the sword and set it on fire. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then they went down to fight the Canaanites in the hill country, the Negev, and the western foothills. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judah attacked the Canaanites in Hebron (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>called Kiriath<i>-</i>arba before</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) and defeated Sheshai, Ahiman, and Talmai.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jud 1:1-10</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From there they attacked Debir (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>called Kiriath<i>-</i>sepher before</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>). </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Caleb said, <i>“Whoever takes Keriath-sepher, I’ll give my daughter Achsah as a wife.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Othniel son of Kenaz, Caleb’s younger brother, took it, and <i>Caleb</i> gave her to him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When she came to him, she urged him to ask her father for a field. She got down from her donkey, and Caleb asked, <i>“What do you want for yourself?”  </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“A present. Since you’ve put me in the Negev, give me springs.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Caleb gave her the upper and lower springs.</span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The descendants of Moses’ father-in-law, the Kenite, moved from the City of Palms to the Wilderness of Judah in the Negev near Arad, and settled with the people. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judah went with Simeon and defeated the Canaanites in Zephath and “destroyed” it; so the city was called Hormah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judah also took Gaza, Ashkelon, and Ekron, and their territories. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was with Judah, and he gained possession of the hill country, but he couldn’t drive out the people in the plain; they had iron chariots. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They gave Hebron to Caleb, like Moses had said. He drove out Anak’s three sons. <b>                                                                                                                                           </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jud 1:11-20</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But <u>Benjamin</u> didn’t drive out the Jebusites in Jerusalem; they still live there among them.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joseph</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> went up against Bethel and the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was with them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joseph’s house spied out Bethel (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>called Luz before</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>). </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The spies saw a man coming out of the city, and said, <i>“Show us how to get into the city, and we’ll have mercy on you.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He showed them. They struck it with the sword, but they let him and his family go.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went to the Hittites and built a city he called Luz. That’s still its name.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Manasseh</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> didn’t drive out the people in Beth-shean, Taanach, Dor, Ibleam, or Megiddo and their villages. The Canaanites kept living there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Israel grew stronger, they put them to forced labor; but they didn’t completely drive them out. <b>   </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jud 1:21-28</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ephraim</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> didn’t drive out the Canaanites in Gezer, who kept living among them.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Zebulun</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> didn’t drive out the people in Kitron or Nahalol, who kept living among them, subject to forced labor.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Asher</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> didn’t drive out the people in Acco, Sidon, Ahlab, Achzib, Helbah, Aphik, or Rehob. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They lived among the Canaanites.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Naphtali</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> didn’t drive out the people in Beth-shemesh or Beth-anath. They lived among those Canaanites, who became forced labor.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Amorites pressed the <u>Dan</u>ites back into the hill country. They wouldn’t let them come down to the plain. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They went on living in Mount Heres. Aijalon, and Shaalbim. But Joseph’s house pressed heavily on them and subjected them to forced labor. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The border of the Amorites ran from the ascent of Akrabbim, from Sela and up.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jud 1:29-36</span></b></span></p>
