## Ecclesiastes Chapter **1**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>The Words of the Preacher, Son of David, King in Jerusalem <strong><sup>2</sup></strong><em>“</em><em>Completely pointless</em><em>,” </em>the Preacher says; <em>	</em><em>“Completely pointless</em><em>!</em><em> </em><em>It’s all </em><em>pointless</em><em>.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>What do people get out of their work under the sun?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>One generation passes away and another comes along, but the earth just goes on and on.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>The sun comes up, the sun goes down, and hurries back to where it comes up again.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>The wind blows south, the wind blows north; it just keeps circulating around and around.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Eccl 1:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>The rivers flow to the ocean; yet the ocean doesn’t run over; they flow again to where they flowed before.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>Everything is too tiresome to talk about; eyes aren’t satisfied by seeing; ears aren’t fulfilled by hearing.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>What has been is what will be; what’s been done is what’ll be done; there’s nothing new under the sun.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>Is there anything you can say, <em>“</em><em>Look, tha</em><em>t’s new”</em>?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><strong><sup>1</sup></strong>People don’t remember the past; later, they won’t remember the future either. The ones that come after you won’t remember you.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Eccl 1:7-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>I, the Preacher, have been king over Israel in Jerusalem. <strong><sup>13</sup></strong>I decided to examine with wisdom everything people do under the sky. It’s a grievous job God has stricken them with. <strong><sup>14</sup></strong>I’ve looked at everything they do under the sun. It’s all pointless, like chasing the wind.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>What’s crooked you can’t straighten; what’s missing you can’t get back.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>I said to myself, <em>“I’</em><em>m wiser </em><em>than anybody </em><em>that’s ruled</em><em> Jer</em><em>usalem before;</em><em> </em> <em>	</em><em>I’ve </em><em>gained</em><em> a wealth of knowledge.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>I determined to experience everything from wisdom to madness and pointless behavior. I came to realize that it’s all like chasing the wind.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><strong><sup>18</sup></strong>There’s much grief in much wisdom; increasing knowledge increases pain.<strong>	     </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Eccl 1:12-18</p>
