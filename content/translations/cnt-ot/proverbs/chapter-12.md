## Proverbs Chapter 12

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Whoever loves discipline loves knowledge; whoever hates to be corrected stays ignorant.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> The LORD gives grace to the good, but condemns those who concoct evil.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Sinfulness brings instability, but godliness grows deep roots.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> A good wife is her husband’s crown, but one that embarrasses him is like cancer in his bones.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> You can trust the thinking of the righteous, but the advice of sinners is treacherous.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The words of sinners are like a murderous ambush, but the words of the godly rescue people.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 12:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> The sinful are overthrown and disappear, but the godly stand firm.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> People are commended for using good sense; people with perverted minds are despised.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> The humble who work as servants are better off than those who honor themselves and go hungry.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Good people care about their animals’ life; sinful people are cruel to everything.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Plow your ground and you’ll have plenty to eat; chasing after fantasies shows poor judgment.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Sinners are jealous of each other’s plunder, but the goodly produce <em>their own things</em>.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 12:7-12</p>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Their own words trap sinful people; good people avoid trouble like that.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Good people gain satisfaction from the effects of their words; their actions also<em> </em>return to reward them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Fools think their way is the right way; but the wise are willing to listen.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Fools fly off the handle when insulted, but the prudent stay calm.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Honest witnesses tell the truth; false ones just make up something.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Cutting remarks spoken rashly thrust like daggers, but wise words heal wounds.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 12:13-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> A truthful tongue lasts a lifetime, but lying lips don’t endure.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Deceit lies in hearts that lay plots, but advisors of peace have joy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Harm tends not to fall on good people; sinners are always in trouble.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> The LORD detests liars but delights in reliable people.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> Prudent people hold back telling how much they know; foolish people talk too much.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> The diligent rule; the lazy end up enslaved.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 12:19-24</p>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> Anxiety weighs people down; an encouraging word raises them up.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Godly people serve as guides for their friends; sinful ones get them lost.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Lazy people don’t achieve their goals; diligent ones gain precious possessions.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Life lies along the road good people follow; there’s no death there.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 12:25-28</p>
