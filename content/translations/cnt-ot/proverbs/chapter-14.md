## Proverbs Chapter 14

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> A wise woman builds up her home; a foolish one tears it down with her own hands.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>We show respect for the LORD by pursuing godliness; being crooked in our ways means we don’t respect him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The tongues of fools become a rod for their back; the lips of the wise save them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>A manger stays clean where there are no oxen, but abundant crops come in by their strength.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> A truthful witness tells no lies; a false one lies with every breath.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Scoffers look for wisdom but don’t find it; knowledge comes easy for insightful people.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 14:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Stay away from foolish people; you won’t learn anything from them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>Prudent people know where they’re going, but fools mislead themselves.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Fools make fun of guilt, but the godly favor the guiltless.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><strong><sup>10</sup></strong>The heart knows its bitterness; no one else can share its joy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> The house of the sinful faces demolition, but the tent of the godly stands steady.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><strong><sup>12</sup></strong>There is a way that seems right, but it leads to death.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 14:7-12</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Laughter can hide a heavy heart; joy can give way to grief.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><strong><sup>14</sup></strong>Perverse people get a fill of their own ways; good people get satisfaction from theirs.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Simpleminded people believe anything, the prudent watch where they step.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><strong><sup>16</sup></strong>The wise take caution and get away from sin, but fools are arrogant and reckless.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Short-tempered people do foolish things; people despise people that scheme.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><strong><sup>18</sup></strong>The naïve inherit foolishness, but the prudent wear knowledge like a crown.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 14:13-18</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Evil bows to good; sinners <em>will bow </em>at the gates of the godly.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><strong><sup>20</sup></strong>Even neighbors dislike the poor, but lots of people like the rich.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Whoever despises another commits sin, but those gracious to the poor find happiness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>22</sup></strong>Won’t they take a loss that devise evil? Those who devise good receive kindness and truth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> There’s profit in work; mere talk produces poverty.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><strong><sup>24</sup></strong>The wise wear their wealth like a crown; the foolish dress in their own foolishness.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 14:19-24</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> A truthful witness saves lives, but a false witness is a traitor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><strong><sup>26</sup></strong>Those who respect the LORD feel confident; their children have a reliable refuge.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Respecting the LORD provides a fountain of life so people can avoid the snares of death.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><strong><sup>28</sup></strong>A growing population speaks well for a king; what’s the point of a prince without subjects?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Wise people control their temper; short-tempered people show their lack of good sense.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><strong><sup>30</sup></strong>A peaceful heart makes a healthy body, but jealousy is like cancer of the bone.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 14:25-30</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> People that oppress the poor insult their Maker; those who are gracious to them honor him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><strong><sup>32</sup></strong>Sinful people get crushed by the disasters they bring on themselves, but the righteous have a refuge when they die.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> Wisdom rests in intelligent people, but it’s <em>not</em> evident among fools.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><strong><sup>34</sup></strong>Goodness raises the quality of a nation; sin’s a disgrace to any people.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> A king favors a servant that acts wisely, but he lashes out at one that disgraces him.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 14:31-35</strong></span>
