## Proverbs Chapter 14

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> A wise woman builds up her home; a foolish one tears it down with her own hands.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> We show respect for the LORD by pursuing godliness; being crooked in our ways means we don’t respect him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The tongues of fools become a rod for their back; the lips of the wise save them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> A manger stays clean where there are no oxen, but abundant crops come in by their strength.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> A truthful witness tells no lies; a false one lies with every breath.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Scoffers look for wisdom but don’t find it; knowledge comes easy for insightful people.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 14:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Stay away from foolish people; you won’t learn anything from them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Prudent people know where they’re going, but fools mislead themselves.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Fools make fun of guilt, but the godly favor the guiltless.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The heart knows its bitterness; no one else can share its joy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> The house of the sinful faces demolition, but the tent of the godly stands steady.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> There is a way that seems right, but it leads to death.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 14:7-12</p>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Laughter can hide a heavy heart; joy can give way to grief.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Perverse people get a fill of their own ways; good people get satisfaction from theirs.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Simpleminded people believe anything, the prudent watch where they step.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> The wise take caution and get away from sin, but fools are arrogant and reckless.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Short-tempered people do foolish things; people despise people that scheme.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> The naïve inherit foolishness, but the prudent wear knowledge like a crown.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 14:13-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Evil bows to good; sinners <em>will bow </em>at the gates of the godly.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Even neighbors dislike the poor, but lots of people like the rich.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Whoever despises another commits sin, but those gracious to the poor find happiness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> Won’t they take a loss that devise evil? Those who devise good receive kindness and truth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> There’s profit in work; mere talk produces poverty.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> The wise wear their wealth like a crown; the foolish dress in their own foolishness.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 14:19-24</p>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> A truthful witness saves lives, but a false witness is a traitor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Those who respect the LORD feel confident; their children have a reliable refuge.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Respecting the LORD provides a fountain of life so people can avoid the snares of death.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> A growing population speaks well for a king; what’s the point of a prince without subjects?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Wise people control their temper; short-tempered people show their lack of good sense.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> A peaceful heart makes a healthy body, but jealousy is like cancer of the bone.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 14:25-30</p>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> People that oppress the poor insult their Maker; those who are gracious to them honor him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> Sinful people get crushed by the disasters they bring on themselves, but the righteous have a refuge when they die.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> Wisdom rests in intelligent people, but it’s <em>not</em> evident among fools.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> Goodness raises the quality of a nation; sin’s a disgrace to any people.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> A king favors a servant that acts wisely, but he lashes out at one that disgraces him.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 14:31-35</p>
