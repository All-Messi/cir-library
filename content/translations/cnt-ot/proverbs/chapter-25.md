## Proverbs Chapter 25

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> More of Solomon’s Proverbs That the Men of Hezekiah, King of Judah, Transcribed</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> It’s God’s privilege not to reveal things it’s a king’s responsibility to figure them out.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> A king’s mind is as unsearchable as the sky is high and the earth is deep.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Remove the dross from silver, and a silversmith can make something out of it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Keep corrupt <em>advisors</em> away from a king, and goodness will make his throne secure.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Don’t claim a privileged position in a king’s presence or put yourself in a place designated for nobles.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> It’s better to be told to come up higher than to be put down lower in the presence of a prince. What you’ve seen</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> don’t rush to court over; what will you do at the end of the trial if the one you accuse puts you to shame?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> When you’re arguing with your neighbor, don’t betray another person’s confidence;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> people may find out and accuse you of gossiping, and you won’t regain your good reputation.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:6-10</p>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Words fitly spoken in the proper place are like golden apples in a silver setting.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> A wise correction to an attentive ear is like a gold earring or jewelry.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> An employer’s reliable messenger refreshes like cold snow at harvest time.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> People that brag falsely about what they’ll do are like clouds and wind without rain.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Patience can persuade a prince; a soft tongue can break a bone.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Have you found honey? Don’t eat too much of it and make yourself sick.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Don’t visit your friends too often; they’ll get tired of you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Someone that slanders is like a club, a sword, a sharp arrow.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:11-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Depending on undependable people in hard times is like chewing with a broken tooth or walking on a sprained ankle.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Singing to a heavy heart is like removing your coat on a cold day or <em>pouring </em>vinegar on lye.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> If your enemies are hungry, feed them; if they’re thirsty, give them a drink;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> you’ll be dumping hot coals on their head, and the LORD will reward you.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:19-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> A north wind brings rain; a back-biting tongue brings angry looks.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> It’s better to live in the corner of a housetop than inside a house with a contentious woman.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> Good news from a distant land is like cool water to a weary soul.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Good people that cave in to sinful people are like trampled springs and polluted wells.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Don’t eat too much honey; don’t seek your own honor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Someone that’s out of control is like an unwalled city; it’s open to attack.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:23-28</p>
