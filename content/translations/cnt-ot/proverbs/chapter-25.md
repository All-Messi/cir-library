## Proverbs Chapter **25**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>More of Solomon’s Proverbs That the Men of Hezekiah, King of Judah, Transcribed</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>It’s God’s privilege not to reveal things it’s a king’s responsibility to figure them out.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>3</sup></strong>A king’s mind is as unsearchable as the sky is high and the earth is deep.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>Remove the dross from silver, and a silversmith can make something out of it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>5</sup></strong>Keep corrupt <em>advisors</em> away from a king, and goodness will make his throne secure.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>Don’t claim a privileged position in a king’s presence or put yourself in a place designated for nobles.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><strong><sup>7</sup></strong>It’s better to be told to come up higher than to be put down lower in the presence of a prince. What you’ve seen</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>don’t rush to court over; what will you do at the end of the trial if the one you accuse puts you to shame?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>When you’re arguing with your neighbor, don’t betray another person’s confidence;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><strong><sup>0</sup></strong>people may find out and accuse you of gossiping, and you won’t regain your good reputation.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:6-10</p>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>Words fitly spoken in the proper place are like golden apples in a silver setting.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><strong><sup>12</sup></strong>A wise correction to an attentive ear is like a gold earring or jewelry.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>An employer’s reliable messenger refreshes like cold snow at harvest time.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><strong><sup>14</sup></strong>People that brag falsely about what they’ll do are like clouds and wind without rain.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>Patience can persuade a prince; a soft tongue can break a bone.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><strong><sup>16</sup></strong>Have you found honey? Don’t eat too much of it and make yourself sick.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>Don’t visit your friends too often; they’ll get tired of you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><strong><sup>18</sup></strong>Someone that slanders is like a club, a sword, a sharp arrow.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:11-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>Depending on undependable people in hard times is like chewing with a broken tooth or walking on a sprained ankle.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><strong><sup>20</sup></strong>Singing to a heavy heart is like removing your coat on a cold day or <em>pouring </em>vinegar on lye.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>If your enemies are hungry, feed them; if they’re thirsty, give them a drink;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>you’ll be dumping hot coals on their head, and the LORD will reward you.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:19-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>A north wind brings rain; a back-biting tongue brings angry looks.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><strong><sup>24</sup></strong>It’s better to live in the corner of a housetop than inside a house with a contentious woman.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup>Good news from a distant land is like cool water to a weary soul.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><strong><sup>26</sup></strong>Good people that cave in to sinful people are like trampled springs and polluted wells.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup>Don’t eat too much honey; don’t seek your own honor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><strong><sup>28</sup></strong>Someone that’s out of control is like an unwalled city; it’s open to attack.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 25:23-28</p>
