## Proverbs Chapter 5

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Pay attention to my wisdom, son; listen to what I understand</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> so you can use discretion and express knowledge.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> An immoral woman’s lips drip honey; her speech is more soothing than oil;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> but in the end she’s as bitter as wormwood, as sharp as a two-edged sword.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Her feet go down to death; her steps lead to Sheol.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> She doesn’t ponder the path of life; she wanders without realizing it.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 5:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Listen to me, sons; don’t turn aside from what I’m telling you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Stay away from her; don’t go near her house,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> so you won’t be giving your vigor to others and your years to someone cruel;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> so a stranger won’t be filled with your strength and the fruit of your act go to an alien;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> and you groan at the end of life because you misused your body;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> and you say, <em>“I hated instruction!</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	</em><em>my heart despised</em><em> being corrected</em><em>!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>I</em><em> didn’t listen to</em><em> my teachers</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	or pay </em><em>attention to my instructors!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>I’ve</em><em> come to the point of </em><em>total ruin</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and </em><em>have to</em><em> face public disgrace</em><em>.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 5:7-14</p>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Drink water from your own cistern, fresh water from your own well.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Should your spring be spilled like water running down the street?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Let it be for yours alone, not for a stranger too.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 5:15-17</p>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Let your fountain be blessed; be happy with the wife of your youth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Like a lovely deer, a graceful doe, be satisfied with her breasts; be stimulated by her love.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Son, why be stimulated by an immoral woman? why embrace the bosom of a stranger?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> The LORD watches everyone to see which direction they’re going.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> Their own iniquities capture the wicked; they get caught in the web of their own sins.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> They die from ignorance and get lost over foolishness.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 5:18-23</p>
