## Proverbs Chapter 13

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Wise children accept their father’s discipline, but scoffers don’t pay attention to correction.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>From the influence of your words, people respond willingly; but treacherous people want to force things through.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Be careful what you say, and you’ll save yourself; talk too much, and you’ll do yourself in.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>Lazy people want but don’t get; the diligent have a rich supply.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Good people hate lies, but sinners act disgracefully.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Goodness protects the blameless; sinfulness undermines the sinful.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 13:1-6</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> One person acts rich but is poor; another pretends to be poor but is wealthy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>The rich can pay a ransom to save their lives, but the poor don’t get kidnapped for ransom.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> The light of the godly shines bright, but the lamp of the sinful flickers out.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><strong><sup>10</sup></strong>Insolence causes division; accepting advice brings wisdom.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Easy wealth soon dwindles away; what we work for increases.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><strong><sup>12</sup></strong>Hope delayed makes a heart sick, but a longing fulfilled is a tree of life.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 13:7-12</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Instruction destroys people that hate it; a commandment rewards those who respect it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><strong><sup>14</sup></strong>Wise teaching is your fountain of life for avoiding the snares of death.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Good sense leads to blessing, but a treacherous mind creates hardship.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><strong><sup>16</sup></strong>Prudent people act with insight; fools flaunt their foolishness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> An unreliable messenger stumbles into trouble, but a faithful envoy can bring detente.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><strong><sup>18</sup></strong>We risk poverty and shame by neglecting self-discipline; honor comes to us if we value correction.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 13:13-18</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Seeing dreams come true is pleasant to the soul, but fools hate to quit sinning.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><strong><sup>20</sup></strong>When you live with the wise you become wise; companions of fools will suffer with them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Misfortune follows sinners, but prosperity rewards the righteous.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>22</sup></strong>Good people leave an inheritance for their grandchildren; sinners lay up wealth for the godly.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> Even fallow ground yields enough to feed the poor, but injustice sweeps it all away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><strong><sup>24</sup></strong>Those who spare the rod don’t love their children; if you care about them, you’ll discipline them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> Good people have enough to satisfy their appetite, but a sinner’s stomach never feels full.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 13:19-25</strong></span>
