## Proverbs Chapter 24

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Don’t envy bad people or want to be around them;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>their minds devise violence; their lips stir up trouble.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Wisdom builds a house; intelligence establishes a home;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>knowledge fills its rooms with precious, pleasant wealth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> The wise are strong; knowledge increases their strength;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>they wage war with effective strategy; they win the victory because of their many advisors.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Wisdom is out of the reach of fools; they have nothing to say in the city gate.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> People call them schemers who plot misfortune;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> devising foolishness is sin; scoffing is detestable.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 24:1-9</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><strong><sup>10</sup></strong>If adversity makes you weak, you’re not very strong to start with.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Rescue those unjustly sentenced to death; save those who are staggering to their execution.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><strong><sup>12</sup></strong>If you try to excuse yourself by claiming ignorance, won’t he know better than that who weighs your heart? Doesn’t he know you that keeps your soul? Won’t he render to you what you deserve?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Son, eat honey; it’s good; honeycomb tastes sweet.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>Understand that wisdom is like that to your soul; if you find it, you’ll have a bright future, and your hope won’t fade away.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 24:10-14</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Don’t lie in wait at godly people’s house, you wicked; don’t raid their resting place.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><strong><sup>1</sup></strong><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Good people fall seven times and get up again; but for sinners, one disaster is more than enough.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Don’t be glad when your enemies stumble or celebrate when they fall.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>The LORD won’t be pleased; he’ll turn his anger away from them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Don’t worry about sinners or envy them;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> they have no future; their lamp will flicker out.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 24:15-20</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Son, revere the LORD, respect the king, and don’t associate with unstable people;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>their disaster will rise up unexpectedly, and ruin you with them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> These are sayings of the wise: Showing partiality in judgment is unjust.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>Anyone that tells sinners they’re good, people will curse and nations abhor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><strong><sup>5</sup></strong>But those who rebuke sinners will receive delight; blessing will come to them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Giving an honest answer is like a kiss on the lips.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 24:21-26</p>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Finish your work in the field first; then come and build your house.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Don’t testify against someone for no reason or deceive by what you say.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Don’t say, <em>“I’ll do to him what he’s done to </em><em>me;</em> <em>	I’ll render </em><em>to </em><em>him his deeds</em><em> in </em><em>return</em><em>.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> I went past a lazy person’s field, past the vineyard of someone without common sense.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><strong><sup>1</sup></strong>Thistles had taken it over; nettles covered the ground; its walls were broken down.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> I thought about what I saw; I looked and learned:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><strong><sup>34</sup></strong>Your poverty will descend on you like a raider; your need <em>will </em><em>attack you</em> like an armed robber.<strong>	</strong><strong>Prov 24:27-34</strong></span>
