## Proverbs Chapter **21**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>A king’s heart in the LORD’s hand is like a stream he can direct where he wants.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Your own way seems right to you, but the LORD weighs your heart.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>The LORD wants goodness and justice more than sacrifice.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>Arrogant eyes and a proud heart are wicked people’s lamp—and sin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Diligent planning gives you an advantage; getting in a hurry gets you poverty.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Getting rich by lying is a dissipating vapor that ends in death.<strong>	</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>Sinful people’s violence sweeps them away because they refuse to do what’s right.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>A guilty person’s road is full of curves, but the conduct of the pure makes a straight path.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>It’s better to live in the corner of a housetop than inside the house with a contentious woman.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><strong><sup>10</sup></strong>Evil people desire evil and don’t show compassion to anyone.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>Punishing scoffers makes them wise; instructing the wise makes them wiser still.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><strong><sup>12</sup></strong>Good people watch out for the sinful and turn them to ruin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>If you shut out the cry from the poor, you’ll cry out yourself and not be heard.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><strong><sup>14</sup></strong>A secret gift calms anger; a present turns it away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>Good people enjoy carrying out justice; justice distresses sinners.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><strong><sup>16</sup></strong>If you stray from understanding, you’ll end up among the dead.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>People that love pleasure become poor; they who love wine and comfort never get rich.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><strong><sup>18</sup></strong>Sinners are ransoms for the righteous; the unfaithful replace the good.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 21:13-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>It’s better to live in the desert than with a contentious, “complainy” woman.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><strong><sup>20</sup></strong>Precious treasure and oil are in the houses of the wise, but foolish people waste them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>Pursue goodness and loyalty, and you’ll arrive at life, goodness, and honor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>22</sup></strong>The wise scale the walls of the powerful and bring down the fortress they trust in.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>Watch your mouth and guard your tongue, and you’ll stay out of trouble. <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>24</sup></strong>“Scoffers” is the name for proud, haughty people that conduct themselves with arrogance.<strong>	</strong></span>
