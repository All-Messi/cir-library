## Exodus Chapter 37

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Bezalel made <u>the </u><u>A</u><u>rk</u> out of acacia wood: 45 x 27 x 27ins high. <strong><sup>2</sup></strong>He overlaid it with pure gold inside and out, made a gold border around it, <strong><sup>3</sup></strong>and cast 4 gold rings for it, 2 on each side. <strong><sup>4</sup></strong>He shaped poles out of acacia wood overlaid with gold <strong><sup>5</sup></strong>and put them in the rings for carrying it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> He formed a <u>mercy seat </u>out of pure gold: 45 x 27in. <strong><sup>7</sup></strong>It had 2 hammered gold cherubs for the ends of the mercy seat, <strong><sup>8</sup></strong>and were one piece with it. <strong><sup>9</sup></strong>Their wings spread upward, covering the mercy seat and facing each other.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ex 37:1-9</p>

### The Table


<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Then he made <u>the table</u> out of acacia wood: 36 x 18 x 27in high. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>He overlaid it with pure gold, put a molding around it, <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>formed a rim for it a hand’s width, and a gold molding for the rim. <strong><sup>13</sup></strong>He cast 4 gold rings for the bottom 4 corners <strong><sup>14</sup></strong>close to the rim for holding the poles that carried the table. <strong><sup>15</sup></strong>He shaped the poles out of acacia wood overlaid with gold. <strong><sup>16</sup></strong> Out of pure gold he fashioned the utensils for the table: dishes, pans, bowls, and jars for pouring liquid offerings.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ex 37:10-16</p>

### The Lampstand


<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> He formed <u>the lampstand</u> out of pure hammered gold with its base, shaft, cups, bulbs, and flowers as one piece. <strong><sup>18</sup></strong>There were 6 branches, 3 coming out of each side. <strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>It had </em>cups shaped like almond <em>blossoms</em>, a bulb, and a flower on each branch. <strong><sup>20</sup></strong>On the lampstand there were 4 cups shaped like almond <em>blossoms</em>, its bulbs, and its flowers. <strong><sup>21</sup></strong>There was a bulb under each of the three pairs of branches coming out of the <em>lampstand</em>. <strong><sup>22</sup></strong>Their bulbs and branches were one piece with <em>the lampstand</em>; the whole thing was a single hammered work of pure gold. <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>Out of pure gold he formed its 7 lamps with its snuffers and trays. <strong><sup>24</sup></strong>He fashioned it and its utensils out of 75½ lbs <sup>a talent</sup> of pure gold.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ex 37:17-24</p>

### The Altar of Incense


<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> He made <u>the altar of</u><u> incense</u> out of acacia wood: 18 x 18 x 36in high. Its horns were one piece with it. <strong><sup>26</sup></strong>He overlaid its top, sides, and horns with pure gold, and made a gold molding around it. <strong><sup>27</sup></strong>For each side under its molding he made two gold rings to hold the poles for carrying it. <strong><sup>28</sup></strong>He fashioned the poles out of acacia wood overlaid with gold. <strong><sup>29</sup></strong>He made the holy anointing oil and pure fragrant incense out of spices—an incense maker’s work.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ex 37:25-29</p>
