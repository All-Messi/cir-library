## Nehemiah Chapter 5


### Nehemiah Stops the Oppression of the Poor

<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Some were saying, <i>“There are a lot of us along with our sons and daughters. We need more food to survive.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Others said, <i>“We’re mortgaging our fields, vineyards, and houses for grain because of the famine.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yet
</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>others were saying, </span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I thought it over and contended with the nobles and rulers, <i>“You’re charging your fellow Jews interest?!” </i>I convened a large assembly against them </span></span>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They couldn’t think of anything to say. <b>                                                                   </b></span></p>

### Nehemiah’s Generosity


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From the day the king appointed me governor of Judah, my kinsmen and I had never eaten the governor’s food allowance—from the <b>twentieth to thirty-second year of King Artaxerxes</b> (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>12 years</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The former governors had laid burdens on the people and took bread and wine from them besides 1 lb <sup>40 shekels</sup> of silver. Even their assistants lorded it over the people. I didn’t do that because I respected God. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I applied myself to the work on the wall. We didn’t buy any land; my servants gathered there to work. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There were 150 Jews and officials at my table, besides those who came from nations around. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I had an ox and six choice sheep prepared each day as well as birds; every ten days I provided wine in abundance. But I still didn’t demand the governor’s food allowance; the people were in heavy servitude. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God, remember me for the good I’ve done for them.<b>                                                                                                  </b></span></span></p>
