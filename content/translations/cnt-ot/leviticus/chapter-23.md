## Leviticus Chapter 23

### Sabbaths and Festivals

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-2</sup></strong>The LORD told Moses to tell Israel, 	<strong>Sabbaths</strong> <em>“The </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> has appointed these times: </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>six days you can work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath for complete rest, a holy convocation. It’s a Sabbath to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> wherever you live. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>“These are his appointed times, holy convocations that you’ll declare at their appointed times. </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>The fourteenth day, first month at twilight is the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>’s Passover. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Then on the fifteenth, there’s a Festival of Unleavened Bread to him. Eat bread without yeast for a week. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>The first day, hold a holy convocation; don’t work. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>Present an offering by fire to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> </em><em>for a week. On the seventh day, hold a holy convocation; don’t work.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:1-8</p>

### Firstfruits


<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>“Count seven Sabbaths from the day after the Sabbath when you brought the bundle for the wave offering. </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>Count 50 days to the day after the seventh Sabbath. Then present a new grain offering to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>17</sup></strong><em>Take in 2 </em>loaves<em> of bread for a wave offering, made from .22</em> bushel <sup>.2 ephah</sup><em>. They’ll consist of fine flour baked with yeast as the first of the harvest to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Along with the bread, present 7 year-old male lambs without defect, a bull from the herd, and 2 rams. They’ll be a burnt offering along with their grain offering and liquid offerings. </em>It’s <em>an offering by fire of an aroma pleasing to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>Also offer 1 male goat for a sin offering and 2 yearling male lambs for a peace offering. </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>The priest will wave them with the bread from the first part of the harvest for a wave offering with 2 lambs in front of the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. They’re holy to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> for the priest. </em><strong><sup>21</sup></strong><em>On that same day, make a proclamation. Hold a holy convocation. Don’t work. It’s an ongoing permanent statute wherever you live. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>“When you harvest, don’t harvest the corners of your field or gather the gleanings from your harvest. Leave them for the poor and foreigners</em><em>. </em><em><u>I’m the </u></em><em><u>L</u></em><em><u>ORD</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:15-22</p>

### Feast of Trumpets


<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> The LORD told Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <em>“</em><em>These are the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>’s scheduled</em><em> </em><em>holidays</em><em> </em><em>that you’re to declare as holy convocations, to present burnt offerings to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>, grain offerings, sacrifices, and liquid offerings, </em>each<em> day’s matter as prescribed. </em><strong><sup>38</sup></strong><em>They’re in addition to </em>the ones on<em> the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>’s Sabbaths.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:33-38</p>

<span class="verse" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <em>“The fifteenth day, seventh month, when you’ve gathered your crops, celebrate the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>’s festival for a week. Rest on the first and eighth days. </em><strong><sup>40</sup></strong><strong><sup>-41</sup></strong><em>On the first day, take the foliage from beautiful trees, palm branches and boughs of leafy trees and willows by the stream. Every year celebrate a festival to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> your God for a week. It’s an ongoing permanent statute for the seventh month. </em><strong><sup>42</sup></strong><em>The native-born in Israel will live in booths for a week </em><strong><sup>43</sup></strong><em>to remind your generations that I had the Israelites live in booths when I brought them out of Egypt. </em><em><u>I’m the </u></em><em><u>L</u></em><em><u>ORD</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> Moses declared to Israel the LORD’s specified holidays.<em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:39-44</p>
