## Leviticus Chapter 11

### Ceremonially Clean and Unclean Animals

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-2</sup></strong>Again the LORD told Moses and Aaron to tell the Israelites, <em>“Y</em><em>ou can eat </em><em><u>animals</u></em><em> </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>that have </em><em>a split hoof and chew cud.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><strong><sup>-6</sup></strong><em>Don’t eat </em><em>anything</em><em> that just chew</em><em>s</em><em> cud or</em><em> only</em><em> </em><em>has</em><em> </em><em>a </em><em>split hoo</em><em>f: a camel or rock-badger or rabbit. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>Don’t eat a pig</em><em>;</em><em> it has a </em><em>split</em><em> hoof but doesn’t chew cud.</em> <strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>Don’t eat their </em><em>meat or touch their carcasses; t</em><em>hey’re</em><em> ceremonially</em><em> unclean.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 11:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>“You can eat anything in the </em><em><u>water</u></em><em>—seas and rivers—that has fins and scales. </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><strong><sup>-11</sup></strong><em>Any marine life that doesn’t have fins and scales is repulsive to you; detest their carcasses. [</em><strong><sup>12</sup></strong><em>]</em><em> </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>“Detest these </em><em><u>birds</u></em><em> and don’t eat them: eagles, vultures, buzzards, </em><strong><sup>14</sup></strong><em>kites, falcons of any kind, </em><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>ravens of any kind, </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>ostrices, owls, sea gulls, hawks of any kind, </em><strong><sup>17</sup></strong><em>little owls, cormorants, ibises, </em><strong><sup>18</sup></strong><em>water hens, pelicans, carrion vultures, </em><strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>storks, herons of any kind, hoopoes, or bats.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>“Detest most</em><em> winged </em><em><u>insects</u></em><em> that walk on four legs.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>21</sup></strong><em>Eat only the ones</em><em> </em><em>that have</em><em> jointed legs </em><em>and hop</em><em> on the ground</em><em>: </em><strong><sup>22</sup></strong><em>any kind of locust, cricket, or grasshopper. </em><em>[</em><strong><sup>23</sup></strong><em>]</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 11:9-23</p>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>“Touching a carcass makes you ceremonially unclean. </em><strong><sup>25</sup></strong><em>If you pick it up, you have to wash your clothes and be unclean until evening. </em><strong><sup>26</sup></strong><em>Touching a ceremonially unclean animal—that doesn’t chew cud and split hoofs—makes you unclean. </em><strong><sup>27</sup></strong><em>Any four-footed animal that walks on paws is ceremonially unclean. Touching its carcass makes you unclean. </em><strong><sup>28</sup></strong><em>If you pick it up, wash your clothes and be unclean till evening.</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 11:24-28</p>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <em>“These things that crawl on the ground are ceremonially unclean: moles, mice, big lizards of any kind, </em><strong><sup>30</sup></strong><em>gechos, crocodiles, lizards, sand lizards of any kind, and chameleons. </em><strong><sup>31</sup></strong><em>Whoever touches them when they’re dead is unclean till evening. </em><strong><sup>32</sup></strong><em>Anything they fall on when they’re dead is also unclean: wooden articles, clothes, skins, sacks, anything you use. Put them in water till evening, and they’ll be clean.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 11:29-32</p>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <em>“Any clay container that one of these ceremonially unclean things falls into is ceremonially unclean. Break it. </em><strong><sup>34</sup></strong><em>Any food </em>in it<em> that water gets</em><em> on </em><em>is </em>likewise <em>unclean. Water or any liquid a person might drink in any container becomes unclean. </em><strong><sup>35</sup></strong><em>Anything that part of their carcass falls on becomes unclean. Smash an oven or stove; they’re permanently unclean. </em><strong><sup>36</sup></strong><em>A spring or cistern that collects water is clean even though the one that has touched their carcasses is unclean. </em><strong><sup>37</sup></strong><em>If any part of their carcass falls on any seed for sowing, it’s clean. </em><strong><sup>38</sup></strong><em>But, if water gets on that seed, it’s ceremonially unclean.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 11:33-38</p>

<span class="verse" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <em>“</em><em>If an animal dies that you have for food, </em><em>touching</em><em> its carcass </em><em>makes you</em><em> </em><em>ceremonially </em><em>unclean till evening.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>40</sup></strong><em>If you pick it up or eat some of it, wash your clothes and be unclean till evening.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 11:39-40</p>

<span class="verse" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <em>“Don’t eat anything that crawls on the ground: </em><strong><sup>42</sup></strong><em>anything that crawls on its belly, walks on four legs, or has many feet is detestable. </em><strong><sup>43</sup></strong><em>Don’t make yourselves </em><em>ceremonially unclean by them. </em><strong><sup>44</sup></strong><em>Consecrate yourselves and be holy. </em><strong><sup>45</sup></strong><em><u>I’m the </u></em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> </em><em>that brought you up from Egypt to be your God. Be holy because I’m holy.” </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v46"><sup class="verse-num">46</sup> That’s the law about animals, birds, marine life, or what crawls on the ground <strong><sup>47</sup></strong>so you can distinguish ceremonially clean from unclean, edible from not edible.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 11:41-47</p>
