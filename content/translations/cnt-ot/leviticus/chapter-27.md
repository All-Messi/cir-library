## Leviticus Chapter 27

### Laws About Vows

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-2</sup></strong>Again the LORD told Moses, <em>“When people make difficult vows, appraise them before the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>Appraise a male that’s 20 to 60</em><em> years old</em><em> at</em><em> </em><em>20 oz of silver </em><em><sup>50 shekels of the sanctuary</sup></em><em> </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>and </em><em>a female</em><em> at</em><em> </em><em>12 oz </em><em><sup>30</sup></em><em> </em><em><sup>shekels</sup></em><em><sup> </sup></em><em>of silver</em><em>.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>Appraise a male that’s 5 to 20 years old at 8 oz </em><em><sup>20 shekels</sup></em><em> and a female at 4 oz </em><em><sup>10 shekels</sup></em><em>. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Appraise a male that’s a month to 5 years old at 2 oz </em><em><sup>5 shekels</sup></em><em> of silver and a female at 1⅕ oz </em><em><sup>3</sup></em><em> </em><em><sup>shekels</sup></em><em>. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>Your appraisal for males above 60 will be 6 oz </em><em><sup>15 shekels</sup></em><em> and 4 oz </em><em><sup>10</sup></em><em> </em><em><sup>shekels</sup></em><em> for females. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>But if you can’t afford that appraisal, you can have the priest appraise you according to your means. </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>If it’s an animal you can offer to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>, it will be holy. </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><em>You won’t exchange it good for bad or vice versa. If you do exchange it, both it and the substitute become holy. </em><strong><sup>11</sup></strong><strong><sup>-12</sup></strong><em>But if it’s a ceremonially unclean animal, which people don’t offer to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>, the priest will appraise it. His appraisal is final. </em><strong><sup>13</sup></strong><em>But if you ever want to buy it back, add a fifth to </em>the <em>appraisal</em><strong>.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 27:1-13</p>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>“If you consecrate your house to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>, the priest will appraise it. His appraisal is final. </em><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>But if you want to buy it back, add a fifth to the appraisal.</em><strong> </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 27:14-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>“If you consecrate part of your field to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>, the priest’s appraisal will be in proportion to the seed for sowing it: 11 bushels </em><em><sup>1 homer</sup></em><em> of barley at 20 oz </em><em><sup>50 shekels </sup></em><em>of silver. </em><strong><sup>17</sup></strong><em>If you consecrate your field in relation to the Year of Jubilee, it will stand according to the priest’s appraisal. </em><strong><sup>18</sup></strong><em>If you consecrate your field after the Year of Jubilee, the priest will calculate the price in proportion to the years till the </em>next<em> Year of Jubilee and deduct it from its appraisal. </em><strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>If you ever want to buy it back, add a fifth to the appraisal. </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>If you sell it, you can’t buy it back. </em><strong><sup>21</sup></strong><em>When it reverts in the Year of Jubilee, it will be holy to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> like a field set apart and will belong to the priest. </em><strong><sup>22</sup></strong><em>If you consecrate a field to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> that you’ve bought—</em><em>not a part of your own property—</em><strong><sup>23</sup></strong><em>then the priest will calculate for it the amount of </em>your<em> valuation as holy to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>24</sup></strong><em>In the Year of Jubilee, the field will go back to its </em>original<em> owner. </em><strong><sup>25</sup></strong><em>Appraisals will be in shekels of the sanctuary (20 gerahs per shekel).</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 27:16-25</p>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <em>“However, you can’t consecrate firstborn animals—ox or sheep; they </em>already <em>belong to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>27</sup></strong><em>But if it’s a ceremonially unclean animal, you can buy it back according to its appraised value plus a fifth. If you don’t buy it back, the priest will sell it for its appraised value. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <em>“Nevertheless, nothing devoted to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>—man, animal, or field—can be sold or bought back. It’s most holy. </em><strong><sup>29</sup></strong><em>No one that’s been sentenced to death can be ransomed. Execute that person.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 27:26-29</p>

### Laws About Tithes


<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <em>“Set apart to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> a tenth of the land, produce of the ground and fruit of the trees. </em><strong><sup>31</sup></strong><em>If you want to buy back part of that tenth, add a fifth to it. </em><strong><sup>32</sup></strong><em>Set apart to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> every tenth member of a herd and flock. </em><strong><sup>33</sup></strong><em>Whether it’s good or bad, you don’t need to substitute for it. If you do, both it and the substitute become holy, and you can’t buy them back.” </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> Those are commandments the LORD gave Moses for Israel at Mount Sinai.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 27:30-34</p>
