## Leviticus Chapter 25

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-2</sup></strong>The LORD told Moses at Mount Sinai to tell Israel, <em>“When you go into the land I’m giving you, the ground will have a rest to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>Plant your fields, prune your vineyards, harvest your crops for six years. </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>But the seventh year, the ground will rest to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. Don’t plant your fields or prune your vineyards. </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>Don’t store up volunteer growth or gather grapes from untrimmed vines. Let the ground lie feral for a year. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>You’ll have for food what the ground grows on its own during its sabbath year—yourselves, your male and female slaves, hired hands, and foreigners living with you. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>Your livestock and the wild animals on your land will have its produce to eat </em>directly<em>.  </em><em>	        </em><em> </em><strong>Lev 25:1-7</strong> <strong>The </strong><strong>Year of Jubilee</strong></span>
