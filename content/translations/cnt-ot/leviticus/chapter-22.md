## Leviticus Chapter 22

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-2</sup></strong>The LORD told Moses to tell Aaron and his sons, <em>“Handle the holy </em>gifts carefully<em> that the Israelites dedicate to me so you don’t profane my name. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>Don’t approach the holy </em>gifts<em> while you’re ceremonially unclean. I’ll remove you from me. </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>If you’re a leper or have a discharge, you can’t eat the holy </em>gifts<em> till you’re clean. Touching anything made ceremonially unclean by a corpse or seminal emission, </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>or touching any teeming thing that makes any person unclean, </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>makes you unclean till evening. </em><em>You can’t eat the sacred </em>gifts <em>till</em><em> </em><em>you</em><em> take a bath.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>When the sun sets, you’ll be clean and can eat them. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>You can’t eat </em>an animal<em> that dies or is torn to pieces by wild animals; that makes you ceremonially unclean. </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>Keep my charge so you won’t be considered defiled and die for profaning my name. </em><em><u>I’m the </u></em><em><u>L</u></em><em><u>ORD</u></em><em><u> that sanctifies them</u></em><em>.</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 22:1-9</p>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>“No layman, visitor with a priest, or hired hand can eat the sacred gifts. </em><strong><sup>11</sup></strong><em>But a slave that a priest buys with his own money can eat them; so can </em>slaves<em> born in his house. </em><strong><sup>12</sup></strong><em>If a priest’s daughter marries a layman, she can’t continue to eat the </em><em>gifts</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>13</sup></strong><em>But if she </em><em>becomes</em><em> a widow or is divorced, doesn’t have any children, and goes back to her father’s family, she can eat her father’s food. </em><strong><sup>14</sup></strong><em>If any of you laymen unintentionally eat a sacred gift, add a fifth to it and give it to the priest. </em><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>Laymen are not to profane the sacred gift Israelites offer to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>; </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>so they’re fined for it</em><em>. </em><em><u>I’m the </u></em><em><u>L</u></em><em><u>ORD</u></em><em><u> that sanctifies them</u></em><em>.’”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 22:10-16</p>

### Unacceptable Sacrifices


<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> The LORD told Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>“A newborn ox, sheep, or goat will remain with its mother for a week. After that it’s acceptable as an offering by fire to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>28</sup></strong><em>But don’t slaughter an ox or sheep and its young the same day. </em><strong><sup>29</sup></strong><em>When you offer a sacrifice of thanksgiving to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>, do it so he will accept you. </em><strong><sup>30</sup></strong><em>Eat it the same day; don’t leave any till morning. </em><strong><sup>31</sup></strong><em>Keep my commandments that way. </em><strong><sup>32</sup></strong><em>Don’t profane my holy name; treat me as holy in Israel. </em><em><u>I’m the </u></em><em><u>L</u></em><em><u>ORD</u></em><em><u> that sanctifies you</u></em><em>, </em><strong><sup>33</sup></strong><em>who brought you out of Egypt to be your God.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 22:26-33</p>
