## Leviticus Chapter 24

### Pure Oil and Holy Bread

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The LORD said to Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>“Tell the people to bring you clear oil from pressed olives that can keep a lamp burning continually. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><strong><sup>-4</sup></strong><em>Outside the curtain of Testimony in the Tent of Meeting, Aaron will keep </em>the lamps on the lampstand<em> in order from evening to morning continually.</em> It’s<em> an ongoing permanent statute. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>“</em><em>Take </em><em>fine flour and bake </em><em>12</em><em> cakes</em><em>. Put .22 bushels</em><em> </em><sup>.2 ephah</sup><em><sup> </sup></em><em>in each one. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Set them in two stacks, six to a stack on the pure </em><em>gold</em><em> table. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>Put pure </em><em>frankincense beside each stack for the bread as God’s portion, </em><em>an offering by fire to the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>Every Sabbath </em>Aaron <em>will set this bread in order in front of the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>, a permanent covenant for Israel. </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>It’ll be for Aaron and his sons to eat in a sacred place. It’s the most sacred to </em>them<em> from the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>’s offerings by fire, </em>their <em>permanent portion.”</em><strong>  </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 24:1-9</p>

### A Blasphemer Stoned


<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The son of an Israelite woman and Egyptian father went out among the people and got into a fight with an Israelite man in the camp. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>The woman’s son blasphemed the Name and cursed; so they brought him to Moses. (His mother was Shelomith, daughter of Dibri, tribe of Dan.) <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>They put him in custody till the LORD made his commandment clear to Moses.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> The LORD told Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>“Take</em><em> him outside the camp</em><em>. The ones who</em><em> heard him </em><em>will </em><em>put their hands on his head</em><em> and</em><em> the </em><em>community</em><em> </em><em>will </em><em>stone him.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>Tell the people, ‘If anybody curses God, he’s considered guilty. </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>The community is to stone anybody that blasphemes the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em>. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>“Execute a murderer, </em><strong><sup>18</sup></strong><em>but anybody that kills an animal will make it good, life for life. </em><strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>If one person injures another, do the same thing to that person: </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>fracture for fracture, eye for eye, tooth for tooth.  </em>[<strong><sup>21</sup></strong>]<strong><sup> </sup></strong><strong><sup>22</sup></strong><em>There’ll be one standard for you and a foreigner</em><em>. </em><em><u>I’m the </u></em><em><u>L</u></em><em><u>ORD</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> Moses told the people, and they took outside the camp the one that had cursed, and stoned him.</span>
