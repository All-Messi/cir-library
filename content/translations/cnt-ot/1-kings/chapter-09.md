## 1 Kings Chapter 9


### The LORD Appears to Solomon


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126243241"><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Solomon had finished building the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple, the king’s palace, and everything he wanted to do, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> appeared to him a second time like he did at Gibeon. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ve heard your prayer and consecrated this Temple you’ve built by putting my name there permanently. My eyes and heart will always be there. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If you’ll follow me like your father did in the integrity of heart and godliness, do everything I tell you, keep my statutes and ordinances, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>then I’ll make your throne secure over Israel permanently like I promised David, ‘You’ll always have a descendant on Israel’s throne.’ </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“But if you or your sons turn away from me to serve and worship other gods, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ll remove Israel from the land I’ve given them, and throw away the Temple I’ve consecrated. Israel will become a proverb, a byword among the nations. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>This Temple will become a pile of ruins. Everybody that goes by will be amazed and gasp, ‘Why has the </span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>L</span></i><i><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> done this to this land and this Temple?’ </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>People will say, ‘Because they forsook the </span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> their God, who brought their ancestors out of Egypt. They adopted other gods and worshiped and served them.’”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                     </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 9:1-9</span></b></span></p>


### Solomon’s Agreement with Hiram


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At the end of the 20 years it took him to build the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple and the king’s palace, King Solomon gave Hiram 20 towns in Galilee. (</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hiram, king of Tyre, had supplied him with all the cedar, cypress, and gold he wanted.</span><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hiram came out from Tyre to see them, but he didn’t like them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“What are these towns you’ve given me, brother?”</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> They’re still called The Land of Cabul [<i>A Land as Good as Nothing</i>]. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Nevertheless</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, Hiram sent <i>Solomon</i> 4½ tons <sup>120 talents</sup> of gold.</span></span></p>


### Solomon’s Other Achievements


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126243583"><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>This is the conscripted labor King Solomon levied to build the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple, his own palace, the Millo, the wall of Jerusalem, Hazor, Megiddo, and Gezer. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Pharaoh, king of Egypt, had gone up and captured Gezer, burned it, killed the Canaanites in it, and gave it as a dowry to his daughter, Solomon’s wife. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Solomon rebuilt Gezer and the Lower Beth-horon, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Baalath, Tamar in the Wilderness of Judah, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>his storage cities, cities for his chariots and cavalry, and everything he wanted to build in Jerusalem, Lebanon, and the land he ruled. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>To this day Solomon has conscripted laborers from the Amorites, Hittites, Perizzites, Hivites, and Jebusites </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>that Israel couldn’t destroy. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But he didn’t conscript Israelites for forced labor. They were soldiers, officials, officers, captains, chariot commanders, and cavalry.          </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 9:10-22</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>550 chief officers oversaw Solomon’s work.                                        </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As soon as Pharaoh’s daughter came up from The City of David to the palace Solomon had built for her, he built the Millo.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Three times a year Solomon offered burnt offerings and peace offerings on the altar he built to the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, burning incense with them. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>And so he finished the Temple.</span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126243673"><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He also built a fleet of ships in Ezion-geber, near Eloth on the shore of the Red Sea in Edom. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hiram sent experienced seamen with the fleet along with Solomon’s men. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They went to Ophir and brought back 15¾ tons <sup>420 talents</sup> of gold to Solomon.              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 9:23-28</span></b></span></p>
