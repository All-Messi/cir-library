## Job Chapter 6

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>Job answered:</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>The arrows of the Almighty are </em>piercing<em> me;</em> <em>	</em><em>	my spirit is absorbing their poison;</em> <em>	</em><em>	God’s terrors are attacking me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>My mouth won’t touch them;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	they’re disgusting to me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>“Oh that I could receive what I ask for,</em> <em>	</em><em>	that God would grant me what I long for!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>I can’t get any help from inside me;</em> <em>	</em><em>	I can’t deliver myself.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 6:8-13</p>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>“Friends should be kind to people in despair; </em> <em>	</em><em>	otherwise, they’ll lose reverence for the Almighty.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>My brothers have been unreliable,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	like water in wadis you can’t depend on.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>Now they’re turbulent with melting ice and snow;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>now they’re dry and silent;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	when it’s hot, they stop running.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Their course winds into wasteland and disappears.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>They’re disappointed;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	when they arrive, they’re frustrated.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>You’ve been like that to me;</em> <em>	</em><em>	you see my misfortune and get scared.</em></span>
