## Job Chapter 3

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>Afterward Job cursed the day he was born: </strong>[<strong><sup>2</sup></strong>]</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>“Perish the day I was born, </em> <em>	</em><em>	the night I was conceived.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>May that be a dark day;</em> <em>	</em><em>	let God above not care about it</em> <em>	</em><em>	or any light shine on it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>Let darkness and gloom claim it,</em> <em>	</em><em>	dark clouds cover it, and darkness make it terrifying.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>Let darkness seize that night;</em> <em>	</em><em>	let it not celebrate among the days of the year</em> <em>	</em><em>	or be counted among the months.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>Let that night be barren,</em> <em>	</em><em>	and no joyful shout be heard in it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>Let them curse it who curse the daytime,</em> <em>	</em><em>	who dare to arouse Leviathan.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>Let its morning stars be blacked out;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	let it hope for light, but not get any,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	or see the eyelids of the morning;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>because it didn’t shut my mother’s womb</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	or hide trouble from me.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 3:1-10</p>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>“Why didn’t I die at birth—</em> <em>	</em><em>	 just come out of the womb and die?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>Why did the knees receive me</em> <em>	</em><em>	and the breasts that nursed me?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>Then I would’ve lain down in peace;</em><em>	</em><em>	</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	I would’ve slept and been at rest</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>with kings and advisors </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	that rebuilt ruins for themselves,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>with rulers that had gold</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and filled their houses with silver.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>Why wasn’t I a miscarriage that was discarded,</em> <em>	</em><em>	that never saw the light of day?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>Sinners stop raging in death,</em> <em>	</em><em>	and the weary rest.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Captives are at ease together;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they don’t hear a taskmaster.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>The least and the greatest are there;</em> <em>	</em><em>	slaves are free from their masters.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 3:11-19</p>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>“Why does light come to those who are suffering,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and life to the bitter;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>to people who long for death and it doesn’t come,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	who dig for it more than for hidden treasure;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>who celebrate greatly,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	glad to find the grave?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>Why is light given to someone with no future,</em> <em>	</em><em>	someone God has hemmed in with difficulties? </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>I sigh at the sight of food</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and pour out groans like water;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <em>because what I’m afraid of comes at me,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and what I dread happens to me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <em>I’m not at ease, I’m not at peace;</em> <em>	</em><em>	I’m not at rest; only trouble is coming.”</span>
