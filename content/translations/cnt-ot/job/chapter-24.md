## Job Chapter 24

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“Why doesn’t the Almighty maintain </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	a regular court schedule for judging?</em> <em>	</em><em>	Why do those who know him wait for him in vain?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>Some remove landmarks,</em> <em>	</em><em>	seize flocks and eat them,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>drive away donkeys that belong to the fatherless,</em> <em>	</em><em>	take widows’ oxen for pledges.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>They run the needy off the road;</em> <em>	</em><em>	the poor people have to hide </em>from them<em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>Like wild donkeys in the desert, </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em>the poor<em> labor at foraging for food,</em> <em>	</em><em>	looking for prey in the wild to feed their children.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>They gather fodder in the field</em> <em>	</em><em>	and glean sinners’ vineyards.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>They spend the night without enough clothes</em> <em>	</em><em>	to protect them against the cold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>They’re soaked from mountain rain</em> <em>	</em><em>	and huddle in the rocks for lack of shelter.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>“Others snatch the fatherless from the breast</em> <em>	</em><em>	and take pledges against the poor.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>They make the naked go without clothes</em> <em>	</em><em>	and take away sheaves from the hungry.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>They press out oil between the olive rows</em> <em>	</em><em>	and tread winepresses </em>for others<em> yet suffer thirst.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>From the city, the people groan,</em> <em>	</em><em>	the wounded cry out for help,</em> <em>	</em><em>	but God ignores their prayers.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:9-12</p>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>“Others rebel against the light;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they refuse to know its ways or stay on them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>A murderer gets up at dawn</em> <em>	</em><em>	to kill the poor and needy;</em> <em>	</em><em>	at night he’s a thief.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>An adulterer waits for twilight and says,</em> <em>	</em><em>	‘Nobody will see me’;</em> <em>	</em><em>	he disguises himself.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>In the dark they break into houses</em> <em>	</em><em>	and sleep in the daytime;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they’re not accustomed to light.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>Morning is the same to them as dense darkness; </em> <em>	</em><em>	they’re allies with the terrors of deep gloom.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:13-17</p>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>“But they’re swiftly carried away like foam on a creek;</em> <em>	</em><em>	their land is cursed;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they’re afraid to go in their own vineyards.</em><strong>	</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>Sheol consumes sinners</em> <em>	</em><em>	like drought and heat consume snow.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>Their mothers forget about them,</em> <em>	</em><em>	maggots feed on them till they’re not remembered;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they’re blown over like trees </em>in a windstorm.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>They prey on barren and childless women</em> <em>	</em><em>	and fail to help </em>needy <em>widows.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>But God uses his power to drag away the rich; </em> <em>	</em><em>	they may get established, </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	but they have no guarantee of life</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>He may let them live in security, </em> <em>	</em><em>	but he keeps an eye on their progress in life.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>They’re honored for a little while; then they’re gone;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they’re brought low and fade away like everything else,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	cut off like heads of grain.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <em>If that’s not so, who can prove me wrong</em> <em>	</em><em>	and make my speech pointless?”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:18-25</p>
