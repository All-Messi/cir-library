## Job Chapter 11

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>Zophar the Naamathite answered:</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>“Should your barrage of words go unchallenged,</em> <em>	</em><em>	and you exonerate yourself by talking a lot?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>Will your babble shut people up?</em> <em>	</em><em>	Will you mock and nobody rebuke you?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>You claim your beliefs are pure</em> <em>	</em><em>	and you’re innocent in God’s presence;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>but I just wish God would speak out against you</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>and disclose to you the secrets of wisdom,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	because sound wisdom is not simple;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	be assured that God doesn’t remember </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	just one part of your sin.</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 11:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>“Can you find out the deep things of God</em> <em>	</em><em>	or probe the limits of the Almighty?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>They’re higher than the sky—what can you do?</em> <em>	</em><em>	They’re deeper than Sheol—what can you know?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>They measure longer than the world</em> <em>	</em><em>	and wider than the sea.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>If he passes through or imprisons</em> <em>	</em><em>	or calls to judgment, who can stop him?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>He knows worthless people;</em> <em>	</em><em>	he knows about their sin without having to investigate.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>A stupid man can no more become intelligent</em> <em>	</em><em>	than a wild donkey can have a baby.</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 11:7-12</p>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>“If you set your heart right,</em> <em>	</em><em>	you can raise your hands and pray to him.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>If there’s sin in you, get rid of it;</em> <em>	</em><em>	don’t let it live in your house.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>Your life will become brighter than noonday;</em> <em>	</em><em>	darkness will be like morning.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Then you’ll have confidence because of hope,</em> <em>	</em><em>	be protected, and rest in safety.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>You’ll lie down, and no one will disturb you;</em><em>	</em> <em>	</em><em>	others will ask for your blessing.</em></span>
