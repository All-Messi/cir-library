## Job Chapter 37

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“My heart pounds when I think about this;</em> <em>	</em><em>	it feels like it’s out of place.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>Listen closely to his thundering,</em> <em>	</em><em>	the rumbling that comes from his mouth.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>He unleashes his lightning under the whole sky;</em> <em>	</em><em>	it travels to the horizon in every direction.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>After it his roars;</em> <em>	</em><em>	he thunders with his majestic voice</em> <em>	</em><em>	and doesn’t hold anything back.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>God thunders wondrously</em> <em>	</em><em>	and does extraordinary things we can’t comprehend.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>He tells the snow to fall on the ground</em> <em>	</em><em>	and the rain to be heavy.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>He stops everyone’s labor </em> <em>	</em><em>	so they can watch his work.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 37:1-7</p>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>Animals head for their dens</em> <em>	</em><em>	and stay in their lairs.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>The stormy wind comes from its rooms,</em> <em>	</em><em>	and the driving winds bring the cold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>God’s breath makes ice,</em> <em>	</em><em>	and the wide expanse of water freezes. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>He loads up dark clouds with moisture;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they disperse his lightning flashes.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>They change direction by his guidance;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they do whatever he commands across the world.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>He makes these things happen either to punish his people</em> <em>	</em><em>	or to water the earth and show his love.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 37:8-13</p>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>“Listen, Job, </em> <em>	</em><em>	stop and think about God’s wondrous works.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>Do you know how he controls the clouds</em> <em>	</em><em>	or makes lightning flash in them?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>Can you explain the layers of dark clouds,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	the wonders of One that knows everything,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>you whose clothes are hot</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	because the south wind isn’t blowing?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Can you spread out the sky like he does,</em> <em>	</em><em>	as hard as a bronze mirror?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>Teach us what to say to him;</em> <em>	</em><em>	we can’t draw up our case without your help!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>Should somebody let him know I want to speak?</em> <em>	</em><em>	Does anyone ever want to be destroyed?</em><strong> </strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>“We can’t look at the bright light in the sky</em> <em>	</em><em>	after the wind has cleared it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>Golden splendor comes from the north;</em> <em>	</em><em>	awesome majesty surrounds God.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>We can’t imagine how extremely powerful the Almighty is;</em> <em>	</em><em>	yet he doesn’t violate justice and abundant goodness.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>So people everywhere revere him</em> <em>	</em><em>	because he doesn’t value people that are wise in their own conceit.”</em></span>
