## Job Chapter 39

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“Do you know when mountain goats give birth?</em> <em>	</em><em>	Do you ever see a deer calving?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>Can you count the months of their gestation</em> <em>	</em><em>	or learn when they have their fawns?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>They crouch down to deliver their young.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>Their offspring grow strong out in the open;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they leave home and never return.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:1-4</p>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>“Who turned the wild donkey loose?</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	Who untied its ropes?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>I gave it the open country for a home,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	desert land to live in?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>It scorns city turmoil </em> <em>	</em><em>	and doesn’t have to listen to a driver shout at it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>It roams the mountains for pasture,</em> <em>	</em><em>	looking for anything green.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:5-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>“Will a wild ox consent to serve you</em> <em>	</em><em>	and spend the night at your manger?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>Can you harness it </em>to plow <em>in a furrow?</em> <em>	</em><em>	Will it harrow your fields?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>Will you depend on it because it’s strong</em> <em>	</em><em>	and leave your work for it to do?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>Will you trust it to bring your grain home</em> <em>	    and deliver it to your threshing floor?</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:9-12</p>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>“The ostrich’s wings flap happily,</em> <em>	</em><em>	but they don’t compare  </em> <em>	</em><em>	with the wings and feathers of a stork.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>She abandons her eggs in the ground</em> <em>	</em><em>	to stay warm in the sand.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>She forgets that a foot may crush them</em> <em>	</em><em>	or a wild ox may tramp on them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>She’s harsh toward her young as if they weren’t hers;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	though her work may be pointless, she doesn’t care,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>because God hasn’t made her wise</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	or given her intelligence.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>When she jumps up to run,</em> <em>	</em><em>	she laughs at a horse and its rider.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:13-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>“Do you give a horse its strength</em> <em>	</em><em>	or cover its neck with a mane?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>Can you make it leap like a locust?</em> <em>	</em><em>	Its majestic snorting is terrifying!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>It paws in the valley and frolics in its strength</em> <em>	</em><em>	and charges out to battle.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>It mocks fear and</em> <em>	</em><em>	doesn’t shy away from the sword.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>The quiver rattles against it,</em> <em>	</em><em>	the flashing spear and javelin</em> too<em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>It doesn’t stand still when the bugle signals;</em> <em>	</em><em>	with trembling rage, it races over the ground.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <em>As often as the bugle blows, it says, ‘Aha!’</em> <em>	</em><em>	It smells a battle a long way off,</em> <em>	</em><em>	the captain’s thundering, and the battle cry.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:19-25</p>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <em>“Does a hawk soar because you’re so smart</em> <em>	</em><em>	as it stretches its wings toward the south?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>Does an eagle mount up at your command</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and build its nest up high?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <em>It lives and lodges on the cliffs,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	in inaccessible places on the rocky crag.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <em>From there it spies out prey</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	that it can see from far away.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <em>Even its young gulp down blood;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	they’re right there when the kill goes down.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:26-30</p>
