## Job Chapter 12

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>Job answered:</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>“No doubt, you’re the people,</em> <em>	</em><em>	and wisdom will die with you!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>But I’m as smart as you are;</em> <em>	</em><em>	I’m not your inferior.</em> <em>	</em><em>	Who doesn’t know things like that?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>I’m a laughingstock to my friends,</em> <em>	</em><em>	one who called on God and he answered,</em> <em>	</em><em>	a just and blameless man; </em> <em>	</em><em>	yet I’m a laughingstock.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>In the thoughts of people at ease, </em> <em>	</em><em>	 there’s contempt for disaster;</em> <em>	</em><em>	they’re ready to give a shove to those whose feet are slipping.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>Destroyers prosper;</em> <em>	</em><em>	those who provoke God may feel secure,</em> <em>	</em><em>	but he’ll bring them under his control.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 12:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>“Ask the animals, they’ll teach you;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	the birds will explain.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>Speak to the earth; it will inform you;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	have the fish in the sea tell you.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>Who among them doesn’t know </em> <em>	</em><em>	that the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em><em> has brought this disaster on me?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>The life of every living thing lies in his hand,</em> <em>	</em><em>	including the breath of each person.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>Doesn’t the ear test words</em> <em>	</em><em>	like the palate tastes food?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>Old people have wisdom;</em> <em>	</em><em>	long life brings insight.</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 12:7-12</p>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>“He has wisdom and power;</em> <em>	</em><em>	counsel and insight lie in him.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>He tears down, and no one can rebuild;</em> <em>	</em><em>	he shuts people in, and nobody can release them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>If he withholds water, things dry up;</em> <em>	</em><em>	if he sends it, the land floods.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>Strength and sound wisdom lie in him;</em> <em>	</em><em>	deceived and deceiver are under his control.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>He makes advisors walk barefoot</em> <em>	</em><em>	and makes fools out of judges.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>He takes off kings’ belts</em> <em>	</em><em>	and binds their waists with a rope.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 12:13-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>He makes priests walk barefoot</em> <em>	</em><em>	and overthrows those who feel secure.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>He silences the ones that people trust</em> <em>	</em><em>	and takes away the discernment of elders.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>He pours contempt on nobles</em> <em>	</em><em>	and disarms the strong.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>He reveals what’s deep in the dark</em> <em>	</em><em>	and brings darkness into light.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>He makes nations great, then destroys them;</em> <em>	</em><em>	he enlarges nations, then lets them go into exile.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>He takes away understanding from the chiefs of the people</em> <em>	</em><em>	and makes them wander in pathless desolation.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <em>They grope in the dark with no light;</em> <em>	</em><em>	he makes them stagger like drunks.</span>
