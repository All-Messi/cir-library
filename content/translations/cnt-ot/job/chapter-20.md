## Job Chapter 20

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>Zophar the Naamathite answered:</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>“My thoughts make me answer</em> <em>	</em><em>	because I’m in turmoil inside.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>I’ve listened to your insults,</em> <em>	</em><em>	and I’m answering from what I understand.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>Don’t you know from of old—</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	from the time </em>God<em> put people on earth—</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>that the triumph of sinners is short-lived;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	the joy of the godless is temporary.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 20:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>Though they’re as tall as the sky,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	though their heads are in the clouds,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>they’ll perish forever like their own feces;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	people that saw them before will say, ‘Where’d they go?’</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>They’ll fly away like a dream, </em> <em>	</em><em>	and nobody will find them; </em> <em>	</em><em>	they’ll vanish like a nightmare. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>People that saw them before won’t see them again;</em> <em>	</em><em>	 their family won’t have them around anymore.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>Their offspring will have to give back </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	the wealth they’d confiscated</em> <em>	</em><em>	 and beg from the poor</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>They may have youthful health,</em> <em>	</em><em>	but it’ll lie down with them in the dust.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 20:6-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>“Though evil is sweet in their mouth</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and they hide it under their tongue,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>though they like the taste of it </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and won’t spit it out,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>yet the food in their stomach will ferment </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	into the venom of asps.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>Though they swallow wealth, they’ll vomit it up;</em> <em>	</em><em>	God will purge it from their stomach.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>They’ll suck the poison of asps;</em> <em>	</em><em>	the viper’s tongue will slay them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>They won’t admire the streams</em> <em>	</em><em>	that flow with honey and curds.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>They’ll lose everything they worked for and couldn’t swallow;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	they won’t even enjoy the profits from their trading,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>because they’ve oppressed the poor</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	and seized houses they didn’t build.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>“Since their greed is never satisfied,</em> <em>	</em><em>	they won’t keep anything they enjoy.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>There won’t be anything left for them to devour;</em> <em>	</em><em>	their prosperity won’t last.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>They’ll have so much stuff they can’t move;</em> <em>	</em><em>	the full force of their misery will stack up against them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>While they’re filling their stomach,</em> <em>	</em><em>	God will be sending his fierce anger at them</em> <em>	</em><strong><em><sup>	</sup></em></strong><em>and raining it on them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>They may escape an iron weapon</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	only to have a bronze arrow point run them through.</em></span>
