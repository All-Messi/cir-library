## 2 Kings Chapter 25


### The Fall of Jerusalem


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126414640"><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The
<b>tenth day, tenth month, ninth year </b>of his reign, Nebuchadnezzar and his army came against Jerusalem, camped against it, and built a siege wall around it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The city lay under siege till <b>Zedekiah’s eleventh year</b>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The
<b>ninth day, fourth month</b>, the famine had become so severe in the city that the common people didn’t have any food. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then
<i>the Babylonians</i> broke into the city, and the soldiers fled at night through the gate between the two walls by the king’s garden, though the Babylonians were all around the city. They went by way of the Arabah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the army chased the king and overtook him in the plains by Jericho, and his whole army scattered. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They captured him and took him to the king of Babylon at Riblah, and he passed sentence on him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They executed Zedekiah’s sons in front of him and then put out his eyes. They bound him with bronze restraints and took him to Babylon.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The <b>seventh day, fifth month, nineteenth year</b> of Nebuchadnezzar, Nebuzaradan, commander of the guard and an official of the king, came to Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He burned the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple, the king’s palace, and all the houses in Jerusalem, including every important building. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Babylonian army tore down the walls around Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He exiled the rest of the people in the city, the deserters, and the rest of the population. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He left some of the very poor to take care of the vineyards and fields.<b>                        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 25:1-12</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126414868"><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Babylonians broke to pieces the bronze pillars, the stands, and the bronze sea that were in the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple, and carried the bronze to Babylon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They took away the pots, shovels, snuffers, spoons, and bronze items used in the <i>Temple
</i>service. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The captain of the guard took away the firepans and basins, what was fine gold and silver, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the two pillars, the sea, and the stands Solomon had made for the Temple; the bronze from these was more than they could weigh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The two pillars were bronze plated 27ft tall. Their capitals were 4<b>½</b>ft high with a bronze network and pomegranates all the way around. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The commander of the guard took Seraiah the chief priest, Zephaniah the second priest, the three officers of the Temple, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 official that oversaw the soldiers, 5 of the king’s advisers found in the city; the secretary for the commander of the army, who mustered the common people; and 60 men found in the city. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He took them to the king of Babylon at Riblah, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>who executed them there in Hamath. Judah was in exile from its land.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Nebuchadnezzar appointed Gedaliah son of Ahikam son of Shaphan as governor for the people he left in the country. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the commanders of the forces and their men heard it, they came to Gedaliah at Mizpah: Ishmael son of Nethaniah, Johanan son of Kareah, Seraiah son of Tanhumeth the Netophathite, Jaazaniah son of the Maacathite, and their men. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Gedaliah swore to them and their men, <i>“Don’t be afraid of the Babylonians. Live in the land and serve the king of Babylon, and you’ll be okay.”</i><b>      </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 25:13-24</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126415018"><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But in the <b>seventh month</b>, Ishmael son of Nethaniah son of Elishama from the royal family, came with ten men and struck Gedaliah down. He died along with the Jews and Babylonians that were with him in Mizpah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then the people, least and greatest, and the commanders of the forces went to Egypt for fear of the Babylonians. </span></span></p>


### Jehoiachin Released


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The <b>twenty-seventh day, twelfth month, thirty-seventh year</b> of Jehoiachin’s exile, Evil-merodach, king of Babylon, in his first year, released him from prison. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He spoke graciously to him and set his throne above the other kings in Babylon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoiachin changed out of his prison clothes and had his meals in the king’s presence the rest of his life. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king gave him a regular serving each day.  <b>       </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 25:25-30</span></b></span></p>
