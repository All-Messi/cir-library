## 2 Kings Chapter **25**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>The <strong>te</strong><strong>nth day, tenth month, ninth year </strong>of his reign, Nebuchadnezzar and his army came against Jerusalem, camped against it, and built a siege wall around it. <strong><sup>2</sup></strong>The city lay under siege till <strong>Zedekiah’s</strong><strong> eleventh year</strong>. <strong><sup>3</sup></strong>The <strong>ninth day, fourth month</strong>, the famine had become so severe in the city that the common people didn’t have any food. <strong><sup>4</sup></strong>Then <em>the </em><em>Babylonians</em> broke into the city, and the soldiers fled at night through the gate between the two walls by the king’s garden, though the Babylonians were all around the city. They went by way of the Arabah. <strong><sup>5</sup></strong>But the army chased the king and overtook him in the plains by Jericho, and his whole army scattered. <strong><sup>6</sup></strong>They captured him and took him to the king of Babylon at Riblah, and he passed sentence on him. <strong><sup>7</sup></strong>They executed Zedekiah’s sons in front of him and then put out his eyes. They bound him with bronze restraints and took him to Babylon.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>The <strong>seventh day, fifth month, nineteenth year</strong> of Nebuchadnezzar, Nebuzaradan, commander of the guard and an official of the king, came to Jerusalem. <strong><sup>9</sup></strong>He burned the LORD’s Temple, the king’s palace, and all the houses in Jerusalem, including every important building. <strong><sup>10</sup></strong>The Babylonian army tore down the walls around Jerusalem. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>He exiled the rest of the people in the city, the deserters, and the rest of the population. <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>He left some of the very poor to take care of the vineyards and fields.<strong>	</strong></span>
