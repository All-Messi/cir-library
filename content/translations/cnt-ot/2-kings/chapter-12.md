## 2 Kings Chapter 12


### Joash Repairs the Temple


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126340599"><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>seventh year of Jehu</b>, <b>Jehoash</b> became king, and ruled for <b>40 years in Jerusalem</b>. His mother was Zibiah of Beersheba. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His whole life he did what was good like Jehoiada instructed him, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>except he didn’t take away the high places. The people still sacrificed and burned incense on them.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4-5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoash told the priests, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><a name="_Hlk137207574"><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Let the priests take the money for sacred things that’s brought into the L</span></i></a><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple and make any repairs on the Temple—money collected from the census, payments for vows, and voluntary offerings.” </span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>By his <b>twenty-third year</b>, the priests <i>still </i>hadn’t made the repairs.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said, <i>“Why aren’t you repairing them? Don’t take any more money from your acquaintances. Hand it over for the damages to the Temple.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The priests agreed not to take any more money and to let others do the repairs.<b>
                         </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 12:1-8</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126340831"><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Jehoiada took a chest, bored a hole in the lid, and put it on the right side the altar, where people come into the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple. The priests that guarded the threshold put the money in it that people brought in. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the chest was getting full, the king’s secretary and the high priest counted it and tied it up in bags. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11-12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They weighed out the money to the ones that oversaw the Temple. They paid it to the carpenters, builders, masons, and stonecutters, <i>and</i> bought the lumber, hewn stone, and any <i>other </i>costs for the repairs. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But they didn’t make silver cups, snuffers, bowls, bugles, any gold or silver items from the money. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They gave it to the repairmen </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and didn’t require any accounting from them because they were honest. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Money from guilt offerings and sin offerings didn’t come into the Temple because it belonged to the priests.<b>                           </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 12:9-16</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At that time, Hazael, king of Aram, went and conquered Gath, then turned toward Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoash/<i>Joash</i> took the sacred things that Jehoshaphat, Jehoram, and Ahaziah had dedicated, along with his own sacred things and the gold in the treasuries in the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple and the king’s palace, and sent it to Hazael. So he let Jerusalem alone.
</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Joash/<i>Jehoash</i> are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20-21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His officers led a conspiracy, and Jozacar son of Shimeath with Jehozabad son of Shomer assassinated him at Beth-millo on the way down to Silla. They buried him with his ancestors in The City of David. His son <b>Amaziah</b> became king.<b>
                        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 12:17-21</span></b></span></p>
