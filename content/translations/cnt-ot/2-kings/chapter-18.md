## 2 Kings Chapter 18


### Hezekiah Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>third year of Hoshea</b>, <b>Hezekiah</b> son of Ahaz became king in Judah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was 25 and ruled for 29 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Abi daughter of Zechariah. </span><a name="_Hlk126378548"></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did what was right like David did. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He removed the high places, broke down the sacred pillars, cut down the Asherah poles, and broke in pieces the bronze “serpent” Moses had made, because the Israelites had been burning incense to it; they called it Nehushtan. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He trusted in the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> God of Israel so that no king in Judah was like him before or after. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He held to the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and kept the commandments he gave Moses. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was with him and he prospered wherever he went. He revolted against the king of Assyria </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and defeated the Philistines all the way to Gaza and its territory, from outpost to fortified city.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>fourth year of King Hezekiah</b> <b>(</b></span><b><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>seventh year of <u>Hoshea</u></span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span></b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, Shalmaneser, king of Assyria, came up and laid siege to Samaria. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At the end of three years, he captured it (</span><b><span style='font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>sixth year of Hezekiah</span></b><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, <b>ninth year of Hoshea</b></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He exiled Israel to Assyria and put them in Halah, on the Habor, the Gozan River, and in towns of the Medes. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did it</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> because they wouldn’t obey the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> their God. They sinned against his covenant in everything Moses commanded.<b> </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 18:1-12</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126378802"><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>fourteenth year of King Hezekiah</b>, Sennacherib, king of Assyria, attacked the fortified towns in Judah and took them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah sent to the king of Assyria at Lachish, <i>“I’ve done wrong. Withdraw from me; whatever you impose I’ll bear.” </i>The king of Assyria required 11⅓ tons <sup>300 talents of</sup> silver and 1¼ tons <sup>30 talents</sup> of gold. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He gave him all the silver in the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple and the treasuries in the king’s house. </span><b><sup><span style='font-size:16.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>16</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah cut the <i>gold</i> off the doors of the Temple and the doorposts he’d overlaid and gave it to him.<b>                                                        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 18:13-16</span></b></span></p>


### Sennacherib Threatens Jerusalem


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then the king of Assyria sent the Tartan, the Rabsaris, and the Rabshakeh from Lachish with a large army. They went up to Jerusalem and stood by the conduit of the upper pool, on the highway to the Fuller’s Field. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they called to the king, Eliakim son of Hilkiah, who managed the palace; Shebnah the secretary, and Joah son of Asah the recorder came out to them. </span><a name="_Hlk126378862"></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Rabshakeh said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Tell Hezekiah the great king of Assyria says, ‘What’s this confidence you have? </span></i><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Your advice and strength for war are empty words. Who are you relying on that you’ve rebelled against me? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You’re relying on Egypt that crushed reed as a staff. If anyone leans on it, it will pierce their hand. That’s the way Pharaoh is to everybody that relies on him. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But if you tell me you’re trusting in the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> your God, isn’t he the one whose high places and altars Hezekiah has taken away and told Judah and Jerusalem to worship at this altar in Jerusalem?’ </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Come now, make a deal with my master, and I’ll give you 2,000 horses if you can put riders on them. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>How can you drive off </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>even <i>one of my master’s least officers and rely on Egypt for chariots and cavalry? </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Haven’t I come up with the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s approval to destroy this place? He told me to attack this country and destroy it.”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> 2 Kg 18:17-25</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126378947"><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Eliakim, Shebnah, and Joah told the Rabshakeh, <i>“Speak to us in Aramaic; we understand it. Don’t speak to us in Hebrew in front of the people on the wall.”
</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But he shouted in Hebrew, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Has my master sent me just to you and your master to say this? </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hasn’t he sent me <i>to the people sitting on the wall, doomed to eat their own feces and drink their own urine with you?” </i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“The great king of Assyria says, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>‘Don’t let Hezekiah deceive you; he can’t deliver you. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t let him make you trust in the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, “The L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> will deliver us. This city won’t be delivered to the king of Assyria.” </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t listen to him. Surrender and come out to me. Eat from your own grapevines and fig trees, and drink water from your own cisterns, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>till I come and take you away to a land like yours, with grain and new wine, bread and vineyards, olive trees and honey so you won’t die. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Have any nation’s gods delivered its people from the king of Assyria? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Where are the gods of Hamath and Arpad, of Sepharvaim, Hena, and Ivvah? Have they delivered Samaria from me? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Who among the gods of the countries have delivered them from me, that the L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> should deliver Jerusalem from me?’”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The people didn’t respond; the king told them not to answer him.<i> </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Eliakim, Shebna, and Joah went to Hezekiah with clothes torn and told him what the Rabshakeh had said.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> 2 Kg 18:26-37</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>    </span></b></span></p>
