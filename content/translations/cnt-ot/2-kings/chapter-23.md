## 2 Kings Chapter 23


### They took word back to the king.
2 Kg 22:8-20



### Josiah Renews the Covenant


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126411787"><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then the king gathered the elders of Judah and Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He and the men of Judah, the residents in Jerusalem, the priests and prophets—people least and greatest—went up to the Temple. He read <i>to them</i> the covenant they’d found. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He stood by the pillar and made a covenant with the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> to keep his commandments, testimonies, and statutes with all his heart and soul—to carry out the covenant written in that scroll. The people entered into the covenant.    </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 23:1-3                  </span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he told Hilkiah the high priest, the priests of the second order, and the doorkeepers to remove from the Temple the items made for Baal, Asherah, and the host of heaven. He burned them outside Jerusalem in the fields by the Kidron and carried their ashes to Bethel. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did away with the idolatrous priests Judah’s kings had appointed to burn incense in the high places in the towns of Judah and around Jerusalem, those who burned incense to Baal, the sun, moon, and constellations—the host of heaven. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He removed the Asherah pole from the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple to the Kidron and burned it. He ground it to powder and threw it on the graves of the common people. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He tore down the house of the male cult prostitutes in the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple, where the women were weaving hangings for the Asherah pole. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he brought the priests from the towns in Judah and defiled the high places where the priests had burned incense from Geba to Beersheba. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126412500"></a><a name="_Hlk126412891"><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He tore down the high places to the left of the gate of Joshua, governor of the city. </span></a><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The priests of the high places couldn’t serve at the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s altar in Jerusalem, but they were allowed to eat bread without yeast with the other priests. <b>                                                                  </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 23:4-9</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He defiled Topheth in the Valley of Hinnom so nobody could burn a son or daughter as an offering to Molech. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He removed the horses Judah’s kings had dedicated to the sun—at the entrance of the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple, by the quarters of Nathan-melech the official, which were in the precincts. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>He burned the chariots of the sun. </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king broke down the altars that were on the roof, the upper room of Ahaz, that the kings of Judah had made. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>He broke down</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> the altars Manasseh had made in the two courts of the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple. He pulverized them there and threw their dust in the Kidron. </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He defiled the high places east of Jerusalem and to the right of the Mount of Corruption that Solomon had built for Ashtoreth the abomination of the Sidonians, Chemosh the abomination of Moab, and Milcom the abomination of the Ammonites. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He broke the sacred pillars in pieces, cut down the Asherah poles, and desecrated their places by spreading human bones in them.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 23:10-14</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126413227"><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In addition, he dismantled the altar at Bethel and the high place Jeroboam</span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> had made. He demolished its stones, ground them to lime, and burned the Asherah pole. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he turned around and saw tombs on the hillside, he had bones out of them burned on the altar to defile it like the message from the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> that the man of God had delivered. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he said, <i>“What’s this monument?”</i> The men in the city told him, <i>“It’s the grave of the man of God from Judah that declared what you’ve done against the altar at Bethel.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told them not to disturb his bones; they didn’t disturb them or the bones of the prophet from Samaria.                                                </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 23:15-18</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>    </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Josiah removed the shrines from the high places in the towns of Samaria—<i>structures</i> the kings of Israel had built. He did to them what he’d done in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>On the altars he killed the priests associated with the high places and burned human bones on them. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Then he went back to Jerusalem.                   </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told the people, <i>“Celebrate Passover to the L</i></span><i><span style='font-size: 9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> your God as written in this scroll.” </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They hadn’t celebrated it since the time of the judges or during the time of the kings of Israel and Judah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But in the <b>eighteenth year of King Josiah</b>, they observed it in Jerusalem. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                                                                            </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 23:19-23</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126413626"><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Josiah removed the mediums, spiritists, household gods, idols, and detestable things in Judah and Jerusalem, to conform to what the scroll said that Hilkiah found. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>No king before or after him turned to the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> with all his heart, soul, and might, according to the whole Law of Moses. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> didn’t withdraw his fierce anger against Judah that Manasseh had provoked, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ll remove Judah from me like I did Israel. I’ll throw Jerusalem aside, my chosen city, and the Temple that I said I’d identify with.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Josiah are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>During his reign, Pharaoh Neco, king of Egypt, went up to the king of Assyria to the River Euphrates. King Josiah went to engage him. When <i>Pharaoh</i> saw him, he killed him at Megiddo. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His officers hauled his body in a chariot from Megiddo to Jerusalem and buried him in his own tomb. The people took his son Jehoahaz and anointed him king.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 23:24-30</span></b></span></p>


### Jehoahaz Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoahaz</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was 23 and ruled for <b>3 months</b> in Jerusalem. His mother was Hamutal, daughter of Jeremiah of Libnah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did evil like his ancestors. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Pharaoh Neco imprisoned him at Riblah in Hamath so he couldn’t reign in Jerusalem. He imposed on the country a fine of 3¾ tons <sup>100 talents</sup> of silver and 75½ lbs <sup>a talent</sup> of gold. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He made <b>Eliakim</b> son of Josiah king in place of Josiah and changed his name to <b>Jehoiakim</b>. He took Jehoahaz away to Egypt, and he died there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoiakim gave Pharaoh the silver and gold, but he taxed the land to do it. He exacted the silver and gold according to his evaluation <i>of their wealth</i>.      </span></span></p>
