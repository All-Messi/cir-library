## 2 Kings Chapter 8


### The Shunammite’s Land Restored


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Elisha told the woman whose son he’d revived, <i>“Take your family and live temporarily wherever you can. The L</i></span><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> has called for a seven-year famine here.” </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They went to stay with the Philistines. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Seven years later, she came back and appealed to the king for her house and farm. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was talking to Gehazi, the man of God’s servant, <i>“Tell me the great things Elisha has done.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As he was telling him how he’d restored life to <i>a boy</i>, she appealed to the king for her house and farm. Gehazi said, <i>“My lord the king, that’s the woman, and that’s her son!” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the king asked her, she told him about it. He appointed an officer to give her back everything, including the crops since she left.<b>             </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 8:1-6</span></b></span></p>


### Hazael Murders Ben-Hadad


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126334754"><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Elisha went to Damascus. Benhadad, king of Aram, was sick and heard that the man<i> </i>of God was there<i>.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told Hazael, <i>“Take him a gift and ask the L</i></span><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> by him if I’ll recover.” </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hazael went to meet him with a gift, every kind of good thing in Damascus—40 camel loads. He stood in front of him and said, <i>“Your son Benhadad, king of Aram, has sent me to ask you if he’ll recover from his sickness.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Elisha said,<i> “He’ll recover, but the L</i></span><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> has shown me that he’ll certainly die.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He stared at him till he was embarrassed, and the man of God cried. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hazael said, <i>“Why are you crying, Sir?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Because I know the terrible things you’ll do to Israel. You’ll set their fortresses on fire, kill their young men with the sword, dash their little ones to pieces, and rip open their pregnant women.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“What am I, a dog, to do things like that?”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Elisha answered, <i>“The L</i></span><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> has shown me that you’ll be king of Aram.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went back to his master, who said, <i>“What did Elisha say to you?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“He said you’d recover.”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day <i>Hazael</i> dipped a blanket in water and pressed it over Benhadad’s face to kill him. Hazael became king.<b>   </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 8:7-15</span></b></span></p>


### Jehoram Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126335179"><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>fifth year of </b><u>Joram</u>/<i>Jehoram </i>son of Ahab, king of Israel—<i>who ruled at the time of </i>Jehoshaphat, king of Judah—<b>Jehoram</b> son of Jehoshaphat became king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was 32 and ruled for <b>8 years in Jerusalem</b>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He followed Israel’s kings like Ahab had done because he’d married Ahab’s daughter. He did evil, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>but for David’s sake the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> wasn’t willing to destroy Judah. He’d promised to always give him a lamp through his descendants.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>During his reign, Edom revolted from Judah and appointed a king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joram and his chariots crossed over to Zair. He got up at night and struck at the Edomites that had surrounded him and the captains of the chariots, but his army fled to their tents. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Edom has been in revolt against Judah ever since. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Joram/<i>Jehoram</i> are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He died and they buried him in The City of David. His son Ahaziah became king. <b>                                                         </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 8:16-24</span></b></span></p>


### Ahaziah Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126335367"><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>twelfth year of </b><u>Joram</u><i>/</i>Jehoram son of Ahab, king of Israel, <b>Ahaziah</b> son of Jehoram began to rule. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was 22 and ruled for 1 year in Jerusalem. His mother was Athaliah, granddaughter of Omri, king of Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did evil like Ahab’s family <i>had done</i> because he was Ahab’s son-in-law. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went to war along with Joram against Hazael, king of Aram in Ramoth-gilead, and the Arameans wounded him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>King Joram came back to Jezreel to heal up.
