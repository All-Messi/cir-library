## 2 Kings Chapter 16


### Ahaz Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>seventeenth year of Pekah</b>, <b>Ahaz</b> son of Jotham became king. </span><a name="_Hlk126378250"></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was 20 and ruled for <b>16 years in Jerusalem</b>. He didn’t do what was right like David did. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He lived like the kings in Israel, and even burned his son as an offering like the horrible things the nations did that the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> had <i>originally</i> driven out. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He sacrificed and burned incense on the high places and hills under every green tree. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Rezin, king of Aram, and Pekah, king of Israel, came up to Jerusalem. They laid siege to Ahaz but couldn’t conquer him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At that time, Rezin recovered Elath and cleared the Judeans out of it entirely. Arameans came to Elath and still live there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahaz sent ambassadors to Tiglath-pileser, <i>“I’m your servant and vassal. Come up and deliver me from the kings of Aram and Israel, who are rising up against me.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahaz took the silver and gold in the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple and the king’s treasuries and sent a gift to the king of Assyria. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went up and conquered Damascus, exiled <i>its people </i>to Kir, and executed Rezin.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                                2 Kg 16:1-9</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126378178"><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>King Ahaz went to Damascus to meet Tiglath-pileser and saw the altar at Damascus. He sent to Urijah the priest the pattern of the altar, its model, and details. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Urijah built an altar like it before Ahaz got back. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he got there and saw it, he went up to it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He burned his burnt offering and meal offering, and poured his liquid offering and sprinkled the blood of his peace offerings on the altar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The bronze altar that was in front of the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> he brought from between his altar and the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple and put it on the north side of his altar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he commanded Urijah, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“On the great altar, burn the morning burnt offering, the evening meal offering, the king’s burnt offering and meal offering, with the people’s burnt offering, meal offering, and liquid offerings. Sprinkle on it the blood of the burnt offering and sacrifice. But the bronze altar will be for me to ask by.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;'><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Urijah did what he said.<b>                                                                                               </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 16:10-16</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk126378105"><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Ahaz cut off the borders of the stands and removed the laver from them. He took down the sea from over the bronze oxen and put it on a stone pavement. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Because of the king of Assyria, he took from the L</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s Temple the covered way for the Sabbath that they’d built in the Temple, as well as the outer entry for the king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Ahaz are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Books of Judah</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He died and they buried him in The City of David. His son <b>Hezekiah</b> reigned.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Kg 16:17-20</span></b></span></p>
