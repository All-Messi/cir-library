## Esther Chapter 6


### The King Honors Mordecai


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpFirst" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk124859270"><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That night the king couldn’t get to sleep, so he had them bring the record book and read it to him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Chronicles </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>wrote that Mordecai had reported about Bigthana and Teresh, two of the king’s eunuchs, the doorkeepers that had tried to lay hands on King Ahasuerus. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king asked, <i>“What honor’s been given to him for that?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>His attendants said, <i>“Nothing.”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king said, <i>“Who’s in the court?”</i> Haman had just come into the outer court of the king’s palace to speak to him about hanging Mordecai on the gallows he’d built. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king’s aides said, <i>“Haman’s standing in the court.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> <i>“Let him come in.”</i>                                                                                              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Esther 6:1-5</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Haman came in, the king asked him, <i>“What should be done for a man the king wants to honor?”  </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Haman said to himself, <a name="_Hlk118203981"><i>“Who would he want to honor more than me?” </i></a></span><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said<i>, </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Have them bring a royal robe you’ve worn, a horse you have ridden, and a royal crown that’s been on your head. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hand the robe and the horse to one of your most noble princes and have them array him. Have them lead him on horseback through the city square and proclaim, ‘This is what should be done for a man the king wants to honor.’”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                    </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Esther 6:6-9</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king told Haman, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><a name="_Hlk124859375"><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Hurry and take the robes and the horse like you said, and do that for Mordecai the Jew that’s sitting at my gate.</span></i></a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> That’s what I’ll <i>do for the man I want to honor.”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Haman took the robe and horse and arrayed Mordecai. He led him on horseback through the city square and proclaimed, <i>“This is what’s done for the man the king wants to honor.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Mordecai went back to the king’s gate, but Haman hurried home, mourning with his head covered. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told Zeresh and his friends what happened. His wise men and Zeresh said, <i>“If Mordecai is a Jew that you’ve begun to fall in front of, you won’t overcome him. You’ll fall to him.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>While they were still talking, the king’s eunuchs arrived and took him to the banquet Esther had prepared.<b>                                                                                                                                       </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Esther 6:10-14</span></b></span></p>
