## Isaiah Chapter 36


### Sennacherib Invades Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk86931040"></a><a name="_Hlk123306516"><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> </a><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the fourteenth year of King Hezekiah</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, Sennacherib king of Assyria attacked the fortified towns in Judah and took them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <a name="_Hlk148474527"><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>He sent the Rabshakeh from Lachish to Jerusalem with a large army. </span></a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They took position by the aqueduct to the upper pool on the road to the Fuller’s Field. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Eliakim son of Hilkiah, the palace administrator; Shebna, the court secretary; and Joah son of Asaph, the royal historian, went out to meet them.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Rabshakeh said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Tell Hezekiah the great king of Assyria says, ‘What’s this confidence you claim to have?
</span></i><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Empty talk can’t substitute for military strategy and power. Who are you depending on when you rebel against me? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Are you depending on <u>Egypt</u> as a staff? If you lean on that splintering reed, it’ll puncture your hand. That’s the way Pharaoh operates with everybody that depends on him. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If you tell me you’re trusting in <u>the </u>L</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> your God, isn’t he the One whose high places and altars Hezekiah has closed down, and made Judah and Jerusalem worship at this altar </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>in Jerusalem<i>? </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Come on, make a deal with my master: I’ll give you 2,000 horses if you can put riders on them. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Can you drive off even the least of my master’s contingents even with the help of Egypt’s chariots and cavalry? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Anyway, haven’t I </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>actually <i>come up at the L</i></span><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ORD</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>’s behest to destroy this place? He’s told me to attack this land and destroy it.’”      </span></i></span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>‘Don’t let Hezekiah deceive you; he can’t deliver you. </span></i></span>

<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>till I come and take you away to a land like yours, a land with grain and new wine, bread and vineyards. </span></i></span>
