## Isaiah Chapter 27

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> At that time, the LORD will punish Leviathan with his fierce, big strong sword, Leviathan the swift, slithering snake. He’ll kill the dragon that’s in the sea.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> In time to come, Jacob will take root; Israel will sprout and blossom, and fill the world with fruit.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Has <em>the </em><em>L</em><em>ORD</em> struck <em>Israel</em> the way he struck <em>Israel’s</em> strikers? Has he slain Israel the way he slew <em>Israel’s</em> slayers?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> No, he contended with <em>Israel</em> by banishing them, expelling them with his fierce east wind.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> So that’s how he’ll purge Jacob’s iniquity; that’s what pardoning its sin will cost, when <em>God</em> makes <em>pagan </em>altar stones like pulverized chalk, when he leaves no Asherah pole or incense altar standing.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The fortified towns will be deserted, their houses empty as in open country; calves will graze there and bed down; they’ll chew on brush and branches there.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> When those branches dry up, they’ll break off and women will gather them for kindling; this nation doesn’t use any sense; so their Maker won’t show them any mercy; their Creator won’t be gracious to them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Yet in time, Israel, the LORD will start gleaning from the Euphrates <em>River</em> to the River of Egypt and gather you up one by one. <strong><sup>13</sup></strong>A large trumpet will blow, and the exiles who were perishing in Assyria and Egypt will come to Jerusalem to worship the LORD on his holy mountain.</span>
