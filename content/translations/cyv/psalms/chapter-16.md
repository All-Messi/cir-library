## Psalms Chapter 16

### A Mikhtam of David

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Protect me, Yahveh; you’re my refuge.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> I say, “You’re my Lord; apart from you, I don’t have anything good.”</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The godly people on earth are the noble ones I delight in.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Trouble multiplies for those who rush off to some other god; I won’t pour out blood offerings with them or even say their names.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Yahveh is my permanent possession, my cup; he holds in safekeeping what’s allotted to me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The boundary lines of my inheritance fall in good places; it’s a beautiful land.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 16:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> I’ll bless Yahveh that guides me; even at night my heart teaches me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> I keep himY continually beside me, at my right hand so I don’t get unsettled.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> So I’m happy and celebrate; my body will also rest secure</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> because you won’t abandon me in Sheol, or let your Holy One decay.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> You’ll show me how to live; there’s total joy when you’re around, with eternal delights with you.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 16:7-11</p>
