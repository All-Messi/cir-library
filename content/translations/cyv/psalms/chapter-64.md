## Psalms Chapter 64

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Listen to my complaint, God; keep me from living in dread of my enemies.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Hide me from the secret plots of bad people, from the schemes of sinners</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> that sharpen their tongue like a sword, that aim bitter words like arrows,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> that shoot at innocent people from hiding places, and do it suddenly without qualms.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> They latch onto sinful purposes and talk secretly about setting traps; they say, “Who will notice?”</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> They look for ways to be unjust, “We’re ready with a well-conceived plot,” because the human mind and heart are cunning.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 64:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> But God will wound them with an arrow</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> and make them trip over their tongues! Everybody that sees them will shake their heads.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Then everybody will be respectful and talk about what God’s done and ponder what he’s made.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Good people will be happy in Yahveh and take refuge in him; the godly in heart will honor him.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 64:7-10</p>
