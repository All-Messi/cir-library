## Psalms Chapter **44**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>God, our ancestors have told us what you did in their time long ago.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>You drove out nations and planted this one; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>you struck them and scattered them abroad.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup><em>Our ancestors</em> didn’t conquer the land with their sword; their own arm didn’t save them. <u>Your</u> right hand, <u>your</u> arm, the light of <u>your</u> presence <em>did it</em> when you showed them favor.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 44:1-3</p>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>You’re my King, God; ordain victories for Jacob <em>in our day too</em>.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>By your power we’ll push back our adversaries and tramp down our attackers.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>We don’t trust in our bow; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>our sword won’t save us.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><u>Y</u><u>ou</u> are the one that must save us from our enemies and disgrace those who hate us.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>We take pride in you always and give you thanks forever.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 44:4-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>Yet now you’ve set us aside to dishonor; you don’t go out with our armies.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>You’ve made us retreat from our enemies; those who hate us have plundered our land.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>You’ve butchered us like sheep and scattered us among the nations.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>You sell us cheap and make no profit from doing it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>You make us a disgrace to our neighbors, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>something to scoff at,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>a byword the world over, a laughingstock among the nations.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 44:9-14</p>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>All day long we’re disgraced and humiliated</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>because people insult us; we have enemies and avengers everywhere.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>That’s all come on us though we haven’t forgotten you or been unfaithful to your covenant.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>Our heart hasn’t deserted you; we haven’t strayed from your way;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>yet you’ve crushed us in a place filled with jackals and covered us with the shadow of death.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 44:15-19</p>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup>If we’d forgotten you or raised our hands to a foreign god,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>wouldn’t you have found it out? You know all our inner secrets.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup>But for the sake of your honor, our lives are always in danger; we’re like sheep headed for slaughter.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>Wake up, Yahveh; why are you sleeping? don’t reject us forever.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup>Why ignore us and forget our suffering and oppression?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup>We’re struck down to the dirt; our bodies stick to the ground.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup><strong>26</strong></sup>Get up and help us; redeem us like your enduring love suggests.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 44:20-26</p>
