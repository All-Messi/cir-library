## Psalms Chapter **105**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><strong>Be grateful to </strong><strong>Yahveh</strong>; call on him; tell the nations what all he’s done.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>Sing praises to him; talk about the amazing acts.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Take pride in his holy name; let the people be happy that rely on him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>Rely on Yahveh and his strength; depend on him always.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Remember his amazing miracles, the judgments he’s rendered,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>descendants of Abraham his servant, children of Jacob, his chosen ones!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>He’s Yahveh our God, who renders judgment all over the world.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:1-7</p>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>He remembers his eternal covenant, the message he commanded to a thousand generations,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup><em>his covenant </em>with <u>Abraham</u>, his oath to <u>Isaac</u>.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>his statute confirmed to <u>Jacob</u> as an ongoing covenant.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>He swore, <em>“I’ll give </em><em>you Canaan to inherit</em><em>.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>When they were just a few, temporary residents,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>they wandered from nation to nation, from kingdom to kingdom.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>He didn’t let anybody oppress them; he reprimanded kings on their behalf: <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>“Don’t touch my anointed ones;</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>don’t harm my </em><em>prophets</em><em>.”</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:8-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>He called for a famine in the land and cut off their food supply.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>He sent a man ahead of them, Joseph, sold as a slave.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>They hurt his feet with restraints <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and put an iron collar around his neck</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>till <em>God’s</em> message came to him <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and revealed what kind of man he was.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup>The king released him; the people’s ruler let him go.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>He made him master of his household, steward over everything he owned,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup>even to imprison his officials if he saw fit so he could teach his elders wisdom.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:16-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>Then Israel/<em>Jacob</em> went down to Egypt <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and lived as a foreigner in the land of Ham.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup><em>God</em> multiplied his people and made them stronger than their enemies,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup>whose hearts he turned against his people to mistreat them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup><strong>26</strong></sup><em>Then </em>he sent Moses his servant and Aaron his chosen one</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup>to perform <em>his </em>amazing acts among them, miracles in the land of Ham:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup><strong>28</strong></sup>he engulfed their land in<strong> </strong><strong>darkness</strong> <em>when </em>they defied him and his message;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup><strong>29</strong></sup>he turned their <strong>water to blood</strong> and killed their fish;<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:23-29</p>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup><strong>30</strong></sup><strong>frogs</strong> overran the land even into the king’s bedrooms;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup><strong>31</strong></sup>swarms of<strong> flies</strong> came, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and <strong>gnats</strong> infested the whole region;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup><strong>32</strong></sup>he gave them <strong>hail</strong> for rain, and lightning flashed in the land;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup><strong>33</strong></sup>he struck down their grapevines and fig trees <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and stripped their trees;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><sup><strong>34</strong></sup>he summoned countless young <strong>locusts</strong> to come</span>

<span class="verse" id="v35"><sup><strong>35</strong></sup>and eat the vegetation in the country and devour the produce from the ground;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v36"><sup><strong>36</strong></sup>he struck the <em>Egyptians’</em> <strong>firstborn</strong>, the first issue of their strength.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:30-36</p>

<span class="verse" id="v37"><sup><strong>37</strong></sup>Then he brought Israel out with silver and gold; nobody among the tribes faltered.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v38"><sup><strong>38</strong></sup>The Egyptians were glad when they left because they’d come to dread them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v39"><sup><strong>39</strong></sup>He spread a cloud over them and sent fire to light up the night.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v40"><sup><strong>40</strong></sup>They asked, and he brought quail and satisfied them with manna from the sky.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v41"><sup><strong>41</strong></sup>He opened a rock and water came out and ran into the desert like a stream.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v42"><sup><strong>42</strong></sup>He remembered his sacred promise to Abraham his servant.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v43"><sup><strong>43</strong></sup>He brought his people out with joy and celebration.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v44"><sup><strong>44</strong></sup>He gave them the lands of nations, and they harvested what those nations had grown;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v45"><sup><strong>45</strong></sup><em>he did it </em>so they would keep his statutes <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and observe his laws. <strong>Hallelujah!</strong><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 105:37-45</p>
