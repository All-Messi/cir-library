## Psalms Chapter **33**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Sing for joy to Yahveh, good people; praise is fitting for the godly.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>Give him<sup>Y</sup> thanks with a lyre; sing praises to him with a ten-string harp.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Sing a new song to him; play skillfully and shout for joy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>You can believe what he<sup>Y</sup> says and rely on what he does.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>He loves goodness and justice, and fills the world with his enduring love.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 33:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>Yahveh made the sky with a word, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>all its host with the breath from his mouth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>He gathered the water in the ocean and stored up the depths in reservoirs.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>The whole world must respect him and stand in awe of him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>He spoke and it happened; he commanded and it occurred.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>Yahveh overrides the counsel of nations and frustrates the peoples’ plans.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>His counsel stands forever; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>his plans <em>endure</em> across the generations.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 33:6-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>The nation is blessed whose God is Yahveh, the people he’s chosen for his inheritance.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>Yahveh looks down from heaven at everyone;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>from where he sits, he sees the whole human race.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>He made their hearts; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>so he understands what they do.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>A king doesn’t prevail by his <em>large </em>army; a soldier doesn’t save himself by his great strength.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>A warhorse gives false hope for victory; it doesn’t deliver anyone by its power.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 33:12-17</p>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>Yahveh watches over those who respect him, who place their hope in his enduring love</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>to deliver them from death and keep them alive at famine time.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup>We wait for him, our help and shield,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>and celebrate in him because we trust in him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup>Send down your enduring love to us, Yahveh, because we’ve put our hope in you.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 33:18-22</p>
