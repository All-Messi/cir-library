## Psalms Chapter **38**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Yahveh, don’t rebuke me in your wrath or discipline me in your furious anger.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>Your arrows have pierced deep into me; your hand has come down hard on me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Because of your indignation, my body’s not sound; by my sin I’ve ruined my health.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>My iniquities have piled up higher than my head and weighed me down.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>My wounds have festered and grown foul. My foolish choices <strong><sup>6</sup></strong>have doubled me over; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>I grieve all the time;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>I’m burning up with fever; my health is broken.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>I’m numb and crushed, and groan from inner conflict.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 38:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>Yahveh, you know what I long for; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>you hear my sighs.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>My heart’s pounding, my strength’s failing; the sparkle in my eyes is gone.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>My friends and loved ones stand back from my sickness; my relatives stay well away from me.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>Those who want to kill me lay snares for me; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>the ones that want to injure me threaten<strong><sup> </sup></strong>destruction, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong>constantly plotting treachery.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>But like someone deaf, I don’t hear; like a mute I don’t speak.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>Yes, I’m like somebody that can’t hear, someone with no arguments to offer.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>I’m depending on you, Yahveh; you’ll have to answer <em>for me</em>:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>Don’t let them celebrate over me or gloat when my foot slips.</span>
