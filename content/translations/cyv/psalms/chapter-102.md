## Psalms Chapter 102

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Listen to my prayer, Yahveh! Let my cry come to you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Don’t turn away from me in my distress; when I call, answer quickly.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> My days dissipate like smoke; my bones lie scorched on a hearth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> My heart is weak and withers away like grass; I have no appetite 5in my loud groaning; I’m skin and bones.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> I’m like a pelican in the desert, like an owl in desolate places;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> I lie awake like a lonely bird on a housetop.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 102:1-7</p>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> My enemies taunt me all day; they ridicule me and use my name as a curse.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> I eat ashes for food and mix my drink with tears</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> because of your indignation, because you’ve thrown me away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> My days are like a shadow; I wither like the grass.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> But you live forever, Yahveh; you endure for all generations.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Have mercy on Zion; it’s time to be gracious to it; the appointed time has come.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Your servants delight in its very rocks and have regard even for its dust.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> So the nations will respect Yahveh; kings will respect your honor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Yahveh will build up Zion; he’ll appear in his glory.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> He’ll have regard for the destitute; he won’t disregard their prayer.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 102:8-17</p>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Write this for the next generation, so people not yet born will praise Yahveh:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> He looked down from his holy height; from heaven he gazed at earth</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> to hear the groaning prisoners, to free those doomed to die;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> so people will celebrate Yahveh in Zion and praise him in Jerusalem</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> when the nations gather and the kingdoms assemble to serve him.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 102:18-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> He weakened my strength in the course of my life and shortened it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> I say to him, My God, don’t take me away in the middle of life; you live throughout all generations.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> You founded the earth long ago; the sky is your handiwork.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Even they will perish, but you’ll remain; they’ll wear out like a robe, and you’ll change them like clothes.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> But you’ll be the same; your years won’t end.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Your people’s children will continue in security; their descendants will thrive in your presence.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 102:23-28</p>
