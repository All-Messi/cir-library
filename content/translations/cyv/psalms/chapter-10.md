## Psalms Chapter **10**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Why stand so far away, Yahveh? <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>Why hide when trouble comes?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>Sinners hunt down the poor in arrogance and get themselves caught in their own schemes.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>They brag about their sinful desires; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>they bless the greedy and curse Yahveh.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>In their arrogance, they don’t trust in him; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>they <em>just </em>keep acting like there’s no God.<em>	</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 10:1-4</p>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Yet they always <em>seem to</em> prosper; they don’t see your coming judgments from on high; they sneer at adversaries</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>and keep telling themselves, <em>“We’re all set</em><em>;</em> <em>	</em><em>	nothing bad is ever going to happen to us</em><em>.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>They curse, deceive, and oppress; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>scheming and sin are on the tip of their tongue.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>They lie in ambush in villages, kill innocent people in private, and search for the unfortunate in stealth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>They lurk in hiding places like lions in a den and sneak around to pounce on helpless people when they snare them in their nets.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>They crouch and slink down; the unfortunate fall under their power.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>They tell themselves, <em>“God </em><em>won’t remember</em><em>;</em> <em>	he </em><em>doesn’t pay attention to what’s going on</em><em>; </em> <em>	</em><em>he</em><em> won’t even </em><em>see it.”</em><em>	</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 10:5-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>Raise up your hand, Yahveh; remember the helpless.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>Why do sinners renounce you? Why tell themselves you<em> </em>won’t call them to account?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>You <u>do</u> see trouble and distress; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong>so you <u>will</u> take care of it. Poor people commit themselves to you, and you defend orphans.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>Break the arms of bad people; search out sinners till there’s none left to find.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>Yahveh, you’re King forever; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>nations perish at your hand.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>You listen to what the humble need and increase your mercy toward them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>You vindicate the orphan and the oppressed so worldly people can’t abuse them anymore.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 10:12-18</p>
