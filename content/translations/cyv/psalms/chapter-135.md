## Psalms Chapter **135**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><strong>Hallelujah!</strong> Praise Yahveh; praise him, his servant,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>you that stand in his Temple, <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>in the courts of our God!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Praise him because he’s good; sing his praises because he’s gracious,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup>because he’s chosen Jacob for himself, Israel for his own possession.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 135:1-4</p>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>I know Yahveh is great, that he’s above all gods.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>He does what he wants in the sky, on earth, in the seas and all <em>their </em>depths.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>He makes clouds come up from the horizon; he sends lightning with the rain and brings the wind out of his storehouses.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 135:5-7</p>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>He struck Egypt’s firstborn, people and animals.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>He performed signs and wonders on Pharaoh and his people.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>He struck many nations <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>and slew powerful kings:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>Sihon, king of the Amorites; Og, king of Bashan; as well as the kingdoms in Canaan.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>He gave their land as an inheritance to his people Israel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>His name lasts forever; his renown <em>endures</em> throughout all generations. <strong>	</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>Yahveh will vindicate his people and have mercy on his servants.<strong> </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 135:8-14</p>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>The hand-crafted idols the nations have are just silver and gold</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>with mouths that can’t talk, with eyes that can’t see,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>with ears that can’t hear; and they can’t breathe.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>The ones that make them are just like them, everybody that trusts in them too.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 135:15-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>House of Israel, <strong>bless </strong><strong>Yahveh</strong>; House of Aaron, <strong>bless </strong><strong>Yahveh</strong>;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup>House of Levi, <strong>bless </strong><strong>Yahveh</strong>; you that respect Yahveh, <strong>bless </strong><strong>him</strong>.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>From Zion<strong> b</strong><strong>less </strong><strong>Yahveh</strong>, who lives in Jerusalem. <strong>Hallelujah!</strong><strong>	</strong></span>
