## Psalms Chapter 94

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Yahveh God of vengeance, shine!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Rise up, Judge of the whole world; repay the proud.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> How long will you let sinners celebrate?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> How long will they pour out their arrogant words, vaunt themselves up</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> crush your people, attack your heritage</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> murder widows and foreigners and orphans,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> and say, “Yahveh’s not watching; Jacob’s God is not paying attention.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 94:1-7</p>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> You silly people, you pay attention; when will you ever understand?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Can’t he hear, who created the ear? Can’t he see, who formed the eye?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Won’t he that rebukes nations, discipline you; doesn’t the Source of knowledge know what you’re doing?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Yahveh knows that your thoughts are nothing but hot air.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> The ones you discipline are blessed, Yahveh, the ones you teach out of the Law;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> so you can grant them relief in adversity, till you dig a pit for the wicked.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Yahveh won’t abandon his people or forsake his inheritance.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> The administration of justice will be good again, and the godly will follow it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Who else will stand up for me against evil people? Who else will take a stand for me against sinners?</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 94:8-16</p>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> If Yahveh hadn’t helped me, I would soon have occupied the silent place.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> When I cried out, “I’m slipping,” his enduring love kept me from falling.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> When my anxious thoughts multiplied, he cheered me up.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Can corrupt rulers be his allies and plot schemes with their wicked laws?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Can they gang up on good people and condemn the innocent to death?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> No, because Yahveh is my fortress; my God the Rock is my refuge.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> He’ll bring their sinfulness back on them and destroy them with their own evil; Yahveh our God will wipe them out.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 94:17-23</p>
