## Psalms Chapter **45**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>My heart is overflowing with a noble theme as I recite my verses to the King; my tongue is like the pen of a gifted poet.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup>You’re more handsome than the sons of men; gracious words stream from on your lips; God has blessed you forever.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Strap your sword to your thigh, Mighty Warrior, in your splendor and majesty! <strong><sup>4</sup></strong> In your majesty, ride on to victory, defending truth, humility, and goodness; have your right hand display awesome deeds.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Your sharp arrows pierce your enemies’ hearts; nations fall under you.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 45:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>Your throne is eternal, God; your kingdom’s scepter is a godly one.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>You love goodness and hate sinfulness; so God, your God, has anointed you with more happiness than your companions.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>Your robes have the fragrance of myrrh, aloes, and cassia; stringed instruments entertain you in ivory palaces.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>Your<sup>mas</sup> maids of honor include kings’ daughters; <strong><sup>	</sup></strong>at your<sup>mas</sup> right hand stands the queen in gold from Ophir.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 45:6-9</p>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>Listen, daughter; pay attention; forget your people and your father’s family</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>The king delights in your beauty; since he’s your lord, bow to him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>The people from Tyre will shower you<sup>fem</sup> with gifts; wealthy citizens will sue your<sup>fem</sup> favor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>The princess looks beautiful in her gown interwoven with gold.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>She’ll be led to the king in embroidered robes with bridesmaids in her train.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>With happiness and celebration, they’ll enter the palace of the king.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ps 45:10-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>Your<sup>mas</sup> sons will take the place of your<sup>mas</sup> fathers; you’ll<sup>mas</sup> make them rulers over many lands.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>I’ll perpetuate your<sup>mas</sup> memory in every generation; the nations will be eternally grateful to you<sup>mas</sup>.</span>
