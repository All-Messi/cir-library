## 2 Chronicles Chapter 29


### Hezekiah Rules Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> became king when he was 25 and ruled for 29 years in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did what was right like David did. </span></span></p>


### Hezekiah Reopens the Temple


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>first month of his first year</b>, he reopened the doors of </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple and repaired them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He brought in the priests and Levites and gathered them in the courtyard on the east. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Listen, Levites. Consecrate yourselves and the Temple of Yahveh God of our ancestors. Remove ceremonial uncleanness from the Holy Place. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Our ancestors have been unfaithful and done what he forbids. They’ve turned their backs on where he lives. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They’ve shut the doors of the vestibule and put out the lamps. They haven’t burned incense or offered burnt offerings in the Holy Place. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So God’s wrath has been against Judah and Jerusalem. He’s made them something to abhor, be amazed at, hissed at, as you can see. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Our ancestors fell by the sword. Our sons, daughters, and wives are in captivity because of it. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I intend to renew the covenant with Yahveh God of Israel so his furious anger will turn away from us. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Sons, don’t neglect your responsibilities any longer. Yahveh has chosen you to stand in his presence, perform service to him and</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> his people</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, and burn incense.”        </span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 29:1-11</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Levites got to work: </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Mahath Ben-Amasai and Joel Ben-Azariah from the Kohathites; </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Kish Ben-Abdi and Azariah Ben-Jehallelel from the descendants of Merari; </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joah Ben-Zimmah and Eden Ben-Joah from the Gershonites; </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Shimri and Jeiel from the sons of Elizaphan;</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> Zechariah and Mattaniah from the descendants of Asaph; </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehiel and Shimei from the descendants of Heman; and </span></span></p>


### Shemaiah and Uzziel from the sons of Jeduthun.                                   2 Ch 29:12-14


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They gathered their relatives, consecrated themselves, and went in to cleanse </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple like the king said by Yahveh’s words. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The priests went into the inner part of the Temple to cleanse it. Everything unclean they found they brought out to the court, and the Levites carried it down to the Kidron valley. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They began the consecration on the <b>first day, first</b> <b>month</b>. On the eighth day, they went into the vestibule. They consecrated </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple in eight days and finished on the <b>sixteenth</b>. </span></span></p>


### The Temple Rededicated


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then they went in to King Hezekiah and said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:34.65pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“We’ve cleansed the whole Temple, the altar for burnt offering with its utensils, and the table for the showbread with its utensils. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We’ve prepared and consecrated the utensils King Ahaz discarded during his unfaithfulness. They’re in front of the altar.”                                                                                                                </span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 29:15-19</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then King Hezekiah got up early and gathered the city officials and went up to </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They brought seven bulls, rams, lambs, and male goats for a sin offering for the kingdom, the sanctuary, and Judah. He told the priests, descendants of Aaron, to offer them on the altar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They slaughtered the bulls, rams, and lambs and sprinkled the blood on the altar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They brought the male goats for the sin offering in the presence of the king and the assembly, and laid their hands on them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The priests slaughtered them and purged the altar with their blood to atone for Israel, because the king ordered the burnt offering and the sin offering for all Israel.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he stationed the Levites in </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple with cymbals, harps, and lyres like David, Gad the seer, and Nathan the prophet had commanded. The command came from Yahveh through his prophets. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Levites stood with David’s musical instruments and the priests with </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>their</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> trumpets. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah gave the order to offer the burnt offering on the altar. When the burnt offering began, the song to </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh also began with the trumpets accompanied by David’s instruments. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>While the whole assembly worshiped, the singers sang and the trumpets blew till the burnt offering finished. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then the king and everybody present with him bowed in worship. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>King Hezekiah and the officials directed the Levites to sing praises to </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh with the words of David and Asaph the seer. They sang praises with joy and bowed in worship.          </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 29:20-30</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah said, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Now that you’ve consecrated yourselves, come near and bring sacrifices and thank offerings to Yahveh’s Temple.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> The assembly brought sacrifices and thank offerings, and everybody that wanted to brought burnt offerings. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The assembly brought 70 bulls, 100 rams, and 200 lambs as burnt offerings. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The consecrated things were 600 bulls and 3,000 sheep. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There weren’t enough priests to skin all the burnt offerings. So their relatives the Levites helped them till they finished and </span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>more</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> priests had consecrated themselves. The Levites were more conscientious about consecrating themselves than the priests were. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Besides the large number of burnt offerings, there was the fat of the peace offerings and the liquid offerings for the burnt offerings. In that way, they restored the service in </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Hezekiah and the people celebrated what God had prepared for them, because the thing had come about suddenly.  </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 29:31-36</span></b></span></p>
