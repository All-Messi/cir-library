## 2 Chronicles Chapter 24


### Joash Repairs the Temple


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joash</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was 7 when he became king and ruled for 40 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Zibiah from Beersheba. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did what was good all the time Jehoiada was alive. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoiada took two wives for him, and he became the father of sons and daughters. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After that, Joash decided to restore Yahveh’s Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He gathered the priests and Levites, and said, <i>“Go out to the towns in Judah and collect money to repair your God’s Temple annually; do it right away.” </i>But the Levites didn’t act right away. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He summoned Jehoiada the chief priest, <i>“Why haven’t you required the Levites to bring in from Judah and Jerusalem the tax Moses laid on Israel for the Tent of Testimony?” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The sons of Athaliah had broken into God’s Temple and used the holy things for the images of Baal. </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>24:1-7</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king had them make a chest to set outside by the gate into Yahveh’s Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They made a proclamation in Judah and Jerusalem to bring to Yahveh the tax Moses had levied in the desert. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The officers and common people celebrated and brought in their levies and dropped them in the chest till they’d finished. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Whenever the Levites saw there was a lot in the chest, they’d bring it to the king’s officer, and his secretary and the chief priest’s officer would empty it and take it back to its place. They did that daily and collected a lot of money. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king and Jehoiada gave it to the ones that serviced the<sup>Y</sup> Temple. They hired masons, carpenters, and artisans in iron and bronze to repair the Temple, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and the work progressed. They restored it to its specifications and reinforced it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they finished, they brought the rest of the money to the king and Jehoiada. They made it into utensils for the<sup>Y</sup> Temple, for its service, the burnt offering, gold and silver pans and utensils. They offered burnt offerings continually all the time Jehoiada was alive.</span><b><span style='font-size: 10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                     2 Ch 24:8-14</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>             </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoiada died at the good old age of 130. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They buried him in The City of David among the kings because he’d done well in Israel for God and his Temple. </span></span></p>


### Jehoiadah’s Reforms Reversed


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But after <i>Jehoiada’s</i> death, the officials of Judah came and bowed to the king, and he listened to them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They abandoned the Temple of Yahveh God and worshiped Asherah poles and idols; so wrath came on Judah and Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He sent prophets to bring them back to him, but they wouldn’t listen. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then God’s Spirit came on Zechariah Ben-Jehoiada the priest. He stood above the people and said<i>, “God says, ‘Why do you sin against Yahveh’s commands and not prosper? Because you’ve left him, he’s left you.’” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They plotted against him, and at the king’s command stoned him to death in the</span><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Y</span></sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> Temple court. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So Joash the king didn’t remember the kindness <i>Zechariah’s</i> father Jehoiada had shown him; he murdered his son. As he died, he said, <i>“May Yahveh see and repay you!”</i></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                     2 Ch 24:15-22</span></b></span></p>


### The End of Joash’s Reig


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At the turn of the year, the Aramean army came up against him. They came to Judah and Jerusalem, killed the people’s officials, and sent their spoil to the king in Damascus. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Though the Arameans came with a small army, Yahveh delivered a large army to them because they’d left him</span><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Y</span></sup><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. They carried out judgment on Joash. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They left him seriously wounded. When they’d gone, his own officials plotted against him for murdering Jehoiada’s son. They assassinated him in his bed and buried him in The City of David, but not in the tombs of the kings. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The ones that plotted against him were Zabad Ben-Shimeath the Ammonitess and Jehozabad Ben-Shimrith the Moabitess. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Commentary on the Book of the Kings</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> gives accounts about his sons, the many oracles against him, and the restoration of God’s Temple. His son Amaziah became king.     </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 24:23-27 </span></b></span></p>
