## 2 Chronicles Chapter 25


### Amaziah Rules Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amaziah</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was 25 and ruled for 29 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Jehoaddan of Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did what was good, but not with all <i>his</i> heart. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As soon as his kingdom was firmly in hand, he executed the officials that had assassinated his father. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He didn’t put their children to death, like Yahveh commanded in the Law of Moses: <i>“Don’t execute parents for offspring or offspring for parents. Execute each person for that person’s sin.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amaziah gathered Judah and appointed them according to their families under commanders of thousands and hundreds throughout Judah and Benjamin. He took a census of men 20 and older and found 300,000 elite men, fit for war and handling spear and shield. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He hired 100,000 trained soldiers out of Israel for <a name="_Hlk145088343">7550 lbs <sup>100 talents</sup> </a>of silver. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But a man of God came to him and said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t let Israel’s army go with you; Yahveh is not with Israel or Ephraim. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <a name="_Hlk148359693"><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Even if you go strong into the battle, God will still make you stumble </span></i></a><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>in front of the enemy. He has power to help or hinder.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amaziah said, <i>“But what will we do for the </i>7550 lbs <sup>100 talents</sup> <i>of silver I’ve given Israel’s troops?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>The man of God answered, <i>“Yahveh has a lot more to give you than that.”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amaziah dismissed the troops from Ephraim, and they went home disgruntled.<b>       `</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch</span></b><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>25:1-10</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amaziah gathered his courage, led his people to the Valley of Salt, and struck down 10,000 people from Seir. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judah also captured 10,000 alive and brought them to the top of the cliff. They threw them down and threw them down to their death. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But
<i>Israel’s</i> troops that Amaziah sent back, raided the towns in Judah between Beth-horon and Samaria, struck down 3,000 of them, and plundered a lot of spoil.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After Amaziah came from slaying the Edomites, he brought the gods from Seir. He set them up as gods, bowed to them, and burned incense to them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s anger burned against him. He sent him a prophet, <i>“Why seek gods that haven’t delivered their own people from you?”</i>                                                                                                                                                                 </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                                                                            </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 25:11-15</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As he was talking to him, the king said, <i>“Did we appoint you a royal counselor? Shut up! Why get struck down?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>The prophet stopped and said, <i>“God has planned to destroy you because you’ve done that and ignored my advice.”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amaziah took counsel and sent to Joash (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ben-Jehoahaz Ben-Jehu, king of Israel</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>),
<i>“Come. Let’s face each other.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joash sent back to him, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“A thistle in Lebanon sent to a cedar in Lebanon, ‘Give your daughter to my son in marriage.’ But a wild animal in Lebanon passed by and tramped down the thistle.
</span></i><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You said, ‘I’ve defeated Edom.’ You’ve become proud. Stay home. Why stir up trouble to make yourself and Judah fall?”                                                              </span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 25:16-19</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Amaziah wouldn’t listen because it fit with Yahveh’s plan to God deliver them to <i>Joash</i> because they’d looked to Edom’s gods. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joash, king of Israel, went up and faced Amaziah at Beth-shemesh in Judah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He defeated Judah, and they fled to their tents. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joash captured Amaziah at Beth-shemesh and took him to Jerusalem. He tore down the wall from the Gate of Ephraim to the Corner Gate, 600ft. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He took all the gold and silver, the items in God’s Temple with Obed-edom, the treasures of the king’s palace, and hostages too, and went back to Samaria.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amaziah lived 15 years after the death of Joash Ben-Jehoahaz, king of Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Amaziah, first to last, are recorded in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Book of the Kings of Judah and Israel</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From the time Amaziah turned away from following </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh, they plotted against him in Jerusalem. He fled to Lachish, but they sent assassins after him and killed him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They brought him back on horses and buried him with his ancestors in The City of David<sup>ms</sup>.
</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 25:20-28</span></b></span></p>
