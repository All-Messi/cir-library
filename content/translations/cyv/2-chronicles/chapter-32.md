## 2 Chronicles Chapter 32


### Assyria Invades Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After those faithful acts, Sennacherib, king of Assyria, invaded Judah, laid siege to fortified towns, and intended to break into them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Hezekiah saw he’d come and meant to attack Jerusalem, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>he, his officers, and soldiers decided to cut off the water supply from the springs outside the city. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Many people came together and stopped up the springs feeding the stream that flowed through the region, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Why should Assyria’s kings come and find abundant water?”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He took courage and rebuilt the wall that had been torn down, erected towers on it, built an outside wall, strengthened the Millo in The City of David, and made a great number of weapons and shields. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He appointed military officers over the people, and gathered them in the square at the city gate to encourage them,                                                   </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Be strong and courageous; don’t be afraid or intimidated by Assyria’s king or his host. The One that’s with us is greater than the one that’s with him. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>All he’s got is a physical army, but we have Yahveh our God to help us and fight our battles.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The people trusted what he said.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                                        2 Ch 32:1-8</span></b></p>


### Sennacherib Threatens Jerusalem


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After that, Sennacherib sent officers to Jerusalem while he was laying siege to Lachish with his forces. </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He sent them </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>against Hezekiah and all Judah that were in Jerusalem, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Sennacherib, king of Assyria, says,</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>‘What are you trusting in that you stay in Jerusalem under siege? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Isn’t Hezekiah misleading you to give yourselves over to die by hunger and thirst, and saying Yahveh your God will deliver you from the king of Assyria? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hasn’t the same Hezekiah taken away his high places and altars, and told Judah and Jerusalem to worship at one altar and burn incense on <u>it</u>? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t you know what my predecessors and I have done to all the countries? Were their gods able to deliver their land from me? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Who among them that my predecessors destroyed, could deliver his people from me? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t let Hezekiah mislead you. No god could deliver his people from me and the kings before me. How much less <u>your</u> God?’”           </span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 32:9-15</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His officers spoke against </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh God and Hezekiah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He wrote letters to insult </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh God of Israel:</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:31.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“None of the nations’ gods have delivered their people from me. Hezekiah’s god won’t deliver his people </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>either</span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.” </span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They shouted it out in the language of Judah to the people on the wall to terrify them so they could take the city. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They talked about the God of Jerusalem like the other people’s hand-made gods.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But King Hezekiah and the prophet Isaiah Ben-Amoz, prayed about that and appealed to Heaven. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh sent an angel that destroyed every powerful soldier, commander, and officer in the camp. <i>Sennacherib</i> went back to his own land humiliated. When he entered the temple of his god, <i>two</i> of his own sons killed him with a sword. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh saved Hezekiah, the people in Jerusalem, and the rest and guided them on every side. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Many people started bringing gifts to </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh at Jerusalem and special presents to Hezekiah so that all the nations regarded him highly after that.                                                                                                                                         </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 32:16-23</span></b></span></p>


### Hezekiah’s Sickness and Recovery


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At that time, Hezekiah became deathly ill. He prayed to </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh, and he answered him and gave him a sign. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25-26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah and the people in Jerusalem humbled themselves, so </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s wrath didn’t come on them during his lifetime. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah had immense wealth and honor. He made treasuries for silver, gold, precious stones, spices, shields, and all kinds of valuable articles; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>as well as storehouses for grain, wine, and olive oil; pens for all kinds of livestock, and shepherds for the flocks. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He built towns for himself and acquired flocks and herds in abundance. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezekiah was the one that stopped the upper outlet of Gihon and diverted the water to the west side of The City of David. He succeeded at everything. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Even in regard to envoys from Babylon who asked about the wonder that had happened, God left him alone to test him, to know what was in his heart.</span></span></p>


### Summary of Hezekiah’s Reign


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of his acts and his deeds of devotion are recorded in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Vision of the Prophet</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Isaiah Ben-Amoz</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Book of the Kings of Judah and Israel</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He died and they buried him in the upper section of the tombs of David’s descendants. The people in Judah and Jerusalem honored him when he died. His son Manasseh became king.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                          2 Ch 32:24-33   </span></b></span></p>
