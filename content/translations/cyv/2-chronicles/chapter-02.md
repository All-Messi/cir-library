## 2 Chronicles Chapter 2


### Preparations for Building the Temple


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Solomon decided to build a temple for Yahveh’s name and a royal palace for himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He assigned 70,000 to transport materials, 80,000 to quarry <i>stone </i>in the mountains, and 3,600 to supervise them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He sent word to Hiram, king of Tyre, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“As you sent my father David cedars for his palace, do for me. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’m getting ready to build a temple for Yahveh my God and dedicate it to him for burning fragrant incense and offering burnt offerings morning and evening on Sabbaths, new moons, and Yahveh’s appointed festivals—items of worship required permanently in Israel. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The temple I’m going to build will be great, because our God is greater than all </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>other
<i>gods. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But who can build him a temple? The sky, the highest heavens, can’t contain him; who am I to build him a temple, except for burning incense to him? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Send me a man skilled at working in gold, silver, brass, and iron; in purple, crimson, and violet fabrics; a man that knows how to engrave and work with the skilled men I have in Judah and Jerusalem that my father provided. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Send me cedar, cypress, and algum wood from Lebanon; your woodsmen know how to cut timber in Lebanon. My men will work with yours </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>to prepare an abundance of it for the great, wonderful temple I’m going to build. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ll give your woodsmen that cut the logs 220,000 bushels </span></i><i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>20,000 kors</span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> of crushed wheat, 220,000 bushels </span></i><i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>20,000 kors</span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> of barley, 180,000 gallons </span></i><i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>20,000 baths </span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>of wine, and 180,000 gallons </span></i><i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>20,000 baths</span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> of oil.”
</span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 2:1-10</span></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>       </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hiram wrote back, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Since Yahveh loves his people, he’s made you their king. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Bless Yahveh God of Israel that made earth and sky and gave King David a wise son endowed with discretion and understanding, who will build a temple for Yahveh and a royal palace for himself. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’m sending Huram-abi, an intelligent master craftsman, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the son of a woman from Dan and a father from Tyre. He knows how to work in gold, silver, bronze, iron, wood, and stone, as well as in purple, violet, linen, and crimson fabrics. He knows how to do all kinds of engravings and carry out any design you assign him. He can work with your skilled men and those of my lord David. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Send us the wheat and barley, oil and wine you’ve offered. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We’ll cut whatever timber you need and bring it to you on rafts by sea to Joppa so you can transport it up to Jerusalem.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Solomon counted the foreigners in Israel according to David’s census and found there were 153,600 of them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He appointed 80,000 to quarry <i>stones </i>in the mountains, 70,000 to transport materials, and 3,600 to supervise the workers.            </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 2:11-18</span></b></span></p>
