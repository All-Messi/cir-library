## 2 Chronicles Chapter 12


### Shishak [Shoshenq I] Invades Judah


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Rehoboam’s kingdom got secure and strong, he and Israel turned away from the Law of Yahveh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In
<b>his</b> <b>fifth year</b>, because they weren’t faithful to Yahveh, Shishak [Shoshenq I], king of Egypt, came up against Jerusalem </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>with 1,200 chariots and 60,000 horsemen. The Lybians, Sukkiim, and Nubians came with him without number. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He captured the fortified towns in Judah and advanced to Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Shemaiah the prophet came to Rehoboam and Judah’s officials that had fled to Jerusalem to escape Shishak, <i>“Yahveh says you’ve left him, so he’s left you to Shishak.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The officials of Israel and the king humbled themselves, <i>“Yahveh is good.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Yahveh saw that, he told Shemaiah, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:40.5pt;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“They’ve humbled themselves, so I won’t destroy them. I’ll grant them some measure of deliverance and not pour out my anger on Jerusalem by Shishak. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But they’ll become his slaves so they’ll learn </span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the difference between<i> serving me and serving other countries.”                                                                                                </i></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 12:1-8</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Shishak [Shoshenq I] came up against Jerusalem and took the treasures out of Yahveh’s Temple and the king’s palace. He took everything, including the gold shields Solomon had made. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>King Rehoboam made bronze shields to replace them and committed them to the commanders of the guard that guarded the king’s palace. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As often as the king went into Yahveh’s Temple, the guards would carry them and then bring them back to the guard room. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he humbled himself, Yahveh turned his anger away, so he didn’t destroy him completely. Conditions in Judah were tolerable.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>King Rehoboam strengthened himself in Jerusalem. He was 41 when he began to rule, and he ruled for 17 years in the city where Yahveh had chosen to identify with.
<i>Rehoboam’s</i> mother was Naamah the Ammonitess. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did evil because he didn’t set his heart to look to Yahveh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The acts of Rehoboam, first to last, are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of Shemaiah the Prophet </i>and<i> The Chronicles of Iddo the Seer</i> by genealogical enrollment. There was war between Rehoboam and Jeroboam constantly. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Rehoboam died and they buried him in The City of David, and his son Abijah became king.                                                                                                         </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 12:9-16</span></b></span></p>
