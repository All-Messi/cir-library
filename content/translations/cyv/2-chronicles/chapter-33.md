## 2 Chronicles Chapter 33


### Manasseh Rules Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Manasseh</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was 12 and ruled for 55 years in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did evil like the nations </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh drove out. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He rebuilt the high places Hezekiah had torn down, erected altars for the images of Baal, set up Asherah poles, and worshiped and served the stars in the sky. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He built altars in </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple where Yahveh had said, <i>“I will identify with Jerusalem permanently.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He built altars for the host of heaven in the two courts of </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He burned his sons as an offering in the Valley of (Ben)Hinnom. <a name="_Hlk148710081">He angered Yahveh with lots of sins he did: witchcraft, fortunetelling, sorcery, consulting mediums and spiritists. </a></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he put in God’s Temple the carved image of the idol he’d made about which God had told David and Solomon his son, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ve chosen to identify myself permanently with this Temple and with Jerusalem out of all the tribes in Israel. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I won’t again remove Israel from the land I’ve appointed for their ancestors, if only they’ll observe everything I’ve commanded them in the Law, statutes, and ordinances I gave through Moses.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Manasseh misled Judah and Jerusalem to sin more than the nations </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh originally destroyed in front of the Israelites.        </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 33:1-9</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh spoke to Manasseh and his people, but they didn’t pay any attention. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So he brought the commanders of Assyria’s army against them. They captured Manasseh with hooks, bound him with bronze chains, and took him to Babylon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he was in distress, he appealed to </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh and humbled himself greatly in the presence of his ancestors’ God. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he prayed to him, God was moved by his appeal, and brought him back to Jerusalem to his kingdom. Then Manasseh knew </span><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh was God.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After that, he built an outer wall for The City of David on the west side of the Gihon in the valley, as far as the entrance to the Fish Gate. It encircled Ophel and it was very tall. Then he put army commanders in the fortified towns of Judah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He removed the foreign gods and the idol from </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s Temple as well as the altars he’d built <i>earlier </i>on the mountain of Yahveh and in Jerusalem, and dumped them outside the city. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He set up </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s altar and sacrificed peace offerings and thank offerings on it. He ordered Judah to serve Yahveh God of Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The people still sacrificed in the high places, but only to </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh.
                                                         </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Ch 33:10-17</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Manasseh, including his prayer to God and the words of the seers that spoke to him in </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh’s name, are in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Israel</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Also recorded in </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Chronicles of the Seers</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> are his sin and unfaithfulness, the sites where he built high places, set up Asherah poles and carved images before he humbled himself and appealed to God. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Manasseh died, and they buried him in his own house. His son Amon became king. </span></span></p>


### Amon Rules Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Amon</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> was 22 and ruled for 2 years in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did evil like Manasseh had done. He sacrificed to the carved images his father had made and served them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He didn’t humble himself like his father did, but multiplied his guilt. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Finally, his own officials murdered him in his own house, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>but the people killed them and made his son Josiah king.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                2 Ch 33:18-25</span></b></span></p>
