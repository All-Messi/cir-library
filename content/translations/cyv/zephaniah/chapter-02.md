## Zephaniah Chapter **2**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Get together, go ahead, assemble, shameless nation,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>before the decree takes effect— before that day <em>of repentance </em>passes by like chaff— before Yahveh’s burning anger comes down on you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Look to him, you humble in the land who’ve followed his commands. Aspire to goodness and humility; maybe Yahveh will protect you when his anger comes.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Zeph 2:1-3</p>

### Judgment Against Philistia


<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup><u>Gaza</u> will be abandoned; <u>Ashkelon</u>, desolate. <u>Ashdod</u> will be driven out at midday; <u>Ekron</u>, uprooted.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><strong><sup>5</sup></strong>Misfortune awaits people along the coast, nation of Cherethites! Yahveh’s message speaks against you, <u>Canaan</u>, land of the Philistines; I’ll destroy you till there’s no one left.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>The seacoast will be open range with camps for shepherds and folds for flocks.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><strong><sup>7</sup></strong>It will serve as pasture for the remnant from Judah. In the evening they’ll lie down in the houses in Ashkelon, because Yahveh will take care of them and restore their fortune.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Zeph 2:4-7</p>

The remnant of my people will plunder them; 	the remainder of my nation will take their land.”	Zeph 2:8-9
