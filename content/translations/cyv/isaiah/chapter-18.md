## Isaiah Chapter 18

### An Oracle About Nubia

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Ah, land of fluttering wings, land beyond the headwaters of the Nile in Nubia,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> that sends envoys by sea in papyrus boats— go swiftly, ambassadors, to <strong>a nation tall and </strong><strong>smooth</strong><strong>,</strong> 	<strong>a people</strong><strong> feared far and wide,</strong> 	<strong>	a powerful, oppressive nation</strong> 	<strong>	</strong><strong>	crisscrossed by rivers.</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> All of you that live in the world, look when I raise the signal on the mountains, listen when I blow the ram’s horn,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> because Yahveh has told me: <em>“I’ll </em><em>watch quietly</em><em> from my </em><em>Temple,</em><em> </em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	</em>as quietly as<em> </em>a <em>cloud forming</em><em> in the </em><em>summer sunshine</em><em>,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>	like</em><em> dew </em><em>condensing</em><em> at</em><em> harvest</em><em> time</em><em>.”</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Is 18:1-4</p>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Before harvest, as soon as the bud blossoms and the flowers start turning into grapes, he’ll snip off the new shoots with pruning shears and lop off the spreading branches.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Your huge army will be left dead for the mountain buzzards and wild animals on the ground. The buzzards will spend the summer picking at the corpses; the wild animals will spend the winter gnawing on them till harvest time.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> At that time, <strong>a </strong><strong>nation</strong><strong> </strong><strong>tall</strong><strong> and </strong><strong>smooth</strong> will pay homage to Yahveh of Hosts— 	<strong>a people</strong><strong> feared far and wide,</strong> 	<strong>	</strong><strong>	</strong><strong>	</strong><strong>a powerful, oppressive nation</strong> 	<strong>	</strong><strong>	</strong><strong>	</strong><strong>	</strong><strong>crisscrossed by rivers</strong><strong>.</strong> <em>They’ll bring their gifts</em> to Mount Zion, the place that wears the name Yahveh of Hosts.<strong></span>
