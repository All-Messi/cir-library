## Isaiah Chapter 5

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> For the one I love, let me sing a song about his vineyard: The one I love had a vineyard on a fertile hill.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> He spaded up the ground, picked up the rocks, and planted the best grapevines in it; he built a tower there and hewed out a stone wine vat; he expected it to produce good grapes, but it produced wild ones. 3"People in Judah and Jerusalem, judge between me and my vineyard.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> What could I have done for it that I didn't do? Why did it produce bitter grapes instead of sweet ones?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Let me tell you what I'm going to do with it: I'll cut down the hedge around it and burn it; I'll tear down the wall and let animals trample it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> I won't take care of it, prune it, or hoe it; briars and thorns will take it over; I'll tell the clouds not to rain on it anymore."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> So my people will go into exile for ignoring me; their honored ones will starve; their common people will be parched with thirst.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Sheol will increase its appetite and eat its fill; Jerusalem's nobles and celebrating throngs will go down to it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Commoners will be humbled, important people abased, proud ones brought low.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> But Yahveh of Hosts will be exalted by his judgments; the holy God will display his holiness by his goodness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Then lambs will graze in their pasture; foreigners will eat among the ruins of the wealthy.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Misfortune awaits those who pull iniquity behind them with ropes made out of lies, who harness falsehood and hook up their carts to sin,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> who say, "Let the Holy One of Israel hurry so we can see what he's doing; let his purpose come close so we can find out what it is!"</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Misfortune awaits those who call bad good and good bad, who substitute darkness for light and light for darkness, who replace bitter with sweet and sweet with bitter.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Misfortune's in store for people that think they're smart, that consider themselves clever.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> who acquit the guilty for a bribe and deprive the innocent of justice.</span>
