## Isaiah Chapter **25**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Yahveh, you’re my God. I’ll honor and thank you for doing the wonderful things you planned long ago.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>You’ve reduced fortified towns to ruble; the foreigners’ palaces will never be rebuilt.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><strong><sup>3</sup></strong>So strong people will honor you; towns in ruthless nations will come to revere you,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>because you’ve defended the helpless and needy in their despair; you’re a shelter from the storm and a shade from heat, because the breath of ruthless people is like a storm beating on a wall</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><strong><sup>5</sup></strong>or stifling heat in drought. You weaken the threats of foreigner powers like a cloud’s shadow lessens heat; the raucous war songs of the ruthless fall silent. 	<strong>Is</strong><strong> 25:1-5</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>On this mountain Yahveh of Hosts will hold a lavish banquet for everybody in the world, a banquet of aged wine and choice meat. <strong><sup>7</sup></strong>Here he’ll remove the shadow that lies over every country, the veil that covers the nations. <strong><sup>8</sup></strong>He’ll swallow up death for all time, wipe away everyone’s tears, and remove the abuse of his people everywhere. Yahveh has said so.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>At that time, people will say, <em>“This is the God we’ve waited for </em><em>to </em><em>save us.</em><em> He’s</em><em> </em><em>Yahveh</em><em>; </em><em>let’</em><em>s </em><em>celebrate</em><em> and be </em><em>glad he’s done it</em><em>.”</em> <strong><sup>10</sup></strong>His hand will rest on this mountain, and Moab will be trodden down like straw in the urine of a manure pit. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>He’ll subdue Moab like a swimmer pushes down water. He’ll end its pride and slight-of-hand. <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>He’ll bring down its impregnable fortifications and throw them on the ground.	<strong>Is</strong><strong> 25:6-12</strong> <strong>A Song of Praise to the L</strong><strong>ORD</strong></span>
