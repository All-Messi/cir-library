## Isaiah Chapter 25

### -23 	Praise for Judgment and Salvation

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Yahveh, you’re my God. I’ll honor and thank you for doing the wonderful things you planned long ago.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> You’ve reduced fortified towns to ruble; the foreigners’ palaces will never be rebuilt.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> So strong people will honor you; towns in ruthless nations will come to revere you,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> because you’ve defended the helpless and needy in their despair; you’re a shelter from the storm and a shade from heat, because the breath of ruthless people is like a storm beating on a wall</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> or stifling heat in drought. You weaken the threats of foreigner powers like a cloud’s shadow lessens heat; the raucous war songs of the ruthless fall silent.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Is 25:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> On this mountain Yahveh of Hosts will hold a lavish banquet for everybody in the world, a banquet of aged wine and choice meat. <strong><sup>7</sup></strong>Here he’ll remove the shadow that lies over every country, the veil that covers the nations. <strong><sup>8</sup></strong>He’ll swallow up death for all time, wipe away everyone’s tears, and remove the abuse of his people everywhere. Yahveh has said so.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> At that time, people will say, <em>“This is the God we’ve waited for </em><em>to </em><em>save us.</em><em> He’s</em><em> </em><em>Yahveh</em><em>; </em><em>let’</em><em>s </em><em>celebrate</em><em> and be </em><em>glad he’s done it</em><em>.”</em> <strong><sup>10</sup></strong>His hand will rest on this mountain, and Moab will be trodden down like straw in the urine of a manure pit. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>He’ll subdue Moab like a swimmer pushes down water. He’ll end its pride and slight-of-hand. <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>He’ll bring down its impregnable fortifications and throw them on the ground.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Is 25:6-12</p>
