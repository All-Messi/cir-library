## Jeremiah Chapter 46

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> A Message from Yahveh to Jeremiah About the Nations</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> A Message to Egypt about Pharaoh Necho's Army That Was by the Euphrates River at Carchemish, Which Nebuchadnezzar Defeated in the Fourth Year of Jehoiakim Ben-Josiah, King of Judah A Message About Egypt 3"Line up the shields and bucklers, and advance for battle!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Harness the horses, mount the steeds, take your positions, put your helmets on! Polish the spears; put on the coats of mail!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> But what do I see? Egypt's army is panicking; they're retreating. Their soldiers are being defeated; they're fleeing without looking back. They're terrorized in every direction," Yahveh says.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The fast can't flee; the strong can't escape. They've stumbled and fallen in the north by the Euphrates.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Who is this that's flooding like the Nile, like rivers out of their banks?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Egypt floods like the Nile, like rivers whose water surges. It has said, "I'll flood the land; I'll destroy city and residents."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Advance, horses; drive wildly, chariots, so the infantry can march forward: Nubia and Put, that wield the shield, Lybians, that bend the bow.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> That day belongs to the Lord Yahveh of hosts, a time of revenge on his enemies. The sword will devour till it's satisfied and drink its fill of their blood. There'll be a slaughter for the Lord Yahveh of hosts in the north by the Euphrates.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Go up to Gilead and buy salve, virgin daughter in Egypt! In vain do you use lots of medicines; they won't heal you up.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> The nations have heard about your humiliation; the whole world has heard your distress cry. One soldier stumbles over another, and they both fall together.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> The Message Yahveh Spoke to Jeremiah About Nebuchadnezzar Coming to Strike Egypt 14"Declare it in Egypt, announce it in Migdol, tell it in Memphis and Tahpanhes; 'Be prepared and stand ready because the sword has taken everybody around you.'</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Why are your soldiers prostrate? Because Yahveh has beat them down.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> He made many stumble; they have fallen over one another. Then they said, 'Get up! Let's go back to our own people, to our native land, away from the oppressor's sword.'</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> They cried out there, 'Pharaoh, king of Egypt, is just a lot of noise; he's let the stated time pass by!' 18"As I live," the King says, whose name is Yahveh of Hosts, "somebody is coming like Tabor among the mountains or like Carmel by the sea.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Get your baggage ready for exile, daughter that lives in Egypt, because Memphis will be desolate, burned down, and uninhabited.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Egypt is a pretty heifer, but a horsefly is coming from the north!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Egypt's mercenaries are like grain-fed calves; even they have retreated and fled together. They didn't stand their ground; their catastrophe has descended on them; it's their time to be punished. 22"Egypt is as silent as a snake slithering away, because its enemies are moving in on it like an army, like woodsmen with axes.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> They'll cut down its forest; it certainly won't exist anymore, even though it's too numerous to count.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> Egypt has been humbled, delivered to the powerful ones up north."</span>

Afterwards people will live in Egypt like before. 27"But you, Jacob my servant, don't be afraid or intimidated! I'll rescue you from a long way off and your descendants from where they're captive. Israel will come back and be secure, with nobody to make him shudder.</span>
