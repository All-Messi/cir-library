## Jeremiah Chapter 42

### Jeremiah’s Warning to Stay in Judah

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Then Johanan, his commanders, Jezaniah Ben-Hoshaiah, and the people least to greatest came <strong><sup>2</sup></strong>to Jeremiah, <em>“Please honor our petition. Pray for us to Yahveh your God for this remnant. There are just a few of us left, as you can see.</em><strong> </strong><strong><sup>3</sup></strong>Pray <em>that Yahveh will tell us what we should do.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Jeremiah said, <em>“I’ll pray to him and not keep back a word of his answer.”</em><strong><em> </em></strong><strong> </strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> They said, <em>“May Yahveh be a true and faithful witness against us if we don’t obey his whole message. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Whether</em><em> pleasant or not, we’ll listen so things will go well with us.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Jer 42:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Ten days later, a message came to Jeremiah. <strong><sup>8</sup></strong>He called Johanan, his commanders, and the people,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>“Yahveh</em><em> says, </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><em>‘If</em><em> you stay in this land, I’ll plant you and build you </em><em>up, and</em><em> relent from the catastrophe I’ve inflicted on you. </em><strong><sup>11</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> be afraid; I’ll deliver you from the king of Babylon </em><strong><sup>12</sup></strong><em>and</em><em> show you </em><em>mercy</em><em> so he’ll have mercy and return you to your own land. </em><strong><sup>13</sup></strong><em>But</em><em> if you say, </em><strong><sup>14</sup></strong><em>“We’ll</em><em> go stay in Egypt, where we won’t be near war, hear the bugle, or go hungry,” [</em><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>]</em><em><sup> </sup></em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>then</em><em> the sword you fear will catch up with you, the famine you’re anxious about will follow soon after you arrive, and you’ll die there. </em><strong><sup>17</sup></strong><em>Everybody</em><em> that goes to Egypt will die by sword, famine, and disease. Nobody will survive the catastrophe I’ll bring.’”</em><strong>     </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Jer 42:7-17</p>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>“‘As</em><em> I’ve poured out my anger on the people in Jerusalem, I’ll pour it out on you in Egypt. You’ll become a curse, something abhorred, a disgrace. You won’t be coming back here.’ </em><strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>Yahveh</em><em> is telling you today not to go to Egypt! </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>You’re</em><em> only deceiving yourselves. You said, ‘Pray to Yahveh our God; we’ll do whatever he says.’ </em><strong><sup>21</sup></strong><em>But</em><em> you’re not doing it. </em><strong><sup>22</sup></strong><em>So</em><em> understand that you’ll die by sword, famine, and disease where you want to go live.”</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Jer 42:18-22</p>
