## Jeremiah Chapter 9

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Oh that my head was a body of water and my eyes a fountain of tears, so I could weep day and night for my people slain!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> I wish I had an inn in the desert, so I could escape from my people; because they're all adulterers, a pack of liars. 3"They bend their tongues like bows; lies, not truth, dominate the land. The people move from one sin to another; they don't know me. 4"Be leery of your neighbor; don't trust your own brother, because every brother takes advantage of brother and every neighbor slanders.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> They all deceive their friends; nobody tells the truth. They've taught their tongues how to lie; they wear themselves out sinning.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Am I not going to punish them for such things and avenge myself on a nation like that? 10"I'll weep and wail for the mountains and take up a dirge for the pastures, because they're laid waste, so nobody travels that way.
