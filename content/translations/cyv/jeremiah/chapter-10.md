<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>There’s none like <u>you</u>, Yahveh; you’re great and powerful.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><strong><sup>7</sup></strong>Who wouldn’t respect you, King of nations? <strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>	</sup></strong>It’s your due! There’s no one like you among the wise in any kingdom.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>But <u>their</u> instruction is completely foolish; they’re nothing but wood!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>People</em> bring beaten silver from Tarshish and gold from Uphaz; craftsmen and goldsmiths make the idols; they dress them in violet and purple, artisans’ work.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><strong><sup>0</sup></strong>But Yahveh is a real God, a living God and everlasting King. The ground shakes at his anger; the nations can’t endure his indignation.<strong>	</strong><strong>Jer 10:6-10</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><strong><sup>3</sup></strong>When he speaks, there’s thunder in the rain above; he makes clouds come up from the horizon, makes lightning for the rain, and sends wind out of his storehouses.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><strong><sup>5</sup></strong>They’re worthless objects to ridicule; when they’re punished, they perish.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Jacob’s portion is not like them; <em>his God</em> has made everything; Israel is his inheritance; his name is Yahveh of Hosts.<strong>	</strong><strong>Jer</strong><strong> </strong><strong>10:11-16</strong> <strong>The Looming Destruction</strong></span>
