<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> A Message from Yahveh came to Me: 2"Go proclaim in Jerusalem that Yahveh says, 'I remember your devotion when you were young, your love as my betrothed, your following me in the open country through uncultivated land.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Israel was holy to me, the first fruits of my harvest. Everybody that tried to eat some of it became guilty, and disaster fell on them.'"</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Yahveh says, "What did your ancestors find wrong with me that they left me and followed emptiness and became empty themselves?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> They didn't say, 'Where's Yahveh that brought us out of Egypt and led us through the wilderness, through deserts and pits, drought and deep darkness, through land nobody crossed, where nobody lived?'</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> I brought you to a productive land to eat the good things it produced, but you defiled it and made my inheritance detestable.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> The priests didn't say, 'Where's Yahveh?' <span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Sail to Cyprus and see; send to Kedar and look carefully to see if there's anything like this!</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Has any nation ever changed gods to what weren't gods? But my people have exchanged their honor for what are worthless.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Be appalled at that, sky; shudder and be dismayed.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Young lions have roared at him loudly; they've made his country a wasteland and destroyed his towns without residents.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> What are you doing on the road to Egypt to drink the water in the Nile? Why go to Assyria to drink the water of the Euphrates?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Your own sins will correct you; your apostasies will rebuke you. On every tall hill under every green tree, you've lain down like a prostitute.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Yet I planted you as a choice stock, as faithful seed. How then have you turned yourself into the degenerate sprouts of a wild vine?</span>
