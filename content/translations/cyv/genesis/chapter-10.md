## Genesis Chapter **10**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>The Generations of <u>Shem</u>, <u>Ham</u>, and <u>Japheth</u>, Noah’s Sons and Their Descendants</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup><u>Japheth</u> was the father of <u>Gomer</u>, Magog, Madai, <u>Javan</u>, Tubal, Meshech, and Tiras.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><u>Gomer</u> was the father of Ashkenaz, Riphath, and Togarmah.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup><u>Javan</u> was the father of Elishah, Tarshish, Kittim, and Dodanim.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Those descendants spread inland from the coasts into their nations, each by language and clan. <strong><sup>	</sup></strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Gen 10:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><u>Ham</u> was the father of <u>Cush</u>, <u>Mizraim</u>, Put, and <u>Canaan</u>.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><u>Cush</u> was the father of Seba, Havilah, Sabtah, <u>Raamah</u>, and Sabteca. <u>Raamah</u> was the father of Sheba and Dedan.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup>Cush was also the father of Nimrod, who became prominent in the world, <strong><sup>9</sup></strong>a great hunter before Yahveh. It became an expression, <em>“Like </em><em>Nimrod, a great hunter </em><em>before</em><em> </em><em>Yahveh</em><em>.”</em><em> </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong>His kingdom stretched across Babel, Erech, Accad, and Calneh in Shinar. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>From that land he went to Assyria and built Nineveh, Rehoboth-Ir, Calah, <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>and Resen, a big city between Nineveh and Calah.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup><u>Mizraim</u> became the father of the Ludim, Anamim, Lehabim, Naphtuhim, <strong><sup>14</sup></strong>Pathrusim, Casluhim (ancestors of the Philistines), and Caphtorim.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Gen 10:6-14</p>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup><u>Canaan</u> became the father of Sidon, <em>his firstborn</em>, Heth, <strong><sup>16</sup></strong>the Jebusites, Amorites, Girgashites, <strong><sup>17</sup></strong>Hivites, Arkites, Simites, <strong><sup>18</sup></strong>Arvadites, Zemarites, Hamathites. Afterwards the Canaanite clans spread out <strong><sup>19</sup></strong>from Sidon—on the way to Gera—as far as Gaza across to Sodom, Gomorrah, Admah, and Zeboiim as far as Lasha.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup>Those were Ham’s descendants by clan, language, territory, and nation.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Gen 10:15-20</p>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><u>Shem</u>, Japheth’s older brother, became the ancestor of Eber’s descendants. <strong><sup>22</sup></strong>Shem’s sons were Elam, Asshur, <u>Arpachshad</u>, Lud, and <u>Aram</u>.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup><u>Aram</u>’s sons were Uz, Hul, Gether, and Mash.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup><u>Arpachshad</u> became the father of <u>Shelah</u> Avi-<u>Eber</u>.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup><u>Eber</u> had two sons. One <em>was</em> <u>Peleg</u>; in his time the earth divided. <u>Joktan</u> was <em>his </em>brother, <strong><sup>26</sup></strong>and father of Almodad, Sheleph, Hazarmaveth, Jerah, <strong><sup>27</sup></strong>Hadoram, Uzal, Diklah, <strong><sup>28</sup></strong>Obal, Abimael, Sheba, <strong><sup>29</sup></strong>Ophir, Havilah, and Jobab. <strong><sup>30</sup></strong>They lived from Mesha all the way to Sephar in the eastern hill country.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup><strong>31</strong></sup>Those were Shem’s descendants by clan, language, territory, and nation.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup><strong>32</strong></sup>Those were the clans of Noah’s descendants by genealogy, language, and nation. Nations spread out from them after the flood.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Gen 10:21-32</p>
