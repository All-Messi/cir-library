## Genesis Chapter 10

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The Generations of Shem, Ham, and Japheth, Noah's Sons and Their Descendants</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Japheth was the father of Gomer, Magog, Madai, Javan, Tubal, Meshech, and Tiras.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Gomer was the father of Ashkenaz, Riphath, and Togarmah.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Javan was the father of Elishah, Tarshish, Kittim, and Dodanim.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Those descendants spread inland from the coasts into their nations, each by language and clan.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Ham was the father of Cush, Mizraim, Put, and Canaan.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Cush was the father of Seba, Havilah, Sabtah, Raamah, and Sabteca. Raamah was the father of Sheba and Dedan.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Cush was also the father of Nimrod, who became prominent in the world, 9a great hunter before Yahveh. It became an expression, "Like Nimrod, a great hunter before Yahveh." 10His kingdom stretched across Babel, Erech, Accad, and Calneh in Shinar. 11From that land he went to Assyria and built Nineveh, Rehoboth-Ir, Calah, 12and Resen, a big city between Nineveh and Calah.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Mizraim became the father of the Ludim, Anamim, Lehabim, Naphtuhim, 14Pathrusim, Casluhim (ancestors of the Philistines), and Caphtorim.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Canaan became the father of Sidon, his firstborn, Heth, 16the Jebusites, Amorites, Girgashites, 17Hivites, Arkites, Simites, 18Arvadites, Zemarites, Hamathites. Afterwards the Canaanite clans spread out 19from Sidon—on the way to Gera—as far as Gaza across to Sodom, Gomorrah, Admah, and Zeboiim as far as Lasha.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Those were Ham's descendants by clan, language, territory, and nation.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Shem, Japheth's older brother, became the ancestor of Eber's descendants. 22Shem's sons were Elam, Asshur, Arpachshad, Lud, and Aram.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> Aram's sons were Uz, Hul, Gether, and Mash.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> Arpachshad became the father of Shelah Avi-Eber.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> Eber had two sons. One was Peleg; in his time the earth divided. Joktan was his brother, 26and father of Almodad, Sheleph, Hazarmaveth, Jerah, 27Hadoram, Uzal, Diklah, 28Obal, Abimael, Sheba, 29Ophir, Havilah, and Jobab. 30They lived from Mesha all the way to Sephar in the eastern hill country.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> Those were Shem's descendants by clan, language, territory, and nation.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> Those were the clans of Noah's descendants by genealogy, language, and nation. Nations spread out from them after the flood. The Confusion of Languages</span>
