## Genesis Chapter 11

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The whole world was speaking the same language. 2As people spread east, they found a plain in Shinar and settled there. 3They said, "Let's make bricks and fire them thoroughly." They used brick for stone and tar for mortar. 4"Let's build a city and a tower that reaches the sky. Let's make a name for ourselves so we won't get scattered out all over the world."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Yahveh came down to see the city and tower they were building, 6"They're one set of people with the same language. They're starting to do this, and they'll be able to do anything they want to.  7Let's confuse their language so they can't understand each other."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Yahveh scattered them across the world, and they stopped building their city. 9They called the city Babel because that's where Yahveh "confused" the language of the world.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Shem's Generations Shem was 100 years old when Arpachshad was born, two years after the flood. 11He lived 500 years after that and had sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Arpachshad had lived 35 years when Shelah was born. 13He lived 403 years after that and had sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Shelah had lived 30 years when Eber was born. 15He lived 403 years after that and had sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Eber had lived 34 years when Peleg was born. 17He lived 209 years after that and became the father of sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Peleg had lived 30 years when Reu was born. 19Peleg lived 209 years after that and had sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Reu had lived 32 years when Serug was born. 21He lived 207 years after that and had sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> Serug had lived 30 years when Nahor was born. 23He lived 200 years after that and had sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> Nahor had lived 29 years when Terah was born. 25He lived 119 years after that and had sons and daughters.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Terah had lived 70 years when Abram, Nahor, and Haran were born.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Terah's Generations Terah was the father of Abram, Nahor, and Haran Avi-Lot. 28Haran died before his father Terah in Ur of the Chaldeans, where he was born.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Abram and Nahor married.
