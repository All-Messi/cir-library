## Genesis Chapter 26


### Isaac and Abimelech


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Another famine came over the region besides the one in Abraham’s time. Isaac went to Gerar, to Abimelech, king of the Philistines. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh appeared to him, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t go down to Egypt. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Stay here, and I’ll be with you and bless you. I’ll give your descendants these lands and keep the oath I swore to your father. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ll multiply your descendants like stars in the sky. Your seed will bless all the nations in the world </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because Abraham obeyed me, kept my charges, commandments, statutes, and laws.”</span></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So Isaac lived in Gerar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the men there asked about his wife, he said, </span><i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“She’s my sister.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> He was afraid to say, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“My wife,”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> afraid the men would kill him on account of Rebekah; she was beautiful. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he’d been there a long time, Abimelech looked out the window, and saw Isaac caressing Rebekah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He called him, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“She has to be your wife! Why say she was your sister?”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“For fear that I’d die on account of her.’”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abimelech said, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>“What have you done to us? One of the men might easily have lain with your wife, and you would have brought guilt on us.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abimelech charged everybody, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ll execute whoever touches this man or his wife.” </span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gen 26:1-11</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Isaac sowed </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>grain</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, and the same year it yielded 100 times as much as he planted. Yahveh blessed him, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and he kept getting richer and richer till he was extremely wealthy. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He had flocks, herds, and such a large household that the Philistines got jealous.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They filled the wells his father’s servants had dug. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abimelech told Isaac</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>, “Go away from us. You’re too big to be around us.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Isaac left and settled down in the Valley of Gerar.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He dug again the wells they’d dug in Abraham’s time. The Philistines had stopped them up after Abraham died. He named them the same as his father did. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But when Isaac’s servants dug in the valley and found a well with flowing water, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the herdsmen at Gerar “quarreled” with his herdsmen, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“That’s our water!”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> so he named the well Esek. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They dug another well, and they “quarreled” over that one too; so he named it Sitnah.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He moved and dug another well. They didn’t quarrel over it; so he named it Rehoboth because he said, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Finally, Yahveh has made ‘room’ for us to prosper here.”
</span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gen 26:12-22</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went up to Beersheba, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and Yahveh appeared to him that night, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’m your father’s God. Don’t be afraid; I’m with you. I’ll bless you and multiply your descendants out of regard for Abraham.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He built an altar and called on Yahveh. He pitched his tent there, and his servants dug a well.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abimelech came to him from Gerar with Ahuzzath his advisor and Phicol his commander. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Isaac asked them, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Why did you come to me? You despise me and drove me away.” </span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“We can see Yahveh’s been with you, so we said, ‘Let’s take an oath between us now.’  Let us make a treaty with you: </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>you won’t harm us like we haven’t done anything but good to you and sent you away on good terms. You’re Yahveh’s blessed.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He held a feast for them, and they ate </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>together</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Gen 26:23-30</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the morning they got up early and exchanged oaths. Isaac saw them off, and they left on good terms. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The same day, Isaac’s servants came in and told him about the “well” they’d dug, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“We found water.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So he called it Shibah. The town’s name is still Beersheba. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Esau was 40, he married two Hittite wives: Judith Bat-Beeri and Basemath Bat-Elon. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They were a grief to Isaac and Rebekah.                                                                                                                                              </span></span></p>
