## 1 Chronicles Chapter 11


### David Anointed King


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Israel gathered to David in Hebron, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“We’re your flesh and bone. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Saul was king, you led out and brought in Israel. Yahveh your God said you would shepherd his people and be their leader.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The elders in Israel came to him, and he made a treaty with them in Yahveh’s presence. They anointed him king according to Yahveh’s message through Samuel.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then David and all Israel went to Jerusalem (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jebus</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>). The Jebusites, natives in the land, lived there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They told David, <i>“You won’t get in here”</i>;<i> </i>but he captured the fortress of Zion (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The City of David</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He had said, <i>“Whoever strikes down the Jebusites first will be general </i>of the army<i>.”</i> Joab Ben-Zeruiah went up first, so he became the general. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David lived in the fortress; so they called it The City of David. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He built the city all around from the Millo to the surrounding area, and Joab repaired the rest of Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David became greater and greater because Yahveh was with him.                                                                                                                                    </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Ch 11:1-9</span></b></span></p>


### David’s Mighty Men


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>These are David’s leading powerful warriors, who greatly supported him in his kingdom, along with all Israel, in making him king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They were Jashobeam son of a Hachmonite, chief of the 30. He killed 300 men with his spear at one time. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After him was Eleazar Ben-Dodo the Ahohite, one of the three powerful men. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was with David at Pasdammim when the Philistines converged there for battle. There was a barley field, and the people were fleeing from the Philistines. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They took their stand in the middle of the plot, defended it, and struck down the Philistines. Yahveh gave them a great victory.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Three of the 30 chief men went down to the rock to David into the cave near Adullam while the Philistine army was camping in the Valley of Rephaim. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Davd was in the stronghold</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>while the Philistine garrison was in Bethlehem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David was thirsty and said<i>, “Oh that somebody would give me a drink from the well by the gate at Bethlehem!” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The three broke through the Philistine camp and drew water from the well at Bethlehem and took it to David. But he wouldn’t drink it; he poured it out to Yahveh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“In the presence of my God, I can’t do that. Should I, in effect, drink these men’s blood who risked their lives to bring it?”</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> Those were exploits the three powerful men did.    </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Ch 11:10-19</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abishai, Joab’s brother, was leader of the 30. He killed 300 with a spear; he had a reputation as well as the 30. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Of the three in the second rank, he had the greatest honor and became their commander. But he didn’t measure up to the three.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Benaiah Ben-Jehoiada, son of a fearless warrior from Kabzeel, powerful in deeds, struck down the two sons of Ariel of Moab. He also went down and killed a lion inside a pit on a snowy day. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He killed an Egyptian that was 7ft 6. The Egyptian had a spear like a weaver’s beam, but <i>Benaiah</i> went down to him with a club and snatched the spear from him and killed him with it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24-25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Benaiah was renowned among the 30, but he didn’t measure up to the three. David appointed him over his guard.                            </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Ch 11:20-25</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The elite soldiers in the armies were Asahel brother of Joab, Elhanan Ben-Dodo from Bethlehem, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Shammoth the Harorite, Helez the Pelonite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ira Ben-Ikkesh the Tekoite, Abiezer the Anathothite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Sibbecai the Hushathite, Ilai the Ahohite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Maharai the Netophathite, Heled Ben-Baanah the Netophathite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ithai Ben-Ribai from Gibeah of the sons of Benjamin, Benaiah the Pirathonite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hurai of the brooks of Gaash, Abiel the Arbathite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Azmaveth the Baharumite, Eliahba the Shaalbonite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the sons of Hashem the Gizonite, Jonathan Ben-Shagee the Hararite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahiam Ben-Sacar the Hararite, Eliphal Ben-Ur, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hepher the Mecherathite, Ahijah the Pelonite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hezro the Carmelite, Naarai Ben-Ezbai, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joel brother of Nathan, Mibhar Ben-Hagri, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Zelek the Ammonite, Naharai the Berothite, the armor bearer for Joab Ben-Zeruiah, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ira the Ithrite, Gareb the Ithrite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Uriah the Hittite, Zabad Ben-Ahlai; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v42" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Adina Ben-Shiza the Reubenite, a chief in Reuben and 30 with him; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v43" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hanan Ben-Maacah and Joshaphat the Mithnite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v44" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Uzzia the Ashterathite, Shama and Jeiel sons of Hotham the Aroerite, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v45" id="v45"><sup class="verse-num">45</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jediael Ben-Shimri and Joha his brother, the Tizite; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v46" id="v46"><sup class="verse-num">46</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Eliel the Mahavite and Jeribai and Joshaviah sons of Elnaam, and Ithmah the Moabite; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v47" id="v47"><sup class="verse-num">47</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Eliel, Obed, and Jaasiel the Mezobaite.         </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Ch 11:26-47</span></b></span></p>
