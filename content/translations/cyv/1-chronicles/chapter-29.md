## 1 Chronicles Chapter 29


### Offerings for the Temple


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>David told the assembly, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“God alone has chosen Solomon.  He’s young and new to the work. It’s an important undertaking; the Temple won’t be for some mortal but for Yahveh God. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>I’ve provided as much </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>material <i>as I could: gold, silver, bronze, iron, and wood—plus large amounts of onyx, gems for settings, antimony, colored stones, marble—all kinds of precious stones. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>In addition, I’m giving my own gold and silver: </span></i><a name="_Hlk145085547"></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>113¼<sup> </sup>tons <i><sup>3,000 talents</sup> of gold from Ophir, </i>264¼ tons<i> <sup>7,000 talents</sup> of refined silver</i></span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> for overlaying the walls of the Temple, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>and the gold and silver items the craftsmen will make. Who will offer willingly, consecrating himself to Yahveh today?”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>         </span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>                                                                                                        1 Ch 29:1-5</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The heads of families made freewill offerings, as did the tribal leaders, the commanders of thousands and hundreds, and the officers over the king’s work. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They gave for the service of God’s Temple <a name="_Hlk145086630">188¾ tons <sup>5,000 talents </sup>of gold, 185 lbs <sup>10,000 darics</sup> of gold, 377½ tons <sup>10,000 talents </sup>of silver, 679½ tons <sup>18,000 talents</sup> of brass, and 3775 tons <sup>100,000 talents</sup> of iron</a>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Whoever had precious stones gave them to the treasury in the care of Jehiel the Gershonite. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The people celebrated because they had given willingly and offered freely with all their heart. David also celebrated greatly.</span><b><span style='font-size: 10.0pt;line-height:115%'>        1 Ch 29:6-9</span></b></span></p>


### David’s Prayer of Praise


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He blessed Yahveh in the presence of the assembly,  </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“You’re blessed forever, Yahveh God of our ancestor Israel. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>You have the greatness, the power, the glory, the victory, and the majesty because everything in the sky and on earth belongs to you. The kingdom belongs to you, the exalted head over everything. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Wealth and honor come from you. Power and strength are with you. Your control makes everyone great and strong. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>We thank you and praise your glorious name.</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“But who am I and what’s my people that we can offer as willingly as this? Everything comes from you, and what we’ve given you is from your own things. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>We’re foreigners, nomads like our ancestors. Our time on earth is like a shadow; it’s not permanent. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>This abundance we’ve provided for building you a Temple comes from you and belongs to you. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>I know you test the heart and are delighted with godliness. With a godly heart I’ve freely offered everything and seen your people offering freely and joyfully. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Yahveh God of Abraham, Isaac, and Israel, always keep such purposes and thoughts in your people’s hearts and direct their hearts to you. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Give Solomon my son a whole heart for keeping your commandments, testimonies, and statutes; and for accomplishing everything so he can build the Temple I’ve made provisions for.”   </span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>1 Ch 29:10-19</span></b></span></p>


### Solomon Anointed as King


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Then David told the assembly, <i>“Bless Yahveh your God.”</i> The whole assembly blessed Yahveh God of their ancestors. They bowed their heads in worship to him, and paid homage to the king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They sacrificed to Yahveh, and the next day offered burnt offerings—1,000 bulls, 1,000 rams, and 1,000 lambs with their liquid offerings, and abundant sacrifices for all Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They ate and drank in Yahveh’s presence that day with great happiness.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>They confirmed Solomon as king and anointed him as ruler for Yahveh and Zadok as priest. </span><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Solomon replaced David. He prospered and all Israel obeyed him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The leaders, elite soldiers, and King David’s sons pledged allegiance to him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Yahveh highly elevated him in front of Israel and placed such royal majesty on him as hasn’t been on any <i>other </i>king in Israel.</span></span></p>


### The Death of David


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>David ruled all Israel </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>for 40 years, 7 in Hebron and for 33 in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He died at a good old age, full of wealth and honor, and <b>Solomon</b> reigned. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The acts of King David, from first to last, are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of Samuel the Seer, The Chronicles of Nathan the Prophet</i>, and <i>The Chronicles of Gad the Seer</i>, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>with accounts of his rule and strength, and the </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" ><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>circumstances that came on him, on Israel, and on the surrounding kingdoms.        </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%'> 1 Ch 29:20-30</span></b></p>
