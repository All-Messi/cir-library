## 1 Chronicles Chapter 27

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> These were heads of families, commanders of thousands and hundreds, and their officers that served the king in regard to the divisions that came in and went out month by month all year. Each division numbered 24,000.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Jashobeam Ben-Zabdiel was responsible for the first division for the first month. 3He descended from Perez and was chief of the commanders of the army for the first month.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Dodai the Ahohite and his division were responsible for the second month with Mikloth as chief officer.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> The third commander of the army for the third month was Benaiah Ben-Jehoiada the priest, as chief. 6This Benaiah was the most elite soldier of the 30 and responsible for 30. His son Ammizabad was responsible for his division. 	1 Ch 27:1-6</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> The fourth for the fourth month was Joab's brother Asahel and his son Zebadiah.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> The fifth for the fifth month was commander Shamhuth the Izrahite.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> The six for the sixth month was Ira Ben-Ikkesh the Tekoite.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The seventh for the seventh month was Helez the Pelonite, descendants of Ephraim.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> The eighth for the eighth month was Sibbecai the Hushathite from the Zerahites.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> The ninth for the ninth month was Abiezer the Anathothite from the Benjamites.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> The tenth for the tenth month was Maharai the Netophathite from the Zerahites.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> The eleventh for the eleventh month was Benaiah the Pirathonite, descendant of Ephraim.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> The twelfth for the twelfth month was Heldai the Netophathite of Othniel. 	1 Ch 27:7-15 Tribal Leaders</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> In charge of the tribes of Israel The chief officer for the Reubenites was Eliezer Ben-Zichri; for the Simeonites, Shephatiah Ben-Maacah;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> for Levi, Hashabiah Ben-Kemuel; for Aaron, Zadok;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> for Judah, Elihu, David's brother; for Issachar, Omri Ben-Michael;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> for Zebulun, Ishmaiah Ben-Obadiah; for Naphtali, Jeremoth Ben-Azriel;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> for Ephraim, Hoshea Ben-Azaziah; for the Manasseh half-tribe, Joel Ben-Pedaiah;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> for the Manasseh half-tribe in Gilead, Iddo Ben-Zechariah; for Benjamin, Jaasiel Ben-Abner;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> for Dan, Azarel Ben-Jeroham. Those were the officers of the tribes of Israel.	 1 Ch 27:16-22</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> But David didn't count the ones under 20 because Yahveh had said he would multiply Israel like the stars in the sky. 24Joab Ben-Zeruiah had started to count them but didn't finish. Wrath came on Israel because of it, and the number wasn't included in The Chronicles of King David.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> Azmaveth Ben-Adiel oversaw the king's storehouses. Jonathan Ben-Uzziah oversaw the storehouses in the country, towns, villages, and towers.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Ezri Ben-Chelub oversaw the farmers that plowed the ground.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Shimei the Ramathite oversaw vineyards. Zabdi the Shiphmite oversaw what the vineyards produced and what was stored in cellars.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Baal-hanan the Gederite oversaw the olive and sycamore trees in the western foothills. Joash oversaw the stores of oil. 	1 Ch 27:23-28</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Shitrai the Sharonite oversaw the cattle that grazed in Sharon. Shaphat Ben-Adlai oversaw the cattle in the valleys.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> Obil the Ishmaelite oversaw the camels. Jehdeiah the Meronothite oversaw the donkeys.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> Jaziz the Hagrite oversaw the flocks. Those oversaw David's property.	1 Ch 27:29-31</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> Jonathan, David's uncle, was an intelligent advisor and secretary. Jehiel Ben-Hachmoni tutored David's sons.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> Ahithophel was advisor to the king. Hushai the Archite was the king's friend.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> Jehoiada Ben-Benaiah and Abiathar succeededpl Ahithophel. Joab commanded the king's army.	    1 Ch 27:32-34 David's Charge to Israel</span>
