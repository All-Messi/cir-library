## 2 Samuel Chapter 14


### Absalom Returns to Jerusalem


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Joab sensed that the king yearned for Absalom. </span></span>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Act like you’re in mourning. <span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Now the whole family has risen up and told me to hand over the one that struck his brother so they can put him to death for the life of his brother, and destroy the heir too. They’ll put out my ember that’s left and won’t leave my husband a name or a remnant.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Go on home. <p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The woman said, <i>“My lord, the sin is on me and my father’s family. You and your</i></span><i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>throne<sup> </sup>are innocent.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He said, <i>“Bring me whoever says anything. He won’t bother you again.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She said<i>, “Invoke Yahveh your God so the avenger won’t destroy my son.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“As sure as Yahveh lives, not one of his hairs will fall to the ground.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">2 Sam 14:9-11</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Then she said, <i>“Let me say something.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Go ahead.”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <a name="_Hlk126065361"><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Why plan such a thing then against God’s people? </span></i></a><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>In saying this, you’ve<sup> </sup>convicted yourself for not bringing back the one you’ve banished. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>We’ll die like water spilled on the ground that can’t be retrieved. Yet God doesn’t take away life but plans ways to bring back the one he banishes. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>I’ve come to you because the people have made me afraid. I thought, ‘I’ll speak to the king. Maybe he’ll do what I ask. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He’ll deliver me from the man that would destroy me and my son from God’s inheritance.’ </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Then I thought, ‘My lord’s word will comfort me, because he’s like an angel of God in discerning good and bad.’ Yahveh your God be with you.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He answered, <i>“Don’t hide anything I’m about to ask you. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Is Joab involved in this with you?”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“As sure as you’re alive, nobody can stray away from anything you’ve</span></i><i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt; line-height:115%'> </span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>said. Joab told me to say that. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He did it to change how things are going. But you are wise, like an angel of God. You know what’s </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>going on <i>in the world.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The king told Joab, <i>“I’ll do it. Bring him back.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Joab fell on his face and blessed him. <i>“I know you’re pleased with me today. You’ve done what I asked.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'> </span><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Joab went to Geshur and brought Absalom to Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>But the king said, <i>“Have him come back to his own house without seeing me.” </i>He came back without seeing <i>David</i>.                                                </span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When he cut his hair (</span>at the end of every year because it was so thick<span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>), he weighed it at <a name="_Hlk145154996">5 lbs <sup>200 shekels, royal standard</sup></a>. </span></span>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Absalom lived in Jerusalem for two years without seeing the king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Then he sent for Joab so he could send him to the king, but Joab wouldn’t come. He sent again and he wouldn’t come. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Absalom told his servants,<i> “Joab’s barley field is next to mine. Set it on fire.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Joab went to Absalom’s house and asked, <i>“Why did your servants burn my field?” </i></span></span></p>
