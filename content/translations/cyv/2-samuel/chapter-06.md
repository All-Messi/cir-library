## 2 Samuel Chapter 6


### The Ark Brought to Jerusalem


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David gathered elite troops from Israel again, 30,000. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He and the troops went to Baale-judah to bring up God’s Ark called The Name: Yahveh of Hosts Enthroned Above the Cherubs.<i> </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They placed the Ark on a new cart to bring it from Abinadab’s house that was on the hill. Uzzah and Ahio, Abinadab’s sons, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>were bringing the Ark on the cart; Ahio was walking ahead of it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Meanwhile, David and the House of Israel were celebrating enthusiastically in Yahveh’s presence with all kinds of instruments made out of fir: lyres, harps, tambourines, castanets, and cymbals. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But when they came to Nacon’s threshing floor, Uzzah grabbed the Ark because the oxen nearly upset it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh lashed out and struck him down for his disrespect. He died there by the Ark. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David was mad because Yahveh had “lashed out against Uzzah.” The place is still called Perez-uzzah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David was afraid of Yahveh that day, <i>“How can the Ark come to me?”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He wasn’t willing to take it to The City of David. He took it aside to the house of Obed-edom of Gath. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It stayed there three months, and Yahveh blessed him and his family.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>King David heard that Yahveh had blessed Obed-edom’s house and everything he had because of the Ark, so he brought it to The City of David with celebration. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the ones carrying the Ark had gone six steps, he sacrificed an ox and a grain-fed calf. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was dancing in Yahveh’s presence with all his energy, wearing a linen ephod. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He and the House of Israel were bringing up the Ark with shouting and the sound of rams’ horns. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As it came into the city, Michal, Saul’s daughter, looked out the window and saw him leaping and dancing, and she despised him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They brought the Ark and set it in its place inside the tent David pitched for it, and he offered burnt offerings and peace offerings. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he’d finished offering them, he blessed the people in the name of Yahveh of hosts. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He passed a cake of bread, a portion of meat, and a cake of raisins to every man and woman in the crowd. Then everybody went home.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                             </span></b></span></p>
