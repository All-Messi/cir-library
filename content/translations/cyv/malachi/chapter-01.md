## Malachi Chapter 1


### Conversational Yahveh Covenant


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;text-align:center'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>An Oracle from Yahveh to Israel Through Malachi</span></span></p>


### Jacob Loved More Than Esau


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh says,<i> “I’ve loved you.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> But you ask, <i>“What do you mean ‘loved us’?”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <i>“Wasn’t Esau Jacob’s brother? Yet I’ve loved Jacob </i></span><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and set Esau aside, made his hill country desolate, and left his inheritance for desert jackals.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Esau’s descendants claim, <i>“We’ve been beaten down, but we’ll come back and rebuild the ruins.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            But Yahveh of Hosts says, <i>“They may rebuild, but I’ll tear </i>it <i>down </i>again<i>. People will call <a name="_Hlk123035070">them The Evil Country, The Nation Yahveh Is Angry with Forever.” </a></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>You’ll see this and say, <i>“May Yahveh be honored beyond Israel’s boundaries!”</i></span></span></p>


### Blemished Sacrifices


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh says to priests that despise him,<i> “A son honors his father, and a servant respects his master. Then if I’m a father, where’s my honor? If I’m a master, where’s my respect?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“But you ask,</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> <i>‘Dishonored you? How?’”</i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You present defiled sacrifices on my altar.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“But you ask, ‘Defiled sacrifices, how?’”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“By disrespecting my altar. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When you offer the blind, lame, and sick, isn’t that disrespect? Why not offer something like that to your governor? Would he be happy with you? Would he accept you?”</span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Mal 1:1-8</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Will you ask me to bless you and be gracious to you? With offerings like that, why would I be gracious to anybody? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If only there’d be one among you that would shut the </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Temple
<i>doors so you wouldn’t light fire on my altar for nothing! I’m not happy with you, and I won’t accept your offerings. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From sunrise to sunset, I intend to be honored as great the world over, wherever there’s incense and pure grain offerings. </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“But you profane me by saying, ‘It won’t hurt anything to defile Yahveh’s table; the sacrifices for it are no big deal.’ </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They’re so tiresome!’ </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:4.5pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You blow it off and bring what you stole, something lame or sick, so </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>you can say<i> you brought an offering! </i></span><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A curse on the cheat that vows a male from his flock but sacrifices a blemished one. What <u>is</u> this?!</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> <i>I’m a great King to be respected the world over!”           </i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Mal 1:9-14</p>
