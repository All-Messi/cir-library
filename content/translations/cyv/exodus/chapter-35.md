## Exodus Chapter 35

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Moses gathered the community and said, "Yahveh has commanded these things: 2'You can work for six days, but set the seventh day aside to himY as a complete rest. Whoever works on it will be executed. 3Don't start a fire in your houses on the Sabbath.'" Materials for the Tabernacle</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Moses told them, "Yahveh has commanded 5to take an offering for himY from everybody that's willing to contribute: gold, silver, and bronze;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> blue, purple, and scarlet material; fine linen; goats' hair, 7rams' skins dyed red, dolphin skins; acacia wood;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> olive oil for lighting, spices for anointing oil and fragrant incense;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> onyx stones and setting stones for the ephod and chest pouch. 10'The artisans need to make everything I'veY commanded:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> the Tabernacle, its tent and covering, its hooks and boards, its bar, posts, and bases;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> the Ark and its poles; the mercy seat, the dividing curtain;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> the table, its poles, utensils, and the Bread of the Presence;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> the lampstand for the light and its utensils, lamps, and olive oil;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> the altar of incense, its poles, anointing oil, and fragrant incense; the curtain across the Tabernacle entrance;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> the altar for burnt offerings with its bronze grating, poles, utensils, basin, and stand;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> the curtains for the court, its posts and bases, the screen for the entrance to the court;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> the pegs for the Tabernacle and the court and their cords;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> the woven vestments for performing service in the Holy Place, the holy vestments for Aaron the priest and his sons.'"</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> The community left. 21Everyone whose heart and spirit stirred him brought a contribution for the work on the Tent of Meeting, its service, and holy vestments.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> Men and women brought brooches, earrings, signet rings, bracelets, and gold articles; and offered them to Yahveh.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> People that had blue, purple, and scarlet material; fine linen; goats' hair, rams' skins dyed red, and dolphin skins brought them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> People that could contribute silver or bronze brought it. Men that had acacia wood brought it for any work of the service. 25-26Skilled women brought blue, purple, and scarlet material; fine linen, and goats' hair they'd spun.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Rulers brought onyx stones and stones for setting the ephod and chest pouch.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> They brought spices and olive oil for the light, anointing oil, and fragrant incense.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Israelite men and women that were moved to bring material for the work Yahveh commanded, brought it to him as a free-will offering. Bezalel and Oholiab</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> Moses told the people, "Yahveh has appointed Bezalel Ben-Uri Ben-Hur from the tribe of Judah. 31He has filled him with wisdom, understanding, knowledge, and craftsmanship 32to make designs for working in gold, silver, and bronze; 33for cutting stones for settings, carving wood, and doing all the creative work. 34He and Oholiab Ben-Ahisamach from the tribe of Dan, are willing to teach these skills. 35God has given them the skill to engrave, design, embroider, and weave blue, purple, and scarlet material and fine linen.</span>
