## Exodus Chapter 27


### The Bronze Altar


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:9.0pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Make an altar out of acacia wood, 7½ x 7½ x 4½ft high </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>with a horn on each corner. Make them one piece with the altar and overlaid with bronze. </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:9.0pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Make bronze pails for removing the ashes, bronze shovels, basins, forks, and fire pans. </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:9.0pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Make a network of bronze grating for it, and on the network put four bronze rings at the corners. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Put the grating under the ledge of the altar so it reaches halfway up the altar.</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:9.0pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“For the altar make acacia poles overlaid with bronze. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Insert the poles into the rings on the two sides of the altar for carrying it. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Make it hollow with planks like you saw on the mountain.                         </span></i></span></span></p>

### The Court of the Tabernacle


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:9.0pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“</span></sup></i><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Make a court for the Tabernacle. Its south side will have curtains made out of finely twined linen 150ft long for one side. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’ll have 20 posts with 20 bronze bases. The hooks and bands on the posts will be silver. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Make the north side of the court the same way. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The west end of the court will have 75ft of curtains with 10 posts with their 10 bases. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The east end of the court will be 75ft wide. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The curtains for one side of the entrance will be 22½ft long with 3 posts and their 3 bases. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Make the other side of the entrance the same way. </span></i><b><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>16</span></sup></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The entrance to the court will be a screen 30ft wide woven out of blue, purple, and scarlet </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>material </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>with finely twined linen; with 4 posts and their 4 bases. </span></i><b><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>17</span></sup></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The posts around the court will have silver bands with silver hooks and bronze bases. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The court will be 150 x 75ft with </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>curtains</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> 7½ft high and bronze bases </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>under its posts. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Make out of bronze the Tabernacle’s utensils of service, its pegs, and the pegs in the court.                                                                                                           </span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ex 27:9-19</p>


### Oil for the Lamp


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:9.0pt; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Charge the people to bring pure oil from mashed olives for a continually burning light in the lamp. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the Tent of Meeting, outside the curtain in front of the Testimony, Aaron and his sons will keep the light burning from evening to morning in front of Yahveh—a permanent statute for Israel’s descendants.</span></i></span></span></p>
