## Exodus Chapter 38


### The Altar of Burnt Offering


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he built <u>the altar for burnt offering </u>out of acacia wood: 7½ x 7½ x 4½ft high. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He formed horns on its four corners one piece with it and overlaid it with bronze. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He made bronze utensils for the altar: pails, shovels, basins, meat hooks, and fire pans. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He fashioned a bronze grating under the edge of the altar that reached halfway up, </span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> and cast 4 rings for the 4 ends of the grating to hold the poles. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He formed the poles out of acacia wood overlaid with bronze, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and inserted them into the rings for carrying it. He made it hollow with planks.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He created <u>the laver</u> out of bronze with a bronze base, from the mirrors of the women that served at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting.                                                                                                                                              </span></span></p>

### The Courtyard


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he built <u>the court</u>. For the south side he made curtains out of finely twined linen 150ft long. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There were 20 posts with 20 bronze bases with hooks on the posts and silver bands. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The north side was 150ft long with 20 posts, 20 bronze bases, and silver hooks and bands on the posts. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The west side was 75ft long with 10 posts, 10 bases, and silver hooks and bands for the posts. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The east side was 75ft long. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The curtains on one side of the entrance were 22½ft long with 3 posts and 3 bases. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The other side was the same. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He made the curtains out of finely twined linen. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The bases for the posts were bronze; their hooks and bands were silver, and their tops were overlaid with silver.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The screen</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> for the court entrance was woven out of blue, purple, and scarlet </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>material; </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and finely twined linen. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The 4 posts and their bases were bronze, their hooks were silver, with silver overlay on the tops and bands on the posts. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The pegs for the Tabernacle and the court were bronze.                                                                                                                                             </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ex 38:9-20</p>
