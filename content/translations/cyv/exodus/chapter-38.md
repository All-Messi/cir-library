## Exodus Chapter 38

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Then he built <u>the altar for burnt offering </u>out of acacia wood: 7½ x 7½ x 4½ft high. <strong><sup>2</sup></strong>He formed horns on its four corners one piece with it and overlaid it with bronze. <strong><sup>3</sup></strong>He made bronze utensils for the altar: pails, shovels, basins, meat hooks, and fire pans. <strong><sup>4</sup></strong>He fashioned a bronze grating under the edge of the altar that reached halfway up, <strong><sup>5</sup></strong>and cast 4 rings for the 4 ends of the grating to hold the poles. <strong><sup>6</sup></strong>He formed the poles out of acacia wood overlaid with bronze, <strong><sup>7</sup></strong>and inserted them into the rings for carrying it. He made it hollow with planks. <strong>Basin for Washing</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> He created <u>the laver</u> out of bronze with a bronze base, from the mirrors of the women that served at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ex 38:1-8</p>

### The Courtyard


<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Then he built <u>the court</u>. For the south side he made curtains out of finely twined linen 150ft long. <strong><sup>10</sup></strong>There were 20 posts with 20 bronze bases with hooks on the posts and silver bands. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>The north side was 150ft long with 20 posts, 20 bronze bases, and silver hooks and bands on the posts. <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>The west side was 75ft long with 10 posts, 10 bases, and silver hooks and bands for the posts. <strong><sup>13</sup></strong>The east side was 75ft long. <strong><sup>14</sup></strong>The curtains on one side of the entrance were 22½ft long with 3 posts and 3 bases. <strong><sup>15</sup></strong>The other side was the same. <strong><sup>16</sup></strong>He made the curtains out of finely twined linen. <strong><sup>17</sup></strong>The bases for the posts were bronze; their hooks and bands were silver, and their tops were overlaid with silver.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <u>The screen</u> for the court entrance was woven out of blue, purple, and scarlet <em>material; </em>and finely twined linen. <strong><sup>19</sup></strong>The 4 posts and their bases were bronze, their hooks were silver, with silver overlay on the tops and bands on the posts. <strong><sup>20</sup></strong>The pegs for the Tabernacle and the court were bronze.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ex 38:9-20</p>

### Inventory of Materials


<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Those are the items for the Tabernacle of Testimony that Moses commanded for the Levites’ service, <em>as recorded </em>by Ithamar Ben-Aaron the priest. <strong><sup>22</sup></strong>Bezalel Ben-Uri Ben-Hur, from the tribe of Judah, made everything Yahveh told Moses. <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>Working with him was Oholiab Ben-Ahisamach from the tribe of Dan, an engraver, artisan, and weaver in blue, purple, and scarlet <em>material</em>; and fine linen.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Ex 38:21-23</p>
