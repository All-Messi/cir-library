## 2 Kings Chapter 8


### The Shunammite’s Land Restored


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Elisha told the woman whose son he’d revived, <i>“Take your family and live temporarily wherever you can. Yahveh has called for a seven-year famine here.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>They went to stay with the Philistines. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Seven years later, she came back and appealed to the king for her house and farm. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He was talking to Gehazi, the man of God’s servant, <i>“Tell me the great things Elisha has done.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>As he was telling him how he’d restored life to <i>a boy</i>, she appealed to the king for her house and farm. Gehazi said, <i>“My lord the king, that’s the woman, and that’s her son!” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When the king asked her, she told him about it. He appointed an officer to give her back everything, including the crops since she left.<b>        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%'>2 Kg 8:1-6</span></b></span></p>


### Hazael Murders Ben-Hadad


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Elisha went to Damascus. Benhadad, king of Aram, was sick and heard that the man<i> </i>of God was there<i>.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>He told Hazael, <i>“Take him a gift and ask Yahveh by him if I’ll recover.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Hazael went to meet him with a gift, every kind of good thing in Damascus—40 camel loads. He stood in front of him and said, <i>“Your son Benhadad, king of Aram, has sent me to ask you if he’ll recover from his sickness.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Elisha said,<i> “He’ll recover, but Yahveh has shown me that he’ll certainly die.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He stared at him till he was embarrassed, and the man of God cried. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Hazael said, <i>“Why are you crying, Sir?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Because I know the terrible things you’ll do to Israel. You’ll set their fortresses on fire, kill their young men with the sword, dash their little ones to pieces, and rip open their pregnant women.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“What am I, a dog, to do things like that?”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Elisha answered, <i>“Yahveh has shown me that you’ll be king of Aram.”</i>
</span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He went back to his master, who said, <i>“What did Elisha say to you?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“He said you’d recover.”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The next day <i>Hazael</i> dipped a blanket in water and pressed it over Benhadad’s face, to kill him. Hazael became king.<b>  </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 8:7-15</span></b></span></p>


### Jehoram Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>In the <b>fifth year of </b><u>Joram</u><i>/Jehoram</i> Ben-Ahab, king of Israel—<i>who ruled at the time of </i>Jehoshaphat, king of Judah—<b>Jehoram</b> Ben-Jehoshaphat became king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He was 32 and ruled for 8 years in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He followed Israel’s kings like Ahab had done because he’d married Ahab’s daughter. He did evil, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>but for David’s sake Yahveh wasn’t willing to destroy Judah. He’d promised to always give him a lamp through his descendants.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>During his reign, Edom revolted from Judah and appointed a king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Joram and his chariots crossed over to Zair. He got up at night and struck at the Edomites that had surrounded him and the captains of the chariots, but his army fled to their tents. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Edom has been in revolt against Judah ever since. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The rest of the acts of Joram/<i>Jehoram</i> are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He died and they buried him in The City of David. His son Ahaziah became king. <b>                                                         </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 8:16-24</span></b></span></p>


### Ahaziah Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>In the <b>twelfth year of </b><u>Joram</u>/<i>Jehoram </i>Ben-Ahab, king of Israel, <b>Ahaziah</b> Ben-Jehoram began to rule. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He was 22 and ruled for 1 year in Jerusalem. His mother was Athaliah, granddaughter of Omri, king of Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He did evil like Ahab’s family <i>had done</i> because he was Ahab’s son-in-law. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He went to war along with Joram against Hazael, king of Aram in Ramoth-gilead, and the Arameans wounded him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>King Joram came back to Jezreel to heal up. Then Ahaziah went down to see Joram because he’d been wounded.<b>  </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 8:25-29</span></b></span></p>
