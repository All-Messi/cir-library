## 2 Kings Chapter 14


### Amaziah Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>In the <b>second year of Joash</b>/<i>Jehoash</i> Ben-Joahaz, king of Israel, <b>Amaziah</b> Ben-Joash became king of Judah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He was 25 and ruled for 29 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Jehoaddin of Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He did what was good, but not like David did. He did what Joash his father had done, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>but he didn’t take away the high places. The people still sacrificed and burned incense on them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>As soon as the kingdom was firmly in hand, he executed the officials that had assassinated his father. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He didn’t execute their sons, as the Law said that Yahveh commanded Moses, <i>“Don’t execute parents for offspring or offspring for parents. Execute each person for that person’s sin.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He killed 10,000 Edomites in the Valley of Salt, took Sela, and named it Joktheel to this day.                                                                     </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 14:1-7</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He sent ambassadors to Jehoash </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%'>(Ben-Jehoahaz Ben-Jehu)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>, king of Israel<i>, “Come; let’s face each other.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Jehoash, king of Israel, sent to Amaziah, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“The thorn bush in Lebanon sent to the cedar in Lebanon, ‘Give your daughter to my son in marriage.’ But a wild animal in Lebanon passed by and tramped down the thorn bush. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>You’ve defeated Edom and become proud. Enjoy your honor and stay home. Why stir up trouble so you and Judah fall?”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>But Amaziah ignored him; Jehoash went up and faced Amaziah at Beth-shemesh in Judah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Israel defeated Judah and each man fled home. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Jehoash captured Amaziah at Beth-shemesh, came to Jerusalem, and tore down the wall from the Gate of Ephraim to the Corner Gate—600ft</span><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He took the gold, silver, and utensils in Yahveh’s Temple and the king’s treasuries, as well as hostages, and went back to Samaria. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The rest of the acts of Jehoash, the strength of his rule, and his war with Amaziah are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Israel.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He died and they buried him in Samaria with the kings of Israel. His son Jeroboam</span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>II</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> became king.<b>        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 14:8-16 </span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Amaziah lived 15 years after the death of Jehoash. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The rest of the acts of Amaziah are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>A conspiracy against him in Jerusalem caused him to flee to Lachish, but they sent assassins and killed him there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20-21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The people in Judah took his son Azariah/<i>Uzziah</i> and made him king at 16. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He built Elath and restored it to Judah after the king died. </span></span></p>


### Jeroboam II Rules in Israel


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>In the <b>fifteenth year of Amaziah</b>, <u>Jeroboam</u></span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>II</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Ben-Joash, king of Israel, became king in Samaria <i>and ruled</i> for 41 years. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He didn’t abandon the sins of Jeroboam</span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>I</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Ben-Nebat that got Israel to sin. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He restored Israel’s border from Lebo-hamath as far as the Sea of Arabah in keeping with Yahveh’s message through Jonah Ben-Amittai, prophet from Gath-hepher. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Yahveh saw that Israel’s suffering was bitter, whether bond or free, and there was nobody to help them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Since Yahveh hadn’t yet said he’d blot out Israel, he saved them by Jeroboam</span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>II</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The rest of the acts of Jeroboam</span><b><sup><span style='font-size: 14.0pt;line-height:115%'>II</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>, the strength of his rule, and how he fought and recovered Damascus and Hamath for Israel (</span><i>cities </i>that had belonged to Judah<span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>), are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Israel.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He died and his son <u>Zechariah</u> became king.<b>        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 14:17-29</span></b></span></p>
