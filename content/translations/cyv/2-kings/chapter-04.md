## 2 Kings Chapter 4


### The Widow’s Oil Jar


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>The wife of a man from the company of prophets cried out to Elisha, <i>“My husband’s dead. You know how he respected Yahveh. A creditor has come to take my two children as his slaves.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“What can I do? What do you have in the house?” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“Nothing but a jug of oil.”</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Borrow containers from your neighbors—not just a few. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>You and your sons go in and shut the door. Pour </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>the oil<i> into those containers and set the full ones aside.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She went in and shut the door. They brought her the containers, and she poured <i>out the oil</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>When they were full, she told her son, <i>“Bring me another one.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He said, <i>“There aren’t any more.”</i> The oil stopped. </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She went and told the man of God. </span></span></p>


### He said, “Sell the oil to pay your debt. You and your sons can use the rest.”        2 Kg 4:1-7



### The Shunammite’s Son Resurrected


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Elisha passed over to Shunem, where a well-to-do woman lived. She persuaded him to eat<i> there</i>. So, whenever he went by, he’d stop there to eat. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She told her husband, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“I sense that this is a holy man of God. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Let’s build a small upper room with walls, and put a bed, table and chair, and lampstand in there for him. When he comes, he can turn in there.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>One day he came and turned in to the upper room and rested. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He told Gehazi his servant to call the Shunammite.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>When she came, </span><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Elisha
</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>told him to tell her,<i> “You’ve taken care of us so often. What can I do for you? Do you want me to speak to the king or the commander of the army for you?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She answered, <i>“I live among my own people.”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He asked Gehazi, <i>“What can we do for her?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“She doesn’t have a son, and her husband is elderly.”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“Call her.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She came to the doorway. </span><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He said, <i>“At this time next year, you’ll be holding a son.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“Man of God, don’t give me false hope!” </span></i><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She did get pregnant and had a son at that time the next year.<b>  </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%'>2 Kg 4:8-17</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>When the boy was growing up, he went out to his father and the harvest crew one day, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>and told him, <i>“My head hurts! my head hurts!”</i> He told his servant, <i>“Carry him to his mother.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When he got him to her, the boy sat on her lap till noon and died. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She went up and laid him on the man of God’s bed, shut the door, and went out. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She called her husband, <i>“Send me a servant and a donkey so I can go quick to the man of God and back.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“Why go to him today? It’s not a new moon or Sabbath.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She said, <i>“It’ll be good.”</i> </span><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>She saddled the donkey and told her servant, <i>“Drive the animal! Don’t slow down unless I tell you.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She went to the man of God on Mount Carmel. When he saw her at a distance, he said to Gehazi, <i>“That’s the Shunammite. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Run and ask her if she and her husband and boy are all right.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She answered, <i>“Yes.”</i> <b>                                                                                          </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 4:18-27</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>When she came to the man of God on the hill, she took hold of his feet. Gehazi came to pull her away, but he said, <i>“Let her alone; she’s upset. Yahveh hasn’t revealed it to me.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>She said, <i>“Did I ask you for a son? Didn’t I say not to give me false hope?”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He told Gehazi, <i>“Gird up*, take my staff, and go. Don’t greet anybody. If anybody greets you, don’t answer. Lay my staff on the boy’s face.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The mother swore, <i>“As sure as Yahveh lives and you live, I won’t leave without you.” </i>He went along. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Gehazi went on ahead and laid the staff on the boy’s face, but he didn’t make a sound or respond. <i>Gehazi</i> went back and told him, <i>“The boy hasn’t woken up.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When Elisha went into the house, the boy was dead on the bed. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He went in, shut the door, and prayed to Yahveh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He went and lay down on the child, mouth to mouth, eyes to eyes, hands to hands, and stretched out on him. The child’s body got warm. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He came back and paced back and forth in the house, and went up and stretched out on him <i>again</i>. The boy sneezed seven times and opened his eyes. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He called Gehazi, <i>“Call her.”</i> When she came in, he said, <i>“Pick up your boy.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She fell at his feet and bowed to the floor. She picked up the boy and went out.    </span><b><span style='font-size: 10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 4:28-37</span></b></span></p>


### The Toxic Gourds


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>When Elisha got back to Gilgal, there was a famine in the land. As men from the company of prophets were sitting with him, he told Gehazi, <i>“Put on a large pot and heat stew for them.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>One of them went out into the field to gather plants, found a wild vine, and gathered his lap full of gourds from it. He came and sliced them into the pot of stew. They didn’t know <i>what they were</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They poured it out for the people to eat. After they’d eaten a little, they cried out, <i>“Man of God, there’s poison in this stew!” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He said, <i>“Bring some</i> <i>meal.”</i> He threw it into the pot and said, <i>“Pour it out for the people to eat.”</i>  Then there wasn’t anything harmful in it.</span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v43" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>His attendant said, <i>“What? How can I feed 100 men with this?”</i> But he said, <i>“Give it to them; Yahveh says they’ll eat and have some left over.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v44" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He set it in front of them, and they ate and had some left over.<b>    </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%'>2 Kg 4:38-44</span></b></span></p>
