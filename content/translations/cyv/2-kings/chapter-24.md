## 2 Kings Chapter 24

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>In</span><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Jehoiakim’s reign, Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon, came up, and Jehoiakim served him for 3 years, then revolted. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Yahveh sent bands of Babylonians, Arameans, Moabites, and Ammonites against him to destroy Judah like he’d warned by his prophets. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>At Yahveh’s command, that all came on Judah because of Manasseh’s sins </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>and the innocent blood he’d filled Jerusalem with. Yahveh wouldn’t forgive. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The rest of the acts of Jehoiakim are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He died and his son Jehoiachin became king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The king of Egypt didn’t come out of his country again, because the king of Babylon took everything he had from the River of Egypt to the Euphrates River.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt; line-height:115%'>     2 Kg 24:1-7</span></b></span></p>


### Jehoiachin Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Jehoiachin</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'> was 18 when he became king and ruled for 3 months in Jerusalem. His mother was Nehushta Bat-Elnathan from Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He did evil like his father.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>At that time, the officers of Nebuchadnezzar came up to Jerusalem and laid siege to it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He came while they were besieging it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Jehoiachin, his mother, advisors, commanders, and officials went out to him. The king of Babylon took him captive in <b>the eighth year of his reign</b>.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He carried the treasures out of Yahveh’s Temple and the king’s palace, and cut up the gold articles Solomon had made in the Temple—like Yahveh said. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>He led into exile Jerusalem, the commanders, trained soldiers (10,000 captives), craftsmen, and smiths. Only the poorest people remained. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He led Jehoiachin to Babylon along with his mother, his wives, officials, leading men of the country, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>the brave men (7,000), the craftsmen and smiths (1,000), the ones strong and fit for war. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He made his uncle <b>Mattaniah</b> king and changed his name to Zedekiah. </span></span></p>


### Zedekiah Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Zedekiah</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'> was 21 and ruled for 11 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Hamutal Bat-Jeremiah of Libnah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He did evil like Jehoiakim. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Because of Yahveh’s anger, this happened in Jerusalem and Judah till he threw them out from his presence. Zedekiah revolted against the king of Babylon.                                                                                                                           </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 24:8-20</span></b></span></p>
