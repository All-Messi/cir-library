## 2 Kings Chapter 17


### Hoshea Last King of Israel


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>In the <b>twelfth year of Ahaz</b>, <u>Hoshea</u> Ben-Elah became king of Israel in Samaria <i>and ruled</i> for 9 years. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He did evil, but not as bad as the previous kings of Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Shalmaneser, king of Assyria, attacked him, and he had to pay tribute <i>to Assyria</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>But the king of Assyria discovered a plot he was laying. He’d sent ambassadors to So, king of Egypt, and withheld the annual tribute to Assyria; so <i>Shalmaneser</i> shut him up bound in prison. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Then he invaded the whole country and went up to Samaria and laid siege to it for 3 years. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>In the <b>ninth year of Hoshea</b>, the king of Assyria conquered the city and took Israel into exile in Assyria. He settled them in Halah and Habor on the Gozan River and in towns of the Medes.<b>    </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 17:1-6</span></b></span></p>


### Israel Exiled over Sin


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>That happened because they sinned against Yahveh their God, who had brought them up from Egypt from under Pharaoh. They’d feared other gods, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>followed the customs of the nations Yahveh had <i>originally </i>driven out, and the customs Israel’s kings had brought in. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Israel secretly did what wasn’t right: </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>they built high places in their towns from watchtower to fortified city; </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>they put sacred pillars and Asherah poles on every tall hill under every green tree; </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>they burned incense on the high places—things that provoked Yahveh. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>They served idols Yahveh told them not to serve, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>even though he warned Israel and Judah through his prophets and seers, <i>“Turn from your sins and keep my commandments, my statutes—the whole Law I’ve commanded your ancestors through the prophets.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>But they ignored him. They stiffened their necks like the ancestors who didn’t believe in Yahveh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They rejected the statutes and the covenant he made with their ancestors and <i>dismissed
</i>his warnings. They became vain and followed the nations around them that he commanded them not to be like.<b>               </b></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>                                                                                                                              </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 17:7-15</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>They made molten images, <i>that is, </i>two calves and an Asherah pole. They worshiped the host of heaven and served Baal. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They burned their sons and daughters as offerings, and practiced fortunetelling and sorcery. They provoked Yahveh by selling themselves to sinning. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He was angry with them and removed them away from him. None were left except the tribe of Judah.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Judah didn’t keep his commandments <i>either</i>, but followed the customs Israel brought in. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Yahveh rejected Israel’s descendants, oppressed them, and delivered them to plunderers till he’d thrown them out. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>When he’d torn Israel away from David’s dynasty, they made Jeroboam</span><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>I</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Ben-Nebat king. He drove them away from Yahveh and got them to commit a great sin. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Israel</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> continued to follow in his sins </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>till Yahveh removed <i>Israel</i> away from him. Israel has been in exile in Assyria ever since.</span></span></p>


### Foreigners Settled in Samaria


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The king of Assyria brought people from Babylon, Cuthah, Avva, Hamath, and Sepharvaim, and settled them in the towns in Samaria in place of the Israelites. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When they first started living there, they didn’t respect Yahveh. He sent lions among them and killed some of them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They spoke to the king of Assyria, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“The nations you exiled into the towns in Samaria don’t know the Law of the god of the land. He has sent lions among them to kill them.” </span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Then the king commanded, <i>“Bring one of the priests you exiled, and have him live there and teach them the Law of the god of the land.”</i> <b>                                                                                                                                        </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 17:16-27</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>One of the priests they’d exiled came and lived at Bethel and taught them how to respect Yahveh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>But every nation in the towns where they lived still made gods of their own and put them at the pagan shrines the people of Samaria had made. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The people from Babylon made Succoth-benoth, the people from Cuth made Nergal, the people from Hamath made Ashima, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>the Avvites made Nibhaz and Tartak, the people from Sepharvaim burned their children in the fire to Adrammelech and Anammelech their gods. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They also feared Yahveh and appointed priests for the high places, who acted for them in the shrines there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>They feared Yahveh <u>and</u> served their own gods like they did where they came from.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>      2 Kg 17:18-33</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>They’ve kept the earlier customs ever since. They don’t respect Yahveh or follow his statutes, ordinances, Law, or commandments to Jacob’s descendants, that he named Israel </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>and made a covenant with, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Don’t fear other gods, bow to them, serve them, sacrifice to them. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Fear Yahveh that brought you out of Egypt with great power and an outstretched arm. Bow to him and sacrifice to him. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Observe forever the statutes, ordinances, Law, and commandment he wrote for you. [</span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>] </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Fear Yahveh your God, and he’ll deliver you from your enemies.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>  </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They didn’t listen; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>while they feared Yahveh, they also served their idols. Their children and grandchildren still do that.</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>        2 Kg 17:34-41</span></b></span></p>
