## 2 Kings Chapter 5


### Naaman’s Leprosy Healed


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Naaman, commander of Aram’s army, was a great man with his master and highly respected, because by him Yahveh had given victory to Aram. He was a mighty soldier, but he was a leper. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The Arameans had gone out in bands and taken captive a little girl from Israel, who became a maid for Naaman’s wife. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She told her mistress<i>, “I wish my master would go see the prophet that’s in Samaria. He’d cure his leprosy.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Naaman went in and told his master what she’d said. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The king said, <i>“Go ahead. I’ll send a letter to the king of Israel.”</i>
</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            He left with <a name="_Hlk145079086">755 lbs <sup>10 talents</sup> </a>of silver, <a name="_Hlk145079219">150 lbs <sup>6,000 shekels</sup> of gold</a>, and 10 changes of clothes. </span><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He took the letter to king <i>Ahab</i> and said, <i>“Along with this letter, I’ve sent my servant Naaman so you can cure his leprosy.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When he read it, he tore his clothes,
</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Am I God, to kill and make alive, that this man is sending me a message to cure a man from leprosy? He’s trying to pick a fight.”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>
</span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 5:1-7</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>When Elisha heard the king had torn his clothes, he sent him a message, <i>“Why tear your clothes? Send him to me. He’ll know there’s a prophet in Israel.”</i>
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Naaman went with his horses and chariots, and stood at Elisha’s door. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Elisha sent his messenger to him, <i>“Go dip yourself seven times in the Jordan </i>River<i>. Your flesh will be restored, and you’ll be clean.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Naaman was furious and left, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“I thought surely he’d come out and call on Yahveh his God, and wave his hand over the place to cure a leper. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Aren’t Abanah and Pharpar, rivers of Damascus, better than any river in Israel? Couldn’t I have dipped myself in them to be clean?”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:.25in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He turned around and left in a rage. </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>His officers came up to him, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Father, if the prophet had told you to do something difficult, wouldn’t you have done it? How much more when he </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>just <i>says to dip yourself to be clean?”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He went down and dipped himself seven times in the Jordan. His flesh became like a little child’s, and he was clean. <b>  </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 5:8-14</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He went back to the man of God with his company, stood in front of him, and said, <i>“Now I know there’s no God except in Israel. Please accept a present from me.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>But <i>Elisha</i> swore, <i>“As sure as Yahveh lives that I serve, I won’t take a thing.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He urged him to take it, but he refused. </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Naaman said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“If not, please let me at least have two mules’ loads of dirt, because I won’t offer burnt offering or sacrifice to any god but Yahveh. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>May Yahveh pardon me when my master goes into Rimmon’s temple to worship and leans on my hand and I bow there too.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He told him, <i>“Have a good day.”</i></span></span></p>


### He went away to go home.
2 Kg 5:15-19


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Gehazi thought, <i>“My master has spared this Naaman the Aramean by not accepting what he brought. As sure as Yahveh lives, I’ll run and accept something from him.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>When Naaman saw somebody running after him, he got down out of the chariot to meet him, <i>“Is everything all right?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Everything’s fine. My master sent me to say that two young men from the school of the prophets just came from the hill country of Ephraim. Please give them <a name="_Hlk145079400">75½ lbs <sup>a talent</sup> of silver </a>and two changes of clothes.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Naaman said, <i>“Please take <a name="_Hlk145079354">151 lbs <u><sup>two</sup></u><sup> talents</sup></a>.”</i>
<b>                                                </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 5:20-23a</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>He urged him and put <a name="_Hlk145079541"><i>151 lbs <u><sup>two</sup></u><sup> talents</sup></i> of silver </a>in two bags with two changes of clothes, and gave them to two of his men to carry ahead of him. </span><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When Gehazi got to the hill, he took them and put them in the house, and they left. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>But when he went inside, Elisha said,
<i>“Where’ve you been?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>“I didn’t go anywhere.”</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Didn’t my spirit go with you when the man turned from his chariot to meet you? Is it a time to receive money and clothes, olive orchards and vineyards, sheep and oxen, male and female servants? </span></i><a name="_Hlk138276028"></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> </a><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>You and your descendants will permanently have to cope with leprosy like Naaman’s</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.”
</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:.25in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He went away as a leper as white as snow.<b>                                                                    </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 5:23b-27</span></b></p>
