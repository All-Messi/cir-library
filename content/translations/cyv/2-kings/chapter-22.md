## 2 Kings Chapter 22


### The Book of the Law Found


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Josiah</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'> was 8 when he became king and ruled for 31 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Jedidah Bat-Adaiah from Bozkath. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He did what was good and followed the ways of David. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>In the <b>eighteenth year of King Josiah</b>, he sent Shaphan Ben-Azaliah Ben-Meshullam, the secretary, to Yahveh’s Temple, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Go up to Hilkiah the high priest so he can count the money the people have brought to Yahveh’s Temple that the doorkeepers have gathered. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>Have them deliver it to the workmen that oversee the Temple so they can repair it—</span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>carpenters, builders, and masons—and buy the timber and cut stone for repairs. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They won’t need to account for the money they receive. They’re honest.” </span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>                                                                                                                                                </span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:.5in;'><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>                                                                                                                                                            </span></sup></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 22:1-7</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Then Hilkiah the priest told Shaphan the secretary<i>, “I’ve found the Book of the Law in the Temple.” </i>He gave it to him to read, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>and Shaphan brought word to the king,
<i>“Your officials have emptied out the money they found in the Temple and delivered it to the workmen that oversee the Temple.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Hilkiah has also given me a scroll.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> He read it to the king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When he heard it, he tore his clothes. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He told Hilkiah, Ahikam Ben-Shaphan, Achbor Ben-Micaiah, Shaphan the secretary, and Asaiah the king’s adviser,  </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Go ask Yahveh for me, for the people, and for Judah about what this scroll says. Yahveh’s great anger is burning against us. Our ancestors ignored what it says and haven’t done what’s written for us to do.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>They went to Huldah the prophetess, wife of Shallum Ben-Tikvah Ben-Harhas, keeper of the wardrobe. (</span>She lived in Jerusalem’s Second Quarter.<span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>) </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>She told them, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Yahveh God of Israel says to tell the man that sent you, </span></i><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>‘I’m going to bring disaster on this place and its people like the scroll says. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>They’ve left me and burned incense to other gods. My anger burns against this place, and it won’t be put out. [</span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>]</span></i><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>But I’ve listened to you because you became sensitive and humbled yourself when you heard about the desolation I’ve cursed this place and its people with; you tore your clothes and cried.  </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>I’ll let you pass on to your ancestors in peace. You won’t see the disaster I’m going to bring here.”’”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>  </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.25in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They took word back to the king.<b>                                                                                     </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 22:8-20</span></b></p>
