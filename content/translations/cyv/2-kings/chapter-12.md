## 2 Kings Chapter 12


### Joash Repairs the Temple


<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>In the <b>seventh year of Jehu</b>, <b>Jehoash</b> became king, and ruled for 40 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Zibiah of Beersheba. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>His whole life he did what was good like Jehoiada instructed him, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>except he didn’t take away the high places. The people still sacrificed and burned incense on them.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-bottom: 0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4-5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Jehoash told the priests, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in;margin-bottom:0in; margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“Let the priests take the money for sacred things that’s brought into Yahveh’s Temple and make any repairs on the Temple—money collected from the census, payments for vows, and voluntary offerings.”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><a name="_Hlk137207722"><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>By his <b>twenty-third year</b>, the priests <i>still </i>hadn’t made the repairs. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He said, <i>“Why aren’t you repairing them? Don’t take any more money from your acquaintances. Hand it over for the damages to the Temple.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The priests agreed not to take any more money and to let others do the repairs.<b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 12:1-8</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>But Jehoiada took a chest, bored a hole in the lid, and put it on the right side the altar, where people come into Yahveh’s Temple. The priests that guarded the threshold put the money in it that people brought in. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When the chest was getting full, the king’s secretary and the high priest counted it and tied it up in bags. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11-12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They weighed out the money to the ones that oversaw the Temple. They paid it to the carpenters, builders, masons, and stonecutters, <i>and</i> bought the lumber, hewn stone, and any <i>other </i>costs for the repairs. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>But they didn’t make silver cups, snuffers, bowls, bugles, any gold or silver items from the money. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>They gave it to the repairmen </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>and didn’t require any accounting from them because they were honest. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>Money from guilt offerings and sin offerings didn’t come into the Temple because it belonged to the priests.<b>                           </b></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>                                                                                                                              </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 12:9-16</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>At that time, Hazael, king of Aram, went and conquered Gath, then turned toward Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Jehoash/<i>Joash</i> took the sacred things that Jehoshaphat, Jehoram, and Ahaziah had dedicated, along with his own sacred things and the gold in the treasuries in Yahveh’s Temple and the king’s palace, and sent it to Hazael. So he let Jerusalem alone.
</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-bottom:0in;margin-bottom:0in;margin-top:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%'>The rest of the acts of Joash/<i>Jehoash</i> are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%'>His officers led a conspiracy, and Jozacar Ben-Shimeath with Jehozabad Ben-Shomer assassinated him at Beth-millo on the way down to Silla. They buried him with his ancestors in The City of David. His son <b>Amaziah</b> became king.                                                                     </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Kg 12:17-21</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>  </span></span></p>
