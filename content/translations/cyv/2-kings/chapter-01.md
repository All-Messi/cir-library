## 2 Kings Chapter **1**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>After Ahab died, Moab revolted against Israel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup><u>Ahaziah</u> fell through the lattice in his upper room in Samaria and seriously injured himself. He told messengers to go ask Baal-zebub, Ekron’s god, if he’d recover<em>. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong>But the angel of Yahveh told Elijah the Tishbite, <em>“Go meet the king’s messengers and say, ‘Are you asking Baal-zebub because there’s no God in Israel?</em>’ <strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>So</em><em> Yahveh says you won’t get out of bed. You’ll die.” </em> Then Elijah left.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>When the messengers came back, he said, <em>“Why’d you come back?”</em> <strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>“A</em><em> man came up to us and said to go back and tell you that Yahveh says, ‘Are you asking Baal-zebub because there’s no God in Israel? You won’t get out of bed.’”</em> <strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>“What</em><em> did he look like?”</em> <strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>“He</em><em> was wearing a leather belt and clothes </em><em>made out of</em><em> hair.” </em> <em>“That was Elijah the Tishbite.” </em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">2 Kg 1:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>The king sent him a captain with</span>
<p class="section-ref">his 50</p>

### men to arrest him. He went up to Elijah, who was sitting on a hill, “Man of God, the king says to come down!” 10“If I’m a ‘man of God,’ fire from the sky will consume you and your 50 men.” That’s what happened.


<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>He sent another captain with</span>
<p class="section-ref">his 50</p>

### men. “Man of God, the king says to come down now!”  12“If I’m a ‘man of God,’ fire from the sky will consume you and your 50 men.” That’s what happened.


<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>He sent a third captain with</span>
<p class="section-ref">his 50</p>

### men. When the captain came up, he knelt to Elijah, begging him, “Man of God, please value my life and the lives of these 50 servants of yours. 14Fire from the sky consumed the first two captains with their men. Regard my life as worth something to you.”


<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>The angel of Yahveh told Elijah, <em>“Go with him; don’t be afraid of him.” </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup><em>Elijah</em> told <em>Ahaziah</em>, <em>“Yahveh says, ‘Is there no God in Israel? Since you’ve asked Baal-zebub, you won’t get out of bed.’”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>He died, and <u>Jehoram</u> became king in the <strong>second year of Jehoram</strong> Ben-Jehoshaphat, king of Judah, because <em>Ahaziah</em> didn’t have a son. <strong><sup>18</sup></strong>The rest of his acts are recorded in <em>The Chronicles of the Kings of Israel</em>.<strong>	 </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">2 Kg 1:9-18</p>
