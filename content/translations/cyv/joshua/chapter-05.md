## Joshua Chapter 5


### Circumcision at Gilgal


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span></i><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>5<sup>:</sup></span></b><b><sup><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1</span></sup></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Amorite kings west of the Jordan and the Canaanite kings by the sea, heard that Yahveh had dried up the Jordan for the Israelites to cross. Their hearts melted; there was no spirit in them.</span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At that time, Yahveh told Joshua to make flint knives and circumcise the Israelites this second time<i>. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did that at Gibeath-haaraloth. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The fighting men who came out of Egypt </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>had been circumcised, but the ones born in the desert hadn’t. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Israelites lived there 40 years till those fighting men died. Yahveh swore they wouldn’t see the land flowing with milk and honey that he’d promised their ancestors. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So Joshua circumcised the offspring Yahveh raised up to replace them. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>After the circumcising, they stayed in the camp till they healed up. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh told Joshua, <i>“I’ve ‘rolled away’ from you the disgrace of Egypt today”</i>;<i>
</i>so the place has been called Gilgal ever since.                                                                                 </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>While the people camped in Gilgal, they kept Passover on the fourteenth of the month in the evening in the plains by Jericho. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day, they began eating the produce in the land—parched grain and cakes without yeast in them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The manna stopped the next day; they didn’t have it anymore. They ate the produce in Canaan that year.                                                      </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Josh 5:1-12</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Joshua was near Jericho, he looked up and saw a man with his sword drawn. He went to him and asked, <i>“Are you for us or our enemies?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“I’ve come to command Yahveh’s army.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joshua fell on his face in homage, <i>“What do you want me to do?” </i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Take your shoes off. Where you’re standing is holy.” </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joshua obeyed.</span></span></p>
