## Joshua Chapter 17

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Manasseh</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, Joseph’s firstborn, received an allotment. Gilead and Bashan went to Machir, Manasseh’s firstborn and father of Gilead, because he was a soldier. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Allotment came to the rest of Manasseh’s male descendants by clans: the sons of Abiezer, Helek, Asriel, Shechem, Hepher, and <u>Shemida</u>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>However, Zelophehad Ben-Hepher (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ben-Gilead Ben-Machir Ben-Manasseh</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) only had daughters: Mahlah, Noah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Tirzah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They approached Eleazar the priest, Joshua, and the elders, <i>“Yahveh</i> <i>told Moses to give us an inheritance among our relatives.” </i>So he gave them an inheritance among their uncles. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ten sections fell to Manasseh besides Gilead and Bashan beyond the Jordan, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because Manasseh’s daughters received an inheritance with his sons. Gilead belonged to the rest of Manasseh’s sons.</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Josh 17:1-6</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Manasseh’s territory reached from Asher to Michmethath, east of Shechem. Then the boundary went south to En-tappuah. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The land of Tappuah belonged to Manasseh, but the town of Tappuah on the boundary of Manasseh belonged to Ephraim. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then the boundary went down to Brook Kanah. The towns there, south of the stream, among the towns in Manasseh, belonged to Ephraim. Then Manasseh’s boundary went on the north side of the stream and ended at the Mediterranean Sea. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The land to the south was Ephraim’s, that to the north was Manasseh’s, and the Mediterranean Sea formed its <i>western </i>boundary. The north reached Asher; the east reached Issachar. </span><a name="_Hlk146176520"></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Also in Issachar and Asher, Manasseh had Beth-shean/<i>Beth-shan</i>, Ibleam, Dor, En-dor, Taanach, and Megiddo, each with their surrounding settlements. (The third was Naphath.) </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yet Manasseh’s descendants couldn’t conquer those towns, so the Canaanites kept living there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Israel grew stronger, they put them to forced labor, but didn’t completely drive them out.
</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Josh 17:7-13</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The tribe of Joseph asked Joshua, <i>“Why have you given us only one territory though we’re numerous because Yahveh</i> <i>has blessed us?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“If the hill country of Ephraim is not big enough, go up into the wooded land and clear ground in the territory of the Perizzites and Rephaim.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“The hill country’s still not big enough. The Canaanites that still live in the lowlands have iron chariots, those in Beth-shean/</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Beth-shan<i> and its settlements and those in the Valley of Jezreel.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Joshua told the House of Joseph, Ephraim and Manasseh, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You’re numerous and strong. You won’t just have one lot </span></i><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>but the hill country too. Though it’s wooded land, clear it and take possession of it to its full extent. You’ll drive out the Canaanites too though they’re strong and have iron chariots.”             </span></i></span></span></p>
