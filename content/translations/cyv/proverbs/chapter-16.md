## Proverbs Chapter 16

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> We have intentions, but Yahveh provides the proper course of action.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Our ways may seem pure to us, but Yahveh weighs our motives.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Commit your work to Yahveh, and you'll succeed.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Yahveh has a purpose for everything, even for sinners in troubled times.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Yahveh detests arrogant people and makes sure they're punished.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Loyalty and faithfulness atone for sin; respecting Yahveh avoids evil.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> When we please Yahveh, he even makes our enemies live in peace with us.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> A little with goodness is better than a lot with dishonesty.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> You can make your plans, but Yahveh directs the steps you'll take.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> A king needs to make inspired decisions; he can't afford to err in his judgments.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Yahveh requires accurate scales and balances; he sets the standard for every weight in the bag.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> An evil king is especially detestable; a throne needs to be based on goodness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Godly words delight a king; he loves people that tell the truth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> A king's anger is a death sentence; the wise will appease it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> There's life in a king's countenance; his blessing is like clouds that bring spring rain.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Getting wisdom is better than getting gold! Choose insight above silver.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> The road of the upright leads away from evil; those who watch where they're going preserve their life.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Pride goes before destruction, and an arrogant spirit before a fall.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Better to nurture a humble spirit than share plunder with the proud.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> By attending to Yahveh's word, we find what's good; he blesses us who trust in him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> The wise in heart we consider insightful; their pleasant words seem more persuasive.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> Wisdom is a fountain of life to the wise, but foolishness disciplines fools.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> The wise think carefully about what they say, which adds power to their comments.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> Pleasant words are like honeycomb, sweet to the soul and healthy for the body.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> There's a way that seems right, but it leads to death.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> The workers' appetite works for them because their hunger drives them on.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Worthless people plot evil; what they say is like a scorching fire.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Perverse persons spread conflict; slanderers alienate friends.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Violent people tempt their friends and lead them off in the wrong direction.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> Those who wink devise perverse things; they who purse their lips bring about evil.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> A gray head is a crown of honor gained by a good life.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> Those who are slow to lose their temper are stronger than the powerful; the ones that rule their spirit are better than troops that capture a city.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> We cast lots in the lap, but Yahveh determines how they fall.</span>
