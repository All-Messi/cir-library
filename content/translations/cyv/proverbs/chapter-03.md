## Proverbs Chapter **3**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Son, don’t forget what I’ve taught you; take my commandments to heart;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>they’ll add peace and length to your life.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Don’t let kindness and truth slip away from you; tie them around your neck; write them on your heart.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>That way you’ll find blessing and have a good reputation with God and people.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>Trust in Yahveh with all your heart; don’t rely on your own perception.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup>Acknowledge him in everything you do, and he’ll make straight paths for you to follow.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>Don’t get the idea that you’re smart; respect the LORD and turn your back on sin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>It will heal your body and refresh your very bones.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 3:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>Honor Yahveh with your substance, the firstfruits of your crops.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>Your barns will be full; your vats will overflow with new wine.<strong> </strong><strong>	</strong><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 3:9-10</p>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>Son, don’t reject Yahveh’s discipline or give up when he corrects you;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup>he<sup>Y</sup><sup> </sup>corrects the ones he cares about, like a father that <em>disciplines</em> a son he delights in.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>They are blessed who find wisdom, that gain insight.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup>It’s more profitable than silver; it brings greater gains than fine gold.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>It’s more precious than jewels; nothing you desire can compare with it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup>Its left hand holds wealth and honor; long life is in its right hand.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><strong><sup>7</sup></strong>Its ways are pleasant; its paths are peaceful.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup>It’s a life-giving tree for people that pick its fruit; everybody that holds onto it is happy.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 3:11-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>Yahveh founded the earth with wisdom and established the heavens with understanding.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup>He broke up the depths with his knowledge, and caused dew to settle from the sky.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 3:19-20</p>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>Son, keep sound wisdom and discretion nearby; don’t let them out of your sight.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup>They’ll refresh your soul and adorn your neck.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>Then you’ll walk along your road surefooted without stumbling.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup>When you lie down, you won’t feel afraid; when you go to bed, your sleep will be sweet.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup>Don’t be afraid of sudden panic or the ruin that sinful people may bring;</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>because you’ll have confidence with Yahveh; he’ll keep your foot out of the snare.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 3:21-26</p>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup>Don’t withhold good from people that need it when you can give it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup><strong>28</strong></sup>Don’t tell them, <em>	“Come back tomorrow, and you can have it.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup><strong>29</strong></sup>Don’t devise misfortune against the one that’s living safely beside you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup><strong>30</strong></sup>Don’t be difficult with people if they’ve done you no wrong.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><strong><sup>31</sup></strong>Don’t envy the violent or choose to do what they do.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup><strong>32</strong></sup>Yahveh detests crooked people, but he’s close to godly ones.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><strong><sup>33</sup></strong>He<sup>Y</sup> puts a curse on the house of the sinful, but blesses the homes of the righteous.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v34"><sup><strong>34</strong></sup>He scoffs at scoffers, but is gracious to the humble.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v35"><strong><sup>35</sup></strong>The wise inherit honor; the foolish get disgrace.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 3:27-35</p>
