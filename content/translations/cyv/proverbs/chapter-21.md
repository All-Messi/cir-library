## Proverbs Chapter 21

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> A king’s heart in Yahveh’s hand is like a stream he can direct where he wants.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Your own way seems right to you, but Yahveh weighs your heart.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Yahveh wants goodness and justice more than sacrifice.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Arrogant eyes and a proud heart are wicked people’s lamp—and sin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Diligent planning gives you an advantage; getting in a hurry gets you poverty.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Getting rich by lying is a dissipating vapor that ends in death.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 21:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Sinful people’s violence sweeps them away because they refuse to do what’s right.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> A guilty person’s road is full of curves, but the conduct of the pure makes a straight path.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> It’s better to live in the corner of a housetop than inside the house with a contentious woman.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Evil people desire evil and don’t show compassion to anyone.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Punishing scoffers makes them wise; instructing the wise makes them wiser still.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Good people watch out for the sinful and turn them to ruin.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 21:7-12</p>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> If you shut out the cry from the poor, you’ll cry out yourself and not be heard.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> A secret gift calms anger; a present turns it away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Good people enjoy carrying out justice; justice distresses sinners.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> If you stray from understanding, you’ll end up among the dead.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> People that love pleasure become poor; they who love wine and comfort never get rich.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Sinners are ransoms for the righteous; the unfaithful replace the good.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 21:13-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> It’s better to live in the desert than with a contentious, “complainy” woman.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> Precious treasure and oil are in the houses of the wise, but foolish people waste them.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> Pursue goodness and loyalty, and you’ll arrive at life, goodness, and honor.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> The wise scale the walls of the powerful and bring down the fortress they trust in.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> Watch your mouth and guard your tongue, and you’ll stay out of trouble.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> “Scoffers” is the name for proud, haughty people that conduct themselves with arrogance.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 21:19-24</p>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> Lazy people’s desires <em>end up </em>killing them because they refuse to work.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> The greedy never get enough while the godly give liberally.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>God</em><em> </em>despises sinners’ sacrifices, especially when they bring them with selfish motives.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> False witnesses perish, but good listeners testify forever.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Sinners bluff, but the godly think before they act.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> No wisdom, insight, or counsel can prevail against Yahveh.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> A horse is trained for battle, but victory comes from Yahveh.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 21:25-31</p>
