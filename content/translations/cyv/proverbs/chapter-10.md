## Proverbs Chapter 10

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Proverbs of Solomon A wise son makes a happy father, but a foolish one grieves his mother.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Ill-gotten gain doesn't gain you anything, but goodness delivers you from death.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Yahveh won't let good people go hungry; he thwarts what the wicked want.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> A lazy hand makes for poverty; a diligent one produces wealth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> They who gather crops in the summer are smart; those who sleep at harvest time are shameful.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Blessings come to the good, but the sinful harbor violent intentions.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Good people leave blessed memories behind, but sinful people's names rot away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> The wise in heart accept commands, but babbling fools come to ruin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> If you live with integrity, you'll live with confidence, but people soon find out if you're crooked.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The one that winks causes trouble; babbling fools come to ruin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Good people's words are life-giving fountains; What sinners harbor discord.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Hatred stirs up conflict, but love atones for all sins.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Wisdom comes from people that have insight; but a rod is for the back of fools.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> The wise store up knowledge, but ruin is at hand for foolish people.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> For the rich, wealth is a fortress; For the poor, poverty is their ruin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> Life is good people's pay; punishment is bad people's payback.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> Listening to instruction puts you on the route to life; abandoning correction gets you lost.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Hiding hatred makes you a liar; spreading slander makes you a fool.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> Talking too much makes you likely to sin; so be wise and limit what you say.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> The words of good people are like fine silver; what sinners say isn't worth repeating.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> What good people say feeds many; foolish people starve to death from ignorance.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> Yahveh's blessing makes you rich, and he doesn't add sorrow to it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> Sin is fun for fools; so is wisdom for the wise.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> What sinners fear tends happen; what good people want they tend to receive.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> After a windstorm passes, sinners have been blown away, but the godly have a firm foundation.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> To those who hire them, lazy people irritate like vinegar on teeth and smoke in the eyes.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> Respecting Yahveh lengthens your life, sinfulness cuts it short.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> Good people's hopes result in happiness, but the expectations of the sinful fade away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> Yahveh's way is your fortress if you're godly, but it destroys sinners.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> Good people won't be displaced, but the sinful will be expelled from their land.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> The mouth of good people overflows with wisdom, but the perverse tongue of the wicked will be cut off.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> The good say what's acceptable, but the sinful say perverse things.</span>
