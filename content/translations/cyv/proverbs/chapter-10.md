## Proverbs Chapter **10**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Proverbs of Solomon A wise son makes a happy father, but a foolish one grieves his mother.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Ill-gotten gain doesn’t gain you anything, but goodness delivers <em>you </em>from death.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup>Yahveh won’t let good people go hungry; he thwarts what the wicked want.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>4</sup></strong>A lazy hand makes for poverty; a diligent one produces wealth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup>They who gather crops in the summer are smart; those who sleep at harvest time are shameful.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>6</sup></strong>Blessings come to the good, but the sinful harbor violent intentions.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 10:1-6</p>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup>Good people leave blessed memories behind, but sinful people’s names rot away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><strong><sup>	</sup></strong><strong><sup>8</sup></strong>The wise in heart accept commands, but babbling fools come to ruin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup>If you live with integrity, you’ll live with confidence, but people soon find out if you’re crooked.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><strong><sup>10</sup></strong>The one that winks causes trouble; babbling fools come to ruin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup>Good people’s words are life-giving fountains; What sinners harbor discord.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><strong><sup>12</sup></strong>Hatred stirs up conflict, but love atones for all sins.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>Wisdom comes from people that have insight; but a rod is for the back of fools.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><strong><sup>14</sup></strong>The wise store up knowledge, but ruin is at hand for foolish people.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 10:7-14</p>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup>For the rich, wealth is a fortress; For the poor, poverty is their ruin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><strong><sup>16</sup></strong>Life is good people’s pay; punishment is bad people’s payback.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup>Listening to instruction puts you on the route to life; abandoning correction gets you lost.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><strong><sup>18</sup></strong>Hiding hatred makes you a liar; spreading slander makes you a fool.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup>Talking too much makes you likely to sin; <em>so </em>be wise and limit what you say.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><strong><sup>20</sup></strong>The words of good people are like fine silver; what sinners say isn’t worth repeating.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 10:15-20</p>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup>What good people say feeds many; foolish people <em>starve to</em> death from ignorance.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><strong><sup>22</sup></strong>Yahveh’s blessing makes you rich, and he doesn’t add sorrow to it.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>Sin is fun for fools; so is wisdom for the wise.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><strong><sup>24</sup></strong>What sinners fear tends happen; what good people want they tend to receive.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup>After a windstorm passes, sinners have been blown away, but the godly have a firm foundation.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><strong><sup>26</sup></strong>To those who hire them, lazy people irritate like vinegar on teeth and smoke in the eyes.<strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 10:21-26</p>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup>Respecting Yahveh lengthens your life, sinfulness cuts it short.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><strong><sup>28</sup></strong>Good people’s hopes result in happiness, but the expectations of the sinful fade away.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup><strong>29</strong></sup>Yahveh’s way is your fortress if you’re godly, but it destroys sinners.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><strong><sup>30</sup></strong>Good people won’t be displaced, but the sinful will be expelled from their land.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup><strong>31</strong></sup>The mouth of good people overflows with wisdom, but the perverse tongue of the wicked will be cut off.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><strong><sup>32</sup></strong>The good say what’s acceptable, but the sinful <em>say</em> perverse things.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Prov 10:27-32</p>
