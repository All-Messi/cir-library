## Amos Chapter 1

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The Message of Amos, Shepherd from Tekoa, That He Saw About Israel at the Time of Uzziah, King of Judah, and Jeroboam Ben-Joash, King of Israel, Two Years Before the Earthquake</span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> He said, "Yahveh will roar from Zion; he'll thunder from Jerusalem. The shepherds' pastures will dry up; the grass on Carmel's summit will wither away." God's Judgment on Israel's Neighbors</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Yahveh says, "For three sins of Damascus and for four, I won't hold back its punishment:e said, He said they threshed the people of Gilead with sledges that had iron teeth.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> So I'll send down fire on the house of Hazael, consume the fortresses of Benhadad,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> break the bars on the gates of Damascus, eliminate the people in the Valley of Aven and the one that holds the scepter from Beth-eden. The people of Syria will go to Kir in exile."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Yahveh says, "For three sins of Gaza and for four, I won't hold back its punishment: they deported an entire population to Edom.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> So I'll send down fire on the walls of Gaza and consume its fortresses.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> I'll eliminate the people from Ashdod and the one that holds the scepter in Ashkelon. I'll unleash my power on Ekron, and the remnant of Philistines will perish."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Yahveh says, "For three sins of Tyre and for four, I won't hold back its punishment: they delivered an entire population to Edom and broke the treaty between kin.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> So I'll send down fire on the walls of Tyre and burn its fortresses."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Yahveh says, "For three sins of Edom and for four, I won't hold back its punishment: they pursued their brothers with a sword and tossed mercy aside. In their anger they slashed them again and again; they kept up their fury without end.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> So I'll send down fire on Teman and burn the fortresses in Bozrah."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Yahveh says, "For three sins of the Ammonites and for four, I won't hold back their punishment: they ripped open pregnant women in Gilead when they extended their borders.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> So I'll kindle a fire on the walls of Rabbah and burn its fortresses amid war cries like a storm.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> Their king will go into exile with his officials."</span>
