## Leviticus Chapter 23

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Yahveh told Moses to tell Israel, <em>“Yahveh has appointed these times: </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>six</em><em> days you can work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath for complete rest, a holy convocation. It’s a Sabbath to Yahveh wherever you live. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>“These</em><em> are his appointed times, holy convocations that you’ll declare at their appointed times. </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> fourteenth day, first month at twilight is Yahveh’s Passover. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Then</em><em> on the fifteenth, there’s a Festival of Unleavened Bread </em><em>to</em><em> him. Eat bread without yeast for a week. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> first day, hold a holy convocation; don’t work. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>Present</em><em> an offering by fire to Yahveh</em><em> </em><em>for a week. On the seventh day, hold a holy convocation; don’t work.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>“Count</em><em> seven Sabbaths from the day after the Sabbath when you brought the bundle for the wave offering. </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>Count</em><em> 50 days to the day after the seventh Sabbath. Then present a new grain offering to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>17</sup></strong><em>Take</em><em> in 2 </em>loaves<em> of bread for a wave offering, made from .22</em> bushel <sup>.2 ephah</sup><em>. They’ll consist of fine flour baked with yeast as the first of the harvest to Yahveh. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Along</em><em> with the bread, present </em><em>7 year-old</em><em> male lambs without defect, a bull from the herd, and 2 rams. They’ll be a burnt offering along with their grain offering and liquid offerings. </em>It’s <em>an offering by fire of an aroma pleasing to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>Also</em><em> offer 1 male goat for a sin offering and 2 yearling male lambs for a peace offering. </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>The priest will wave them with the bread from the first part of the harvest for a wave offering with 2 lambs in front of Yahveh. They’re holy to Yahveh for the priest. </em><strong><sup>21</sup></strong><em>On</em><em> that same day, make a proclamation. Hold a holy convocation. Don’t work. It’s an ongoing permanent statute wherever you live. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>“When</em><em> you harvest, don’t harvest the corners of your field or gather the gleanings from your harvest. Leave them for the poor and foreigners. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:15-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Yahveh told Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <em>“</em><em>These</em><em> are </em><em>Yahveh’s scheduled</em><em> </em><em>holidays</em><em> </em><em>that you’re to declare as holy convocations, to present burnt offerings to Yahveh, grain offerings, sacrifices, and liquid offerings, </em>each<em> day’s matter as prescribed. </em><strong><sup>38</sup></strong><em>They’re</em><em> in addition to </em>the ones on<em> Yahveh’s Sabbaths.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:33-38</p>

<span class="verse" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <em>“The</em><em> fifteenth day, seventh month, when you’ve gathered your crops, celebrate Yahveh’s festival for a week. Rest on the first and eighth days. </em><strong><sup>40</sup></strong><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>41</sup></strong><em>On</em><em> the first day, take the foliage from beautiful trees, palm branches and boughs of leafy trees and willows by the stream. Every year celebrate a festival to Yahveh your God for a week. It’s an ongoing permanent statute for the seventh month. </em><strong><sup>42</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> native-born in Israel will live in booths for a week </em><strong><sup>43</sup></strong><em>to</em><em> remind your generations that I had the Israelites live in booths when I brought them out of Egypt. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> Moses declared to Israel Yahveh’s specified holidays.<em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 23:39-44</p>
