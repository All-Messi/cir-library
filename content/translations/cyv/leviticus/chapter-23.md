## Leviticus Chapter 23

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Yahveh told Moses to tell Israel, "Yahveh has appointed these times: 3six days you can work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath for complete rest, a holy convocation. Eat bread without yeast for a week. 7The first day, hold a holy convocation; don't work. 8Present an offering by fire to Yahveh for a week. On the seventh day, hold a holy convocation; don't work." 9-10Yahveh told Moses to say to Israel, "When you harvest the land I'm giving you, take a bundle of the first part of the harvest to the priest. 11The day after Sabbath he'll wave it back and forth in front of Yahveh for you to be accepted. 12When you wave it, offer a male lamb a year old without defect for a burnt offering. 13Its grain offering will be .22 bushel .2 ephah of fine flour mixed with olive oil, an offering by fire to Yahveh for a pleasing aroma, with its liquid offering, 3pts Â¼ hin of wine. 14Before you take the offering to your God, don't eat bread or roasted grain from the new growth. It's an ongoing permanent statute wherever you live. 15"Count seven Sabbaths from the day after the Sabbath when you brought the bundle for the wave offering. 16Count 50 days to the day after the seventh Sabbath. Then present a new grain offering to Yahveh. 17Take in 2 loaves of bread for a wave offering, made from .22 bushel .2 ephah. They'll consist of fine flour baked with yeast as the first of the harvest to Yahveh.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> Along with the bread, present 7 year-old male lambs without defect, a bull from the herd, and 2 rams. It's an offering by fire of an aroma pleasing to Yahveh. 19Also offer 1 male goat for a sin offering and 2 yearling male lambs for a peace offering. 20The priest will wave them with the bread from the first part of the harvest for a wave offering with 2 lambs in front of Yahveh. They're holy to Yahveh for the priest. 21On that same day, make a proclamation. Hold a holy convocation. Don't work. It's an ongoing permanent statute wherever you live. 22"When you harvest, don't harvest the corners of your field or gather the gleanings from your harvest. Leave them for the poor and foreigners. I'm Yahveh your God." 23-24Yahveh told Moses to tell Israel, "The first day, seventh month, hold a complete rest, a reminder by blowing bugles, a holy convocation. 25Don't work but present an offering by fire to Yahveh."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Yahveh told Moses, 27-28"The tenth day, seventh month, is The Day of Atonement, a holy convocation. Rest on the first and eighth days. 40-41On the first day, take the foliage from beautiful trees, palm branches and boughs of leafy trees and willows by the stream. Every year celebrate a festival to Yahveh your God for a week.
