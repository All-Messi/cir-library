## Leviticus Chapter 23

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Yahveh told Moses to tell Israel, <em>“Yahveh has appointed these times: </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>six</em><em> days you can work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath for complete rest, a holy convocation. It’s a Sabbath to Yahveh wherever you live. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Along</em><em> with the bread, present </em><em>7 year-old</em><em> male lambs without defect, a bull from the herd, and 2 rams. They’ll be a burnt offering along with their grain offering and liquid offerings. </em>It’s <em>an offering by fire of an aroma pleasing to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>19</sup></strong><em>Also</em><em> offer 1 male goat for a sin offering and 2 yearling male lambs for a peace offering. </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>The priest will wave them with the bread from the first part of the harvest for a wave offering with 2 lambs in front of Yahveh. They’re holy to Yahveh for the priest. </em><strong><sup>21</sup></strong><em>On</em><em> that same day, make a proclamation. Hold a holy convocation. Don’t work. It’s an ongoing permanent statute wherever you live. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Yahveh told Moses, <strong><sup>27</sup></strong><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>28</sup></strong><em>“T</em><em>he</em><em> tenth day</em><em>, seventh month, is The Day of A</em><em>tonement</em><em>, </em><em>a holy convocation.</em><em> Fast and</em><em> </em><em>don’t work; </em><em>present an offering by fire to </em><em>Yahveh to atone for yourselves to Yahveh your God. </em><strong><sup>29</sup></strong><em>Any</em><em> of you that won’t fast </em><strong><sup>30</sup></strong><em>and</em><em> refrain from working that day, I’ll destroy from among the people. </em><strong><sup>31</sup></strong><em>It’s</em><em> a permanent statute wherever you live. </em><strong><sup>32</sup></strong><em>On</em><em> the ninth of the month at evening, rest till the next evening.”</em><em>	</em><strong>Lev 23:26-32</strong> <strong><sup>33</sup></strong><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>34</sup></strong>Again Yahveh told Moses to tell Israel, <em>“The fifteenth day, seventh month is the Festival of Booths to Yahveh</em><em> </em><em>for a week. </em><strong><sup>35</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> first day is a holy convocation. Don’t do any kind of work. </em><strong><sup>36</sup></strong><em>For</em><em> a week, present an offering by fire to Yahveh. On the eighth day, hold a holy convocation and present an offering by fire to Yahveh. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> Moses declared to Israel Yahveh’s specified holidays.<em> </em><em>	</em><strong>Lev 23:39-44</strong></span>
