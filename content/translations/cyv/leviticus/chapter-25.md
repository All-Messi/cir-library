## Leviticus Chapter 25

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Yahveh told Moses at Mount Sinai to tell Israel, <em>“When you go into the land I’m giving you, the ground will have a rest to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>Plant</em><em> your fields, prune your vineyards, harvest your crops for six years. </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>But</em><em> the seventh year, the ground will rest to Yahveh. Don’t plant your fields or prune your vineyards. </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> store up volunteer growth or gather grapes from untrimmed vines. Let the ground lie feral for a year. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>You’ll</em><em> have for food what the ground grows on its own during its sabbath year—yourselves, your male and female slaves, hired hands, and foreigners living with you. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>Your</em><em> livestock and the wild animals on your land will have its produce to eat </em>directly<em>.</em><em>	 </em><strong>Lev 25:1-7</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v45"><sup class="verse-num">45</sup> <em>from</em><em> foreigners living among </em><em>you;</em><em> </em> <em>you can acquire possessions</em><em> </em><em>from the families they produce in your country; </em><strong><sup>46</sup></strong><em>you</em><em> </em><em>can will</em><em> them to your heirs and</em> <em>use them as permanent slaves. </em> <em>But with Israelites, don’t mistreat one another that way.</em><strong></span>
