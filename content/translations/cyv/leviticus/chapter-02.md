## Leviticus Chapter 2

### Grain Offerings

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“A</em><em> </em><strong><em>grain offering</em></strong><em> to Yahveh will consist of fine flour with olive oil and frankincense added. </em><strong><sup>2</sup></strong><em>T</em><em>ake</em><em> it to the sons of Aaron the priests. </em>The priest<em> will take a handful of the mix and offer it up in smoke on the altar as God’s portion, an aroma pleasing to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> rest is for Aaron and his sons, something most holy of the offerings to Yahveh by fire.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 2:1-3</p>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>“When</em><em> you bring a grain offering baked in an </em><em><u>oven</u></em><em>, it’ll be flat cakes of fine flour mixed with olive oil or wafers with no yeast in them, basted with olive oil. </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> you make it on a </em><em><u>griddle</u></em><em>, make it out of fine flour without yeast and </em><em>mixed</em><em> with olive oil. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Break</em><em> it up into pieces and pour the oil on it. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> you make your grain offering in a </em><em><u>pan</u></em><em>, make it out of fine flour mixed with olive oil. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>When</em><em> you bring a grain offering like that, present it to the priest at the altar. </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>He’ll</em><em> take out God’s portion and offer </em><em>it</em><em> up in smoke by fire, an aroma pleasing to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> rest is for Aaron and his sons, something most holy from the offerings to Yahveh by fire.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 2:4-10</p>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> </em><em>put yeast or honey in</em><em> grain offering</em><em>s. </em><strong><sup>12</sup></strong><em>For</em><em> an offering of the first part of the harvest</em><em>, bring </em><em>it</em><em> to </em><em>Yahveh</em><em>, but </em><em>it’s</em><em> </em><em>not </em><em>to </em><em>rise up</em><em> as</em><em> a </em><em>pleasing</em><em> aroma on the altar.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>13</sup></strong><em>Season</em><em> grain offering</em><em>s</em><em> with salt</em><em> to put</em><em> the salt of </em><em>your God’s</em><em> </em><em>covenant in</em><em> </em><em>them</em><em>.</em><em>  </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>“If</em><em> you bring a grain offering of the first part of the harvest, bring heads of crushed new growth roasted in fire, </em><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>and</em><em> put olive oil and incense on them. </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> priest will offer them up in smoke as God’s portion.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 2:11-16</p>
