## Leviticus Chapter 22

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Yahveh told Moses to tell Aaron and his sons, <em>“Handle the holy </em>gifts carefully<em> that the Israelites dedicate to </em><em>me</em><em> so you don’t profane my name. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> approach the holy </em>gifts<em> while you’re ceremonially unclean. I’ll remove you from me. </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> you’re a leper or have a discharge, you </em><em>can’t eat the holy </em>gifts<em> till you’re clean. Touching anything made ceremonially unclean by a corpse or seminal emission, </em><strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>or</em><em> touching any teeming thing that makes any person unclean, </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>makes</em><em> you unclean till evening. </em><em>You can’t eat the sacred </em>gifts <em>till</em><em> </em><em>you</em><em> take a bath.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>When</em><em> the sun sets, you’ll be clean and can eat them. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>You</em><em> can’t eat </em>an animal<em> that dies or is torn to pieces by wild animals; that makes you ceremonially unclean. </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>Keep</em><em> my charge so you won’t be considered defiled and die for profaning my name. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> that sanctifies them</u></em><em>.</em><strong> </strong><strong>Lev 22:1-9</strong><em>	 </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>“No</em><em> layman, visitor with a priest, or hired hand can eat the sacred gifts. </em><strong><sup>11</sup></strong><em>But</em><em> a slave that a priest buys with his own money can eat them; </em><em>so</em><em> can </em>slaves<em> born in his house. </em><strong><sup>12</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> a priest’s daughter marries a layman, she can’t continue to eat the </em><em>gifts</em><em>. </em><strong><sup>13</sup></strong><em>But</em><em> if she </em><em>becomes</em><em> a widow or is divorced, doesn’t have any children, and goes back to her father’s family, she can eat her father’s food. </em><strong><sup>14</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> any of you laymen unintentionally eat a sacred gift, add a fifth to it and give it to the priest. </em><strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>Laymen</em><em> are not to profane the sacred gift Israelites offer to Yahveh; </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>so</em><em> they’re fined for it</em><em>. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> that sanctifies them</u></em><em>.’”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 22:10-16</p>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> Yahveh told Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>“A</em><em> newborn ox, sheep, or goat will remain with its mother for a week. After that it’s acceptable as an offering by fire to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>28</sup></strong><em>But</em><em> don’t slaughter an ox or sheep and </em><em>its</em><em> young the same day. </em><strong><sup>29</sup></strong><em>When</em><em> you offer a sacrifice of thanksgiving to Yahveh, do it so he will accept you. </em><strong><sup>30</sup></strong><em>Eat</em><em> it the same day; don’t leave any till morning. </em><strong><sup>31</sup></strong><em>Keep</em><em> my commandments that way. </em><strong><sup>32</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> profane my </em><em>holy name; treat me as holy in Israel. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> that sanctifies you</u></em><em>, </em><strong><sup>33</sup></strong><em>who</em><em> brought you out of Egypt to be your God.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 22:26-33</p>
