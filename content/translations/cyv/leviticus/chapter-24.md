<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup>Yahveh said to Moses, <strong><sup>2</sup></strong><em>“Tell</em><em> the people to bring you clear oil from pressed olives that can keep a lamp burning continually. </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>Outside</em><em> the curtain of Testimony in the Tent of Meeting, Aaron will keep </em>the lamps on the lampstand<em> in order from evening to morning continually.</em> It’s<em> an ongoing permanent statute. </em> <strong><sup>5</sup></strong><em>“</em><em>Take</em><em> </em><em>fine flour and bake </em><em>12</em><em> cakes</em><em>. Put .22 bushels</em><em> </em><sup>.2</sup><sup> ephah</sup><em><sup> </sup></em><em>in each one. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Set</em><em> them in two stacks, six to a stack on the pure </em><em>gold</em><em> table. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>Put</em><em> pure </em><em>frankincense beside each stack for the bread as God’s portion, </em><em>an offering by fire to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>Every</em><em> Sabbath </em>Aaron <em>will set this bread in order in front of Yahveh, a permanent covenant for Israel. </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>It’ll</em><em> be for Aaron and his sons to eat in a sacred place. <span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup>The son of an Israelite woman and Egyptian father went out among the people and got into a fight with an Israelite man in the camp. <strong><sup>11</sup></strong>The woman’s son blasphemed the Name and cursed; so they brought him to Moses. (His mother was Shelomith, Bat-Dibri, tribe of Dan.) <strong><sup>12</sup></strong>They put him in custody till Yahveh made his commandment clear to Moses.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup>Yahveh told Moses, <strong><sup>14</sup></strong><em>“Take</em><em> him outside the camp</em><em>. <span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup>Moses told the people, and they took outside the camp the one that had cursed, and stoned him.</span>
