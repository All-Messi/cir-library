## Leviticus Chapter 19

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>-</sup></strong><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Yahveh told Moses to tell the whole community of Israel, <em>“</em><em>Be holy because I’m holy.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>3</sup></strong><em>Respect</em><em> your parents and keep my Sabbaths. </em><strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> turn to idols or make molten gods. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>“When</em><em> you offer a peace offering to Yahveh, offer it so he’ll accept it. </em><strong><sup>6</sup></strong><em>Eat</em><em> it the day you offer it and the next day. Burn what’s left over till the third day. </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> you eat it on the third </em><em>day,</em><em> Yahveh</em> <em>won’t accept it. </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>You’ll</em><em> be guilty of profaning what’s holy to him and be excluded from the community.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 19:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>“When</em><em> you harvest, don’t harvest the corners of your field or pick up the gleanings of your harvest. </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> glean your vineyard or pick up its fallen fruit. Leave it for the needy and foreigners. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.</em><em>	</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> steal, lie, or deal falsely with one another. </em><strong><sup>12</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> swear falsely by me and profane my name. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> oppress or rob anyone or keep a hired hand’s wages overnight. </em><strong><sup>14</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> curse the deaf or trip the blind. Respect your God. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em>.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 19:9-14</p>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>“Be</em><em> just when you judge. Don’t be partial to the poor or </em><em>defer</em><em> to someone with status. Judge fairly. </em><strong><sup>16</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> slander or act against anyone’s life. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>“You</em><em> can reprove but not </em><em>sin</em><em> against or hate a fellow citizen. </em><strong><sup>18</sup></strong><em>Don’t</em><em> take revenge or bear a grudge. Love your neighbor as yourself.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 19:15-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>“Keep</em><em> my statutes. Don’t crossbreed two kinds of cattle, sow your field with two kinds of seed, or wear a piece of </em><em>clothes</em><em> that blends two kinds of material. </em><strong><sup>20</sup></strong><em>If</em><em> a man lies carnally with a slave woman that’s engaged to another man </em><em>but</em> <em>not yet </em><em>redeemed or given her freedom</em><em>, punish them. Don’t put them to death; she wasn’t free. </em><strong><sup>21</sup></strong><em>He’ll</em><em> take a ram to Yahveh at the entrance to the Tent of Meeting. </em><strong><sup>22</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> priest will atone for him with the ram, and he will be forgiven.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 19:19-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>“When</em><em> you go into the land and plant fruit trees, consider their fruit forbidden for three years. </em><strong><sup>24</sup></strong><em>In</em><em> the fourth year, their fruit will be holy, a praise offering to Yahveh. </em><strong><sup>25</sup></strong><em>Th</em><em>e</em><em> fifth year, you can eat </em><em>it</em><em> so it’ll increase for you. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> eat anything with blood in it or practice fortunetelling or soothsaying. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> trim off </em><em>the</em><em> </em><em>hair on your temples</em><em> or</em><strong><em> </em></strong><em>trim</em><em> the edges of your beard.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> make cuts on your body for the dead or wear tattoos. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em>.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 19:23-28</p>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> profane your daughter by making her a prostitute, so your country won’t fall into prostitution and fill up with lewdness. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <em>“Keep</em><em> my Sabbaths and respect my sanctuary. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> defile yourself by appealing to mediums or spiritualists. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <em>“Stand</em><em> up for the gray headed, honor the aged, respect God. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> mistreat foreigners living in your country. </em><strong><sup>34</sup></strong><em>Treat</em><em> them like the natives among you. Love them like yourself. You were foreigners in Egypt. </em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> your God</u></em><em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <em>“</em><em>Don’t</em><em> cheat in judgment in measuring weight or volume. </em><strong><sup>36</sup></strong><em>Have</em><em> accurate balances, weights, ephahs, and hins. </em><strong><sup>37</sup></strong><em>Observe</em><em> my statutes and ordinances. </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em><u> that brought you out of Egypt</u></em><em>.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Lev 19:29-37</p>
