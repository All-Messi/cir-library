## Job Chapter **28**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><em>“There’s</em><em> a mine for silver</em> <em>and a refinery for gold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup><em>Iron</em><em> comes out of the ground,</em> <em>and people can smelt copper.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> use a torch in the darkness </em> <em>and go in as </em><em>far</em><em> they can, </em> <em>searching for the ore in gloom and deep shadow.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> sink mine shafts far from where people live</em> <em>and go down them, dangling </em>on ropes, <em>swinging back and forth.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup><em>People</em><em> grow food on top of the </em><em>ground;</em> but<em> far below, the ground melts as if by fire.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 28:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup><em>Its</em><em> rocks yield lapis lazuli,</em><em>	</em> <em>whose flecks glitter like gold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><em>Birds</em><em> of prey can’t get </em>to it; <em>no falcon has seen it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup><em>Proud</em><em> animals don’t set foot in </em><em>it;</em> <em>fierce lions don’t prowl there.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup><em>Miners</em><em> find flint</em> <em>and tear down mountains to get at it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> chisel out tunnels in the rock</em> <em>to discover precious stones.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> dam up trickling streams</em> <em>and bring hidden treasure out to light.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 28:6-11</p>

### 12“But where can people discover wisdom? Where is intelligence located?


<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup><em>People</em><em> don’t know what it’s </em><em>worth;</em> <em>they can’t </em><em>find it</em><em> where people live.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup><em>The</em><em> deep says, ‘It’s not here</em><em>’;</em> <em>the sea says, ‘It’s not inside </em><em><u>me</u></em><em>.’</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup><em>Pure</em><em> gold doesn’t exchange for </em><em>it;</em> <em>silver doesn’t weigh out equal to its price.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup><em>Its</em><em> value can’t be calculated in </em><em>the gold</em><em> from Ophir,</em> <em>in precious onyx or lapis lazuli.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 28:12-16</p>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup><em>Gold</em><em> or crystal can’t match </em><em>it;</em> <em>it can’t be traded for articles made from fine gold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup><em>Coral</em><em> and crystal can’t be mentioned with </em><em>it;</em> <em>acquiring wisdom is more important than buying pearls.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup><em>The</em><em> topaz in Nubia can’t compare to </em><em>it;</em> <em>it can’t be measured in terms of pure gold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>Where</em><em> then does wisdom come from?</em> <em>Where’s it located?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><em>Nothing</em><em> alive can see </em><em>it;</em> <em>it’s hid from the birds in the sky.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup><em>Abaddon</em><em> and Death say,</em> <em>‘We haven’t heard any report about it.’</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 28:17-22</p>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup><strong>26</strong></sup><em>when</em><em> he decreed the way rain should form</em> <em>and the course a thunderbolt should follow,</em></span>
