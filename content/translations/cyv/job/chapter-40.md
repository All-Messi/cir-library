## Job Chapter **40**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><strong>Yahveh</strong><strong> said to Job,</strong> <strong><sup>2</sup></strong><em>“Will</em><em> a faultfinder spar with the Almighty?</em> <em>Let the one that argues with God answer that.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup><strong>Job</strong><strong> said,</strong> <strong><sup>4</sup></strong><em>“I’m</em><em> insignificant; what kind of answer can I give?</em> <em>I’ve put my hand over my mouth.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup><em>I’ve</em><em> spoken once or twice; that’s </em><em>enough;</em> <em>I don’t have anything else to say.”</em><em>	</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 40:1-5</p>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup><strong>Yahveh</strong><strong> answered him out of the storm:</strong> <strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>“Gird</em><em> up* like a </em><em>man;</em> <em>I’ll ask you </em>some questions<em>, and you </em><em>answer</em><em> me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup><em>Will</em><em> you really try to discredit me to justify yourself?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup><strong>9</strong></sup><em>Do</em><em> you have an arm like mine?</em> <em>Can you thunder like me?  </em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 40:6-9</p>

### 10“Go ahead and put on eminence and dignity; dress yourself with honor and majesty.


<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup><em>Pour</em><em> out your overflowing </em><em>anger;</em> <em>look at everybody that’s proud and humble them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup><em>Look</em><em> down on everybody that’s </em><em>arrogant;</em> <em>walk on sinners where they stand.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup><em>Bury</em><em> them in the dust </em><em>together;</em> <em>imprison them in the hidden place.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup><em>Then</em><em> I’ll acknowledge to you</em> <em>that your own right hand can save you.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 40:10-14</p>

### 15“Look at Behemoth, which I made as well as I did you; it eats grass like an ox.


<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> has strong loins</em> <em>and powerful muscles in its stomach.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> swings its tail like a cedar </em><em>beam;</em> <em>the sinews in its thighs intertwine.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup><em>Its</em><em> bones are like bronze </em><em>tubes;</em> <em>its limbs, like iron bars.</em><strong>	</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup><em>It’s</em><em> a prime example of </em><em>my what</em><em> I’ve </em><em>created;</em> <em>only the One that made it need bring his sword!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>The mountains produce </em><em>its</em><em> food,</em> <em>where wild animals play.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> lies down under the lotus plants</em> <em>and hides among the reeds in the marsh.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup><em>The</em><em> lotus plants give it </em><em>shade;</em> <em>the willows by the stream surround it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> doesn’t care if the river rages</em> <em>or worry if the Jordan rushes at its mouth.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup><em>Can</em><em> anyone capture it when it’s awake?</em> <em>Can anybody trap it and put a ring in its nose?</em></span>
