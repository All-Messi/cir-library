## Job Chapter 9

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>Job</strong><strong> answered:</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>He</em><em> does great, unimaginable things,</em> <em>	</em><em>	</em><em>countless </em><em>amazing </em><em>deeds</em><em>.</em><strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>“If</em><em> he passes by me, I don’t see </em><em>him;</em> <em>	</em><em>	if he goes by, I can’t sense him.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>God</em><em> won’t turn away his </em><em>anger;</em> <em>	</em><em>	Rahab’s helpers cower below him.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>Even</em><em> if I were innocent, I couldn’t answer </em><em>him;</em> <em>	</em><em>	I’d have to appeal to my accuser for mercy.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>Though</em><em> I’m good, my mouth will condemn </em><em>me;</em> <em>	</em><em>	though I’m innocent, it’ll pronounce me guilty.</em><strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>“I’m</em><em> innocent, but it doesn’t </em><em>matter</em><em>;</em><em> </em> <em>	</em><em>	I despise my life.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>The</em><em> earth has been turned over to </em><em>sinners</em><em>;</em> <em>	</em><em>	he </em><em>blinds the eyes</em><em> of his judges.</em> <em>	</em><em>	If it’s not him, who is it?</span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>If</em><em> I say, ‘I’ll forget about my </em><em>complaint;</em> <em>	</em><em>	I’ll leave my sad countenance and be cheerful,’</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <em>He</em><em>’ll</em><em> condemn</em><em> me</em><em> </em><em>anyway;</em> <em>	</em><em>	why should I try for nothing?</em><strong>	</strong></span>
