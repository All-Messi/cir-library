## Job Chapter **24**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><em>“Why</em><em> doesn’t the Almighty maintain </em> <em>a regular court schedule for judging?</em> <em>Why do those who know him wait for him in vain?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup><em>Some</em><em> remove landmarks,</em> <em>seize flocks and eat them,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup><em>drive</em><em> away donkeys that belong to the fatherless,</em> <em>take widows’ oxen for pledges.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> run the needy off the </em><em>road;</em> <em>the poor people </em><em>have to</em><em> hide </em>from them<em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup><em>Like</em><em> wild donkeys in the desert, </em> the poor<em> labor at foraging for food,</em> <em>looking for prey in the wild to feed their children.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> gather fodder in the field</em> <em>and glean sinners’ vineyards.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> spend the night without enough clothes</em> <em>to protect them against the cold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup><em>They’re</em><em> soaked from mountain rain</em> <em>and huddle in the rocks for lack of shelter.</em><em>	</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:1-8</p>

### 9“Others snatch the fatherless from the breast and take pledges against the poor.


<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> make the naked go without clothes</em> <em>and take away sheaves from the hungry.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> press out oil between the olive rows</em> <em>and tread winepresses </em>for others<em>, yet</em><em> suffer thirst.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup><em>From</em><em> the city, the people groan,</em> <em>the wounded cry out for help,</em> <em>but God ignores their prayers.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:9-12</p>

### 13“Others rebel against the light; they refuse to know its ways or stay on them.


<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup><em>A</em><em> murderer gets up at dawn</em> <em>to kill the poor and </em><em>needy;</em> <em>at night he’s a thief.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup><em>An</em><em> adulterer waits for twilight and says,</em> <em>‘Nobody will see me</em><em>’;</em> <em>he disguises himself.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup><em>In</em><em> the dark they break into houses</em> <em>and sleep in the </em><em>daytime;</em> <em>they’re not accustomed to light.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup><em>Morning</em><em> is the same </em><em>to</em><em> them as dense </em><em>darkness;</em><em> </em> <em>they’re allies with the terrors of deep gloom.</em><em>	</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:13-17</p>

### 18“But they’re swiftly carried away like foam on a creek; their land is cursed; they’re afraid to go into their own vineyards.


<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup><strong>19</strong></sup><em>Sheol</em><em> consumes sinners</em> <em>like drought and heat consume snow.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>Their</em><em> mothers forget about them,</em> <em>maggots feed on them till they’re not </em><em>remembered;</em> <em>they’re blown over like trees </em>in a windstorm.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> prey on barren and childless women</em> <em>and fail to help </em>needy <em>widows.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup><em>But</em><em> God uses his power to drag away the </em><em>rich;</em><em> </em> <em>they may get established, </em> <em>but they have no guarantee of life</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup><em>He</em><em> may let them live in security, </em> <em>but he keeps an eye on their progress in life.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup><em>They’re</em><em> honored for a little while; then they’re </em><em>gone;</em> <em>they’re brought low and fade away like everything else,</em> <em>cut off like heads of grain.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup><em>If</em><em> that’s not so, who can prove me wrong</em> <em>and make my speech pointless?”</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 24:18-25</p>
