## Job Chapter 30

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“But</em><em> now people younger than me </em><em>make</em><em> fun of me,</em> <em>people whose fathers I wouldn’t let </em> <em>run with my sheep dogs.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>What</em><em> good was their strength to me,</em> <em>fellows whose vigor was </em><em>gone?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>They</em><em> were haggard from hunger and want</em> <em>and clawed the dry ground in the wastelands.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>They</em><em> picked wild greens from among the bushes</em> <em>and ate the roots of broom shrubs.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>People</em><em> drove them </em><em>away;</em> <em>they shouted at them like they were thieves.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>So</em><em> they lived in </em><em>ravines</em><em>, caves, and </em><em>rocks;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>they</em><em> sounded like animals in the bushes</em> <em>and huddled together under the nettles.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>A</em><em> senseless, unnamed bunch,</em> <em>they were outcasts from society.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:1-8</p>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>“Now</em><em> </em>their sons<em> mock me with songs </em> <em>and use my name as a byword.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>They’re</em><em> appalled at me and shrink </em><em>back;</em> <em>they don’t hesitate to </em><em>spit in</em><em> my face.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>Because</em><em> God has removed my bowstring and cast me down,</em> <em>people don’t hesitate to argue with me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>This</em><em> rabble opposes me,</em> <em>drives me out, lays traps for me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>They</em><em> tear up my road and profit from my </em><em>misfortune;</em> <em>nobody restrains them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>They</em><em> come at me as if through a wide </em><em>breach;</em> <em>they roll in like a storm.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>They</em><em> terrorize me</em><em>	</em> <em>and chase my honor like the </em><em>wind;</em> <em>my prosperity dissipates like a cloud.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:9-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>“My</em><em> life ebbs out of </em><em>me;</em> <em>depression has taken hold of me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>At</em><em> night my bones ache,</em> <em>and soreness gnaws at me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>My</em><em> clothes are tattered from </em><em>violence;</em> <em>they hang on me like a coat.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:16-18</p>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>“You</em><strong><em>’</em></strong><em>ve</em><em> thrown me in the </em><em>mud;</em> <em>you’ve made me like dust and ashes.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>I</em><em> call out to you, but you don’t </em><em>answer;</em> <em>I stand up, but you turn your attention elsewhere.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>You’ve</em><em> become cruel</em> <em>and use your power to persecute.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>You</em><em> pick me up and blow me around in the </em><em>wind;</em> <em>you destroy me in the storm.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>I</em><em> know you’re going to bring me to death,</em> <em>the destiny of </em><em>all the</em><em> living.</em><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:19-23</p>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>“Don’t</em><em> people in a pile of ruins hold out their hands</em> <em>and in their misery call out for help?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <em>Haven’t</em><em> I cried for people whose life is hard?</em> <em>Wasn’t I </em><em>grieved</em><em> for the needy?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <em>When</em><em> I expected good, evil </em><em>came;</em> <em>when I needed light, darkness arrived.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>I’m</em><em> in turmoil inside; I can’t </em><em>relax;</em> <em>days of misery lie ahead of me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <em>I</em><em> go </em><em>around mourning</em><em> without </em><em>comfort;</em> <em>I stand up in the assembly and call out for help.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <em>My</em><em> skin is ashen,</em> <em>and I’m burning up with </em><em>fever</em><em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <em>My</em><em> harp plays music for </em><em>mourning;</em> <em>my flute moans with those who weep.</span>
