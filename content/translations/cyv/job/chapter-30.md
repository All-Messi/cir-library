## Job Chapter **30**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><strong><sup>:1</sup></strong><em>“But</em><em> now people younger than me </em><em>make</em><em> fun of me,</em> <em>people whose fathers I wouldn’t let </em> <em>run with my sheep dogs.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup><em>What</em><em> good was their strength to me,</em> <em>fellows whose vigor was </em><em>gone?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> were haggard from hunger and want</em> <em>and clawed the dry ground in the wastelands.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> picked wild greens from among the bushes</em> <em>and ate the roots of broom shrubs.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup><em>People</em><em> drove them </em><em>away;</em> <em>they shouted at them like they were thieves.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup><em>So</em><em> they lived in </em><em>ravines</em><em>, caves, and </em><em>rocks;</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><em>they</em><em> sounded like animals in the bushes</em> <em>and huddled together under the nettles.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup><em>A</em><em> senseless, unnamed bunch,</em> <em>they were outcasts from society.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:1-8</p>

### 9“Now their sons mock me with songs  and use my name as a byword.


<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup><em>They’re</em><em> appalled at me and shrink </em><em>back;</em> <em>they don’t hesitate to </em><em>spit in</em><em> my face.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup><em>Because</em><em> God has removed my bowstring and cast me down,</em> <em>people don’t hesitate to argue with me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup><em>This</em><em> rabble opposes me,</em> <em>drives me out, lays traps for me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> tear up my road and profit from my </em><em>misfortune;</em> <em>nobody restrains them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> come at me as if through a wide </em><em>breach;</em> <em>they roll in like a storm.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> terrorize me</em><em>	</em> <em>and chase my honor like the </em><em>wind;</em> <em>my prosperity dissipates like a cloud.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:9-15</p>

### 16“My life ebbs out of me; depression has taken hold of me.


<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup><em>At</em><em> night my bones ache,</em> <em>and soreness gnaws at me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup><em>My</em><em> clothes are tattered from </em><em>violence;</em> <em>they hang on me like a coat.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:16-18</p>

### 19“You’ve thrown me in the mud; you’ve made me like dust and ashes.


<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>I</em><em> call out to you, but you don’t </em><em>answer;</em> <em>I stand up, but you turn your attention elsewhere.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><em>You’ve</em><em> become cruel</em> <em>and use your power to persecute.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup><em>You</em><em> pick me up and blow me around in the </em><em>wind;</em> <em>you destroy me in the storm.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup><em>I</em><em> know you’re going to bring me to death,</em> <em>the destiny of </em><em>all the</em><em> living.</em><strong> </strong><strong>	</strong><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:19-23</p>

### 24“Don’t people in a pile of ruins hold out their hands and in their misery call out for help?


<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup><em>Haven’t</em><em> I cried for people whose life is hard?</em> <em>Wasn’t I </em><em>grieved</em><em> for the needy?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup><strong>26</strong></sup><em>When</em><em> I expected good, evil </em><em>came;</em> <em>when I needed light, darkness arrived.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup><em>I’m</em><em> in turmoil inside; I can’t </em><em>relax;</em> <em>days of misery lie ahead of me.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup><strong>28</strong></sup><em>I</em><em> go </em><em>around mourning</em><em> without </em><em>comfort;</em> <em>I stand up in the assembly and call out for help.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup><strong>29</strong></sup><em>I’ve</em><em> become a brother to jackals,</em> <em>a companion of owls.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup><strong>30</strong></sup><em>My</em><em> skin is ashen,</em> <em>and I’m burning up with </em><em>fever</em><em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v31"><sup><strong>31</strong></sup><em>My</em><em> harp plays music for </em><em>mourning;</em> <em>my flute moans with those who weep.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 30:24-31</p>
