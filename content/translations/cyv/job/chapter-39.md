## Job Chapter **39**

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><em>“Do</em><em> you know when mountain goats give birth</em><em>?</em> <em>Do you ever see </em><em>a deer</em><em> calving?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup><strong>2</strong></sup><em>Can</em><em> you count the months of their gestation</em> <em>or learn when they have their fawns?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup><em>They</em><em> crouch down to deliver their young.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup><em>Their</em><em> offspring grow strong out in the </em><em>open;</em> <em>they leave home and never return.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:1-4</p>

### 5“Who turned the wild donkey loose? Who untied its ropes?


<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup><em>I</em><em> gave it the open country for a home,</em> <em>desert land to live </em><em>in?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> scorns city turmoil </em> <em>and doesn’t have to listen to a driver shout at it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup><strong>8</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> roams the mountains for pasture,</em> <em>looking for anything green.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:5-8</p>

### 9“Will a wild ox consent to serve you and spend the night at your manger?


<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup><em>Can</em><em> you harness it </em>to plow <em>in a furrow?</em> <em>Will it harrow your fields?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup><em>Will</em><em> you depend on it because it’s strong </em> <em>and leave your work for it to do?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup><strong>12</strong></sup><em>Will</em><em> you trust it to bring your grain home</em> <em>and deliver it to your threshing floor?</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:9-12</p>

### 13“The ostrich’s wings flap happily, but they don’t compare  with the wings and feathers of a stork.


<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup><em>She</em><em> abandons her eggs in the ground</em> <em>to stay warm in the sand.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup><em>She</em><em> forgets that a foot may crush them</em> <em>or a wild ox may tramp on them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup><em>She’s</em><em> harsh toward her young as if they weren’t </em><em>hers;</em> <em>though her work may be pointless, she doesn’t care,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup><em>because</em><em> God hasn’t made her wise</em> <em>or given her intelligence.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup><em>When</em><em> she jumps up to run,</em> <em>she laughs at a horse and its rider.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:13-18</p>

### 19“Do you give a horse its strength or cover its neck with a mane?


<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>Can</em><em> you make it leap like a locust?</em> <em>Its majestic snorting is terrifying!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> paws in the valley and frolics in its strength</em> <em>and charges out to battle.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> mocks fear and</em> <em>doesn’t shy away from the sword. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup><em>The</em><em> quiver rattles against it,</em> <em>the flashing spear and javelin</em> too<em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> doesn’t stand still when the bugle </em><em>signals;</em> <em>with trembling rage, it races over the ground.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup><em>As</em><em> often as the bugle blows, it </em><em>says, ‘</em><em>Aha!’</em> <em>It </em><em>smells</em><em> a battle a long way off,</em> <em>the captain’s thundering, and the battle cry.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:19-25</p>

### 26“Does a hawk soar because you’re so smart as it stretches its wings toward the south?


<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup><em>Does</em><em> an eagle </em><em>mount</em><em> up at your command</em> <em>and build its nest up high?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup><strong>28</strong></sup><em>It</em><em> lives and lodges on the cliffs,</em> <em>in inaccessible</em><em> places on the rocky crag.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup><strong>29</strong></sup><em>From</em><em> there it spies out prey</em> <em>that it can see from far away.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup><strong>30</strong></sup><em>Even</em><em> its young gulp down </em><em>blood;</em> <em>they’re right there when the kill goes down.</em><strong> </strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 39:26-30</p>
