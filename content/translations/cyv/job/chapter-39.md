## Job Chapter 39

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“Do</em><em> you know when mountain goats give birth</em><em>?</em> <em>Do you ever see </em><em>a deer</em><em> calving?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>Can</em><em> you count the months of their gestation</em> <em>or learn when they have their fawns?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>They</em><em> crouch down to deliver their young.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>Their</em><em> offspring grow strong out in the </em><em>open;</em> <em>they leave home and never return.</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 39:1-4</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>I</em><em> gave it the open country for a home,</em> <em>desert land to live </em><em>in?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>It</em><em> scorns city turmoil </em> <em>and doesn’t have to listen to a driver shout at it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>It</em><em> roams the mountains for pasture,</em> <em>looking for anything green.</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 39:5-8</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>Can</em><em> you harness it </em>to plow <em>in a furrow?</em> <em>Will it harrow your fields?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>Will</em><em> you depend on it because it’s strong </em> <em>and leave your work for it to do?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>Will</em><em> you trust it to bring your grain home</em> <em>and deliver it to your threshing floor?</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 39:9-12</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>She</em><em> abandons her eggs in the ground</em> <em>to stay warm in the sand.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>She</em><em> forgets that a foot may crush them</em> <em>or a wild ox may tramp on them.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>She’s</em><em> harsh toward her young as if they weren’t </em><em>hers;</em> <em>though her work may be pointless, she doesn’t care,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>because</em><em> God hasn’t made her wise</em> <em>or given her intelligence.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>When</em><em> she jumps up to run,</em> <em>she laughs at a horse and its rider.</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 39:13-18</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>Can</em><em> you make it leap like a locust?</em> <em>Its majestic snorting is terrifying!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>It</em><em> paws in the valley and frolics in its strength</em> <em>and charges out to battle.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>It</em><em> mocks fear and</em> <em>doesn’t shy away from the sword. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>The</em><em> quiver rattles against it,</em> <em>the flashing spear and javelin</em> too<em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>It</em><em> doesn’t stand still when the bugle </em><em>signals;</em> <em>with trembling rage, it races over the ground.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <em>As</em><em> often as the bugle blows, it </em><em>says, ‘</em><em>Aha!’</em> <em>It </em><em>smells</em><em> a battle a long way off,</em> <em>the captain’s thundering, and the battle cry.</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 39:19-25</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>Does</em><em> an eagle </em><em>mount</em><em> up at your command</em> <em>and build its nest up high?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <em>It</em><em> lives and lodges on the cliffs,</em> <em>in inaccessible</em><em> places on the rocky crag.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <em>From</em><em> there it spies out prey</em> <em>that it can see from far away.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <em>Even</em><em> its young gulp down </em><em>blood;</em> <em>they’re right there when the kill goes down.</em><strong> </strong><strong>Job 39:26-30</strong></span>
