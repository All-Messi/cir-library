<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup><strong>1</strong></sup><strong>God</strong><strong> answered Job out of the whirlwind:</strong> <strong><sup>2</sup></strong><em>“Who</em><em> is this that confuses my counsel</em> <em>with ignorant words?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup><em>Who</em><em> specified its measurements</em> <em>or stretched out a line on it?</em> <em>Surely you know!</em></span>

<p class="section-ref">Job 38:8-11</p>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup><em>The</em><em> earth is stamped like clay under a </em><em>seal;</em> <em>its features stand out like folds in a robe.</em></span>

<p class="section-ref">Job 38:12-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup><em>Have</em><em> the gates of death been revealed to </em><em>you;</em> <em>have you seen the doors </em><em>into</em><em> deep darkness?</em></span>

<p class="section-ref">Job 38:16-18</p>

### 19“Where’s the road to where light lives? Where’s the place for darkness,


<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>so</em><em> you can take it to its territory</em> <em>and make out the paths to its home?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><em>You</em><em> know because you were born there, right!</em> <em>and you’ve lived so long!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup><em>Have</em><em> you entered the storehouses for snow</em> <em>or seen where I keep the hail</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup><strong>23</strong></sup><em>I</em><em> reserve for troubled times,</em> <em>for the time of war and battle?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup><strong>24</strong></sup><em>Where’s</em><em> the road to the source of light</em> <em>or where the east wind scatters over the earth?</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 38:19-24</p>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup><strong>33</strong></sup><em>Do</em><em> you know the laws of the sky</em> <em>or manage the earth with them?</em> <strong><sup>34</sup></strong><em>“Can</em><em> you shout to the clouds</em> <em>to get enough water to cover you?</em></span>
