## Job Chapter 38

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>God</strong><strong> answered Job out of the whirlwind:</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>Gird</em><em> up* like a </em><em>man;</em> <em>I’ll ask you some questions, and you </em><em>answer</em><em> me.</em><em>	</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>Where</em><em> were you when I laid the foundation for the earth?</em> <em>Tell me if you have any </em><em>idea</em><em>.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>Who</em><em> specified its measurements</em> <em>or stretched out a line on it?</em> <em>Surely you know!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>What</em><em> did its pylons rest on?</em> <em>Who laid its cornerstone</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>when</em><em> the morning stars sang together</em> <em>and all God’s angels shouted for joy?</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 38:1-7</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>when</em><em> I clothed it with clouds</em> <em>and swaddled it with deep darkness,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>when</em><em> I set its boundaries,</em> <em>bolted its doors,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>and</em><em> said, ‘You can’t go beyond this </em><em>point;</em> <em>here’s where your proud waves stop’?</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 38:8-11</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>so</em><em> it can spread to the ends of the earth</em> <em>and </em><em>bring to an end</em><em> night’s evil?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <em>The</em><em> earth is stamped like clay under a </em><em>seal;</em> <em>its features stand out like folds in a robe.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>Light</em><em> is withheld from sinners,</em> <em>and their uplifted arm is broken.</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 38:12-15</p>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>Have</em><em> the gates of death been revealed to </em><em>you;</em> <em>have you seen the doors </em><em>into</em><em> deep darkness?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Do</em><em> you have any idea how big the earth is?</em> <em>Tell me if you know that.</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 38:16-18</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>so</em><em> you can take it to its territory</em> <em>and make out the paths to its home?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>You</em><em> know because you were born there, right!</em> <em>and you’ve lived so long!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>Have</em><em> you entered the storehouses for snow</em> <em>or seen where I keep the hail</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>I</em><em> reserve for troubled times,</em> <em>for the time of war and battle?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>Where’s</em><em> the road to the source of light</em> <em>or where the east wind scatters over the earth?</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 38:19-24</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <em>to</em><em> bring rain on land with no people—</em> <em>on a vacant desert—</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <em>to</em><em> satisfy desolate land</em> <em>and make grass sprout?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <em>Does</em><em> rain have a father?</em> <em>Who has begotten the drops of dew?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <em>Whose</em><em> womb did ice come out of?</em> <em>Who gave birth to frost from the sky?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <em>Water</em><em> gets as hard as </em><em>rock;</em> <em>its surface freezes over and imprisons the deep.</em><strong> </strong><strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 38:25-30</p>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <em>Do</em><em> you know the laws of the sky</em> <em>or manage the earth with them?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <em>when</em><em> dust hardens into clumps</em> <em>and forms </em><em>clods</em><em>?</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 38:36-38</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <em>when</em><em> they crouch in their dens</em> <em>and lie in wait in their lairs?</em></span>
