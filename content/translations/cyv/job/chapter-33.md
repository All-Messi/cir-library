<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup><strong>3</strong></sup><em>What</em><em> I’m saying expresses my </em><em>uprightness;</em> <em>my lips declare knowledge sincerely.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup><strong>4</strong></sup><em>The</em><em> Spirit of God has made </em><em>me;</em> <em>the breath of the Almighty gives me breath.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup><strong>5</strong></sup><em>Answer</em><em> me if you </em><em>can;</em> <em>state your case; take your stand.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup><strong>6</strong></sup><em>I</em><em> belong to God like you </em><em>do;</em> <em>I’ve been formed out of clay too.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup><strong>7</strong></sup><em>I</em><em> shouldn’t be a </em><em>threat</em><em> </em><em>you;</em> <em>I won’t come down hard on you.</em><strong>	</strong></span>

### 8“You’ve spoken where I could hear you; I’ve heard what you said: 9‘I’m pure, without sin,	 innocent, not guilty of anything.


<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup><strong>10</strong></sup><em>He</em><em> invents pretexts against me</em> <em>and considers me his enemy.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup><strong>11</strong></sup><em>He</em><em> puts my feet in stocks</em> <em>and watches my paths.’</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 33:8-11</p>

### 12“Let me tell you, you’re not right in this, because God is greater than you are.


<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup><strong>13</strong></sup><em>Why</em><em> do you complain </em> <em>that he won’t account for everything he </em><em>does?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup><strong>14</strong></sup><em>God</em><em> speaks in one way, maybe </em><em>two;</em> <em>though people don’t notice it—</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup><strong>15</strong></sup><em>in</em><em> a dream, in a vision at night,</em> <em>when people are sound asleep in bed.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup><strong>16</strong></sup><em>Then</em><em> he opens their ears and warns them,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup><strong>17</strong></sup><em>so</em><em> he can turn them aside from doing wrong</em> <em>and keep them from their pride.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup><strong>18</strong></sup><em>He</em><em> holds them back from the Pit</em> <em>and their lives from death by the sword.</em><em>	</em></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 33:12-18</p>

### 19“God disciplines people with pain on their beds, with constant aching in their bones;


<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup><strong>20</strong></sup><em>so</em><em> they lose their appetites </em> <em>and even their favorite food disgusts them.</em><em>	</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup><strong>21</strong></sup><em>Their bodies waste away, </em> <em>and their bones show.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup><strong>22</strong></sup><em>Then</em><em> they draw near the </em><em>Pit;</em> <em>they come close to the angels that bring death.</em><strong>	</strong></span>
<p class="section-ref">Job 33:19-22</p>

### 23“If there’s an angel to mediate for some people— one out of a thousand— to say what’s right about them,

<span class="verse" id="v25"><sup><strong>25</strong></sup><em>Let</em><em> their bodies become fresh with youth</em> <em>and return to their youthful vigor.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v26"><sup><strong>26</strong></sup><em>Then</em><em> they’ll pray to God, and he’ll accept them</em> <em>so</em><em> they can see his face with joy</em> <em>and restore their good standing.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup><strong>27</strong></sup><em>They’ll</em><em> sing to </em>other <em>people and say,</em> <em>‘I sinned and perverted what’s right,</em> <em>but he didn’t hold it against me.</em></span>
