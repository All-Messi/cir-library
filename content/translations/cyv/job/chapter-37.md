## Job Chapter 37

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <em>“My</em><em> heart pounds when I think about </em><em>this;</em> <em>it feels like it’s out of place.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>Listen</em><em> closely to his thundering,</em> <em>the rumbling that comes from his mouth.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>He</em><em> unleashes his lightning under the whole </em><em>sky;</em> <em>it travels to the horizon in every direction.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>After</em><em> it his </em><em>roars;</em> <em>he thunders with his majestic voice</em> <em>and doesn’t hold anything back.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>God</em><em> thunders wondrously</em> <em>and does extraordinary things we can’t comprehend.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>He</em><em> tells the snow to fall on the ground</em> <em>and the rain to be heavy.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>He</em><em> stops everyone’s labor </em> <em>so</em><em> they can watch his work.</em><strong>	</strong><strong>Job 37:1-7</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>Animals</em><em> head for their dens</em> <em>and stay in their lairs.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>The</em><em> stormy wind comes from its rooms,</em> <em>and the driving winds bring the cold.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>God’s</em><em> breath makes ice,</em> <em>and the wide expanse of water freezes. </em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <em>He</em><em> loads up dark clouds with </em><em>moisture;</em> <em>they disperse his lightning flashes.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>They</em><em> change direction by </em><em>his</em><em> </em><em>guidance;</em> <em>they do whatever he commands across the world.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>He</em><em> makes these things happen either to punish his people</em> <em>or to water the earth and show his love.</em><em>	</em><strong>Job 37:8-13</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>Do</em><em> you know how he controls the clouds</em> <em>or makes lightning flash in them?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>Can</em><em> you explain the layers of dark clouds,</em> <em>the wonders of One that knows everything,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>you</em><em> whose clothes are hot</em> <em>because the south wind isn’t blowing?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Can</em><em> you spread out the sky like he does,</em> <em>as hard as a bronze mirror?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <em>Teach</em><em> us what to say to </em><em>him;</em> <em>we can’t draw up our case without your help!</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <em>Should</em><em> somebody let him know I want to speak?</em> <em>Does anyone ever want to be destroyed?</em><strong> </strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <em>Golden</em><em> splendor comes from the </em><em>north;</em> <em>awesome majesty surrounds God.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <em>We</em><em> can’t imagine how extremely powerful the Almighty </em><em>is;</em> <em>yet he doesn’t violate justice and abundant goodness.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <em>So</em><em> people everywhere revere him</em> <em>because he doesn’t value people that are wise in their own conceit.”</em> <strong>Job 37:14-24</strong></span>
