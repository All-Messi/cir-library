## Job Chapter 22

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <strong>Eliphaz</strong><strong> the Temanite answered.</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <em>“What</em><em> benefit can a mortal be to God?</em> <em>Aren’t even the wise only useful to themselves?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>Is</em><em> the Almighty delighted if you’re good?</em> <em>What good is it to him if you’re perfect?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>Surely</em><em> it’s not because you revere him that he’s reproving you,</em> <em>that he’s bringing judgment against you.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <em>Isn’t</em><em> it more likely that you’re sinful,</em> <em>that your sins are endless?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>Maybe</em><em> you’ve exacted pledges from your friends </em> <em>and stripped off their clothes as security.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <em>Maybe</em><em> you’ve withheld water from the fainting</em> <em>and food from the hungry.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <em>Maybe</em><em> you think the land belongs to the powerful,</em> <em>that only </em><em>you</em><em> honorable types have a right to live in it.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <em>Maybe</em><em> you’ve sent widows away with nothing</em> <em>and crushed the fatherless.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>So</em><em>, there are traps everywhere around you,</em> <em>and panic overwhelms you.</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <em>But</em><em> you </em><em>say, ‘</em><em>So how can he see what I’m doing?</em> <em>Can he judge through such darkness?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <em>Will</em><em> you stay </em><em>on</em><em> the ancient way</em> <em>that sinful people have walked,</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <em>people</em><em> snatched away before their time,</em> <em>whose foundations a river washed away?</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <em>They</em><em> tell God, ‘Get away!’</em> <em>‘What good is the Almighty to us?’</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <em>Yet</em><em> he’s filled their houses with good things—</em> <em>(but I stay away from sinners’ counsel).</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> 'Our adversaries are destroyed; fire has consumed their wealth.'</span>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <em>“Submit</em><em> to God; make peace with him,</em> <em>and good things will happen to you.</em></span>
