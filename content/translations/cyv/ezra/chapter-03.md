## Ezra Chapter 3


### The Altar Is Rebuilt


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the <b>seventh month</b> came and the Israelites were in the towns, they came together in Jerusalem. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jeshua Ben-Jozadak, his brothers the priests, Zerubbabel Ben-Shealtiel, and his brothers built God’s altar for offering burnt offerings as written in the Law of Moses, the man of God. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They set up the altar on its original site despite their fear of the people around them. They offered burnt offerings to Yahveh morning and evening. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They celebrated the Festival of Booths as written and offered the set number of burnt offerings daily as required by the Law. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From then on, there was continual burnt offering for new moons and the annual festivals Yahveh had consecrated. Everyone offered a freewill offering to Yahveh. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>From the <b>first day, seventh month</b>, they offered burnt offerings, but the foundation of the Temple hadn’t been laid. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then they donated money to the masons and carpenters as well as food, drink, and olive oil to the Sidonians and Tyrians to bring cedar from Lebanon by sea to Joppa with permission from Cyrus.<b>
</b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ezra 3:1-7</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The <b>second month, second year</b> after arriving at God’s Temple, Zerubbabel, Jeshua, the rest of the priests and Levites, and everybody from the captivity began the work. They appointed the Levites 20 years and older as supervisors. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>To oversee the workmen, Jeshua with his sons and relatives stood united with Kadmiel and his sons (all descendants of Judah/<i>Hodaviah</i>) and with the Levites from the family of Henadad. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the builders had laid the foundation, the priests stood in their apparel with trumpets. The Levites, descendants of Asaph, <i>stood </i>with cymbals to praise Yahveh according to King David’s directions. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They sang, praising and thanking Yahveh, <i>“He’s good; his love for Israel endures forever.”
</i>The people shouted when they praised him because the foundation had been laid. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yet many priests, Levites, and heads of families—old men who’d seen the first Temple—cried out loud. Many others shouted out loud with so much joy </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>that people couldn’t tell the shout of joy from the outcry of weeping. They could be heard for a long way.<b>
</b></span></span></p>
