## Ezra Chapter 1


### Conversational Yahveh Covenant



### Cyrus Lets the Exiles Return


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <b><span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the first year of Cyrus, king of Persia, to fulfill his message through Jeremiah, Yahveh stirred up Cyrus. He sent a written proclamation everywhere in his kingdom:</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><b><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         2</span></sup></b><b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Cyrus, king of Persia, says, ‘Yahveh God of heaven has given me the kingdoms of the world and appointed me to build him a temple in Jerusalem, in Judah. </span></i></b><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Whoever is from his people, your God be with you! Go up</span></i></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and rebuild the Temple of Yahveh God of Israel, the God in Jerusalem. </span></i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>4</span></sup><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Wherever there’s a survivor, support him with silver and gold, supplies and livestock, plus freewill offerings.’”           </span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                                  </span></b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ezra 1:1-4</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><a name="_Hlk145582359"><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> </a><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God stirred up the leaders of the tribes from Judah and Benjamin along with the priests and Levites to go to Jerusalem to rebuild his Temple. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Their neighbors encouraged them with silver and gold items, with goods, livestock, and valuables, plus freewill offerings. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Cyrus brought out the items from Yahveh’s Temple that Nebuchadnezzar had carried to the temple of his gods. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He had Mithredath the treasurer bring them out and count them out to Sheshbazzar, ruler of Judah: </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>30 gold basins, 1,000 silver basins, 29 censers, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>30 gold bowls, 410 silver bowls, 1,000 other articles—</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>5,400 gold and silver items in all. Sheshbazzar took them up with the exiles from Babylon to Jerusalem.          </span></span></p>
