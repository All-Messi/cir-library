## Deuteronomy Chapter 34


### The Death of Moses


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Moses went up from the plains of Moab to Mount Nebo, to the top of Pisgah across from Jericho. Yahveh showed him the land, Gilead to Dan; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Naphtali, Ephraim and Manasseh; the land of Judah to the Mediterranean Sea; </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>the Negev and the Valley of Jericho, the City of Palms as far as Zoar. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told him, <i>“This is the land I swore to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, ‘I’ll give it to your descendants.’ I’ve let you see it, but not enter it.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So Moses died in Moab. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh
</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>buried him in an unknown place in a valley in Moab across from Beth-peor. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Although he was 120 when he died, his sight was still clear, and his energy was good. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Israel mourned him for 30 days in the plains of Moab.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Joshua was wise because Moses had laid his hands on him. The Israelites listened to him and did what Yahveh told Moses. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Since then no prophet has risen in Israel that Yahveh related to directly. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There was nobody like him </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>for the signs and wonders Yahveh sent him to do in Egypt against Pharaoh, his officials, and his country, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and for the great power and terrifying deeds he performed for Israel to see.              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Deut 34:1-12</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>  </span></span></p>
