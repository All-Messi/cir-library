## 1 Kings Chapter 22


### Ahab and the False Prophets


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Three years passed without war between Aram and Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>In the <b>third year</b>, <b>Jehoshaphat</b>, king of Judah, came down to visit the king of Israel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king of Israel said to his officials, <i>“Do you know that Ramoth-gilead belongs to us, and we’re still not doing anything to take it back from the king of Aram?”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He asked Jehoshaphat, <i>“Will you go with me to battle at Ramoth-gilead?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoshaphat said, <i>“I’m like you, my troops like yours, my horses like yours.”</i> </span><span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He added, <i>“Let’s ask first for a message from Yahveh.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king of Israel gathered the prophets—about 400 men, <i>“Should we attack Ramoth-gilead?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>“Yes, Yahveh will deliver it to you.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Jehoshaphat said, <i>“Isn’t there still a prophet of Yahveh we can ask?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king said, <i>“There’s still one, Micaiah Ben-Imlah, but I despise him; he never prophesies anything good about me.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t say that.”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 22:1-9</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The two kings were sitting on their thrones, arrayed in their robes at the threshing floor by the gate of Samaria. The prophets were prophesying there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Zedekiah Ben-Chenaanah made iron horns for himself and said, <i>“Yahveh says you’ll gore the Arameans with these till they’re consumed.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The prophets were all prophesying, <i>“Attack Ramoth-gilead. Yahveh will deliver it to you.”</i></span></span></p>


### Micaiah Prophesies Against Ahab


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The messenger to Micaiah told him<i>, “The prophets agree in giving a favorable report to the king. Make your report like theirs.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Micaiah swore,
<i>“As sure as Yahveh lives, I’ll say what he tells me.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he came, the king said, <i>“Micaiah, should we attack Ramoth-gilead?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>He answered, <i>“Go right on up there! Yahveh is going to <u>deliver</u> it to you!!”</i> </span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king said, <i>“How many times do I have to make you swear not to tell me anything but the truth in Yahveh’s name?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <i>“I saw Israel scattered on the mountains</i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                  like sheep without a shepherd.</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Yahveh said, ‘They don’t have a master </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>anymore<i>;</i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                  they need to go home in peace.’”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                     </span></b><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 22:10-17</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king told Jehoshaphat, <i>“Didn’t I tell you he wouldn’t prophesy anything good about me?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Micaiah said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Listen to Yahveh’s message. I saw him<sup>Y</sup> on his throne and the host in heaven standing on his left and right. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He said,</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> <i>‘Who will lure Ahab to go up and fall at Ramoth-gilead?’</i> <i>One said this, another said that.</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then a spirit came forward,</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> <i>‘I’ll lure him.’</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>‘How?’</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>‘By being a deceiving spirit in his prophets.’ </span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>‘Go do it.’”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Micaiah said, <i>“Yahveh has put a deceiving spirit in your prophets. He’s declared disaster for you.”</i><b>
</b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 22:18-23</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Zedekiah came up and hit him on the cheek, <i>“How did Yahveh’s Spirit pass from me to speak to you?”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You’ll see when you’re hiding in an inner room.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king said, <i>“Take Micaiah to Amon, governor of the city, and to Joash my son. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Tell them the king says to put this man in prison and feed him sparingly with bread and water till I come back safely.’” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Micaiah said, <i>“If you ‘come back safely,’ Yahveh hasn’t spoken by me. Listen </i>to that<i>, all of you.”</i></span></span></p>


### Ahab Killed in Battle


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahab</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> and
<i>Jehoshaphat</i> went up against Ramoth-gilead.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><b><i><sup><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>30</span></sup></i></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahab
</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>told Jehoshaphat, <i>“I’ll disguise myself and go into the battle, but you wear your
</i>royal <i>robes.” </i>So the king of Israel disguised himself and went into battle. </span><span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king of Aram had told the 32 captains of his chariots, <i>“Don’t fight anybody great or small except the king of Israel.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v32" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When the captains saw Jehoshaphat <i>in his royal robes</i>, they said, <i>“That has to be the king of Israel,”</i> and they turned aside to attack him, and he cried out. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they saw it wasn’t the king of Israel, they turned back. <b>                   </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 22:24-33</span></b></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>An <i>Aramean</i> archer drew his bow at random and hit the king of Israel in a joint in the armor. He told the chariot driver, <i>“Turn around and get me out of the fight. I’m seriously wounded.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The battle raged that day; the king was propped up in his chariot in sight of the Arameans and died that evening. The blood from the wound ran down onto the bottom of the chariot. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v36" id="v36"><sup class="verse-num">36</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then a shout went through the army just before sundown, <i>“Every man to his city and country.”</i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The king died; they took him to Samaria and buried him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They washed the chariot by the pool of Samaria. The dogs licked up his blood (</span><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>where the prostitutes took baths</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) like Yahveh had said. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Ahab, the ivory palace he built, the towns he built are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Israel</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His son <u>Ahaziah</u> became king. <b>                             </b></span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 22:34-40</span></b></span></p>


### Jehoshaphat Rules in Judah


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoshaphat Ben-Asa became king in Judah in the <b>fourth year of Ahab</b>, king of Israel.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v42" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was 35 and ruled for 25 years in Jerusalem. His mother was Azubah Bat-Shilhi. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v43" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He followed the ways of Asa in doing what was right, but he didn’t take away the high places. People sacrificed and burned incense there. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v44" id="v44"><sup class="verse-num">44</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He also made peace with the king of Israel. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v45" id="v45"><sup class="verse-num">45</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The rest of the acts of Jehoshaphat, the strength he showed in his rule, the wars he fought, are recorded in <i>The Chronicles of the Kings of Judah</i>. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v46" id="v46"><sup class="verse-num">46</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He expelled from the land the remnant of shrine sodomites from his father’s reign.
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v47" id="v47"><sup class="verse-num">47</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There wasn’t any king in Edom; a deputy served as king. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v48" id="v48"><sup class="verse-num">48</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jehoshaphat built ships from Tarshish to go to Ophir for gold, but they didn’t make it; they wrecked at Ezion-geber. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v49" id="v49"><sup class="verse-num">49</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Ahab’s son Ahaziah said to Jehoshaphat, <i>“Let my men go with yours in the ships.” </i>But Jehoshaphat wouldn’t let them. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v50" id="v50"><sup class="verse-num">50</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He died and they buried him in The City of David. His son <b>Jehoram</b> became king.                                                              </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 22:41-50</span></b></span></p>


### Ahaziah Rules in Israel


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v51" id="v51"><sup class="verse-num">51</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ahaziah became king of Israel in Samaria in the <b>seventeenth year of Jehoshaphat</b>, king of Judah, and ruled for 2 years. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v52" id="v52"><sup class="verse-num">52</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He did evil like his parents and Jeroboam<b><sup>I</sup></b> that got Israel to sin. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v53" id="v53"><sup class="verse-num">53</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He served and worshiped Baal and made Yahveh God of Israel angry like his father had done.                                                                                                                                          </span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 Kg 22:51-53</span></b></span></p>
