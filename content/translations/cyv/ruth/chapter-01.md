## Ruth Chapter 1


### Conversational Yahveh Covenant



### Elimelech’s Family Moves to Moab


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judges were ruling when a famine came over the country. A man from Bethlehem in Judah went with his wife and sons to stay in Moab. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His name was Elimelech, his wife was Naomi, and his sons were Mahlon and Chilion, Ephrathites. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Elimelech died and Naomi was left with her two sons. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They married Moabite wives Orpah and Ruth. They lived there about ten years, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and Mahlon and Chilion died too. So Naomi lost her husband and both sons.                                                                                                       </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ruth 1:1-5</p>


### Naomi and Ruth Return


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6-7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>With her daughters-in-law she started to go back from Moab, because she’d heard that Yahveh had visited his people and given them food. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>She told them, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Both of you, go to your mother’s house. May Yahveh deal graciously with you, as you have with the dead and with me. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>May he grant you to find a home in your husband’s house.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>She kissed them. They cried </span><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and said to her, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“No, we’ll go with you to your people.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Naomi said, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Go back, my daughters. Why go with me? Can I still have sons to become your husbands? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’m too old to have a husband. If I said I have hope, even if I should have a husband tonight and have sons, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>would you wait till they were grown? Would you hold back from marrying? No, it’s really bitter for me that Yahveh has worked against me.”  </span></i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ruth 1:6-13</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then they cried out loud again. Orpah kissed her mother-in-law, but Ruth clung to her. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Naomi said, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>“See, your-sister-in-law has gone back to her people and her gods. Go back with her.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Ruth said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t ask me not to follow you. I’ll stay where you stay. Your people will be my people. Your God will be my God. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ll die where you die and be buried there. Yahveh do so to me and more if even death separates me from you.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>  </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Naomi saw she was determined to go with her, she didn’t say anything more.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The two of them went on to Bethlehem. When they got there, the whole town was stirred. The women said, </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Is this Naomi?”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t call me Naomi, call me Mara. The Almighty has dealt ‘bitterly’ with me. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I went away full; he brought me back empty. Why call me Naomi? Yahveh has made me suffer; the Almighty has brought me misfortune.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>So Naomi came back, and Ruth came with her. They reached Bethlehem at the beginning of barley harvest.<b>     </b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Ruth 1:14-22</p>
