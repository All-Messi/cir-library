## Ecclesiastes Chapter 5

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When you go to God’s Temple, go there to listen rather than offer mindless sacrifice and not realize you’re sinning. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t think impulsively or speak rashly in God’s presence. He’s in heaven, you’re on earth; so don’t say too much. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Working too much fosters false dreams, and talking too much makes you a fool. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When you make a vow to God, don’t delay paying it. He doesn’t delight in fools; pay what you vow. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’d be better not to vow than not to pay it. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t sin in what you say; don’t say in God’s presence that you misspoke. Why have him angry with what you say and destroy your work? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When daydreams grow and words multiply, emptiness comes. Respect God, instead.</span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Eccl 5:1-7</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t be shocked if you see the poor oppressed and justice denied in the realm. One official oversees another official, higher officials oversee <u>them</u>, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and the king that cultivates the land to his own advantage. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>People that love money aren’t satisfied with money; those who love wealth <i>aren’t satisfied</i> with wealth. That’s pointless too. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The more you make, the more people there are that want to get you to spend it. So what’s the advantage of being an owner except to have something to look at? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Working people’s sleep is pleasant, whether they eat a little or a lot. But the full stomach of the rich doesn’t let them sleep.<b>          </b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Eccl 5:8-12</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>There’s a grievous problem I’ve seen under the sun: wealth hoarded to the hoarder’s hurt. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he lost that wealth in a bad venture and had fathered a son, he couldn’t support him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He came naked from his mother’s womb; he’ll go like he came. He won’t take anything with him from his labor. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>That’s a grievous thing: people die like they’re born; so what do they get out of working for the wind? </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Their whole life they eat in the dark, oppressed, sick, and angry.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ve seen something that’s fitting and good: to eat, drink, and enjoy your work under the sun for the few years God gives you; that’s your reward. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It’s also God’s gift to all he’s given wealth. He’s enabled them to eat from it, receive their reward, and enjoy what they do. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They won’t dwell on how long they’re going to live, because God keeps them occupied with happiness.<b>                                             </b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Eccl 5:13-20</p>
