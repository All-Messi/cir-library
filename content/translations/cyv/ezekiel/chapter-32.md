## Ezekiel Chapter 32

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The first day, twelfth month, twelfth year, a message from Yahveh came to me: 2"Son of man, raise a lamentation over Pharaoh, Egypt's king. Tell him, 'You consider yourself a young lion among the nations, but you're like a sea monster in the ocean. You splash around in your rivers, stir up the water with your feet, and muddy the rivers.'"</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The Lord Yahveh says, "I'll cast my net over you along with a host of nations; they'll haul you up from the water.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> I'll dash you on the ground, toss you in an open field, let the birds light on you, and have the animals gorge on you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> I'll scatter your bodies on the mountains, and fill the valleys with your carcasses.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The land will soak up your blood all the way to the mountains; I'll fill the ravines with you.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> When I blot you out, I'll veil the sky, darken the stars, and cover the sun with a cloud; the moon won't shine.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> I'll darken the lights in the sky above you and cover your land in darkness.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> I'll trouble many nations when I take you captive among them to lands you haven't known. 10They'll be appalled at what's happened to you. Because of you their kings will be horribly afraid when I brandish my sword at them. When you fall, they'll shudder every minute for their whole life."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> The Lord Yahveh says, "The sword of Babylon's king will come on you. 12I'll make your large army fall by the swords of powerful men, all of them tyrants over nations. They'll crush Egypt's pride and destroy its army.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> I'll destroy its livestock from beside the waterways; nobody's foot or animal's hoof will muddy them anymore.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> I'll make their waterways soak up the water and make their streams flow like olive oil."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> When I make Egypt desolate and the land lacking in what filled it, when I strike everybody that lives there, they'll know I'm Yahveh.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> That's the dirge the nations will chant over Egypt and its army."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> The fifteenth day, first month, twelfth year, a message from Yahveh came to me: 18"Son of man, wail for Egypt's army. Along with the powerful nations, take it down to the nether world with those who go down to the Pit. 19'Who are you more beautiful than? Go make your bed with the uncircumcised.'</span>
