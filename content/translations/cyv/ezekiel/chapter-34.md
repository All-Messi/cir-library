## Ezekiel Chapter 34

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> A message from Yahveh came to me: 2"Son of man, prophesy against Israel's shepherds. 'The Lord Yahveh says, "Misfortune awaits you, shepherds that feed yourselves! Shouldn't shepherds feed the flock? 3You eat the healthy and dress with their wool. You slaughter the healthy sheep without feeding the flock. 4You don't strengthen the sickly, heal the diseased, bandage up the crippled, bring back the scattered, or look for the lost. With force and severity, you bully them. 5They're scattered all over the world because they didn't have a shepherd; they became prey for wild animals. 6My flock wandered around in the mountains and tall hills; nobody looked for them. 7'So, shepherds, listen to Yahveh, [8-9] 10"Give me my sheep. You're fired. I'll protect my flock so you can't eat them anymore."'"</span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> The Lord Yahveh says, "I'll look for my sheep myself. 12Like a shepherd does when he's among them, I'll bring them back from where they scattered on a cloudy, gloomy day. 13-14I'll gather them from other countries, feed them on good pasture, and graze them on Israel's high mountains, by its streams, in populated places. 15I'll lead my flock to rest, 16bandage up the crippled, doctor the sick, watch over the healthy and strong. I'll feed them with justice."</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> The Lord Yahveh says, "My flock, I'll decide between one sheep and another, between rams and billy goats. 18Do you care so little about feeding on good pasture that you tramp it down? Do you care so little about drinking clean water that you muddy it up with your hooves? 19My flock has to eat what you tramp down and drink what you muddy up!"</span>

I've said so. 25"I'll make a peace treaty with them and get rid of dangerous animals so they can be safe in the countryside and sleep in the woods. 26I'll make them and the places around my mountain a blessing.
