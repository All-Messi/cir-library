## Ezekiel Chapter 10


### Yahveh’s Glory Leaves the Temple


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then in the expanse above the cherubs, I saw something like a sapphire stone shaped like a throne. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He spoke to the man dressed in linen, <i>“Go between the turning wheels under the cherubs and fill your hands with coals. Scatter them over the city.”</i> I watched him go in.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The cherubs were standing on the right side of the Temple when he went in, and a cloud filled the inner court. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The glory of Yahveh rose up from the cherubs to the threshold of the Temple. The cloud filled the Temple, and the bright glory of Yahveh filled the court. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The cherubs’ wings could be heard all the way to the outer court—like God Almighty when he speaks.</span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>All four looked alike as if one wheel was inside another wheel. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they moved they went in any of four directions without turning from the direction they faced. </span></span>

<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I heard the wheels called “whirling wheels.” </span></span>
