## Judges Chapter 5

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Deborah and Barak sang that day, 2"Bless Yahveh! because the leaders led in Israel, the people volunteered.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Hear, you kings; listen, you officials; I'll sing to Yahveh; I'll praise Israel's God.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Yahveh, when you went out from Seir, when you marched from Edom, the ground quaked; the sky dripped; yes, the clouds dripped water.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> The mountains shook in front of Yahveh; you, Sinai, shook in front of Israel's God. 6"In the days of Shamgar Ben-Anath, in the days of Jael, caravans ceased; travelers kept to the back roads.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> The rural people ceased in Israel; they ceased till you arose, Deborah, as a mother in Israel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> When the people chose new gods, war was in the gates. Not a shield or spear was seen among 40,000 in Israel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> My heart goes out to Israel's commanders, who volunteered willingly among the people. Bless Yahveh. 10"Sing, you that ride white donkeys, who sit on rich carpets and travel on the highway.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> The remnant of the nobles came down; Yahveh's people came down for him against the powerful.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> They set out to the valley from Ephraim; they followed you, Benjamin, with your kin. The commanders marched down from Machir; from Zebulun they marched who carry the marshal's staff.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> The stars fought from the sky; from their courses they fought Sisera.</span>
