## 1 Samuel Chapter 26


### David Spares Saul’s Life Again


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The people of Ziph came to Saul at Gibeah and said, <i>“Isn’t David hiding on the Hill of Hachilah east of Jeshimon?” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul went down to the Wilderness of Ziph with 3,000 chosen men to look for him. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He camped on the Hill of Hachilah beside the road east of Jeshimon. David was staying in the open country. When he saw that Saul had come after him, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>he sent out spies to make sure that Saul was coming. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He went to where Saul and his men had camped and saw where he lay beside Abner Ben-Ner, his general. The army was asleep around him. </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David said to Ahimelech the Hittite and Abishai Ben-Zeruiah, Joab’s brother, <i>“Who will go down with me to Saul in the camp?” </i>Abishai said, <i>“I’ll go.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They went down at night. Saul was sleeping inside the camp, with his spear stuck in the ground by his head.<b>     </b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">1 Sam 26:1-7</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abishai told David, <i>“God has delivered your enemy to you. Let me strike him with the spear to the ground with one stroke. I won’t strike him a second time.”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But David said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Don’t destroy him. Who can kill Yahveh’s anointed and not be guilty? </span></i><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As sure as Yahveh lives, he’ll strike him, or he’ll die, or he’ll go down in battle. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh forbid that I should kill him. Take the spear by his head and the jug of water, and let’s go.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David left with the spear and jug. Nobody saw or knew anything. Nobody woke up. A sound sleep from Yahveh had fallen on them all.<b>        </b></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">1 Sam 26:8-12</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David crossed back over to the other side and stood on top of the hill a good way off. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He shouted to the army and to Abner, <i>“Won’t you answer, Abner?”</i> </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Abner said, <i>“Who are you, shouting to the king?”</i>
</span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David asked him<i>, </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Aren’t you a man? Who is like you in Israel? Why not protect the king? Someone came over to destroy him. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>What you’ve done isn’t good. As sure as Yahveh lives, you all need to die; you didn’t protect Yahveh’s anointed. See the king’s spear and the jug of water that was by his head.”</span></i><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                            1 Sam 26:13-16</span></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>  </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul recognized David’s voice, <i>“Is that you, my son?” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'> </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height: 115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David said,<i> </i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“It’s me, my lord the king. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Why are you chasing me? What have I done? </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If Yahveh has stirred you up against me, let him accept an offering. But if it’s people, they’re accursed by Yahveh because they’ve driven me out from Yahveh’s inheritance and told me to go serve other gods. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Don’t let my blood fall to the ground away from Yahveh’s presence. You’ve come out to look for a single flea, like somebody hunting a partridge in the hills.”</span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul said, <i>“I’ve sinned. Go on </i>back home<i>. I won’t try to harm you again because you’ve valued my life today. I’ve played the fool and made a serious mistake.” </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David answered, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“The king’s spear! Have one of the young men come and get it. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh will repay each man for his goodness and faithfulness. He delivered you to me, and I refused to reach out against you. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As I highly valued your life today, so may Yahveh highly value my life and deliver me from all distress.” </span></i></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul said, <i>“You’re blessed, son. You’ll accomplish a lot and certainly end up winning.” </i></span></span></p>
