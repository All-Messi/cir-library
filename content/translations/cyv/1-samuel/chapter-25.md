## 1 Samuel Chapter 25


### The Death of Samuel


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Samuel died and Israel gathered to mourn for him. (</span><span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>His wife Abigail was intelligent and beautiful, but he was harsh and rude in his dealings, a descendant of Caleb.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)

<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David heard about it in the open country. </span></span>

<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I’ve heard you have shearers. <span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ask your young men and they’ll tell you. <span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But Nabal answered, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Who’s David? <p class="section-ref">1 Sam 25:9-13</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“David sent messengers from the countryside to greet our master, and he scorned them. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yet the men were good to us. <span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They were a wall to us day and night the whole time we tended sheep together. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Think what you should do; </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%; font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David<i>’s planning to do something bad to our master and his household. <p class="section-ref">1 Sam 25:14-17</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Abigail hurried and loaded donkeys with 200 loaves of bread, 2 wineskins of wine, 5 sheep already fixed, a bushel of roasted grain, 100 clusters of raisins, and 200 cakes of figs. </span></span>

<span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As she was riding her donkey down under cover of the mountain, she met David and his men. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David had said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“For nothing I’ve guarded everything this man has on the open range so that he hasn’t missed anything. </span></b></span></span></p>

She fell on her face at his feet, bowed to the ground, and said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Put the blame on me, my lord. <span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>As sure as Yahveh lives and you live, since he’s restrained you from shedding blood to avenge yourself, may your enemies be like Nabal. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Give this gift to the young men. </span></i></span>

<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>If anybody attacks you, you’ll be in the bundle of the living with Yahveh your God. <span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he keeps his promise and appoints you ruler over Israel, </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v31" id="v31"><sup class="verse-num">31</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>what’s happened won’t cause you grief for having shed blood unnecessarily to avenge yourself. <p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Bless Yahveh God of Israel, who sent you. </span></i><span class="verse verse-v33" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Bless you for your discretion. <span class="verse verse-v34" id="v34"><sup class="verse-num">34</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Nevertheless,<i> as sure as Yahveh lives, who kept me from harming you, if you hadn’t come so soon, I wouldn’t have left Nabal one male by morning.”  </i></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v35" id="v35"><sup class="verse-num">35</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David accepted what she’d brought. <i>“Go home on good terms. </b></span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v37" id="v37"><sup class="verse-num">37</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he’d sobered up, she told him everything. His heart died; he became like a stone. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v38" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Some ten days later, Yahveh struck him, and he died.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v39" id="v39"><sup class="verse-num">39</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When David heard Nabal was dead, he said, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Bless Yahveh, who pleaded the cause of his insult to me, and kept me from doing something bad. <p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>Then David sent a proposal to Abigail to be his wife. </span><span class="verse verse-v40" id="v40"><sup class="verse-num">40</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When his servants came to her at Carmel, they said, <i>“David has sent us to you to take you as his wife.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v41" id="v41"><sup class="verse-num">41</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>She got up and bowed to the ground, <i>“I’ll wash his servants’ feet.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v42" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>She got up quick and rode a donkey with her five maids attending her. She followed David’s messengers and became his wife. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v43" id="v43"><sup class="verse-num">43</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David had also taken Ahinoam of Jezreel; they both became his wives. </span></span>
