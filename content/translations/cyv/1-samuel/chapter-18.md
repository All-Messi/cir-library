## 1 Samuel Chapter 18


### Saul’s Jealousy of David


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When he’d finished talking to Saul, Jonathan was drawn to David and loved him as himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul took him that day and didn’t let him go back home. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jonathan made a pact with him because he loved him as himself. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jonathan took off his robe and gave it to him, along with his armor, sword, bow, and belt. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David went out wherever Saul sent him and was successful. Saul set him over the fighting men. The people and Saul liked that arrangement.</span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When David came back from killing the Philistine, the women came out of the towns, singing and dancing, to meet King Saul with tambourines, celebration, and musical instruments. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>They sang as they played, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>         </span></b><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“Saul has slain his thousands,</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in; margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            David his ten thousands!”</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>It made Saul mad. <i>“They’ve attributed ten thousands to David, but thousands to me. What more can he have but the kingdom?”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He was suspicious of David after that.                                                                                                           </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">1 Sam 18:1-9</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in;'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span><span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The next day a tormenting spirit from God came powerfully over Saul, and he raved in the house while David was playing the harp as usual. Saul had a spear in his hand </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v11" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt; line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and threw it, thinking he’d pin <i>David</i> to the wall, but David dodged him twice. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul was afraid of him, because Yahveh was with him, but he’d left Saul. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul put him out away from him and appointed him as commander of a thousand.  <i>David</i> came and went with the people. [</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v14" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14-15</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>]
</span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v16" id="v16"><sup class="verse-num">16</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>All Israel and Judah loved him.</span></span></p>


### David Marries Saul’s Daughter


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then Saul told David<i>, “Here is my older daughter Merab. I’ll give her to you as a wife. Just be brave for me and fight Yahveh’s battles.”</i> Saul was thinking, <i>“I won’t be against him; the Philistines will.” </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David said, <i>“Who am I and what is my life or my father’s family in Israel, that I should be the king’s son-in-law?”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>At the time she would have been given to David, she was given to Adriel of Meholah.          </span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">1 Sam 18:10-19</p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v20" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Now Saul’s daughter Michal was in love with David. They told Saul, and he liked that. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v21" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He thought, <i>“I’ll give her to him as a snare. The Philistines will oppose him.”</i> Saul told David a second time, <i>“You can be my son-in-law today.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v22" id="v22"><sup class="verse-num">22</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He told his men, <i>“Tell David privately, ‘The king likes you, and we do too. Go ahead and become his son-in-law.’”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v23" id="v23"><sup class="verse-num">23</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When Saul’s men told David that privately, he said, <i>“Does it seem trivial to you to become the king’s son-in-law? I’m a poor man with no reputation.”</i> </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v24" id="v24"><sup class="verse-num">24</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul’s men reported what David said. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v25" id="v25"><sup class="verse-num">25</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Saul said, <i>“Tell him the only dowry I want is 100 Philistine foreskins to avenge me of my enemies.”</i> Saul thought he’d get David killed by the Philistines. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v26" id="v26"><sup class="verse-num">26</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When his men told David, he was glad to be the king’s son-in-law. Before the deadline, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v27" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David along with his men went and killed 200 Philistines and brought their foreskins to the king. So Saul gave him Michal. </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v28" id="v28"><sup class="verse-num">28</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But when Saul saw that Yahveh was with him and all Israel loved him, </span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v29" id="v29"><sup class="verse-num">29</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>he was more afraid of him and became his constant enemy.  <b>                                     </b></span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-bottom:0in; '><span class="verse verse-v30" id="v30"><sup class="verse-num">30</sup> <span style='font-size: 12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The commanders of the Philistines kept attacking. As often as they did, David had more success than any other officer Saul had; so he was greatly respected.
</span></span></p>
