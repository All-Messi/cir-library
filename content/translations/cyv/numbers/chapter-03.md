## Numbers Chapter 3

<span class="verse" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The Generations of Aaron and Moses When Yahveh Spoke to Moses on Mount Sinai 	<strong>Duties of Levites</strong></span>

<span class="verse" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> The sons of Aaron were Nadab, the firstborn, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar—<strong><sup>3</sup></strong>anointed and ordained as priests. <strong><sup>4</sup></strong>In the Wilderness of Sinai, Nadab and Abihu died from offering before Yahveh fire different from what he’d commanded. They didn’t have any children, so Eleazar and Ithamar served as priests during Aaron’s lifetime.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Yahveh told Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>“</em><em>Set</em><em> the tribe of Levi </em><em>aside</em><em> </em><em>to serve Aaron</em><em>.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>7</sup></strong><em>They’ll</em><em> perform duties for him and the whole </em><em>community</em><em> </em><em>at</em><em> the </em><em>Tent of Meeting</em><em>.</em><em> </em><em>They’ll service the </em><em>T</em><em>abernacle </em><strong><sup>8</sup></strong><em>and</em><em> keep the furnishing</em><em>s</em><em> of the </em><em>Tent of Meeting</em><em> in assisting with the duties of the </em><em>people</em><em>.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>9</sup></strong><em>G</em><em>ive</em><em> </em><em>the Levites to Aaron and his sons.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>10</sup></strong><em>A</em><em>ppoint</em><em> Aaron and his sons to </em><em>carry out the duties of the</em><em> priesthood</em><em>;</em><em> execute any layman that </em><em>tries to come</em><em> near.”</em></span>

<span class="verse" id="v11"><sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Again Yahveh told Moses,</span>

<span class="verse" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <em>“I’ve</em><em> taken the Levites </em><em>from</em><em> the </em><em>people in place of the firstborn</em><em>.</em><em> </em><strong><sup>13</sup></strong><em>The</em><em> firstborn</em><em> </em><em>belong</em><em> to me</em><em>.</em><em> When</em><em> I struck </em><em>down the firstborn</em><em> in Egypt, I</em><em> set apart to myself the firstborn</em><em> </em><em>people and animals in Israel</em><em>.</em><em> </em><em>The Levites </em><em>belong to me;</em><em> </em><em><u>I’m </u></em><em><u>Yahveh</u></em><em>.”</span>
<p class="section-ref">Num 3:5-13</p>

<span class="verse" id="v14"><sup class="verse-num">14</sup> Yahveh told Moses in the Wilderness of Sinai, <strong><sup>15</sup></strong><em>“</em><em>Count</em><em> the </em><em>descendant</em><em>s of Levi by </em><em>families—</em><em>every male a month old and up.”</em> <strong><sup>16</sup></strong>Moses counted them:</span>

<span class="verse" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> the sons of Levi: Gershon, Kohath, and Merari.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> the clans descended from Gershon: Libni and Shimei.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> the clans descended from Kohath: Amram, Izhar, Hebron, and Uzziel.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v20"><sup class="verse-num">20</sup> the clans descended from Merari: Mahli and Mushi.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Num 3:14-20</p>

<span class="verse" id="v21"><sup class="verse-num">21</sup> The Libnites and Shimeites descended from <u>Gershon</u>. <strong><sup>22</sup></strong>Their males a month old and up numbered 7,500. <strong><sup>23</sup></strong>They camped west of the Tabernacle, behind it. <strong><sup>24</sup></strong>Eliasaph Ben- Lael was family leader among the Gershonites. <strong><sup>25</sup></strong>The sons of Gershon serviced the Tent of Meeting, including the Tabernacle and tent, its covering, the screen for the door to the Tent of Meeting, <strong><sup>26</sup></strong>the hangings around the court, and the screen for the door to the court around the Tabernacle, the altar, and its cords.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Num 3:21-26</p>

<span class="verse" id="v27"><sup class="verse-num">27</sup> The Amramites, Izharites, Hebronites, and Uzzielites descended from <u>Kohath</u>. <strong><sup>28</sup></strong>Their males a month old and up numbered 8,600; they serviced the sanctuary. <strong><sup>29</sup></strong>They camped south of the Tabernacle. <strong><sup>30</sup></strong>Elizaphan Ben-Uzziel was family leader among the Kohathites. <strong><sup>31</sup></strong>The sons of Kohath serviced the Ark, the table, the lampstand, the altars, the utensils of the sanctuary they perform service with, and the screen.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v32"><sup class="verse-num">32</sup> Eleazar Ben-Aaron, the chief leader from Levi, oversaw the ones that performed duties in the sanctuary.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Num 3:27-32</p>

<span class="verse" id="v33"><sup class="verse-num">33</sup> The Mahlites and Mushites descended from <u>Merari</u>. <strong><sup>34</sup></strong>Their males a month old and up numbered 6,200. <strong><sup>35</sup></strong>Zuriel Ben-Abihail was family leader of Merari. They camped north of the Tabernacle. <strong><sup>36</sup></strong>The sons of Merari serviced the frames of the Tabernacle, its bars, posts, bases, equipment, <strong><sup>37</sup></strong>together with the posts around the court with their bases, pegs, and cords.</span>

<span class="verse" id="v38"><sup class="verse-num">38</sup> Moses, Aaron, and his sons camped east of the Tabernacle and performed duties in the sanctuary for the obligation of Israel. Any layman that would come near was to be executed. <strong><sup>39</sup></strong>The Levites that Moses and Aaron counted a month old and up numbered 22,000.</span>
<p class="section-ref">Num 3:33-39</p>

<span class="verse" id="v42"><sup class="verse-num">42</sup> Moses counted Israel’s firstborn: <strong><sup>43</sup></strong>22,273 males a month old and up.</span>
