## Numbers Chapter 19


### Water of Purification


<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-left:4.5pt; '><span class="verse verse-v1" id="v1"><sup class="verse-num">1</sup> <span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Yahveh told Moses and Aaron, </span></span></p>

<p class="MsoListBulletCxSpMiddle" style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:.5in; margin-bottom:10.0pt;margin-left:.5in;'><span class="verse verse-v2" id="v2"><sup class="verse-num">2</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“This is a statute of Yahveh, ‘Tell the Israelites to bring you an unblemished red heifer that’s never worn a yoke. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v3" id="v3"><sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Give it to Eleazar to take outside the camp and slaughter in his presence. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v4" id="v4"><sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>He’ll take some of its blood with his finger and sprinkle it toward the front of the Tent of Meeting seven times. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v5" id="v5"><sup class="verse-num">5</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then the heifer will be burned in front of him—hide, meat, blood, and refuse. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v6" id="v6"><sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The priest will take cedar wood, hyssop, and scarlet material, and throw it onto the burning heifer. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v7" id="v7"><sup class="verse-num">7</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then he’ll wash his clothes, take a bath, and afterward come into the camp, but he’ll be ceremonially unclean till evening. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v8" id="v8"><sup class="verse-num">8</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The one that burns it will also wash his clothes, take a bath, and be unclean till evening. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v9" id="v9"><sup class="verse-num">9</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A man that’s clean will gather the heifer’s ashes and deposit them outside the camp in a clean place, and the community will keep it as water for removing impurity from sin. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v10" id="v10"><sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The one that gathers the ashes will wash his clothes and be unclean till evening. A permanent statute for Israelites and foreigners living among them.                        </span></i></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Num 19:1-10</p>

<span class="verse verse-v12" id="v12"><sup class="verse-num">12</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Purify yourself with the water on the third and seventh days, and you’ll be clean. If you don’t, you’re still unclean; </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v13" id="v13"><sup class="verse-num">13</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>you’re defiling Yahveh’s Tabernacle, and you’re excluded from Israel.</span></i></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v15" id="v15"><sup class="verse-num">15</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Every open container will be unclean.                                                                                                                 </span></i></span></span></p>

<span class="verse verse-v17" id="v17"><sup class="verse-num">17</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then for the unclean person they’ll take some ashes from the burnt purification from sin and add flowing water in a basin. </span></i></span>
<span class="verse verse-v18" id="v18"><sup class="verse-num">18</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A clean person will dip hyssop in the water and sprinkle </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>it<i> on the tent, its furnishings, the people that were there, and the one that touched the bone, the one slain, the one dead </i>of natural causes, <i>or the grave. </i></span></span>
<span class="verse verse-v19" id="v19"><sup class="verse-num">19</sup> <i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Then the clean </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family: "Times New Roman",serif'>person<i> will sprinkle the unclean </i>persons <i>on the third and seventh days. On the seventh day the clean purify the unclean. Then they’ll wash their clothes, take a bath, and be ceremonially clean by evening.                                                  </i></span></span></p>

<p class="section-ref">Num 19:16-19</p>
