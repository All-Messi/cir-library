<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>GAMALIEL’S
ARGUMENT FOR THE RESURRECTION OF JESUS</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Virgil
Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The
resurrection of Jesus provides a plausible explanation for his followers’
continued and growing zeal for claiming him as the Messiah although he had been
killed. Messiah indeed “abides forever” as per the Old Testament witness, but
killing him does not necessarily eliminate him from that role if he
resurrected, especially if he arose and ascended to God with the understanding
that he was to return and pick up the Messianic work in whatever fashion that proves
to be. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>I.
Recorded Elements of Gamaliel’s Speech Incorporated into the Above Synthesis:
Acts<b> 5</b>:33-42</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            A.
Gamaliel was a Pharisee, a believer in resurrection.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>B.
Judas and Theudas examples </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Claiming to be “somebody”
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>suggests a
Messianic claimant more than just a zealot leader.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            They must
have been at least zealot movements if not Messianic ones.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            The
Romans presumably took them out.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When they were killed,
their movements fell apart. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            C.
Put them forth <u>a little while</u> (“time may tell”)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Time
may clarify whether the Jesus movement will turn militant.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        The
Jesus movement may prove to be something perhaps compatible with </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>national Judaism
(cp. the Judaizers’ conceptualization later).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jesus might soon
return as claimed; after that, the Jewish national expectancy </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>might come into
play.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            D.
If from people, it will be overthrown.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Romans
will take care of it. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            E.
If from God, you will not overthrow it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The need to
entertain the possibility of resurrection and its contribution to making </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>sense of the
anomaly the religious leaders were witnessing</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        The
need to see if the Messianic “kingdom” could be figurative (John <b>18</b>:36-37)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        The
need to consider whether a suffering Messiah is a prophetic possibility</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            F.
Be <u>careful</u> what you do to these men.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Do
not get crosswise with the Romans.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Do
not incur God’s displeasure if the Jesus movement is legitimate.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            G.
Lest you find yourselves fighting against God </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A comment
especially suited to Sadducees</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>II.
Additional Contextual Considerations</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            A.
Christianity is a person-centered system. Remove the center, remove the
movement.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            B.
Messiah abides forever: John <b>12</b>:34 &lt; Daniel <b>7</b>:13-14; Ezekiel <b>37</b>:25;
Psalm <b>110</b>:4). Christ crucified was a major stumbling block to Jewish
acceptance of Jesus as Messiah (1 Corinthians <b>1</b>:23). Defending the
“suffering motif” for Messianic expectancy received considerable attention
prophetically in Jesus’ teaching to the disciples (Matthew <b>16</b>:21-23, <i>etc</i>.);
the Emmaus instruction (Luke <b>24</b>:26; Isaiah <b>53</b>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>C.
The strong Sadducean objection to the Christian proclamation about resurrection:
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Acts
<b>4</b>:1-4 (healing of the lame man at Gate Beautiful; <i>“grieved that they
were </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>preaching
resurrection in connection with Jesus”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) + apostles’ signs and </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>wonders and Sadducees’ “jealousy”
(Acts <b>5</b>:17-18); the apostles’ testimony about Jesus’ resurrection (Acts <b>4</b>:33).
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            D.
Recent notable miracles: lame man, series of noted miracles (Acts <b>5</b>:12-16),
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>mysterious release
of apostles from prison the previous night (Acts <b>5</b>:17-</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>23). Those reports were on top of the
resurrection of Lazarus and the report of the guard at Jesus’ tomb (Matthew <b>27</b>:62-66
+ <b>28</b>:11-15).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            E.
Boldness of common men against national leadership: Acts <b>4</b>:13, 19; <b>5</b>:29-32</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            F.
Roman strictures on executions done by the Jews themselves: John<b> 18</b>:31-32,
<i>etc</i>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            G.
Apostles’ willingness to risk suffering to the point of death: <i>“people will
not suffer </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>unto
death for what they know is false.” </span></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            H.
The non-military character of “the Jesus movement” (at least for the present) in
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>       
                            contrast to the Judas and Theudas examples</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        The
Jesus movement is not following the historical <u>pattern</u>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The Jesus movement
is not following the <u>expected pattern</u>: killing the man </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>should have shown
he was not the Messiah.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>III.
Back-Offs Suggested</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Preliminary
note: Any objection to the synthesis above should deal with <u>all</u> the
factors in Gamaliel’s speech in a consistent alternative synthesis. Suggesting
a different take on this or that point taken individually falls short of what
is necessary to remove the power of the synthesis.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A.
Let God take care of it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Gamaliel’s
listeners may find themselves fighting against God. He envisions something in
the Jesus movement that is plausible. Besides, God’s “taking care of something”
may be indirect through his followers, who are to redouble their efforts rather
than <i>“let go and let God.”</i> Lots of evil in the world God does not take
care of.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            B.
If it is evil, it will not last. Evil tends to self-destruct. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
1.0in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Gamaliel
is not necessarily speaking about a general historical principle. He is speaking
in the context of the Messianic expectation. To everyone acquainted with the current
Christian proclamation, it was obvious that his followers were presenting Jesus
as Messiah, not just as a prophet or reformer comparable to John the Baptist. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
1.0in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Besides,
Gamaliel’s plea is <i>“for a little while.” </i>The self-destruction of evil
may play itself out over decades or centuries. If it is not “sufficiently
false,” it may not self-destruct at all. From a Christian perspective, Eastern
religions, Islam, and Judaism illustrate long-lived movements that, from a
Christian perspective, are not true as total systems.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            C.
By every conceivable point he could muster, Gamaliel was trying to dampen
exuberant Jewish nationalism, and so incur the wrath of Rome. The nation
already had enough of an anti-Roman reputation; it did not need to get caught
up in what, to the Roman eye, would look like infighting. The wrath of Rome was
a powerful deterrent. Rome might decide to come and <i>“take away our place and
nation”</i> (John <b>11</b>:48). Caiaphas, the presumed leader of the present
persecution, made that very comment. (It also turned out to be predictive, if
it is true that the sect of the Sadducees ceased to exist after the destruction
of the temple in A.D. 70.) The Jews might get by with <i>“killing one man to
save the nation,”</i> but a general persecution and killing spree is another
story.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>              
         Under this heading could come something of a response to the
“preliminary note” above. Since Gamaliel is addressing a diverse audience,
different “reasons” could take aim at different “reasoners” without necessarily
participating in an in-concert alternative to this synthesis. Some points might
deter fellow Pharisees in the Sanhedrin that had not become “secret disciples”
of Jesus. Resurrection as a solution to a dead eternal-Messiah might have value
with such. Likewise, “the fighting against God.” The Romans-oriented points
might speak to the Sadducees, especially the ones that controlled the Sanhedrin.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        The
damper stands with fellow Pharisees because resurrection does answer the
suffering Messiah issue—as reinforced by the predictive prophecy that spoke to
that point, by the extreme number and kinds of miracles in and after Jesus’
ministry, and by the inherent power of the salvation-from-sin message in
Messiah’s purpose for coming at all.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> 
                      The damper stands with the Sadducees because of the
unbelievable resistance to the reports and observations of extreme miracle
during and after Jesus’ ministry, particularly the resurrection of Lazarus and the
report of the guards from Jesus’ tomb. People today might mot feel persuaded by
the reports because our perspective depends on records we could claim are
unreliable. But the “unreliable-report” approach would not work with contemporaries
so close to the events. They were too many and too extreme to make that other
than obscurantism. The Jesus movement was not following the expected pattern,
and it did not have a militant character. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Gamaliel’s
comments are not “merely” dampers. They would not have any “dampening” power if
they were not true as part of the synthesis above.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Gamaliel
may not have had in mind all that we have attributed to his line of thought. His
time perspective was not as good as ours. The “drag” of his previous
convictions as a Jewish Pharisee would have made it more difficult to
appreciate the power of the points he himself was making. Nevertheless, we
should have confidence now; the fact that we are here shows we should be here. The
empty-tomb resurrection of Jesus and the existence of his body the church stand
or fall together.</span></p>
