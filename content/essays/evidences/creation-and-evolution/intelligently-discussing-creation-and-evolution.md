<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>INTELLIGENTLY DISCUSSING CREATION AND EVOLUTION</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The
presentation here is not for <u>solving</u> the creation-evolution problem, but
for <u>clarifying</u> the issue and <u>correcting</u> inappropriate thinking
patterns.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Identifying
the Issue</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Creationism
as such vs. the Christian formulations of it. There is a difference between
creation as a concept and the Judaeo-Christian—or even Islamic—formulation of
it. The Christian formulation gets into additional matters, which vary
according to the manner interpreters handle the relevant biblical materials.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Chronometry.
Creationism <i>vs</i>. evolution does not particularly correspond with
questions about the age of the universe. The latter point is also important,
but a young universe does not necessarily parallel creationism. Evolution calls
for an ancient universe, so any indication that the universe is young does
argue against evolution and for creationism; but creationism can occur over a
long time as well as take place in a relatively short time. It is a more
flexible concept in regard to chronometry.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Process
<i>vs</i>. origin. The controversy is not about the origin of base matter <i>per
se</i>, but about how the base level came to its present state. The origination
of matter could be called creation all right, which could then be followed by
evolution. Evolution does not necessitate materialism or atheism even though it
allows for such viewpoints.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            How
much intervention is also not the point in creationism. Whether frequently or
infrequently, divine intervention as such is all that is required.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            History
<i>vs</i>. science. Even if macro-evolution is possible, that does not prove
that is what happened. The essential issue is one of history rather than
science. Science cannot deal with historical, universals, or supernatural
matters as such.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Definition
of Terms</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            To
think intelligently about creation-evolution, the terms “evolution” and
“creation” need to correspond to the natural contrasts in the realities
involved. The distinctive difference between the two approaches is that
creation appeals to agency outside the natural order; evolution does not. Evolution
envisions the development from beginning to present as a process that can be
carried forward solely by laws internal to the natural order. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            As
a result, “creation” should not, for example, be used to mean simply bringing
things to present conditions, but as referring more specifically to bringing
things to present conditions by exogenous causation. In this light, “creating
by evolution” is a nonsensical expression. Likewise, “evolution” should not be
used to designate a process like the evolution of the Corvette. The process of
change in such a case, appeals to external causation—the designers of the
vehicle.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Neither
should “creation” mean the origination of mass-energy <i>per se</i>. That would
allow for evolution as the means for carrying the process forward to current
levels. However, the precise point in creationism is that mechanisms internal
to the natural order are insufficient for doing that very thing. Both above
concepts could fall within the meaning of “theistic evolution.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A
distinction is needed between micro- and macro-evolution. Micro-evolution
refers to observable change; macro-evolution proposes to extrapolate changes of
degree into changes of kind.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Law
of Parsimony</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The
simpler explanation must also be a sufficient explanation. If simpler means
fewer elements involved harmoniously in the process, evolution is simpler
causation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Burden
of Proof</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The
task is not to disprove evolution, but to discover which explanation is more
likely.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Certainty</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            As
with all real-world matters, the quest is not for absolute certainty, but
greater likelihood.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The
crucial issue in evolution is the source of the truly new. Creationism has mind
and power to penetrate the future and provide the new; evolution has to provide
an alternative, endogenous origin of the new.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Gestalt</span></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Can
we seriate the origin of interdependent parts? Can a mechanic change a
Harley-Davidson into a Lincoln Continental and keep it running the whole time? Even
if that could be done, it would be illustrating creationism rather than
evolution.</span></p>
