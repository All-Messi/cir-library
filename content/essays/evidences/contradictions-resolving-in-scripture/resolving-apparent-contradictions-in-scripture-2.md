<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>RESOLVING APPARENT CONTRADICTIONS IN SCRIPTURE</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Resolving apparent contradictions in
scripture calls for methods of interpretation. The approach assumes the
inerrancy of scripture; brain teasers often become paradoxes in wording rather
than contradictions in reality. The approach also assumes that scripture is
divine in such a way that makes it harmonious throughout. The mechanisms
available for harmonizing scriptures with each other and with experience amount
to the same things as the mechanisms used for properly interpreting human
language. The same things we do to clear up ambiguities we do to resolve
difficulties. Apparent difficulties normally arise because either or both sides
of the dilemma are unclear, but people do not realize that at the time they
sense a problem with the text. The following list is a summary of mechanisms
for resolving discrepancies.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      1.    Is there a <u>textual problem</u> in one
of the verses? English Bibles often give marginal notations where significant
textual issues occur.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      2.    Is there a <u>translation problem</u> in
any of the references? Checking different translations can be helpful.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      3.    Is there a difference between the <u>meanings
of terms</u>? Sometimes a shift has occurred in the English word at two points as
evidenced by a difference in Greek or Hebrew words. Comparing translations may
help identify this problem.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      4.    Is there a <u>literal-figurative variable</u>
that is responsible for the tension between texts? The literal-figurative
distinction relates to the genre issue, whether there may be exaggeration for
emphasis, and the like.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      5.    Is there a <u>technical-non-technical
variable</u> in the terms?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      6.    Could there be a <u>restrictive-non-restrictive
variable</u> in the wording?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      7.    Do the contexts have a different <u>frame
of reference</u>? Are they addressing the same <u>topic</u> in both places?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      8.    Are there different <u>intended contrasts</u>?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      9.    Is <u>allegorizing the comparison</u> what
sets up a problem with other scriptures?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      10.  What possibility exists for confusing <u>sequence
of presentation and sequence in the referent</u>—whether of chronological
order, logical order (importance, conditions), <i>etc</i>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      11.  Can an <u>inappropriate model</u> be the
source of the difficulty?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      12.  Is there a <u>difference in idiom</u>
between biblical languages and modern English, which then creates an
unconscious misunderstanding of one of the passages? Words of particular
importance here include <i>all</i>, <i>is</i>, <i>can</i>(<i>not</i>), <i>necessary</i>,
<i>forever</i>, <i>prophecy</i>, <i>fulfill</i>, <i>give</i>, <i>nature</i>, <i>judge</i>.
There could also be linguistic interchange of nature, state, and action terms.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      13.  If the issue is over something to be done,
there may be an <u>advice-commandment</u> <u>distinction</u> at work.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      14.  What difference would a <u>permissive-prescriptive</u>
and/or <u>direct-indirect variable</u> bring to the texts?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      15.  A <u>general <i>vs</i>. absolute variable</u>
may be at work, with one statement being a generality and another that deals
with an exception assumed in the generality statement. Implied <u>cultural
limitation</u> applies to this point. Perhaps one context is addressing a
specific <u>unusual situation</u> that is an exception to the rule, or
generality, stated by the other. One may be a negligible matter of <u>degree</u>
that the other overlooks.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      16.  Is <u>relative negative</u> possible in one
of the references?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      17.  Is a <u>different audience/circumstance</u>
envisioned? The point includes dispensational distinctions.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      18.  Sometimes it helps to compare translations,
and commentaries and specialized treatments are useful in seeing through
paradoxes. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            On problem passages, we do well to work
out more than one solution. (1) Sometimes the various solutions we can come up
with are aspects of one larger answer. (2) Later we may discover that one
solution does not work for reasons we did not realize at the time we made it. We
still have the others to fall back on if we have seen more than one way of
handling it. That increases our confidence in the text. Besides, (3) it gives us
a sense of objectivity if more than one option is available in ambiguous cases.</span></p>
