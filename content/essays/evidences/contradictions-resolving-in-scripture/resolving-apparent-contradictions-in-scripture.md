<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>RESOLVING APPARENT CONTRADICTIONS IN SCRIPTURE</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Resolving apparent
contradictions in scripture calls for proper methods of interpretation. The
approach assumes the inerrancy of scripture; brain teasers often become
paradoxes in wording rather than real contradictions. The approach also assumes
that scripture is divine in such a way that makes it harmonious throughout. The
mechanisms available for harmonizing scriptures with each other and with
experience amount to the same mechanisms for interpreting human language in general.
What we do to clear up ambiguities we do to resolve difficulties. Apparent
difficulties arise because either or both sides of a dilemma are unclear, but readers
do not realize that when they sense a problem with the text. The following list
is a brief list of mechanisms for resolving apparent discrepancies.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.    Is there a <u>textual problem</u>
in one of the verses? English Bibles often give marginal notations at places
where significant textual issues exist.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.    Is there a <u>translation
problem</u> in any of the references? Checking for different translations is
helpful.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.    Is there a difference between
the <u>meanings of terms</u>? Sometimes the fact that a shift has occurred in
the English word at two points is evidenced by a difference in Greek or Hebrew
words. Comparing translations may help identify this problem.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.    Is there a <u>literal-figurative
variable</u> that could be responsible for the tension between texts? The
literal-figurative distinction relates to the genre issue, whether there may be
exaggeration for emphasis, and the like.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  5.    Is there a <u>technical-non-technical
variable</u> in the terms that appear?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  6.    Could there be a <u>restrictive-non-restrictive
variable</u> at work in the wording?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  7.    Do the contexts have a
different <u>frame of reference</u>? Is the same <u>topic</u> addressed in both
places?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  8.    Are there different <u>intended
contrasts</u> involved?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  9.    Is there any possibility for <u>allegorizing
the comparison</u> that in turn sets up a problem with other scriptures?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  10.  What possibility exists for
confusing <u>sequence of presentation and sequence in the referent</u>—whether
of chronological order, logical order (importance, conditions), <i>etc</i>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  11.  Can an <u>inappropriate model</u>
be the source of the difficulty?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  12.  Is there a <u>difference in
idiom</u> between biblical languages and modern English, which then creates an
unconscious misunderstanding of one of the passages? Words of particular
importance here include <i>all</i>, <i>is</i>, <i>can</i>(<i>not</i>), <i>necessary</i>,
<i>forever</i>, <i>prophecy</i>, <i>fulfill</i>, <i>give</i>, <i>nature</i>, <i>judge</i>.
There could be cases also of linguistic interchange of nature, state, and
action terms.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  13.  If the issue is about something
to be done, there may be an <u>advice-commandment</u> <u>distinction</u> at
work.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  14.  What difference would a <u>permissive-prescriptive</u>
and/or <u>direct-indirect variable</u> bring to the text?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  15.  A <u>general <i>vs</i>.
absolute variable</u> may be at work with one statement being a generality and
another that deals with an exception assumed in the generality statement. Implied
<u>cultural limitation</u> applies to this point. Perhaps one context is
addressing a specific <u>unusual situation</u> that is an exception to the rule
stated by the other. One may be a negligible matter of <u>degree</u> that the
other decides not to overlook.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  16.  Is <u>relative negative</u>
possible in one of the references?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  17.  Is a <u>different
audience/circumstance</u> addressed, including dispensational distinctions?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  18.  It may help to compare
translations and commentaries and specialized treatments useful in seeing
through paradoxes.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        It is well to work out more
than one solution. (1) Sometimes the various solutions we can come up with are
aspects of one larger answer. (2) Later we may discover that one solution does
not work for reasons we did not know about at the time we made it. We still
have the others to fall back on if we have seen more than one way of handling
it. This procedure increases our confidence in the text. Besides, (3) it gives us
an added sense of objectivity if we see more than one answer in ambiguous
cases.</span></p>
