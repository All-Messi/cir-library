<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span

Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>        I. Derivation of </span><span
lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סֶלָה</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span>  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span lang=HE
dir=RTL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>               </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A.  From
the root </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סלה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span>, “to rest” or “be silent.” </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סֶלָה</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span> (penult accent) is taken as a pausal form of </span><span
lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סֶלָה</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span lang=HE style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span dir=LTR></span> </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(ultima
accent), directing the leader to pause or be silent presumably for an
instrumental interlude (Gesenius).It always has the same form.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>               B. 
From </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סלה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span>, “to lift up” (Sampey, Leupold, and “most modern scholars”). It
is thus a direction </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                     1.
to the orchestra “to raise” the volume of the accompaniment momentarily—like a <i>fortissimo</i>
or <i>sforzando</i>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                     2. 
to the reader “to increase” the volume of the reading.                                 </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                     3. 
to the congregation “to lift up” their voices in benediction—like an <i>amen</i>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      II. Against <span
lang=HE dir=RTL>סלה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span dir=LTR></span>, “to be
silent”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>               A.  It
is a rather doubtful Hebrew root that does not seem to occur anywhere in the
text of the Old Testament.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>               B.  It
would necessitate stopping the reading (though perhaps only for a pause like a <i>sforzando</i>,
or emphatic blast of the accompaniment).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                     1. 
</span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סֶלָה</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span> occurs (rarely) in the middle of a verse: Psalm<b> 55</b>:19; <b>57</b>:3;
Habakkuk <b>3</b>:3; </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                         <b>3</b>:9.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                     2. 
It sometimes occurs where there is no natural break in thought (see VII).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>               C.  <i>Selah</i>,
“to be silent,” would be superfluous at the end of a psalm.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                     1. 
Passages (see Chart IV): Psalm <b>3</b>:8; <b>9</b>:20; <b>24</b>:10; <b>46</b>:11;
<b>88</b>:10</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                     2. 
For some reason, the Septuagint omits all the <i>selahs</i> at the end of the
psalms except for Psalm <b>9</b>, which it combines with Psalm <b>10</b>. Whatever
the reason, the LXX translators did not seem to understand <span lang=HE
dir=RTL>סלה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span lang=HE><span dir=LTR></span> </span>to
mean “be silent” because they did not translate it that way.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>     III. In support of
the root </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סלה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span>, “to lift up”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>               A.  It
is a less obscure root—although that fact might tell against this derivation
since its meaning was evidently uncertain by the time of Aquila (died about
A.D. 150). Perhaps the meaning was lost during the exile, when the temple
performance of the psalms was not possible.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>               B.  The
usages of </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סלה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span> in the text of the Old Testament (Job <b>28</b>:16, 19; Psalm <b>119</b>:118;
Lamentations <b>1</b>:15) seem to come from this root.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      1. It means
“to value” in Job <b>28</b>:16, 19 (“to lift up,” hence, “to suspend,” then “to
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>          weigh”).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      2.  It means
“to condemn” in Psalm <b>119</b>:118; Lamentations<b> 1</b>:5. What can be
raised </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>          is
light; hence, unimportant. <i>Light</i> and <i>weighty</i> are used this way in
English for </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:45.0pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>          “insignificant”
and “important” respectively.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.0pt;text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>C.  They are all poetic
passages (as were the Psalms and Habakkuk).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
45.0pt;line-height:normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>D. 
The Job passages show that it was a root in use at a time early enough for all
the usages in the Psalms.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>       E.  The LXX always
translates </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סלה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span> with </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>διάψαλμα</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
Symmachus and Theodotian usually do also.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      1.  </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>διάψαλμα</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>is a word of
expression rather than silence. </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Διά</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>means “through” or </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>           “th(o)roughly”;
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ψάλλω</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> means “to pluck
an instrument.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      2.  If </span><span
lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סלה</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span> meant “silence” (for a musical interlude to be raised), the
Septuagint </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>           would
not be translating directly, but would be using a word for what </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>           occurred
during the pause rather than referring to the pause itself.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>F.   In Job <b>21</b>:12
the root </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סלה</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span
dir=LTR></span>, meaning “to lift up,” is used in a musical context similar </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.35pt;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      to the
proposed usage of </span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:14.0pt;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>סֶלָה</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'><span dir=LTR></span>:
<i>“They sing [lift up] to the timbrel and harp and </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:45.0pt;text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      rejoice at the
sound of the pipe.”  </span></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
45.0pt;line-height:normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>G.  <i>Selah
</i>does not necessitate interrupting the reading in those places where <i>selah</i>
would seem to interrupt the thought.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:45.0pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>
