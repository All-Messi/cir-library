margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span

margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:10.0pt'> </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:10.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt'>    </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>1.    the
only canonical New Testament epistle without any statement of authorship (John’s
three epistles do not very clearly identify their author either.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    2.    has the most extensive use of the Old
Testament numerically and proportionately of any New Testament book. Every
major point in the book is based on an Old Testament reference.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    3.    the only New Testament book to develop the
priesthood theme in connection with Jesus</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    4.    the only New Testament writing to assign Old
Testament texts to the Holy Spirit without mentioning the human writer (<b>3</b>:7-11;
<b>10</b>:15-17; cp. <b>9</b>:8)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    5.    makes the longest continuous quotation from
the Old Testament (Jeremiah <b>31</b>:31-34 in <b>8</b>:8-12)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    6.    the only New Testament book to cite Psalm <b>110</b>:4
as a Messianic prophecy and to develop its implications</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    7.    the only New Testament book to use
onomatopoeia (because of Psalm <b>95</b>:11 in the LXX translation at <b>94</b>:11):
<b>3</b>:11</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    8.    the only New Testament book to identify the
Messiah as our pioneer/author/captain/ forerunner (</span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>ὰρχηγός</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>[<i>archēgos</i>]) in its more etymological sense: <b>2</b>:10;
<b>12</b>:2 (cp. Acts<b> 3</b>:15; <b>5</b>:31, the only other New Testament
usages)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    9.    the most rhetorically polished writing in
the New Testament</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  10.    contains the only reference to the temptation
of Jesus in Gethsemane outside the gospels (<b>5</b>:7)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  11.    is the only book to indicate that the
crucifixion occurred outside the city wall of Jerusalem (<b>13</b>:12)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  12.    the only book to use the characterizing
phrase <i>“draw near (to God)”</i>: <b>4</b>:16; <b>7</b>:25; <b>10</b>:1, 22;<b>
11</b>:6 (<b>12</b>:18); <b>12</b>:22. cp. 1 Peter <b>2</b>:4</span></p>
