<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>PECULIARITIES OF JOHN’S GOSPEL</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>1.   Emphasizes the corporeality of the resurrected
Christ as well as the fleshliness of Jesus </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      incarnate</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2.   Has the largest percentage of the gospel devoted
to the triumphal entry to the ascension; </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      chapters <b>14-17</b> record just record what
happened from the upper room to the arrest.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>3.   Written around the synoptics’ account; so written
subsequent to them</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>4.   Much higher percentage of teaching-preaching, or
discourse, material proportionately </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      (known as a “spiritual gospel”)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>5.   More about the Holy Spirit than in the synoptics;
the only New Testament writing to use                    the word “Comforter/Advocate/Counselor”
(</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>παράκλητος</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>, <i>paraklētos</i>) for the Holy Spirit.                                   1
John also uses the term, but in reference to Christ (1 John <b>2</b>:1).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>6.   Characteristic word/abstractions: light/darkness,
life, love, truth, abide, the Jews</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>7.   Exhibits a love for antitheses</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif'>8. <span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Notes how the apostles later remembered and then
understood:<b> 2</b>:11, 17, 22; <b>12</b>:16; (note </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      14</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>:26);
<b>20</b>:8</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>9.   What the thoughts of the disciples were: <b>11</b>:13;
<b>12</b>:16</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>10. Notes how the apostles did not understand or know:
<b>21</b>:4; <b>20</b>:9</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>11. More emphasis on the deity of Jesus, his
Messiahship, and his inner consciousness</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>12. Only gospel to use the double “verily” in introducing
comments: <b>1</b>:51; <b>3</b>:3, 5, 11; <b>5</b>:19, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      24, 25; <b>6</b>:26, 32, 47, 53; <b>8</b>:34,
51, 58; <b>10</b>:1, 7; <b>12</b>:24; <b>13</b>:16, 20, 21, 38; <b>14</b>:12; <b>16</b>:20,
23; </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      <b>21</b>:18* (one single “verily” is used in
John to close <b>21</b>:25). On two occasions John’s </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      double “verily” parallels a single “verily” in the
synoptics: John <b>13</b>:21 (= Matthew <b>26</b>:21; </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Mark <b>14</b>:18) and <b>13</b>:38 (= Matthew <b>26</b>:34;
Mark <b>14</b>:30). </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Matthew uses the single expression most
frequently of the gospels (32 times); Mark </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      uses it 15 times and Luke only 7 times (8 times
including Luke <b>24</b>:53<sup>ms</sup>). Only two of </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Luke’s have parallels (Luke <b>18</b>:29 =
Matthew <b>19</b>:28; Mark<b> 10</b>:29 and Luke <b>21</b>:32 = </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Matthew <b>24</b>:34; Mark <b>13</b>:30). Luke <b>4</b>:24;
<b>12</b>:37; <b>13</b>:35; <b>18</b>:17; <b>23</b>:43 [<b>24</b>:53] have no</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      synoptic parallels.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>13. The gospel we depend on for knowing that Jesus’
ministry lasted for more than three </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      years, instead of perhaps just one year (John’s multiple
references to Passovers)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>14. Mentions <i>“the disciple whom Jesus loved”</i> (<b>13</b>:23;
1<b>9</b>:26; <b>20</b>:2; <b>21</b>:7, 20). It is the only</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      gospel that makes any attempt to identify its
author (<b>21</b>:20 + 24).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>15. Contains a purpose statement: <i>“that you might
believe”</i> (<b>20</b>:31; <b>19</b>:35; cp. Luke<b> 1</b>:1-4). In </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      fact, John emphasizes believing: </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>πιστεύω</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
(</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>pisteuō</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>, to believe)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> appears 97 times <i>vs.</i> 11 times I n Matthew, 15
times in Mark, and 9 in Luke; the noun </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt'>πίστις</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>(</span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>pistis</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>, belief)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> does not appear).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>16. John contains several “I am” statements”: [<b>4</b>:26];
<b>6</b>:[20], <u>35</u>, <u>41</u>, <u>48</u>, <u>51</u>; [<b>7</b>:34, 36];<b>
<u>8</u></b><u>:12</u>, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      [18, 23<span style='position:relative;
top:-3.0pt'>2</span>, 24, 28, 58]; [[<b>9</b>:9]];<b> 10</b>:<u>7</u>, <u>9</u>,
<u>11</u>, <u>14</u>; <b><u>11</u></b><u>:25</u>;<b> <u>14</u></b><u>:6</u>; <b>15</b>:<u>1</u>,
<u>5</u>; [<b>17</b>:14, 16; <b>18</b>:5, 6, 8]. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Arranged according to topics, the main “I am”
statements are as follows:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            a.   I am the bread of life: <b>6</b>:35,
41, 48, 51 (connected with feeding the 5,000)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            b.   I am the light of the world: <b>8</b>:12
(connected with healing of the blind man)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            c.   I am the door of the sheep: <b>10</b>:7,
9</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            d.   I am the good shepherd: <b>10</b>:11,
14</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            e.   I am the resurrection and the life: <b>11</b>:25
(connected with resurrecting Lazarus)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            f.    I am the way, the truth, and the
life: <b>14</b>:6</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            g.   I am the true vine: <b>15</b>:1, 5</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Parentheses signify that some of the “I am”
statements parallel a topically related miracle.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>17. Uses Roman time of day whereas the synoptists
apparently follow the Jewish custom, where numbering of hours starts at
daybreak rather than midnight. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>18. Places a/the cleansing of the temple near the
beginning of Jesus’ ministry (<b>2</b>:13-17) rather </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      than during the final week (Matthew <b>21</b>:12-13;
Mark <b>11</b>:15-17; Luke <b>19</b>:45-46)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>19. Supplements the synoptic gospels. The feeding of
the five thousand is the only miracle </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      besides the resurrection that John shares with
the other gospels.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>20.  </span><i><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>Κ</span></i><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>ingdom</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
occurs on only two occasions (<b>3</b>:3, 5; <b>18</b>:36<span
style='position:relative;top:-3.0pt'>3</span>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>21. The author thrusts himself into the narrative more
than the other gospels do. That feature may have something to do with not
naming himself.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>22. Shows a more obvious concern for apologetics and
polemics</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            a.   Eyewitness testimony: <b>1</b>:14; <b>2</b>:11;
<b>19</b>:34-35; <b>20</b>:30-31; <b>21</b>:24-25 (cp. 1 John <b>1</b>:1-4)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            b.   The witness of John the Baptist: <b>5</b>:31-35</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            c.   The witness of miracles:<b> <u>5</u></b><u>:36-38</u>;
<b>2</b>:11, 23; <b>3</b>:2 (<b>4</b>:54; <b>6</b>:2); <b>6</b>:14 (<b>6</b>:26),
30); <b>7</b>:31; </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  <b>9</b>:16; <b>10</b>:41-42; <b>11</b>:47;
<b>12</b>:18 (note <b>12</b>:37); <b>20</b>:31-31</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            d.   The witness of the Old Testament
scriptures: <b>5</b>:39-47</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>23. Uses the terminology <i>the Jews</i> (as part of a
generally greater distancing of himself and </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Jesus from the Jewish environment)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>24. Has no parables of the sort found in the
synoptics. Whereas they have parables, he has </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      discourses.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>25. Only New Testament book to use </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>πνεῦμα</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt'> </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>(<i>pneuma</i>) to mean “wind”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>26. The only gospel to use Jacob’s ladder as an image
for Christ’s role as prophet and priest </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      and savior (John <b>1</b>:51 &lt; Genesis <b>28</b>:10-12ff.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>27. Only gospel to appeal to the Old Testament
incident about the brazen serpent (<b>3</b>:14-15)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>28. Has no account of the institution of the Lord’s
Supper even though it expands extensively</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      on the events in the upper room that same night</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>29. Different people receive attention, particularly
Nathanael, Thomas, and Philip</span></p>
