<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>     I. A combination of doctrines in one group,
rather than two or three doctrines held by </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         different groups in the Colossian church</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  The epistle itself contains no hint
that the author writes to more than one group.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Historical criticism depreciates needless
multiplication of persons or events. The simpler explanation more likely holds
true (law of parsimony).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  The apostle moves back and forth from
the elements of one teaching to the elements of the other, which he would
presumably not do if writing to separate groups. Note especially the connecting
particles between verses that appear to refer to separate heresies.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  The tendencies of contemporary Jewish
religious thought made possible such a combination as appears in Colossae.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Later, developed Gnosticism exhibits
the same kind of combination found in Colossae, which shows that the
combination was historically possible. Kabbalism illustrates (from a later
time) the actual combining of Judaism and Gnosticism.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>   II. Elements of the heresy</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  Gnosticism</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   Intellectual exclusivism notable
in its hidden wisdom, exclusive mysteries, and privileged class</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   The contention for the
gospel’s universality in contrasst to intellectual exclusivism; see <b>1</b>:27,
28; <b>2</b>:2, 3; <b>4</b>:3</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Frequent use of <i>wisdom</i>
(</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>σοφία</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>, <i>sophia</i>); <i>intelligence</i> (</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>σύνεσις</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt'> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>synesis</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>); <i>knowledge</i> (</span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>γνῶσις</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>, <i>gnōsis</i>);
[<i>perfect</i>]<i> knowledge</i> (</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐπίγνωσις</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>, <i>epignōsis</i>) with reference to the gospel; <i>angels</i>
(</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἄγγελοι</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>, <i>angeloi</i>); <i>mystery</i> (</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>μυστήριον</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>,
<i>mystērion</i>); <i>ruler</i> (</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἄρχων</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>, <i>archōn</i>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   Speculative tenets of Gnostic
cosmogony and theology</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   Against the doctrine of
successive created mediation from good spirit to evil matter, Paul sets forth the
doctrine of one eternal Son whose mediatorial function he exercised in both material
and spiritual creation (see <b>1</b>:16-20; <b>2</b>:8, 9).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   The apostle adopts
Gnostic terminology with a higher content: <i>pleroma</i> (</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>πλήρωμα</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>,
<i>plērōma</i>: <b>1</b>:19; <b>2</b>:9; angels = voluntary humility?)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   Gnostic asceticism</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   Drinks (<b>2</b>:16)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Touch not, taste not,
handle not.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Denounces ascetic
practices on their moral (not Mosaic-legal) aspects as drawing too much
attention to the affections of a higher sphere and dying to the world (<b>2</b>:8,
20-22; <b>3</b>:1-5)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
