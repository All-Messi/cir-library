<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>1.   Change of situation</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            During the process of writing, Paul
received word that the situation in Corinth had taken a turn for the worse
again. As a result, the rest of the letter has more of a stern character.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2.   Different letter</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            2 Corinthians <b>1-9</b> represents a
substantial part of the fourth Corinthian correspondence, while <b>10-13</b>
represents part of a lost “severe letter” written between what we know as 1 and
2 Corinthians.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>3.   Change of audience</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The Corinthian church had a mixed
consistency. Chapters <b>1-9</b> addressed those who respected Paul’s
leadership and honored his directives and teachings. Chapters <b>10-13</b> aimed
more at those who resisted his responsibility for, and authority over, the
Corinthian church.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>4.   Aspects of approach</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            In the first nine chapters, Paul spoke in
a more conciliatory tone because he genuinely appreciated the progress in
solving the problems at Corinth, solutions enhanced by his painful visit, by
Titus’ interim work, and by the reception they gave to 1 Corinthians. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            In the last part, he spoke about another
aspect of his responsibility and struck negative notes of a disciplinary nature.
Encouragement and appreciation on the one hand combined with warning and
discipline on the other. He put the positive first as a strategy to enhance
their reception.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Mood shifts appear elsewhere in biblical
literature and are handled the same way by various scholars: Philippians <b>3</b>:1
<i>vs</i>.<b> 3</b>:2-<b>3</b>:19; <b>4</b>:1;<b> 4</b>:3; Isaiah <b>1-39</b> <i>vs</i>.
<b>40-66</b>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Jeffery T. Reed lists the following
reasons for mood shifts in <i>Journal of Biblical Literature</i>, spring 1996, p.
64. A sudden shift might be more natural in an informal personal letter:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>             (a) Similar shifts occur in Romans <b>16</b>:17-20;
1 Corinthians <b>15</b>:58; and Galatians <b>3</b>:1; <b>4</b>:21, which have
not resulted in multiple-letter theories.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>             (b) The author may have stopped writing
and started again a few days later, perhaps having received news about problems
(at Philippi) or just taking up new subject matter. The intervening time had
changed Paul’s mood.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (c) That a shift in tone demands a
multiple-letter theory is impossible to verify.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (d) There are semantic and topical
connections between the two parts.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            [(e) In Philippians there is little
consensus on where the interpolation begins.]</span></p>
