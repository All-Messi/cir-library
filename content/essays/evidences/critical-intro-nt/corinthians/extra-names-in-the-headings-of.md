margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> II. Application of the Data</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              A.    <u>Bearer</u> seems to be
eliminated because Tychicus bore Colossians, yet Timothy appears in the
heading; and Timothy was the bearer of 1 Corinthians, but Sosthenes is in the
heading.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              B.    <u>Amanuensis</u> appears to be
eliminated.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                     1. In Romans, written by Tertius,
no additional name appears in the heading (cp. 1 Peter <b>5</b>:12, although
the non-Pauline epistles have no extra names mentioned in their headings).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                     2. 1 and 2 Thessalonians have two
people in the headings.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                3. Philemon may be
written by Paul’s own hand (19), yet Timothy is in the </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                    heading.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              C.    Prominent people with Paul at the
time of writing seems inadequate. The salutations at the end include such
people and the number of them would have been too great to include in a
salutation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              D.    <u>Present fellow workers</u>
originally with Paul during the founding of the church he is addressing. Timothy
is in all but one letter that has extra people in the salutation. All the
churches that received letters with Timothy in the heading Paul founded after
Timothy began to serve as an extension of the apostolic office—from the second
journey onward. Paul counted Timothy as an especially reliable aide
(Philippians <b>2</b>:20). Even as these workers both addressed the people
during the founding of the church, so also they now address them by letter
without necessarily implying that the second name is co-author. The letters’
contents were the sentiments of both men named in the heading. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              E.    Co-authors is too strong. In
letters like 1 Corinthians, a second name appears in the heading, but the
contents are given under the name of <i>“I [Paul]”</i> (1 Corinthians <b>1</b>:4,
10, 11, 12, 14, <i>etc</i>.).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        The same reason need not apply
to every case of an added name in the salutation, and the added name could have
more than one reason for being there. Salutations also occur at the end of
letters, so sending greeting may not explain the second (and third) name in the
heading. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              Romans has no one else in the heading;
Paul did not found the Roman church personally; but then again he did not directly
found the Colossians church in all probability; yet he and Timothy both address
the church in the heading of the Colossian letter. There may be a difference
between the Roman and Colossian situations since the Colossian church came into
existence indirectly through Paul’s fellow workers evidently while Paul labored
in and around Ephesus (Colossians <b>2</b>:1). Yet again, a number of those he addresses
in Romans <b>16</b> were also converts—or at least close acquaintances—of Paul
from other places before. That still leaves a difference between converts and
members of the Paulline circle whose ministries he directed.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              A final datum occurs in 1 Thessalonians,
where Paul says, <i>“We wanted to come to you—even I, Paul, time and again—but
Satan hindered us.”</i> The “I” and “us” together might be a case of editorial
“we” or inclusive of his earlier fellow workers plus him as a group.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Values of remembering the letter bearer in New
Testament studies</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              A.    Postal systems were not so
dependable as modern postal delivery.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              B.    A bearer could confirm the
contents of the letter and vouch for its authenticity (cp. Acts <b>15</b>:24-27),
a feature Paul also provided for with his own handwritten salutation (2
Thessalonians<b> 3</b>:17; cp. 1 Corinthians <b>16</b>:21; Galatians <b>3</b>:11;
Colossians <b>4</b>:8; Philemon 19; note also Romans <b>16</b>:22).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              C.    The bearer could personalize the
communication, make known the historical circumstances surrounding the writer,
and the like (Colossians<b> 4</b>:7-9; Ephesians <b>6</b>:21-23; 1 Corinthians <b>16</b>:17
+ <b>7</b>:1?).</span></p>
