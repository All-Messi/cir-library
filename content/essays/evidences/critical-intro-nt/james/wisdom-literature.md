<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Introduction</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>      The
New Testament contains several literary genres: </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <u>gospels</u>
(Matthew, Mark, Luke, John)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <u>history</u>
(Acts)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <u>epistles</u>
(Romans-Hebrews, 1 Peter-3 John)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <u>apocalyptic</u>
(Revelation)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <u>wisdom
literature</u> (Sermon on the Mount, James, Jude).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>I.
Content</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <u>Wisdom
literature</u> has some affinities with <u>law</u>. They both deal with human
behavior and so use the imperative mood extensively. Wisdom, however, is a
larger category than law even as advice is larger than commandment. The
accompanying diagram shows their relationship. Advice <i>vs.</i> commandment,
opinion <i>vs.</i> faith and fact, procedure <i>vs.</i> fundamental morality
represent distinctions between the two types of material. Advice and
commandment differ as to <u>intent</u> and <u>scope</u>. The advice in wisdom
literature is not interested just in what </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><img width=279 height=136
src="/cir-library/images/essays/wisdom-literature/image001.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>   
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>people
“must do,” but in what they “best do.” It goes beyond sheer <u>moral living</u>
to <u>smart living</u>. In the areas where it goes beyond sheer morality,
advice is not necessarily consistent for <u>everyone</u> in all <u>cases</u>, <u>places</u>,
and <u>times</u>. It is not something that “has” to be done. When people do not
take advice, they do not <u>commit a sin</u>; they <u>take a risk</u>. Advice
is not concerned just with behavior relative to a standard, but also with behavior
relative to result—what is <u>effective</u> and <u>expedient</u>. It deals with
application in real situations.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>II.
Structure</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            As
to structure, wisdom literature especially has a pattern that characterizes
biblical writing in contrast to the ideal pattern of presentation in Western
culture. The goal in Western rhetoric is to have parallel major headings with
two or more parallel supporting points under them with the overall presentation
moving toward the desired conclusion. As anyone can see</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><img width=159 height=104
src="/cir-library/images/essays/wisdom-literature/image002.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>from
trying to “outline” biblical materials, this kind of analysis does not easily
impress itself on most biblical writings—except perhaps in broad sections. It
is artificial to their structure because they were not constructed with that
format in mind. What was not put into them cannot be extracted from them. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Biblical
materials—as well as most “Western” rhetoric, frankly—adopt something more like
a daisy pattern. A central theme or purpose of writing acts as the principle of
selectivity for what is put into the document. Individual items become petals
around the</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><img width=234 height=219
src="/cir-library/images/essays/wisdom-literature/image003.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>central
concern. The format is more flexible, capable of rather loose to more highly structured
format. So to speak, the petals may not be sequenced in any particular order,
but then again in some cases the author might use a deliberate sequence within
the pattern. Not all the petals have to be the same size; that is, some points
may be treated more briefly than others.</span></p>
