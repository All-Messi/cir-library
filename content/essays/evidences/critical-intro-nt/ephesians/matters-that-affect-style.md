<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>MATTERS THAT AFFECT STYLE</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Higher critics do not allow a wide enough
range of style variance in New Testament authors. The items below have
implication for greater variety in style.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.75in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>1.   Subject matter          Complex
problems tend to cause complex presentations. Certain (topic)                                subjects
call for special vocabulary. What we are trying to combat  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       determines terminology:
“faith and works” (Judaism), “fullness” (Gnosticism), priesthood (new subject).
For the sake of meeting the minds of the ones we want to address, we use the
vocabulary of the other in reference to our own subject so there can be a
correlation of parts of thought systems, or functional equivalents. Becoming/being
a Christian we might call a natural “high” because of the modern drug scene. Vocabulary
generated by such circumstances will be peculiar to the circumstance (cp<i>. “circumcision
of the heart,” “fullness of the godhead,” “armor of salvation”</i>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.75in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>2.   Amanuensis             Change in
amanuensis from one letter to another may affect the (help)                                   wording
 of  the  letter:  oral  <i>vs</i>.  written,  different   writers,  ways </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.75in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       amanuenses
are used.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
0in'><span style='font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif'>3. <span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Pressure                   The press  of the immediate
circumstances and the demands of the  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (time)                    problem  foster  hurried
 composition,  which  tends away from more</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       reflective
presentation and toward more conversational style. If Paul in prison found out
someone was going somewhere, for instance, he might have written a letter more
hurriedly than normal to take advantage of this way to send his communication. The
more time people have to think through their presentation, the more tightly they
may <u>organize</u> it and the more precisely they may <u>word</u> it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>4.   Readership                     We do not write
the same way to a country church as to one in </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      (audience)                modern suburbia. The cultural
expectancy of an area may contribute </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>       (culture)                  to the stiltedness
of expression. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:135.0pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-117.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>5.   Stage of Life            Over
time, people tend to adjust their vocabulary and way of speaking. Expectancy
about ways of speaking may vary with the age of the speaker. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:31.5pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-13.5pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>6.   Recent experiences  Sometimes what
we have been doing or the people we have been          (environment)                      around 
 cause  us   to  pick  up  on   expressions,  vocabulary, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-13.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                     speaking styles
that are fresh and new to us. (That is the reason they impress us.) Illustration
comes in the fact that no two letters are closer than Colossians and Ephesians,
written at the same time. The mood shift in 2 Corinthians may result from such
a thing as well.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>7.   Speech habits           Authors can “get on” an
expression or manner of speaking, so the</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       term, phrase,
or grammatical phenomenon may appear several times in this letter or sermon,
but nowhere else (cp. <i>“in the heavenlies”</i> in Ephesians, to a lesser
extent <i>“God forbid” </i>in Romans, and <i>“I am” </i>in John).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Style considerations in New Testament studies
affect the authorship of Ephesians (<i>vs</i>. the other “nine letters” of
Paul), the Pastoral letters, *Hebrews, *2 Peter (<i>vs</i>. 1 Peter),
*Revelation (<i>vs</i>. the other Johannine writings), synoptic studies, Acts <b>1-12</b>
<i>vs</i>. <b>13-28</b>; the genuineness of Mark <b>16</b>:9-20; as well as the
unity of 2 Corinthians, Philippians, and Matthew (the Great Commission, <b>28</b>:18-20).
(*The ancient church acknowledged stylistic questions here.) Maybe the
stylistic differences did not bother the ancients as much because Greek was
their mother tongue and they had a better feel for how a native speaker might
vary,</span></p>
