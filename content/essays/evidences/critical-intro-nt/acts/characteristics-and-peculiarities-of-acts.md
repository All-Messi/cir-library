<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    1.    Along with Luke, Acts has short paragraphs
that summarize periods of time the author </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>does not handle in detail: <b>1</b>:12-14; <b>2</b>:43-47;
<b>4</b>:32-35; <b>6</b>:7; <b>8</b>:1-3, 25, 40; <b>12</b>:24-25; <b>15</b>:33-35,
41;<b> 19</b>:21-22; <b>28</b>:30-31. They serve as links between the high
points the author has highlighted.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    2.    On some occasions the author introduces a
brief note about someone that will play a major role later in the account: <u>Saul</u>
at the stoning of Stephen <b>(7</b>:58;<b> 8</b>:1), <u>Barnabas</u> as among
those who sold land to support for the needy (<b>4</b>:36-37), <u>John Mark</u>
as the one whose mother owned the upper room where the disciples awaited Peter’s
fate (<b>12</b>:12).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    3.    In the mouth of Jesus, Luke states what
could become the geographical outline of Acts (<b>1</b>:8): Jerusalem, Judaea,
Samaria, the ends of the earth.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    4.    The only New Testament document to refer to
Christianity as “The Way”: <b>9</b>:2; <b>19</b>:9, 23; <b>22</b>:4; <b>24</b>:14,
22; cp. <b>18</b>:25, 26 (note John <b>14</b>:6; also Romans <b>5</b>:2;
Ephesians <b>2</b>:18; Hebrews <b>10</b>:20)*</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    5.    The only writing besides 1 Peter <b>4</b>:16
to refer to followers of Jesus as “Christians” (<b>11</b>:26; <b>26</b>:28)*. Note
also the <i>“of Christ”</i> expression in 1 Corinthians<b> 1</b>:12; <b>3</b>:23.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    6.    Contains the only dominical saying outside
the gospels (<b>20</b>:35; note also 1 Corinthians <b>11</b>:24, 25-26)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    7.    Has all eleven usages of the expression “together”
(</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ὁμοθύμαδον</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>) except one (Romans <b>15</b>:6): <b>1</b>:14; <b>2</b>:1,
46; <b>4</b>:24; <b>5</b>:12; <b>7</b>:57; <b>8</b>:6; <b>12</b>:20; <b>15</b>:25;
<b>18</b>:12; <b>19</b>:29.*</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    8.    Except for Matthew <b>12</b>:41, Luke-Acts has
the double-noun <i>“men _____ ”</i>: Luke <b>7</b>:37 (sinner woman?); <b>11</b>:32;
<b>17</b>:12; <b>24</b>:19; Acts <b>1</b>:16;<b> 2</b>:22, 29, 37;<b> 3</b>:12,
14; <b>5</b>:35;<b> 7</b>:2; <b>8</b>:27; <b>11</b>:20;<b> 13</b>:6, 15, 16,
26, 38; <b>15</b>:7, 13; <b>16</b>:9; <b>17</b>:22; <b>21</b>:28; <b>22</b>:1; <b>23</b>:1,
6; <b>28</b>:17*</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    9.    Luke-Acts is the only two-volume work in the
New Testament.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  10.    Provides the only historical framework for tracing
the activities in the first thirty-five years of the church. It supplies the
historical framework for at least a majority of Paul’s epistles.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>   11.    The only New Testament writing perhaps
addressed to a government official (Theophilus; <b>1</b>:1).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  12.    Except for Ephesians <b>5</b>:18, Luke-Acts
alone uses the filling imagery for the Spirit.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-31.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  13.    Frequently (79 times) uses <i>“Jews”</i>
especially in chapters <b>9-28</b>, dedicated more to non-Judaean territories.
Only 2 appear in chapters <b>1-8</b>: <b>2</b>:5, 10). John’s gospel has 68.</span></p>
