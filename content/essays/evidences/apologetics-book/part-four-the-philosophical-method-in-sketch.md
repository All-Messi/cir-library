<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   Other alternatives fall out by
default, not by direct disproof.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        That procedure works because
to do otherwise would require shouldering the negative burden of proof. It offers
a positive rather than confrontational pattern for <i>“feeling after God and
finding him”</i> (Acts <b>17</b>:27).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   The choice is for the best, not
for the one with no difficulties.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Choosing religious commitment does
not happen in a vacuum as if the alternative to choosing Christ were to make no
religious choice at all. Instead, we choose in a context of comparison; so our
choice is not between this and nothing, but between this and that. It is a
matter of choosing the best alternative. The Hebrew writer uses that approach
for encouraging his readers not to forsake Christianity in favor of Judaism. He
portrays Christianity as <u>better</u>, not Judaism as false.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        This point relates to the
observation (a) that Christianity does not take a defensive stance. For one
thing, it is not natural to let people raise difficulties for us to answer
about our own faith. In that setup, their queries would not even have to
represent an consistent alternative. Since we are handling these questions in
real life, what others ask should come from at least a tentative alternative. That
is so because on this issue others must take some viewpoint. Otherwise, they could
get so theoretical that answers would not really help. People sometimes act as
if they have no commitment because then they do not have to defend anything. Christianity
offers something people want if they recognize it. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        For another thing, (b) the
positive presentation must be done anyway for the hearer to see the whole and appreciate
the apologetic that comes from systematic advantage. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        For a third thing, (c) we have
to take the best alternative we know until we find something better. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Finally, (d) the kind of
certainty applicable to real-life matters is psychological certainty, more than
logical certainty. There is always room for a kind of uncertainty in real-world
matters even regarding the truth. There can be a breakdown between referent and
perception, between perception and conception, and conception and communication
to other people.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3. Choosing against an alternative
