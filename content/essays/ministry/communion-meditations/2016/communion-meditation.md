<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:.25in;text-align:center'><span

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>New
Year’s</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:6.0pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>God
has given us a wide range of freedom in which to use our power of choice. We
live within a broad arrow of options. As long as our choices fall within the
limits of that arrow, we are moving along toward the Lord’s intended destiny
for us. The garden in Genesis <b>2</b> pictures our circumstance before our
Creator. Adam and Eve could eat the fruit from countless trees; they were to
leave just one of them alone.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:6.0pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>We
are looking forward to a new year of making a living and enjoying other
activities. James (<b>4</b>:13-15) tells us to preface such intentions with <i>“if
God will.”</i> God has an overriding will in such matters because, for example,
there are ways of making a living that depart the values the faith calls for.
It is conceivable in an individual person’s case that God may have a more
specialized purpose. If so, of course, his leading will be explicit enough to
make that clear.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:6.0pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The
night before his crucifixion, Jesus demonstrated the principle. After praying
that the “cup” of crucifixion be taken away, he honored the Father’s overriding
will by saying, <i>“Not my will but yours be done.”</i> That mentality carried
on through the events the next day.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:6.0pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>When
we observe the emblems today that memorialize those events of yesteryear, we
agree in our own situation to Christ’s sentiments displayed in them. We submit
our considerable freedom to the overriding will of God.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:24.0pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>This
year we keep our choices within the bounds of freedom under the principle <i>“if
the Lord is willing, I’ll do this or that.”</i></span></p>
