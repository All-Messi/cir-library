<p>Funny how little things stick with you. When I was in the third grade, a photographer came to our school and took pictures of us. When the individual billfold-size shots came, on the little package in blue letters, I remember seeing, <em>“A picture is worth a thousand words.”</em> That was my first exposure to the well-known expression.</p>
<p>Today we say these emblems “speak to us”; they’re “worth a thousand words.” They’re a visual message that brings back what took place real-ly so long ago. Grape juice and unleavened bread portray to our mind’s eye the shed blood and given body that says, <em>“I care about you this much.”</em></p>
<p>Together let’s see what the pictures say so concisely, so vividly, so significantly.</p>
