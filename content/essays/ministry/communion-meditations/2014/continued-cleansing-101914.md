<p>The Lord’s Supper brings the Lord’s life and death into our present circumstance. That connection between past and present is made even greater by John’s comment, <em>“The blood of Jesus Christ continues to </em><em>absolve</em><em> us from all sin” </em>(1 John <strong>1</strong>:7).</p>
<p>Christ’s self-sacrifice is not something finished and done with. Since we continue to <em>“fall short of the glory of God” </em>(Romans<strong> 3</strong>:23), we need continual cleansing from sin. With these emblems we acknowledge his ongoing involvement in our ongoing relationship to God.</p>
