<p>Human government has a divinely sanctioned role in society.  It serves to restrict evil and keep citizens from committing crimes (Romans <strong>13</strong>:1-7). At this table of remembrance, we observe an even higher calling, calling to do what we ought to do (a positive role), not just to refrain from evil (a negative role) or maintain what is already good (a neutral role, as in <em>“do no harm”</em>). The occasion that has prompted this observance set a high standard for human resolve. In doing the Father’s will, Jesus did not just further a neutral circumstance; he established a positive purpose in the face of opposition—violent opposition at that, one that took his life in horrible fashion.</p>
<p>These emblems speak to us about our role in the world, a role that calls for more than submitting to the restraint from evil and more than “not rocking the boat.” This meal calls for doing what we ought aside from force or restraint. It calls for initiative to do what is honorable and good to further the positive role of Christ’s church.</p>
