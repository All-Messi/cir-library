<p>We consider the beginning of the year as a time to start over. Gathering at the Lord’s table relates to that same idea. Having a clean slate feels good. It gives us a sense of release from whatever went before that would hinder going forward. We are remembering here a unique event that provides us with that feeling of release from past failures that can weigh us down and sap the energy for trying again.</p>
<p>So drawing our minds back toward the One who loves us that much and loved us that way as a relief from life’s greatest problem: alienation from each other and separation from God. In this observance we need to think on these things so we can reap the greatest benefit of all time anywhere: reconciliation and all its benefits for helping us lean into what lies ahead.</p>
