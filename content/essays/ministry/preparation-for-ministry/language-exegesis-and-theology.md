<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in;line-height:18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>   Biblical languages,
texts, and topics naturally combine for study that undergirds personal growth
and Christian ministry. Acquaintance with the original languages of scripture <u>raises
options</u> not suggested by the English, <u>eliminates possibilities</u>
English wording might allow, and gives access to additional <u>study tools</u>.
Furthermore, a knowledge of Greek and Hebrew facilitates the <u>evaluation</u>
of points made by other commentators on the biblical text, a great advantage to
ministers, because we are constantly called upon to deal with doctrinal issues
purportedly based on “the original.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in;line-height:18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Exegetical study follows
the layout of given <u>books</u>. It follows the author’s thought as he moves
through various topics that bear on the practical needs of his readers. Doctrinal
study approaches scripture topically. It assembles all materials on given <u>subjects</u>
so a person can comprehend them as a unit and the Christian system as a whole.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in;line-height:18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Proper understanding of
Christian truth establishes the foundation for outreach, edification, and
protection. Zeal must arise from content in order for conversion to occur in those
addressed by the gospel. Methods of evangelism and education must fit the
message once for all time delivered to the saints. The more we understand the
gospel, the more effectively we can serve in ministry roles that extend the
church, build it up, and preserve it against threats from the outside and from within.
We must know <u>what</u> in order to know <u>how</u>.</span></p>
