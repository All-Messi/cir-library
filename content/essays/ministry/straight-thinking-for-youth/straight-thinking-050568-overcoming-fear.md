<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>OVERCOMING FEAR</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Realize that fear can be sin. In the Book of Revelation, John lists
people that will have their part in what he calls the lake of fire and sulfur.
The first kind is the fearful; the second is the unbelieving. Something’s wrong
with our belief in God and our faith in Christ if we are constantly beset with
fear. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Revelation<b>
21</b>:8</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Forget superstitious fears. From time immemorial, peoples have let their
imaginations run away with them, supposing that all sorts of mysterious evils
control our world, but people enlightened by God’s revelation should understand
that a righteous God controls this universe. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Psalm <b>24</b>:1</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Know what’s what. There are two things wrong with ignorance: we fear what
we shouldn’t and don’t fear what we should. An educated fear may save our life.
Furthermore, if we know what to be afraid of, we’ll learn either how to handle
it or how to steer clear of it. As a result, we’ll be less fearful. Learning
what to fear helps us overcome fear itself. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Proverbs <b>1</b>:33</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Remember who’s who. We may fear death and those who might try
to kill us. But if we understand that death is not an end to life but a change
in life, we can close our eyes in peace when persecution overcomes us. If we
fear God to the extent that we obey him, we have no reason to fear people who
oppose him. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Matthew <b>10</b>:28-33</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Obey God. God has shown us his power, and he has shown us
that he uses it for the good and against the bad. We need not therefore live in
morbid fear of him who saved us from our sins by sending us his Son; in fact,
we love him for it and obey him because of it. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>5</b>:6-12; 1 John <b>4</b>:18; Johnn <b>14</b>:15;
1 John <b>4</b>:18</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Act as under God’s direction. Moses left Egypt twice, the first time in
fear, the second in faith. He fled the first time because he had jumped the gun
in trying to deliver his people, but the second time he wasn’t afraid of
Pharaoh, because he knew that the God who told him to deliver Israel was
directing what he did. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Acts <b>7</b>:23-29</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>See the One that’s invisible. There is comfort in knowing
that we’re not alone; we have desire to try, encouragement to continue, and
confidence that we can do what we have to. If we believe God is with us, we’ll
overcome our fear. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Psalm <b>139</b>:7-12; <b>27</b>:1; Hebrews <b>11</b>:27</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
