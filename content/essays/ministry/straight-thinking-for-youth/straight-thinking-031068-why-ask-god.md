<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>WHY ASK GOD?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. “Jesus, son of David, have mercy on me!” </span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Beside the dusty road that led from
Jericho to Jerusalem, a blind beggar sat facing the voice that could command
his eyes to see. But instead of a command a question came, <i>“What do you want
me to do for you?”</i> What! Didn’t Jesus know? In fact, didn’t everybody that
was there know? (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Mark <b>10</b>:47, 51</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. “Your Father knows what you need before you ask him.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> So did Jesus, and so did everybody
else on the Jericho road. Obviously, the blind man was begging for his sight.
But why did Jesus ask Bartimaeus to ask when he already knew what he wanted?
Why do we pray for what God knows we need? (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>6</b>:8</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>There is a difference between believing that God <i>can</i> give a blind
man sight and believing that God <i>will</i> give a blind man sight if he asks,
and God wants us to have that greater trust in him. But didn’t Bartimaeus already
believe that Jesus would indeed give him sight if he asked? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark <b>10</b>:48</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>There is a difference between a man’s faith that God will
give him sight <i>if</i> he asks and the faith that <i>asks</i>. Jesus wanted
to increase people’s faith by expecting them to ask. In fact, his miracles were
more for increasing the recipient’s faith in him as God’s Son than for
relieving the person’s suffering. There were lots of blind people in Jesus’ day
and since that he hasn’t healed. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read James <b>1</b>:5-6</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. “God sends rain on the just and unjust.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> So, what’s the point of a righteous man’s
prayer for rain? We gain an appreciation for the common good things in everybody’s
life when we stop to thank him for them and ask him to keep giving them.
Besides, in praying, we come face to face with our absolute dependence on God
for everything and on his constant care for us all. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>5</b>:45</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>But isn’t there more to the purpose of prayer than for developing proper
attitudes toward God? Don’t we feel that by praying to the Father, he will
grant benefits that would not have come had we not prayed? Yes. We remember
Elijah, who even prayed that it wouldn’t rain, and it didn’t for three and a
half years; and he prayed for rain, and it rained. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>5</b>:17-18</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. “The effective prayer of righteous people
accomplishes a lot.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>
It strengthens our faith in God, especially when God clearly answers <i>yes</i>;
it impresses on us our dependence on him, and makes us appreciate the goodness
of the One that provides for all our needs, asked for or not. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>5</b>:1</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
