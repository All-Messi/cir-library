<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE CROSS, THE CHRIST, AND YOU</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Jesus was not the only one that ever died on a cross. In times of war, the
Romans crucified hundreds of captives simultaneously. By this means they
frequently destroyed criminals and slaves that committed major offenses. Jesus
was not the only one that died on a cross, but he was the only one that died
that way intentionally. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Luke<b> 23</b>:34; John <b>10</b>:17-18</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday.  </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The cross has become an ornament of beauty, but the “old rugged cross” of
Calvary was an ornament of shame and agony. There is nothing pretty about a man
bleeding to death while convulsing in pain. The best man that ever lived died
the worst death ever known. Why? To conquer sin and demonstrate how bad it is.
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark<b> 14</b>:32-36;
<b>15</b>:25</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Victims of crucifixion usually did not die for at least thirty-six hours;
Jesus was on the cross only about six hours. The blood and water that came from
his side indicate that he died from “extreme mental agony and sorrow” rather
than just from thirst, infection, bleeding, and exhaustion. People’s sins
literally broke his heart. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Mark 1<b>5</b>:25; Luke <b>23</b>:44-46; John <b>19</b>:34</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>In A. D. 70, the Roman army under Titus destroyed Jerusalem.
Josephus, a first-century Jewish historian, tells about the countless
crucifixions of Jews at that time, many of the same people that had rejected Jesus
earlier. Unlike all these people, Jesus was a perfectly good man, but he died
for sins—not his, but ours. What else does he need to do to prove he loves us?
What have you done to prove your love for him? (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>14</b>:27; Matthew<b> 10</b>:24-33</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Would you suffer like Jesus suffered? If not, you are no
follower of him. That’s a hard fact. Christianity is not an easy way to get to
heaven, but it is the <i>only</i> way to get there. As a servant of Jesus, we
are not better than our Master. We too must be serious about our heavenly
Father’s business. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
<b>14</b>:27; Matthew <b>10</b>:24-33.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>People usually have enough respect not to laugh at someone that’s dying,
even if he deserves to die; but the Roman soldiers and Jewish religious leaders,
and fellow victims made fun of Jesus. Friends and relatives usually gather
close to a loved one, but Jesus’ disciples stood afar off. Even God seemed far
away. Remember the crucifixion scene the next time people laugh at you while
you stand for Jesus all by yourself. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>27</b>:39-46.</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. “Come down from the cross!”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> was the taunt thrown at Jesus by the
hypocritical high priests. But the Great Physician refused to heal himself
because his death was necessary for fulfilling God’s plan. We also have a
responsibility to God, so let’s not allow the jeers of ignorant people drive us
from it. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark <b>15</b>:31;
Luke <b>12</b>:50; <b>23</b>:34</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>
