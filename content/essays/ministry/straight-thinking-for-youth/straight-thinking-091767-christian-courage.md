<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:6.0pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>CHRISTIAN COURAGE</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> What is the worst thing that has ever happened to you? Maybe a member of
your immediate family died in a fire that burned your home. A car wreck may
have injured you permanently. Do not blame God. Take courage with the assurance
that he permits calamities as a means of helping us mature through suffering. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>12</b>:5-10.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Remembering people less fortunate can serve to encourage us. The fact
that they, too, are dealing with difficulties reminds us that God expects us all
to maintain hope in his promises. He won’t let us be tested more than we can handle.
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>12</b>:1;
1 Corinthians <b>10</b>:13</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>A great cloud of witnesses surrounds us. The previous saints have shed
their testimony to us, and the present crowd is watching our response. We must
strive hard in every crisis because people are looking for a source of strength
to help them cope. They should be able to find strength by observing our
attitudes toward adversity. Let us look to Jesus, the Captain of our faith, and
see in him the example we follow. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>12</b>:1-3</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.).<i> </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Now is the time to develop proper attitudes toward material
possessions. If we fill our adult years with consuming desires for money,
expensive clothes, and a luxurious home, we will not easily accept being
deprived of them. If some catastrophe keeps us from obtaining these material things,
we may lose heart and begin to despair, because our faith has not been centered
on eternal values. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Hebrews <b>10</b>:32-35</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Amid trying circumstances, we can cast our anxiety on the Lord because we
know he will care for us. Where there is no obsession with maintaining life at
all costs, we can concentrate on godly pursuits. Though only death may bring
relief from persecution, we have courage for the present through confidence in
God’s provision for the future. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>8</b>:28; 1 Peter <b>1</b>:5-7</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Some young folks do bring on themselves a “multitude of sorrows” because
of undisciplined living, but misery doesn’t necessarily indicate God’s
disfavor. Jesus never promised an easy life to us who follow him. Classmates may
disapprove of us as a result of our meeting God’s approval. If we maintain
friendships with those that people ridicule because they’re Christians, we will
gain courage when our faith is critically challenged. (Read 1 Peter <b>5</b>:8-9.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> The greatest encouragement we can have from youth to old age is the hope
that Jesus promised every faithful disciple. Today’s labors are small in
comparison to tomorrow’s great reward. How short are earth’s sorrows in
contrast to heaven’s eternal joys! (Read Romans <b>8</b>:18-25; 1 Peter <b>5</b>:10.)</span></p>
