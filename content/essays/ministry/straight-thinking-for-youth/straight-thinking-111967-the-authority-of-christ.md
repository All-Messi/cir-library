<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE AUTHORITY OF CHRIST</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Christ is <i>“head over all things to the church.” </i>That’s why the
religion he established is called “Christianity.” So the church he founded
should exalt its Head in name and claim and deed by yielding to his authority
alone. All and only his commands should govern its members and determine its
course. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Ephesians <b>1</b>:21-22</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>When Jesus told a paralytic man his sins were forgiven, the scribes of <br>
Capernaum supposed they had caught Jesus in a clear-cut case of blasphemy,
because they reasoned to themselves, <i>“Who can forgive sins but God?” </i>But
Jesus demonstrated his authority on earth to forgive sins by giving them all a
visible proof; he had the paralytic get up from his mat and carry it away. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark <b>2</b>:1-12</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>While Jesus and the disciples were crossing the Sea of Galilee, a storm
suddenly blew down from the surrounding hills. The apostles cried out for help
because these lifelong fishermen knew the deadly peril they were in. All Jesus
did was say, <i>“Peace, be still,”</i> and the wind and waves became quiet. <i>“What
kind of man is this?”</i> This was the God-man Jesus that has authority over
the universe he created. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Matthew <b>8</b>:23-27</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>God’s angels are powerful beings of the spiritual world that
have served his grand purposes since before the world began. But the writer of
Hebrews points out that Jesus <i>“has inherited a more excellent name than
they.” </i>Christ himself said that he had authority over even the angels, and
could call more than six thousand of them to protect him from his enemies if he
so desired. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Hebrews<b> 1</b>:4; Matthew <b>26</b>:53</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Peter refused Cornelius’ attempt to bow to him in worship
when he came to present the gospel to Cornelius’ household. One of the powerful
angels in the Book of Revelation also refused to let John bow down to him. But
Jesus always accepted people’s worship, and the angels of heaven were
themselves commanded to worship him. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Acts <b>11</b>:25-26; Revelation <b>22</b>:8-9; Hebrews <b>1</b>:6;
Mark <b>5</b>:6</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Each of us will take our turn in front of the judgment bar in heaven. The
One we will all stand in front of is the very Christ that in his death for our
salvation gave us the perfect example of yielding to authority. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 2 Corinthians <b>5</b>:10;
Matthew <b>26</b>:38-42</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The One the angels worship is worthy of our worship. The One
the wind and waves obey is worthy of our obedience. The One that made us and
supplies all our needs is worthy of our love. Jesus has authority in heaven and
earth, but we must decide to yield to it. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Revelation <b>5</b>:4-5</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
