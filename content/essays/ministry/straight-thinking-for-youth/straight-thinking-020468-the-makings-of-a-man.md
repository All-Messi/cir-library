<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE MAKINGS OF A MAN</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> <i>“And he was rich</i>.” Zacchaeus, the tax collector, had plenty of
what most young people hope to have when they become adults. A lot of people
have a continuing objective of having a bigger income. Young people make plans
to get a college education because they realize that it will probably enable
them to get a higher paying job. But “people’s lives do not consist of the
abundance of what they own.” (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Luke <b>12</b>:15</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.) What is your goal in life?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. “Zacchaeus was a chief tax collector.” </span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>He held a position where he could
pull strings that would dump in his lap as much as he wanted. The Roman
government required a certain amount of taxes from each province in the empire,
but many chief publicans increased the local tax payment and kept the excess
for themselves. If you obtain a position of authority, do you think you can be adult
enough to resist the temptation of graft and corruption? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>1</b>:12</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. “He was short.” </span></i><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Our teen years involve a time of notable growth spurts.
Boys measure frequently to make sure they’re still growing. They look forward
to being tall enough to tease mom and dad about being short. But the measure of
a man is the size of his heart, not how high his head is. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Samuel <b>9</b>:2, 16; <b>15</b>:23;
Luke <b>19</b>:8.) </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>How
tall are you?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. “He climbed up a sycamore tree.” </span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Young people tend to be overly sensitive
about what others think. It’s part of the quest for self-esteem. But Zacchaeus,
a public official, threw away his false face of dignity and pride in order to
fulfill his purpose. And, as is so often the case, the text doesn’t indicate
that anyone thought his actions were strange. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>15</b>:12-14</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. “He hurried.” </span></i><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>We learn a lot about Zacchaeus by noticing what he got
excited about. Likewise, we tell a lot about ourselves by what we’re
enthusiastic about. Out of the many exciting things to do these days, we must
select the important ones and give our energies to them if we want to make
anything out of ourselves. There’s not enough time to do everything. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read <b>6</b>:11-12</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. “He welcomed him gladly.” </span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>That joy was not dampened by the
critical crowd<i>—“a man that’s a sinner.”</i> Zacchaeus was more interested in
the Lord’s proposal to stay at his house. The one that receives Jesus must
receive him joyfully or not at all, because even the Lord of heaven and earth
does not force open our hearts; he stands at its door and knocks. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Revelation <b>3</b>:20</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. “He tried to see Jesus.” </span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Zacchaeus overcame the press of the
crowd and the disadvantage of his stature in order to get a glimpse of Jesus.
There are always plenty of things that stand between us and Christ, but we must
overcome them if we want the Lord to abide with us and bring salvation to our
house. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>19</b>:9</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
