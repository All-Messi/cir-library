<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>WILL YOU PAY THE PRICE?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>PRICELESS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The
value of salvation cannot be calculated. The glories of living eternally in
heaven are a welcome exchange for the torments of dying forever in hell. If you
were ever saved from drowning, you know how grateful a person is for being
spared. You recognized for the first time the preciousness of your life on
earth. What could you possibly give in return for eternal life in heaven? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>16</b>:26</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>EXPENSIVE.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>
Salvation is expensive in terms of what it demands. Nothing valuable is cheap,
and nothing worthwhile is easy. It is no wonder that salvation costs so much
and requires the utmost commitment. Everything you accomplish and accumulate in
the many years ahead you must turn over to him for the promise of salvation.
Living with God requires living for him, so save yourself at all costs. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>13</b>:45-46</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>A BARGAIN. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>In
comparison to what it costs, salvation is a bargain. Eternity is a long time to
enjoy the reward for a few years’ service. The perfect bliss of heaven in
return for our imperfect lives would be unbelievable if God had not promised it
to us, and release from hell is a bargain at any price. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Revelation <b>7</b>:13-17; <b>21</b>:7-8</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'>FREE.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>
Salvation is free; we don’t lose a thing by accepting it now. Friends may try
to us how much we are missing, but their brand of “good times” will soon be
gone; the old cronies will be gone too. The home town will be “dead”; only the
memories will remain of good times no longer possible; school’s out for good!
The smart thing is to replace the frivolous fun with Christian living; it’s
free and permanent. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
1 Timothy <b>6</b>:6-10</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>A GIFT.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Salvation
is a gift from God. There are a lot of things money can’t buy and life can’t
bring; salvation is one of them. We are not good enough to deserve heaven nor
too good to go to hell. We will never earn salvation from sin and death and
eternal destruction, but we can meet the stipulations on which God has promised
to give it to us. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Ephesians<b> 2</b>:8-9</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>AN OFFER.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> God
offers salvation to everyone, but he does not force it on anyone. There will be
no one in heaven that does not want to be there. There will be no one kept away
from hell that wants to go there. Say “yes” to God’s offer; you lose nothing
and gain everything. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
1 Peter <b>3</b>:9.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>A QUESTION.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> You
know young people your own age that have been killed in automobile accidents.
Did their lives give them any hope of having heaven? You too may never finish
high school; is there any hope for you? <i>“Have you thought what it means to
be lost?”</i> Then you are beginning to see what it means to be saved. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Corinthians <b>6</b>:9-11</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
