<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>Showers of Blessings</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>041766</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'> SPECIAL BLESSINGS.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>
Although some blessings come impartially to everybody, special blessings come
to the ones that God has given special responsibilities. Showers of rain and
floods of light make everybody happy, but showers of manna came only to the
wandering Israelites that God was delivering from Egyptian slavery. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>20</b>:23; Acts<b> 14</b>:15-17;
Exodus <b>16</b>:4-12</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>UNEXPECTED BLESSINGS.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Lot <i>“entertained angels unawares.”</i> He offered his home to two
strangers as a shelter against the night and the sin of Sodom. God confirmed
his righteousness by saving him from destruction. We don’t anticipate some
blessings and opportunities as we just move along through our routines. God
rewards us simply for living acceptably day in and day out. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>25</b>:14-30</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>UNUSED BLESSINGS. </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>We should not expect anything more
from God if we don’t use what he’s already given us. Gifts and talents don’t
have much practical value unless we use them. And if we don’t, those gifts and
talents will pass from us and go others, because to everyone that uses more
will be given, and the ones that don’t use what they have will lose them. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>25</b>:14-30</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday.</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'> </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>MISUSED
BLESSINGS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The ground God
waters is cursed if it grows weeds instead of crops. Likewise, people are
cursed when they use God’s blessings to his shame rather than to his glory. Unproductive
ground is burned off. What will happen to us that use good minds for evil
thoughts and healthy bodies for sinful deeds?</span><span style='font-size:
9.0pt;line-height:115%'> (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Hebrews <b>6</b>:8.</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>REJECTED BLESSINGS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Rejecting God’s blessings is probably worse than neglecting them. We make
no effort when we neglect. But when we reject, we make effort against God.
Hearing the Word and rejecting it will be as disastrous in our own day as the
forty years of wandering were to the rebellious Israelites who, after God saved
them from Egypt, refused to trust him enough to follow him into Canaan. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Numbers <b>14</b>:1-35</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday.</span></i><i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>USED
BLESSINGS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Blessings
used properly are like rain absorbed by soil that has high yields to those who
have plowed ground. If we use blessings properly, they will lead to other
blessings from God. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Hebrews <b>6</b>:7</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>GREATEST BLESSINGS.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>
Since the day mankind began to sin, God has been unfolding his plan for saving
them from sin. <i>“In the fulness of time</i>,<i>”</i> we today have the
greatest responsibility toward God, because we have received the most “Shower
of Blessings.” (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Hebrews <b>2</b>:1-3</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

line-height:115%'>Virgil Warren, <u>Straight</u>, April 24, 1966, p11</span></p>
