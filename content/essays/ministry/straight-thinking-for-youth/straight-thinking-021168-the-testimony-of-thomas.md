<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE TESTIMONY OF THOMAS</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> <i>“Let’s go die with him.”</i> “Doubting Thomas” refers to one of Jesus’
apostles. But notice his proposal to the other apostles as they were preparing
to accompany Jesus on his death mission to Jerusalem. Rather than doubt, these
words testify to notable faith and courage. His commitment to Jesus was not a
fair-weather fancy; it prompted the determination to give his “last measure of
devotion.” (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>11</b>:16</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. “How can we know the way?”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> A student shows interest by asking
questions. Thomas was no bump on a log when it came to learning what Jesus
wanted him to know. He wasn’t afraid to reveal his ignorance either. We should find
ourselves asking questions about God and the Bible, showing the concern kind of
concern Thomas had. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
John <b>14</b>:1-6</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. “Unless I see . . . .”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Thomas wanted firsthand assurance
that Jesus had risen from the dead. Jesus rebuked him for his unwillingness to
believe even his fellow apostles when they told him about the Lord’s appearance
to them. The blind refusal to believe only what we have seen establishes an
impossible demand about knowing, because we have to know far more than what we
can personally experience. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read John <b>20</b>:29</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Wednesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> <i>“Unless I put my finger into the print of the nails . . .
.”</i> Thomas was not satisfied with seeing. Maybe he wanted to be sure the
apostles had not seen a ghost, especially since the appearance they referred to
was behind barred doors. He couldn’t believe their eyes; he wanted the proof of
his own touch. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
John <b>20</b>:19-20; Luke <b>24</b>:25</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. “Unless I . . . thrust my hand into his side, I
won’t believe it.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>
He had watched the Roman soldier jam his spear into Jesus’ limp body to see
whether he was dead; he had seen the blood and water drain down his side. No
doubt he was dead. If Thomas could put his hand in that wound, he could have no
doubt that Jesus was alive again. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read John <b>19</b>:33-34; <b>20</b>:25.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> <i>“My Lord and my God!” </i>When Jesus appeared a week later to all eleven
apostles, he offered to let Thomas to go ahead and put his finger into the nail
print and his hand into the wound—and believe. The outcry came immediately, <i>“My
Lord and my God!”</i> It was the <i>“shout heard ’round the world!”</i> and it
still reverberates in our ears today. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read John<b> 20</b>:26-28</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. “These are written so you can believe.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Another apostle in the group Jesus
appeared to that Sunday has given us this account of Christ’s encounter with
Thomas; so, though we doubt like Thomas, we can see with his eyes, feel with
his fingers, and believe John’s record that <i>“Christ the Lord is ris’n today”!</i>
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>20</b>:30-31</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
