<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>MAN AMONG MEN</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> The differences between people’s preferences become more acute as we
grow into adulthood. But we must always remember that God bountifully blessed
us by placing man among men. He thereby made possible a whole realm of
happiness in social living. The giving of every good fortune brings the
responsibility or receiving it. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>14</b>:1-12.</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Confident people act differently than those who doubt their ability,
because people’s estimate of themselves greatly affects their behavior.
Likewise, any evolutionists that regard themselves as animals quite likely behave
differently from Christians that are aware of their likeness to righteous deity;
animals don’t live by moral principles. Don’t imitate those who want freedom
for their sins by denying their kinship to God. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Proverbs <b>23</b>:7; Genesis <b>1</b>:26-27</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>When we become adults, we would give all we own to atone for the cross
words and rebellious attitude of our younger years. The determination to be
totally independent is what labels us as immature. If we want people to regard
us as adults, we have to act like adults. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Ecclesiastes <b>11</b>:9-10.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>If a teammate avoids passing us the ball, we won’t help the
game by treating him the same way. Revenge doesn’t accomplish anything
anywhere. Our conduct toward others tends to reflect their attitude toward us.
It’s up to us to improve conditions by overcoming evil with good. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>12</b>:17-21</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>What did you want to be when you grew up? Now that you are
growing up what do you expect to be when you die? Your occupation determines
your preparation, and what you expect to be as a spirit determines your preparation
as a man. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>11</b>:24-26</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Does your decision to take part in the activities and work of the church
depend on whether your friends will also come or serve? Don’t be a half-hearted
Christian that lacks the self-discipline to set the pace in the race for
eternal life. If you can’t bear to be separated from your friends now to serve
the Lord, don’t hope to be separated from them later when God chooses the ones
he’ll reward. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke
<b>9</b>:26</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>As “a man among men,” you naturally tend to do what other people
do, but the test of right action is not the opinion of your teachers,
classmates, or “steady.” The customs of society must defer to the commandments
of God, because people’s dictates can get tainted by ignorance, selfishness,
and secret desires. Be “a man among men” by being a God-like man. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read <br>
Colossians <b>2</b>:8</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
