<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE DECISION TO DIE</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>What kind of person do you think you would be if you had other parents,
if you were a boy instead of a girl, if you were living in a different place in
another time? Would you still be a Christian? Maybe you aren’t a Christian; do
you think you would have accepted Christ if things were different somehow? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Jeremiah <b>31</b>:29-30</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Judas Iscariot was the worst product of the best environment. His failure
points out the fact that good surroundings do not necessarily produce good
people. Even an apostle of Jesus in the presence of perfect influence yielded
to temptation. But if Noah could be a good man in his wicked world, no one
should suppose that Judas was just a victim of circumstance or a slave of fate.
Neither can any of us excuse ourselves on that basis. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>12</b>:1-6</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>We can make something bad out of anything. Judas stumbled over the little
pile of coins people gave them to buy food for Jesus and the twelve. Judas
couldn’t blame his sin on the Lord who made him treasurer for the group.
Neither can we blame God for misusing the gifts that he has placed in our
environment for our benefit. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read John <b>12</b>:1-6</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. “The soul that sins will die.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Judas was a son of Abraham with all <i>“the
rights and privileges that pertain thereunto,”</i> but his ancestry didn’t keep
him from sin and death. God judges each of us by the deeds done in our own body
whether good or bad, and warns that the road to eternity leads from deeds to
destiny. Living is a deliberate thing. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Ezekiel <b>18</b>:4, 5-13; Luke <b>3</b>:8-9; Romans <b>2</b>:6</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Count yourself fortunate if you have Christian parents. You
may not appreciate their constant influence till you go away on your own, but
accept their help now in strengthening your will to meet temptation later. Your
forefathers’ faith won’t prevent your eternal destruction. Remember Judas, the
descendant of Abraham. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Acts <b>1</b>:24-25</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>God created us with the power of thought and the gift of choice.
Consequently, we’re not machines that run on heredity and environment. We are
persons, whose lives depend on our <i>use</i> of heredity and our <i>reaction</i>
to environment. We’ll be whatever we will to be; we’ll do whatever we will to
do; and we will what we do. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Ezekiel <b>18</b>:21-32</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Heredity and environment are the post and crossbeam on which
today’s social evolutionists would crucify the human race. But our eternal
death hangs on our own free will to die. The last scene in the life of Judas
Iscariot shows him as the one that put the noose around his neck and cast
himself down from the tree of life. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>27</b>:3-5</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
