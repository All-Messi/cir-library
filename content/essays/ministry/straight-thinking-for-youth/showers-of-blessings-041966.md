<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>Showers of Blessings</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>041966</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>SALVATION. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Our
greatest blessing is salvation. We remember Christ’s death and resurrection
throughout the year as well as at “Easter” time, but we note that about this
time in the spring about two thousand years ago, Christ died and arose from the
tomb. In doing so, he made a way to deliver us from our past sins; in so doing,
he also provided continued forgiveness of subsequent sins through prayer. He
gave obedient people hope of eternal life in heaven. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Galatians <b>4</b>:10-11; Romans
<b>4</b>:24-25; Acts <b>8</b>:13, 18-22</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:justify'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span
style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>HUMAN ASSOCIATIONS. </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>God understood the makeup of the
people he made. Because he built us with a need for social life, he considered
it not good that we should live alone. So, he established marriage, the home,
and other acquaintances that help quench our thirst for personal associations.
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Genesis <b>2</b>:18-25</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>SPIRITUAL ASSOCIATIONS</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>. It’s amazing that we can fellowship with God. Yet we do have this
ongoing association with him through the Holy Spirit who dwells within us as an
“earnest” (or downpayment) on our eternal inheritance, through Jeus who has
promised to be with us till the end, and through God in personal prayers and
devotion to him. We use these spiritual associations to satisfy our need for
relationships. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Ezekiel <b>37</b>:26-27; Ephesians <b>1</b>:13-14; Matthew <b>28</b>:20</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'>PHYSICAL NECESSITIES</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>. God blesses us with every physical blessing we need—the
food we eat, the water we drink, the clothes we wear, the shelter that protects
us from the elements. These things alone make us realize our blessedness, not
counting the other benefits we have identified already. </span><span
style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>6</b>:25-32</span><span style='font-size:
9.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>MENTAL POWERS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>We
are not like the unconscious trees of the woods or the animals of the field
because he has given us the ability to think and remember in a way that is
beyond comparison with anything else he created on earth. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Isaiah <b>46</b>:8-9; 2 Peter <b>1</b>:12-15</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>PHYSICAL POWERS</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>. Unlike
grass on a hillside, we move about and carry out physical exercise. Our
physical nature also combines with our mental capabilities as we maintain
complete dominion over the dumb beasts of the earth.</span><span
style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'> (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Genesis <b>1</b>:27-28</span><span style='font-size:
9.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>‘Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'>EMOTIONAL POWERS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>We are distinguished from all other earthly creations by our
emotional nature. We can feel sad; we can be happy. But the greatest difference
lies in our ability to love other people that we can see as well as God that we
cannot see. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 John
<b>4</b>:20-21</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Virgil Warren, <u>Straight</u>, April 10, 1966, p13</span></p>
