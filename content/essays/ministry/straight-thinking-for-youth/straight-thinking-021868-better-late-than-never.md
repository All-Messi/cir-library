<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>BETTER LATE THAN NEVER</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>“Today is the happiest day of my life.” These were the words of Mr. Carr,
who was recently baptized one week after his seventy-seventh birthday.
Christians are happy when a person finally comes to Christ even though he has
lived so long without him. It is better to come late than never to come at all,
but is it not be better to have the joy of serving Jesus even from the days of
your youth? (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Ecclesiastes <b>11</b>:9-10</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>One night early in Jesus’ ministry, a Pharisee named Nicodemus went to
speak with the new prophet in Israel. This full-grown man was startled at the
command to be born again! But Jesus was talking about the conversion birth—the start
of a new spiritual life we should not neglect because we’re too busy, too
afraid, or too proud. Someday it will be too late! (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>3</b>:1-5</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Hundreds knew Nicodemus as a ruler of the Jewish nation. We wonder how
many of them supposed that he was party to the crime that his fellow rulers
committed in killing Christ. How many people really know you? Are you sure they
understand everything you do? You will not always have a chance to correct
false impressions later. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Luke <b>24</b>:19-20</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Like Joseph of Arimathea, Nicodemus seems not to have made
public his personal faith in Christ. If he had, the Jewish leaders could not
have said that no scholar of the scriptures had accepted Jesus’ claim to be the
Christ. Let’s not hesitate to speak up for Christ while we’re young. Why wait
till his enemies announce victory before we take up the battle? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>7</b>:45-48; <b>19</b>:38</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Nicodemus was a member of the Sanhedrin. This court had
decreed that anyone who confessed Christ would be put out of the synagogue. If
Nicodemus had confessed Christ openly, he may have been cast out of the
Sanhedrin itself. But why should any of us try to continue as leaders of the
people if it means failing to enter the kingdom of God? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>9</b>:22</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Nicodemus was rich. If he had been put out of the Sanhedrin, he probably
would have lost his source of wealth. But is keeping his wealth meant losing
Christ, would he not have been better off losing the wealth? Do you suppose
that using his money to buy a hundred pounds of aloes and myrrh to bury his
Lord with, compensated for his earlier reluctance? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>19</b>:39-40</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Unlike Nicodemus, most of us don’t have to overcome the
temptation of wealth, position, fear, or fame; yet we hesitate longer than he
did to profess openly our Christian faith. We can make a clean break with such
a past, its sin, and its hesitation. Better late than never? Why not now? (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read John <b>1</b>:5</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
