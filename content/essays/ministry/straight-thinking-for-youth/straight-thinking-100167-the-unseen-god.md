<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE UNSEEN GOD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> The most obvious things are not always the easiest to see. Did you ever
look at a road map and finally discover that the name you were searching for
was spelled in the largest letters on the map? The most certain fact ever known
is that God exists; once people come to believe in God, they can’t imagine how
they ever didn’t. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Luke <b>24</b>:13-32</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> <i>“No one has ever seen God.”</i> We believe in the invisible things of
the physical realm; we can also accept by faith the spiritual God we cannot
see. Our faith is reasonable because we realize that everything in the
limitless universe originally had to come from something of sufficient power to
produce it. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 John
<b>4</b>:12</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> <i>“Fools say in their heart, ‘There’s no God.’” </i>Your science
instructor might be offended if you said his house looked like no one had
designed it. Yet that same teacher may stand before you in a classroom and
conjecture that the heavens and earth just happened somehow or other. How
fool-ish to reject God as the only rational explanation of the whole present
order of things! Intelligence is the father of design; chance is the author of
confusion. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Psalm <b>14</b>:1</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>From the minute complexity of the one-called euglena to the
host of vast solar systems, there is an orderliness that implies a purpose in
it all. Pattern signifies purpose. God has a plan in everything he has designed
including us. We fulfill our purpose of being <i>“to the praise of his glorious
grace.”</i> (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Ephesians <b>1</b>:3-12</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> The heavens declare the glory of their Creator. The writers of some
textbooks insult our intelligence by asking us to believe that the magnificent
earth and sky is the handiwork of chance. A lovely painting does not come from an
accidental spattering of color; neither did the star-spangled heavens come
except by the intent and genius of the Artist. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Psalm <b>19</b>:1-2</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> We believe the unseen on the basis of the seen. The visible material
universe enables us to see that the invisible God exists, that he is powerful,
intelligent, infinite in glory, and has a purpose in his creation. But only the
Bible reveals what that purpose is, how we fit into it, and what we need to do
to fulfill our intended role. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Ephesians <b>1</b>:13-14</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>We don’t see<i> </i>God now, but we know him. We can learn
about him in his word, even though finite people cannot understand infinite
God. No one has seen God yet, because he is a spirit; but when we leave the
physical body, we will see him as he is because we’ll have a spiritual body
like his. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 John <b>3</b>:2</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
