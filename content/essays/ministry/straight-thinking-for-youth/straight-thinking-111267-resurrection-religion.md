<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>RESURRECTION RELIGION</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Job said, <i>“There is hope for a tree if it’s cut down, that it will
sprout again”</i>; but, <i>“If a man dies, will he live again?</i>” Jesus
answered this haunting question by showing that people do not go <i>into</i>
the grave, but <i>through</i> it <i>to</i> heaven on the other side. Is it
incredible that the corpse in a coffin will live again? Not anymore! (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Job <b>14</b>:7-14</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The resurrection of Jesus makes other people feel a connection with the
spiritual realm. Like a possession of a deceased relative that unites us with
the one that has passed beyond the veil, the empty tomb at the foot of Golgotha
is a permanent memorial to the conquest of death and a visible association
between the temporal and the eternal. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read 2 Corinthians <b>4</b>:16, 18</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Occasionally we hear someone say that if there were a God, he would not
allow friends or loved ones to die. Now that Jesus has risen from the dead as
proof and prefigure of our own resurrection, no one has a right to bear a
grudge against God, because the death of righteous people is promotion to
better things rather than an end to it all. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Corinthians <b>15</b>:22-23; 42-49, 55-58</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The disciples huddled together in the gloomy midday darkness
on crucifixion day. Their despondent sorrow and fear of the Jewish leaders left
them on resurrection day. Like these disciples who preached the Pentecost
gospel in front of their enemies, our belief in the resurrection of Jesus gives
us a confidence and joy that will overcome the world. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>23</b>:49; John <b>19</b>:19-20;
1 Corinthians <b>15</b>:19</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The resurrection of Jesus gives us a firm faith that he is
the Christ. The tremendous impact of his preaching and miracles and glorious
claims simply does not fit with his shameful death between two criminals in the
end. What an anticlimax! Besides, he had claimed that he would rise from the
dead. What a fraud! But he did rise! What a finish! (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>16</b>:21.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>We believe that Jesus shed his blood on the cross of Calvary to cleanse
the sins of people that believe on him. But if Jesus had not resurrected, why
would we think his death differed from the death of those he died with? Only
the victim on the middle cross left us an empty grave! (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark <b>15</b>:27; 1 Corinthians
<b>15</b>:17</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>If Christ had not arisen, there would have been no Christian
religion. At his death his disciples lost all hope, and his enemies claimed
victory. Since Jesus’ preaching revolved around his claim to deity, his message
could not be true if he could be conquered: how can men kill God? Truly the
Christian religion is resurrection religion. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Acts <b>5</b>:34-39</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
