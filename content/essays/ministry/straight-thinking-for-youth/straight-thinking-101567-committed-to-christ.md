<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>101567</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>COMMITTED TO CHRIST</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>You committed yourself and your life to Christ by obeying God’s plan of
salvation to the extent of being baptized. You must not submit to the
temptation of the teen years when the lure of sins is especially strong. If you
forsake the love you had for Christ at the first, you relinquish the hope you
have through him in the end. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Revelation <b>2</b>:4, 5, 7.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. “I can’t; I must.”</span></i><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'> We go to school several hours a day; then we practice
football, basketball, track, or baseball; we are in class plays, band, and
chorus; we attend all the school functions; we study many hours a week at home;
are we keeping your commitment to Christ? Too many good things are not good if
they crowd out Christ. We would be refusing Christ if we say no to his
servants. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>13</b>:7,
22</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Young people often have someone they want to be like. It is good to have
a good goal; too often the idols of teenagers may not be good. Let Jesus be our
example as we grow intellectually, physically, spiritually, and socially. He is
not an image set in stone; he is an ideal for life. So we commit ourselves to
being like him. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Luke <b>2</b>:42, 52</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>By the time we reach our sophomore year, we begin developing
ideas about our future occupation. We should also decide early that whatever we
do we will fit it into the purposes of Christ. Tomorrow’s only witnesses will
be those of us who commit our lives to him today. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Colossians <b>3</b>:17</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Popularity is a popular pursuit among teenagers. But since
our schoolmates do not always prefer what fits with our Christian commitment, we
cannot expect to be pleasing both to them and to Christ all the time. We can
have <i>“favor with God and people”</i> but not necessarily with God and all people.
It is better to have genuine popularity with a few. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>4</b>:4</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Have you ever helped win someone to Christ? Have you ever tried? If we
are firmly committed to what he stands for, we will be concerned that our
friends believe the important things we believe. Transform other people’s
practices rather than conform to theirs. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>12</b>:2.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>One life—just one chance to amount to something. A lasting
Christian influence is our best gift to our friends, and so it is the most
satisfying possession of our own. Combine your vocation and your commitment to him
by being an effective witness in whatever profession you choose. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>16</b>:25-26</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
