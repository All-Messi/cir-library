<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>IT TAKES FAITH</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Of all things, Moses refused to be the son of Pharaoh’s daughter. That
refusal cost him the pharaohship. A comparison of the Old Testament record with
Egyptian history seemingly shows that Moses could have been in line for the
throne of Egypt. How many people would turn down the presidency of the United
States to serve God? It takes faith to refuse. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>11</b>:24.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Moses had become an adult when he left the palace in Egypt. The kind of
faith he demonstrated is a matter for grown-ups with mature minds. Young
person, the teen years have the highest fatality rate in regard to spiritual
interest and Christian faithfulness. This is the very time when God expects you
to take your stand for the truth. Those who are grow-up should act like it
faithwise. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1
Corinthians <b>16</b>:13</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Appetites are strongest when they’re young, but Moses rejected the sinful
pleasures of court life. Sin may bring satisfaction, but only for a season. The
pleasure of sin is a madness of the moment; it only picks us up to let us down.
Young people may enjoy living in sin, but they don’t live happily ever after. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>11</b>:25</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Instead of wandering aimlessly after the desert mirages of
pleasure and sin, Moses chose <i>“to suffer ill treatment with the people of
God.”</i> We will not be the only ones who suffer for God; fellow Christians
throughout the world will share ill treatment with us. And even as the pleasure
of sin is for a season, so also will be the affliction of God’s people; but the
punishment for sin and the reward for suffering are eternal. Choose wisely. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Peter <b>5</b>:8-9</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>He looked <i>“unto the recompense of reward.”</i> The reward
motive for serving God should not be considered selfish one; but if it is so
called, we must add that there is nothing wrong with self-ishness as long as
personal interest does not come at the expense of general welfare and as long
as we do not gain our reward at the expense of someone else. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>11</b>:26</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The genius of Christianity is that we cannot receive our own reward
without helping other people receive it too. We must lead the people of bondage
through the deserts of life and the waters of Jordan, looking to ourselves
lest, having saved others, we should only see the mountains of the heavenly
Jerusalem from the peak of our earthly Pisgah. We can’t make it into the
promised land alone. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
1 Corinthians <b>9</b>:27; Deuteronomy <b>34</b>:1-4</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. “By faith he forsook Egypt.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> It’s easier to refuse the bird in
the bush than to let go of the one in the hand, but Moses left the throne room
of Egypt for the deserts of Sinai, and forsook being king of earth to be
servant of heaven. It takes faith to forsake. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>11</b>:27</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
