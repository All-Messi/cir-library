<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>Showers of Blessings</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>040366</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>SOURCE</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>. When Jesus
returned to the Father, he ascended into the sky and a cloud received him out
of sight. Showers, coming from the sky, often symbolize the source of
blessings—heaven; because <i>“every good gift and every perfect gift comes from
above--from the Father.”</i> (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Mark <b>16</b>:19; Acts <b>1</b>:1-11; James <b>1</b>:17</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>ABUNDANCE</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>. Showers
symbolize proper amount, abundance, and lasting effect. God does not storm us
with blessings, and he doesn’t give us mere sprinklings. Dew, though it pictures
gentleness, passes with day comes. <i>“There will be showers of blessings.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>CONSTRUCTIVENESS.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Blessings comfort and aid the ones
they come to. A cloudburst may wash the ground and break down the stalks of
ripening grain. A shower softens the dirt for easy growing, brightens the
scenery for greater beauty, and waters the plants of the field and the trees of
the orchards to grow and produce their fruits. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Ezekiel <b>34</b>:26-27</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'>UNIVERSALITY. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>When showers come, they fall on every blade of grass in the pasture,
not just on certain ones. Even so, God’s <i>“sun rises on the evil and on the
good, and he sends rain on the just and the unjust.”</i> These examples from
nature illustrate to us that God gives many blessings to everyone, whether
they’re obedient or not. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Zechariah <b>10</b>:1; Matthew<b> 5</b>:48</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>CONCERN. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>People
need food, drink, and clothing, which all depend on the rain showered down from
above. Since God chooses to send the rain, he shows his concern for everybody.
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Peter <b>5</b>:7;
Matthew <b>6</b>:26, 30-33</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>EFFECRTIVENESS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Showers
accomplish what they are sent to do; they don’t return to the atmosphere
without making the ground <i>“bring forth and bud”</i> or without <i>“giving
seed to the sower and bread to the eater.”</i> God’s blessings do not return to
him void, but prosper in the service he sent them to perform. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Isaiah <b>55</b>:10-11</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>EVIDENCE.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> One
value of the blessings we experience is the additional evidence they give of
God’s existence, his greatness, and his goodness. They give reason for being
grateful to him. Showers of blessings make everyone everywhere without an
excuse for rejecting or perverting the commandments he gave to guide us to
present happiness on earth and eternal blessedness in heaven. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read <b>19</b>:1; Acts <b>14</b>:17;
Romans<b> 1</b>:20-23</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>).</span></p>

line-height:115%'>Virgil Warren, <u>Straight</u>, April 3, 1966, p11</span></p>
