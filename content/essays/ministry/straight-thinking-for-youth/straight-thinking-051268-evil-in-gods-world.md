<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>EVIL IN GOD’S WORLD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. “It was very good.”</span></i><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'> God’s original estimate of his wonderful world may
not seem to be correct in this time of war and riots and sin. Consequently,
people that distrust God and doubt his word need answers to questions like
these: <i>“If God is good, where did evil come from? It he is all-powerful, why
doesn’t he do something about it”</i> (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Genesis<b>1</b>:31</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Satan is the source of sin. God did not bring evil into his creation; the
devil sinned against God, and God cast him out of heaven. When God made mankind
and put them on earth, Satan tempted them to disobey God; so Satan introduced
sin into the good world God had made. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read John <b>8</b>:44; 2 Peter <b>2</b>:4; Genesis <b>3</b>:1-19</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>God created us with free will; we can choose even to disobey
him of we want to. Likewise, even Satan was not evil by creation but by choice.
We don’t blame God for Satan’s sin or for our sin in the world. (R</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>ead Romans <b>2</b>:4; Titus <b>2</b>:1-5</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The evil that’s in the world does not remain because of God’s
inability to overcome it, but because of his patience with sinners. He realizes
that our physical desires can lead us astray by temptation. Rather than being
critical of God for not removing sinners from the world, we should thank him
for giving <u>us</u> a second chance. That is to say, <u>we</u> are among the
ones that God would be removing because we are part of the problem. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 2 Peter <b>2</b>:9</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Although God himself did not bring sin into the world he is
responsible for it now that it’s here. He has provided a way of overcoming sin
by giving his God to die in our place on our behalf, and by making the benefit
of Chris’s sacrifice our for the accepting. Instead of destroying sinners, he
chose to give them an opportunity to repent from their sin. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrew <b>2</b>:14-18</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>. <i>“There’ll come a time.”</i> Although God is patient with our sins, his
patience cannot continue forever because, if he withheld his power against
evil, he would not be meeting his righteous responsibility to rid the world of
it. Even as we’re thankful for God’s patience with our sin, even so we are
thankful that his patience is not forever. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read 2 Peter <b>3</b>:10-18</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The eternal triumph of God over evil requires death,
judgment, and hell: death to keep sinful people from living forever, judgment
to separate them from the righteous, and hell to keep them separate. Human
sinfulness does not contradict divine righteousness. In fact, God’s
righteousness provides our only hope of salvation from our sins. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Genesis <b>3</b>:22-24; Hebrews <b>9</b>:27;
Revelation <b>20</b>:10,14-15</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>
