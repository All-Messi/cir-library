<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>DEAD FAITH</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>In chapter 2 of his epistle, James, the Lord’s brother, fires away at
people that do nothing about their belief in Christ. Like tracer bullets, his
warnings come again and again throughout the chapter. <i>“Faith, if doesn’t
have works, is dead by itself”</i>; <i>“people are justified by works, not by
faith”</i>; <i>“faith without works is dead.”</i> His meaning becomes clearer
if we use “belief” in place of “faith.” Believing does not change anything till
it does something. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
James <b>2</b>:17-26.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Other people can’t see what we believe in our mind; they have to look at
what we do in our life. If all we have is intellectual belief that’s not
demonstrated in practical action, no one where we study, work, or play ever
profits from it, imitates our life, or glorifies God because of it. Besides,
belief alone does not produce its benefits in our own life. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>5</b>:14-16</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>If a cold, hungry beggar asks for food and clothes, I don’t help him much
by saying, <i>“I’m convinced that your biggest problem is what will get you
first, the hunger or the cold; and I hope you find food and clothes.”</i> The
beggar would be colder than ever and hungrier than before. Faith without works:
nobody is better off because of it. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read James <b>2</b>:15-16</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The only claim some people may have on heaven is that Jesus
ate with them and taught in their streets. They believed what they heard and saw,
but they didn’t do anything about it. It’s not the one that says <i>“Lord,
Lord”</i> but the one that does his will, that can enter the kingdom on
Judgment Day. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke
<b>13</b>:25-26; Matthew <b>7</b>:21</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Do what you can. God doesn’t ask for what you can’t do, but
he does expect you to do what you can. A woman in Bethany anointed Jesus with
expensive ointment, and he established a memorial testimonial for her because
she did it in demonstration of her faith: wherever the gospel would be
preached, the preachers should say of her, <i>“She did what she could.”</i> (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark <b>14</b>:3-9</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Act now. The faith you have now you can measure by what you do now; good
intentions don’t count. People young and old will end up lost because of good
intentions. So to speak, too often they stood listening as the invitation hymn
closed, <i>“Almost, but lost.”</i> (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Acts <b>24</b>:24-25</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sinful people cannot earn salvation; God must give it, and
indeed he has promised to give it to those who have faith, those who put their
faith in his Son. God gives salvation, but we must have faith strong enough
that we will act now on what we believe. He gives salvation by considering us
righteous on the condition that we commit ourselves to the One who <u>is</u>
righteous, which means committing ourselves to his values and purposes.
Otherwise,<i> “faith without works is dead.”</i> (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Ephesians <b>2</b>:8; James <b>2</b>:26</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.) </span></p>
