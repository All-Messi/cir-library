<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE CHURCH OF TOMORROW TODAY</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The church means a lot to many young people. <u>Straight</u> carries
articles about outstanding teens, the kind that seem to spend every spare
minute at the church, that find a satisfaction in doing a good job for God, and
are willing to try anything for him. Such examples are good ones to follow and
better ones to set. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
John <b>6</b>:27.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Not every student makes A’s, but parents, teachers, and administrators
exert tremendous pressure on young people to make good grades in order to get
into the crowded colleges and universities across our land. We’ve got to hand
it to students that must struggle with their lessons, but love their church
enough to attend all its services regardless of homework that night or tests
the next day. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Ecclesiastes <b>12</b>:12</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Strong Christian conviction show up in young folk that bring their
girlfriends or boyfriends to church with them. We witness an even more
encouraging sign of a rather somber variety when a faithful Christian teen
breaks up with a friend that continually refuses to have anything to do with
the church. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 2
Corinthians <b>6</b>:14-15</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Your home may be one of the many households where you are the
only one concerned with the affairs of the church. Brothers and sisters
couldn’t care less, and parents show a polite indifference to appeals from
members of the congregation. You represent those who are worthy of the Lord
because you give him first place in your life. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>10</b>:37</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Steve was a Jewish lad from New York City. He converted to Christianity despite
his parents’ strong opposition. As a result, they disinherited him and, after
orthodox Jewish custom, held his funeral as if he’d died. We thank God for
young folk like Steve who rise above all obstacles in order to minister in the
church of Jesus Christ. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Luke <b>12</b>:51-53</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Ronnie repeatedly got in trouble and appeared before the judge’s stand
many times. “Juvenile delinquent” proper described him. Today he’s an example
of Christian living and is preparing for service in the kingdom. He loves the
church because it has done so much for him. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke<b> 7</b>:41-48</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Have you ever seen an outstanding athlete or musician pass up
a sizeable scholarship? Christian colleges are filled with students that have turned
down offers like these in order to prepare for specialized service. Consecrated
young people are the hope of the church. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>11</b>:8-16</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.) </span></p>
