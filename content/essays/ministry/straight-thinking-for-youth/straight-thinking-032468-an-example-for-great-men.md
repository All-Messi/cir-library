<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>AN EXAMPLE FOR GREAT MEN</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> The apostle Paul grew up in Tarsus, Cilicia, an education center of his
time. He studied in Jerusalem under Gamaliel, one of Judaism’s most famous
rabbis. He led the early Jewish persecutions against the church in Jerusalem.
After his notable conversion, he became the most zealous propagator and
defender of the faith the world has known. In this respect, he was the greatest
man that ever lived. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Acts<b> 22</b>:3; <b>9</b>:1-6, 17-20, 31; Galatians 1:<b>13</b>-14; 1
Corinthians <b>15</b>:9-10</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The mighty and noble and wise constitute a minority among us, so the
gospel has fewer of them to call, and fewer of them answer the call. Society
admires the mighty; the noble glorify themselves; wise men may think they don’t
need God. But Paul led countless numbers to admire and glorify and depend on
God. He is a refreshing exception to the musty majority. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Corinthians<b> 1</b>:18-31</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The greater the man the greater the opposition he generates. Luke’s
outline history of the early church records eight attempts to kill Paul,
besides the uproars that Jews continually stirred up against him. Young,
talented Christians soon learn that evil adversaries still concentrate on their
most capable opponents. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Acts <b>10</b>:23, 29; <b>14</b>:5, 19; <b>17</b>:5, 21-31; <b>23</b>:12-13;<b>
25</b>:1-3</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>A teenager’s talent is often his destruction. A young guitar
player may get lost in the darkness of the nightclub where he performs. A
gifted mechanic may crash against the wall of a nearby underpass. The road to
ruin is paved with the misused talents of younger people. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>15</b>:11-24</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>With his vast armies, Alexander the Great conquered the
Mediterranean world and beyond, spreading Greek culture in three continents.
With a few co-workers, Paul spread the gospel from Jerusalem to Rome,
conquering people’s hearts and minds for Christ. Whose work remains as the best
and most lasting contribution to civilization? (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>13</b>:20-21</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Paul is gone, and the leaders of the church from then till now are gone
too. The next thirty years will skim off another layer of the milk of humanity.
If no new cream of the crop rises to the top, your generation will amount to
nothing more than skimmed milk. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read 2 Timothy <b>4</b>:1-6</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Everybody looks for greatness in promising youth. If you have
a promising future, you’ll be in demand. The world is looking for great men to
lead it; Satan goes after great men to destroy it; God wants great men to save
it. Now is the time to come to the aid of your world by taking the apostle <i>par
excellence</i> as your example of a great man for great men. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Timothy <b>4</b>:12;<b> 6</b>:3-16</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
