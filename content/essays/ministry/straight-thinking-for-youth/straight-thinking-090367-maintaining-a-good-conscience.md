<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>MAINTAINING A GOOD CONSCIENCE</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>BE AWARE.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> We have
a good conscience if it’s free from the guilt feeling. The trouble is, we’re
like almost everyone else; we probably do something every day that’s wrong and then
feel guilty about it afterwards. How can we maintain a clear conscience when
we’ve offended someone, including God? The first thing in curing a bad
conscience is to understand that sin ruins a good one. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Acts <b>24</b>:16</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>BE SURE. </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Sometimes we feel guilty when we
shouldn’t. Only by thinking about God’s word can we be sure of what displeases him.
When publicly accused as a criminal, Paul replied that his conscience was
clear. Knowing we are not in error gives us a good conscience. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Corinthians <b>8</b>:4-8;
Romans <b>7</b>:7; Acts <b>24</b>:1-16</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>RECTIFY. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>If our
conscience bothers us because we wronged someone, we won’t feel right till we
make amends. If the friend does not accept the apology, we have to just let it
go and deal with the consequences. More than likely, though, if we’re mean it, other
people will restore our relationship. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>19</b>:7-10.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'>BE CLEANSED. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>When we sin against other people, we also sin against God who make them.
Our friends may have forgiven us, but what about him? Besides being sorry for
sin, we need to commit ourselves to Jesus as God’s righteous Son, and identify
with him in baptism in order to have his blood cleanse the guilt from our
conscience. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Acts <b>22</b>:6-16;
Hebrews <b>10</b>:1-10</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>PRAY.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> Prayer is an
important part of overcoming an accusing conscience. When our conscience
catches us red-handed, we should be mature enough to admit it and confess it.
We’re not hiding anything from God by not owning up to it in prayer; he already
knows. Admitting we have sinned puts us at his mercy to forgive us if he will.
He will; that’s the point. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Hebrews <b>4</b>:12-13; 1 John <b>1</b>:8-10</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>HAVE FAITH</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>. Have
you experienced that gnawing guilt even after prayer? You don’t need to <i>beg</i>
God to do what he has <i>volunteered </i>to do; just let him. He’s not trying
to see how many people he can make feel bad or send to hell. Don’t suppose he doesn’t
understand; Jesus knows perfectly well what it’s like to be human, and he
intercedes for us. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Hebrews <b>4</b>:14-15; 1 Timothy <b>2</b>:5, 8.</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>SIN NO MORE.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> No
sin is so terrible that we are hopelessly alienated from God. We need not brood
over past failure; forget it. Resolve to meet this day with a heart of zeal.
You need not be afraid of defeat; God is on your side. If he is for you, who
can be against you? We maintain a good conscience by knowing that we are doing
our best for the Master. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Romans <b>8</b>:31-39</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
