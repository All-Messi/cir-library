<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>RESOLVED:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>TO BE TRUE. </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“There
are those who trust you.”</span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Being able to
trust us brings so much satisfaction to people that care about us and place a
high value on our character. But the fullest confidence leaves the emptiest
feeling if it’s lost. If we have never looked into a face filled with the
disappointment of betrayed trust, we never want to; if we have, we will never
want to again. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Hebrews <b>13</b>:18.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>TO BE PURE. </span><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“There are those who care.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> We should do what’s right whether
anyone cares or not, but knowing that others are interested adds incentive to
“keep clean.” Let’s never get the idea that no one cares what we do. Christians
should not aim to please people particularly, but we should try to please good
people and God. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Hebrews <b>12</b>:1-3</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>TO BE STRONG.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> <i>“There is much to suffer.”</i> If
religion doesn’t hurt, it’s no good. Christ prescribed a cross a day for his
followers. Other people, recognizing the values of the church, have tried to
have its benefits without its pain. But God’s rejection of all designs for a
painless cross is symbolized by Christ’s rejection of the wine mixed with gall
and myrrh to deaden the sufferings of crucifixion. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>27</b>:33-36</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'>TO BE BRAVE. </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>“There is much to dare.”</span></i><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'> We have to be willing to take a dare. Society dares us
to be different; we have to take that dare. When current fashions do not accord
with a Christian sense of modesty, when prevalent dating customs do not follow
Christian ideals, if the group’s plans for the New Year’s Eve party don’t fit
with the principles of decency, we must dare to be an exception (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>12</b>:2</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>TO BE HUMBLE.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> <i>“You
know your weakness,”</i> the secret sin even our friends don’t know about. What
they don’t know may not hurt them, but it can hurt us if we are not humbled
when we consider that it is known to God and will be revealed to openly
someday. Humility is a virtue produced by sin and is that by which sin in turn
is overcome. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>12</b>:1-3</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>TO BE PRAYERFUL </span><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“You must keep close to God.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> A person wonders whether some young
people ever pray except at church. Failing to pray shows the lack of feeling
dependent on God, and it raises a serious question about a our love for him. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>18</b>:1-7.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>TO BE FAITHFUL </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“You
must keep the path Christ trod.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Treading the “straight and narrow” is like walking a
tightrope—a difficult way to keep and a fatal one to lose. Watch that your step
is in line with the will of Christ. Resolve to keep doing the same old things
that need to be done, to begin doing what ought to be done, and to quit
everything else. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Matthew <b>7:</b>14; 1 John <b>2</b>:24-29</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
