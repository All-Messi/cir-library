<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'>Showers of Blessings</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:.25in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>041766</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>PRAYER. </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>“The
prayer of a good person accomplishes a lot.”</span></i><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'> God gives liberally to us if we ask in faith for
blessings that accord with his will. He is approachable. When we pray, he will
not rebuke us for being so presumptuous as to ask him for something. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>1</b>:5-6; <b>4</b>:3; <b>5</b>:16b;
Matthew <b>7</b>:7</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>WORK</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>. </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>When we pray for spiritual blessings,
there are things we must do. The condition for the fulfillment of any appropriate
request is <i>“If you do your part.”</i> There is no value in asking God for
new clothes if we’re not willing to work to buy them. Prayer never serves as a
shortcut or substitute for work. </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>(Read James <b>2</b>:14-26.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday.</span></i><i><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'> </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>PATIENCE.
</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>After much prayer and
hard work, a desired blessing may not come. God’s response to our prayers and
work may not be “No” so much as “Not now.” The blessing we look for may hinder our
spiritual maturity at this time. Besides, some things operate like planted
seeds that call for patience until the harvest. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>5</b>:7-8</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> </span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>PERSEVERANCE.
</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>We need to keep our eyes
on one goal in order to receive blessings. We cannot have one eye on heaven and
the other on the world and think we’ll be receiving the Lord’s blessings. There’s
also the type of perseverance that carries us through temptation, trials, and
even persecution for righteousness’ sake. The blessing in such cases is the “crown
of life.”</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'> (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read <b>1</b>:2-4, 6-8, 12; <b>5</b>:10-11</span><span
style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>OBEDIENCE. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Knowing
our needs, God has laid down principles for us to follow to be happy. When we
disregard these commandments, we are not living in harmony with our nature and
needs or his purposes. Consequently, we don’t feel happy or fulfilled. We need
to obey to have a “full” life of blessings here and eternal life of blessings
hereafter.</span><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'> (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>1</b>:22-25</span><span
style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)   </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>SHARING. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>All
blessings come ultimately from God, but he may channel them through other
people. Whether the blessings from God through others reach us depends on
whether the ones coming through us reach them. If they don’t receive theirs
from us, we may not receive ours from them. In addition, some things like love
increase in our own experience only by giving them away to other people. Blessings
increase by passing through some to others. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>6</b>:38; Matthew <b>7</b>:12</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>CONTENTMENT. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Being
satisfied with what we have is a practical way of being blessed with what we
need. Happiness, the real blessing we are looking for, often does not come
otherwise. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>1</b>:9;
1 Timothy <b>6</b>:5-6</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Virgil Warren, <u>Straight</u>, April 17, 1966, p6</span></p>
