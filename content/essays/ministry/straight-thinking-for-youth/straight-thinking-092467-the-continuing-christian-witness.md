<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE CONTINUING CHRISTIAN WITNESS</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Since the first days of the church’s existence, Christians have assembled
on Sunday. The apostles’ teaching, fellowship, breaking of bread, and prayer
distinguish this gathering from others. Our regular attendance at church is a
personal testimony to those outside the Lord’s body; it is an example to other
young people of the congregation. Sharing in worship strengthens us for a
persistent witness for Christ. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Acts <b>2</b>:41-42; Hebrews <b>10</b>:22-25</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> The school-day routine provides opportunities to illustrate how Christ
modifies normal living. Classmates observe that we do not copy from others or
cheat on tests. They never hear a teacher calling us down for talking or
disrupting class. In short, they are aware of our Christianity in more ways
than we might think. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
1 Thessalonians <b>5</b>:22</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> If there is anywhere that fans will notice us as Christians, it is on a
sports field or floor. They may be sitting far up in the bleachers, but they can
see very well. They can hear too. If we are in the bleachers, a different kind
of response should come from us than from a spectator that is not Christian
when the referee makes a good call—for the other team. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Colossians <b>3</b>:17.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Christian youth are recognized by the activities they are
in; they are also known by what they’re not in, what they don’t do, and where
they don’t go. Where we go plus what we do equals what we are. Christian
entertainment assumes its own proper forms. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Peter <b>4</b>:14-15</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> A car can be a good gauge for determining how much of a Christian we
are. The average person on the street can tell what kind of youth we are by the
way we drive. So to speak, we have to let up on our testimony for Jesus when we
tramp down on the gas. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
1 Peter <b>2</b>:13-17</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Friday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Are your parents Christians? Rather frequently young people grow up in
homes where neither parent has accepted Christ. They often influence their
children against Christ. On the other hand, a persistent Christian son or
daughter may be able to lead parents to Christ. Keep your colors true, your
light bright in an effort ot win the ones you love. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Ephesians <b>6</b>:1-3</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> When the lessons are finished and there is no place to go, do we think
to study our Bible? Is that the only time? Throughout the rest of life, we will
seldom be at a loss for something to do. If we give Christian activity first
place now, it will be easier to discipline ourselves later when the
responsibilities of home, job, and civic duty seem to demand preference over
service in the church. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Luke <b>9</b>:23-25, 59-62</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>
