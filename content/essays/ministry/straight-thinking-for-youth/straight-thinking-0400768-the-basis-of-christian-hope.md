<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE BASIS OF CHRISTIAN HOPE</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> We hope for good things, not bad. We experience Christian hope as the
assured expectation of future good. It looks to the future because it becomes
reality as the future becomes the present. We have assured hope because God has
made the promise; we expect it because his promises are not conjectures or
probabilities, but history revealed ahead of time. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>8</b>:24</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The beating heart of Christian hope is the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
God has not entrusted that precious truth to the corruption that creeps into oral
tradition. Instead, he has provided us with four written accounts of Christ’s
mission and has committed to writing the guarantee of the hope we have in him.
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>1</b>:1-4</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The life and ministry of Jesus proceeded forward for over three years open
and aboveboard. He taught publicly in front of the thousands who followed him.
He died before the whole nation that had gathered in Jerusalem for the Passover
Feast. Jews from all over the world heard the first public announcement of his
resurrection and could see his empty tomb right outside the wall of the capital
city. <i>“This thing was not done in a corner.”</i> (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Act <b>26</b>:12-26</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Christian hope rests on reliable testimony of credible
witnesses. The facts of Christ’s life and teachings have been set down for us
by intelligent eyewitnesses and courageous men of high moral character. It is
unbelievable that the highest moral code in history would have been devised by
liars. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 2 Peter <b>1</b>:16-18</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>We believe God will fulfill the promises he’s made to us
because he has always fulfilled his promises to his people before. A thousand
years ahead of time, he said that the flesh of the Messiah would not see
corruption. Consequently, we have hope that he will also raise us from the dead
like he promised two thousand years ago. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Act <b>2</b>:25-31</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Christian hope arises from our firm trust in God’s ability to do what he decides.
We’re amazed by his power displayed in nature around us and history before us.
We’re thankful that his power combines with his love for mankind and his desire
to give hope to those who have no hope. <i>“Thanks to God for his unspeakable
gift.”</i> (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Ephesians <b>3</b>:1-21</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>If there’s no reason to live, life isn’t worth living. Young
people the world over stand with knives against their throats, <i>“tired of
living and scared of dying,”</i> and old man river just keeps rolling along.
These people will never escape the river of life on a beer bottle; they need the
hope we have to give them reason to live again and try again, because <i>“Where
there’s no vision [hope], the people perish.”</i> (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Proverbs <b>29</b>:18</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
