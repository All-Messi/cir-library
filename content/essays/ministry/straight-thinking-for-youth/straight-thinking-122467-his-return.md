<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>HIS RETURN</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>CERTAIN. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Jesus promised,
<i>“I’ll come back.”</i> The time is not specified, but the promise is sure. Every
attempt to predict the date of Christ’s return has utterly failed. The Lord
himself did not know when it would be; we don’t need to know either. The time
is not important because the fact is certain. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark <b>13</b>:26-27, 32</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>UNIVERSAL </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>It is no
secret, <i>“Every eye will see him.” </i>For the first time in history, every
person living and dead will see the same thing at the same time. The very
soldiers that hammered the spikes through Christ’s hands and feet will watch
him come down from the clouds. It will be a moment to remember—forever. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew<b> 25</b>:44-<b>51</b>:1</span><span
style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:115%'>.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>UNEXPECTED. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The
proof of a servant’s loyalty is whether he serves when he has a chance to do as
he pleases and whether he cares when the master’s gone away. Grown weary with
righteousness, a Christian might seek an interlude of drunkenness and sensuous
living, expecting the Lord to tarry a long while. What a pitiful picture as he
staggers off to hell with the rest. When a man gets careless, he gets caught. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>24</b>:44-51</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;
line-height:115%'>FINAL. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>God
destroyed people of Noah’s day, not because they were <i>“eating and . . . marrying,”</i>
but because that’s <i>all</i> they were doing. They were like those at Christ’s
return: absorbed in daily routines and the search for pleasure. God won’t give
them a second chance when he shuts the door of deliverance<i>. </i>(</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>24</b>:37-42.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>FEARFUL </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>In the
fall of 1939, residents north of Lawrenceburg, Indiana, panicked under the
midnight sky. High in the distant heavens a radiant light appeared and spread
in a funnel-shaped illumination that lit up the countryside with its shimmering
brilliance. People were on their knees begging God to save their souls. The
effects of the northern lights that night were nothing compared to what will
grip people’s hearts when Christ does come. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Revelation <b>6</b>:15-16.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>GLORIOUS. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The
glory of the Lord will exceed the brightness of the sun when he comes with
legions of angels to assemble the saints of all the ages. What a gathering that
will be, and what a joy to be part of it! How happy we will be to see all those
who have lived and died in the faith, and to meet the founder of that faith
face to face! (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1
Corinthians <b>15</b>:23, 51-55</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:9.0pt;line-height:
115%'>SOON. </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The fact
that Christ did not come today doesn’t mean he’ll never come. Instead, it means
that his return is one day nearer than it was yesterday. The only way to
prepare is to figure on today’s being the last day to get ready. Don’t wait for
tomorrow to make a believer out of you. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>2 Peter <b>3</b>:10-13; James <b>5</b>:8-9</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
