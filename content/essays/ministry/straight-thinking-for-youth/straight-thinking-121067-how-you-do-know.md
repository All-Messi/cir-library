<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:1.0pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>HOW YOU DO KNOW</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>One significant question is, <i>“How do people know they have the Holy
Spirit?”</i> Some believers think we can just <i>“feel it in hour bones.”</i>
But we cannot just rely on “feelings.” The Spirit of the Lord, who gave Samson
his extraordinary strength, left him because he told Delilah that the secret of
his great strength lay in his long hair. But <i>“he didn’t know the Lord had left
him.” </i>(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Judges <b>13</b>:2-5,
24-25; <b>14</b>:5-6, 19; <b>16</b>:15-17, 20</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Everything good includes what a Christian should be and do. If what
characterizes our lives is not good, we cannot claim to be Christians or to
have the Holy Spirit. If there’s no love in the heart, no joy in the spirit, or
no peace in the mind, God’s Spirit does not dwell in us. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Galatians <b>5</b>:22-24</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Some Christians associate speaking in other languages, working miracles,
or prophesying with the presence of the Holy Spirit. But we do not necessarily
lack the Spirit if we are not able to do these things. We don’t anticipate God
demonstrating his presence through miracles today as prominently as he did in
New Testament times. At that time the power to work miracles came only by a
special gift of Christ or by the laying on of the apostles’ hands. But there is
a way in which a Christian has the gift of the Spirit: committing themselves to
Jesus Christ. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Acts
<b>8</b>:12-18; <b>2</b>:38</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>God never breaks a promise. We cannot see the reward, but we
know whether heaven awaits us. God promises it in the Bible, and we know if we
are going what he tells us to do to go there. We also know whether we have the
Holy Spirit because God promises that in his word, and we know if we are doing
what he tells us to do to receive the Spirit. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 2 Peter <b>3</b>:9a; 2 Corinthians <b>1</b>:20a;
Ephesians <b>1</b>:13-14</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The first time the gospel was preached, Peter promised the
Spirit to his hearers and their descendants and to everyone who would answer
the call. We were not there that Pentecost Sunday, nor have we probably descended
from those who were. But we are among those “afar off” that can know we have
God’s Spirit if we follow God’s plan of salvation. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Acts <b>2</b>:38-39</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. “Well begun, half done”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> is sadly true. People receive the
Spirit when they become members of Christ’s body. But people may backslide; and
though they once had the Holy Spirit, they may be like Samson who lost that presence
of the Spirit. We know we have God’s Spirit if we are continuing in the
commandments he has set forth. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read 1 John <b>3</b>:24</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The Spirit’s presence in our life might be seen more easily
by others than by ourselves; they can see more by standing away than we can see
up close. The good influence of our life on other people will give us assurance
that we do know him. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
1 Corinthians <b>2</b>:1-4</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>
