<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>CHRISTIAN RESPONSIBILITY</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>How much responsibility do we have? That depends on how much
responsibility God has given us. He holds us responsible for what we can know.
We do well not to take firm views on how he will handle the heathen who’ve
never heard of him. But we that have Bibles in our homes cannot claim ignorance
of our God-given responsibility, which includes making known to the unreached
the fullness of the Bible’s sacred contents. (</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Ezekiel <b>3</b>:18-21</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>A demoniac that Jesus had delivered from the terrors of demon-possession
wanted to continue in the presence of his Savior, but Jesus told him to go back
to his house and friends and tell them what great things the Lord had done for
him. God gives each of us the task of making our circumstance our mission
field. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>8</b>:38-38</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>How much responsibility do we have? That depends on how much we have
accepted, because God can give as much as we can take. But accepting
responsibility is a matter of eternal importance and a skill of some
difficulty. We should take enough but not too much. In the Judgment, God will
compare the task we could have taken with the one we took, and the one we took
with how well we fulfilled it. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read James <b>3</b>:1</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>At Christ’s command, the Gadarene demoniac went back to face
the people that knew him as he was the day before. What an encouragement to
overcome our self-conscious fear! Though he had never “preached” before, the
man took up the challenge of this incredible ministry. What an example of
acceptance! (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Mark <b>5</b>:1-5,
20</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>How much responsibility we have depends on how much we have
performed. Teachers do not grade us by the difficulty of their assignments or
by whether we agree to do them. They grade us by what we do and how well we do
them. Christian responsibility can be a difficult subject; let’s not flunk it.
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Peter <b>1</b>:17</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>The demoniac did what Jesus sent him to do. Throughout the territory east
of the Sea of Galilee, he became a walking, talking testimony to God’s power.
The effect of his efforts became evident when Jesus returned to a warm welcome in
the Decapolis instead of a cold rejection. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>8</b>:35-37, 40</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>God has <i>given</i> us significant responsibilities; we do
not want to be like some that may not even <i>accept</i> them—or like others who
may accept one after another but seldom <i>perform</i> anything to completion.
We become encouraging examples of responsibility when what God gives us, what
we accept, and what we perform are one and the same. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 2 Timothy<b> 4</b>:7-8</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
