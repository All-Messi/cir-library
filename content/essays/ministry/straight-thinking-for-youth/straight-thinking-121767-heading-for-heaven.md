<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>HEADING FOR HEAVEN</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Heaven is not a come-as-you-are party. God doesn’t call us at the last
minute and expect us to put in an acceptable appearance. We know far enough in
advance that when death or the Lord’s return issues our final summons, we
should be prepared to accept it. We must come then and come as we are; but, if we’re
not ready, it will be no party for us. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>22</b>:1-14.</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Heaven’s not a retirement plan; it’s a career advancement. When we
finish our assignment here, we are in line for a promotion, not a pension. If we
want a position in heaven, we need to remember that God operates on the
principle that people faithful in little things will be faithful in big things
too. If we don’t shirk our work now, God will give us a place of responsibility
later. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>25</b>:14-30;
Luke <b>16</b>:10-12</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Which direction are we going in life? If we’re just drifting along in the
breeze, we’ll never make it to heaven. If we’re making no effort to avoid hell,
that’s where we’re going, because the river of life empties into the lake of
fire. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>2</b>:1</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Nobody would think of planning a trip the way some people
set out for heaven. They neglect to study the Map and suppose they can get
there by going any direction they please. We had better go by the Map; or, when
we get to the end of the road, we will not be where we wanted to go. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Proverbs <b>14</b>:12</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Let’s be honest with yourselves; are we acting as if we have
a long time to get to heaven? Or that just a little righteous living will go a
long way? If we calculate getting to heaven in terms of miles and minutes, it’s
farther than we realize and later than we think. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>12</b>:20; Matthew <b>24:</b>36-44</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Judging from the weak efforts many making to escape hell, wouldn’t we say
nearly everybody thinks that, when it comes right down to it, they will get to
heaven? As a matter of fact, the Bible says that only a few will be saved out
of the billions that have lived on the earth. Consequently, the majority are of
us in for a sad surprise someday. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Romans <b>9</b>:27; <b>11</b>:5</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Watch where you’re going! Did you ever have your eyes wide open and fall
in a hole because you weren’t looking where you were going? Let that be a
lesson to you. You have to keep your mind on what you’re doing while you’re
heading for heaven. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
Ephesians <b>5</b>:15-17</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
