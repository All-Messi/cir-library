<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>THE DECEITFULNESS OF SIN</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Do you think of yourself as sinful? Like most people, you probably feel
you are pretty decent since so many people you know are worse than you are. As
a result, you may associate sin with extreme evil in others, but the slightest
sin in your life is enough to classify you as a sinner too. As long as you
suppose you’re good, you are ensnared in sin’s trap of deceit. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 John <b>3</b>:4; <b>1</b>:8;
James <b>2</b>:1-11</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Time can heal a broken heart, but sin uses time deceitfully to harden the
heart. Did you feel a little uneasy in the crowd at your first big-league game?
Weren’t you sickened by the stench of beer and the smell of cigarette smoke
that rose slowly from the stadium that evening? Do you still feel uneasy, or have
you gotten used to it now? (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Ephesians <b>4</b>:17-24</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. “Once won’t hurst” </span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>is sin’s advertisement; <i>“no
obligation”</i> is its sales pitch. One glass at a friendly party might seem
harmless enough—over a whole lifetime, but that’s not the full story. Yielding
to sin is like eating potato chips; you can’t take just one. From a glass to a
gallon, from once to always is sin’s plan of damnation. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read James <b>1</b>:14-16</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'> Wednesday. “But it’s fun.” </span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>It may be; that’s the way it is with
sin. Sinners have fun sinning or they wouldn’t sin. But there is a difference
between fun and joy. Fun is for a while; joy lasts forever. That’s why we call
sin “fun” and righteousness “joy”; sin can’t keep its promises—very long. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Hebrews <b>11</b>:24-25</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Another of sin’s deceitful tactics is to cast reflections at
the motives of truth. Sin wants us to believe that Christianity is a “don’t-do”
way of living, that we forfeit our freedom when we defer to Christ’s authority,
and that the preacher’s hope in persuading us to attend church is to keep up
the attendance when he preaches, and so on and on. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Genesis <b>3</b>:1-9</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>In the younger set and on up, the spirit of our time hesitates to call
things black or white. It assigns them to a “gray area” between virtue and
vice. Sin would have us think there is a vast “no man’s land” between good and
bad; but, when we cross the border from good, we’re in bad. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 John <b>5</b>:17a</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Falsehood specializes in half-truth as its master method of
deceit. Satan emphasizes God’s love for all to soothe the conscience of those
who serve Christ off and on, who worship him a little, and are faithful for a
while. Sin avoids the fact of God’s justice because God’s justice does not
allow him to save those who should be destroyed. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Luke <b>3</b>:8-9, 16-17</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
