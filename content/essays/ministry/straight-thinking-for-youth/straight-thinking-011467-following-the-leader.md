<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>FOLLOWING THE LEADER</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Do you want to be a leader? There’s some prestige associated with being
in the spotlight. What satisfaction in making decisions and having others go
along with them! And then, wouldn’t it be gratifying to have people come to you
for advice before making their decisions? Leadership ability is extremely
important in the eyes of the world, but learning to follow is the first art in
becoming an effective leader. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Luke <b>22</b>:25</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Most people are not natural leaders. It takes a special combination of opportunities,
abilities, and personality to produce an outstanding leader. Although we can
develop our talents to a significant extent, we may end up playing a supportive
role. And there’s nothing wrong with that. Both the world and the church need
good followers, but the secret to being a good follower is choosing a good
leader. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Peter <b>2</b>:21-25</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Someone that wants to lead for the sake of giving orders is never a good
leader. When you’re looking for a leader, consider whether the person has first
been a good follower, someone that’s willing to forfeit personal desires in
order to help someone else, someone that’s primarily concerned with the best
interests of the followers. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Read Matthe <b>20</b>: 20-28.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Think for yourself whether you’re leading or following. Followers
aren’t people that let others do their thinking for them. In fact, to make any
contribution at all, followers must do their own thinking and determine for
themselves what should be done and how best to do it. After all, if the program
fails, the followers will suffer along with the rest of the group. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 3 John 11</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Don’t try to be the “big show” if you don’t have what it
takes. We won’t help ourselves by hogging the ball from a “crack shot”; we’ll
just make our whole team lose. Remember, the more responsibility we take, the
more criticism we’ll get if we fail. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read James <b>3</b>:1; Philippians <b>2</b>:3-11</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Be satisfied to follow. We are no less important to the cause than the
leader is. The “second fiddler” makes the melody more beautiful by playing an
accompaniment to it. A good coach and a good team are both needed to win a
tournament. The great apostle Paul by himself could never have evangelized the
Gentile world as much as he did. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Colossians <b>4</b>:7-14</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>We all both lead and follow. Those less able will probably
look to us for guidance, and we in turn look to others. In one part of the whole
process, we may need to follow; in another part we may need to take the lead.
But the important thing for all of us to remember is that we must look to
Jesus, because we must follow him if we are going to lead. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>8</b>:19-23</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
