<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>DECISIONS! DECISIONS!</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Sunday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Life is a flood of decisions: to be or not to be, to do or not to do, to
speak or be silent. Some decisions are more important than others; some are
easier to make; some can be altered from time to time; others, once made, are
final forever. Whether we know it or not, whether we like it or not, whether
we’re ready or not, life goes on—a rushing torrent of decisions. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read 1 Kings <b>3</b>:5-9.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Monday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Lazy people must be the world’s most miserable people—tired of sleeping
and too lazy to get up. Their unhappy state does not even let them enjoy the
only thing they do. Sleep is a nightmare for people that aren’t tired; their
bed is a tossing, turning torment. Such is the punishment for “lazies,” whose
biggest fault is closing their eyes to the decisions that life demands of them.
(</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Proverbs <b>6</b>:6-11</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Tuesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>Success isn’t luck; it’s mostly a long, hard grind. The most successful
way to fail is to choose the favors of chance or hesitate at the prospect of
sweat, blood, and tears. Anything worth having is worth suffering for. Bare
your back to the lash; strain every muscle to turn your millstone of fortune. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Proverbs <b>13</b>:11</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Wednesday. </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>The teen years are decision years. Unfortunately, we must
make many of life’s serious decisions when we are least qualified to make them.
We can get some help by observing what has made other people succeed or fail.
We can profit also from the advice of adults who know what young people are up
against. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Proverbs <b>12</b>:15</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Thursday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> What are you going to do when the storms of opposition drive you to and
fro like a reed in the wind? How will you go about making up your mind? Will
you take the easy way out or the right way? The only good way out is to do what’s
good. Each time you bend you get weaker, but the longer you stand firm the
stronger you grow. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read
James <b>1</b>:12</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Friday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Nobody likes middle-of-the-roaders, fence-straddlers, people that will
go along with anything and want to please everybody. They’re no good as leaders;
they can’t be trusted as  followers. They aren’t accepted by either side
because they want to be friends with both. Neither people nor God can use
followers that won’t make up their minds. (</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Read Revelation <b>3</b>:15-16</span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%'>Saturday.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'> Everyone must make decisions on top of decisions, but one choice we all must
come to grips with now. What are we going to do with Jesus? We cannot avoid
this issue by doing nothing, because doing nothing is doing something; it’s deciding
against him. Whoever is not for him is against him. Decide. (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%'>Read Matthew <b>12</b>:30</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>.)</span></p>
