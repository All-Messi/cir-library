<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>THE METHODS AND MANNER OF MINISTRY</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>   I.    <u>In Anticipation of Ministry</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry to other people proceeds out
of a relationship to God and then out of relationship to them. <u>Vertical and
horizontal interpersonal relationship reinforce each other</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               In general, personal relationship with
people must <u>precede</u> spiritual ministry to them. <u>Authenticating
yourself</u> has to occur before are likely to let you lead them or turn to you
when they need help. A minister is best able to influence other people if first
they are convinced that he can be influenced by them—if he is listening rather
than just telling or commanding.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               The <u>motives</u> of ministry are love
more than duty. When we minister out of love, we do it because we want to and
we will work harder with less sense of being “at work.” One result of this
attitude toward ministry is less “<u>burnout</u>.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               There are always certain jobs in
ministry that you do not look forward to doing. If you take the attitude that
you <u>care about</u> these people, you will have better luck overriding your
impulses/aversion against doing what needs to be done. You will unconsciously
communicate an attitude toward the persons involved that will disarm them in
response to you. Love was a way of overcoming the fear of rejection: “Perfect
love casts out fear.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>II.     <u>Practice of Ministry</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><span style='text-decoration:none'>&nbsp;</span></span></u></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               <u>Personal involvement</u> contrasts
with professional services and procedures as the basic feel for ministry. Otherwise
ministry can turn into something like being checked out at the grocery store. You
cannot minister to people “from a distance.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry includes <u>demonstration</u>
of the product you are trying to get people to “buy into.” So to speak, <u>people
experience God by experiencing us</u> (cp. John <b>14</b>). </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               The “connectors” in ministry are
interpersonal influence rather than legal authority on conceptual transmission
(words). Ministry goes on in the <u>personal medium</u> rather than the legal
profession authoritarian one. Attitude toward ministry is as important as
anything in it. <u>Behavioral communication</u> is more than non-personal
skills, awareness of <u>methods and procedures</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry is <u>individualized</u> as
much as possible rather than made into a <u>routine</u>, or <u>unalterable
process/pattern</u>, where “one approach fits all.” This recognizes the <u>differences</u>
between people, but more importantly the importance of each one. We are not
into a “mass production” system. <u>Interpersonalism</u> implies that the
manner of ministry must <u>balance individuals and groups</u> so as not to let
the group routine carry on to the extent that some individuals get lost in the
shuffle. On the other hand, certain individuals’ welfare and preference or
desires for attention cannot be catered to so much that the (rest of the) group
is neglected or offended.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry behaviorally communicates to
people that they are <u>worth something</u>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry is <u>need oriented</u>. It
is, so to speak, “student orient” rather than “teacher centered.” Doing every
thing for them, however, is not the point because that would mean treating them
as if they were not capable of functioning as persons–self-responsible beings.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               <u>Interdependency</u> is the format of
ministry (Paul’s body figure). Ministry requires “getting in touch” with people
and risking entanglement in their lives, which can be an emotional thing.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry involves <u>mutuality</u>
because “ministers” also need to be ministered to. It is not just a matter of
some who need no help helping those who need it. Note Paul’s concern to be made
glad by the Corinthians (2 Corinthians). A minister is also someone who can be
affected by other people; he is not the “unmoved mover” of others.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               We have to concentrate on <u>effectiveness
more than efficiency</u>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               “Team work” has a place in ministry. More
than one person works in bring about the general result: “one plants, one
waters, etc.” We want to stay out of each other’s way and encourage each other
rather than concentrate on advising and bossing and evaluating each other. We
do not want to structure things in ministry so we can take all the credit.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Interpersonalism <u>de-emphasizes
structure</u>, authority, and following “channels.” It minimizes the question
of who has the authority here.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               One strategy for evangelism is to build
new relationships with non-Christians as a basis for evangelizing them. The
same technique is be used within the church as well in the effort to help
Christians grow. It is a technique for ministry that any Christian can use.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry is a <u>whole-person process</u>.
It is whole persons impacting whole persons with all the individual factors
that that involves on both sides of the relationship. Persuasion/changing
people is best brought about by appeal to emotion, will, and mind, not just
mind or will or emotion.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>III.    <u>Consequences of Ministry</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry is for personal and
interpersonal <u>ends</u>. It is for re-establishing personal relationship with
God and for growing people into behaving more maturely to enhance that
relationship.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               The purpose of ministry is <u>conversion
before condemnation</u> (John<b> 3</b>:16). Our approach to ministry will not
be aimed at producing guilt (guilt manipulation) so much as inviting people to
start over.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry has as its goal changing
people to the point where they operate from internal initiative.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry is not getting people into a <u>saved
state</u> so much as it is into a <u>growth pattern</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Ministry is directed to the whole
person; so we deal with all aspects of a person–body, mind, emotion, spirit. When
we care about a person, we care about the person, not just one aspect of him. </span></p>
