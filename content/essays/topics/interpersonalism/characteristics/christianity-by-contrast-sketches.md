<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Law
of Contrast/Law of Opposition. Ideas are clearest in contrast to their parallel
alternatives. The principle applies to <u>clear thinking</u> and proper interpretation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The
whole provides the context for its parts. The character of the whole sets the
character of its constituents. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Continuity
of kind between cause and effect, purpose and result, problem and solution: Matthew
<b>7</b>:16-20; <b>12</b>:33-35; Luke<b> 6</b>:43-45, James <b>3</b>:12.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Good
and bad are always figured relative to purpose.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Basic
elements of reality: being, action, relationship</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Kinds
of processes: hypostatic, legal, metaphysical, interpersonal</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ways
of affecting other people’s actions: influence, authority, and force.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Misidentifying
the <u>problem</u> as some non-interpersonal matter misdirects to the wrong <u>solution:
</u>works as doing, miracle as necessary manifestation, individual identity (to
be overcome in the sea of ultimate non-differentiation), mixture of spirit and
matter, loss of form in matter as the cause of evil, conflict of national
identities, inability to respond as due to ontic depravity</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Proposed

departure from its interpersonal character and purpose. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:

picture that are not already there in secondary position. Such matters
represent foreign elements brought into the Christian endeavor.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
text-indent:.5in'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>Major Hindrances to the Christian Enterprise</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
text-indent:.5in'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Judaizing:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <b>Legalism
and nationalism: </b>The Judaizers attempted to retain law and nationalism and
add to it the message that Jesus fulfilled the Messianic expectancy. To put it
differently, they attempted to add Messianism to Mosaism.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Constantinian
Perversion</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            In
about A.D. 312, Constantine started combining Christianity and the political
state. Along with that move toward nationalizing Christianity came the rebirth
of something like what the original Judaizers envisioned. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> Augustinianism</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <b>Hypostasis:</b>
Augustine attempted to formulate a philosophy of evil that would replace the
Manichean idea that matter and spirit corresponded with evil and good. Instead
of saying that <u>matter</u> is evil, Augustine tried to say that evil is a
deprivation in the <u>form of matter </u>relative to its pristine condition. He
attempted to account for natural and moral evil in that same way. As a result, it
brought in the unbiblical notion of natural depravity and its accompanying
requirements of biological transfer plus miracle to reverse the biologically
inherited fallen nature when it comes to salvation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Calvinism</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <b>Legalism:</b>
John Calvin and his heirs have combined Augustine’s hypostatic thinking with
legal process and produced an array of unbiblical doctrines in various
combinations: unconditional election, limited atonement, irresistible grace,
perseverance of the saints, eternal security, guilt transfer, ontic
regeneration. These elemental departures supernaturalize and legalize salvation,
Christian living, and the Christian experience.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>Christianity and Other Religions</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The
General Proposition: The things that have perverted the faith over the
centuries are approximately the things that destroy its distinctiveness from
other religious systems. They amount to some kind of non-interpersonal elements
that are not already in the Christian system in secondary position.</span></p>
