<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            In scripture, <i>“spirit”</i>
<i>vs. “flesh” </i>stands for the interpersonal center of life in distinction
to an egocentric or ethnocentric one. It is the transcendent <i>vs.</i> the
imminent. The terminology evidently came about because flesh takes something
into itself to find fulfillment. Persons, however, must give of themselves to find
fulfillment. “Flesh” stands for an individualism that fosters a <u>competitive
mentality</u>. “Spirit” stands for an interpersonalism that fosters <u>unifying
love</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Sanctification occurs mostly
by dynamic increase in the process of interpersonal interaction. The spiritual
exercises to produce that sanctification are various kinds of personal
interaction. The spiritual fruit comes from social interaction. The products of
sanctification have the character of what produces them—the law of the harvest,
<i>“after their kind.”</i> Spiritual exercises contrast with individualism,
materialism, or political process.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The fruit of the spirit
(spiritual fruit) is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, trust/trustworthiness/faithfulness,
humility, and self-control (Galatians <b>5</b>:22-23). Some are blessings from
the other person(s); some are virtues we produce in ourselves; they are all
interpersonal. They contrast with negative interpersonal experiences—fruit of
the flesh: fornication, uncleanness, lasciviousness, idolatry, sorcery,
enmities, strife, jealousies, wrath, factions, divisions, parties, envying,
drunkenness, reveling (Galatians <b>5</b>:19-21). The other virtue and vice
lists in the New Testament contain comparable items separated out by the same flesh-spirit
variable.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <i>“Fruit of the spirit”</i>
in Galatians <b>5</b> means spiritual fruit in contrast to fleshly fruit. Spiritual
means intangible, interpersonal, invisible, psychological, transcendent reality
in distinction to tangible, impersonal, visible, material reality. The point is
not that the Holy Spirit miraculously deposits them in the human soul, but that
they naturally result from a new interpersonal orientation with God. All the
spiritual fruits arise from fellowship with God, who is spirit (or “a spirit”;
John <b>4</b>:24). So, they do not depend on circumstance or fade with time.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Peace is not so much <u>inner</u>
as <u>inter</u> peace. It comes from a sense of acceptance and well-being
before God and his people. It comes from what is right and what lasts. Likewise,
all for all the spiritual fruits. </span></p>
