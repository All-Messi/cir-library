<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Parenting takes place first
by interpersonal means. It is on top of interpersonal process that any
authority comes, and that authority is bounded in its application by the
distinctive purposes at work in this special setting for interpersonal process.
The interpersonal circumstance sets the <u>attitude</u> parents take toward
their children. Children are smaller but not less. If love is the medium of
interpersonal relationships, <u>love becomes the medium for parenting</u>. The <u>means</u>
and <u>manner</u> of parenting are primarily interpersonal influence instead of
physical force, intimidation, authoritative control, manipulation, and the like.
One of the main means of interpersonal influence is <u>modeling</u> what we
want to teach, commend, command, and so on.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Parents are to <u>provide
for their offspring</u> the skills of personal relationship. That is
essentially what <u>teaching</u> is (education). The motive of discipline is
the better quality of personal life they will have.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We want to provide them with
<u>self-esteem</u> as persons, and security that comes from interpersonal
acceptance. We provide them with self-esteem when we esteem them.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:
18.0pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Kids are not for fulfilling
parents’ aspirations. They are not for being <u>used</u>; that would reduce their
personhood. We can let them make a <u>difference</u> by letting them have input
into decisions that affect them, by taking time with them, by listening to
them.</span></p>
