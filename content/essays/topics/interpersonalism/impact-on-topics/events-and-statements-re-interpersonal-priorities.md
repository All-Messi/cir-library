<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>The Priority of Interpersonal over <u>Formal</u> Matters</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>1. The Sabbath was made for man, not man for the
Sabbath (Mark <b>2</b>:27; cp. Exodus <b>23</b>:12d; Deuteronomy<b> 5</b>:14c)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-22.5pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2.   <i>“Today you will be with me in paradise”</i>
(Luke <b>23</b>:43).  No gift for cleansing offered.  In fact, there was no
provision at all in the Law for the forgiveness of murder or any capital crime.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>3.   David and his men ate the showbread that was
legally available only for the priests (Matthew <b>12</b>:1-8; Mark<b> 2</b>:23-28;
Luke <b>6</b>:1-5; 1 Samuel <b>21</b>:6).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>4.   Jesus told the woman of Sychar that salvation was
in fact of the Jews, but God looks for people to worship him anywhere in spirit
and truth (John <b>4</b>:19-26).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>5.   Hezekiah’s dealing with Passover observance by
people not prepared for it after the ceremonial requirements of the Law (2
Chronicles <b>30</b>:13-22)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>6.   <i>“Circumcision profits if you <u>do</u> the Law;
if you <u>transgress</u> it, your circumcision is become uncircumcision.  <u>So,
if the uncircumcision keeps the ordinances of the Law, will not his
uncircumcision be reckoned for circumcision</u>? and will not the
uncircumcision, which is natural, if it fulfill the Law, judge you who with the
letter and circumcision transgress the Law?”</i> (Romans<b> 2</b>:25-27).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>7.   Micah <b>6</b>:68 (cp. Deuteronomy <b>10</b>:12-13)
shows the priority of personal commitment and behavior over ceremonial
observance of <u>animal sacrifices</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-22.5pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-31.5pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>8.   Abraham’s relationship to God preceded
circumcision and the Law (Romans<b> 4</b>:6-15, <i>etc</i>.).</span></p>
