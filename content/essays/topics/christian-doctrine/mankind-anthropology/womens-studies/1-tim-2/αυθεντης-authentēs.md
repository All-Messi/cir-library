<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>The importance of this comment lies in the fact that </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>αὐθεντέω</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
is equated simply with <i>“being a leader of”</i> rather than implying
domination or aggressively, assertively exercising leadership and authority. Mr.
Wolters concluded as does Baldwin and Barnett/Harris that Wilshire’s decision
for <i>“domineer, be aggressive”</i> does not hold for the period from the six
centuries prior to A.D. 311. A significant portion of time should be devoted to
studying this little problem.  </span></p>
