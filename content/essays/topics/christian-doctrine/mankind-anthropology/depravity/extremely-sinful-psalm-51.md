<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
line-height:normal'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>EXTREMELY
SINFUL: Psalm 51</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
line-height:normal'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
line-height:normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Virgil
Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center;
line-height:normal'><b><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in;line-height:normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Psalm
<b>51</b> records David’s response to his sin with Bathsheba:  heading of the
Psalm, <i>“When Nathan the prophet came to him after he had gone into Bathsheba”
</i>(2 Samuel <b>11-12</b>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David’s redeeming
quality was admission of his sin, his willingness to repent, and his concern to
reach out to God for forgiveness: Psalm <b>51</b>:1-4, 7-17, which is the bulk
of the Psalm.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David
felt totally characterized by sin as when we say someone is a “born liar.” </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Similar comments are <i>“He is a
born salesman”</i>; “it’s second nature, like “breathing in and </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            breathing out” (<i>My
Fair Lady</i>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David took full
responsibility for his sin: <i>“Against you, and you only have I sinned and done
what is evil in your sight. So you are justified when you condemn.”</i> He is
not </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>blaming his sin on
being born that way or claiming his mother conceived him in adultery.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>David’s being <i>“born
in sin”</i> (<b>51</b>:5) is comparable to his statement in Psalm <b>58</b>:3, <i>“The
wicked go astray from the womb, speaking lies.” </i>Of course, these babies did
not talk immediately after birth; the point is, they “<i>have always been liars,”</i>
characteristically so. Likewise in <b>51</b>:3 David says, <i>“My sin is always
before me.”</i> Total degree is expressed by total time. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Other
expressions involving babies and birth:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Psalm <b>22</b>:9-10
makes a positive statement about babies: <i>“You that brought me </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      [David] out
of the womb have made me trust you on my mother’s breasts.”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> The point is that
David has had to trust God all his life. It is a stretch to say that brand new
infants are literally trusting their mothers. They are hardly making any
deliberate response in any way. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Luke <b>10</b>:21,
<i>“I praise you, Father, . . . for hiding these things from the wise and intelligent,
and revealing them to babies.”</i> People that are “like babies” (in their
simplicity of faith/trust toward God) are called “babies.” </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Matthew<b> 21</b>:16
draws on Psalm<b> 8</b>:2 when he responds to the religious leaders’ objection
to the unbridled enthusiasm of the children at the Triumphal Entry, <i>“Out of
the mouth of nursing babies, you have prepared praise for yourself.”</i> God is
worthy of praise all the time by absolutely everybody.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Romans<b> 2</b>:19-20
chastises religious leaders that think of themselves as so superior to other
people that they presume to guide, enlighten, correct, and teach as if others
are babies in comparison to themselves. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>John <b>9</b>:34
quotes the Pharisees as telling the man born blind, <i>“You were altogether born
in sins,” </i>as an explanation of the man’s blindness. The implication is that
they did not consider themselves to be born in sin. Sinfulness at birth had
nothing to do with his condition; neither did his parents’ sin (<b>9</b>:3).
Sinfulness is not an explanation for experiencing tragedy.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>1 John <b>5</b>:16:
<i>“sin unto death”</i> uses the other end of the time of life to talk about
characteristic sin, a sin that is not just a “slip up”; it is ongoing, repetitious
sin. Compare our comment, <i>“Talk something to death.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in;line-height:normal'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“You are always
making fun of me.” </span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Well,
that is not true about every comment one person makes to another. It is an
exaggeration for emphasis.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Note the threefold
manner of emphasis using time to express it: from birth, always, till death.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Note
Paul’s comment in Romans <b>7</b>:9, <i>“I was alive apart from the law once,
but when the commandment came, sin came alive, and I died.” Paul was not born
sinful. Sin is not imputed until knowledge of the law takes place. We call it
“the age of accountability.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Quick
notes on natural depravity (fallen nature biologically inheritable from Adam
and Eve’s sin)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            1.
<b>Unnecessary</b> </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                  for
explaining <u>universal sinfulness</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Adam and Eve
sinned prior to “the Fall” that supposedly brought the depravity that </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                              causes
everybody to sin.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Sufficient
explanation for universal sin without speculating an in-born depravity <u>evil
influence</u>: Satan <u>creates doubt</u> about God’s motives: <i>“he knows you
will </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;text-indent:4.5pt;line-height:normal'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>    become like
him.”</span></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      Satan <u>denies</u>
Adam and Eve will die like God said.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      Satan makes
false promises, half-truths: <i>“you will become like God/god’s</i>.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;line-height:normal'><u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>viewpoint of consciousness</span></u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, which can become
selfishness/self-centeredness.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      Satan
claimed the fruit would make a person wise, know good and evil.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>pervertible
physical desires</span></u><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
(good for food)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Genesis <b>3</b>
does not say our first parents lost any <u>spiritual capacity</u>, just <u>physical</u>
elements: death, pain, labor to produce food, clothes, and shelter</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      <u>circumstances</u>:
dismissed from the Garden, separated from the tree of life,</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      <u>separation
from God</u>: sense of nakedness, fear </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                  for
explaining <u>why people cannot save themselves</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Perfection
is the standard.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                              “Nobody’s
perfect.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                              Adam
and Eve were dismissed from the Garden after one sin.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                              Once
perfection is lost, by the nature of what it is, a sinner cannot regain it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        Salvation
is reconciliation between persons (2 Corinthians <b>5</b>:18-19). </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>      Forgiveness and
restored friendship are always pronounced by the person sinned against.
Repentance by the sinner does not restore the relationship: Esau was rejected
in regard to his relinquished right of the first-born even though he sought it
diligently with tears (Hebrews<b> 12</b>:17). Repentance by the sinner does not
produce forgiveness by the one sinned against.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            2.
<b>Inadequate basis</b></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;line-height:normal'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The texts appealed
to are exaggerations for emphasis: Psalm <b>51</b>:5; <b>58</b>:3. They would
create <u>contradiction</u> with other texts if taken literally: Romans <b>7</b>:9.
They would be <u>unreal</u> if taken literally: Psalm <b>58</b>:3.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            3.
Impossible concept </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;line-height:
normal'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                        </span></b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Sin is an act,
which by nature cannot be inherited.    </span></p>
