<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>             Once sin entered the human race, it moved
from person to person, from generation to generation down to our own day. We get
it started it on our own and keep it moving “unassisted”; we get a lot of help
from other people! In this section we are thinking about the ways our ignorance
can get sin started and keep it moving without the input other people often
give. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>             There are different kinds of ignorance. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>             (1) One kind is <u>simply not knowing</u>
that something is wrong. We may never have been told that something is not good
for us. We may not have been told what consequences it brings. Sometimes
effects take a while, or they are subtle enough that we do not connect what we
are doing and the bad that comes from it. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>             Can you think of something you started
doing without realizing it was bad for you or for the people around you?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              (2) Another kind of ignorance is the
fact that <u>God is invisible</u> (John <b>1</b>:18; Colossians<b> 1</b>:15; 1
Timothy <b>6</b>:16; Hebrews <b>11</b>:27; 1 John <b>4</b>:12, plus many Old
Testament passages like <b>33</b>:20). </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              Can you see how this fact may contribute
to your own sinning?  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              How can we keep his invisibility from
having this effect on our living?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              Why do you suppose God does not “show
his face to us” if his invisibility has that effect on us?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:22.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              (3)  A third kind of ignorance is <u>the
viewpoint from which we look at things</u>. We look at the world from our own
eyes, sol we do things that harm or offend other people. This results in what
we call self-centeredness.</span></p>
