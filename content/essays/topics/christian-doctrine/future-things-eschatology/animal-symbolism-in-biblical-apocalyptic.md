<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Apocalyptic is a special class within
prophetic literature. The word <i>apocalyptic</i> means “having to do with revelation,”
<i>The Apocalypse </i>being another name for the last book of the New
Testament. Apocalyptic literature is characterized by cosmic scope, cataclysmic
events, confrontation between good and evil (dualism); emphasis on divine
sovereignty and transcendence, which can foster a sense of determinism; angelic
involvement, pessimism about humankind, interest in the consummation of
historical process. The literary genre came from oppressed minorities in
oppressing times and sought to create hope and bring comfort to people with it.
Visions predominate as the mode of revelation, and they are described to the
reader with a few explanatory notes. Animal symbolism and numerology are two
formal mechanisms used in such writings. The former helps create the unrealism,
surrealism, or dream-like nature of biblical and extra-biblical apocalyptic.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Among the Israelites apocalyptic
literature proliferated during inter-testamental times in works like <i>1 Enoch</i>,
<i>2 Enoch</i>, <i>Jubilees</i>, <i>Testament of the Twelve Patriarchs</i>, <i>Psalms
of Solomon</i>, <i>Assumption</i>, <i>4 Ezra</i>, <i>Apocalypse of Baruch</i>. After
apostolic times apocalyptic literature emerged from the “Christian” community
in works like the <i>Ascension of Isaiah</i>, the <i>Apocalypse of Peter</i>,
the <i>Coptic Apocalypse of Peter</i>, the <i>Apocalypse of Paul</i>, the <i>Apocalypse
of John</i>, and the <i>Christian Sibylline Books</i>. Extra-biblical
apocalyptic writings are mostly anonymous, possibly because in the inter-testimental
period there was no succession of prophets; so writers would put their thoughts
into the mouths of respected saints from earlier times. The biblical examples
listed chronologically are the book of Joel generally, Isaiah <b>24</b>-<b>27</b>
(the “Little Apocalypse”); the Book of Ezekiel, especially chapters <b>33-48</b>;
Daniel <b>2</b>, <b>7-12</b>, much of Zechariah, apocalyptic being mixed in
with non-apocalyptic prophecy; the Olivet Discourse (Matthew <b>24-25</b>; Mark
<b>13</b>; Luke <b>21</b>), and smaller pieces like 1 Thessalonians <b>4</b>:1-<b>5</b>:11
and 2 Thessalonians <b>2</b>:1-12. The notable New Testament example, of
course, is the Revelation of John.</span></p>

margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  Basic proposition: there are only
three “beasts” in Revelation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:85.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.  <b>16</b>:13 (the source of
three frog-like, unclean spirits): the dragon, the beast, and the false prophet</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:85.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.  <b>19</b>:19-<b>20</b>:3: the
beast (<b>19</b>:19-20), the false prophet (<b>19</b>:20); and the dragon, the
old serpent, devil, Satan (<b>20</b>:2)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   <b>20</b>:10: the devil that
deceived them, the beast, and the false prophet</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Beasts mentioned in Revelation</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   The (great red) dragon: <b>12</b>:1-17;
<b>16</b>:13; <b>20</b>:1-3, 7-10</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   The sea beast: <b>13</b>:1-10</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   The earth beast:<b> 13</b>:11-18</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   The beast in <b>11</b>:7</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  5.   The beast with the image: <b>14</b>:9-12</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  6.   The false prophet: <b>16</b>:13;
<b>19</b>:20; <b>20</b>:10</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  7.   The scarlet-colored beast: <b>17</b>:3-18</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  The identification of the beasts</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   The “earth beast” (<b>13</b>:11-18)
equals the false prophet (<b>16</b>:13; <b>19</b>:20).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   Both worked signs <u>in
the sight of the beast</u> (<b>13</b>:13-15; <b>19</b>:20).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   <i>Prophet</i> is an
appropriate term for a beast who spoke on behalf of another</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (<b>13</b>:12, 14b,
15b-17; </span><span dir=RTL></span><b><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='font-size:
12.0pt'><span dir=RTL></span>15</span></b><span lang=HE dir=RTL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>:20</span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt'><span dir=LTR></span>; </span><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>16</span></b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>:13).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Only three satanic
creatures had been mentioned in Interlude I (<b>12</b>:1-<b>14</b>:20) before
the listing in 16:13, the first being a definite reference and the others most
naturally in the same order as they appeared in the previous text. The only
other single beast prior to chapter <b>12</b> is in <b>11</b>:7, the one coming
up out of the abyss. <i>Abyss</i> is a word not previously used in Revelation
except with reference to the hordes of locusts in <b>9</b>:1, 2, 11; hence, it
looks forward to the beasts introduced in Interlude I.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   The earth beast (<b>13</b>:11-18)
does not equal the sea beast (<b>13</b>:1-10).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   “Another beast” (<b>13</b>:11);
“first beast” (<b>13</b>:12)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   The earth beast performs
signs in the sight of the sea beast (<b>13</b>:12).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   The earth beast causes
people to worship the image of the sea beast (<b>13</b>:15).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   The dragon (<b>12</b>:1-17; <b>16</b>:13;
<b>20</b>:1-3, 7-10) does not equal the sea beast (<b>13</b>:1-10): the dragon
gave him his power, throne, and great authority (<b>13</b>:2).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   The scarlet-colored beast (<b>17</b>:3-18)
does not equal the dragon (<b>12</b>:1-17; <b>16</b>:13; <b>20</b>:1-3, 7-10).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   The dragon is definitely
Satan (<b>12</b>:9), a person; but the scarlet-colored beast is a composite of
person, place, and thing; that is, the “beast” itself (<b>20</b>:11), seven
hills (<b>20</b>:9), and an evil political system (<b>20</b>:10-11a). The heads
are both mountains and kings (<b>20</b>:9, 10).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   One is called a dragon (</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>δράκων</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>);
the other is called a beast (</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>θηρίον</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>).  </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>Δράκων</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> seems to mean reptile (<b>12</b>:9; <b>20</b>:2-3)
while </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>θηρίον</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> is a more general word for undomesticated animals
(mammal in <b>13</b>:2, snake in Acts<b> 28</b>:4-5).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  5.   The sea beast (<b>13</b>:1-10)
equals the scarlet-colored beast (<b>17</b>:3-18).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   The sea beast came up out
of the sea (<b>13</b>:1); and the harlot, sitting on the beast (<b>17</b>:3),
was sitting on many waters (<b>17</b>:2).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Both had seven heads and
ten horns (<b>13</b>:1; cp. 17:3).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   The sea beast had
blasphemous names on its heads (<b>13</b>:1, 6). The scarlet-colored beast was “full
of blasphemous names” (<b>17</b>:3). Blasphemy is mentioned only with these two
beasts.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        d.   The sea beast had <u>one
head smitten</u>, but its <i>death stroke was healed</i> (<b>13</b>:3).  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>The scarlet-colored beast was, <u>is not</u>, and <i>is
about to come out of the abyss</i>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        e.   Only in the sea-beast
section (<b>13</b>:8) and the scarlet-beast section (<b>17</b>:8) is the phrase
<i>not written in the [Lamb’s] book of life </i>used, except for references
near the beginning of Revelation and at the actual judgment scenes near the
end.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        f.    After mentioning the
healed death-stroke, the text says that “the whole earth” wondered at the beast
(<b>13</b>:3). After saying that the scarlet beast was, is not, and is about to
come, the angel states that “they who dwell on the earth” will wonder at the
beast (<b>17</b>:8).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:85.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  6.  The sea beast (<b>13</b>:1-10)
equals the beast with an image (<b>14</b>:9-12), because both have an image
made of them (<b>13</b>:14-15;<b> 14</b>:9).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  7.  The sea beast <b>(13</b>:1-10)
equals the beast in<b> 11</b>:7.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   They are both said to
overcome the saints (<b>11</b>:7; <b>13</b>:7), the only two places in
Revelation where saints are said to be overcome.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   The sea beast has been
shown to equal the scarlet beast. The three beasts arise on their own
initiative; that is, it is not a matter of freeing them from confinement as
with a key (<b>11</b>:7; <b>17</b>:8, 10).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Both have authority over
peoples, tribes, tongues, and nations (<b>11</b>:9; <b>13</b>:7).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        d.   The first point of
difficulty in this identification is that there is no other single </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>θηρίον</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
that is said to ascend from the abyss, but the </span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>θηρίον</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>of <b>11</b>:7 cannot be a separate beast. That would
make at least four beasts in Revelation, but the book seems to allow for only
three when all final identifications have been made. The </span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>δράκων</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> does come
out of the abyss after the millennium because he is released from his prison,
the abyss. But the dragon’s release from the abyss does not seem to fit the<b>
11</b>:7 statement:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-117.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    A </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>θηρίον</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> (<i>thērion</i>,
undomesticated animal), rather than a </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt'>δράκων</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> (<i>drakōn</i>), is to
come up.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    The </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>θηρίον</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
that comes out of the sea seems to triumph over the saints <u>after</u> his
coming up, whereas the dragon does not succeed against God after the
millennium.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    The </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>θηρίον</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt'> </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>of<b> 11</b>:7 seems to arise
freely from his “abyss” whereas </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       Satan is
released from the abyss after the millennium.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (4)    The beast and the
false prophet are thrown directly into the lake of </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>fire and brimstone immediately after their defeat by
the King of kings.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (5)    The </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>δράκων</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
is distinguished from the sea beast of whom the scarlet beast is the presumed
equal, and it is said of the scarlet beast also that he comes up from the abyss
(<b>17</b>:8).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>VII. General Observations and Conclusions</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
color:windowtext'>There are</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> enough
repeated elements to imply either that the images were intentionally chosen or
that the visions themselves were similar. Two possibilities exist in such
cases: either the subsequent author used a similar description because it was
as appropriate to his own subject as it had been to the similar event that
supplied his terminology, or he intentionally hinted that he was speaking of
the same thing as that previous vision.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The second alternative seems especially
likely when (1) several parallels exist between two prophecies, (2) the
previous prophecy had not been fulfilled by the time the second author wrote,
(3) the thing prophesied is not apt to recur frequently, (4) the parallel
elements are not common ideas, and (5) such elements are not found in scripture
except in these cases.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Care must be taken in equations, however. The
dragon of Revelation 12 has seven heads, ten horns, and (seven) diadems. The
sea beast of Revelation 13 also has seven heads, ten horns, and (ten) diadems. Nevertheless,
they are not the same thing, because the dragon gives the sea beast his power (<b>13</b>:2).
Though related, they are not identical.  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Animal symbolism in biblical
apocalyptic comes primarily in Daniel and Revelation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  In animal symbols something out of
character is involved in almost every case.  Frequently the beasts are
composites of various animals, as with John’s description of the locusts in
Revelation <b>9</b>. Sometimes animal and human features are combined, as in
the strong angel of Revelation <b>10</b>. Daniel’s fourth beast and Ezekiel’s
living creatures combine various animals’ characteristics with both human and
mineral elements. Examples also come up where something simply “odd” is
pictured, such as the three ribs between the bear’s teeth in Daniel <b>7</b>:5
or unrealistic colors of the horses of Revelation<b> 6</b>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  Chronologically speaking, animal
symbolism (indeed, biblical apocalyptic itself) comes from the time of the
seventy-year exile (Ezekiel, Daniel, Zechariah) and the first seventy years of
the apostolic age. Apocalyptic teaching is mostly limited to the last week of
Jesus’ ministry; even there animal symbolism is practically non-existent. Pauline
apocalyptic also lacks this rather exotic touch—perhaps because animal
symbolism appears almost exclusively in the narration of apocalyptic <u>visions</u>,
where the author is in a state of ecstasy presumably observing visual
enactments before his mind’s eye.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The imagery of subsequent apocalyptic is
in keeping with previous symbolism, perhaps even built on it. The locusts of
Revelation <b>9</b> reflect the symbolism in Joel.  Sometimes imagery is
reminiscent of pre-apocalyptic, or non-apocalyptic, notably the serpent
symbolism for Satan (cp. Genesis <b>3</b>) and the Lamb symbolism for Christ
(cp. sacrificial laws).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            On the whole, there does not seem to be
much evolution in apocalyptic animal symbolism.  Composites appear from Ezekiel
onward. Good-connotation symbolism also comes from that time, Isaiah himself
having even previously good connotations associated with the description of the
seraphs (Isaiah 6), who elsewhere appear in apocalyptic contexts.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Generally speaking, then, in
apocalyptic more is said about continuity than about development.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   Animal symbolism more
frequently carries a bad connotation (two-thirds of the time according to Chart
VI).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   Horns usually refer to kings,
and beasts usually refer to kingdoms.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   Undomesticated animals
predominate two to one.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        4.   </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>No</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
correspondence exists between domesticated animals and good connotation, as one
might expect. Only five of the twelve domesticated animals have good
connotations. Conversely, seven of the twenty-four wild animals carry a good
connotation; so undomesticated animals tend to connote bad things, but that
fact simply correlates with the predominance of evil connotation for animal
symbolism in general.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  5.   In the previous charts various
equations have been made between different </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        animal symbols. They may be

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        i.    Locust-scorpion-horn-man-woman-lion

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            There are nine discrete symbols in
biblical apocalyptic with the twenty-four elders, the two witnesses, God the
Father, and the Holy Spirit left as never being represented by animal
symbolism. Apocalyptic writers tended to express “things” in animal symbolism.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Appendix A</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Three numbers in apocalyptic seem to indicate
the same time period: forty-two months; 1260 days; and time, times, and a half.
The first appears in Luke <b>21</b>:24, Revelation <b>11</b>:2; <b>13</b>:5. The
1260 days are mentioned in Revelation <b>11</b>:3 and <b>12</b>:6. <i>Time,
times, and a half</i> shows up in Daniel <b>7</b>:25; <b>12</b>:7; and
Revelation <b>12</b>:14.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The forty-two months evidently equal the
1260 days. (1) There are 1260 days in forty-two, thirty-day months. (2)
Revelation <b>11</b>:2-3 joins these numbers together, saying that Jerusalem is
trodden underfoot for forty-two months immediately before saying that the two
witnesses prophesy 1260 days.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Time, times, and a half equals 1260 days
since the woman who brought forth the man-child fled from the dragon into the
wilderness, where she was nourished 1260 days (Revelation <b>12</b>:6). In
Revelation <b>12</b>:14 the statement is repeated using the expression <i>time,
times, and half a time</i>. Such a curious number presumably means the same era
in Daniel<b> 7</b>:25 and<b> 12</b>:7, both of which occur in contexts that
deal with the end times (fourth kingdom and resurrection passages).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            If these equations are correct, certain
matters occur simultaneously:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (a)  The length of time during which
the saints of the most high are given into the hands of the “fourth beast,”
before the kingdom is given to the saints (Daniel<b> 7</b>:23-27);</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (b) The time of breaking in pieces
the holy people referred to by the man in linen </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        hovering above the River Chebar
in Babylon (Daniel <b>12</b>:5-9);</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (c)  The “time of the Gentiles”
during which Jerusalem would be trodden underfoot (from A.D. 70) and the Jews
would be captives of the nations (Luke <b>21</b>:24; Revelation <b>11</b>:2);</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (d) The duration of the prophesying
by the “two witnesses” (“Moses” and </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        “Elijah”? Israel and the
church? Revelation <b>11</b>:3);</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (e)  The time during which the woman
arrayed with the sun (Israel?) is nourished </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
