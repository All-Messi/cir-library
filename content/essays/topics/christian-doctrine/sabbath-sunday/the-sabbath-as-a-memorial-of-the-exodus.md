<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  I. Sabbath observance was not a memorial of the
creation. If it were, it would have been observed as long as this creation
stands: from the beginning, by all nations, till the end of time.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> II. The Sabbath is a memorial only of the exodus.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  A memorial will not be a memorial of
two unrelated events. The emphasis here is on realities. The creation and the Sabbath
are both associated with the week, but they are not related by their nature.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>B.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Hebrews <b>3-4</b> shows that
exodus-entrance and God’s creation rest are not related realities.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

</span></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>1.                                       
    The ministries of Moses and Joshua were one </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:2.0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        ministry in that the exodus-entrance
was one</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:2.0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        set historical<b> </b>sequence.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2.   The exodus-entrance is dissociated from the <i>“rest
of God”</i> in Genesis<b> 2</b>:1-4 according to Hebrews<b> 4</b>:8: <i>“If
Joshua had given them rest</i> [in entering Canaan, David] <i>would not have
spoken afterward of another day.”  </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   God’s rest does not find
its human counterpart in the conquest of Canaan, but in something yet future to
Psalm <b>95</b>. The “rest” in Canaan is not the reality that partakes in the <i>“rest
of God”</i> in creation as per Genesis <b>2</b>:3.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Genesis <b>2</b>:3 was
written in terms of the Jews to whom it was first given as an explanation or
origins at the time of Moses.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  Scripture identifies the Sabbath as a
memorial of one of these of these dissociated realities. Deuteronomy <b>5</b>:15
does not relate to the creation in meaning and purpose (contrast Exodus <b>20</b>:7-11;
<b>31</b>:17; cp. Genesis<b> 2</b>:3).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  D.  It is not, then, a memorial of
the other.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        <img width=335 height=108
src="/cir-library/images/essays/the-sabbath-as-a-memorial-of-the-exodus/image001.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Reasoning from greater to lesser, we conclude
that if God rested, mankind will one day rest. Since Hebrews <b>3-4</b> shows
that the creation rest corresponds with heaven, not Canaan, the two are unrelated.
Since Deuteronomy <b>5</b> relates Sabbath to the exodus, Sabbath is not associated
with creation, because a memorial will not be a memorial of two unrelated
things.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            MEANING        </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Deuteronomy <b>5</b>:15 gives the
reason <u>there was a day</u> of observance: it was a memorial of the exodus.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            FORM</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  The reason the day was observed <u>weekly</u>
is given in Exodus <b>20</b>:7-8: it shares in the pattern for presenting God’s
creation rest.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  The reason the day was the <u>seventh
day</u> is given in Exodus <b>20</b>:7-8: it was on the analogy of God’s
creation rest.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  The reason the day was observed as a
<u>rest day</u> is given in Exodus <b>31</b>:17: it was on the analogy of God’s
creation rest and was to remind them that they were resting from Egyptian
bondage.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.75in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      III. Hence, when Israel ceases to be God’s
national people, the memorial of its founding no longer obtains. The Christian
church is not a constitutional continuation of national Israel.</span></p>
