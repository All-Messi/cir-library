margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>FREQUENCY OF OBSERVING THE LORD’S SUPPER</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Since communion is primarily an
interpersonal act, there is no inherent necessity in regard to frequency. Therefore,
it is a matter of <u>positive commandment</u> or <u>apostolic precedent</u>
rather than <u>necessary inference</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>I. Range of Practices Encountered in the modern church</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  Annual</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Quarterly</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  Monthly</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  Weekly</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Daily (at each meeting of the
Christians)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            F.   Matter of opinion</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>II. Observations Favoring Weekly Observance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  1 Corinthians <b>16</b>:2 + <b>11</b>:18-20:
gathered weekly + when gathered, observed </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Supper</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                          1. <u>Note
the suggestion that “lay by” means at home</u>. But Paul says, <i>“. . . that
there be no collections when I come.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   Paul’s instructions in 1
Corinthians <b>16</b>:1ff. were the same as he gave to other churches. Presumably
then weekly gathering, hence, weekly communion, was at least as generalized as
his collection for the poor saints in Jerusalem.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Acts <b>2</b>:42 (“continued
steadfastly”)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   “Continued steadfastly”
describes activity during a relatively brief time </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        frame.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   “Breaking of bread” appears in
a list of religious observances; hence, it does </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        not likely refer here to a
common meal.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   The evidence for weekly
gathering of Christians for worship is no stronger </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        than the evidence for weekly
gathering to observe the Lord’s Supper.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  <u>Qualify the “the” argument</u>: “the”
+ date + <u>repetitive</u> action may equal “every”; but while 1 Corinthians <b>16</b>:2
labels a recurring event, Acts <b>20</b>:7 does not (cp. Matthew <b>28</b>:1;
Mark <b>16</b>:2; Luke <b>24</b>:1).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  <u>Avoid the “as often as” argument</u>:
in 1 Corinthians <b>11</b>:25-26, “as often as” (</span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>ὁσάκις</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> [<i>hosakis</i>])
does not particularly mean “often.” It does not mean frequency, but <u>correlative</u>
frequency (cp. Revelation <b>11</b>:6).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  We might be able to say that the
disciples met distinctively not primarily, to observe the Lord’s Supper.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> III. Arguments for Annual Observance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Annual observance depends primarily on an
allegorical comparison between the Lord’s Supper and the Passover.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  Items of parallel</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   The Lord’s Supper was
instituted at a Passover meal.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   The Passover meal was commanded
to be observed “forever” (Exodus<b> 12</b>:14, 17, 24).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   As the basis for salvation
Jesus is the functional equivalent of the Passover lamb, was sacrificed by
crucifixion at Passover time, and is called <i>“the Lamb of God that takes away
the sin of the world”</i> (John <b>1</b>:29).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   The Passover meal was an annual
observance (Exodus <b>13</b>:5, 10) associated not only with the memorializing
of the passing over of the death angel, but also with the passing over out of
the land of Egyptian slavery.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Response to the argument</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  The frequency issue must be seen as
related to the meaning of the rite.  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                          1.   It is
clear from New Testament witness that the Lord’s Supper was observed more often
than once a year.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   The Passover meal was observed
in memory of the exodus whereas the Lord’s Supper is observed in memory of the
sacrificial death of Christ. Neither event in itself had anything about its
nature that implied something about the frequency of observance. The exodus was
memorialized by a weekly observance (the Sabbath) and an annual observance (the
Passover). Both the exodus and the crucifixion were one-time occurrences. The
Lord’s Supper in memory of the latter could as easily be weekly—on the analogy
of Sabbath—as it could be annual—on the analogy of Passover. The decision
regarding frequency does not arise then from inherent meaning but from
authoritative choice—by God, by the apostles. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   As to frequency and meaning, the
Lord’s Supper is no more the functional-equivalent continuation of Passover
than baptism is the functional-equivalent continuation of circumcision.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   Another difference is that the
Passover lamb was a <u>basis</u> for the forgiveness of sins and was not then
the same thing as the Lord’s Supper, the <u>memorial of the basis</u> for
forgiveness. If they are thus so different, not much can be transferred from
one to the other. Therefore, they are not necessarily of the same frequency.  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>IV. Daily Observance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The argument for daily observance depends
mainly on Acts <b>2</b>:46: <i>“daily . . . breaking bread at home.” “Continued
steadfastly”</i> is used both in<b> 2</b>:42 and <b>2</b>:46 in connection with
the expression “break bread.” In <b>2</b>:42, however, “break bread” occurs in
a list of religious activities while in <b>2</b>:46 it stands alongside of <i>“they
took their food with gladness and singleness of heart.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We can see from 1 Corinthians <b>11</b>
(and probably from Acts <b>20</b>:7) that the early Christians did not observe
the Lord’s Supper daily in those places. Unless we assume a lack of uniform
practice, the Christians in Jerusalem were not observing communion daily; and
the expression “breaking of bread” in Acts<b> 2</b>:46 does not refer to the
ordinance.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Even if <b>2</b>:46 referred to the Lord’s
Supper, we do not establish an obligation or precedent for daily communion. Precedent
requires uniform practice in the apostolic age.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <i>“Continuing <u>daily</u> together in
the temple”</i> does not have to apply beyond the phrase in which it stands;
that is, it does not have to apply to “breaking bread” and “receiving food” as
well.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            One countering consideration is the
accompanying statement “they received their nourishment with gladness.” The
comment could be seen as distinguished from “breaking bread” rather than
expansive of it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> V. Sunday Observance Only?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Although every <u>clear</u> case of
observance occurred on the first day of the week, limiting the observance of
communion to Sunday is an unnecessary inference. Even the institution of the Lord’s
Supper did not take place on Sunday, but on Thursday night. We would not expect
some impropriety, then, in observing this memorial act at other significant occasions
besides a weekly assembly of believers. For that reason, we suspect, Christians
have an inclination to include remembering the Lord in this way at Christmas
Eve services or even weddings. They serve to reference significant observances
of this type back into the central originating event in our faith.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>VI. Observance More Than Once on Sunday</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Some congregations have both morning and
evening services; others have multiple morning services. What should people do
who preach twice or three times on Sunday morning? Should they partake of the
supper in just one of the services or observe the meal with each group of
worshipers? What about people that attend morning and evening service? Should
the emblems be passed to everyone again? Should the ones absent in the morning
identify themselves and have the elements given only to them while the others
watch?  Should those who take the Lord’s Supper to shut-ins receive the emblems
with each person, or is each person expected to take this “common meal” alone?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            None of these matters, of course, are
addressed in the New Testament, so each person has to judge for himself what
seems appropriate in terms of the interpersonal nature of Christianity and in
terms of the practical requirements in the specific setting where he serves. There
appears to be nothing in the nature of the meal or its meaning that would make
multiple observance improper.</span></p>
