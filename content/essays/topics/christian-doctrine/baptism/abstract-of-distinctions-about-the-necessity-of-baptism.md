<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>ABSTRACT OF DISTINCTIONS ABOUT THE NECESSITY OF
BAPTISM</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>1. A distinction needs to be made between <u>standard
situations</u> and <u>special cases</u>. Denying the necessity of baptism applies
only to special cases. Special cases include those in which </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (a) a person would be <u>risking health or
life</u> in being baptized, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (b) people <u>honestly misunderstand</u> that
baptism is immersion or should have been a personal adult response of their
own, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (c) someone is “killed on the way to the
baptistry.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2. A distinction needs to be made between <u>intended
meaning</u> and <u>perceived meaning</u>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>3. A distinction needs to be made between <u>formal</u>
and <u>dynamic acts</u>. Formal acts are not inherently necessary to
interpersonal process, whereas dynamic acts like faith, repentance, moral
behavior <i>are</i> inherent to relationship to other persons—God and other
people.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>4. There is a distinction between <u>the necessity of
baptism</u> and <u>the kind and degree of its     necessity</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>5. There is a difference admittedly between <u>God’s
having to include baptism in salvation</u> and, <u>when he does include it,</u>
<u>including it as a legal requirement</u>. The interpersonal nature of the
covenant determines that baptism is not a legal requirement. Baptism does not
have <u>inherent</u> necessity because it does not have <u>legal</u> necessity.
When God established this—which he did not have to establish, he did not
establish something with legal necessity but interpersonal necessity.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>6. There is a difference between <u>God’s sticking by
his “rule”</u> about being baptized and <u>our reading into his commandment a
kind or degree of necessity that he did not mean</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>7. There is a distinction between <u>fellowship</u>
and <u>leadership</u> when in special cases we consider the reasons for not
being baptized are insufficient.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>8. We distinguish between other people’s <u>disagreeing
with the New Testament</u> and <u>their disagreeing with “our” understanding of
it</u>. Respecting the latter case does not mean we are considering truth
itself relative or unattainable; we only acknowledge that we all can honestly misunderstand.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>9. There is a distinction between <u>misobedience</u>
and <u>disobedience</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>   10. There is a distinction between things <u>equally
true</u> and things <u>equally important</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>11. There is a distinction between <u>legal</u>, <u>natural,
</u>and <u>logical necessity</u>, on the one hand, and <u>interpersonal
necessity</u>, on the other.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>12. There is a distinction between sincerity not making
you <u>right</u> and sincerity not making you <u>forgivable</u>. Sincerity does
not change the truth itself, but it does change the attitude another person can
take toward the sincere person who honestly misunderstands the truth. Making
you forgivable is not just something that can happen after you repent after
being corrected; it can be something able to be overlooked before you get
corrected. Sincerity does not make you right, but it does make you forgivable.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>13. There is a distinction between <u>speaking in
principle</u> and <u>speaking in particular</u> about an individual person. In
talking about the non-inherent necessity of baptism, we are not implying
anything about the state of any person.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>14. There is a distinction between <u>the nature of
baptism as formal act</u> and <u>the subject relative to which that formal act
is performed</u>. The fact that baptism is associated with salvation rather
than with Christian worship, church organization, or Christian fellowship does
not change the degree and nature of its necessity. The kind and degree of
necessity pertains to the nature of the act as formal <i>vs</i>. dynamic, not
the relatively greater decisiveness of the topic (salvation <i>vs</i>.
post-salvation, or non-salvation matters).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>15. We know that baptism is not inherently/legally
necessary because</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (a) baptism stands in an interpersonal
covenant, so it is interpersonal.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (b) formal acts are not inherent to
interpersonal relationships.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (c) we know how God has dealt with the other
failures in special cases to do other formal observances. He has looked at the
heart’s motives and intention.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (d) statements on baptism address standard
situations.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>16. (a) Baptism is a <u>formal, performative act</u> <i>vs</i>.
a<u> dynamic act</u>. God is not as concerned about the outward formal behavior
as he is about the intent of the heart.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (b) If not being baptized is a matter of <u>honest
misunderstanding</u> rather than <u>disobedience</u>, it belongs among “special
cases” or “hard cases.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (c) <i>“Necessary/essential for salvation” </i>is
an <u>ambiguous expression</u>. It has been intended to mean baptism is “part
of” the process of salvation, but it can sound like it means salvation is not
possible without it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>17. Denying inherent necessity is for <u>purposes of
fellowship</u> in <u>special and hard cases</u>. It does not address</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (a) what we <u>preach</u> to unbelievers.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (b) what we <u>teach</u> believers so they
may know the way of the Lord more perfectly.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (c) what we <u>practice</u> when we baptize
people.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (d) the <u>eternal destiny</u> of any person.
That is God’s business.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>18. On special cases and hard cases, we </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>cannot simply suspend judgment</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> and leave it up to God. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (a) The issue is not <u>their actual eternal
destiny or actual present state,</u> but <u>our present fellowship</u>. It is
not simply a matter of <i><u>their</u></i><u> relationship to <i>God</i></u>;
it is equally a matter of <i><u>our</u></i><u> relationship to <i>them</i></u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (b) Baptism is something people <u>do</u>,
not just something they <u>understand</u>. Likewise, our fellowship with them
is something we do, not just something we understand. We cannot, then, handle
the issue the way we handle millennial questions, the exact view of the
trinity, the precise way divine foreknowledge is compatible with human free will,
questions about canonicity of specific Bible books, the interpretation of somewhat
unclear passages that deal with historical or scientific matters.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>19. Baptism is related to <u>salvation</u> more than
to <u>church membership</u> or <u>Christian witness</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>20. The baptism-circumcision comparison in Romans <b>2</b>:25-29
(or elsewhere) does not lie so much at the level of the <u>ordinances</u>
themselves, as at the more general level of <u>formal acts</u> and the
different kinds of covenants in which they stand.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>21. Baptism did not come in the “room of circumcision”
as if there were a change of signs for the <u>same covenant</u>, to which certain
amendments were then made. Rather, there has been a change of covenants, which
have different formal acts of initiation into these <u>different covenants</u>.
The different covenants are also different <u>kinds</u> of covenants so that
their respective signs have correspondingly different meanings.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>22. By getting rid of local church membership, we stay
out of officialness that forces us to decide the salvation status of given
individuals. It would seem right to say that those who are part of the
universal, invisible church have a right to participate in any local, visible
expression of it. We keep fellowship in purely interpersonal terms and relate
in Christ to the extent of our considerable common ground.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>23. Baptism was not established for </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (a) <u>God’s benefit</u> (so he could tell
when a person is saved), </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (b) for <u>the result</u> (as if it produced
the result or we produced the result by doing it), but</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         (c) for <u>our benefit</u> (to help us define
ourselves, crystallize our self-awareness; in this respect it is like a
commencement exercise or, more exactly, like receiving the properly endorsed
diploma).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>24. It is necessary to do whatever God commands about anything.
The issue about baptism is not whether it is necessary, but the sense in which
it is necessary. Systems/categories/acts can have a different kind of
necessity. The kind of necessity in (1) <u>logical</u> deduction is different
from the kind of necessity in (2) <u>natural</u> processes or (3) <u>legal</u>
operations, and all three are different from the kind of necessity in (4) <u>interpersonal</u>
processes. The first three are deterministic, while the latter is not. The latter
is not deterministic because a person has a will (as commander), and there is a
distinction between subjective intention and objective fact, among other
things. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Within interpersonal operations, the
kind of necessity is further defined in terms of whether the act in question is
(a) <u>inherently interpersonal</u> or (b) <u>interpersonalized</u>; that is,
whether it is really interpersonal or symbolic. Inside interpersonal necessity
also is the question about (c) <u>the necessity of personal consistency</u>,
that is, whether God would be “going back on his word” if he saved someone who
unintentionally misunderstood a performative act God commanded as a condition
for salvation. The answer to that depends on what kind of necessity God <u>meant</u>
when he established the act, which brings us back to what we would expect him
as a person to mean in commanding a performative act in connection with an
interpersonal process. In theory, different performative acts can fulfill the
same role, so one performative act is not inherently necessary. It could only
be absolutely necessary if God so dictated that it should be the one.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>25. The necessity or importance of baptism is not
determined by <u>the importance of the subject/meaning</u> that the commandment
is attached to, but by <u>the nature of the act itself</u> relative to that
meaning. Both the interpersonal meaning and the performative act should be
present. In doing the act, however we should not think of the formal part as if
it were such that (a) doing it would be valid even <u>if the consequences would
come without the interpersonal</u>, or that (b) the absence of the formal for
appropriate reasons would mean the invalidity of one’s status with Christ—<u>as
if the consequences could not come even if the formal were missing for
inescapable reasons.</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>         </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
color:windowtext'>  Alexander Campbell’s <i>Lunenburg Letter</i> is something many
people among the Christian Churches/Churches of Christ</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> may not know about; or if they do, they consider it
an inconsistency in his thinking. Nevertheless, he says rather forcefully, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:49.5pt;text-align:justify'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>“I cannot, therefore, make any one duty the standard
of Christian state or character, not even immersion into the name of the
Father, of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit, and in my heart regard all that
have been sprinkled in infancy without their own knowledge and consent, as
aliens from Christ and the well-grounded hope of heaven “Salvation was of the
Jews,” acknowledged the Messiah; and yet he said of a foreigner, an alien from
the commonwealth of Israel, a Syro-Phoenician, “I have not found so great faith—no,
not in Israel.” . . . There is no occasion, then, for making immersion, on a
profession of the faith, absolutely essential to a Christian—though it may be
greatly essential to his sanctification and comfort.”</span></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:49.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>           </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
color:windowtext'>This approach and spirit may not always be maintained as time
goes on.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> As with many movements, this one
that to a great extent derived from him may have crystallized over time, and
many contemporary participants in it have little idea that thoughts like this
one from Campbell’s later years were entertained in that founding age. Alexander
Campbell cannot be of any more importance to a Christian as a measure of sound
teaching than can Luther, Calvin, Augustine, or Aquinas; but if people are
inclined to respect his opinions, they do well to remember that this was one of
them. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>26. The necessity of baptism is not determined by <u>the
importance of the meaning</u> that the commandment is attached to, but by <u>the
nature of the act itself</u> relative to that meaning.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        Notation: Both the interpersonal meaning and
the performative act should be present. We should not, however, in doing the
act, think of the formal part as if it were such that (1) the doing of the
formal act would be valid even if the interpersonal were not there—<u>as if the
consequences would come without the interpersonal [proxy baptism]</u>, or that
(2) the absence of the formal for appropriate reasons would mean the invalidity
of one’s status with Christ—<u>as if the consequences could not come if the
formal were missing for inescapable reasons</u>. This last point relates to the
fact that none of us speak to the salvation status or eternal destiny of any
other person.</span></p>
