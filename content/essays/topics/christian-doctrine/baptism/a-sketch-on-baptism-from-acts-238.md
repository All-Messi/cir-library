<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                               
parallel possibilities)          </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Notations</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            The
remission is based on identity with the One who is what we are called to be and
aspire to be. God is willing to <u>consider</u> us righteous on the basis of
identity with the One who <u>is</u> righteous. The remission is not based on
the act of baptism <i>per se</i>, but on the One we commit to in doing it. [<b>identity
with Messiah</b>]</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            We
do not cause the remission by being baptized. We only meet the condition God
has appointed for <u>his</u> choice to pronounce the remission on the terms he
chose. [<b>God’s choice</b>]</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            The
change from “lost” to “saved” does not occur in us, but in God’s choice based
on the Messiah. We simply indicate our willingness for that to happen—our
willingness to accept the remission as indicated by our being baptized into him—including
all that identity with him entails. [<b>condition, not cause</b>]</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            The
shorthand expression “baptism for remission of sins” can be misheard by other
people who are concerned that what we do does not save us. Characteristically
using the shorthand expression may also unconsciously mislead us into
misunderstanding the value we should attach to what we do in the matter.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Baptism
in water has an <u>appointed</u> meaning, not an <u>inherent</u> one. It would
not <u>have</u> to mean what it does if it were not for God’s appointing that
meaning to it. Other meanings could be, and have been, assigned to the act: Baptized
to Moses (1 Corinthians <b>10</b>:1-2)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>                                                              Baptized
to Paul (1 Corinthians <b>1</b>:14-15)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
