<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>          1.  The
narrowing scope from Genesis <b>1</b>:1 till Moses’ time in history. See “The
Structure of Genesis-Deuteronomy.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>          2.  Comment
about the Sabbath. <b>2</b>:1-3 comes from a day when God had enacted Sabbath
observance as a memorial of the exodus (Deuteronomy <b>5</b>:12-15). The <u>purpose</u>
of the Sabbath was to be a memorial of the exodus, when God “created” Israel (Isaiah<b>
43</b>:1</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>5,
<i>“I am Yahveh, your Holy One, the Creator of Israel . . .”</i></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>);
the <u>manner of observance</u> correlated with God’s creative work: as a rest,
weekly, at the end of the week. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>          3.  Comment
about marriage: <b>2</b>:24. The observation about “leaving father and mother,”
<i>etc</i>., does not come from <u>God</u>’s mouth; God is “he” in the
surrounding material (but Matthew <b>19</b>:3?). As an anticipatory comment, it
seems a early for <u>Adam</u> to be making an observation about factors not yet
part of his own experience or based on anything the account gives about God’s
revelation to him. That leaves <u>Moses</u> as the source of the commandment
(example of a Hebrew “imperatival future”).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>          4.  Historical<span
style='color:black'> and </span>geographical markers for Eden and early events
in Genesis. Place names (patronymics) reflect the viewpoint in the Middle East
(and Palestine?) at the time of writing after the flood: <b>2</b>:8-17, <i>etc</i>.
Note Havilah, where <i>“there <u>is</u> gold, bdellium, and onyx”</i>; Tigris, Euphrates,
Pishon, and Gihon.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span

16,<i> etc</i>. Exodus<b> 6</b>:2-3 says, </span><span dir=RTL></span><span
