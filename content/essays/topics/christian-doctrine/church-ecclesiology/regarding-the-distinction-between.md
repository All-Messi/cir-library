<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>EVANGELIST AND ELDER IN THE LOCAL CHURCH</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            In theory, two positions operating in
relationship may differ from one another in authority in at least six ways:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (a)   as to its being resident <u>in</u>
one <i>vs.</i> passing <u>through</u> the other from the first;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (b)   as to the <u>area</u> of work
to be done where authority is exercised;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (c)   as to the <u>level</u> at
which it is exercised, the first being higher than the second;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (d)   the second receives authority
from the first;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (e)   as to the <u>time</u>
exercised;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (f)    as to the <u>basis</u> of it;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (g)   as to <u>implied powers and
necessary judgments</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (1)  On certain occasions an office that
does not possess authority may serve to extend a superior office’s authority as
in the case of elders who might have deacons supervise other people’s
activities (cp. the apostles’ use of evangelists for this same type of
activity). The office of evangelist might have authority passing through it,
but the authority of eldership is resident in it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (2)   The area of authority might be
different as if, for example, the elder exercised </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    authority in spiritual matters and
the deacons in material matters.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (3)  The level might be different by
putting evangelist authoritatively above or below </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    the elder.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (4)  The elders might give authority to
the evangelist once he gets on the scene.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (5)  Evangelist might have authority over
a congregation <u>until</u> elders are elected. In practice, he probably uses
natural rather than official leadership    until that time.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            (6)  As to the different <u>basis</u> on
which the “right” is founded, (a) the right may be founded on <u>service</u>. Paul
argues his right of apostleship in 2 Corinthians on the service that he has
rendered; he has “earned the right” to be heard and followed (<b>11</b>:16-<b>12</b>:13;
cp. 1 Corinthians <b>9</b>:2; 1 Thessalonians <b>5</b>:12-13). The people he
has served “ought” to follow him. If authority finally rests on service, leadership
on any basis rests on service.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (b) The right may be founded on the <u>message</u>
proclaimed. If authority rests in the message proclaimed, it may be seen to be
in the message by virtue of the fact that <u>only in what has been revealed</u>
to him by God does he speak with authority. In matters beyond that area he does
not speak with authority though it is in the realm of optional, permissive
things and does not contradict the letter or spirit of the message he has been
given.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (c) The right may be founded on the <u>qualities</u>
of the person. Such a person is heard because he knows what he is talking about
(2 Corinthians<b> 6</b>:6; <b>11</b>:6), because of his experience with Christ
(2 Corinthians <b>12</b>:1-6), because of his demonstrated commitment to the
Person in the gospel (2 Corinthians <b>11</b>:23-27), because of his living
concern for the people he leads (2 Corinthians <b>22</b>:28-29), because of his
trustworthiness (2 Corinthians <b>4</b>:2), or because of other personal
characteristics germane to the work he leads (2 Corinthians <b>6</b>:1-10).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (d)  The right may rest in the <u>office</u>
itself. If it is resident in the office itself, it is not limited to areas of
revelation (as in “b” above); it is authoritative beyond revelation. Right is
not tied simply to previous service or greater life quality. Consequently, the
elder would be <u>more than</u> a natural leader; he is an authorized leader as
well. This last position is presumably the correct one.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Elders lead on all four bases ideally
because they first are men of developed qualities above their peers, hence, natural
leaders in their group. They must serve; they teach a revealed message having
authority in it; they have authority in procedural and operational matters
beyond revelation. The nature of their work involves supervising <u>other
people’s activities</u>, not just proclaiming truth <u>to</u> them (prophet,
evangelist) or doing something <u>for</u> them (deacons).  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Evangelists, on the other hand, are
authoritative in that the <u>message</u> they proclaim is authoritative. Evangelists
as evangelists do not supervise other people’s activities beyond <u>revelational</u>
matters. Their work <u>as a function</u> ends where the New Testament ends (not
so of elders) and does not go beyond it into unrevealed <u>procedural</u>
matters. Manifestly, then, their office does not contain supervisory authority;
only the revelation to which they appeal has this authority. An evangelist does
not have to go beyond the authority of the New Testament revelation to
aggregate believers and organize them with elders and deacons (1 Timothy <b>3</b>),
and lead them in the <u>process of</u> discipline (1 Timothy <b>5</b>:19),
because the organizational aspect of the local church is prescribed in the New
Testament, and with all authority (Titus <b>2</b>:15) he commands them to order
themselves in this fashion and exercise discipline. Titus and Timothy had
letters written to them for this precise purpose—as a present authority for
their actions in Ephesus and Crete at Paul’s behest. Of course, beyond <u>revelation</u>
an evangelist may give direction in procedural matters, but his basis is not <u>in
his office</u> as evangelist, but on his <u>personal qualities</u>, <u>previous
service</u>, or natural leadership abilities.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            In the case of evangelist in relation to
the people, the people follow him peculiarly because <u>he</u> said so
(natural, personal basis), not because the <u>evangelist</u> said so
(position), not because he has helped them (service), but because the
evangelist’s <u>Bible</u> says so (message).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            In the case of evangelist in relation to
the elders, it is worth noting the following points:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (a)   An evangelist is never said to
be <u>responsible</u> for the flock; elders are. One person is held responsible
for the activities of others only when he has supervisory authority over them;
otherwise, he has no right to speak for them or be held guilty if they err.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (b)   No evangelist is seen to be
directing beyond the message; elders are.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (c)   It cannot be shown that
evangelists have authority in the position; with elders </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          it can be shown.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (d)  None of the authority words
used of apostles, Christ, and elders are ever used </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          of evangelists.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  (e)   None of the authority figures
used of Christ, apostles, or elders are ever used </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          of evangelists.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  We may close these notes with this
theoretical premise: the highest present position in an authoritative system is
itself authoritative. That position is evidently the eldership as a set of
qualified men.</span></p>
