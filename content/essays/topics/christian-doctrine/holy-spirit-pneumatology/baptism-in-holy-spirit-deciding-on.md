<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'> DECIDING ON “BAPTISM IN THE HOLY SPIRIT” </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Several issues arise with whether
baptism in the Spirit occurs today. It is then not a question that can be
answered until there is agreement on a New Testament definition of terms and a
correct understanding of certain gifts.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>1. How does scripture use the expression <i>baptism in
the Spirit</i>?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  Are those who speak of it today using the term the
same way, or are they loading it with a foreign meaning or one that is too
restrictive?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  *In scripture <i>baptism in the
Spirit</i> is an alternative expression equal to <i>gift of the Spirit</i> and
therefore variable as to whether it has a supernatural component or
miraculous/evidential element. It does not have anything particularly to do
with supernatural enablement to perform some ministry or overcome some
weakness. It is applied to <u>every Christian</u> just as surely as “gift of
the Spirit” applies to every believer, and <u>belongs to the conversion process
itself</u> rather than to a separate experience.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2. What is speaking in tongues/languages?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  People who associate language-speaking
with baptism in the Holy Spirit often assume that <i>“speaking in languages”</i>
is not necessarily a human language.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  *All language speaking in the New
Testament fits with the characteristics of contemporary human languages. We
find no basis for supposing that “languages” may be angelic languages, ecstatic
utterance, or some other kind of non-linguistic expression.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>3. What is the purpose of the occurrence?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  In many people, it is an experience
that shows God has accepted the person and perhaps that he is supernaturally
enabling the person to perform some ministry or receive some capacity for
Christian growth.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.25in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>4. Does scripture talk about any second
definite experience in believers’ lives as a uniform expectancy we should
anticipate or even pray for?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                   </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Those who talk about baptism in the
Holy Spirit in the modern setting may connect it with passages that recount the
laying on of hands to impart some spiritual gift. But such texts are not
necessarily talking about laying hands on everyone present, and they are
associated with <u>supernatural</u> enablement for ministry. The problem then
is created by taking a term that should be used broadly (as equal to gift of the
Spirit) and making a technical expression out of it. It gets narrowed to
something supernatural and mixed in with enablement for ministry. The
expression <i>baptism in the Spirit</i>, however, does not occur in contexts
about imposition of hands or imparting a spiritual gift. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.25in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>5. Are miraculous phenomena to be
expected beyond the foundational stage of the church, the apostolic age?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  That seems to be a moot point in the
biblical text. It is safer to make sure we understand properly what the various
gifts are—particularly <i>“language speaking”</i> and how the New Testament
uses terms—and then apply the definitional test to supposed cases today.</span></p>
