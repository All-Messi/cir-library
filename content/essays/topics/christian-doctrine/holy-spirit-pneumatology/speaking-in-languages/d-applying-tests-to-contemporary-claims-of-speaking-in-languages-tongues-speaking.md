style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    Basic
proposition: Much of what goes under the term <i>tongue speaking</i> (<i>glossolalia</i>)
is a matter of overcoming the adult inhibition against babbling, the inhibition
against making meaningless sounds. Factors that help break this inhibition are
mistakenly identified as the energizing work of the Holy Spirit. The phenomenon
itself is then misidentified with New Testament language speaking. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                  Some
expressions of glossolalia are preceded by “driving,” which raises the

young people. Agonizing for a long time may bring about the “gift.” Something has
to “give” under prolonged pressure.     </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-2.5in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                                              of

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                                     [e]     The

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

God. We wonder to what extent Jesus’ objection to sign-seeking applies here
(John <b>4</b>:48?; <b>6</b>:30; 1 Corinthians<b> 1</b>:22). Many of these
signs were requested by people with less than ideal motives: Matthew <b>12</b>:38-39;
<b>16</b>:1-4 = Mark <b>8</b>:11-12 (cp. Luke <b>11</b>:16-30); John<b> 2</b>:18.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                [2]    Dissociation
of sound from meaning </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-2.5in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                                     [a]     Viewed
as a supernatural gift in which speakers </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-2.5in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                                              do
not understand what they are saying.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         [b]    Continuous
repetition of a word tends to separate its sound from its meaning. This very
mechanic that is sometimes used to induce glossolalia: repeat a few sounds over
and over to “loosen up the tongue,” “prime the working of the Spirit,” or “free
the person from inhibition.” If this experience were a gift from God, learning techniques
would be unnecessary and irrelevant.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                     [3]    The
universal sense of inadequacy and need for </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                              acceptance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         The
sense of inadequacy directly compares to the tendency to being caught up in
glossolalic experience. It does not follow, though, that people involved in the
experience are neurotic, psychotic, psychologically weak, highly emotional,
social outcasts, socially inhibited. A sense of inadequacy plagues us all to
some degree, and forms only one correlation with involvement in charismatic
pursuits.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                     [4]    The
revival of a common experience through which </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                              everyone
has passed</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         In
their subconscious, all people have their childhood experiences, when they lacked
association between a meaning and a sound. The glossolalic experience seems
adequately explained as a return to this stage. Everyone has the capacity to
engage in glossolalia, which fits with its not being limited to Christians. Releasing
inhibition is harder for some than others.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                     [5]    Propensity
of some personalities to depend on an </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                              authority
figure (drive for certainty)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         Some
time ago John P. Kildahl, <i>The Psychology of Speaking in Tongues</i>, p. 43,
fnt. 4, related that the McKenzie Mental Health Scale as applied to the Thematic
Apperception Test showed a significantly greater dependency of tongue speakers on
authority figures.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                [6]    Fakery</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         Occasionally
individuals who cease the practice admit that while acceptably practicing
glossolalia, they found themselves thinking they were using this or that sound
too often and had better change it. If glossolalia is a divine gift of an
actual language, the speaker does not control which sounds are in the
utterance.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (c)     Effects

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                     [1]    Tendency

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                              seem

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         [a]
    A sense of acceptance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                  1)    By
God</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                          We
all want certainty about what we believe and who we are. It is quite human to
want a sign from God that he accepts us. When we believe tongue speaking is
such a sign, we can feel relieved of our uncertainty and confident of our
status with God.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                  2)    By
other people</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                          People
are social creatures who want to feel like a part of the group—to “belong.” When
they experience glossolalia, they no longer <u>feel</u> like they are on the
outside. Of course, the problem is that not having the experience leads to a
sense of rejection from God and separation anxiety.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         [b]    A
sense of release from inhibition</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                  Overcoming
the adult inhibition against babbling shares a feeling that other releases have,
thus giving a sense of triumph and freedom.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                         [c]     Boldness
to witness</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                  When
people regard their experience as a sign, it provides a basis for certainty of
salvation, that God exists, and so on; and it emboldens them to witness to
