<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            How God works in us through his Spirit
should have (1) a <u>positive basis</u> rather than one we cannot <u>dis</u>prove.
We should look for (2) what <u>the prediction of the model</u> is in which Christian
operations take place. Interpersonal reality implies what is natural to
interpersonal process. Feelings, urges, spontaneous ideas, and the like we
should understood in an interpersonal sense rather than assigned to the Spirit
in a special sense if we have no way of recognizing that the Spirit is their
source to the exclusion of other possibilities. Finally, we should try to
determine (3) what is left when <u>control tests</u> are applied. If the same experiences
occur in other religions or with non-religious circumstances, we should not assign
those experiences to the Spirit as directly or specially caused. If we cannot
tell the difference, we should not <u>understand</u> them as supernatural, much
less <u>claim</u> that they are supernatural.</span></p>
