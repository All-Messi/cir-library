margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Non-essentialness of
miracle</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              The behavior of miracle
expectancy in non-communicative “gifts” is <u>experience</u>/orientation,
leading either to despondency (for <u>lack</u> of the experiences) or to forced
misidentification of self-exhilaration (from autosuggestion, <i>etc</i>.) with
the “gift” desired.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              That failure draws
attention away from the purpose of miracles to self-satisfaction by miracles, a
formula Jesus repudiated in the stones-to-bread temptation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Holy Spirit                    Me     
   <i> vs</i>.        Holy Spirit         </span><span style='font-size:6.0pt'>                 </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Others</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Means of availing</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              The behavior of deposit
is <u>asking only</u>, instead of asking while doing/trying (note Luke <b>17</b>:5).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              The result [empowerment]
really does not come from a deposit by one person in another; it arises out of
the two functioning conjointly in a way that one gets the credit and the other
does the succeeding.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              The idea is not <i>“Get
out of the way and <u>let</u> the Spirit work”</i> nor <i>“Let go, and let God.”
</i>In these fruits, we do in response to his influence. We need motivational
help, not substitute doing.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              This process involves
active harmony, not passive coexistence. To avail ourselves of the power of the
Spirit, we must <u>do</u> all those things that bring to our conscious minds
the fact that he is real, here, personal, looking, loving, concerned. Motivation
comes in <u>our</u> doing with a loving person; that is, motivation comes in
active interpersonal relationships.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              To the extent of our
conscious awareness of his real presence, we will have power provided directly by
him through that awareness. Unleashing the power of the Spirit is by <u>conforming</u>
through heightened awareness, not by <u>becoming passive</u>. The spiritual
exercises have the value of making us more aware of the real presence of the
invisible Spirit. These exercises include prayer, Bible reading, meditation,
fellowship, communion. From greater awareness comes greater power.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  6.   Place and nature of feeling</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   Place: <u>confirmatory</u>,
arising out of the relationship</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Nature: <u>interpersonal</u>
(as in the feeling of acceptance or rejection) rather than a feeling of
exhilaration or perception (sense of feeling)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  7.   Pattern of receiving and having</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   Not all at once (as in a
deposit) but developed by degrees as we achieve. Putting to death the members
is a process rather than a point.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   The behavior of deposit
is doubt, because we are constantly aware of the fact that we have faults,
weaknesses, sins, and so on.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Not all Christians have
power to the same degree.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  8.   The confirmatory-apologetic
place of fruit-bearing</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        The behavior of righteousness
in non-Christians (biblically defined) does not force us to deny the goodness
of their motives, or force us to affirm the salvation of people who think they
have the Holy Spirit when they have no biblical reason for thinking so. It is
not necessary for us to deny their having the Spirit either. Thinking they have
the Spirit is as powerful an influence (effectively speaking) as if they did
(cp. believing testimony that is false).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Without the Spirit actually, they
may not be as consistent in fruit-bearing, but they <u>can</u> do the <u>kinds</u>
of things they should. The reason that is true may lie (1) in a difference in
the Spirit’s circumstantial and ecclesiastical operation and (2) in people’s going
about empowerment in the wrong way.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        The importance of this
construct manifests itself in doctrinal considerations. For example, Mr. X has
good life quality; he must have the Holy Spirit; he has not been willing to identified
with Christ in Christian baptism; so baptism has nothing to do with having the
Spirit or the salvation it rests on. Because he has good fruit does not mean he
is in the position to <u>teach doctrinal matters</u> or to explain how fruits
are produced.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        In this thought sequence,
ambiguous experience gives rise to doctrine rather than letting clear
revelation do it. (We also do not have to deny Mr. X’s salvation status or his
having the Spirit either; God may dispense his gifts on different conditions
from those to which he has committed himself. We cannot presume on such grace,
however, nor offer conditions of our own choosing.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            <img width=359 height=151 id="Picture 1"
src="/cir-library/images/essays/power-from-the-spirit-book-pt-6/image001.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  The presence of the Spirit is not
necessarily different from thinking we have his imperceivable presence. It is
motivational power that produces fruit. Our motivational level rises in
response to the personal influence of the Spirit, whom we cannot perceive. That
concept is a minimum affirmation that allows for additional, unnecessary and
non-uniform benefits whether (a) supernatural on the person’s ability level
itself (potential, but not essential), (b) providential operation through the
environment (calamity, specially planted experiences like opportunity), (c) the
Spirit’s oversight of the church’s influence in addition to (d) the written
word.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Important elements</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   We know we have the Spirit by
testimony, not feeling (Judges <b>16</b>:20).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   We have the ability to produce
fruit (2 Peter <b>3</b>:9; Ezekiel <b>18</b>:23).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   The lack is motivational.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  In the above diagram, unfounded
knowledge creates the same motivational power as the truth itself because it <u>is</u>
<i>“the truth to him.”</i> Each person has the capacity to produce fruit, and motivation
is a subjective thing.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  We believe this construct also
explains how saved people can end up having a lower life quality than someone that
is not a Christian. Having the presence of God’s Spirit does not help unless
Christians avail themselves of the empowerment that presence can bring. Not
being a Christian—being a “good moral person”—but being motivated by the
presumed presence of God may yield a higher life quality than some people in
the church.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  It does not follow, however, that
the Holy Spirit is of no use, or that he does not have to exist to help me. It
does not follow from the fact that the subjective conviction produces the
result that the objective truth is unnecessary.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  It is only because of previous true
testimony properly believed that people respond the way they do in this
instance. It is true that the Spirit is of the sort someone might believe him is
and that he helps our weaknesses in the way we have described. The error is in
thinking that the person has that promise. I may be just as happy and moved to
work just as hard to fix up a piece of property I think I have bought as I
would if I had signed a legitimate deed that actually made the property mine. It
does not follow, however, that owning the property is unnecessary for the
happiness and motivation to fix it up.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Christianity       
        (1)                motivation                (2)               fruit</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='position:relative;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          Alternative
religious</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                
construct</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  In step (2) Matthew <b>7</b>:15-20; <b>12</b>:23
pertains to consistency between outward behavior and inward motivation. It does
not speak to (1) the truthfulness of the system out of which such motivation
rises.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  In John <b>15</b>:1-8, fruit does
not prove the truth of a system, and that does not hurt anything. Fruit is a
negative test—confirmatory evidence. In other words, if fruit is not occurring,
the relationship does not exist. Fruit is not the most fundamental point of Christianity.
Fruit is a byproduct of personal relationship with God. It is not goodness, but
perfection that is necessary for that relationship. The same answer regarding
good moral people (who say they are better than the people in your church, so
why come?) applies to why non-Christian systems may produce fruit without
proving the total systems are true. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Since fruit is confirmatory (fits
with, but does not establish), we are not compelled to accept the truthfulness
of quasi-Christian and non-Christian belief systems where good character may
appear. Luke admitted that Sergius Paulus was a “prudent” man (Acts <b>13</b>:7).
Paul admitted that some noble people respond the gospel (1 Corinthians<b> 1</b>:26).
<b>The degree of goodness is not the essential problem Christianity addresses,
but the need for perfection that only grace-faith can give because grace is
something God <u>treats us</u> as  having (trust), not something we have
(works). </b></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  All things being equal, however, the
Christian system should engender a <u>better motivation</u> for fruit because
it involves the <u>greatest love display</u> of any religion, and love is the
best “mechanism” for motivation. The capacity for motivation is hard to read
because other factors enter in, factors like (1) the person’s sin level and (2)
the quality of exposure to the belief system.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Before leaving the question about
empowerment through the Spirit actually <i>vs.</i> supposed, we need to
remember the Spirit’s imperceivable operation through circumstance. If we really
do not have the Spirit, that aspect of his work may be missing or at least
weaker.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  Intercession</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Romans <b>8</b>:16, 26-27</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Ephesians <b>2</b>:18?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Advocate: John <b>14</b>:16, 26 (cp.
1 John<b> 2</b>:1 re Jesus)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Unification</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1 Corinthians<b> 12</b>:13</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Empowerment through sheer <u>personal
relationship</u> is <u>part</u> of how the Spirit empowers (I-You <i>vs</i>.
I-He). We do not forget the other three avenues in the process in emphasizing
this one: the <u>Word</u>, <u>Other Christians</u>, and <u>Natural Circumstances</u>
operate alongside sheer presence. <u>Special intervention</u> is another,
unknowable variable that God can use secretly to kick up experiences that
improve those who respond to them appropriately. </span></p>

operation have different spiritual exercises that activate them. <u>Reading</u>/study
activates “Bible.” <u>Fellowship</u> activates “Other Christians.” <u>Prayer</u>
activates “Circumstance.” <u>Meditation/contemplation/communion</u> and the
other three activate “Relationship.” Scripture provides the content; the other
three make it real.</span></p>
