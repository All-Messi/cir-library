<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              A person may be called
to be a missionary, a missionary to Germany, a missionary to Berlin, Germany; a
specific area in Berlin, a particular person in Berlin.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><span style='text-decoration:none'>&nbsp;</span></span></u></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   Questions that occur in light
of the biblical examples of calling and leading</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   In what ways did God call
to specific tasks and direct his special </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              messengers?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              They were clear, observable
ways that they could not confuse with chance, natural phenomena, or subjective
experiences. So, if we cannot tell the difference between natural phenomena and
subjective experience, we should not suppose that what we are experiencing is divine
revelation or special guidance. If it is good as per scripture, it is all right
to do. Since the Spirit is personal, he is not like a <u>force field</u>, and
knowing his will is not like having <u>ESP</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   From the biblical
examples, we have no reason to think God had for every Christian a specific job
to do or that he directed each step each day. There were thousands of God’s
people whom we have no indication that he specially led—like those dispersed at
the stoning of Stephen.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    God is a Father. A
father answers his children when they ask a question. But allegorizing the
comparison can happen here. God always answers, but it is not necessarily an
audible one like <i>“Samuel, Samuel”</i> in the night. Furthermore, he does not
always answer <i>“yes.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    <i>“If the Lord
will”</i> sounds permissive: Acts <b>18</b>:21; 1 Corinthians <b>4</b>:19; <b>16</b>:7;
<u>James <b>4</b>:13-15</u> (Hebrews <b>6</b>:3; Romans <b>1</b>:10; <b>15</b>:32;
1 Peter<b> 3</b>:17; 1 Corinthians <b>12</b>; Romans <b>12</b>:6-8)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-117.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    All things being
equal, why would God rather have “X” do a job than “Y”?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (4)    Interpreters might
suppose that the body figure implies that each of us has a specific job God
wants us to do instead of someone else or some other job (Ephesians <b>4</b>:1-16;
1 Corinthians<b> 12)</b>. But it is one thing to say that there is something
for each of us to do; it is another thing to say that God chooses <u>which</u>
job he wants each one of us to do.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (5) In interpreting passages
related to divine guidance, we need to remember the Semitic tendency to omit
intermediate causes as well as causal distinctions in general.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Is there reason for
supposing that the biblical examples of special calling and special direction
are proportionately the same throughout history as in biblical times?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              There could be less
special attention needed after God’s program has been set in operation. For
four hundred years between the testaments, there was no exact succession of
prophets. Since the Bible is a collection of God’s special acts in history, the
amount of special leading in scripture does not necessarily hold true all the
time. Likewise, the proportion of people specially led in the early church does
not necessarily compare with that of today.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Rather than wonder
whether God calls and guides as directly today, we can put ourselves in the
first-century setting and apply the principles Christians needed to use to
distinguish God’s calling from other influences.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        d.   Aside from special
direction, what considerations determine what we should do with our life? We
can say that he operates <u>by principle</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    The teaching of
scripture                 </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    Needs there are                            
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    Opportunities
that come                       </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (4)    Interests we have</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (5)    Abilities we have</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (6)    Suggestions we receive</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (7)    Maximum influence
possible</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (8)    Doing it with our
strength: Ecclesiastes <b>9</b>:10</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (9)    Prayer</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                            (10)    Sense of the
Spirit’s presence</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Items 2-5 are subject to
modification. People can read, research, study,</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              pray, discuss, develop
skills.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        e.   Do we necessarily need to
know the Spirit is specifically leading us? There could be something special
going on “behind the scenes” at God’s behest that will shape our choices. That
divine guidance does not have to be overt to be real.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              *(1)  What about <i>“throwing
out the fleece”</i> (Judges <b>6</b>:11-40)?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       Judges <b>6</b>:17
is important because it shows that Gideon’s concern was not to determine what
the Lord wanted him to do, but to make sure that it was Yahveh that was wanting
him to do it. Paul did the same thing on the way to Damascus when he asked, <i>“Who
are you, sir?”</i> (In a similar vein, note Xenophon’s <i>Anabasis</i> <b>3</b>:1:4-7.)
We are not being led by the Spirit if we are setting the options and telling
God to choose between them, or if we set the option and ask God’s direction in
helping us fulfill it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-117.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    Affirm the Spirit’s
potential special guidance by promise yet be careful about affirming the Spirit’s
special guidance in a given case.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       How do we tell
the difference between God not opening the door and Satan holding it shut? If
something apparently good does not “succeed,” do we conclude that it was not
God’s will although it was good, the doors seemed to open, and we felt good
about it when we started?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-117.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    To the extent
that God’s will is specific his guidance must be explicit.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       God holds us
responsible for what we can know: Romans <b>3</b>:25; 1 Timothy <b>1</b>:13;
Acts <b>17</b>:30; <b>14</b>:16 (?). If we cannot distinguish his special guiding
from the normal course of events, it seems best not to suppose that he is
specially leading. We can go ahead and do what is right under his general will.
Within any set of circumstances there are opportunities to do many good things
for God’s glory. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (4)    What does it mean
to be sensitive to the will of God?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       Being sensitive
to God’s will does not require a special capacity beyond our Christian
capacities. It means having our heart in the right place so Christian concerns
are in mind as we go about our routine, make choices, and formulate plans. Instead
of trying to see what is invisible, we adopt an <u>attitude</u> of submission
to God’s will.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (5)    What about
answered prayer?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       Prayer is
primarily for benefiting. Answered prayer may confirm faith, but it is not
often an apologetic basis for ours or other people’s faith or guidance.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (6)    God is the more
glorified by the less he has to do for us. God lets us do as much as we can do
without doing it for us.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (7)    Difficulties may
come from supposing direct, specific, knowable guidance in all instances (same
as those under “The Gift[s] of the Spirit”).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (a)     Encourages
the behavior of waiting</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:2.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-2.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                     *(b)    Encourages
reading events as signs to be followed rather than as data to be considered</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:2.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-2.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (c)     Encourages
self-justification; so we may not be sufficiently receptive to advice from
other Christians</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                We may
reason that because we have the Holy Spirit, an idea that comes to mind the
Spirit planted there. Especially is that true if it comes to mind in connection
with a spiritual exercise like Bible reading or prayer. The rule should be that
if we cannot tell the difference between an idea that comes to our mind and
revelation from God, we ought not regard it as revelation even for our own
thinking, much less for directing the activities of other people.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (d)    Tends
toward tempting God</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:2.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-2.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (e)     Requires
secondary explanations when things do not work out as planned, guilt often
being attached to the lack of success</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:2.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-2.0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (f)     The
specific “will of God” is not always kept within the general will of God as it
should be.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (g)    We tend
to think that ideas that come to mind unexplainedly </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                are revelation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (h)    Confuses
result with cause</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                Looking
back over a series of events shows the pattern they followed. </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>That is different from supposing that
special guidance caused the pattern.</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> The
very occurrence of a choice eliminates all the other options at each point, and
a set pattern emerges. When we look back over the past, those other options are
no longer “there” to see; and it can look like God had it planned all along.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       (i)     Creates
false expectancies; when they are not fulfilled, <u>despondence</u> and <u>disappointment</u>
can come, even <u>abandoning</u> the faith because it does not deliver on what
it supposedly promises. It can make us <u>question our relationship</u> with
God. Finally, it makes us susceptible to false teachers who claim to have special
experiences we have wanted to have.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                The
scattering of Christians after the stoning of Stephen (Acts <b>11</b>:19ff.)
may illustrate non-specific will for Christians. At any rate, the fact that
Christians may with good intent do something that in time proves not to be good
or not good indicates that guidance is not exact, special, and direct.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   Model for guidance and other
post-conversion work of the Spirit</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt'><span style='font-size:8.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                                                         
 </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               <img width=303 height=124 id="Picture 1"
src="/cir-library/images/essays/gidance-by-the-spirit-book-pt-5/image002.png">      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>          </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   Dangers in the model </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              We can push concepts to
extremes, to “absolutize” them. In the case of divine guidance, the extremes
are (a) no direct involvement in history and our lives (deism) and (b) direct
involvement in every act and choice we make. As there are dangers in overdoing God’s
prescriptive will for us, so also there are dangers in “under-doing” it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    The supposition
that God is not involved at all and that Christians are on their own and alone
in the world. <u>It could create a sense of abandonment</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    Not properly
crediting God with the accomplishments of ministry and personal growth <u>could
create pride</u>. We could misconstrue success.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    <u>Not properly
crediting him could create an illegitimate sense of guilt</u>, because we see
the result as too dependent on our own actions. We could misconstrue failure.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Ways to guard against the
dangers of the model</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    In <u>attitude</u>
we are willing to let God have prescriptive control down to the smallest detail
of life, while in <u>responsibility</u> we are willing to make the choices on
our own all the way up to the largest ones. That is what has happened in our choosing
salvation.      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    To be invisible
does not mean non-existent.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-117.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    Not to know God
is specifically leading does not mean he is not specifically leading.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (4)    Again, God can be
the more glorified the less he does for us in </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       guidance and
otherwise. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Implications of the model</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1) What is capable of
an alternate explanation is not capable of certainty. We cannot be sure about
something if it has an alternate explanation. If we cannot distinguish the idea
from our own idea, we should not claim the Spirit told us.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    God holds us
responsible only for what we can know.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    To the extent
that God’s will is specific his guidance is explicit.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (4)    God lets us do as
much as we can without doing it for us.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (5)    We can go ahead
with our own judgment if there is no special </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       indication of
his will.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (6)    Let the simpler
explanation explain as much as possible: the law of parsimony means that the
simpler explanation is more likely correct; at least it is safer. Do not appeal
to supernatural explanation if “natural” explanation suffices.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        d.   Effects of the model</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-117.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    <u>Glorification
of God </u>(doing as much as we can without direction or help); a sense of
responsibility</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    <u>Assurance;</u>
a sense of being pleasing to God</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    <u>Freedom</u>; a
sense of self-determination that fits with the fact that we have a will, a
sense of security, and his word. We do not need to read into events the feeling
that God is specially trying to tell us something—if only we could figure it
out.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  5.   Summary: two models for
guidance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   God has a specific will
for each person at each point in life. He wants to direct all our choices
directly and exactly. We need to search for what that will is. The inferences
are:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.5in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                           (1)    If I am not getting
that kind of direction, there is something wrong with my faith.         GUILT</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:135.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-117.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    If I am not
seeking that kind of guidance, I am not being sensitive to God’s direction.     GUILT</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    The things that
are happening to me are signs I should follow rather than data I should
evaluate.      FORCED INTERPRETATION OF EXPERIENCE </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   God may or may not have a
specific will for each of us at each point in life. God does not necessarily want
to direct everything we do—any more than a parent wants to program everything
the children do. We are to be willing to let him make all our choices, but are
willing to make the choices ourselves.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              GOD IS THE MORE GREATLY
GLORIFIED BY THE LESS HE DOES FOR US (as long as we are willing to let him do
with us according to his will).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              The Spirit is always
guiding his people, but that does not mean that he is always guiding them directly,
miraculously, or perceivably. His guidance may involve supernatural
intervention in varying amounts, but he has not <u>promised</u> miraculous, direct
guidance to each of us. So, we do not require that kind of guidance as an
indication that we or others are genuine Christians. Furthermore, guiding
people miraculously and directly is not always, or perhaps often, <u>necessary</u>.
We live by faith more than by sight (2 Corinthians <b>5</b>:7); likewise, Jesus
told Thomas in the resurrection scene that those are blessed who have not
seen—directly experienced—and yet believe. God wants people to trust him
farther than see him. He wants them to trust his promises, more than see his
signs.</span></p>

style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>
