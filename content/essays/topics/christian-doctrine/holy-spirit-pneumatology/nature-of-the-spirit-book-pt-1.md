margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              the Spirit of the Lord”</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>; <i>“There is a good ‘spirit’ among the workers.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  We must distinguish he effects of
the Spirit from the Spirit himself: <u>feelings</u> that come over people, the <u>irrational/non-rational/affective</u>,
<u>spontaneity</u> and <u>uninhibitedness</u>, the <u>creativity</u> (the
origin of ideas that come unexplainably to mind, the “tacit dimension”), <u>insanity</u>,
<u>enthusiasm</u> and boldness, altered states of consciousness, the subjective
dimension, apparently miraculous occurrences—in short, anything unusual or
unexplained.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  The five alternatives denied above
are no more to be associated with the Spirit than with the Son.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Christians tend to supernaturalize
their experiences when they do not know the cause of them. They do so to
glorify God, to be sure; but the implications can be misleading and unhealthy
when natural explanations come clear later.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Effects of the personal emphasis</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   Qualifies the idea of “getting
the Spirit”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Getting the Spirit is
conceived of as getting enthused. Enthusiasm is one of the usages of the
English word “spirit” (“school spirit”). Enthusiasm may result from the
person-Spirit relationship; but since it may come from elsewhere, enthusiasm
and the Holy Spirit do not correlate one-to-one.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   Reduces the tendency to suppose
that one can “perceive,” or “feel,” the Spirit: Samson did not know the Spirit
had left him (note Judges <b>16</b>:20). Experiencing the Holy Spirit is not
like having an electric “thrill.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   Looks away from the necessity of
supernatural manifestation of the Spirit’s presence. Having the Spirit is first
an interpersonal presence rather than a supernatural empowerment. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Natural <i>vs</i>.
supernatural is thought of in connection with natural laws inside creation. What
God and we have in common is not supernatural. We are created in the image of
God. That wherein we are like God is natural, and what transpires between us
within that area is natural process. We are related to a supernatural person,
but the relationship itself is not supernatural.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        So, relationship to God does
not require miracle as part of the experience. Miracle is not interpersonal
process, because in miracle God is the only one acting to produce the result
directly. Interpersonal process is often indirect operation, and there is
response by the other that combines with God’s act to produce the result.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>   <img width=335 height=112 id="Picture 1"

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        In miracle, conditionality is the
only role that we have. We may ask for the effect, but the effect itself comes
from God alone. In influence there is more than request, because the person’s
response joins with influence to produce the result.   </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   Avoids mechanical
conceptualizations like the idea that the Spirit is “transmitted” in succession
or healing by the imposition of hands or insufflation (John <b>20</b>:22). These
are symbolic acts not real causes.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  5.   Avoids the tendency to think
that having the Spirit is like having an idea.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            F.   “Problem” text: In Ephesians <b>6</b>:17,
