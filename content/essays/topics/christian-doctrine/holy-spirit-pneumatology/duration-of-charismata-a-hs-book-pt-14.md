<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>II. Duration of the Supernatural Charismata</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    Introduction </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    Four approaches have arisen regarding
the duration of supernatural charismata.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    1.   Miraculous manifestation was
scaffolding for the original building of the church. After the church was adequately
established, the apologetic base shifted to the record of miraculous manifestation.
Miracle correlates with new revelation; since the new covenant closes
revelation, it does not include progressive revelation, and miraculous authentication
is not ongoing.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    2.   The sign gifts are permanent
throughout the church age, because they are part of the continuing apologetic
for the Christian message. Record of authentication does not replace ongoing
authentication. Advocates differ as to whether every Christian has a
supernatural gift.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    3.   A mediating position supposes
that in pioneering a new field, supernatural may occur until the church has
been firmly established there—in much the same way miracle validated the
original apostolic preaching.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    4.   The idea of “the latter rain”
is that supernatural manifestation will show up again in the church as history
nears the end times. Typically advocates picture the current situation as the
“latter days.” (See below under “The Testimony of History.”)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    Four distinctions apply to the
duration of miracles in the church age.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    1.   The question is not what God <u>can
do</u>, but what he <u>has decided to do</u>; not what he <u>can do</u> but
what he <u>does</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    2.   The question is not whether
God <u>supernaturally intervenes</u> today, but whether he <u>miraculously
supernaturally intervenes</u> today. Answered prayer can involve supernatural
intervention as surely as miracle does, but it is not usually an <u>overt</u>,
visible intervention.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    3.   The question is not what we
believe is happening today, but whether scripture indicates anything about the
duration of miraculous phenomena. The question is about <u>scripture</u> more
than <u>experience</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    4.   The question is not whether
the Spirit <u>exists</u> or <u>does anything</u> today; it is whether he
manifests himself supernaturally in his doing. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

style='font-size:12.0pt'>μέρους</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>. But love
continues, indeed increases by virtue of the directness that fosters greater appreciation.
<u>Trust</u> will exist in the face-to-face relationship, <u>hope</u> will be
possible, and knowledge will continue; but faith and hope will be with respect
to other matters than they now are, and knowledge will be </span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐπίγνωσις</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>, no
longer </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>γνῶσις</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>, experienced (direct) rather than communicated
(indirect).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Parallels between 1 Corinthians<b> 13</b>, Romans <b>8</b>,
and 2 Corinthians <b>5</b>:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               <u>1 Corinthians <b>13</b></u>                        
<u>Romans <b>8</b></u>                        <u>2 Corinthians<b> 5</b></u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><span style='text-decoration:none'>&nbsp;</span></span></u></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              (1) face-to-face                          the
seen (24)                     sight (7)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>then</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              (2)                                              redeemed
body (23)          at home with the Lord=absent </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                           from
physical body (8)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              (1)                                     groaning
in ourselves (24)         present in the body (6)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>now</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              (2) through a mirror</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    darkly (12)                   hope
(24-25)                              faith (7)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Paul says in Romans <b>8</b>:38, <i>“Life . . . does
not separate us from God’s love.”</i> In Corinthians, love is present in this
life and will continue in the next. <i>“For me to live is Christ, to die is
gain”</i> (Philippians <b>1</b>:21).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          2.   Interpretative conclusion:
“The perfect” is the face-to-face relationship that </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                begins at death.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                Paul, speaking of the </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐκ</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>μέρους</span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>vs. </span></i><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>τὸ</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>τέλειον</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>, is talking about the knowledge of Christ in this
life <i>vs.</i> the knowledge of Christ in the next. That distinction is background
for saying, <i>“Do not get caught up in the medium</i> (tongues, <i>etc</i>.) <i>instead
of the message”</i> (the <u>reality</u> of the God-man relationship most
synoptically characterized by love).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                a.   Christianity brings
personal relationship with God through Christ.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      <u>Understanding</u>
is not the point (1 Corinthians <b>8</b>:1; <b>13</b>:2). If people seem to
know <u>anything</u>, they do not yet know what they need to know (1
Corinthians <b>8</b>:3). <u>Abilities</u> are not the point (1 Corinthians <b>13</b>:2),
because they are not ultimately germane to the Christian system understood as a
loving-relationship with God.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                b.   The next life
begins immediately after death: 2 Corinthians <b>5</b>:1-10; Revelation<b> 6</b>:9-11;
Luke <b>23</b>:43; Philippians <b>1</b>:21-24. That fact is implied also by
Christ’s appearance to Stephen at Stephen’s execution (Acts <b>7</b>:55-60).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                c.   1 Corinthians<b>
13</b>:1-<b>14</b>:40 deals only with information and communication gifts: <b>14</b>:6
(tongues, prophecy, knowledge, revelation, teaching, and faith) is in response
to these. Information and communication gifts pertain to indirect knowledge.    </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                d.   Paul deals with
both natural and supernatural gifts in 1 Corinthians <b>12-14</b>, and even in
1 Corinthians <b>13</b>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      Knowledge is not
particularly supernatural in <b>13</b>:8. Knowledge (</span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>γνῶσις</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>vs.</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐπίγνωσις</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>)
will be done away. Knowledge is listed last before <i>“now we know in part”</i>
(</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>γινώσκω</span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>vs.</span></i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐπιγνώσομαι</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>). If we know in part and our </span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>γνῶσις</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> will pass
away (12b), it follows that </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>γνῶσις</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> is not necessarily by supernatural means—though it
could be. Prophesying is not necessarily supernatural.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                e.   Paul’s emphasis
in 1 Corinthians <b>13</b> is appropriate because love is the all-inclusive
term that regulates and defines interpersonal behavior for now and eternity.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      Love partakes
now of a reality yet more fully to come, whereas spiritual gifts are only means
to awareness of the reality. Communications (<b>13</b>:1), abilities (<b>13</b>:2),
and activities (<b>13</b>:3) that do not partake of this reality are not
meaningful (<b>13</b>:1-3). Communication, abilities, deeds become meaningful
only in the context of love, which governs relationship with God.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      Love is then the
central emphasis of 1 Corinthians <b>13</b> in the context of not confusing the
medium of communication with the message communicated. What is communicated is
relationship to Christ. Love is the characteristic feature of our relationship
to him and all others related to him. Love must pervade everything we do, or ur
doing has abandoned the orienting characteristic of the faith. Without love all
prophesying is devoid of meaning because it is devoid of relevance to personal
relationship.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                f.    The data best
fit this construction of Paul’s thought.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                <b>          </b>            (1)    The
approach makes the identification between </span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>τὸ</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>τέλειον</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
and</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    <b>                           </b></span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>πρόσωπον</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt'> </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>πρὸς</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>πρόσωπον</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>that
the parallelism requires.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                               <u>Face
to face</u> and its equivalents suggest that direct-immediate <i>vs.</i>
indirect-mediate is more the point than degree of completeness (a figurative
usage that could arise from the literal situation out of which the expression
arose: face to face; hence, direct; hence, complete). Relevant passages are
Genesis <b>32</b>:20, 30; Exodus <b>33</b>:11; Numbers <b>12</b>:6-8; <b>14</b>:14;
Deuteronomy<b> 5</b>:4; cp. <b>25</b>:16; Galatians<b> 2</b>:1; 2 John 12. 1
John <b>3</b>:2 seems to carry a like idea. 2 Corinthians <b>3</b>:18 says we
behold with unveiled face the glory of the Lord as in a mirror. It has been
taken to mean something comparable to the knowledge of God in the Old
Testament, a comparison suggested by the veil Moses put over his face because
the people of Israel were afraid to look at him after he had faced the glory of
God. The expression “unveiled” is comparable to face-to-face, and its
combination with mirror is striking in connection with 1 Corinthians <b>13</b>.
But that it warrants calling the knowledge people have in the New Testament a
face-to-face knowledge is not necessary. (See <u>canon view</u> below.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                               (a)     Comparing
the two contexts puts “mirror” in a different position. 1 Corinthians puts
mirror over against “face-to-face” and separates them by time (“now” and “then”).
2 Corinthians puts them on the same side in the “now.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                               (b)    Furthermore,
the canon view (see below) says the perfect is the completed revelation by the
end of the century, but 2 Corinthians is speaking in the 50s about this
unveiled situation. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                               (c)     In
2 Corinthians the unveiled is in regard to <u>glory</u>; in 1 Corinthians the
face-to-face is in regard to <u>knowledge</u>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                               (d)   The
comparison in 2 Corinthians is between what “we” have now with what they had in
the Old Testament; the comparison in 1 Corinthians is between what “we” have
now and what “we” will have later. The use of 2 Corinthians <b>3</b>:18 to show
that face-to-face means complete knowledge makes a word comparison rather than
a meaning comparison.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              <b>            </b>            (2)    Paul—and
any Christian—can be on both sides of the situation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                g.   The approach correlates
earthly and heavenly with the present life and the next life. This schematic
captures the idea. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              <img width=342 height=135 id="Picture 1"
src="/images/essays/duration-of-charismata-a-hs-book-pt-14/image001.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      The perfect is
aside from the duration of miraculous gifts because time is not the point. Paul
does not contrast one time in history with a later time in history, but this
life with the next life—a life already begun by saints who have passed beyond
the veil. It is not a contrast of this <u>age</u> with a later time, but of
this <u>stage</u> with the next stage. How long miraculous gifts continue in
this age is not the point; the point is that they are in this stage only. If
they are in this life only, they are temporary. The permanent must govern the
temporary.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          3.    Against the canon view</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                “The perfect” refers
to the completion of new revelation in written form, hence, the completion of
the canon.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                              a.   Under
that view the “we” of the </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐκ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>μέρους</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> cannot be (among) the “we” of the </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>τὸ</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>τέλειον</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      (1) It is not likely
that Paul knew less than what came to comprise the full content of the New
Testament canon.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      (2)     It is
not possible to consider Paul as being in the perfect as well as the imperfect
if <i>perfect</i> means the complete canon; he died too soon.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                               There
may be an outside chance that “we” in the two contrasting statements is
comprised of different personnel as in the idea that we Christians know in part
now, but we Christians will know <i>in toto</i> then, even if the individuals
who make up the “we” change because of time. But it does not seem likely to put
that construction on it since this contrast involving <i>we</i>’s is compared
with the same individual’s growth from childhood to maturity. Besides, the growth
is with respect to persons, not revelation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                b.   “Face-to-face”
cannot be taken literally—its more natural import.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                c.   <i>“I will know
the revelation in the future as the revelation knows me in the present”</i> is
not so natural to say as, <i>“I will know Christ as [directly] as he knows me
now.”</i> (Hebrews <b>4</b>:12, however, is unclear as to whether <i>word</i>
means scripture or <i>Word</i> meaning Christ; the text seems to melt the two
usages together.)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      It may be
possible to say that <i>“I will know the truth [as a result of the completed
revelation] as Christ knows me now.”</i> The objection is still that such an
approach acts as if first-century disciples did not know as completely as
modern Christians who have the written word; that seems to confuse completeness
of knowledge with completeness of inscripturation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                d.   The contrast is
usually (1) bit-by-bit information via individual revelations to those who had
the gifts of prophecy and knowledge as distinguished from (2) a complete
information set available to Christians in the completed Bible.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      That view makes </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐκ</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>μέρους</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> mean “in parts” rather than “in part.” But the point
of the text does not contrast modes of receiving, but directness, degree, or
completeness. The contrast is partial <i>vs.</i> complete, or preliminary <i>vs.</i>
consummate, rather than in parts <i>vs.</i> in one set. It is the kind of
knowledge that comes from someone’s telling about it <i>vs.</i> experiencing it.
The point is not <u>progressive</u> <i>vs</i>. complete (manner), but <u>partial</u>
<i>vs</i>. full (content). We have no basis for saying that first-century
Christians knew only “in part” relative to later content in the canon.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                e.   The canon
approach has no provision for non-miraculous gifts (assuming that faith and
knowledge in <b>13</b>:2, 8 are not miraculous possessions) and yet advocates
use it as an argument for the cessation of miraculous gifts. If non-miraculous
is included here, the argument would require the cessation of non-miraculous gifts
as well. Here the argument proves too much.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                f.    The canon
approach has no provision for explaining why Paul talks only about communicative
and informational gifts in the immediate context of the part <i>vs</i>. the
perfect; yet advocates use it to argue for the cessation of miraculous
manifestation generally. That argument might close the canon, but it would not exclude
miracles of healing. Here the argument proves too little.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                g.   The canon
approach does not explain the change from </span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>γνῶσις</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'> to </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐπίγνωσις</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
in <b>13</b>:12—communicated <i>vs.</i> direct experience, faith <i>vs.</i>
sight, knowing by being told <i>vs. </i>knowing by experiencing.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                h.   The canon
approach does not explain how faith and hope can be in the proposed </span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>ἐκ</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'>μέρος</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> but not in the </span><span lang=EL style='font-size:
12.0pt'>τὸ</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>τέλειον</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                i.    First-century
Christians may have known some things about the future that are not in the canon
now (2 Thessalonians <b>2</b>:5, <i>e.g</i>.). The canon approach assumes not
only that early Christians did not know as much as is in the canon now, but
also that the apostle Paul did not.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          As a matter of fact, genuine
miracle may have ceased at about the time the New Testament was finished. The
latter could be the cause of the former without that correlation being the
point in 1 Corinthians<b> 13</b>. It is not so much that it was complete, but
that it was written, that miracles could now cease. This context deals with
completeness, not written completeness.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          4.   Against the permanence
view</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                “The perfect” refers
to the situation at the coming of Christ with the implication that miraculous
gifts will continue till the end.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                a.   This view does
not take the nearest adequate fulfillment, a general principle for interpreting
prophecy. There is no contextual reason to make “the perfect” wait till Christ’s
return when a nearer fulfillment exists.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                b.   Even if we think
of the face-to-face relationship as not beginning until the parousia, since
more than the miraculous is in the <u>in-part</u> situation, 1 Corinthians <b>13</b>
does not become an argument for the duration of miracles. For reasons not
addressed here, the miraculous aspect of the in-part might stop while the
in-part as such continues in its non-miraculous aspects.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          Of the views set aside the permanence-of-miracle
view is the closest to correct.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          5.   Against the view that “the
perfect” is the unity of the Jewish-Gentile church</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                a.   This view is
aside from this context.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                b.   There is no time
when this degree matter could be said occur.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                        c.   The
childhood of the church fits the infancy figure, but not the face-to-face.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          6.   Against “the perfect” as
love</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>              </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                a.   This view is
usually taken by those opposed to miraculous duration, but it would be as well
suited to the opposite side since love has never been fully experienced.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                b.   There is no time
when this degree matter could be said to be reached; yet the idea of degree
preceding a culmination is not eliminated from this context because of Paul’s
analogy of being an adult <i>vs</i>. being a child.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                          This view supposes that all
the contrasts in 1 Corinthians<b> 13</b> address the same contrast. Paul
evidently has one line of reasoning in mind.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:-4.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>
