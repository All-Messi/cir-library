<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                [1]    To
show off (Matthew <b>4</b>:5-7 = Luke <b>4</b>:9-12)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                [2]    To
benefit the self (Matthew <b>4</b>:1-4 = Luke<b> 4</b>:1-4)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                [3]    To
make money (Acts <b>8</b>:18-34)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                [4]    To
satisfy curiosity (Luke <b>23</b>:8-12)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                     [5]    To
answer a challenge (Matthew <b>12</b>:38-45 = Mark <b>8</b>:11-</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-157.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                                                              13;
Luke <b>11</b>:14-26)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                [6]    To
come down from the cross</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   The moral test</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   John<b> 9</b>:31, 33</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Mark <b>9</b>:39</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   What of Matthew<b> 7</b>:22-23?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    Lying at the
judgment. Jesus may have planted the claims about miracle working as an extreme
example of what does not suffice as a replacement for virtuous living—an exaggeration
for emphasis like Galatians <b>1</b>:8-9; 1 Corinthians <b>13</b>:1-3; Psalms <b>22</b>:10-11;<b>
51</b>:5; <b>58</b>:3. Nevertheless, it could be a real possibility as with <u>Judas
Iscariot</u>, who was part of the Mission of the Twelve (Matthew <b>10</b>:1-15;
Mark<b> 6</b>:7-13; ); the Mission of the Seventy may have included the Twelve
with Judas (Luke 10:1-20),</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    Formerly faithful
with gifts plus subsequent apostasy (<u>never</u> knew?). “Never” might have
the value of saying “as good as never” since a person’s final condition becomes
that person’s eternal state. <u>Balaam</u> was a prophet that God later
rejected.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    God’s speaking
through an office held by an evil person (cp. one view of John <b>11</b>:47-53).
But God does not specially endow someone that opposes him (Acts <b>19</b>:13-16;
note the question re Jonah; Philippians <b>1</b>:15; Galatians <b>1</b>:6-7)? </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-112.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                    (4)    Living
within the general will of God, but not within the special will </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-112.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                                             of
God for that person</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (5)    Lacking love (cp.
1 Corinthians <b>13</b>:1-3)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                            *(6)    People who thought
they were doing miracles but weren’t</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        d.   The moral test does not
mean moral perfection.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (1)    Paul: “chief of
sinners” (1 Timothy <b>1</b>:15)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (2)    Peter: “stood
condemned” (Galatians <b>2</b>:11)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (3)    Samson: (Judges <b>13-16</b>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              </span></b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>(4)    David, though significantly sinful, is called a
man after God’s own </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>heart (in his life? in regard to steadfast commitment
to Yahveh alone?).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (5)    Some Corinthians
were misusing gifts.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (6)    Note the “one”
among the Galatians (<b>3</b>:5) who was supplying them </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                       the Spirit and
working miracles.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (7)    The case of
Balaam raises some unclear points.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              We may wonder how far
God allows people to drift into sin before he removes their special endowments.
The case of Samson shows that God may take gifts away. The answer may relate to
the person’s or involve some relativity factor. Relative to surrounding circumstances,
people like Samson, David, and Solomon were better than most. The fact remains
that Jesus told his disciples no one could work a miracle in his name and soon
speak evil of him (Mark<b> 9</b>:38-39).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              The moral test shows
that God does miracles through a <u>person</u>, not an <u>office/position</u>. Caiaphas
in John <b>11</b>:47-53 probably said more than he knew (cp. Matthew <b>26</b>:6-13;
Mark<b> 14</b>:3-9; John <b>12</b>:1-7 + <b>11</b>:2). Miracles are through a
person, not a <u>formula/incantation/word</u>. There is no “power” in the name
of Jesus as such, a point the seven sons of Sceva learned the hard way (Acts <b>19</b>:13-17).
In the same vein, miracles do not come through a <u>ritual formula</u>. That is
one reason James <b>5</b> does not call for a set pattern to obtain results.
(See essay in the Addenda entitled “James <b>5</b> and Anointing with Oil.”) New
Testament miracles always succeeded.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Applying the moral test
calls for making sure that God has proscribed the behavior; the moral test
converges with the doctrinal test. The Pharisees were trying to apply the moral
test when they told the lame man to praise <u>God</u> because Jesus was a <u>sinner</u>.
The claim that he was a sinner referred to his making mud and smearing it on
the blind man’s eyelids when healing him on the Sabbath. Their flawed understanding
of Mosaic Sabbath law prompted them to dismiss Jesus’ work as </span><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>“not from God.”</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
In this case, they also entertained the idea that God would work a miracle
through someone that was a “sinner.” That combination of mistakes led them to misjudge
what was happening before their very eyes.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Summary of Observations</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Divine miracles are not done through an <u>office</u>
rather than through the <u>person</u>. Apostates, immoral people, false prophets
and teachers cannot do them by virtue of ecclesiastical appointments.  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Divine miracles cannot be done by those
who do not first have <u>salvation status</u>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Divine miracles (<i>vs</i>.
answered prayer) have <u>uniform success</u>. There is no pattern of occasional
success or even usual success.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Divine miracles are done to
enhance faith. They are not sheer displays of the supernatural for <u>entertainment
purposes</u>, <u>satisfying</u> <u>curiosity</u>, <u>personal gain</u> of the
miracle worker, <u>pointless results</u>, mere <u>release from the natural
order</u> and its limitations, or <u>showmanship</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Divine miracles are not ends in themselves
or the focal point of Christian activity; miracles are <u>adjuncts to kingdom
purposes</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Divine miracles do not soon lose their
effectiveness, as when an adrenaline rush from excitement wears off later and
the malady returns.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Divine miracles must be distinguished from
other sources of the supernatural (demonic) or other kinds of events that are
not supernatural (parapsychological, fakery).</span></p>
