<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> II. Application to Holy Spirit Matters</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>             A. The relationship/state/situation with
the Spirit</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        1.   The original sending of
the Spirit: John <b>7</b>:39; Acts<b> 2</b>:1 ff.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        2.   The right to become sons
of God: John <b>1</b>:12</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        3.   The salvation status as
given: Acts <b>11</b>:18; Ephesians <b>2</b>:8</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        4.   The gift of the Holy
Spirit to individuals: Acts <b>2</b>:38; 1 John <b>3</b>:24</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Results that come (a person’s response
to influence): 1 Corinthians <b>3</b>:6 (<i>“God gives the increase”</i>)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.75in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              C.   Acts
done by individuals (“allow”): Daniel <b>2</b>:36-38; 1 Kings <b>12</b>:15;
John 5:26; <b>6</b>:65; <b>19</b>:10-11; Revelation <b>7</b>:2;<b> 9</b>:5; <b>13</b>:5,
7</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.75in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              D.  Slots
of service among God’s people: 1 Corinthians<b> 12</b>:28a; Ephesians <b>4</b>:7,
11 (means “slots” because of the word <i>till</i>) </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.75in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              E.   Persons
filling the slots: Acts <b>20</b>:28 (“made”); 2 Corinthians <b>10</b>:8 (=<b>13</b>:10);
Colossians <b>4</b>:17 (<i>“ministry I received”</i>); 1 Timothy<b> 4</b>:14; 2
Timothy<b> 1</b>:6</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.75in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              F.   Abilities
to accomplish the tasks in these slots (lists of gifts of the Spirit: Romans <b>12</b>:6-8;
1 Corinthians <b>12</b>:28-30; Ephesians<b> 4</b>:7-11)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        1.   Natural abilities: Matthew
<b>19</b>:11 + 12; 1 Corinthians <b>7</b>:5-7 (“continence”)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        2.   Developed skills: Acts <b>4</b>:29
(“boldness”)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        3.   Supernatural endowments: Acts<b>
8</b>:20; <b>10</b>:45;<b> 11</b>:17; 1 Corinthians <b>12</b>:28b</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            G.  Activities to be performed: 1
Corinthians <b>12</b></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  III. A Proposal Regarding Gifts of the Spirit</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Everything a person is able to do or
enabled to do is a gift of the Spirit.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.   Lists of gifts of the Spirit:  Romans<b>
12</b>:6-8; 1 Corinthians <b>12</b>:28-30; Ephesians </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span lang=EL
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>      <b>4</b>:7-11</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.75in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              B.  A
natural ability or developed skill becomes a gift of the Spirit when it comes
under the Spirit’s influence.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.   Weaknesses of the traditional view</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><img width=245 height=95 id="Picture 2"
src="/cir-library/images/essays/gifts-of-the-hs/image001.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in'><span style='font-size:8.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  In the traditional view the word <i>gift
</i>is  associated  with  directly  and  supernaturally  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  deposited capacities.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        1.   It encourages <u>waiting</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        2.   It encourages <u>self-justification
for not doing something</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        3.   It  encourages   <u>false
 expectations</u>,  which   brings  doubt   about   a   person’s </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              relationship to God and
opens a person up to false teachers and misguidance.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        4.   It encourages <u>misinterpretation
of something as a supernatural gift</u> with the implication of unreal
certainty and insusceptibility to correction from other Christians.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.   Strengths of the proposed view</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:8.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                       
<img width=182 height=160 id="Picture 1" src="/cir-library/images/essays/gifts-of-the-hs/image002.png"></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        1.   There is both a <u>sense
of assistance</u> and <u>a sense of responsibility</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        2.   A person can <u>take no
credit</u> for succeeding or <u>cast no blame</u> on the Spirit for </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              failure.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        3.   Everybody has something
to do because <u>everybody has a gift</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        4.   The proposed view <u>affirms
people</u> as God made them.</span></p>
