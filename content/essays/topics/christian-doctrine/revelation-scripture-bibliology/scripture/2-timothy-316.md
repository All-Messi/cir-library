<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Variables in the English wording of this verse</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      1.  <u>all</u> <i>vs.</i> <u>every</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      2.  <u>scripture</u> <i>vs.</i> <u>writing</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      3.  commas <i>vs.</i> no commas [restrictive <i>vs.</i>
non-restrictive adjective “God-breathed”]</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      4.  placement of <u>is</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      5.  <u>and</u> <i>vs.</i> <u>also</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><span style='text-decoration:none'>&nbsp;</span></span></u></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'><span style='text-decoration:none'>&nbsp;</span></span></u></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Two wordings seem to fit the context, which shows
Paul's evident concern to encourage Timothy in a good thing:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      (1) <i>“All/every scripture [is] God-breathed
and profitable . . .”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      (2) <i>“All/every
scripture,                       God-breathed,</i>    [is] <i>also profitable.
. . .”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                        (being)
<i>God-breathed</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                              (because
it is) <i>God-breathed</i>          [NONRESTRICTIVE]</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><i><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>All scripture</span></i><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>
lacks an article on <i>scripture</i>, implying perhaps that Paul is talking
about the kind of thing scripture is rather than about scripture as an
identity, the implication that would be given by the presence of an article.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            As far as words and grammar are concerned,
it is possible to translate the verse as a restrictive statement: (3) <i>“All/every
scripture [that is] God-breathed (is) also profitable . . .”</i> We have not
used that option because it is not likely that Paul would be implying that some
scripture might not be God-breathed. In the restrictive format, it would be
possible for Paul to say, (4) <i>“Every <u>writing</u> [that is] God-breathed
(is) also profitable . . . ,”</i> but in the preceding verses he has already
mentioned <i>“the holy writings/scriptures.”</i> He is talking about what would
have been viewed as “holy” by Lois, Eunice, and himself; so he does not have in
mind the broad sweep of anything that might be called <i>writings</i>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Observations</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.25in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.25in;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>1. <span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>It is easiest to eliminate <i>writing</i>
because the context is talking about the holy writings/scriptures rather than
every writing there is.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.25in;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2. <span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Omitting the commas would make <i>God-breathed</i>
a restrictive expression, which would fit with <i>writing</i>; but since that
is eliminated, we do not take a restrictive meaning for <i>scripture</i>. Though
grammatically possible, the idea is foreign to the setting here: Paul is not
contrasting within scripture but between scripture and no scripture, so to
speak.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.25in;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>3. <span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Placing <i>is</i> before <i>God-breathed</i>
is most natural because it allows <i>and</i> to have its most normal value,
especially since it stands between grammatically equal and conceptually parallel
entries.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.25in;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:0in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>4. <span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>Taking #2 and #3 in combination,
we conclude that <i>God-breathed</i> is non-restrictive (hence, commas) with <i>is</i>
placed afterwards or that it is a predicate adjective parallel to <i>profitable</i>
with <i>is</i> ahead of it.</span></p>
