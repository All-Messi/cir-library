<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            </span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>We
teach that the Bible is God’s revelation to us and that it is the sufficient
guide for what we believe and practice (1 Corinthians<b> 2</b>:7-14; 2 Timothy <b>3</b>:16-17).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We teach that there is one God who is
known to us as Father, Son, and Holy Spirit and that he created the universe
and everything in it (Matthew<b> 28</b>:19; John<b> 14</b>:16-26; <b>16</b>:7-15).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We teach that God created us in his image
and so designed us for fellowship with him and that they have all sinned and
separated themselves from God by their disobedience to his will (Genesis <b>1</b>:1-<b>3</b>:24).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We teach that Jesus Christ is the only
Savior God has appointed and that through him every human person may be brought
back into fellowship with God. Jesus Christ is the unique son of God, the
Messiah promised through the Jewish prophets of old. This Word of God became a
man, lived a sinless life, died by crucifixion, and resurrected from the dead.
His life, death, and resurrection provide the basis of salvation for everyone of
us who puts trust in him as Savior from sin and Lord of life (Romans <b>8</b>:31-34;
Galatians <b>3</b>:13-14; 1 Corinthians <b>6</b>:9-11; Philippians <b>2</b>:5-8;
Hebrews <b>4</b>:14-15; <b>9</b>:11-14; <b>10</b>:10-13; 1 Peter <b>2</b>:21-24;
<b>3</b>:18).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We teach that we are saved by God’s grace
through our faith, not because we have earned God’s favor by achieving our own
moral perfection. We are saved through Christ by identifying with him as the
one who is what we aspire to be as regards the moral quality of our life (John <b>3</b>:9;
Ephesians<b> 2</b>:8-9; 1 John<b> 1</b>:8-10). Identification with Christ means
commitment to him and to his lordship, values and purposes, which we express
formally in Christian baptism as immersion (Matthew <b>28</b>:19-20; Acts <b>2</b>:38;
Galatians <b>3</b>:26-29; Titus <b>3</b>:4-7). </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We teach that salvation means the
forgiveness of sins and consequently the gift of the presence of God’s Spirit
and inclusion in Christ’s body the church (Acts <b>2</b>:38-47).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We teach that the Holy Spirit is a divine
person who is present with all believers (John <b>14</b>:23-36; <b>16</b>:8-15;
Acts <b>5</b>:3-4; <b>8</b>:26-27; Romans <b>8</b>:9; 1 Corinthians <b>12</b>:13;
Ephesians <b>5</b>:18; 1 John <b>2</b>:20, 27; <b>3</b>:9</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            We teach that those who remain faithful
till death have the hope of life in personal relationship with God forever
(Romans <b>8</b>:16-17; 1 Corinthians <b>15</b>:1-58; 2 Corinthians<b> 5</b>:6-8;
1 Thessalonians <b>4</b>:13-18; 1 John <b>3</b>:1-2).</span></p>
