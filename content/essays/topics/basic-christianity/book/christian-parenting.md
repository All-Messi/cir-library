style='font-size:12.0pt'>Basic Principle: <u>The home is a specialized setting
for interpersonal relationship</u>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            On top of the interpersonal comes the
responsibility-authority factor that parenting entails. That <u>understanding</u>
establishes the <u>attitude</u> we take toward children and the way we <u>deal</u>
with them.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Parenting is first and foremost a matter of
relating to our young people as persons. Models for parenting are too often
thought of as like being a (1) policeman, (2) friend, (3) counsellor, or (4)
servant. The better approach is to start with personal relationship and add
responsibility to it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      <u>Love as the medium</u> of personal relations
becomes the medium for marriage and then parenting. If we love our kids, we
automatically adapt love to that special situation. We naturally avoid abuse, exploitation,
or neglect of kids we love.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
