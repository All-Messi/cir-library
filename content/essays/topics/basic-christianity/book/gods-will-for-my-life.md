and direct his special messengers?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              They were clear,
observable ways that could not be confused with chance, natural phenomena, or
normal human experience.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:103.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               b. From the biblical examples, is there
reason to think God had for all Christians a specific job for them or that he directed
each step each day?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Note the thousands of
God’s people that we have no reason to believe were specially led.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;font-family:"Arial",sans-serif'>(1) <span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp; </span></span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>He is a Father. Fathers answer their children when
they ask them a question. But note the allegorizing of a comparison here. God
always answers, but it is not necessarily an audible one like <i>“Samuel,
Samuel”</i> in the night. It can be general will for all his children.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2) <i>“If the Lord will”</i>
sounds permissive: Acts <b>18</b>:21; 1 Corinthians <b>4</b>:19; <b>16</b>:7; <u>James
<b>4</b>:13-15</u> (Hebrews <b>6</b>:3; Romans <b>1</b>:10; <b>15</b>:32; 1
Peter <b>3</b>:17; 1 Corinthians <b>12</b>; Romans <b>12</b>:6-8)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3) All things being equal, why
suppose that God would rather have X do a job than Y?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (4) People sometimes argue from
the body figure that each person has a specific role God wants that person to
do rather than another one: Ephesians <b>4</b>:1-16; 1 Corinthians <b>12</b>.
But it is one thing to say there is a job for everybody; it is another thing to
say God chooses which job he wants each person to do.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (5) In interpreting the
passages related to divine guidance, we should remember that Semitic expression
often omits intermediate causes.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               c.  Is there reason for supposing that
the biblical examples of special calling and direction are proportionately the
same now as in biblical times?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              There could be less
special attention needed after God’s program has been authenticated and set in motion.
A four-hundred-year period lies between the testaments—an intercalary period,
when there was no exact succession of prophets. The Bible is a collection of
God’s special acts in history, so the amount of special leading then does not
necessarily compare with all people throughout history.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Rather than argue
whether God calls directly today, we can presuppose the first-century setting
and apply the principles Christians then would have needed to distinguish his
calling from other kinds of things.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                           d. What determines how people
should conduct their lives and ministry?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (1)   the teaching of scripture</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2)   needs that exist                                                     
(Items 2-5 are subject</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3)   opportunities that come                                       
to modification)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (4)   interests we have</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (5)   abilities we are given</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (6)   suggestions received</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (7)   maximum influence
possible</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (8)   doing it with our
strength: Ecclesiastes <b>9</b>:10</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               e.  Do we need to know the Spirit is
specifically leading us?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                     (1)    What about <i>“throwing
out the fleece”</i> (Judges <b>6</b>:11-40)?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Verse 17 shows that
Gideon was not trying to determine what the Lord wanted him to do. He wanted to
be sure Yahveh was the One speaking to him. (In a similar vein, note Xenophon’s
<i>Anabasis</i> <b>3</b>:1:4-7) The Spirit is not doing the leading if we set
the options and ask him to choose between them, or if we set the option and ask
his direction in fulfilling it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2)   Affirm the Spirit’s
potential special guidance by promise yet be careful about claiming his special
guidance in the situation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              How do we tell the
difference between God not opening the door and Satan holding it shut? If
something apparently good does not “succeed,” do we conclude that it was not
God’s will despite the fact that it was good, the doors seemed to open, and we
felt good about it when we started?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3)   To the extent that God’s
will is specific, his guidance must be explicit.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              God holds us responsible
for what is possible for us to know: Romans <b>3</b>:25; 1 Timothy <b>1</b>:13;
Acts <b>17</b>:30; <b>14</b>:16 (?). If it is not possible to distinguish
between his guiding and the normal course of events, it seems best not to
affirm that he is specially leading and simply go ahead with doing what is good
to do as per his general will. Within any set of circumstances, opportunities
will arise for serving him. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (4)   What does it mean to be
sensitive to God’s will?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Being sensitive to God’s
will does not require a special or supernatural capacity. It means having our
heart in the right place so his concerns are in mind during our daily routine
and make our plans about the future. Instead of trying to see what is invisible
in many cases, we adopt an <u>attitude</u> of deference and proceed accordingly.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (5)   What about answered
prayer?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                     Prayer
is for benefiting and aiding us. Answered prayer may confirm faith, but it is
not often a basis for other people’s faith.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                                     (6)   God
is the more glorified by the less he has to do for us. God lets us do as much
as we can do without doing it for us.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (7)   Difficulties created by
supposing direct, knowable guidance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (a)   Encourages waiting</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:148.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-112.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                               (b)  Encourages reading
events as signs to be followed rather than as data to be evaluated</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:148.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-112.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (c)   Encourages
self-justification, hence we may not be sufficiently receptive to advice from
other Christians</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      People sometimes
reason that because they have the Holy Spirit, an idea that comes forcibly to
mind the Spirit planted there. Especially that is true if it comes during a
spiritual exercise like Bible reading and prayer. The general rule ought to be
that if we cannot tell the difference between an idea that comes to our mind
and divine revelation, then we ought not regard it as revelation even for our
own thinking, much less for directing other people’s beliefs and activities.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (d)   Tends toward
tempting God</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:148.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-112.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (e)   Requires secondary
explanations when things do not work out as planned, guilt often being connected
to the lack of success.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:148.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-112.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (f)    The specific “will
of God” is not always kept within the general will of God as it should be.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:148.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-112.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (g)   Ideas that come to
mind for no apparent reason are assumed to be revelation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (h)   Confuses result
with cause</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                                      Looking back
over a series of events makes clear the pattern they have followed, but the
supposition is that their special guidance caused the pattern. The very
occurrence of the choice eliminates all the other options, and a pattern emerges.
When we look back over the past, those other options are not “there” to be
seen.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              (i)    Can create false
expectancies. When they are not fulfilled, discouragement and disappointment
can come, perhaps even the leaving of the faith because it does not deliver on
it promises. It can make us <u>question our relationship</u> to God. Finally,
it can make us susceptible to false teachers that have claimed experiences we
have not had.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                              Acts <b>11</b>:19ff. may
be an example of non-specific will for Christians. At any rate, the very fact
that Christians may with good intent do something that in time proves not to be
good or successful would seem to indicate that guidance was not exact, special,
or direct.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        4. Model for guidance and other
post-conversion work of the Spirit</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               a.  dangers to be guarded against in this
model </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       We tend to push concepts to
extremes, to “absolutized” them. In the case of divine guidance, the extremes
are (a) no direct involvement with us and (b) direct involvement in our every
act and choice. As there are dangers in over-expecting God’s choosing for us,
so also there are dangers in “under-expecting” it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (1)   the supposition that God
is not involved at all and that Christians are on their own and alone in the
world. <u>It could create a sense of abandonment</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2)   not properly crediting
God with the accomplishments of ministry and </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>personal growth. <u>It could create pride</u>. Success
is misperceived.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3)   <u>It could create an
illegitimate sense of guilt</u> because we see the result </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>as too dependent on our own actions. Failure can be
misperceived.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               b. ways to guard against dangers of the
model</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (1)   In <u>attitude</u> we are
willing to let God have prescriptive control as detailed as he pleases, while
in <u>responsibility</u> we are willing—in light of his revealed will—to make
the choices all the way up to the biggest ones. That, of course, has been the
very thing that happened in choosing salvation itself.      </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2)   To be invisible does not
mean to be non-existent.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3)   Not to know God is
specifically leading does not mean he is not specifically leading.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (4)   God can be the more
glorified by the less he has to do for us. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               c.   implications of the model</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (1)  What is capable of an
alternate explanation is not capable of certainty.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2)   God holds us responsible
only for what we can know.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3)   To the extent that God’s
will is specific his directing is explicit.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (4)   God lets us do as much as
we can without doing it for us.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (5)   We make our own judgment
if he gives no special directing.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (6)   Let the simpler
explanation explain as much as possible: the law of parsimony means that the
simpler explanation is more likely correct. We do not appeal to supernatural
explanation if “natural” explanation is sufficient. In interpretation we call
it minimum affirmation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               d. effects of the model</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (1)   <u>Glorification of God</u></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2)   A sense of responsibility</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3)   <u>Assurance;</u> a sense
of being well pleasing to God</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (4)   <u>Freedom</u>; a sense
of freedom to use our wills based on his word. We do not need to read into
events the feeling that God is trying to tell us something.     </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        5. Summary: two models for guidance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:103.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               a. God has a specific will for each
person at each point in life. He wants to direct our choices. We search for
what that will is. The inferences are:</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (1)   If I am not getting that
kind of direction, there is something wrong with my faith.                  
GUILT</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (2)   If I am not seeking that
kind of guidance, I am not being sensitive to God’s direction.               GUILT</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       (3)   The things that are
happening to me are signs to be followed rather than data to be evaluated.   
FORCED INTERPRETATION OF EXPERIENCE TO AVOID GUILT</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       GOD IS THE MORE GREATLY
GLORIFIED BY THE MORE HE DOES FOR US.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               b. God may or may not have a specific
will for us. He does not necessarily want to direct all our choices. We are
willing to let him choose for us, but are willing to choose for ourselves as
guided by his revelation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.75in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-1.25in'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       GOD IS THE MORE GREATLY
GLORIFIED BY THE LESS HE DOES FOR US as long as we are willing to let him do
with us according to his will.</span></p>
