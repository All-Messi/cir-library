<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'> </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>INTRODUCTION</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        <u>Guiding principle</u>: <i>“I say . . . to
every person among you not to think of yourself more highly than you ought to
think”</i> (Romans <b>12</b>:3). Paul balances two important points: “not more
highly” and “ought to think.” </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        <u>Terminology</u>: a number of words and
expressions get at this concern: self-esteem, self-identity, inner security,
self-respect, self-image, self-worth, psychological strength, self-concept,
self-acceptance, self-awareness, knowing ourselves, how we feel about
ourselves. Self-esteem is not ego.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        <u>Audience</u>: the “self-esteem problem”
applies to everybody. Some of us wrestle with it more than others do,
particularly while we are young, when we have failed in our own eyes, if we do
not have positive support from family, spouse, or friends.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        <u>Relevance</u>: low self-esteem generates <i>dysfunctional</i>,
<i>unfulfilling</i>, and <i>sinful</i> behaviors. Christ came to remove sin
(Colossians<b> 1</b>:14) and give abundant life (John <b>10</b>:10). Besides,
we have a responsibility to exceed the righteousness of the Pharisees (Matthew <b>5</b>:20)
by helping each other live well. Our goal in Christ is edifying others rather
than degrading ourselves. Denying self (Matthew <b>16</b>:24) does not mean
making ourselves zeros or making ourselves out to be as evil. Instead, it means
bringing ourselves under responsibility to God and servanthood to other people
so that self-improvement comes in the context of prioritizing other persons (“self-edification”).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>I. HOW WE CAN TELL WHAT WE THINK OF OURSELVES</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        Two main types of weakness behavior are <u>attention-getting</u>
and <u>defensive</u> behavior. Both stem from low self-esteem at least in part.
We feel the urge to gain attention because other people’s approval helps us
feel good about ourselves. We “defend” ourselves because we suspect other
people do not value us as they ought. If we feel compelled to cover up our weaknesses,
we can profit instead from thinking about legitimate self-esteem in the context
of the Good News.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        A.    How compelled we feel to draw attention
to ourselves</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               1.     If we have to be the boss or we will
not participate, we are weak persons. We are afraid to associate ourselves with
anything less than ideal for fear that other people will evaluate <u>us</u> as
less than ideal.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               2.     If we are the “show off,” the “limelighter,”
“braggard,” the specialist in “one-upmanship,” the guy with the ‘messiah
complex,” “the compulsive talker,” the “smart aleck,” the “mouthy” fellow, we are
trying to raise our self-esteem by proving our ability to capture other people’s
attention and admiration.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               3.      If we are “lingers,” we create
the opposite effect of what we intend. The harder we hold on the more other
people pull away and the less satisfying our experiences with them are. They
pull away because we do not put closure on encounters. We “corner” people and “trap”
them; we pay no attention to the cues that they need to move on. We need to set
goals and get something to do so we have a healthy mix of personal relationships
and worthwhile responsibility.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               4.     If we are “tellers,” we feel
that it would show weakness to listen; it would imply that there was something for
them to teach us.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                    B.    How hard we try to defend
ourselves</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               How much we defend ourselves includes
how much it bothers us to be wrong, and how much we are inclined to “argue a
point to the death.” It is okay to be wrong; other people respect us more if we
are strong enough to admit error. In fact, when we admit weakness, we are
strong (2 Corinthians <b>12</b>:10). Besides, by backing off a little, we
increase other people’s willingness to listen.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        C.    How easily we get offended </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Here belongs “touchy” people that “have
to be handled with kid gloves,” the people that “can’t take a joke,” those who
are suspicious of friendly banter, claiming that it is really intended to be a “cut.”
When we “take things personally,” it shows that we suppose other people do
things to demonstrate their disregard for us. Supposing their dislike for us
comes from our impulse to believe that they see us as second level in quality;
when there is no overt activity on their part that communicates that notion,
the notion originates from within <u>us</u> as an indication of our <u>self</u>-evaluation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        D.    How easily we get discouraged</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               1.     “Quitters,” not just lazy
people, but people that throw up their hands in despair. Characteristically
doing that indicates that we do not have confidence in ourselves to succeed or
to win the respect of other people necessarily involved in the enterprise.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               2.     People who do no finish things
are a little different from “quitters” because they have the gut feeling that
what they do will not matter even if they do get it done.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        E.    How strongly we prefer to be by
ourselves (the “loner,” the inward-directed <i>vs</i>. outward-directed person)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        F.    How easily we compliment others</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       If we criticize a lot, find
fault, tend to put everything down, we do it because the smaller we can make
other people and their activities look, the less difference exists between us
and the quality of what we can do.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        H.    How much time we spend thinking about
ourselves</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        I.     How other people sense by our behavior
what we feel about ourselves</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        One big problem with self-image is that we
have a hard time knowing whether we are exceptional. We cannot tell very easily
how other people feel inside. One value of fellowship with other people and
listening to them is that it gives us a chance to learn how they feel. Another
helpful point is that it does not matter particularly whether we have lower
self-esteem than other people do; if we are doing the Christian things that
edify them and us, we will experience the benefit as contrast with our former
condition.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>II. HOW SELF-IMAGE IS FORMED</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>primarily</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        A.    By feedback from other people</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               Good self-image comes primarily from <u>acceptance</u>
by other people, especially by our “significant others”: parents, spouse,
siblings, peers, God. “I” tend to think of myself the way I think you think I
am.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:103.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               1.     <u>how they respond to us</u>:
what they are willing to do for us, how much time they are willing to take with
us</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:103.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               2.     <u>whether they initiate
activity with us</u>: whether they seek us out or ask our opinion, whether they
initiate conversation with us, whether they invite us into their lives (with
special information; note pattern of information flow)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:103.5pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-67.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               3.     <u>whether they focus on us</u>:
whether they look us in the eye rather than with sideways glances, face us when
they talk to us, and a number of other “body language” cues.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>secondarily</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        B.    by our perceived ability level</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        C.    by our perceived physical appearance</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        D.    by the perceived possessions we have</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        E.    by our perceived accomplishments                                                                         </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        <u>Who I really am</u> is the sum total of my
capacities, associations, and responsibilities. My <u>self-esteem</u> is the
sum total of my perception of my capacities, associations, and
responsibilities. The idea is to bring those two matters into conformity.
Interestingly, Christian people often have no better self-image than people in
the world. Their faith is not working for them in this area.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>III. HOW TO DEVELOP A HEALTHY SELF-IMAGE</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        We have some elements at our disposal: <u>being</u>
(what we are capable of), <u>action</u> (what we do), and <u>relationship</u>
(how we relate to other persons and things). We are (a) created in the <u>image
of God</u>; (b) we exist in our <u>circumstance</u>; and (c) we have purpose
and <u>responsibility</u>. The image of God in us is the interpersonal capacity
(natural). Interpersonal capacity enables us to have personal relationship with
God and other people (existential). Persons can be responsible to fulfill
purposes (eschatological). As a result, we can improve self-image by
recognizing our God-given nature, by participating in fellowship, and by being
responsible in accomplishing purposes. The framework for <u>objective meaning</u>
becomes our framework for developing a <u>subjective sense of worth</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        A.    Remembering that we are created in the
image of God                              (being)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       We are not mere animals or
complex electro-chemical processes. Since we were <u>created</u> as persons by
another Person, we have a basis for dignity without pride. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:81.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-45.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:81.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-45.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        B.    Doing something                                                                                        (action)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:81.0pt;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-45.0pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               1. <u>Being active rather than passive</u>.
We cannot have a good self-image without taking initiative. We do not feel good
about ourselves as long as we have the habit of depending on other people to
prod us along. Unless we take charge of ourselves we will not feel like we are
in charge of anything. Consequently, our capacity for purpose/responsibility is
not fulfilled. Having to be “told” all the time makes us feel forced or
manipulated into action. If we rebel, avoid, or “drag our feet,” we only
increase the pressure other people put on us and their disgust with us. That
translates into even lower self-esteem, and the vicious cycle continues. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       Avoiding responsibility
attempts to gain worth by standing outside the “system” as much as possible; we
are trying to gain self-respect by gaining freedom. But there is no way to have
the benefits of the “system” without being involved in it. In order to feel
free of the system, we have to internalize its purposes–we make them our own–so
we operate from within–by our own purposes (“internal locus of control”; “warm-blooded”
rather than “cold-blooded”).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               2.     <u>Doing rather than feeling</u>,
living by goals and values, not just by feelings and impressions. We need to
base self-esteem on factors we can affect rather than on genetic and
environmental factors that are beyond our control. We are not created equal in
heredity and circumstance. Such items then cannot form a legitimate basis for
self-esteem. But action <u>is</u> something that we can be in charge of.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        C.    Replacing law with grace                                                                          (action)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               1.     <u>Living by faith rather than
by works</u>. The fact is, there are no self-made men. We depend on other
persons whom we trust to supply what we cannot provide. We have to trust that
they are not “out to get us.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               2.     <u>Substituting progress for
perfection</u>. We cannot expect to have a strong self-image as long as we
expect perfection from ourselves. No one can live up to a perfect standard even
though we all keep the ideal as a goal. Here is one of the most important
implications of the gospel for the psychological dimension of human experience.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        D.    Converting competition to caring                                           (action/relationship)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       As long as we tie self-worth to
being better than others, we are doomed to feeling bad about ourselves. By its
very nature, success in competition (a) can provide a positive self-image for
only a small minority of people and then for only a limited time. Even for that
minority (b) their declining years bring deterioration to flesh-based skills
and abilities and erode any sense of worth based on them. Another problem is
that (c) meantime self-worth based on success in competition often creates
personal pride and belittles others. Self-respect degenerates into personal
pride and belittling others when it is set in contrast to other persons and
when it fails to acknowledge God as the source and end of life (1 Corinthians <b>3</b>:7;
Ephesians <b>1</b>:12, 14). To avoid pride, we do not have to make ourselves
worms, but at the same time we do not have to make other people worms either.
Lastly, (d) competition puts us by ourselves.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       Instead of feeling good about
ourselves on the basis of successful competition <u>against</u> others, when we
give of ourselves <u>for</u> the joy of others (love), we get our sense of
worth from the grateful response of those to whom we have given ourselves in
love (cp. Ephesians <b>1</b>:6). Self-worth based on self-giving (a) has no
limitations as to who can use it to enhance self-worth and (b) ties us to other
people in a positive way.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        E.    Changing from a material to a spiritual
orientation                          (relationship)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       We need to measure ourselves
the way God measures us. That means giving up evaluations based on physical
things and other things that “do not count.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        F.    Diversifying our investments</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                       Not <i>“putting all our eggs in
one basket”</i> increases the likelihood of something coming through for us.
Besides, variety is the spice of life. Diversifying helps avoid that locked-in,
held-down feeling. Doing something new once in a while breaks routine and has a
liberating effect on the way we feel.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        G.    Living interpersonally         (relationship)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               1.     Living interpersonally contrasts
with living <u>materially</u>; that would involve <u>im</u>personal living. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               2.     Living interpersonally is
different from trying to live <u>individually</u>; for all practical purposes
that would mean no one else is involved. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               3.     Living interpersonally means not
living <u>passively</u> because then only the other person is acting. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               4.     Living interpersonally means not
living <u>competitively</u>, which is often self- centered and manipulative and
treats other people as if they were not persons. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               5.     Living interpersonally means
living by grace rather than <u>works</u>, because works is earning the result
myself. When I try to earn the result by myself, there is no other person; I am
the only one involved. Living that way, I am not buying into the resources of
others to help bring success and thus good self-image. It also fails to
understand that since life is basically interpersonal, the results most
fulfilling to us are interpersonal ones. In turn that means the persons on both
sides of a relationship have to contribute to it and to its effects; otherwise,
there is no relationship and no effects based on it. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               One of the best ways to develop a good
self-image is to help other people develop good self-images. We need to be
outward directed into the lives of other persons. We are not only responsible
for ourselves but for other people as well (Galatians <b>6</b>:5 + 2). The
interpersonal orientation in life has many advantages including our increased
ability to be patient with the ignorant, erring, and sinful by seeing their
weaknesses and faults as reflections of self-image problems; it makes us more
understanding of them because we also face the same kind of problem to some
degree.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>               In short, living interpersonally means
taking seriously the idea that the image of God equals the interpersonal
capacity. So we need to keep ourselves around good people in fellowship and
around God by prayer, Bible reading, meditation, and other spiritual exercises.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>SUMMARY</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        Self-esteem is based primarily on feedback
from <u>persons</u>. Good self-image comes from accentuating things distinctive
to personhood, interpersonal capacity, and interpersonal relationship. Out of
good self-image springs psychological strength, emotional control, and a host
of stronger behavior patterns.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        The most important point is choosing the right
<u>significant others</u>. (1) We need to choose whose opinion is important to
us. (2) We need to choose whose fellowship we will share to provide occasions
for building self-esteem. (3) We need to help other people with their
self-image because it will result in similar responses toward us–on top of
expressed appreciation and personal fulfillment. That is evidently what Jesus
meant by saying, “It is more blessed to give than to receive” (Acts <b>20</b>:35).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-.5in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>        In short, <u>living right</u> provides the
best source of a sense of worth. Self-esteem is a by-product or righteousness.
Living right takes care of everything at once including a healthy self-image.</span></p>
