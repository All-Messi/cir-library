<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:

style='font-size:12.0pt'> I. GOD’S NATURE</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Much of our psychological strength comes
from the kind of God we serve.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  Oneness of God</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Contrast the eternal, complete unity
of Father, Son, and Spirit with the polytheistic religions: disorientation,
fragmentation, inconsistency, insecurity, distance.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Holiness</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  God is consistent between what he
says and what he does, between what he has been and what he will be. Security
and stability are implicit in the characteristics he has.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  Omnipotence</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  God’s all-powerfulness surely
implies security and relieves frustration as an underlying experience of human
existence.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  We can have the confidence that good
will triumph over evil.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  Omniscience</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  God knows our situation, what is
happening to us. We need not feel abandoned in this world’s hostile
environment.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Love</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:

style='font-size:12.0pt'>II. OUR ACTIVITIES</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  Remembering that other people have
problems too (1 Peter <b>5</b>:8-9)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Sometimes we act like we have
special problems, perhaps as a way of getting attention, maybe as an excuse for
imperfection. It helps us to understand that we are not the only ones with
problems; then we know that the world is not picking on us.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  Doing something (Proverbs<b> 13</b>:4)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Setting some goals—short-range and
long-range.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Getting busy relieves that sense of
unfulfilled yearning. Not doing something usually means wishing, pitying,
feeling despondent. We can think ourselves into depression. We tend to avoid
problems instead of attacking them. Jobs can seem like mountains till you get
started, so not procrastinating helps.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  Doing everything as to the Lord (1
Corinthians <b>10</b>:31)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   Unifies life under one frame of
reference (<i>vs</i>. fragmentation)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   Gives dignity and value to all
activities</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  3.   Helps sort out what to be doing
(Can it be done as to the Lord?)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  4.   Supplies positive motivation in
hard circumstances (Colossians <b>3</b>:22-23 re </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        slaves)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  Recognizing our limitations (2
Corinthians <b>12</b>:9-10; cp. <b>13</b>:4, 9)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  <i>“When I am weak I am strong” </i>means
a healthy awareness of my limitations; it means that I do not set up unrealistic
expectations for myself. Avoiding such expectations means not experiencing
failure relative to those standards. That, in turn, means turning over to the
Lord those matters that are beyond us, and we have a sense of strength as a
result, both in reference to what we can control and to what we cannot.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      Since you are limited,</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Ranking things by importance (Matthew
<b>6</b>:33)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Righteousness precedes peace
(Hebrews <b>7</b>:1).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        Deal with the “queen bee” and
the rest of the swarm will come along. God has planned life with provisions for
every need, including physical ones.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            F.   Reducing life to manageable units
(Matthew <b>6</b>:34)</span></p>
