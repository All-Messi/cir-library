<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            It is not so much the suffering itself
that determines whether we can handle it; it is the attitude we take toward it.
Because of the great amount of persecution and suffering endured by God’s
people over the centuries, the Bible gives a lot of advice on how to think
about pain to deal with it effectively. Major portions of scripture that deal
with suffering-pain-calamity-death are Job, 1 Corinthians, and 1 Peter. Cross
references from these books lead to other shorter sections on this difficult
subject.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>    I. ATTITUDES TOWARD SUFFERING</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt'>What
<u>attitudes</u> can we take toward suffering that help us endure it
faithfully?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  God is on my side in this experience: Romans
<b>8</b>:31-39.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            B.  This suffering is an exercise in
personal development: Hebrews <b>12</b>:11.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            C.  The experience is temporary: Romans <b>8</b>:18;
2 Corinthians <b>4</b>:17-18; Hebrews <b>12</b>:10a, 11a; 1 Peter <b>1</b>:6; <b>5</b>:10</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            D.  This experience will be for my own
good: Psalm<b> 119</b>:71-72; Hebrews <b>12</b>:10b.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            E.   Since God is allowing this, I can
bear it: 1 Corinthians <b>10</b>:13.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            F.   My suffering is probably light
compared to what other people are enduring for Christ: 2 Corinthians <b>4</b>:17;
<u>Hebrews <b>12</b>:4</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            G.  This trial is light compared to the
reward for enduring it: Matthew <b>5</b>:10-12; <b>16</b>:21-<u>27</u>; Romans <b>8</b>:18;
Philippians <b>3</b>:8; 2 Timothy <b>2</b>:12; <u>Hebrews <b>11</b>:26</u>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            H.  We expect suffering in a world like
this one: Matthew <b>5</b>:10-12; 2 Timothy <b>3</b>:12; 1 Peter<b> 4</b>:12</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            I.    Coping successfully with this
experience will remove a hindrance to other people’s conversion: 1 Corinthians <b>6</b>:1-11;<b>
9</b>:12.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            J.   God is giving me an opportunity for
personal growth: Hebrews <b>12</b>:5-13.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            K.  I am praising God by persevering in
this situation: Job <b>1</b>:8; <b>2</b>:3; 1 Peter <b>1</b>:6-7.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            L.   I am not the only one that is going
through this: 2 Corinthians <b>1</b>:3-10; Philippians <b>4</b>:14; 1
Thessalonians<b> 2</b>:14; James <b>5</b>:10-12; 1 Peter <b>2</b>:21-23; <b>3</b>:18;
<b>4</b>:1; <b>5</b>:9.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            M. I am doing this for other Christians
and for Christ: Matthew <b>16</b>:21-23; 2 Corinthians <b>1</b>:6; <b>4</b>:11;
<b>12</b>:10; Philippians <b>1</b>:29; <b>3</b>:8; Colossians<b> 1</b>:24; 1 Thessalonians<b>
1</b>:5.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            N.  Christ is suffering with me: Romans <b>8</b>:17;
Philippians<b> 3</b>:10; 2 Timothy <b>2</b>:11-13; Hebrews<b> 10</b>:33; 1
Peter <b>3</b>:4.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            O.  I am going to take the same attitude
toward suffering that Christ took toward it: Philippians<b> 2</b>:5-11; Hebrews
<b>12</b>:1-3; 1 Peter<b> 4</b>:1.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            P.   In a way, I can be happy because
persecution by evil people indicates my goodness: Matthew<b> 5</b>:10-12; Acts <b>7</b>:52;
2 Corinthians<b> 7</b>:4; 1 Thessalonians <b>2</b>:15; Hebrews <b>10</b>:34; 1 Peter
<b>4</b>:12-14.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Q.   The alternative to suffering is worse
than suffering: Matthew <b>16</b>:24-27.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            R.  God will see to it that my persecutors
receive appropriate reward for their behavior: Philippians <b>1</b>:28;
Revelation <b>13</b>:10; <b>14</b>:12</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>  II.  A MODEL FOR SUFFERING  </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            What <u>model</u> for suffering does the
Proverbial writer <b>(3</b>:11-12) and the writer of Hebrews (<b>12</b>:5-10) offer
for God’s followers to use as an attitude toward it? Are there any adjustments
to the model?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            A.  The Basic Model: a parent’s discipline
(Hebrews <b>12</b>:5-11; Proverbs <b>3</b>:11-12)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  1.   Implied attitudes (Hebrews <b>12</b>:5-12)
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   God cares (Hebrews <b>12</b>:6).
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   Suffering profits me
(Hebrews <b>12</b>:10b).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        c.   Suffering is temporary
(Hebrews <b>12</b>:10a, 11a).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        d.   I am not the only one
that is suffering (Hebrews <b>12</b>:6).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        e.   Suffering gives me
identity (Hebrews <b>12</b>:7-8).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:1.0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Parents do not discipline the
neighbor’s kids. God’s disciplining us </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:1.0in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  marks us as <u>sons</u>, not
illegitimate children.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  2.   Adjustments to the model (1
Corinthians <b>10</b>:13)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        a.   Suffering is allowed
rather than deliberate.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
0in;margin-left:.25in;margin-bottom:.0001pt;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                        b.   The model must be used in

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:-27.0pt;margin-bottom:
