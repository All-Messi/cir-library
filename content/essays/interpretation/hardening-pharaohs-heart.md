<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>How the Heart Is Hardened</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>James <b>1</b>:13-14: God does not tempt us. Our
temptations come from being drawn away by our own desires as a residual effect
of previous sin. What we are like causes us to react like we do.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            Hebrews <b>3</b>:13: 
hardened by the deceitfulness of sin</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            <i>“Hardening”</i> means <i>“insensitivity,
unwillingness to listen.” </i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            <u>Pharaoh’s heart was
hardened</u> by the plagues that should have changed it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            <u>Pharaoh hardened his
heart</u> in response to the plagues that should have changed it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            <u>God hardened Pharoah’s
heart</u> with plagues that should have changed it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>God did not just come in and take the Israelites out. The
plagues provided him an opportunity to demonstrate his power over Pharaoh and
the Egyptian gods and to do so in front of the Israelites, who did not suffer
those plagues. That pattern of the plagues showed Yahweh’s existence, his supremacy,
and his preference for Israel. They did so; he blessed them before he commanded
them to serve him at Sinai.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>The L</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%'>ORD</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> did not just come
in and destroy Egypt to accomplish his purpose. He gave Pharoah a chance to
choose to let the Israelites go. He explained the point of the miracles; he did
not just plague Egypt to be mean. Letting Israel go could have happened without
destroying the land, its people, its resources, and its military defenses.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>God not only showed his power in initiating the
plagues, but even his good faith toward Pharaoh by stopping plagues when
Pharaoh entreated him to do so with the frogs (Exodus <b>8</b>:8-13), the flies
(<b>8</b>:28-31), the hail (<b>9</b>:28-33), the locusts (<b>10</b>:17-19). God
withdrew the plagues after repeated acts of bad faith on Pharaoh’s part (<b>8</b>:15,
32; <b>9</b>:34; <b>10</b>:20; note <b>14</b>:5-9 as well). </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:.5in'><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Yahveh did not give just one but many signs and with
growing power as they went on—cumulative and increasingly obvious and powerful,
culminating in the plague of darkness and the death of the first-born, not to
mention the destruction of Egypt’s army in the Red Sea. These plagues were
miracles, wonders, and signs—<u>miracles</u> in that they were supernatural in
cause, <u>wonders</u> in their impact on observers, and <u>signs</u> in
carrying meaning.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>How God Hardened Pharaoh’s Heart</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            Exodus <b>14</b>:1-9
Pharaoh’s heart was hardened.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>                                       
Who hardened it?                                                    </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>                                    God
hardened it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>                                       
How did God harden it?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:121.5pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-13.5pt'><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Indirectly</span></u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'> (<b>14</b>:14) by turning the
Israelites around into what Pharaoh would think would box them in. What should
not have been a temptation was a temptation for <u>him</u>. What Pharaoh was
like, caused how he responded—hardening his own heart like he did.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>Two Relevant Concepts</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:76.5pt;text-align:justify;text-indent:-40.5pt'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>About God: purpose and result
collapse together under divine omniscience (Exodus <b>11</b>:9; cp. John <b>9</b>:3).
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>God does not do something and then get surprised by its consequences. He
knew ahead of the whole process how Pharaoh would react (Exodus <b>4</b>:21)
all the way to the death of his first-born (<b>4</b>:22-23).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'>            About Hebrew idiom: direct
and indirect, initiated and allowed, are acts worded the same </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-bottom:0in;
margin-left:1.0in;text-align:justify'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%'>way (Job <b>1-2</b>, <b>42</b>; cp. Genesis<b> 43</b>:23;<b> 45</b>:5, 8;
Exodus <b>14</b>:3-4; <b>21</b>:12-14; Judges<b> 21</b>:15; John <b>19</b>:11).
(“streamlining causal distinctions”)</span></p>
