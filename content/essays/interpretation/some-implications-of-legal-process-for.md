<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>SOME
IMPLICATIONS OF LEGAL PROCESS FOR</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>HERMENEUTICS,
FAITH, AND PRACTICE</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>IN
THE CHRISTIAN SYSTEM</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Virgil
Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-top:0in;margin-right:0in;
margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:.5in;text-align:center;text-indent:.5in'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Basic
claim</span></b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>: law is artificial to interpersonal process and
detrimental to Christianity; law impacts interpretation, faith, and practice.
We are calling the Christian worldview “interpersonal” because persons in
relationship are the most original, eternal, supreme, basic, pervasive,
universal, and complex aspect of the system. Christianity’s prominent
vocabulary deals with persons relating positively to one another: love, faith, grace,
mercy, promise, truth </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(in communication)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,
conscience, heart, mind, blessing, righteousness, reconciliation, joy </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(<i>vs</i>.
happiness)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>, humility, peace </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(vs. national
tranquility),</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'> spirit </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(<i>vs</i>. physical
circumcision for naturalization into a physical nation)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
All the features of interpersonalism contrast with their opposites. The first
and second great commandments (cp. Golden Rule) deal with love, the
summarizing, most important, outward-directed pattern of interpersonal action,
which produces the other interpersonal actions. As Micah<b> 6</b>:8 said of
old, <i>“What does the Lord require of you but to do justly, love kindness, and
walk humbly with your God”</i> </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>cp. Deuteronomy <b>10</b>:12-13;
Isaiah <b>66</b>:2</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Christians are under
interpersonalism, not law.</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Christians
do not operate under the Old Covenant as amended by added or subtracted
features, nor are they under a <u>different</u> covenant that is like the one Moses
gave. Christians are under a <u>different kind</u> of covenant that came
through Jesus Christ </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hebrews <b>8</b>:6-13
&lt; Jeremiah <b>31</b>:31-34</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            The
gospel is alternative to Law: <i>“You that want to be justified by law have cut
yourselves off from Christ; you have fallen from grace”</i> </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Galatians
<b>5</b>:4</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. Interpersonal grace does not take
place inside of law, but alternative to it. Law was added to interpersonalism: <i>“the
Law that came 430 years later did not annul the covenant God confirmed
beforehand. That would make promise of no effect”</i> </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Galatians
<b>3</b>:17</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>).</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> So, interpersonalism is more basic
than law, which implies that it better represents God’s intentions for people.
Law could be added to gospel as long as it was not added in a way that vitiated
the more basic, the more original function.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            John
<b>1</b>:17 is one of several texts that make the distinction: <i>“Law was
given through Moses; grace and truth came through Jesus Christ</i> [</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ὁ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>νόμος</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>διὰ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Μωϋσέως</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ἡ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>χάρις</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>καὶ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ἡ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>ἀλήθεια</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>διὰ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Ἰησοῦ</span><span
lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
</span><span lang=EL style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Χριστοῦ</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>].<i>”</i>
<i>“Grace and truth”</i> are a combination, a hendiadys. Grace lies in the
interpersonal realm; truth is reality. Christ brought in interpersonal reality to
replace law.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Summary
point: Not only did interpersonalism precede law </span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Galatians<b>
3</b>:9, 15-19</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, but law predicted its own
replacement by the interpersonalism (Jeremiah</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> <b>31</b>:31)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Characteristics
of law           </span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <u>L</u>aw
has several deficiencies. Laws are <b>abstractions</b>; hence, they are
distinct from the realities they reference, and serve as <b>approximate </b>statements
about those realities and how to function in light of them. Laws can become
extremely detailed in trying to account for every particular that may arise.
Laws rest on the <b>authority</b> of the law giver, not directly on reality
itself. Note again John <b>1</b>:17, “grace and truth.” <i>“The Word became
flesh and lived among us . . . full of grace and truth”</i> (<b>1</b>:14). Later
in <b>4</b>:23, Jesus tells the Samaritan woman that the time will come when “<u>true</u>
worshipers” will worship the Father in “spirit and <u>truth</u>.” John uses <i>true/truth</i>
fifty times in his account of the Lord’s ministry, many more times than the
other gospels; so in his account he emphasizes truth as reality.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Law
tends toward <b>rigidity</b>. That is why a legal mentality absolutizes the
features of law and does not let qualifying aspects of the real situation
affect the application of the abstract rule like they should. A legal mentality
treats the social sphere the same way it treats the natural sphere. Law has a
hard time being flexible. Law tries to nuance the rule must come by appealing
to personal capacities—whether the offended person presses charges, how the
executive and judicial parts of the operation come into play in evaluating
intentionality, <i>etc</i>. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Law
tends toward <b>externalism</b> because it must be actionable. Only when inner
factors like love, heart, conscience, trust, motive, and attitude show up in
external behavior can legal process incorporate them into evaluation. But <u>human</u>
law has a hard time incorporating such factors into its design for living. In<i>
</i><u>divine</u> law, a heart-knowing God can override the rule; but when that
happens, interpersonalism (as distinct from law) supersedes legal process.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Externalism
is evidently part of what Paul means when he talks about the “flesh” in
circumcision for <u>national identity</u>. In the Sermon on the Mount, Jesus
teaches that the commandments should include <u>what leads up to</u> what they specifically
forbid, what is a <u>lesser degree of</u> the mentality involved in the
prohibition. So, by implication, forbidding adultery should forbid the lust
that leads to it </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Matthew <b>5</b>:27-28</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).
</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Forbidding
murder should forbid the anger that <u>results in</u> it </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Matthew
<b>5:</b>21-22</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. Jesus likewise teaches that the Law
forbids what is <u>like it</u>. The prohibition against swearing falsely should
prohibit the mentality that wants to take an oath about everything </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>kind</span></u><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>;
Matthew <b>5</b>:33-37</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. To accomplish the
purpose/spirit of the law, a person has to involve the inner factors that go
with what the Law actually says. The lyrics to an old pop song illustrate the
difference between legal and real righteousness: <i>“You can’t go to jail for
what you’re thinking.”</i> But with a heart-knowing God, <i>“you might ‘go to
hell’ for it.”</i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Laws
tend to fall into <b>quantification</b> in keeping with their need to be
actionable, but in reality many things are degree not category, analog not
digital. Laws quantify so the judicial process can demonstrate consistency, and
so persons under law can know how they will be judged. But <i>“Consistency,
thou art a gem.”</i> The legal mind wants to know how much to give </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Matthew
<b>23</b>:23</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, and how often to pray, forgive </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Matthew
<b>18</b>:21-22</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, or fast </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Luke
<b>18</b>:12</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. The modifications created by knowledge,
motive, and attitude call for more than “one size fits all,” more than
consistency</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Law
is <b>straight-line</b> in its causal flow. It measures out somewhat <b>artificial</b>
punishments, which in themselves have little or nothing to do with getting rid
of a past disobedience and its impact on other people or with changing a
sinner’s future behavior. The prospect of fines, incarceration, corporal
punishment, execution, banishment, and the like tend by fear to reduce the
tendency toward wrongdoing, but they are artificial in that they do not address
the intent behind the offender’s lack of love or remove the actual consequences
of disobedience. So to speak, imprisonment does not restore for the inhabitants
the house the arsonist burned down.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Law
tends toward <b>negativity</b>; that is, it does a better job of telling what
people cannot do than what they should do </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>cp.
the Ten Commandments in the Old Covenant; Galatians<b> 5</b>:22-23</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
Law does not do a good a job of getting rid of the self-centeredness that
characterizes every forbidden sinful act.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            In
consequence of these features of law, additional deficiencies of law <u>specifically</u>
become evident in contrast to the virtues of interpersonalism. The weaknesses
already noted show up particularly in law carried out in the human domain. The
New Testament highlights law’s insufficiency as applied to divine-human
interaction—as noted in the next section.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in'><b><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Differences by being
under interpersonalism</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Two
points about categories introduce this section about the interpersonal category
<i>vs.</i> the legal one. <i>The character of a container qualifies the contents
within it.</i> Jesus illustrates that point in the wineskins parable; some
kinds of things <u>cannot be put in</u> some kinds of containers—like new wine
in old wineskins, like water in a sieve </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Matthew<b>
9</b>:17 Mark <b>2</b>:22 = Lule<b> 5</b>:37-38</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,
like unshrunk cloth mounted on shrunk/Sanforized cloth </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Matthew
<b>9</b>:16 = Mark <b>2</b>:21 = Luke <b>5</b>:36</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
In a more subtle way, items <u>put in</u> a container get affected by the
character of what now contains them, their new “context.” The nature of the
whole has a qualifying effect on the nature of its components. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <i>The
character of the cause determines what kind of results that come from it</i>, <i>“the
law of harvest.”</i> The kind of cause determines the kind of result. All the
way back in Genesis <b>1</b>:11, 12, 21<sup>2</sup>, 25<sup>3</sup>, the
principle comes out in the expression “after their kind” for physical things.
Paul applies the same principle to spiritual things in Galatians <b>5</b>:16-24;
<b>6</b>:7-8 </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>cp. Job <b>4</b>:8; <b>15</b>:31; Proverbs
<b>22</b>:8; Hosea <b>8</b>:7; <b>10</b>:12-13</span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
Walking according to <u>spirit</u> results in certain kinds of behaviors that
contrast with the kinds of results that come from walking according to <u>flesh</u>.
To say it another way, “spiritual results” do not come from “fleshly causes.” Jesus
told Nicodemus, “<i>What’s born out of the physical is physical; what’s born
out of the spiritual is spiritual”</i> (John <b>3</b>:6).<i> </i>As that
principle pertains to relating to God and restoring that relationship,
interpersonal results do not come from legal process any more than <i>“people
gather figs from thorns or grapes from thistles”</i> (Luke<b> 6</b>:44; cp.
James <b>3</b>:12). Relating to God and to other people does not come from
legal causes. These observations relate to the fact that a person cannot “play
the system,” because there is nothing “artificial” in it to fake the result
with.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Interpersonalism
is the most complex system there is. That is because the combination of will,
emotion, creativity, rationality, self-transcendence, together with other forms
of non-determinism occur on both sides of a reciprocating interaction </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>mutuality</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
Legalism is awkward, artificial, and inadequate for achieving positive functioning
in personal living. That is the subtle message New Testament writers advance,
particularly Paul. 2 Peter <b>3</b>:15-16 says, <i>“As in Paul’s epistles . . .
some things hard to understand, which the ignorant and unsteadfast twist . . . to
their own destruction.” </i>Peter says that about relationship to God and
restoring it </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>2 Peter<b> 3</b>:14-15</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <b>Freedom
</b>is a major benefit in the Christian system, as argued, for example, in Paul’s
letter to the Galatians. His interest in freedom from law has two related
aspects: release from the <u>personal perfection requirement</u> </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Deuteronomy
<b>27</b>:26; Leviticus <b>18</b>:3</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>and
release from law’s inability to make imperfect people into perfect ones again </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Acts
</span><b><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>13</span></b><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>:39).</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>
That is a double whammy: the inability to be perfect plus the impossibility to
overcoming imperfection once it is in place.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <u>First</u>,
regarding the perfection necessity—avoiding unrighteousness. Everyone agrees, <i>“Nobody’s
perfect.”</i> In the shift to interpersonalism, people gain access to the
features of personhood from both sides of a relationship. That brings in the
possibility of patience on the other side and the possibility of
becoming/growing on this side—as in becoming sinless (an objective point). It
also brings in the possibility of becoming righteous again <u>in the eyes of
the one sinned against</u>—as in becoming sinless <u>again</u> (a subjective operation
going forward). Interpersonalism brings in the subjective element.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <u>Second</u>,
regarding the justification possibility—obtaining righteousness.
Interpersonalism makes righteousness available through repentance by the
offender plus forgiveness by the offended. In interpersonalism there is an
objective-subjective variable, which law does not provide. Since law merely
defines sin, sensitizes to it, and restrains it </span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Romans
<b>5</b>:20; Galatians <b>3</b>:19</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>,
law has no feature for removing guilt once incurred. Consequently, law brings <b>condemnation</b>.
But interpersonalism says, <i>“by him everyone who trusts is justified from
everything the Law could not justify someone”</i> </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Acts
<b>13</b>:39; cp. Hebrews <b>8</b>:12 &lt; Jeremiah <b>31</b>:34</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
Interpersonalism allows the offended person to “view” offenders as like the
ideal/Ideal they are committed to (righteousness). People would be “viewed as”
righteous if they “were righteous” (<i>“as of debt/owes it to them”</i>;</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Romans
<b>4</b>:1-4</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>). </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>But beyond that, in interpersonalism there
is a “source” outside the self for <u>being viewed as righteous</u> as opposed
to actually <u>being righteous</u>—or somehow having righteousness transferred
from someone else </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>in contradiction to Leviticus
<b>18</b>:3:</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><i><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>“The one that <u>does</u> them will
be righteous by them”</span></i><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. Being viewed as
righteous, however, is all that is necessary from a practical standpoint because
the theoretical issue in itself does not undo practical interpersonal reality.
What matters is the practical manifestation of the “problem,” that is, alienation
and its consequences. To make life work, people do not have to satisfy a
principle; they have to satisfy other persons.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            This
whole interpersonal setup frees the offender from <u>guilt</u> in the interpersonal
sense, that is, freedom from being <u>viewed as guilty</u> by the offended person.
That in turn frees a person from condemnation in the practical sense, freedom
from being treated as guilty. Then the one that has failed has no more
consciousness of being a failure </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hebrews<b>
10</b>:2 and other New Testament passages about the conscience</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Under
law, righteousness is not available from “inside” because people cannot actually
be perfect </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>pursuant to righteousness</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
And, righteousness is not available from the outside because it cannot be
transferred from someone else or viewed upon the sinner by someone else </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>subsequent
to unrighteousness</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>) </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>because there is no
someone else; there is just the law. Furthermore, repentance has no value under
law; being sorry for a crime does not remove the law’s punishment for that
crime. But in the real interpersonal situation, actual righteousness is not the
most relevant matter; the ultimate reality is the relationship; and there can
be a lack of full correspondence between the two matters because people can
change—repent or forgive. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <u>Third</u>,
regarding the nature of the New Covenant: Jeremiah predicted it would
internalize what previously was external, written on stone. The New Covenant
would be written on people’s hearts </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jeremiah
<b>31</b>:31; cp. 2 Corinthians <b>3</b>:2-9</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
When persons are impelled from within, they do not <u>feel compelled</u> from
the outside. They <u>feel free</u> because they are not kept from doing what
they want to do or forced to do what they do not want to do; they have conformed
to what they want to do </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Romans <b>7</b>:13-<b>8</b>:1</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
They operate by what is <u>real</u> instead of by what <u>authority</u>
dictates, which can be artificial and not correspond closely to reality. Internalizing
creates a <u>sense of freedom</u> because people do not feel a difference
between operating from what they have arrived at on their own, and what they
have accepted from a trusted personal source.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <b>Power</b>
in this setting is psychological power: the motivation to act, the strength to
endure, the willingness to try again. This kind of power comes better from interpersonalism
than from law. Being loved by another person provides greater motivational
strength than impersonal idea, goal, pattern, or law does. Love provides
greater power than fear, a hallmark of legal process. Legalism tends toward
negativity. Law is relatively “weak,” or ineffective, through the flesh as when
physical circumcision identified a person with a physical nation.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Interpersonalism
involves <b>holism </b>because it applies to all interpersonal relations, human
and divine.<b> </b>It also addresses the inner </span><span style='font-size:
10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>motive
and attitude</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>) </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>as well as the outer aspects of
persons </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>practice).
Externalism leads to “death” because it leads to separation. </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>A
person may as well be dead if separated from the other person; hence, the
terminology</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>.) </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Addressing the inner means that inner
love influences outward to give direction (spirit)</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>to
using the physical.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            Interpersonalism
brings <b>meaningfulness </b>in that it brings “life” in contrast to law, which
brings “death” </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Galatians <b>5</b>:22-24</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
Law leads to a “dead” end because it does not bring about the desired results. Life
is meaningful existence; life is more abundant when propelled by love for and
from another person than it is by fear of artificial consequences of disobeying
law. Feeling accepted by another is more fulfilling than trying to feel good
about ourselves by doing everything all the time that we should do </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Deuteronomy
<b>27</b>:26</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>. Christ has given us <i>“the new and
‘living’ way”</i> </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Hebrews<b> 10</b>:20</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>.
Truly, then, Jesus came to give us “life,” and give it more abundantly </span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>John
<b>10</b>:10</span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:
"Times New Roman",serif'>)</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>, not only by teaching what but by
being Who.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            <b>Influence</b>
becomes the means of extending the “system” and upgrading the quality of its operation.
The Great Commission outlines an influence (via “teaching”) approach to
expanding the “kingdom” in place of dissemination by authority or force
(warfare). As a result, nobody in the system is ignorant of its Leader or its
method of functioning: <i>“People will not teach their fellow citizens to know
the Lord. They will all know me” </i></span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;
line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>Jeremiah</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'> <b>31</b>:34)</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>;
their being in the system cannot be on any other basis than getting there by personal
response to personal influence (knowing). Christianity is a voluntary system that
draws willing people by positive personal relations, evidence, and
demonstration. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>            How
people understand scripture </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:
115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(hermeneutics) </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>leads
to what they believe </span><span style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;
font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>(faith) </span><span style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>they should do (</span><span
style='font-size:10.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>practice</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>).
A further, more subtle aspect of the situation is the impact of personality on
interpretation, a factor that moves from (3) to (1) in the diagram above. Having
a rigid personality fosters legal<u>ism</u> by projecting onto language usage
what truth feels like to a rigid person, something more akin to being correct in
physical nature and metaphysical logic. Truth-as-rigid contributes toward
requiring literal interpretation whenever possible instead of looking for
authorial personal intent as the primary, universal guiding principle. A reader
recognizes authorial intent by contextual, natural, and rational considerations
relevant to the text.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:0in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
.5in'><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%;font-family:"Times New Roman",serif'>The
drive for exactness and consistency in legalism fosters restrictive <i>vs</i>.
non-restrictive reading of clauses, phrases, adjectives, and adverbs. It
assumes that being more exact is being more correct, a feature of natural and
metaphysical categories. It tends away from natural flexibility and toward abstract
rigidness, and shows itself in linguistic positivism, where <u>terms</u> are
assumed to mean the same thing if possible wherever they appear. Terms get
decontextualized by not being limited or qualified by the subject at hand, <i>etc</i>.
It overdoes—as per the nature of things—the idea that <u>related words</u> have
the same meaning instead of seeing a word’s word picture as suggesting various
nuances depending on context, subject matter, and custom of usage. It tends to take
lists, for example, as homogeneous, sequential, exhaustive, and parallel. Readers
deal with words aside from the nature of their referents and associated
realities—as if the words have a “metaphysical existence” of their own. Interpreters
handle them as formal logic handles them, and in the process confuse validity
with truth.</span></p>
