<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:14.0pt'>CONCEPT INVENTORY</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>Virgil Warren, PhD</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal align=center style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:center'><b><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  For interpreters to do their work
properly, they must be able to bring to the text an awareness of as many <u>linguistic</u>
and <u>conceptual</u> features as appear and are presupposed in the materials they
are trying to understand</span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;color:#7030A0'>. </span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;color:windowtext'>Jesus’ hearers in John <b>7</b>:34-36
wondered whether he would go among the Gentiles or Diasporic Jews when he said,
<i>“You cannot go where I am going.”</i> They could not understand him because
in their minds they did not have the possibility of Messiah’s ascension to
heaven. The same thing happened with the Sadducees when they presented Jesus
with the problem about a woman married to seven brothers in succession. They
did not understand the power of God to generate new possibilities beyond what
they could already conceive of on the basis of past experience.</span><span
style='font-size:12.0pt;color:#7030A0'> </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Below are concepts we need in our concept
inventory in order to think clearly. Deficiencies in our concept inventory
cause us to make equivocations of unlike, but superficially similar, things. We
“bend” the unknown into our known (equivocation); we “fudge” the unfamiliar
into a familiar “pigeon hole.” What happens is reminiscent of (1) a little kid
hearing the word <i>concubine</i> and thinking she heard “cucumber vine.” She
fudged the unknown <u>sound</u> over into a known one. (2) A <u>word</u> that
has a second meaning cannot be heard by people who know only one meaning. In their
mind they supply the one meaning they know in place of the one the speaker meant,
and confusion results—often without their ever suspecting they are confused. The
same kind of thing can happen with (3) <u>concepts</u>. People can “make distinction
without a difference” as well as not distinguish unlike things.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Many concepts below are clustered
together for comparison and contrast with the hope that such an arrangement
fosters greater clarity.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Typical language categories: identity
(who, what), quality, time (when), direction (logical, chronological), location
(where), means, manner (how), frequency/number, cause/purpose/result (why),
condition/concession. Manner in adverbs and verbs corresponds with kind in
adjectives and nouns as shown by the practice of deriving adverbs of manner
(quickly) from adjectives (quick).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>absolute (continuous) <i>vs.</i> decisive </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            To make something a decisive factor does
not make it the monolithic/single </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  characteristic or factor in the
whole.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>      universal <i>vs.</i> absolute: universal means
everywhere or in all cases; absolute deal with </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            highest degree.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>permissive <i>vs.</i> prescriptive (will, for example)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            what is allowed (coming from elsewhere) <i>vs.</i>
what is specific (coming from within)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>prescriptive/ought <i>vs.</i> descriptive/is</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>descriptive <i>vs.</i> evaluative</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  making known the character of
something <i>vs.</i> expressing opinion as to good ro bad, effective or
ineffective, and the like.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>primary proof <i>vs. </i>confirmatory evidence</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  what leads to a conclusion <i>vs.</i>
what fits with a conclusion</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>straight-line causal series,
reciprocation/interdependency, compenetration, transaction, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>dialectic/thesis-antithesis-synthesis</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  In the context of personal
relationships, transaction means that in the very process of one person's
relating to the other the first is shaped by the relating. For example, in
explaining something to the other person, a speaker formulates a way of wording
or modeling it, which very process shapes the speaker’s own way of viewing the
matter and perhaps clarifies it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>objective-subjective</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The difference between objective and
subjective is not clear-cut. If toothpaste or some food makes a person gag, it
could be said that it “was all in his head” because of the way he was looking
at it. While that is true, the fact that he is looking at it that way is
objective to someone else; even more so, the fact that it is gagging him is
objective. After all, that is the reason the subjective is so important. Even
if religion were only the subjective dimension, it would be important because
it impacts the objective (other people, <i>etc</i>.).</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            To some extent interpersonalism transcends
the objective-subjective distinction, because reciprocal relationship involves
both people simultaneously and interdependently. Reciprocal relationship,
interdependency, compenetration blur the objective-subjective variable.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>distinctive <i>vs.</i> most important </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            The Lord’s Supper may be the most
distinctive element in a Christian stated gathering </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  without being the most important. </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Sexual relationship is the distinctive of
marriage, but it may not be the most important </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  aspect of a good marriage.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>“necessary” can be in different senses: interpersonally,
legally, naturally, logically</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            These areas can include appropriate (need
to do “this” to avoid being misunderstood, </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            for example)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>necessary <i>vs.</i> sufficient cause</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>necessary <i>vs</i>. possible</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>direct and indirect causation</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>cause, conditionality, contingency, effect/result</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Under cause, A produces B; under
correlation, A and B both vary relative to x. Contingency is popularly
expressed by saying that something is “the function of” something else. (a)
Correlation means seeing two things as belonging together—as forming a set. (b)
Correlation may imply coextensiveness, but not necessarily. (c) Correlation
means varying together; the second and the first are a function of a third,
more basic matter.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>purpose <i>vs.</i> result</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Result is what arises from something
before it; purpose is the intent to bring about that </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            result.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>contrary <i>vs.</i> contradictory (= different <i>vs.</i>
opposed)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

categories</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>deductive <i>vs.</i> inductive</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Deductive refers to working backward from
a conclusion or full picture to the unvoiced </span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            parts or causes that make it up or lead to
it.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>category and continuum</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>            Category and continuum can relate in the
process of change. For one thing, degrees can exist within the categories. In
interpersonal relationships (and belief changes), what happens is that more and
more experiences “pressure” against the ceiling (or right end of one category
in the overall continuum) until psychologically as well as objectively a
“conversion” into the next category occurs. These “points” in a process are “direction-setting
events/experiences,” “the straw that broke the camel’s back,” “decision
points.”</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>                  Under continuum belong duration
(time), degree (kind), distance (space)</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
style='font-size:12.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-right:-.5in;text-align:justify'><span
